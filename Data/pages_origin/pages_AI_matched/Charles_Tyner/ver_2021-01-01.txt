'''Charles Tyner''' (June 8, 1925 – November 8, 2017) was an American actor.

==Early years==
Tyner was a native of [[Danville, Virginia]] and served in the Army as a combat infantryman in Germany and France during [[World War II]].<ref name="sn">{{cite news |title='Not A Puppie-Kicker' Says Hated Howard |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/34618531/sunday_news/ |accessdate=6 August 2019 |work=Sunday News |date=February 7, 1982 |location=Pennsylvania, Lancaster |page=124|via = [[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

==Career==
In 1957, Tyner made his debut on Broadway in ''[[Orpheus Descending]]''. Two years later, he appeared with [[Paul Newman]] in ''[[Sweet Bird of Youth]]'' on Broadway.<ref name="ibdb">{{cite web |title=("Charles Tyner" search results) |url=https://www.ibdb.com/broadway-cast-staff/charles-tyner-93564 |website=Internet Broadway Database |publisher=The Broadway League |accessdate=6 August 2019 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20190806154430/https://www.ibdb.com/broadway-cast-staff/charles-tyner-93564 |archivedate=6 August 2019}}</ref>

During 1959, that he made his film debut, with an uncredited part in ''[[That Kind of Woman]]''. He worked with Newman again in 1967, as Boss Higgins, the sadistic prison guard in ''[[Cool Hand Luke]]'', and became a regular character actor, appearing in films such as ''[[The Reivers (film)|The Reivers]]'', ''[[Lawman (film)|Lawman]]'', ''[[Harold and Maude]]'', ''[[The Cowboys]]'' and ''[[Emperor of the North Pole]]''. One of his better known roles was alongside [[Burt Reynolds]], in the 1974 prison comedy ''[[The Longest Yard (1974 film)|The Longest Yard]]''. Another one of his better known roles was that of the evil Howard Rodman in the television series ''[[Father Murphy]]''.

Tyner returned to the stage in 1977, but continued to appear in films. He died on November 8, 2017.<ref name=SAG>{{cite magazine |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |title=In Memoriam |url=https://www.sagaftra.org/files/sa_spring18_public.pdf |magazine=SAG-AFTRA Magazine |location=Los Angeles, CA |publisher=SAG-AFTRA |date=Spring 2018 |volume=7 |issue=1 |pages=49 |access-date=17 April 2018 }}</ref>
