He is the co-founder of the Texas Spine and Joint Hospital located in [[Tyler, Texas]].<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Swanson |first1=Holly |title=Alumni Profile: Kevin Pauza |journal=Penn State Medicine ==Career==
Pauza is a physiatrist and a founding partner of the Texas Spine and Joint Hospital in Tyler, Texas.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newjerseynewsroom.com/healthquest/chronic-back-pain-spinal-fusion-may-be-wrong-surgery |title=Chronic Back Pain? Spinal Fusion May be the Wrong Surgery |last1=Weiss |first1=Frances |date=May 8, 2012 |publisher=New Jersey Newsroom |access-date=August 13, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150703075958/http://www.newjerseynewsroom.com/healthquest/chronic-back-pain-spinal-fusion-may-be-wrong-surgery |archivedate=July 3, 2015 }}</ref> Pauza received degrees from [[University of Pennsylvania]], [[Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons]], [[Penn State Milton S. Hershey Medical Center|Penn State College of Medicine]], and [[Lehigh University]].{{Citation needed|date=November 2019}}

Outside of treating patients suffering from chronic lower back pain and degenerative disc disease, Pauza is on the editorial panel of several scientific journals, committees and boards.<ref name="penn">{{cite web| url=http://archive.constantcontact.com/fs059/1103344271496/archive/1105029022742.html| title=Groundbreaking Spinal Treatment Spurs Saudi Interest| date=March 2011| publisher=Penn State College of Medicine| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref> He established the John Dehaan Foundation which annually awards $250,000 for advances in nonsurgical spinal treatment.<ref name="penn"/> In 2008, Penn State College of Medicine recognized Pauza with the Alumni Fellow Award.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://tr22g12.aset.psu.edu/story/182531/2008/10/22/college-medicine-seeks-nominations-alumni-fellow-award| title=College of Medicine seeks nominations for Alumni Fellow Award| date=October 22, 2008| publisher=Pennsylvania State College of Medicine| access-date=August 13, 2015| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304212550/http://tr22g12.aset.psu.edu/story/182531/2008/10/22/college-medicine-seeks-nominations-alumni-fellow-award| archive-date=March 4, 2016| url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Pauza Disc Treatment==
Pauza developed the Pauza Disc Treatment and Biostat System,<ref name="nih">{{cite web| url=https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/results/NCT01011816| title=Treatment of Symptomatic Lumbar Internal Disc Disruption (IDD) With the Biostat System| publisher=U.S. National Institutes of Health| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref> a minimally invasive procedure that repairs and re-grows discs for the treatment of spinal pain and disorders including degenerative disc disease.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.skyvalleychronicle.com/BREAKING-NEWS/TEXAS-DOCTOR-FINDS-REVOLUTIONARY-TREATMENT-FOR-SEVERE-BACK-PAIN-991883| title=Texas Doctor finds Revolutionary Treatment for Backpain| date=May 6, 2012| publisher=Sky Valley Chronicle| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref> Pauza holds a series of 16 patents for a device and Discseel [[biologic medical product|biologic]] that support the treatment. The procedure involves injecting two components of blood plasma, [[thrombin]] and [[fibrinogen]], which combine to make fibrin, into damaged spinal discs<ref name="feel">{{cite web|url=http://feelingfit.com/jointandotherpain/topic/11742-8/story |title=New procedure may offer alternative to spine surgery |last1=Massey |first1=Bob |publisher=Charlotte Sun |access-date=August 13, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130531061236/http://feelingfit.com/jointandotherpain/topic/11742-8/story |archivedate=May 31, 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=http://thedartmouth.com/2010/04/02/cutting-edge-surgery-aids-student/| title=Cutting-edge surgery aids student| date=April 2, 2010| last=Karpis| first=Paulina| publisher=The Dartmouth| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref> for the treatment of spinal conditions such as [[degenerative disc disease]], [[back pain]] and chronic [[low back pain]], or internal disc disruption.<ref name="nih"/>

Pauza served on the advisory board of several medical tech companies. He served as medical advisor overseas for royal families to which he provided treatment.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.cbs19.tv/story/27927846/saudi-arabian-king-abdullah-had-a-strong-link-to-east-texas| title=Saudi Arabian King Abdullah had a strong link to East Texas| last1=Morris| first1=Risa| date=January 23, 2015| publisher=KYTX19| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.kltv.com/story/27927437/late-saudi-king-abdullah-has-strong-connection-in-east-texas| title=Late Saudi King Abdullah has strong connection in East Texas| date=January 23, 2015| publisher=KLTV| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref>
