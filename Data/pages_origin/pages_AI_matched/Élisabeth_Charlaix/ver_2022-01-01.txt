'''Élisabeth Charlaix''' (23 October 1958), is a French physicist. She is a professor at the [[Université Grenoble Alpes]], where she researches [[fluid mechanics]] to the sub-[[nanometre]] level. She was a co-developer of a [[Surface forces apparatus|surface-forces measuring device]] for very small-scale interactions.

==Biography==

She was a post-doctoral researcher at [[ESPCI Paris]], studying the properties of [[Porous medium|porous materials]] in work funded by [[Exxon]]. She joined the [[École normale supérieure de Lyon]] physics laboratory, where she studied [[wetting]] phenomena at the microscopic level and the effects of humidity on [[granularity|granular]] media. After her appointment as professor at [[Claude Bernard University Lyon 1|Claude-Bernard University]], she joined their Department of [[Materials physics|Materials Physics]], which became the ''[[:fr:Laboratoire de physique de la matière condensée et nanostructures|Laboratoire de physique de la matière condensée et nanostructures]]'' (LPMCN) [Laboratory of [[Condensed matter physics|Condensed Matter]] and [[Nanostructure]] Physics]. At LPMCN, she researched fluid dynamics at [[Nanoscopic scale|nanoscales]] ([[Nanofluid|nanofluidics]]) which progressed following the construction of an original surface forces measuring apparatus in 2002, which was developed with Jérôme Crassous and [[:fr:Frédéric Restagno]]; the apparatus uses a combination of [[capacitance]] and [[interferometry]] to measure the forces, with which important data was found concerning the link between wettability and [[Fluid dynamics|hydrodynamic conditions]] at solid-fluid interfaces.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www-lpmcn.univ-lyon1.fr/interfaces_fluides/site/|website=www-lpmcn.univ-lyon1.fr|archive-url=http://archive.wikiwix.com/cache/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww-lpmcn.univ-lyon1.fr%2Finterfaces_fluides%2Fsite%2F|archive-date=15 March 2010|title=Liquids at interfaces' Group|access-date=27 December 2019}}</ref>

In 2010, she joined the interdisciplinary physics laboratory in Grenoble.
