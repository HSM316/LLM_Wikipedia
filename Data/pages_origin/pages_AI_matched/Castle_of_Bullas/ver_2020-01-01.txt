The '''castle of Bullas''' is a [[castle]] located in Peña Rubia de Cehegín, [[Bullas]], [[Murcia]].<ref name=historia>{{Cite book |author=[[History Channel|Historia]] |title=Templarios. Del origen de las cruzadas al final de la Orden del Temple |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=YKZ4CgAAQBAJ&printsec=frontcover |date=October 22, 2015 |publisher=[[Penguin Random House]] Grupo Editorial, S. A. U. |isbn=9788401015731 |location=Madrid |edition=1st |page=417 |chapter=Apéndice I. Los lugares del Temple |editor-last=Martínez |editor-first=Gemma |editor2-last=Mínguez |editor2-first=Nines}}</ref>

Of [[muslim]] origin, it was built between the XII and XIII centuries, before the Castillian conquest.<ref name=net>{{Cite web |url=http://www.castillosnet.org/espana/informacion.php?ref=MU-CAS-026 |title=Castillo de Bullas |access-date=July 12, 2017 |last=Sobrino Torres |first=Ramón |work=CastillosNet |location=Murcia |language=es}}</ref> After the [[Mudéjar revolt of 1264-66]], it was given to the [[Templar Order]] together with the neighbouring area.<ref name=historia/>

In 1286 it was entrusted to the [[Emirate of Granada]], causing the treachery of king [[Sancho IV of Castile]].<ref name=historia/>

Later on, [[Fernán Pérez de Guzmán]] and the Knights Templar recovered it.<ref name=historia/> After the abolition of the order in 1312,<ref>{{cita web |work=CastillosNet |title=Castillo de Alconchel |url=http://www.castillosnet.org/espana/informacion.php?ref=BA-CAS-026&seo=Castillo%20de%20Miraflores |date=April 28, 2015 |access-date=December 22, 2016}}</ref> it belonged to the [[Order of Santiago]].<ref name=historia/>

In the XVII and XVIII centuries, the castle's stones were used for the construction of homes.<ref name=net/> Nowadays only remnants survive of the Casco Viejo.<ref name=net/>
