'''Masud Pathik''' (born 1 August 1979)<ref name=closure>{{Cite web|title=Cinemas under threat of closure: Masud Pathik|url=https://www.newagebd.net/article/113509/cinemas-under-threat-of-closure-masud-pathik|access-date=2020-12-06|website=New Age|language=en}}</ref> is a Bangladeshi filmmaker, poet and lyricist. His feature films ''[[Nekabborer Mohaproyan]]'' (2014) won seven and ''[[Maya: The Lost Mother]]'' (2019) won eight [[Bangladesh National Film Awards]].<ref name=aaa>{{Cite web|url=http://bdnews24.com/entertainment/2016/05/11/nekabborer-mahaproyan-dominates-national-film-award-2014-actors-hasan-imam-rani-sarkar-honoured|title=‘Nekabborer Mahaproyan’ dominates National Film Award 2014; actors Hasan Imam, Rani Sarkar honoured|work=BDNews24|access-date=2016-08-29|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160911120911/http://bdnews24.com/entertainment/2016/05/11/nekabborer-mahaproyan-dominates-national-film-award-2014-actors-hasan-imam-rani-sarkar-honoured|archivedate=2016-09-11}}</ref> Individually, he won the [[Bangladesh National Film Award for Best Lyrics|Best Lyrics]] and [[Bangladesh National Film Award for Best Story|Best Story]] awards.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://moi.gov.bd/sites/default/files/files/moi.portal.gov.bd/files/831a384a_4cd0_4005_a528_6e888b5c063f/Award-2019.pdf|title=Award-2019|language=bn |accessdate=3 December   2020|publisher=Ministry of Information}}</ref> For his poetry, he won the 2013 HSBC Kali O Kalam award for his poem ''Ekaki Jomin''.<ref name=kali>{{Cite web|date=2013-09-18|title=এইচএসবিসি-কালি ও কলম তরুণ কবি ও লেখক পুরস্কার ২০১৩|url=https://www.kaliokalam.com/9093/এইচএসবিসি-কালি-ও-কলম-তরুণ-4/|access-date=2020-12-06|website=কালি ও কলম|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Early life and education ==
Pathik was born on 1 August 1979 at [[Raipura Upazila]] in [[Narsingdi District]].<ref name=closure/> He earned his PhD from the [[University of Dhaka]] on the topic of Bangladeshi Cinema.<ref name=closure/> He took a film appreciation course in 2008 organised by [[Bishwo Shahitto Kendro]] in Dhaka.<ref name=pen/> As of August 2020, he has been researching on film at [[Assam University]].<ref name=closure/>

==Career==
Pathik edits a magazine, ''Bratya'', that focuses on the life and livelihood of the [[subaltern (postcolonialism)|subaltern people]] of Bangladesh. He also acts as the assistant editor of Pothorekha, a literary magazine in Bangladesh, and he is an executive member of Jatiyo Kobita Parishad, Bangladesh. He is the founder and chairman of ''Bratya Film'', and ''Bratya Creation''.<ref name=pen>{{Cite web|date=2017-02-03|title=Growing Up With Pen and Paper: Masud Pathik's journey as a writer|url=https://www.thedailystar.net/star-weekend/star-people/growing-pen-and-paper-masud-pathiks-journey-writer-1355197|access-date=2020-12-06|website=The Daily Star|language=en}}</ref>

Pathik's film '' Nekabbarer Mahaproyan'' won seven National Film awards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://archive.dhakatribune.com/showtime/2016/may/12/39th-national-film-awards-ceremony-held|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160915180200/http://archive.dhakatribune.com/showtime/2016/may/12/39th-national-film-awards-ceremony-held|url-status=dead|archive-date=2016-09-15|title=39th National Film Awards ceremony held - Dhaka Tribune|website=archive.dhakatribune.com|access-date=2016-08-29}}</ref><ref name=aaa/> The original winner of the Best Film Award, ''[[Brihonnola]]'', was disqualified due to plagiarism.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://bdnews24.com/film/2016/04/18/nekabborer-mahaproyan-gets-best-national-film-award-2014-after-brihonnola-fiasco|title=‘Nekabborer Mahaproyan’ gets best National Film Award 2014 after ‘Brihonnola’ fiasco|work=BDNews24|access-date=2016-08-29|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160826101147/http://bdnews24.com/film/2016/04/18/nekabborer-mahaproyan-gets-best-national-film-award-2014-after-brihonnola-fiasco|archivedate=2016-08-26}}</ref>

His film ''[[Maya: The Lost Mother]]'' was based on artist [[Shahabuddin Ahmed (artist)|Shahabuddin Ahmed]]'s painting ''The Woman''.<ref name=pen/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://newagebd.net/134630/masud-pathik-to-make-more-films-on-poems/|title=Masud Pathik to make more films on poems - New Age|date=2015-07-02|language=en-GB|access-date=2016-08-29|url-status=live|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160911091842/http://newagebd.net/134630/masud-pathik-to-make-more-films-on-poems/|archivedate=2016-09-11}}</ref>

Pathik's first poem ''Krishokphool'' was  published in 1996.<ref name=rising/> As of 2017,  he has published 14 books including ''Chashar Put'', ''Ekaki Jomin'', ''Shetu Harabar Din'', ''Chashar Bachan'', ''Langoler Bhubon'' and ''Dadar Khorom''.<ref name=pen/>
