'''Rosemary Kathleen Lavelle''' (born May 14, 1995) is an American professional [[soccer]] player for the [[Washington Spirit]] of the [[National Women's Soccer League]] (NWSL), the highest division of women's professional soccer in the United States, and the [[United States women's national soccer team]]. She started six games for the United States at the [[2019 FIFA Women's World Cup|2019 France]] World Cup, scored three goals, and was awarded the Bronze Ball at the [[FIFA Women's World Cup awards]]. The same year, she was named the sixth best player in the world at the [[The Best FIFA Football Awards 2019]] and was one of three midfielders named to the [[FIFA FIFPro World XI|2019 FIFA FIFPro World XI]].

Lavelle represented the United States at multiple youth levels before making her senior international debut in 2017. After finishing her collegiate career with the [[Wisconsin Badgers]] in 2016, Lavelle began her professional career with [[Boston Breakers]] before moving to [[Washington Spirit]] a year later.

== Early life ==
Lavelle was born in [[Cincinnati|Cincinnati, Ohio]], to parents Marty and Janet, and was raised with three siblings, John, Nora and Mary.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|title=2016 Women's Soccer Roster|url=http://www.uwbadgers.com/roster.aspx?rp_id=2981&path=wsoc|publisher=University of Wisconsin|accessdate=December 9, 2015}}</ref> She played competitive soccer in her hometown of Cincinnati, initially with the Lakota United Soccer Club and later with the Cincinnati United Premier Soccer Club.<ref name="auto1">{{cite web|title=Rose Lavelle|url=http://www.ussoccer.com/players/2014/04/04/11/43/rose-lavelle#tab-5|publisher=[[United States Soccer Federation]] (USSF)|accessdate=December 9, 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151020165207/http://www.ussoccer.com/players/2014/04/04/11/43/rose-lavelle#tab-5|archivedate=October 20, 2015}}</ref> As part of a third-grade book report, Lavelle chose to write about professional soccer star [[Mia Hamm]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/2019/07/09/before-world-cup-triumph-rose-lavelle-dressed-mia-hamm-third-grade-book-project/|title=In 2003, an 8-year-old Rose Lavelle dressed as Mia Hamm for a third-grade book project|last=Allen|first=Scott|newspaper=The Washington Post|date=July 9, 2019|accessdate=July 11, 2019}}</ref> While attending [[Mount Notre Dame High School]] in Ohio, she played on the girl's varsity soccer team all four years. She played as a Badger at the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]] and was added to the roster for the United States Women's National Soccer Team in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rose Lavelle Named as Nominee for US Soccer Young Female Athlete of the Year|url=http://www.cincinnatiunitedsoccer.com/Default.aspx?tabid=292257|website=Cincinnati United|accessdate=December 9, 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151222110953/http://www.cincinnatiunitedsoccer.com/Default.aspx?tabid=292257|archivedate=December 22, 2015}}</ref>

A four-year varsity girl's soccer player at [[Mount Notre Dame High School]], Lavelle was named Cincinnati's Player of the Year by ''[[The Cincinnati Enquirer]]'' her senior year. The same year, she scored 15 goals (38 points) for her team. Lavelle finished her high school career as the team's leading scorer with 57 goals. She was twice-named NSCAA All-Region as a junior and senior and received first-team all-state honors. She was awarded the Greater Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky Sports Women of the Year award in 2013.<ref name="auto"/>

Lavelle played club soccer for Cincinnati United Premier team starting on the U-14 premier league for five years until attending college.<ref name="auto1"/>

===Wisconsin Badgers, 2013–2016===
Accepted to the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]] in 2013, Lavelle was a starting player in her first 19 matches in the [[Big Ten Conference]]. She was named Freshman of the Year, led the team with 73 shots, and her six goals ranked second on the team.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rose Lavelle|url=http://www.ussoccer.com/players/2014/04/04/11/43/rose-lavelle#tab-4|publisher=United States Soccer Federation (USSF)|accessdate=December 9, 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151020165207/http://www.ussoccer.com/players/2014/04/04/11/43/rose-lavelle#tab-4|archivedate=October 20, 2015}}</ref> As a junior, she was named first-team All-American by NSCAA. The last time a Wisconsin Badger achieved this was in 1991.<ref name="auto"/>

==Club career==
===Summer Leagues, 2014–2016===
Lavelle played with the [[Dayton Dutch Lions WFC|Dayton Dutch Lions]] in the [[USL W-League]] during the 2014 season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ddlfc.com/players/rose-lavelle/|title=2014 Lady Lions|publisher=Dayton Dutch Lions}}</ref>
During her college break in the summer of 2015, Lavelle played for the [[Seattle Sounders Women]] of the W-League, where she was named all-league.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://goalwa.wordpress.com/2015/05/12/sounders-women-add-wisconsins-rose-lavelle/|title=Sounders Women add Wisconsin's Rose Lavelle|date=May 12, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://goalnation.com/w-league-2015-all-league-team/|title=W-LEAGUE 2015 ALL-LEAGUE TEAM|date=July 22, 2015|website=|access-date=April 17, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180723065514/http://goalnation.com/w-league-2015-all-league-team/|archive-date=July 23, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>
After the W-League folded she returned to the Lions for the 2016 [[Women's Premier Soccer League]] season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.daytondailynews.com/sports/rose-lavelle-kicking-with-dutch-lions-national-team/kGz5z8Y9H5YzPFbRGkVR0N/|title=Rose LaVelle kicking it with Dutch Lions, U.S. National Team|first=Debbie |last=Juniewicz|date=June 2, 2016 | work= [[Dayton Daily News]]}}</ref>

===Boston Breakers, 2017===
On January 12, 2017, Lavelle was selected as the number one overall draft pick at the [[2017 NWSL College Draft]] by the [[Boston Breakers]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Wisconsin's Rose Lavelle No. 1 overall pick in NWSL draft|url=http://www.foxsports.com/wisconsin/story/wisconsin-badgers-rose-lavelle-no-1-pick-boston-breakers-011217|publisher=Fox Sports|accessdate=January 16, 2017|date=January 12, 2017}}</ref> Lavelle started her professional career well, scoring two goals in eight games and was named Player of the Month for April.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.womenssoccerunited.com/rose-lavelle-nwsl-player-of-the-month/|title=Boston Breakers midfielder Rose Lavelle was voted the National Women's Soccer League Player of the Month for the month of April of the 2017 NWSL season.|accessdate=March 17, 2019|date=May 4, 2017}}</ref> However, after suffering a hamstring injury in June while on international duty, Lavelle would miss over two months and finished the season with only ten appearances with the team.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.excellesports.com/news/boston-rose-lavelle-injury-uswnt/|title=Boston midfielder Rose Lavelle to miss four to six weeks|publisher=Nick Forrester|date=June 14, 2017|website=|access-date=January 25, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180126125518/http://www.excellesports.com/news/boston-rose-lavelle-injury-uswnt/|archive-date=January 26, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> The Breakers then folded before the start of the 2018 season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.oregonlive.com/timbers/index.ssf/2018/01/reports_nwsls_boston_breakers.html|title=Boston Breakers will fold ahead of 2018 NWSL season|first=Jamie |last=Goldberg|date=January 25, 2018 |work=[[The Oregonian]]}}</ref>

===Washington Spirit, 2018–present===
A [[2018 NWSL Dispersal Draft|dispersal draft]] was held by the [[National Women's Soccer League|NWSL]] to distribute Breakers players across the league. Lavelle was the first overall pick by [[Washington Spirit]], who acquired the first pick through a trade with [[Sky Blue FC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://washingtonspirit.com/pro/washington-spirit-selects-uswnt-midfielder-rose-lavelle-with-top-overall-pick-in-nwsl-dispersal-draft/|title=Washington Spirit selects USWNT midfielder Rose Lavelle with top overall pick in NWSL Dispersal Draft|publisher=washingtonspirit.com|date=January 30, 2018|accessdate=January 30, 2018}}</ref> Due to injury and international duty Lavelle only appeared in eleven games for the Spirit in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nwslsoccer.com/players/rose-lavelle|title=Rose Lavelle|accessdate=March 17, 2019}}</ref>

==International career==
Lavelle represented the United States at multiple youth levels. On November 24, 2015, Lavelle was called to train with the United States women's national soccer team.<ref name="auto"/> One of eight players who joined the team's Victory Tour following the [[2015 FIFA Women's World Cup|2015 Women's World Cup]], she earned her first senior international cap on March 4, 2017, during a match against [[England women's national football team|England]] at the [[2017 SheBelieves Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Turer|first1=Adam|last2=Laughman|first2=Melanie|title=U.S. women's soccer team calls up CUP, MND grad Lavelle|url=http://www.cincinnati.com/story/sports/high-school/high-school-sports/2015/11/24/us-wnt-calls-up-cup-mnd-grad-lavelle/76314808/|website=Cincinnati|accessdate=December 9, 2015}}</ref> She was named Player of the Match following the team's 1–0 loss.<ref name="us_debut">{{cite web|title=A Sweet Debut for Rose Lavelle|url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/03/05/21/13/20170305-feat-wnt-a-sweet-debut-for-rose-lavelle|publisher=United States Soccer Federation (USSF)|accessdate=March 6, 2017|date=March 5, 2017}}</ref>

===2019 FIFA Women's World Cup, France ===
In September 2018, Lavelle was named to the national team roster for the [[2018 CONCACAF Women's Championship]], the qualifying tournament for the [[2019 FIFA Women's World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2018/09/20/00/27/20180919-news-wnt-2018-world-cup-qualifying-roster-release|title=U.S. WNT ROSTER NAMED FOR 2018 CONCACAF WOMEN'S CHAMPIONSHIP|accessdate=September 21, 2018|date=September 19, 2018}}</ref> She appeared in all five games for the U.S. and scored three goals. Her final goal of the tournament was scored in the second minute of the championship game against [[Canada women's national soccer team|Canada]], helping the U.S to a 2–0 victory, and their second straight CONCACAF Championship.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.concacaf.com/en/article/canada-v-united-states-recap|title=Lavelle and Morgan lift the United States over Canada for the 2018 CWC title|accessdate=March 17, 2019|date=October 18, 2018}}</ref> 

Lavelle started six games for the U.S. at the [[2019 FIFA Women's World Cup]] in France, scored three goals, and was awarded the Bronze Ball at the [[FIFA Women's World Cup awards]] as the third best player in the tournament.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/2019/07/15/uswnts-rose-lavelle-is-back-world-cup-will-not-play-washington-spirit-this-weekend/|title=USWNT’s Rose Lavelle is back from World Cup but will not play for Washington Spirit this weekend|last=Goff|first=Steven|website=Washington Post|language=en|access-date=2019-11-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.wcpo.com/blogs/sports/rose-lavelle-cincinnatis-world-cup-star-takes-time-to-give-back-to-her-hometown|title=World Cup star gives hometown fans extra serving of charm|date=2019-07-18|website=WCPO|language=en|access-date=2019-11-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/young-rose-lavelle-has-been-a-revelation-for-u-s-soccers-world-cup-run/|title=Young Rose Lavelle has been a revelation for U.S. soccer’s World Cup run|date=2019-07-05|website=The Seattle Times|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-18}}</ref> During the team's first group stage match against [[Thailand women's national football team|Thailand]], Lavelle scored a brace helping the U.S. win 13–0. During the U.S. knockout round match against Spain, she drew a penalty kick that was converted by [[Megan Rapinoe]] to seal the team's 2–1 win and advance to the quarterfinals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/06/24/usa-spain-womens-world-cup-video-highlights-morgan-rapinoe-lavelle|title=Women's World Cup Highlights: USA vs. Spain|website=SI.com|language=en|access-date=2019-11-18}}</ref> Lavelle scored the U.S.' second goal in the final against the [[Netherlands women's national football team|Netherlands]] helping the team win 2–0.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cincinnati.com/story/sports/soccer/2019/07/07/rose-lavelle-scores-goal-fifa-womens-world-cup-final/1668477001/|title=Rose Lavelle scores goal for USWNT in FIFA Women's World Cup final, earns Bronze Ball|last=Clark|first=Dave|website=Cincinnati.com|language=en|access-date=2019-11-18}}</ref>

Lavelle was named one of the world's top 11 players by the [[The Best FIFA Football Awards 2019]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cincinnati.com/story/sports/soccer/fc-cincinnati/2019/09/24/rose-lavelle-named-one-11-best-players-fifa-awards-show/2428506001/|title=Rose Lavelle voted one of the 11 best players in the world at FIFA awards show|last=Brennan|first=Patrick|website=Cincinnati.com|language=en|access-date=2019-11-18}}</ref> (finishing sixth), and was chosen as one of the world's top three midfielders by her professional peers in the 2019 [[FIFA FIFPro World XI]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ktvn.com/story/41087606/rose-lavelle-named-to-fifpro-best-11|title=Rose Lavelle named to FIFPRO Best 11|website=www.ktvn.com|language=en|access-date=2019-11-18}}</ref>
