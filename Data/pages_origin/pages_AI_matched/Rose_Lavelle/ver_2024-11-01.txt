'''Rosemary Kathleen Lavelle''' {{Post-nominals|size=100%|OLY}} ({{IPAc-en|l|ə|ˈ|v|ɛ|l}} {{respell|lə|VEL}}; born May 14, 1995)<ref>{{cite web |url=https://olympics.com/en/paris-2024/athlete/rose-lavelle_1943567 |title=LAVELLE Rose |work=[[Paris 2024 Olympics]] |access-date=11 August 2024}}</ref> is an American professional [[soccer]] player who plays as a [[midfielder]] for [[NJ/NY Gotham FC]] of the [[National Women's Soccer League]] (NWSL) and the [[United States women's national soccer team|United States national team]].

After playing collegiately for the [[Wisconsin Badgers]], Lavelle was drafted first overall by the [[Boston Breakers]] in the [[2017 NWSL College Draft]] before moving to the [[Washington Spirit]] a year later. She then played for [[Manchester City W.F.C.|Manchester City]] and [[OL Reign]] before signing with Gotham FC.

Lavelle represented the United States at multiple youth levels before making her senior international debut in 2017. She started six games for the United States at the [[2019 FIFA Women's World Cup|2019 World Cup]], scoring three goals, and was awarded the [[FIFA Women's World Cup awards#Golden Ball|Bronze Ball]]. The same year, she was named the sixth best player in the world at [[The Best FIFA Football Awards 2019]] and was named to the [[FIFA FIFPro World XI|2019 FIFA FIFPro World XI]]. She scored one goal at the [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2020 Tokyo Olympics]] on the way to a bronze medal and won gold at the [[Football at the 2024 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2024 Paris Olympics]].

== Early life ==
Lavelle was born in [[Cincinnati, Ohio]], to parents Marty and Janet, and was raised with three siblings, John, Nora and Mary.<ref name="auto">{{cite web |title=2016 Women's Soccer Roster |url=http://www.uwbadgers.com/roster.aspx?rp_id=2981&path=wsoc |publisher=University of Wisconsin |access-date=December 9, 2015}}</ref> She played competitive soccer initially with GSSA Sycamore United Club before moving to Lakota United Soccer Club and later with Cincinnati United Premier Soccer Club. She credits her love of soccer to long-time Cincinnati Soccer Trainer Neil Bradford, who began as her trainer at age 8 and predicted her rise to the Women's Soccer National Team as a youth.<ref name="auto1">{{cite web |title=Rose Lavelle |url=http://www.ussoccer.com/players/2014/04/04/11/43/rose-lavelle#tab-5 |publisher=[[United States Soccer Federation]] (USSF) |access-date=December 9, 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151020165207/http://www.ussoccer.com/players/2014/04/04/11/43/rose-lavelle#tab-5 |archive-date=October 20, 2015}}</ref> As part of a third-grade book report, Lavelle chose to write about professional soccer star [[Mia Hamm]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/2019/07/09/before-world-cup-triumph-rose-lavelle-dressed-mia-hamm-third-grade-book-project/ |title=In 2003, an 8-year-old Rose Lavelle dressed as Mia Hamm for a third-grade book project |last=Allen |first=Scott |newspaper=The Washington Post |date=July 9, 2019 |access-date=July 11, 2019}}</ref>

A four-year varsity girls' soccer player at [[Mount Notre Dame High School]], Lavelle was named Cincinnati's Player of the Year by ''[[The Cincinnati Enquirer]]'' in her senior year. The same year, she scored 15 goals (38 points) for her team. Lavelle finished her high school career as the team's leading scorer with 57 goals. She was twice-named NSCAA All-Region, as a junior and senior, and received first-team all-state honors. She was awarded the Greater Cincinnati/Northern Kentucky Sports Women of the Year award in 2013.<ref name="auto"/>

===Wisconsin Badgers, 2013–2016===
Lavelle was accepted to play [[college soccer]] at the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]] in 2013. A four-year starter, Lavelle made 19 appearances as a freshman, scoring 6 goals and registering 7 assists on the way to being named [[Big Ten Conference]] Freshman of the Year.<ref name="freshmanOTY">{{cite web |last1=Corcoran |first1=Dan |title=Wisconsin's Lavelle at top of her class |url=https://badgerherald.com/sports/2013/11/05/wisconsins-lavelle-top-class/ |website=The Badger Herald |date=November 5, 2013}}</ref> Wisconsin won the [[Big Ten Women's Soccer Tournament]] in 2014.<ref name="2014 big10">{{cite web |title=Ring 'em up: Badgers win Big Ten tourney title in thriller |url=https://uwbadgers.com/news/2014/11/9/Ring_em_up_Badgers_win_Big_Ten_tourney_title_in_thriller.aspx |website=Wisconsin Badgers |date=November 9, 2014}}</ref> Lavelle was named Big Ten midfielder of the year consecutively in 2015 and 2016.<ref name="midfielderOTY">{{cite web |title=Badgers' Rose Lavelle Named Big Ten Midfielder of the Year |url=https://thesportsdaily.com/2016/10/28/badgers-rose-lavelle-named-big-ten-midfielder-of-the-year/ |website=The Sports Daily |language=en |date=October 28, 2016}}</ref> In 2015, she was also named first-team All-American by the [[National Soccer Coaches Association of America]] (NSCAA),<ref name="NSCAA">{{cite web |title=Rose Lavelle named first-team All-American by NSCAA |url=https://uwbadgers.com/news/2015/12/4/WSOC_1204154908.aspx |website=Wisconsin Badgers |date=December 4, 2015}}</ref> the first Wisconsin Badger to do so since 1991.<ref name="auto"/>

===Summer Leagues, 2014–2016===
Lavelle played with the [[Dayton Dutch Lions WFC|Dayton Dutch Lions]] in the [[USL W-League (1995–2015)|USL W-League]] during the 2014 season.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://ddlfc.com/players/rose-lavelle/ |title=2014 Lady Lions |publisher=Dayton Dutch Lions |access-date=January 25, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180126184923/http://ddlfc.com/players/rose-lavelle/ |archive-date=January 26, 2018 |url-status=dead}}</ref> During her college break in the summer of 2015, Lavelle played for the [[Seattle Sounders Women]] of the W-League, where she was named in the All-League Team.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://goalwa.wordpress.com/2015/05/12/sounders-women-add-wisconsins-rose-lavelle/ |title=Sounders Women add Wisconsin's Rose Lavelle |date=May 12, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://goalnation.com/w-league-2015-all-league-team/ |title=W-LEAGUE 2015 ALL-LEAGUE TEAM |date=July 22, 2015 |access-date=April 17, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180723065514/http://goalnation.com/w-league-2015-all-league-team/ |archive-date=July 23, 2018 |url-status=dead}}</ref>
After the W-League folded she returned to the Lions for the 2016 [[Women's Premier Soccer League]] season.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.daytondailynews.com/sports/rose-lavelle-kicking-with-dutch-lions-national-team/kGz5z8Y9H5YzPFbRGkVR0N/ |title=Rose LaVelle kicking it with Dutch Lions, U.S. National Team |first=Debbie |last=Juniewicz |date=June 2, 2016 |work=[[Dayton Daily News]]}}</ref>

===Boston Breakers, 2017===
On January 12, 2017, Lavelle was selected number one overall at the [[2017 NWSL College Draft]] by the [[Boston Breakers]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Wisconsin's Rose Lavelle No. 1 overall pick in NWSL draft |url=http://www.foxsports.com/wisconsin/story/wisconsin-badgers-rose-lavelle-no-1-pick-boston-breakers-011217 |work=Fox Sports |access-date=January 16, 2017 |date=January 12, 2017}}</ref> Lavelle started her professional career by scoring two goals in eight games and was named Player of the Month for April.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.womenssoccerunited.com/rose-lavelle-nwsl-player-of-the-month/ |title=Boston Breakers midfielder Rose Lavelle was voted the National Women's Soccer League Player of the Month for the month of April of the 2017 NWSL season. |access-date=March 17, 2019 |date=May 4, 2017 |archive-date=April 11, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230411164050/https://www.womenssoccerunited.com/rose-lavelle-nwsl-player-of-the-month/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> However, after suffering a hamstring injury in June while on international duty, Lavelle missed over two months and finished the season with only ten appearances as a rookie.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.excellesports.com/news/boston-rose-lavelle-injury-uswnt/ |title=Boston midfielder Rose Lavelle to miss four to six weeks |publisher=Nick Forrester |date=June 14, 2017 |access-date=January 25, 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180126125518/http://www.excellesports.com/news/boston-rose-lavelle-injury-uswnt/ |archive-date=January 26, 2018 |url-status=dead}}</ref> The Breakers folded before the start of the 2018 season.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.oregonlive.com/timbers/index.ssf/2018/01/reports_nwsls_boston_breakers.html |title=Boston Breakers will fold ahead of 2018 NWSL season |first=Jamie |last=Goldberg |date=January 25, 2018 |work=[[The Oregonian]]}}</ref>

(women)|Everton]] in the [[2020–21 FA Women's League Cup]],<ref>{{cite web |title=Man City 3–1 Everton |url=https://womenscompetitions.thefa.com/Article/Man-City-3-1-Everton-071020 |website=womenscompetitions.thefa.com}}</ref> scoring her second goal against [[Liverpool F.C. Women|Liverpool]] a month later in the same competition.<ref>{{cite web |title=FA Women's Continental Tyres League Cup report: Liverpool 0–3 Manchester City |url=https://womenscompetitions.thefa.com/en/Article/liverpool-manchester-city-041120 |website=womenscompetitions.thefa.com}}</ref> On January 31, 2021, Lavelle scored her first WSL goal as a substitute during City's 4–0 win over [[West Ham United F.C. ===OL Reign, 2021–2023===
On May 17, 2021, [[OL Reign]] announced the return of Lavelle to the NWSL as an allocated player having acquired her playing rights prior to her move to England in August 2020.<ref name="Reign trade"/><ref>{{cite web |title=Rose Lavelle Joins OL Reign as U.S. Allocated Player |url=https://www.olreign.com/news/lavelle |website=OL Reign |access-date=July 14, 2021 |archive-date=April 11, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230411164041/https://www.olreign.com/news/lavelle |url-status=dead }}</ref>

2022 was Lavelle's best statistical season in the NWSL. She scored five goals over 18 starts for OL Reign and was a crucial component of the franchise's third [[NWSL Shield]] victory.<ref>{{cite web |title=Rose Lavelle |url=https://www.nwslsoccer.com/players/rosemary-kathleen-lavelle/stats?season=bus8n60i95d4ka5frxvgcrspg |website=NWSLSoccer.com |publisher=National Women's Soccer League}}</ref>

Lavelle missed the majority of the 2023 NWSL season due to injury and being rostered for the [[2023 FIFA Women's World Cup]]. She returned for the postseason, helping OL Reign secure a spot in the 2023 NWSL Championship in [[San Diego]]. In the title match, Lavelle scored OL Reign's only goal in a 2–1 defeat to [[Gotham FC]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.nwslsoccer.com/game/ol-reign-vs-nj-ny-gotham-fc-2023-11-11/latest |title=National Women's Soccer League Official Site &#124; NWSL |date=November 13, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231113014339/https://www.nwslsoccer.com/game/ol-reign-vs-nj-ny-gotham-fc-2023-11-11/latest |archive-date=November 13, 2023}}</ref>

=== NJ/NY Gotham FC, 2024–present ===
On January 4, 2024, [[NJ/NY Gotham FC]] announced that Lavelle had signed a three-year deal with the club.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2024-01-04 |title=USWNT duo Rose Lavelle, Emily Sonnett become latest free agent signings for NWSL champions NJ/NY Gotham FC |url=https://www.cbssports.com/soccer/news/uswnt-duo-rose-lavelle-emily-sonnett-become-latest-free-agent-signings-for-nwsl-champions-njny-gotham-fc/ |access-date=2024-01-04 |website=CBSSports.com |language=en}}</ref> On April 28, 2024, Lavelle made her debut for Gotham FC against [[Racing Louisville]] after missing the start of the season due to a lower leg injury. She scored an equalizing goal in stoppage time, leading to a 1-1 draw.<ref>{{cite web |title=Match Recap: Gotham FC Draws Racing Louisville 1-1 After Two Stoppage Time Goals |url=https://www.gothamfc.com/news_article/show/1308396 |website=NY/NJ Gotham FC |date=April 28, 2024}}</ref>

Lavelle showcased an exceptional run of form in her first few months with Gotham FC. Over the 12 matches she appeared in before the Olympic break, she scored five goals and was named to the NWSL's Best XI for the month of June 2024.<ref>{{cite web |title=Gotham FC’s Rose Lavelle and Jenna Nighswonger named to NWSL’s Best XI of the Month, presented by Amazon Prime |url=https://www.gothamfc.com/news_article/show/1314011 |website=Gotham FC |access-date=26 September 2024}}</ref>

==International career==
Lavelle represented the United States at multiple youth levels. On November 24, 2015, Lavelle was called up to train with the senior [[United States women's national soccer team]].<ref name="auto"/> One of eight players who joined the team's Victory Tour following the [[2015 Women's World Cup]], she earned her first senior international cap on March 4, 2017, during a match against [[England women's national football team|England]] at the [[2017 SheBelieves Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Turer |first1=Adam |last2=Laughman |first2=Melanie |title=U.S. women's soccer team calls up CUP, MND grad Lavelle |url=http://www.cincinnati.com/story/sports/high-school/high-school-sports/2015/11/24/us-wnt-calls-up-cup-mnd-grad-lavelle/76314808/ |website=Cincinnati |access-date=December 9, 2015}}</ref> She was named Player of the Match following the team's 1–0 loss.<ref name="us_debut">{{cite web |title=A Sweet Debut for Rose Lavelle |url=http://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2017/03/05/21/13/20170305-feat-wnt-a-sweet-debut-for-rose-lavelle |publisher=United States Soccer Federation (USSF) |access-date=March 6, 2017 |date=March 5, 2017}}</ref>

On June 4, 2024, Lavelle became the 43rd woman to appear in 100 matches for the U.S. Women's National Team, starting in a 3–0 victory over [[South Korea women's national football team|South Korea]].<ref name="wlwt.com">{{cite web |title=Rose Lavelle notches 100th USWNT cap in 3-0 win over South Korea |url=https://www.wlwt.com/article/rose-lavelle-uswnt-100th-cap-cincinnati-soccer/61002736 |website=WLWT |date=June 5, 2024 |access-date=5 June 2024}}</ref>

===2019 FIFA Women's World Cup===
In September 2018, Lavelle was named to the national team roster for the [[2018 CONCACAF Women's Championship]], the qualifying tournament for the [[2019 FIFA Women's World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ussoccer.com/stories/2018/09/20/00/27/20180919-news-wnt-2018-world-cup-qualifying-roster-release |title=U.S. WNT ROSTER NAMED FOR 2018 CONCACAF WOMEN'S CHAMPIONSHIP |access-date=September 21, 2018 |date=September 19, 2018}}</ref> She appeared in all five games for the U.S. and scored three goals. Her final goal of the tournament was scored in the second minute of the championship game against [[Canada women's national soccer team|Canada]], helping the U.S. to a 2–0 victory, and their second straight CONCACAF Championship.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.concacaf.com/en/article/canada-v-united-states-recap |title=Lavelle and Morgan lift the United States over Canada for the 2018 CWC title |access-date=March 17, 2019 |date=October 18, 2018}}</ref>

Lavelle started six games for the U.S. at the [[2019 FIFA Women's World Cup]] in France, scored three goals, and was awarded the Bronze Ball at the [[FIFA Women's World Cup awards]] as the third best player in the tournament.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/sports/2019/07/15/uswnts-rose-lavelle-is-back-world-cup-will-not-play-washington-spirit-this-weekend/ |title=USWNT's Rose Lavelle is back from World Cup but will not play for Washington Spirit this weekend |last=Goff |first=Steven |newspaper=Washington Post |language=en |access-date=November 18, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.wcpo.com/blogs/sports/rose-lavelle-cincinnatis-world-cup-star-takes-time-to-give-back-to-her-hometown |title=World Cup star gives hometown fans extra serving of charm |date=July 18, 2019 |website=WCPO |language=en |access-date=November 18, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/sports/young-rose-lavelle-has-been-a-revelation-for-u-s-soccers-world-cup-run/ |title=Young Rose Lavelle has been a revelation for U.S. soccer's World Cup run |date=July 5, 2019 |website=The Seattle Times |language=en-US |access-date=November 18, 2019}}</ref> During the team's first group stage match against [[Thailand women's national football team|Thailand]], Lavelle scored a brace helping the U.S. win 13–0. During the U.S. knockout round match against Spain, she drew a penalty kick that was converted by [[Megan Rapinoe]] to seal the team's 2–1 win and advance to the quarterfinals.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.si.com/soccer/2019/06/24/usa-spain-womens-world-cup-video-highlights-morgan-rapinoe-lavelle |title=Women's World Cup Highlights: USA vs. Spain |website=SI.com |date=June 24, 2019 |language=en |access-date=November 18, 2019}}</ref> Lavelle scored the U.S.' second goal in the final against the [[Netherlands women's national football team|Netherlands]] helping the team win 2–0.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.cincinnati.com/story/sports/soccer/2019/07/07/rose-lavelle-scores-goal-fifa-womens-world-cup-final/1668477001/ |title=Rose Lavelle scores goal for USWNT in FIFA Women's World Cup final, earns Bronze Ball |last=Clark |first=Dave |website=Cincinnati.com |language=en |access-date=November 18, 2019}}</ref>

Lavelle was named one of the world's top 11 players by [[The Best FIFA Football Awards 2019]]<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.cincinnati.com/story/sports/soccer/fc-cincinnati/2019/09/24/rose-lavelle-named-one-11-best-players-fifa-awards-show/2428506001/ |title=Rose Lavelle voted one of the 11 best players in the world at FIFA awards show |last=Brennan |first=Patrick |website=Cincinnati.com |language=en |access-date=November 18, 2019}}</ref> (finishing sixth), and was chosen as one of the world's top three midfielders by her professional peers in the 2019 [[FIFA FIFPro World XI]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.ktvn.com/story/41087606/rose-lavelle-named-to-fifpro-best-11 |title=Rose Lavelle named to FIFPRO Best 11 |website=www.ktvn.com |language=en |access-date=November 18, 2019 |archive-date=November 18, 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191118042536/https://www.ktvn.com/story/41087606/rose-lavelle-named-to-fifpro-best-11 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===2020 Summer Olympics===
In July 2021, she was named to the roster for the delayed [[Football at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2020 Tokyo Olympics]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Bardahl |first=Jayna |title=Rose Lavelle is headed to her 1st Olympics. Here's how Cincinnati sports fans reacted. |url=https://www.cincinnati.com/story/sports/olympics/2021/06/23/cincinnati-community-reacts-rose-lavelles-1st-olympics-tokyo/5324404001/ |access-date=July 14, 2021 |website=The Enquirer |language=en-US}}</ref> Lavelle appeared in all six games as the team won the bronze medal, losing to Canada 1–0 in the semi-final before beating Australia 4–3 in the third-place playoff.<ref>{{cite web |title=The U.S. Women's Soccer Team Beats Australia To Win Bronze Medal At Tokyo Olympics |url=https://www.npr.org/sections/tokyo-olympics-live-updates/2021/08/05/1025033756/the-u-s-womens-soccer-team-beats-australia-to-win-bronze-medal-at-tokyo-olympics |publisher=NPR |access-date=August 5, 2021 |date=August 5, 2021}}</ref> She scored one goal at the tournament, during a 6–1 group stage win over New Zealand.<ref name="NZ2020">{{cite web |url=https://olympics.com/tokyo-2020/olympic-games/en/results/football/results-women-gpg-000400-.htm |title=Football – New Zealand vs United States – Group G Results |date=July 24, 2021 |website=Olympics |access-date=July 24, 2021 |archive-date=August 10, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210810093221/https://olympics.com/tokyo-2020/olympic-games/en/results/football/results-women-gpg-000400-.htm |url-status=dead}}</ref>

=== 2023 FIFA Women's World Cup ===
Lavelle was named to the national team roster for the [[2023 FIFA Women's World Cup]], though she played limited minutes in the early stages of the tournament due to a knee injury.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-07-27 |title=Rose Lavelle injury: Why USWNT star is on limited minutes at 2023 Women's World Cup |url=https://www.sportingnews.com/us/soccer/news/rose-lavelle-injury-minutes-uswnt-2023-world-cup/b5zymqigsueglcyzmivxtxcb |access-date=2023-08-07 |website=www.sportingnews.com |language=en-us}}</ref> She appeared as a second-half substitute in the U.S.'s group stage matches against Vietnam and the Netherlands, earning one assist. She played all 90 minutes of the U.S.'s third group stage match but received a yellow card in the first half of the game; since she had also received a yellow card in the previous match, she was suspended from the team's Round of 16 game against Sweden.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rose Lavelle picks up second yellow card, will be suspended for USWNT's next match |url=https://www.foxsports.com/stories/soccer/rose-lavelle-picks-up-second-yellow-card-will-be-suspended-for-uswnts-next-match |access-date=2023-08-07 |website=FOX Sports |language=en-US}}</ref> The U.S. ultimately lost to Sweden in a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shootout]] and was eliminated from the tournament.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Peterson |first=Anne M. |title=US loses to Sweden on penalty kicks in its earliest Women's World Cup exit ever |url=https://abcnews.go.com/US/wireStory/us-loses-sweden-penalty-kicks-earliest-womens-world-102053779 |access-date=2023-08-07 |website=ABC News |language=en}}</ref>
