'''William Martin Geldart''' [[CBE]] (7 June 1870 &ndash; 12 February 1922) was a British [[jurist]]. A classical scholar of [[Balliol College, Oxford]], he went on to become [[Vinerian Professor of English Law]] at [[University of Oxford|Oxford]] and a leading [[jurist]] of his day.

==Biography==
Son of the Rev. [[Edmund Martin Geldart]], he was educated at [[Whitgift School]], [[Croydon]]; [[St Paul's School (London)|St Paul's School]], and [[Balliol College, Oxford]], where he was a scholar and in 1890 won the [[Gaisford Prize]] for Greek Verse.<ref name=www>'GELDART, William Martin', in ''Who Was Who'', A & C Black, 1920–2007; online edition by [[Oxford University Press]], December 2007 ''[http://www.ukwhoswho.com/view/article/oupww/whowaswho/U196820 GELDART, William Martin]'' (subscription required), accessed 18 August 2008</ref> He graduated [[Master of Arts (Oxbridge and Dublin)|MA]] in 1892.<ref>[[Joseph Foster (genealogist)|Foster, Joseph]], ''[https://archive.org/details/oxfordmen188018900fostuoft Oxford men, 1880-1892, with a record of their schools, honours and degrees]'' (1893) online at us.archive.org, accessed 18 August 2008</ref>

Elected a Fellow of [[St John's College, Oxford]], in 1892, he was [[called to the bar]] from [[Lincoln's Inn]] in 1896, continuing at St John's until 1899. He was Official Fellow and Lecturer in Law at [[Trinity College, Oxford]], from 1901 to 1909, All Souls Reader in English Law in the [[University of Oxford|University]], from 1906 to 1909, and Vinerian Professor of English Law and a fellow of All Souls College from 1909 to 1922. He was a member of the [[Hebdomadal Council]] from 1905 and chairman of the university's Delegacy for Women Students from 1911 until it was dissolved in 1921.<ref name=www/><ref name=library>[https://www.st-annes.ox.ac.uk/life-here/library/blog/nettleship-iii/ History of the Nettleship Library (III): Jowett Walk] at st-annes.ox.ac.uk, accessed 27 January 2021</ref>

He married his wife Emily in 1905.<ref name=www/>

The law library at [[St Anne's College, Oxford]] is named after Geldart, who left his law books and reports to the women students of the university.<ref name=library/> The law society of the college, 'The Geldart Society', is also named in his honour.<ref name="society">[http://stanneslawsoc.com/history.asp What is St Anne's Geldart Law Society?] at stanneslawsoc.com, accessed 18 August 2008. {{Archive url|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060413225446/http://stanneslawsoc.com/history.asp|date=2006-04-13}}</ref>

==Works==
Geldart was the author of the influential ''Elements of English Law'' (1911), still in print under the title ''Introduction to English Law'' (Oxford University Press, 11th edition, ed. [[David Yardley]]). According to one review "Geldart has over the years established itself as the standard account of English law..."<ref name=society/>

He also coedited  ''Aristophanes Comoediae'', a 1906–1907 edition of the comedies of Aristophanes, with [[Frederick William Hall (academic)|Frederick William Hall]].<ref>David Konstan, ''Aristophanes: Oxford Bibliographies Online Research Guide'' (2010), p. 8</ref>

==Honours==
He was appointed [[Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] in 1917.<ref name=www/>
