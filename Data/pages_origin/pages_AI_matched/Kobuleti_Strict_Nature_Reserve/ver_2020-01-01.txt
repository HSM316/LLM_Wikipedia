'''Kobuleti Strict Nature Reserve''' ({{lang-ka|ქობულეთი}}) is a [[List of protected areas of Georgia|protected area]] in [[Kobuleti Municipality]], [[Adjara]] region of [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]] along the [[Black Sea]] coast in the northern part of the resort town [[Kobuleti]].<ref name="protectedplanet">{{cite web|url=https://www.protectedplanet.net/kobuleti-strict-nature-reserve|title=Kobuleti in Georgia|website=Protected Planet|accessdate=2018-11-02}}</ref>  
''Kobuleti Protected Areas''  were  established in 1998 to preserve  unique wetland ecosystems recognized by the [[Ramsar Convention]].

''Kobuleti Protected Areas'' include '''Kobuleti Strict Nature Reserve'''  and [[Kobuleti Managed Reserve]] located on left and right banks of Shavi Ghele (''black creek'') river respectively.<ref name="apa">{{cite web|url=http://apa.gov.ge/en/protected-areas/cattestone/qobuletis-daculi-teritoriebis-administracia|title=Kobuleti Protected Areas Administration - Agency of Protected Areas of Georgia|website=apa.gov.ge|accessdate=2018-11-09}}</ref>
'''Kobuleti Strict Nature Reserve'''  occupies territory bordered by the Togoni river on the north, the [[Kobuleti]]—[[Ozurgeti]] highway on the east, Shavi Ghele river on the south and Kobuleti suburbs on the west.

== Geography ==
Reserve has flat surface with minor erosion by the rivers of Shavi Ghele  and Togoni and by a few water drains. It is [[peat]] [[bog]] which consists mainly of [[peat moss]] with hardly noticeable surface elevations which are called locally ''peat domes''. These minor elevations rise 1–3 m above it surroundings.  Bog in this area is composed of a single [[peat]] layer with thickness from 5 m up to 9 m.<ref name="vision">[https://www.econatura.nl/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/National-Part-of-the-Black-Sea-vision-GEORGIA.pdf The Black Sea coastal wetlands vision. Georgia] Black Sea Regional Initiative for the wise use of Coastal Wetlands. March 2008 Batumi, Georgia</ref>

== Climate ==
Reserve has humid marine [[subtropical climate]] with  high relative humidity and periodic strong winds. Precipitation is mainly in the form of rain with total annual amount of 1500–2500&nbsp;mm. Fall and winter are primary wet seasons.

== Flora ==
Reserve is very rich in plant species.
Many of plants are of [[boreal ecosystem|boreal]] origin and appeared here during the [[Ice Age]] of the[[Quaternary period]].
Most of the area is covered with various [[peat moss]]es, in particular variety of [[sphagnum]] species, such as [[white sphagnum moss]] and Imeretian sedge ([[Molinia litoralis]]), white beak sedge ([[Rhynchospora alba]]), Caucasian beak sedge ([[:sv:Rhynchospora caucasica|Rhynchospora caucasica]]), Great Pond-sedge ([[Carex riparia]]), buckbean ([[Menyanthes trifoliata]]) and carnivorous round-leaved sundew ([[Drosera rotundifolia]]) to name some.

Local Colchic plants also grow in peat mires including  Pontic rhododendron ([[Rhododendron ponticum]]), Yellow azalea ([[Rhododendron luteum]]), climber ([[Smilax|Smilax excelsa]])<ref>[https://georgianrecipes.net/tag/smilax-excelsa/ Ekala(ეკალა)] ''Georgian Recipes'' A True Taste of Georgia.</ref> and rare, threatened Royal fern ([[Osmunda regalis]]).

== Fauna ==
Kobuleti Protected Areas provide habitat for migrating, nesting and wintering water birds including [[Gallinago media]], which is on the [[IUCN Red List of Threatened Species]],<ref name=status>{{IUCN|id=22693093 |title=''Gallinago media'' |assessor=BirdLife International |assessor-link=BirdLife International |version=2013.2 |year=2012 |accessdate=26 November 2013}}</ref> [[Gallinago gallinago]] and many others.<ref name="vision" />
 
[[Mammals]] are represented by  [[jackal]], Eurasian otter (''[[Lutra lutra]]''), [[badger]], [[Coypu|nutria]], [[hare]], [[Woodmouse]], [[Apodemus|Fieldmouse]], et al. [[Eurasian otter]] is included in the Red List of Georgia.

[[Amphibians]] are represented by Caspian turtle (''[[Mauremys caspica]]'') and [[marsh turtle]], [[Common toad]], [[European green toad|Green toad]], [[Common tree frog]], et al.

[[Reptiles]] are represented by [[Dice snake]], [[Grass snake]], [[Slow worm]], European legless lizard (''[[Pseudopus apodus]]''), Striped lizard (''[[Lacerta Strigata]]''), [[Common newt]], et al.

[[Ichthyofauna]] include such species as [[Northern pike]], [[Crucian carp]], [[European perch]] and [[Silver eel]].<ref>[http://www.georgianholidays.com/sights/protected-areas/kobuleti-protected-area Kobuleti protected area] Travel Company Georgian Holidays</ref>
