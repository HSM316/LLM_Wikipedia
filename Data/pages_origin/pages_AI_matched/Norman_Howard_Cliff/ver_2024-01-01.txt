'''Norman Howard Cliff''' (1925â€“2007) was a British [[Protestant]] author who wrote about Christianity and the history of [[Protestant missions in China]].

==Biography==
Cliff was born in 1925 in [[Yantai]] (formerly, Chefoo) to Howard and Mary Cliff, both pharmacists who were working as missionaries with the [[China Inland Mission]] (CIM). As part of the [[Chefoo School]], a boarding school of the CIM, Cliff was interned by the Japanese at the [[Weixian Internment Camp]] from 1943 until 1945, when the camp was freed by American [[paratroopers]].<ref name="weihsien">{{Cite web|url=http://weihsien-paintings.org/NormanCliff/indexFrame.htm|title=The Life and Testimony of Norman Howard Cliff, 1925-2007|last=Horne|first=Estelle Cliffe|website=Weihsien|access-date=7 February 2018}}</ref>

Cliff later went on to pursue a B.A. from the [[University of South Africa]] and a [[B.Com.]] from [[Rhodes University]]. He worked as a minister and an accountant for the [[United Reformed Church]] in South Africa, before returning to Britain due to poor health.<ref name="weihsien"/> Back in the UK, Cliff earned an [[MPhil]] at the [[Open University]] in 1983, writing a thesis on the theology of [[Watchman Nee]],<ref>{{Cite thesis|last=Cliff|first=Norman Howard|title=The Life and Theology of Watchman Nee, Including a Study of the Little Flock Movement Which he Founded|date=1983|degree=MPhil|publisher=Open University|url=https://www.proquest.com/docview/230634259/}}</ref> and a [[PhD]] at [[Buckingham University]] in  1995, writing a thesis on the history of Protestant missions in [[Shandong]] Province.<ref>{{Cite thesis|last=Cliff|first=Norman Howard|title=A history of the Protestant movement in Shandong Province, China, 1859-1951|date=1995|degree=PhD|publisher=University of Buckingham|id={{ProQuest|304301557}}}}</ref>
