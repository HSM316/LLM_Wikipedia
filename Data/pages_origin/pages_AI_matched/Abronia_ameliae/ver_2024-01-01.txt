'''''Abronia ameliae''''', commonly known as '''Amelia's sand verbena''' or '''heart's delight''', is a species of [[flowering plant]] in the [[Mirabilis jalapa|Four O'clock]] family, [[Nyctaginaceae]], that is [[Endemism|endemic]] to southern [[Texas]] in the [[United States]].<ref name="GRIN"/> It inhabits [[grassland]]s in the deep [[sand]]s of the Holocene sand sheet,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nature.org/wherewework/northamerica/states/texas/files/g3taxaabstracts720041.pdf |title=''Abronia ameliae'' Lundell |work=The Nature Conservancy in Texas |publisher=The Nature Conservancy |accessdate=2009-12-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081104213630/http://www.nature.org/wherewework/northamerica/states/texas/files/g3taxaabstracts720041.pdf |archive-date=2008-11-04 |url-status=dead }}</ref> which is part of the [[Tamaulipan mezquital]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.abisw.org/tt/plants/abrame.htm |first=Bill |last=Carr |title=Amelia's sand-verbena (Abronia ameliae) |work=Rare Plants of the Tamaulipan Thornscrub Ecoregion of Texas |publisher=Association of Biodiversity Information - Southwest |date=August 2005 |accessdate=2009-12-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101118045646/http://www.abisw.org/tt/plants/abrame.htm |archive-date=2010-11-18 |url-status=dead }}</ref> This species was named for Amelia Anderson Lundell, wife of Cyrus Longworth Lundell.<ref>Wrightia 1(1): 54â€“55. 1945. https://www.biodiversitylibrary.org/page/764029#page/72/mode/1up</ref>
