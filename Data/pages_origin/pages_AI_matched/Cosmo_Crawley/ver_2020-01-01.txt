'''Cosmo Stafford Crawley''' (27 May 1904 – 10 February 1989) was an English [[cricket]]er, [[Rackets (sport)|rackets]] player and [[real tennis]] player.

==Early life==
Crawley was the eldest son of [[Arthur Stafford Crawley]], then a [[curate]] at [[St Luke's, Chelsea]]. He was educated at [[Harrow School|Harrow]] and [[Magdalen College, Oxford]].

==Cricket==
Crawley was a right-handed [[batsman (cricket)|batsman]] and a [[Seam bowler|medium pace]] bowler. He made his [[first-class cricket|first-class]] debut during the 1923 County Championship season for [[Hampshire County Cricket Club|Hampshire]] against [[Oxford University Cricket Club|Oxford University]]. This was his only match for the county. Between 1924 and 1925, Crawley played for [[Oxford University Cricket Club|Oxford University]], playing two matches for them. In 1929 after a four-year break from the game, Crawley joined [[Middlesex County Cricket Club|Middlesex]], making one appearance for the county against [[Oxford University Cricket Club|Oxford University]]. As well as representing the aforementioned teams, Crawley also played for the [[Free Foresters Cricket Club|Free Foresters]] and the [[Harlequins cricket team]]. Crawley played in four first-class matches altogether – two of them ''for'' Oxford University and two ''against'' Oxford University.

==Rackets and real tennis==
Crawley played rackets for Harrow, and both rackets and real tennis for Oxford. He won the British amateur rackets doubles championship in 1936 and 1937 with J.C.F. Simpson, and in 1939 and 1946 with [[John Pawle|J.H. Pawle]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tennisandrackets.com/editable/downloads/RacketsResults/AmateurDoubles.pdf |title=Amateur Doubles Championship |publisher=Tennis & Rackets Association}}</ref> He and Pawle also won the 1939 amateur real tennis doubles, beating the 3rd and 4th [[Baron Aberdare|Lord Aberdares]], then aged 54 and 20, in the final.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/1482162/Lord-Aberdare.html |title=Lord Aberdare |newspaper=The Telegraph |date=28 Jan 2005}}</ref>

==Later life==
Crawley joined the insurance broking firm of C.T. Bowring & Co. in 1925 and was a director 1934–67 and a consultant to the firm 1967–73. He was an underwriting member of [[Lloyd's]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.gibbsfamilytree.co.uk/people/view/680 |title=Cosmo Stafford Crawley |website=The Gibbs Family Tree}}</ref> At the outbreak of [[World War II]] he was given an emergency [[Commissioned officer|commission]] in the [[Coldstream Guards]]<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=34698 |supp=y |date=30 September 1939 |page=6649}}</ref> and served until 1945. He died in [[Westminster]], [[London]], on 10 February 1989.
