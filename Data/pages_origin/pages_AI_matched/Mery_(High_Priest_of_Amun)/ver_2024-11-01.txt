'''Mery''' was a [[High Priests of Amun|High Priest of Amun]] from the time of [[Amenhotep II]] ([[Eighteenth dynasty of Egypt|18th Dynasty]]).

Mery was the son of the First prophet of [[Min (god)|Min]] of [[Koptos]], named Nebpehtire. His mother was Hunayt, a Chief nurse of the Lord of the Two Lands. Mery was married to a lady named Dey.<ref>Porter and Moss, Topographical Bibliography: The Theban Necropolis</ref>

On digitalegypt his titles are listed as: Overseer of the Priests of [[Upper Egypt|Upper]] and [[Lower Egypt]], [[High Priest of Amun]], Overseer of the Fields of Amun, Steward of Amun, Overseer of the Granaries (of Amun), Overseer of the Treasury. See UC37790 <ref>http://www.digitalegypt.ucl.ac.uk/thebes/archive/uc37790.gif {{Bare URL image|date=March 2022}}</ref> and UC37791.<ref>http://www.digitalegypt.ucl.ac.uk/thebes/archive/uc37791.gif {{Bare URL image|date=March 2022}}</ref> A black granite statue of Mery can be found in the [[Cairo Museum]] CG 973.
