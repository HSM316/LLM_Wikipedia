'''''Weymouthia''''' is an [[extinct]] [[genus]] of [[Eodiscina|eodiscinid]] [[agnostid]] [[trilobite]]s, which lived at the end of the [[Lower Cambrian]], in what are now the eastern United States, England, Siberia and China.

== Description ==
Like all Agnostida, ''Weymouthia'' is diminutive and the headshield (or [[Cephalon (arthropod head)#Trilobites|cephalon]]) and tailshield (or [[pygidium]]) are of approximately the same size (or [[Pygidium#Trilobites|isopygous]]) and outline. Like all Weymouthiidae, ''Weymouthia'' lacks eyes and rupture lines (or [[Trilobite#Dorsal sutures|sutures]]). The headshield (or [[Trilobite#Cephalon|cephalon]]) is approximately as long as wide, lacks facial sutures and is eyeless. The tailshield (or [[Trilobite#Pygidium|pygidium]]) is about 1.2× as wide as long. Both are effaced except for a furrow close to their borders. The cephalic border bears lateral tubercles. The thorax consists of three segments.<ref name="Treatise">Whittington, H. B. et al. Part O, Treatise on Invertebrate Paleontology. Revised, Volume 1 – Trilobita – Introduction, Order Agnostida, Order Redlichiida. 1997</ref>

== Etymology ==
''Weymouthia'' is named after [[Weymouth, Massachusetts]].<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.weymouth.ma.us/index.php/history/historic-timeline/|accessdate= 3 December 2012|title= Weymouth, MA, history}}</ref>

== Distribution ==
* ''W. nobilis'' occurs in the Lower Cambrian of Massachusetts (Hoppin Slate, North Attleboro), and of the United Kingdom (Comley Quarry, Comley, 52.6° N, 2.8° W).<ref>{{cite journal|last= Hinz|first= I.|year= 1987|title= The Lower Cambrian microfauna of Comley and Rushton, Shropshire, England|journal= Palaeontographica Abteilung A|volume= 198|issue=1-3|pages= 41–100}} cited in {{cite web|title= ''Weymouthia nobilis''|author= Paleobiology Database|url=http://paleodb.org/cgi-bin/bridge.pl?a=basicCollectionSearch&collection_no=79928}}</ref>
