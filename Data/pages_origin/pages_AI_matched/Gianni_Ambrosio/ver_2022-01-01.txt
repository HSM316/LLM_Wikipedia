'''Gianni Ambrosio''' (born 23 December 1943 in [[Santhià]]) is the emeritus bishop of the [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Piacenza-Bobbio]].

== Biography ==

Born in [[Santhià]] on 23 December 1943, he studied [[theology]] in [[Minor seminary|minor]] and [[seminary|major seminaries]] of [[Vercelli]] and on 7 July 1968 he was ordained priest.

In 1970 he obtained a diploma in [[social sciences]] at the [[Institut Catholique de Paris|Institut Catholique]] in [[Paris]] and in 1972 he obtained the specialization-diploma in sociology at the [[École pratique des hautes études]] at the [[Sorbonne University]]. He was a member of the international scientific committee of [[CESNUR]].<ref>Diocese of Piacenza and Bobbio press release, [https://www.diocesipiacenzabobbio.org/download/documenti/Antonino%20d%27Oro%202009.doc “L’Antonino d’oro 2009 al vescovo mons. Gianni Ambrosio”], June 11, 2009.</ref>

In 1995 he graduated in theology in [[Rome]] at the [[Pontifical Lateran University]].

From 1974 to 1988 he taught religion and was parochial vicar in the parishes of [[Santhià]] and [[Moncrivello]]. From 1988 to 2001 he was pastor of the parish of St. Paul in [[Vercelli]]. In 1996 he was named [[honorary prelate]] of [[His Holiness]].

22 December 2007 Pope Benedict XVI appointed him as [[bishop]] of [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Piacenza-Bobbio|diocese of Piacenza]], succeeding to Monsignor [[Luciano Monari]]. He was consecrated bishop in the cathedral of Piacenza on 16 February 2008 by [[Cardinal (Catholicism)|Cardinal]] [[Tarcisio Bertone]].

He retired on 16 July 2020.
