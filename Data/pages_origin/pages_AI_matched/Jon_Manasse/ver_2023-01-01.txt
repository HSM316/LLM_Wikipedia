'''Jon Manasse''' {{IPA|[məˈnæsi]}}<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=17994676|title=Clarinet and Keyboard: Unexpected Connections|year=2008|publisher=American Public Media|accessdate=2019-09-14}}</ref> is an American [[clarinetist]].

Manasse studied clarinet at the [[Juilliard School]] under [[David Weber (clarinetist)|David Weber]].<ref name="parker">{{cite web|url=http://www.parkerartists.com/NewPages/manasse.html|title=Jon Manasse|year=2008|publisher=Parker Artists|accessdate=2009-08-05|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100108080934/http://www.parkerartists.com/NewPages/manasse.html|archivedate=2010-01-08}}</ref> He won a prize in the International Competition for Clarinet in [[Munich]] and was the youngest winner of the International Clarinet Society Competition.<ref name="colorado" />

Manasse has performed at the [[Lincoln Center]] in New York, [[Columbia University]], and [[Rockefeller University]].<ref name="parker" /> He has toured in Japan and Southeast Asia with the [[New York Symphonic Ensemble]], and played in [[Jerusalem]], [[Tel Aviv]] and [[Osaka]].<ref name="parker" /> He has been a guest soloist with the [[National Philharmonic Orchestra|National Philharmonic]], [[Symphony Nova Scotia]], [[National Chamber Orchestra]], [[New York Philharmonic]], and the orchestras of [[Alabama]], [[Annapolis]], [[Bozeman, Montana|Bozeman]], [[Dubuque, Iowa|Dubuque]], Evansville, [[Florida West Coast]], [[Green Bay, Wisconsin|Green Bay]], [[Indianapolis]], [[Jackson, Mississippi|Jackson]], [[Oakland East Bay]], [[Pensacola]], [[Princeton, New Jersey|Princeton]], and [[Richmond, Virginia|Richmond]].<ref name="parker" /> He has also been the guest principal clarinetist of the [[New York Pops Orchestra]], [[Orpheus Chamber Orchestra]], [[New Jersey Symphony Orchestra]], [[St. Louis Symphony Orchestra]], and [[Seattle Symphony Orchestra]].<ref name="parker" /> He was also a member of the [[New York Chamber Symphony]] and the [[Metropolitan Opera]] Orchestra.

Manasse plays with the [[Stamford, Connecticut|Stamford]] Symphony Orchestra, and is the principal clarinetist of the [[American Ballet Theater Orchestra]] and the [[Mostly Mozart Festival Orchestra]].<ref name="parker" /> He became the principal clarinetist of the [[Orchestra of St. Luke's]] in 2008. Jon Manasse and his duo-partner, [[pianist]] [[Jon Nakamatsu]], serve as Artistic Directors of the [[Cape Cod Chamber Music Festival]].<ref name="parker" /> Manasse is a [[Buffet Crampon]] and [[Vandoren]] Performing Artist.<ref name="colorado">{{cite web|url=http://www.coloradocollege.edu/SummerPrograms/SummerFestivaloftheArts/musicfestival/faculty/JonManasse.asp|title=FESTIVAL FACULTY|year=2009|publisher=Colorado College|accessdate=2009-08-05|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100528135334/http://www.coloradocollege.edu/SummerPrograms/SummerFestivaloftheArts/musicfestival/faculty/JonManasse.asp|archivedate=2010-05-28}}</ref> He has been Associate Professor of Clarinet at the [[Eastman School of Music]] since 1995, faculty member of [[Juilliard School]] in 2007, and distinguished artist in residence at [[Lynn University]]'s Conservatory of Music since 2008 in [[Boca Raton]], Florida.<ref name="parker" />
