'''Cornelius Forman Hatfield''' (December 27, 1828 &ndash; June 13, 1910) was a merchant, shipbuilder, ship owner and political figure in [[Nova Scotia]], Canada. He represented Yarmouth County in the [[Nova Scotia House of Assembly]] from 1890 to 1894 as a [[Liberal Party of Nova Scotia|Liberal]] member.

He was born in [[Tusket, Nova Scotia]], the son of Captain James Hatfield and Elizabeth Lent, the daughter of [[James Lent (Nova Scotia politician)|James Lent]]. Hatfield was married three times: first to Deborah Hatfield, then to Sarah Bowlby in 1844 and finally to Marion Anderson. Forman owned general stores at Tusket and Tusket Wedge.<ref>[http://www.argylecourthouse.com/Archives/Business_Records/Forman_Hatfield_General_Store_Tusket_Wedge_NS.php Business Records, Argyle Township Court House & Archives]</ref> He was a [[justice of the peace]] from 1872 to 1910 and served on the municipal council for [[Argyle, Nova Scotia|Argyle township]] from 1902 to 1910, serving as warden from 1907 to 1910. He also served as [[coroner]]. He died in Tusket at the age of 81.

His brother [[Isaac Hatfield|Isaac]] also served in the provincial assembly.
