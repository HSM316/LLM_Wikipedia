Alongside host [[Johnny Carson]] and announcer [[Ed McMahon]], Cohen is believed to be the only person to have worked on the ''Tonight Show'' for Carson's entire run.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/shelly-cohen-dead-johnny-carsons-longtime-tonight-show-assistant-musical-director-was-84-1131977|title=Shelly Cohen, Longtime Music Man on 'The Tonight Show,' Dies at 84|work=The Hollywood Reporter|access-date=2018-08-18|language=en}}</ref>

Cohen was born in Brooklyn, and played both the clarinet and violin as a child. After a tour of duty in the army during the Korean War, Cohen attended Juilliard School of Music while working a job in the NBC mailroom. Bandleader [[Skitch Henderson]] helped Cohen secure a position in the NBC music library, and upon its premiere, on the ''[[The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson|Tonight Show with Johnny Carson]]''. Cohen served as assistant musical director throughout Carson's entire run on the ''Tonight Show'', assisting Henderson, [[Milton DeLugg]], [[Doc Severinsen]] and [[Tommy Newsom]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://carsonpodcast.com/shelly-cohen/|title=Shelly Cohen -|website=carsonpodcast.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-08-18}}</ref>

Cohen's additional musical endeavors include Christmas specials for NBC, the Emmy Awards, a year working for Dick Cavett, founding of the New Horizon Singers and the Pacific Pops Orchestra, and choir direction at St. Mel's Church<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1990-08-17-ca-618-story.html|title=Church Choir Joins L.A.
