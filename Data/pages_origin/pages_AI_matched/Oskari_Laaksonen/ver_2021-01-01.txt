'''Oskari Laaksonen''' (born 2 July 1999) is a Finnish professional [[ice hockey]] [[Defenseman (ice hockey)|defenseman]] who is currently playing with [[Lahti Pelicans]] in the Finnish [[Liiga]] on loan from the [[Buffalo Sabres]] of the [[National Hockey League]] (NHL). He was selected by the Sabres in the third round, 89th overall, of the [[2017 NHL Entry Draft]].

== Playing career ==
Laaksonen as a youth played with Ilves' U16, U18, and U20 teams. After playing for Ilves U20, Laaksonen was drafted 89th overall by the Sabres in the [[2017 NHL Entry Draft]].<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.nhl.com/sabres/news/oskari-laaksonen-sabres-third-round-pick/c-290337418 | title = Meet Oskari Laaksonen, Buffalo's third round pick | website = [[Buffalo Sabres]] | date = 2017-07-10 | accessdate = 2017-07-10}}</ref>

Laaksonen made his Liiga debut during the [[2017-18 Liiga season|2017–18]] season, on 9 January 2018, in a 6-5 defeat to [[HPK]].<ref>{{cite web| url = http://liiga.fi/ottelut/2017-2018/runkosarja/296/kokoonpanot/ | title = Ilves stopped by HPK | publisher = [[Liiga]] | date = 2018-01-09 | accessdate = 2018-01-09}}</ref> He played out the season to contribute with 4 assists in 21 games.<ref>{{cite web| url = https://finnprospects.com/player/oskari-laaksonen/ | title = Oskari Laaksonen player biography | website = finnprospects.com | date = 2018-01-09 | accessdate = 2018-01-09}}</ref>

On 15 June 2020, Laaksonen was signed by the Sabres to a three-year, entry-level contract.<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.nhl.com/sabres/news/buffalo-sabres-sign-oskari-laaksonen/c-317165168 | title = Sabres sign Laaksonen to 3-year, entry-level deal | website = [[Buffalo Sabres]] | date = June 15, 2020 | access-date = June 17, 2020}}</ref> It was announced he would initially continue his tenure with Ilves for the 2020–21 season, however on 21 September 2020, Laaksonen was loaned by the Sabres to fellow Liiga club, Lahti Pelicans.<ref>{{cite web| url =  https://pelicans.fi/fi-fi/article/uutinen/otto-somppi-ja-oskari-laaksonen-lainalle-pelicans-paitaan/4355/ | title = Sompi and Laaksonen join Pelicans | publisher = [[Lahti Pelicans]] | date = 21 September 2020 | accessdate = 21 September 2020 | language = Finnish}}</ref>

Laaksonen appeared for [[Finland men's national junior ice hockey team|Finland]] in 6 junior international friendly games in 2016, before he was later selected to represent his country at the [[2019 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships|2019 World Junior Championships]] in [[Vancouver, British Columbia]], [[Canada]].<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.eliteprospects.com/player/372926/oskari-laaksonen | title = Oskari Laaksonen player profile |  website = eliteprospects.com | date = 2017-06-25| accessdate = 2017-06-25}}</ref> He collected 1 goal in 7 games from the blueline, helping Finland claim the Gold Medal for their 5th title.<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.nhl.com/news/finland-defeats-united-states-to-win-world-junior-championship/c-303601898 | title = Finland defeats United States to win World Junior Championship | website = [[National Hockey League]] | date = 2019-01-06 | accessdate = 2019-01-06}}</ref>
