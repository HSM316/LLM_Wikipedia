'''Dame Margaret Anne Brimble''' {{post-nominals|country=NZL|DNZM|FRS}} (née '''MacMillan''', born 20 August 1961) is a New Zealand chemist. Her research has included investigations of [[shellfish toxins]]<ref name=loreal>{{cite web|title=Five outstanding women scientists receive L'ORÉAL-UNESCO Awards for Women in Science 2007|url=https://www.loreal.com/media/press-releases/2007/feb/five-outstanding-women-scientists-receive-l%E2%80%99or%C3%A9al-unesco-awards-for-women-in-science-2007|publisher=UNESCO|accessdate=7 October 2018|date=2 March 1007}}</ref> and means to treat brain injuries.<ref>{{cite news|title=Brain injury drug developer wins award|url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/science/7983929/Brain-injury-drug-developer-wins-award|accessdate=7 October 2018|newspaper=Stuff.co.nz|date=22 November 2012}}</ref>

==Early life, family, and education==
Brimble was born in [[Auckland]] on 20 August 1961, the daughter of Mary Anne MacMillan (née Williamson) and Herbert MacMillan,<ref name="Whos who">{{cite book |editor-last=Taylor |editor-first=Alister |editorlink=Alister Taylor |title=New Zealand Who's Who Aotearoa 2001 |year=2001 |publisher=Alister Taylor Publishers |location=Auckland |issn=1172-9813 |page=168}}</ref> and was encouraged by her grandmother to value education.<ref>{{cite web|title=Prof. Margaret Brimble, 2007 For Women in Science Laureate for Asia/Pacific (New Zealand)|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=egwmNBAaxRo|work=Video|publisher=L'oreal Women in Science|accessdate=7 October 2018|date=7 December 2009}}</ref> She attended [[Diocesan School for Girls, Auckland]] from 1972 to 1978, and was [[Dux (education)|dux]] in her final year.<ref name="Whos who"/><ref>{{cite book|title=101 Incredible Kiwis|author=Williams, Tony|publisher=Reed|year=2007|isbn=978-0-7900-1178-3|location=Auckland|page=101}}</ref>

She went on to study chemistry at the [[University of Auckland]] from 1979 to 1983, graduating [[Bachelor of Science]] in 1982 and [[Master of Science]] with first-class honours in 1983.<ref name="Whos who"/> She was awarded a New Zealand Commonwealth scholarship to undertake a PhD in organic chemistry at the [[University of Southampton]].<ref name=PhD-356686>{{cite thesis|degree=PhD|publisher=University of Southampton|url=https://copac.jisc.ac.uk/id/21829740?style=html|title=Salinomycin : the bis-spiroacetal moiety|author=Brimble, Margaret Anne|year=1985|id={{EThOS|uk.bl.ethos.356686}}|website=jisc.ac.uk|oclc=59350054}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Professor Margaret Brimble|url=http://www.awis.org.nz/professor-margaret-brimble/|publisher=Association for Women in Sciences New Zealand|accessdate=26 April 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130208033309/http://www.awis.org.nz/professor-margaret-brimble/|archivedate=8 February 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Career and research==
Brimble holds the Chair of Organic and Medicinal Chemistry at the University of Auckland and is also a Principal Investigator in the Maurice Wilkins Centre for Molecular Biodiscovery.

===Awards and honours===
She is a Fellow of the [[Royal Society of New Zealand]] and the [[Royal Society of Chemistry]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.royalsociety.org.nz/events/annual/rutherford-lecture/2013-rutherford-lecture-mastering-molecular-chess/|title=Mastering molecular chess to mine nature's medicine chest|publisher=[[Royal Society of New Zealand]]|date=11 September 2013|accessdate=7 October 2018}}</ref>

Brimble was the first New Zealander to receive the [[L'Oreal-UNESCO Award for Women in Science]], and the second woman to receive the Rutherford medal.<ref>{{cite news|author=Morton, Jamie|title=Feted chemist wins top NZ medal|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=10849066|accessdate=7 October 2018|newspaper=The New Zealand Herald|date=22 November 2012}}</ref>

Brimble was made a [[New Zealand Order of Merit|Member of the New Zealand Order of Merit]] for services to science in the [[2004 New Year Honours]].<ref>[http://www.dpmc.govt.nz/node/381 New Year Honours List 2004]. Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet. Retrieved 7 October 2018.</ref> In the [[2012 New Year Honours (New Zealand)|2012 New Year Honours]], she was promoted to [[Companion of the New Zealand Order of Merit]], also for services to science.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dpmc.govt.nz/node/381|title=New Year honours list 2012|date=31 December 2011|publisher=Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet|accessdate=7 October 2018}}</ref> She was elected a [[Fellow of the Royal Society]] (FRS) in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://royalsociety.org/news/2018/05/distinguished-scientists-elected-fellows-royal-society-2018/|title=Distinguished scientists elected as Fellows and Foreign Members of the Royal Society|publisher=The Royal Society|date=9 May 2018|accessdate=7 October 2018}}</ref> In the [[2019 New Year Honours (New Zealand)|2019 New Year Honours]], she was promoted to [[Dame Companion of the New Zealand Order of Merit]], for services to science.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=12183898|title=Best of NZ: New Year Honours pay tribute to 196 exceptional Kiwis|date=31 December 2018|first=Vaimoana|last=Tapaleao|newspaper=New Zealand Herald}}</ref>
