The '''Genocide Act 1969''' (c. 12) was an [[act of Parliament (UK)|act]] of the [[Parliament of the United Kingdom]]. It gave effect to the Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide approved by the General Assembly of the United Nations on 9 December 1948.

The Act was repealed<ref>The [[International Criminal Court Act 2001]], [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2001/17/section/83 section 83] and [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2001/17/schedule/10 Schedule 10]</ref> for [[England and Wales]] and [[Northern Ireland]]<ref>The [[International Criminal Court Act 2001]], [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2001/17/section/79 section 79(1)(d)]</ref> on 1 September 2001.<ref>The [[International Criminal Court Act 2001 (Commencement) Order 2001]] (S.I. 2001/2161 (C. 69)), [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2001/2161/article/2/made article 2]</ref> It was repealed for [[Scotland]]<ref>The [[International Criminal Court (Scotland) Act 2001]] (asp 13), [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/asp/2001/13/section/1 section 1(3)]</ref> on 17 December 2001<ref>The [[International Criminal Court (Scotland) Act 2001 (Commencement) Order 2001]] (S.S.I. 2001/456 (C. 20)), [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ssi/2001/456/article/2/made article 2]</ref>

As to the repeal of this Act for the [[Isle of Man]], see [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2004/714/article/2/made article 2(e)] of the [[International Criminal Court Act 2001 (Isle of Man) Order 2004]] (S.I. 2004/714).

As to application of this Act to the [[British Antarctic Territory]], see section 5(1)(b) of the [[Administration of Justice Ordinance 1990]] (No 5).<ref>This is described by [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/1992/1300/schedule/paragraph/23/made section 23(1)] of the [[Extradition (British Antarctic Territory) (Commonwealth Countries, Colonies and Republic of Ireland) Order 1992]] (S.I. 1992/1300)</ref>

Archbold Criminal Pleading, Evidence and Practice said that this Act made no provision in relation to [[jurisdiction]] and that it was "doubtful" that genocide committed by a [[British subject]] abroad was an offence under this Act.<ref>[[Archbold Criminal Pleading, Evidence and Practice]]. 1999. Paragraph 19-352 at page 1660.</ref>

==Section 1 - Genocide==
The reference to the [[Advocate General for Northern Ireland]] in section 1(3) was substituted for the reference to the [[Attorney General for Northern Ireland]] on 12 April 2010<ref>The [[Justice (Northern Ireland) Act 2002 (Commencement No.14) Order 2010]] (S.I. 2010/113 (C. 7)), [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/nisr/2010/113/article/2/made article 2]</ref> by [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2002/26/schedule/7/paragraph/26 paragraph 26] of Schedule 7 to the [[Justice (Northern Ireland) Act 2002]].

Section 1(3) was subject to [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1975/59/section/12 section 12] of the [[Criminal Jurisdiction Act 1975]].

Section 1(4) inserted paragraph 20 of List B of Schedule 1 to the [[Criminal Law Act 1967]]. That Schedule was repealed by Part IV of Schedule 11 to the [[Courts Act 1971]].

Section 1(5) was repealed by [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1978/23/schedule/7/part/I Part 1] of Schedule 7 to the [[Judicature (Northern Ireland) Act 1978]].

==Section 2 - Extradition and evidence for foreign courts==
Section 2(1)(b), and the preceding "and", were repealed by section 170 of,[http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1988/33/schedule/16 Schedule 16] to, the [[Criminal Justice Act 1988]].

Section 2(1) was repealed by section 37 of, and Schedule 2 to, the [[Extradition Act 1989]].

The words "the Acts mentioned in subsection (1) of this section, the [[Extradition Act 1873]] and" in section 2(2) were repealed by section 37 of, and [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/33/schedule/2 Schedule 2] to, the [[Extradition Act 1989]].

==Section 3 - Application to Channel Islands, Isle of Man and colonies==
The words "and sections 16 and 17 of the [[Fugitive Offenders Act 1967]]" in section 3(1) were repealed by section 170 of, and [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1988/33/schedule/16 Schedule 16] to, the [[Criminal Justice Act 1988]].

Section 3(1) was substituted by [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1989/33/section/36 section 36(2)] of the [[Extradition Act 1989]].

The power under section 3(2) was exercised by [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/1987/453/article/2/made article 2] of the [http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/1987/453/contents/made Genocide (Anguilla) Order 1987] (S.I. 1987/453).
