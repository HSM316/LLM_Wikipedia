'''Dianne Saxe''' is an internationally recognized Canadian lawyer, rated among the top 25 [[environmental lawyers]] in the world.<ref name="eco">{{cite web|title=Congratulations and welcome to the new Commissioner!|url=http://eco.on.ca/congratulations-and-welcome-to-the-new-commissioner/|website=Environmental Commissioner of Ontario|publisher=Environmental Commissioner of Ontario|accessdate=24 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=About Dianne Saxe|url=http://www.slaw.ca/author/saxe/|website=Slaw: Canada's Online Legal Magazine|accessdate=24 March 2016}}</ref> She was the Environmental Commissioner of Ontario 2015-2019. She is the current Deputy Leader of the [[Green Party of Ontario]], serving with Abhijeet Manay.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nationalobserver.com/2020/11/16/news/dianne-saxe-appointed-deputy-leader-ontario-greens|title=Former environmental watchdog Dianne Saxe appointed deputy leader of Ontario Greens|first1=Emma McIntosh &#124;|last1=News|first2=Politics &#124;|last2=November 16th 2020|date=November 16, 2020|website=National Observer}}</ref>

==Legal career==

Saxe studied law at [[Osgoode Hall Law School]], earning an LL.B. in 1974 and a Ph.D., also from Osgoode, in 1991. She practiced law with the [[Ontario government]] for 14 years, worked in two major law firms and then ran an environmental law boutique firm for 25 years. For example, she represented the [[Association of Municipalities of Ontario]] in their successful $115 million claim against Stewardship Ontario<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.amo.on.ca/AMO-Content/Waste-Management/Blue-Box/Blue-Box.aspx|title=AMO - Blue Box Arbitration|website=www.amo.on.ca}}</ref> for the cost of Ontario's [[Blue box recycling system|Blue Box]] program.

After being the Environmental Commissioner of Ontario, Dr. Saxe reopened her SaxeFacts environmental law practice focussed on climate issues.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://saxefacts.com/|title=Climate Change Toronto and the Climate Crisis|first=Dianne|last=Saxe|website=Saxe Facts}}</ref> She publishes articles,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://saxefacts.com/climate-law-articles/|title=Informative Climate Law Articles|first=Dianne|last=Saxe}}</ref><ref name="opencanada.org">{{Cite web|url=https://opencanada.org/10-principles-to-guide-the-transition-to-a-green-economy/|title=10 principles to guide the transition to a green economy|date=September 16, 2019|website=Open Canada}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nationalobserver.com/2020/04/21/opinion/canadas-murky-bailout-deal-oil-and-gas-will-cost-us-all|title=Canada’s murky bailout deal for oil and gas will cost us all|first=Dianne Saxe &#124; Opinion &#124;|last=April 21st 2020|date=April 21, 2020|website=National Observer}}</ref> and a blog <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://saxefacts.com/climate-risk-management/|title=Learn More in our Climate Risk Management Blog|first=Dianne|last=Saxe}}</ref> and presents on climate issues.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://saxefacts.com/climate-presentations/|title=In-Depth and Informative Climate Presentations|first=Dianne|last=Saxe}}</ref> She was a McMurtry Clinical Fellow at [[Osgoode Hall Law School]] 2019-2020 <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.osgoode.yorku.ca/faculty-and-research/fellowships/mcmurtry-visiting-clinical-fellowship-call-applications/meet-the-mcmurtry-fellow/|title=Meet the McMurtry Fellows|website=Osgoode Hall Law School}}</ref> and is a senior fellow at [[Massey College]].  Dr. Saxe hosts a podcast called Green Economy Heroes featuring interviews with green business leaders.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://saxefacts.com/climate-podcast/|title=Green Economy Heroes Climate Podcast|first=Dianne|last=Saxe}}</ref> She has also been a public support to the youth climate strikes movement in Toronto, and co-hosted a Youth Climate Summit with activists [[Aliénor Rougeot|Alienor Rougeot]] and Emma Lim, and former child and youth advocate of Ontario Irwin Elman during the [[September 2019 climate strikes|September week for climate in 2019]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=ICI.Radio-Canada.ca|first=Zone Environnement-|title=Outiller les jeunes pour la lutte contre les changements climatiques|url=https://ici.radio-canada.ca/nouvelle/1312452/jeunes-lutte-changements-climatiques-greve|access-date=2020-07-25|website=Radio-Canada.ca|language=fr-ca}}</ref>

==Environmental Commissioner of Ontario==

Known for her role as an activist and her work in government and private practice, she was appointed to the position of [[Environmental Commissioner of Ontario]] in 2015 by the [[Legislative Assembly of Ontario]], unanimously, for a five year term.<ref name="osgoode"/><ref name="eco"/><ref>{{cite web|title=Focus: Saxe gearing up for new role as commissioner|url=http://www.lawtimesnews.com/201511235070/focus-on/focus-saxe-gearing-up-for-new-role-as-commissioner|website=Law Times News|publisher=Law Times News|accessdate=24 March 2016}}</ref> The Commissioner was an independent legislative officer, guardian of the [[Environmental Bill of Rights]] and required by the Bill <ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.ontario.ca/laws/view|title=Law Document English View|date=July 24, 2014|website=Ontario.ca}}</ref> to report to the Ontario Legislature each year on Ontario's progress on each of [[energy conservation]], [[environmental protection]] and [[climate change]]. She was also permitted to deliver special reports. As Commissioner, she delivered 17 widely used reports to the Legislature on topics including environmental injustice to First Nations,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ecojustice.ca/environmental-commissioner-report/|title=Environmental Commissioner of Ontario calls upon province to take action in Chemical Valley|date=October 25, 2017|website=Ecojustice}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dragun.ca/environmental-commissioner-of-ontarios-report/|title=Environmental Commissioner of Ontario’s Report|date=November 8, 2017}}</ref> [[electricity]],<ref>https://eco.auditor.on.ca/reports/2018-making-connections/</ref> waste and [[circular economy]],<ref>https://eco.auditor.on.ca/reports/2017-beyond-the-blue-box/</ref> [[endangered species]],<ref>https://eco.auditor.on.ca/reports/2017-good-choices-bad-choices/</ref> [[water pollution]],<ref>https://eco.auditor.on.ca/reports/2018-back-to-basics/</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://globalnews.ca/news/4657993/ontario-environmental-watchdog-sewage/|title=Raw sewage overflowing into waterways at alarming rate: Ontario watchdog|website=Global News}}</ref> [[soil health]]<ref>https://eco.auditor.on.ca/reports/2016-putting-soil-health-first/</ref> and [[climate policy]].<ref name="eco.auditor.on.ca">https://eco.auditor.on.ca/reports/2018-climate-action-in-ontario/</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://saxefacts.com/environmental-policies-in-canada/|title=ECO Reports and Environmental Policies in Canada|first=Dianne|last=Saxe}}</ref> She also gave hundreds of talks across Canada.

The [[Doug Ford]] Conservative government elected in June, 2018, took strong exception to her critique of their rolling back Ontario's climate change laws and policies.<ref name="eco.auditor.on.ca"/><ref>https://docs.assets.eco.on.ca/reports/climate-change/2018/Climate-Action-in-Ontario-Comments.pdf</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.enerquality.ca/2019/03/28/environmental-commissioner-files-final-report/|title=Environmental Commissioner Files Final Report|date=March 28, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tvo.org/article/the-environmental-commissioner-shows-why-the-powers-doug-ford-just-eliminated-are-so-important|title=The environmental commissioner shows why the powers Doug Ford just eliminated are so important|website=TVO.org}}</ref> Soon afterwards, on 15 Nov. 2018, they announced that her position would be abolished by legislation and some of its functions transferred to the Auditor General.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/opinion/contributors/2018/11/19/eliminating-ontarios-environmental-commissioner-a-short-sighted-move.html|title=Opinion &#124; Eliminating Ontario’s environmental commissioner a short-sighted move|date=November 19, 2018|website=thestar.com}}</ref> Hundreds of scientists <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://evidencefordemocracy.ca/en/content/scientists-send-letter-ford-urging-him-reconsider-eliminating-office-environmental|title=Scientists' letter to Ford on the Environmental Commissioner of Ontario|date=December 4, 2018|website=Evidence For Democracy}}</ref> plus the majority of Ontarians polled said that this move would have a negative impact on the environment.<ref>https://secureservercdn.net/198.71.233.47/823.910.myftpupload.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/2018-1328-OPSEU-Economic-statement-Populated-report-FINAL-with-tabs.pdf?time=1591965152</ref> Her last report was even more critical of the [[Doug Ford]] government.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ipolitics.ca/2019/03/27/ontario-environment-commissioner-exits-warning-of-frightening-policies/|title=Ontario environment commissioner exits warning of 'frightening' policies|date=March 27, 2019}}</ref> After 25 years, the position of the Environmental Commissioner of Ontario, including the role of guardian of the Environmental Bill of Rights, ceased to exist when the Bill was amended on April 1, 2019.<ref name="auto"/>

==Awards and recognition==

Dr. Saxe is a recipient of numerous awards, including specialist certifications from [[The Law Society of Upper Canada]],<ref name="osgoode">{{cite web|title=Dianne Saxe '74, '91 (PhD) appointed Environmental Commissioner of Ontario|url=http://www.osgoode.yorku.ca/news/dianne-saxe-74-91-phd-appointed-environmental-commissioner-of-ontario-2|website=Osgoode Hall Law School|publisher=Osgoode Hall Law School|accessdate=24 March 2016}}</ref> [[Osgoode Hall Law School]] Alumni Gold Key for Achievement <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.osgoode.yorku.ca/alumni/alumni-awards/gold-key/gold-key-award-winners/|title=Alumni Gold Key Recipients|website=Osgoode Hall Law School}}</ref> and a 2020 Law Society Medal for exemplary leadership in environmental law.<ref name="LSOmedals">{{cite web|title= Law Society announces 2020 award recipients |url=https://lso.ca/news-events/news/latest-news-2020/law-society-announces-2020-award-recipients|website=Law Society of Ontario|publisher=Law Society of Ontario|accessdate=10 June 2020}}</ref> In August 2020, Dr. Saxe completed her training to become an En-ROADS Climate Ambassador, a member in the international network that leads climate simulation events developed by [https://www.climateinteractive.org Climate Interactive] and the [https://mitsloan.mit.edu/sustainability/ MIT Sloan Sustainability Initiative].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Meet our Ambassadors|url=https://www.climateinteractive.org/tools/en-roads/climate-ambassadors/|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Climate Interactive|language=en-US}}</ref> Dr. Saxe is also a trained [https://climaterealityproject.org/ Climate Reality Leader], having been personally trained by [[Al Gore]] on the climate crisis and its solutions.

==Books and articles==

Her books include Environmental Offences, Corporate Responsibility and Executive Liability, published by Canada Law Book,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.torontopubliclibrary.ca/detail.jsp?Entt=RDM629915&R=629915|title=Environmental offences : corporate responsibility and executive liability|first=Dianne|last=Saxe|via=www.torontopubliclibrary.ca}}</ref> and A Buyer's Guide to Contaminated Land.<ref>https://commentary.canlii.org/w/canlii/1996CanLIIDocs14?zoupio-debug#!fragment//(hash:(chunk:(anchorText:''),notesQuery:'',scrollChunk:!n,searchQuery:'%22dianne%20saxe%22',searchSortBy:RELEVANCE,tab:search))</ref> She was the founder and first author of Ontario Environmental Protection Act Annotated.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://store.thomsonreuters.ca/en-ca/pdp/ontario-environmental-protection-act-annotated/30842944|title=Ontario Environmental Protection Act Annotated|website=store.thomsonreuters.ca}}</ref> Her analyses have been cited with approval by many courts and commentators across Canada.<ref>https://www.canlii.org/en/index.php#search/text=%22dianne%20saxe%22&resultIndex=1</ref>

Her articles have been published in many journals and periodicals for legal<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thelawyersdaily.ca/|title=The Lawyer's Daily|website=www.thelawyersdaily.ca}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mcgill.ca/law/channels/event/environmental-accountability-dianne-saxe-environmental-commissioner-ontario-293897|title=Environmental Accountability with Dianne Saxe, Environmental Commissioner of Ontario|website=Faculty of Law}}</ref>  and general audiences.,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/opinion/contributors/2019/08/25/climate-crisis-puts-corporate-boards-on-the-hot-seat.html|title=Opinion &#124; Climate crisis puts corporate boards on the hot seat|date=August 25, 2019|website=thestar.com}}</ref> such as 10 Principles to Guide the Transition to a Green Economy.<ref name="opencanada.org"/> Some recent articles are posted at [https://saxefacts.com/articles/ SaxeFacts.com].

==Boards of directors==

Dr. Saxe has sat upon a number of public and private boards, including Draxis Health, Solarshare,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.solarbonds.ca/|title=SolarShare|website=SolarShare}}</ref> [[WindShare]] and Evergreen,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.evergreen.ca/|title=Home &#124; Evergreen|website=www.evergreen.ca}}</ref> helped to manage the endowment of the [[Ontario Bar Association]] and is recognized as Board-Ready by Women in Capital Markets.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wcm.ca/women-in-leadership/boards|title=Women in Capital Markets|website=Women in Capital Markets}}</ref>

==Family==

Dr. Saxe is the daughter of Canadian doctor, businessman, media personality and politician [[Morton Shulman]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://search.ontariojewisharchives.org/Permalink/accessions24577|title=Morton Shulman and Saxe family &#124; Ontario Jewish Archives|website=search.ontariojewisharchives.org}}</ref> and the mother of MIT Neuroscience professor [[Rebecca Saxe]], and University of Toronto Civil Engineering Professor, Shoshanna Saxe.
