"'''Sladdbarn'''" is a Swedish term to refer to a child who is [[Birth order|born much later]] than their other siblings. The terms differ from the colloquial English expression "oops baby" which simply refers to any [[unplanned pregnancy|unplanned birth]]. There are similar terms for the concept in many other [[Nordic languages]] and some others.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://arwidson.wordpress.com/2017/05/30/sladdbarn/|title=Sladdbarn|last=Arwidson|first=Thorulf|date=May 13, 2017|website=Ardwidson.wordpress.com|language=sv}}</ref>

==Definition==
There are many opinions about how far apart it should be for the child to considered a sladdbarn.<ref>{{Cite web A commonly held rule of thumb is that it should be 10 years between the children.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.aftonbladet.se/halsa/a/Xwaa5W/aldst-yngst-eller-mellanbarn--sa-paverkas-din-personlighet|title=Äldst, yngst eller mellanbarn – så påverkas din personlighet|last=Franzén|first=Stina|website=Aftonbladet.se|date=August 6, 2015|language=sv}}</ref> Another criterion may be that the child is born long after the first clutch of children and thus is not part of the companionship of their older siblings, missing out on the sibling bonding stage during childhood, developing more as a single child.

==Causes==
Sladdbarn can be born because the parents mistakenly believe that they can [[Menopause|no longer have children due to their age]] and stop using [[contraceptives]]. Another reason for a much later sibling is due to the parents feeling that their first group of children are getting older and they miss having small children around.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metro.se/artikel/ny-unders%C3%B6kning-s%C3%A5-p%C3%A5verkas-vi-av-att-f%C3%A5-barn|title=Ny undersökning: Så påverkas vi av att få barn|last=Bergman|first=Linnea|date=October 30, 2017|website=Metro.se|language=sv|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190102051212/https://www.metro.se/artikel/ny-unders%C3%B6kning-s%C3%A5-p%C3%A5verkas-vi-av-att-f%C3%A5-barn|archive-date=January 2, 2019}}</ref> Sometimes older couples with a more unstable relationship have yet another child in later life due to feeling a need to keep the relationship together when their older children start becoming independent, thus losing the so-called "glue" that kept them from growing apart.

==Effects==
During the early 20th century having a sladdbarn was considered shameful in the country, as it was considered to imply that the parents were overly sexually active in old age.<ref name=Skåne/> In Sweden in the 1960s having a sladdbarn was considered a major economic setback for a working-class family<ref>1966, ''Industria''. Volume 62; Edition: 7–12 – Page 56</ref> but a [[status symbol]] for the richer who could afford it.<ref name=Skåne>Åke Arenhill; ''Nya och illustrerade sagor om Skåne'' 1966</ref> Children whose only siblings are much older than them sometimes report feeling like an [[only child]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2014/apr/05/wish-my-son-wasnt-an-only-child |author=Bradford, Kelly Rose |date=5 April 2014 |work=[[The Guardian]] |title=I wish my son wasn't an only child}}</ref> Many sladdbarn are often accused of being spoiled by their parents according to the older siblings.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.aftonbladet.se/relationer/a/vmnjPl/mina-syskon-kallar-mig-lat-och-slarvig|title=Mina syskon kallar mig lat och slarvig|last=Lidbo|first=Ulrika|date=April 5, 2013|website=Aftonbladet.se|language=sv}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.svd.se/vi-blir-aldrig-fria-fran-vara-syskon|title=Vi blir aldrig fria från våra syskon|last=Lagerblad|first=Anna|date=January 5, 2015|website=[[Svenska Dagbladet]]|language=sv}}</ref> They are also often said to remain childish even in adulthood.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hemmets.se/ola-salo-som-liten-langtade-jag-mycket-efter-pappa/|title=Ola Salo: Som liten längtade jag mycket efter pappa|date=December 26, 2018|website=Hemmets.se|language=sv|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190103004913/https://www.hemmets.se/ola-salo-som-liten-langtade-jag-mycket-efter-pappa/|archive-date=January 3, 2019}}</ref>

==Other terms==
There are many playful synonyms for sladdbarn in the Nordic countries. In [[Swedish language|Swedish]], "efterskott" may be used (a double entendre, meaning "arrears", but literally also "after-shot").{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} In [[Finland Swedish]], there is a term "skrapabulla" (scrape bun, the word comes from the slightly smaller bun that gets created when a baker needs to scrape off the bowl to get enough dough to fill the last cup).{{citation needed|date=November 2020}} In [[Finnish language|Finnish]], the term that is used is "iltatähti" (Evening Star), this after the planet [[Venus]], which becomes visible during dusk, before the sun has gone down completely.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sprakinstitutet.fi/sv/publikationer/sprakspalter/reuters_rutor_1986_2013/2000/dialektala_uttryck|title=Dialektala uttryck - Institutet för de inhemska språken|website=Sprakinstitutet.fi|access-date=24 October 2021}}</ref> In [[Norwegian language|Norwegian]], "attpåklatt" is a commonly used term, being the equivalent of "top-up" or "small refill" of your bowl of porridge.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://naob.no/ordbok/attp%C3%A5klatt|title=attpåklatt|website=[[Det Norske Akademis ordbok]]|language=no}}</ref> [[Danish language|Danish]] uses the term "efternøler".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ordnet.dk/ddo/ordbog?query=eftern%C3%B8ler|title=efternøler|website=ordnet.dk|language=da}}</ref> Compare Danish "efterfølger", meaning "successor" and "nøler" which translates to "hesitate" in English.
