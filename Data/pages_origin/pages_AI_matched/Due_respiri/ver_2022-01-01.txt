"'''Due respiri'''" is a song written by [[Eros Ramazzotti]], Luca Chiaravalli and Saverio Grandi and recorded by Italian singer [[Chiara Galiazzo]]. The song was released as Chiara's debut single on 8 December 2012, immediately after she won the [[X Factor (Italy series 6)|sixth series]] of [[talent show]] ''[[X Factor (Italian TV series)|X Factor]]''.<ref name=VareseNews>{{Cite web|url=http://www.laprovinciadivarese.it/stories/Cronaca/754279/|work=La Provincia di Varese|language=Italian|accessdate=31 August 2013|title=Luca Chiaravalli, bis da record. Il trionfo di Chiara made in Gallarate}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.adnkronos.com/IGN/News/Spettacolo/Musica-X-Factor-domani-ep-su-iTunes-e-singoli-on-air-di-Chiara-e-altri-finalisti_313969912325.html|publisher=[[Adnkronos]]|language=Italian|accessdate=1 September 2013|title=Musica: 'X Factor', domani ep su iTunes e singoli on air di Chiara e altri finalisti|date=7 December 2012}}</ref>

The song debuted at number one on the [[FIMI|Italian Top Digital Downloads]], spending three weeks atop the chart, and it was later certified double platinum by the [[Federation of the Italian Music Industry]].<ref>{{cite certification|region=Italy|type=single|artist=Chiara|accessdate=31 August 2013}}</ref><ref name=ITACharts/>

==Background==
"Due respiri" was written by Eros Ramazzotti, Luca Chiaravalli and Saverio Grandi. According to Chiaravalli, Ramazzotti decided to compose a song for Chiara immediately after hearing her during the first live show of the [[X Factor (Italy series 6)|sixth series]] of ''[[X Factor (Italian TV series)|X Factor]]''. He started to write the song from a piano riff. He later asked Chiaravalli and Grandi to continue working on the song, and they wrote its lyrics in their studio in [[Gallarate]], in Lombardy. Finally, Ramazzotti completed the song, and Chiara contributed modifying the second verse of the song.<ref name=VareseNews/> In an interview to ''[[TGCOM]]'', Chiara also revealed that three different versions of the song exist, and that the one she performed was adapted for her by her ''X Factor'' mentor, [[Morgan (singer)|Morgan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tgcom24.mediaset.it/spettacolo/articoli/1072581/chiara-non-amo-essere-sexy-sono-semplice.shtml|publisher=[[Mediaset]]|work=[[TGCOM]]|author=Andrea Conti|accessdate=1 September 2013|title=Chiara: Non amo essere sexy, sono semplice|date=10 December 2012}}</ref>

The song's lyrics refer to an indissoluble relationship, and Chiara decided to dedicate it to music, explaining that for her "there's nothing like you [music]", as the chorus says.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mattinopadova.gelocal.it/cronaca/2012/11/29/news/due-respiri-il-pezzo-che-eros-ha-scritto-per-chiara-1.6111480|work=Il Mattino di Padova|language=Italian|date=29 November 2012|title=Chiara da brividi con la canzone di Eros. E vola in finale|accessdate=31 August 2013}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cremaonline.it/articolo.asp?ID=22532|author=Rebecca Ronchi|date=11 May 2013|title=Chiara Galiazzo, sold out a Crema. Cresce l’attesa per il concerto della vincitrice dell’edizione 2012 di X-Factor, al San Domenico il prossimo 21 maggio|publisher=Crema On Line}}</ref>

==Live performances==
Chiara performed the song for the first time on 29 November 2012, during the semifinal of the [[X Factor (Italy series 6)|sixth series]] of ''[[X Factor (Italian TV series)|X Factor]]''. "Due respiri" was also the first Italian-language song she performed during the show. After receiving positive comments by the four judged, Chiara advanced to the final.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.lastampa.it/2012/11/30/spettacoli/x-factor-niente-finale-per-daniele-DeeC0eFSPdUigh7vf8SaQO/pagina.html|work=[[La Stampa]]|language=Italian|date=30 November 2012|title=X Factor 6: niente finale per Daniele|author=Ludovica Sanfelice}}</ref> 
On 1 December 2012, while she was still a contestant of the show, Chiara gave her first concert in her hometown, [[Padua]], where she performed "Due respiri", as well as covers of popular songs by other artists.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mattinopadova.gelocal.it/cronaca/2012/12/02/news/la-prima-volta-dal-vivo-di-chiara-al-fishmarket-1.6128769|work=Il Mattino di Padova|language=Italian|date=2 December 2012|title=Il primo concerto di Chiara è al Fishmarket|author=Leandro Barsotti|accessdate=1 September 2013}}</ref>
During the final, which was held on 6 and 7 December 2012, Galiazzo performed the song two additional times, before being announced as the winner of the competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iltempo.it/cultura-spettacoli/2013/02/01/chiara-galiazzo-ha-sbaragliato-gli-altri-concorrenti-ed-la-vincitrice-dell-edizione-2012-di-x-factor-il-programma-in-onda-su-sky-uno-1.1101881|work=[[Il Tempo]]|language=Italian|accessdate=1 September 2013|date=9 December 2012|title=Chiara Galiazzo ha sbaragliato gli altri concorrenti ed la vincitrice dell'edizione 2012 di «X Factor», il programma in onda su Sky Uno}}</ref>
Galiazzo also performed the song during her 2013 Un posto nel mondo tour, both as part of the set list and as an encore.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.padovaoggi.it/eventi/concerti/chiara-galiazzo-concerto-recensione-hydrogen-festival.html|work=Padova Oggi|language=Italian|accessdate=1 September 2013|title=Chiara Galiazzo emozionatissima apre l'Hydrogen Festival|date=30 June 2013|author=François Turatto}}</ref>
