'''''Rhopalosiphum''''' is a genus of [[aphid]] of the family [[Aphididae]] that includes 16 species worldwide.<ref name=Heie1997>{{cite book | last = Heie | first = O. E. | title = The Aphidoidea (Hemiptera) of Fennoscandia and Denmark: Family Aphididae - Subfamily Pterocommatinae and Tribe Aphidini of Subfamily Aphidinae ([[Fauna Entomologica Scandinavica]]) | publisher = Brill Academic Publishers | year = 1997 | location = Leiden, The Netherlands | pages = 314  | isbn = 978-90-04-08088-1}}</ref>  Apart from sucking the phloem of host plants and thereby being treated in agriculture as [[Pest (organism)|pests]], some species are [[Vector (epidemiology)|vectors]] for [[Virus|viral pathogens]].<ref name=Taber1994>{{cite journal | last = Taber | first = S. W. | title = Labile behavioral evolution in a genus of agricultural pests: The Rhopalosiphum plant lice (Hemiptera: Aphididae) | journal = Annals of the Entomological Society of America | volume = 87 | issue = 3 | pages = 311–320 | publisher = [[Entomological Society of America]] | location = Lanham, Maryland | year = 1994 | doi = 10.1093/aesa/87.3.311 }}</ref>
