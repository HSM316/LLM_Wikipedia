'''Khatun Sapnara''' ({{lang-bn|খাতুন সাপনারা}}; born 29 April 1967) is a [[Bangladesh]]i-born [[United Kingdom|British]] [[judge]]. In 2006, she was appointed a [[Recorder (judge)|Recorder]] of the [[Crown Court]], becoming the first person of [[British Bangladeshi|Bangladeshi]] origin in a senior judicial position. In 2014, she was appointed as a [[Circuit judge (England and Wales)|Circuit Judge]] to hear cases in the Crown and [[Family proceedings court|Family Court]].

==Early life==
Sapnara was born in [[Sylhet District]], [[East Pakistan]] (now [[Bangladesh]]) and moved to the [[United Kingdom]] as a child. Her father, the late Mimbor Ali, was honoured by the Bangladeshi government for his significant contribution to the [[Bangladesh Liberation War|liberation of Bangladesh]].<ref name="bbwhoswho08">{{cite book|last1=Karim|first1=Mohammed Abdul|last2=Karim|first2=Shahadoth|title=British Bangladeshi Who's Who|url=http://www.bbwhoswho.co.uk/Profile/Sapnara,%20Ms%20Khatun.pdf|date=July 2008|publisher=British Bangla Media Group|page=190|access-date=1 May 2012|archive-date=1 April 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160401163523/http://www.bbwhoswho.co.uk/Profile/Sapnara,%20Ms%20Khatun.pdf|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="bbwhoswho09">{{cite book |last1=Karim|first1=Mohammed Abdul|last2=Karim|first2=Shahadoth|title=British Bangladeshi Who's Who|url=http://www.bbwhoswho.co.uk/whoswho_09.pdf|date=October 2009|publisher=British Bangla Media Group|page=117|access-date=1 May 2012}}</ref><ref name="history">{{cite news|url=http://www.history.org.uk/resources/general_news_250.html|title=Enforced marriage law forces couple apart|publisher=The Historical Association|date=19 March 2009|access-date=1 May 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924030433/http://www.history.org.uk/resources/general_news_250.html|archive-date=24 September 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Sapnara attended Chadwell Heath School (now [[Chadwell Heath Academy]]). In 1988, she graduated with [[Bachelor of Laws|LLB (Hons)]] from the [[London School of Economics|London School of Economics and Political Science]].<ref name="bbwhoswho08"/><ref name="bbwhoswho09"/><ref name="history"/>

==Career==
In 1990, Sapnara was called to the [[Bar (law)|Bar]] at [[Middle Temple]].<ref name="history" /> She practises from Coram Barristers Chambers in [[London]] and specialises in [[Family law]].<ref name="coramchambers1">{{cite web|url=http://www.coramchambers.co.uk/barristers/profiles/khatun_sapnara |title=Khatun Sapnara |publisher=Coram Chambers |access-date=1 May 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130322083715/http://www.coramchambers.co.uk/barristers/profiles/khatun_sapnara |archive-date=22 March 2013 }}</ref><ref name="tower-hamlets-libdems">{{cite news |last=Jones|first=Meirion|url=http://www.tower-hamlets-libdems.org.uk/en/article/2007/097110/tower-hamlets-liberal-democrats-against-forced-marriage|title=Tower Hamlets Liberal Democrats against Forced Marriage|publisher=Tower Hamlets Liberal Democrats|date=25 February 2007|access-date=1 December 2013}}</ref>

In 2003, Sapnara became the first [[Ethnic group|ethnic]] [[Minority group|minority]] person to be elected to the Family Law Bar Association Committee. In 2004, she was appointed by the [[Lord Chancellor]] [[Charles Falconer, Baron Falconer of Thoroton|Baron Charles Falconer of Thoroton]] to the [[Family Justice Council]], a body of experts which advises the government on all aspects of the family justice system.<ref name="coramchambers1"/> She is an expert in [[Honour killing|"honour-based" violence]],<ref name="welovethisbook">{{cite web|url=http://www.welovethisbook.com/gallery/image/khatun-sapnara|title=Khatun Sapnara|publisher=We Love This Book|access-date=1 December 2013|archive-date=3 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141103174738/http://www.welovethisbook.com/gallery/image/khatun-sapnara|url-status=dead}}</ref> and assisted in formulating and drafting the [[Forced Marriage (Civil Protection) Act 2007]].<ref name="bbc">{{cite news |last=Jones|first=Meirion|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/programmes/newsnight/8165684.stm|title=Enforced marriage law forces couple apart|work=[[BBC News]]|date=24 July 2009|access-date=1 May 2012}}</ref> In 2006, she was appointed as a [[Recorder (judge)|Recorder]] of the Crown.<ref name="bbwhoswho08"/><ref name="bbwhoswho09"/>

In 2012, she was involved in proposals to criminalise forced marriage,<ref name="independent">{{cite news |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/voices/letters/letters-no-need-to-outlaw-forced-marriages-7622132.html|title=Letters: No need to outlaw forced marriages|newspaper=[[The Independent]]|date=6 April 2012|access-date=1 December 2012}}</ref><ref name="guardian">{{cite news |last1=Gill|first1=Aisha|last2=Sapnara|first2=Khatun|url=https://www.guardian.co.uk/commentisfree/2012/apr/09/forced-marriages-criminalising|title=Forced marriages blight lives, but criminalising them would not work|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|date=9 April 2012|access-date=1 May 2012}}</ref> arguing against criminalisation, contending that it was surplus to requirements<ref name="hja">{{cite news |url=http://www.hja.net/legal-news/news-articles-list/directnews-import/should-forced-marriage-be.aspx|title=Should forced marriage be criminalised?|publisher=Hodge Jones & Allen Solicitors|date=11 April 2012|access-date=1 December 2012}}</ref><ref name="bmsd">{{cite news |url=http://bmsd.org.uk/index.php/forced-marriages-undermine-the-values-of-islam/ |title=Forced Marriages Undermine the Values of Islam |website=[[British Muslims for Secular Democracy]] |date=13 April 2012}}</ref> and that the move could deter victims from seeking help. She wrote in a joint newspaper article that it was a "quick-fix solution to a complex and long-standing problem."<ref name="thetimes">{{cite news |last=Gibb|first=Frances|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/legal-eagle-has-broken-ground-in-long-career-r392tn88x|title=Legal eagle Khatun Sapnara has broken ground in long career|newspaper=[[The Times]]|date=30 August 2017|access-date=1 October 2017}}</ref> In June 2012, she appeared on [[BBC Two]]'s ''[[Newsnight]]'', debating against criminalisation of forced marriage with [[Aneeta Prem]] of [[Freedom (charity)|Freedom Charity]].<ref name="Muslim Families, Politics and the Law">{{cite book |last=Grillo|first=Ralph|title=Muslim Families, Politics and the Law: A Legal Industry in Multicultural Britain|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=kW_BCAAAQBAJ&q=Khatun+Sapnara&pg=PA71|year=1988|publisher=[[John Wiley & Sons]]|page=71|isbn=9781472451217}}</ref>

In March 2014, she was appointed as a Circuit Judge sitting at both the East London Family Court and at [[Kingston upon Thames Crown Court]].<ref name="lawgazette">{{cite news|last=Fouzder|first=Monidipa|url=http://www.lawgazette.co.uk/news/more-part-time-judicial-appointments-in-pipeline/5045180.fullarticle|title=More part-time judicial appointments in pipeline|publisher=[[The Law Society Gazette]]|date=19 November 2014|access-date=1 July 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180914025250/https://www.lawgazette.co.uk/news/more-part-time-judicial-appointments-in-pipeline/5045180.fullarticle|archive-date=14 September 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="judiciary">{{cite web |url=http://www.judiciary.gov.uk/announcements/appt-of-a-circuit-judge-sapnara|title=Appointment of a Circuit Judge: Sapnara|publisher=Courts and Tribunals Judiciary|date=4 March 2014|access-date=10 November 2014}}</ref><ref name="coramchambers2">{{cite web|url=http://www.coramchambers.co.uk/congratulations-to-khatun-sapnara-appointed-as-a-circuitjudge/|title=Congratulations to Khatun Sapnara Appointed as a Circuit Judge|publisher=Coram Chambers|date=4 March 2014|access-date=1 July 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304060348/http://www.coramchambers.co.uk/congratulations-to-khatun-sapnara-appointed-as-a-circuitjudge/|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Between 2003 and 2014, Sapnara was Chair of the Ashiana Network,<ref name="independent"/><ref name="hja"/> a refuge and support agency for female victims of domestic violence and forced marriage.<ref name="welovethisbook"/> She has served as director/chair of a number of charities and voluntary sector organisations and lectures widely on issues relating to family law<ref name="bbwhoswho08" /> including to the [[Judicial College]] (formerly the Judicial Studies Board).
