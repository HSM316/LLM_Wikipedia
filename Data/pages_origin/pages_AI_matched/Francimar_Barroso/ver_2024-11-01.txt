'''Francimar Barroso''' (born 29 February 1980) is a Brazilian [[mixed martial arts|mixed martial artist]] currently competing in the [[Heavyweight (MMA)|Heavyweight]] division. A professional competitor since 2004, he has also competed for the [[UFC]], [[Shooto]], and [[Professional Fighters League]].

==Mixed martial arts career==
===Brazilian circuit===
On 12 April 2013, he faced Brazilian prospect Simao Melo da Silva for the WOCS Light Heavyweight Championship. Barroso won the fight via rear-naked choke.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mixedmartialarts.com/mma.cfm?go=stats.fightCard&eid=d2fad54f-afaa-4fb3-90f2-942d3e9a0ef6 |title=Watch Out Combat Show 25 Official Results |publisher=mixedmartialarts.com |date=12 April 2013}}</ref> He vacated the title shortly after the fight, after getting called from the UFC to be a late-fight replacement.

===Shooto Brazil===
Towards the beginning of his career, Barroso competed at Shooto Brazil 11 on 28 March 2009, against Roque Oliver. He won by submission (rear-naked choke).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://wrestlingdata.com/index.php?befehl=shows&show=65215 |title=Shooto Brazil 11 Results and Opinions on the Show |publisher=wrestlingdata.com |date=28 March 2009}}</ref> Shortly after the fight he was signed to a four-fight contract. He fought at the next event as well, fighting Levi da Costa on 30 May 2009. He won the fight by doctor stoppage.

After a long hiatus, Barroso returned on 10 March 2012, when he faced Falco Neto Lopes at Shooto Brazil 28. Barroso won via guillotine choke. On 23 November 2012, Barroso fought for the vacant Shooto Brazil Light Heavyweight championship, as he faced number one contender Cristiano Souza. Barroso won the fight by doctor stoppage to win the championship.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tapology.com/fightcenter/bouts/shooto-brazil-36-francimar-bodo-barroso-vs-cristiano-bob-sapp-souza |title=Francimar Barroso vs. Cristiano Souza |publisher=tapology.com |date=23 November 2013}}</ref> However he vacated the title, little over a year later to fight in the [[Ultimate Fighting Championship|UFC]].

===Desert Force Championship===
In November 2010, Barroso signed with upstart Jordan promotion Desert Force Championship (DFC). He made his DFC debut on 8 December 2010, against Abhijeet Petkar. Barroso won via head kick KO. Then on 19 May 2011, he faced [[Russian people|Russian]] prospect Baga Agaev at DFC 2.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.graciemag.com/2011/05/more-mma-coming-up-in-jordans-desert/ |title=More MMA coming up in Jordan's desert |publisher=graciemag.com |date=16 May 2011}}</ref> He lost the fight when Agaev landed a blistering punch that knocked Barroso down, and then landed multiple knees before the referee stepped in to stop the fight.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mma-core.com/events/DFC_Desert_Force_2/37383 |title=DFC - Desert Force 2 Results |publisher=mma-core.com |date=19 May 2011}}</ref>

===Ultimate Fighting Championship===
Barroso made his UFC debut on 3 August 2013, when he faced fellow [[Brazilian people|Brazilian]] Ednaldo Oliveira, after Oliveira's original opponent [[Robert Drysdale]] was forced out of the bout due to injury.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bloodyelbow.com/2013/7/17/4532514/francimar-bodao-barosso-replaces-injured-robert-drysdale-ufc-163-mma-news |title=Francimar Barroso replaces injured Robert Drysdale at UFC 163 |publisher=bloodyelbow.com |date=17 July 2013}}</ref> Barroso won via unanimous decision.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cagedinsider.com/ufc/ufc-163-results-francimar-barroso-defeats-ednaldo-oliveira-by-unanimous-decision/ |title=UFC 163 Results: Francimar Barroso defeats Ednaldo Oliveira by Unanimous Decision |publisher=cagedinsider.com |date=3 August 2013}}</ref>

Barroso faced UFC newcomer [[Hans Stringer]] at [[UFC Fight Night 38]] on 23 March 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mmafighting.com/2014/2/14/5406112/ufc-fight-night-38-card-complete-with-hans-stringer-vs-francimar |title=UFC Fight Night 38 card complete with Hans Stringer vs. Francimar Barroso |publisher=mmafighting.com |date=14 February 2014}}</ref> He lost the back-and-forth fight via split decision.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://mmajunkie.com/2014/03/ufc-fight-night-38-results-hans-stringer-outworks-francimar-barroso-for-split-decision/ |title=UFC Fight Night 38 results: Hans Stringer outworks Francimar Barroso for split decision |publisher=mmajunkie.com |date=23 March 2014}}</ref>
