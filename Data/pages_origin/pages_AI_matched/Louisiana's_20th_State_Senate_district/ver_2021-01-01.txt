'''Louisiana's 20th State Senate district''' is one of 39 districts in the [[Louisiana State Senate]]. It has been represented by [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] [[Mike Fesi]] since 2020, succeeding fellow Republican [[Norby Chabert]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://senate.la.gov/senators/senbiography.asp?SenID=20|title=District - 20|publisher=Senator Michael "Big Mike" Fesi|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref>

==Geography==
District 20 is located in [[Lafourche Parish, Louisiana|Lafourche]] and [[Terrebonne Parish, Louisiana|Terrebonne Parishes]] along the [[Gulf of Mexico]], including some or all of [[Thibodaux, Louisiana|Thibodaux]], [[Lockport, Louisiana|Lockport]], [[Cut Off, Louisiana|Cut Off]], [[Golden Meadow, Louisiana|Golden Meadow]], [[Houma, Louisiana|Houma]], [[Dulac, Louisiana|Dulac]], and [[Chauvin, Louisiana|Chauvin]].<ref name="Registration"/>

The district overlaps with [[United States Congress|U.S. congressional]] districts [[Louisiana's 1st congressional district|1]] and [[Louisiana's 6th congressional district|6]], and with [[Louisiana House of Representatives]] districts 51, 52, 53, 54, and 55.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dailykos.com/stories/2019/7/30/1848730/-How-do-counties-House-districts-and-legislative-districts-all-overlap-These-new-tools-show-you|author=David Jarman|title=How do counties, House districts, and legislative districts all overlap?|publisher=Daily Kos|access-date=8 October 2019}}</ref>

==Recent election results==
Louisiana uses a [[jungle primary]] system. If no candidate receives 50% in the first round of voting, when all candidates appear on the same ballot regardless of party, the top-two finishers advance to a [[Two-round system|runoff election]].
