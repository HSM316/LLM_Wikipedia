'''Merton Sandler''' (28 March 1926 – 24 August 2014) was a British professor of [[chemical pathology]] and a pioneer in [[biological psychiatry]].<ref name="Guard">{{cite news|last1=Sandler|first1=Dido|title=Merton Sandler obituary|url=https://www.theguardian.com/theguardian/2014/oct/09/merton-sandler-obituary|access-date=11 October 2014|work=The Guardian|date=9 October 2014}}</ref><ref name="Deb">{{cite web|title=Merton Sandler|url=http://www.debretts.com/people-of-today/profile/6859/Merton-SANDLER|website=Debrett's People of Today|access-date=11 October 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141111054836/http://www.debretts.com/people-of-today/profile/6859/Merton-SANDLER|archive-date=11 November 2014}}</ref>

==Education and career==
Sandler grew up in an observant [[Jews|Jewish]] family in [[Salford]]. He studied at the [[Manchester Grammar School]] having won a scholarship, before studying medicine at the [[University of Manchester]]. Following his qualification in 1949, Sandler served two years of National Service in the [[Royal Army Medical Corps]] at [[Shoreham-by-Sea]], attaining the rank of [[Captain (British Army and Royal Marines)|Captain]]. With his prior pathology training, he managed a small hospital laboratory during this period.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=2014-10-27 |title=Professor Merton Sandler - obituary |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/11188647/Professor-Merton-Sandler-obituary.html |access-date=2024-04-16 |website=The Telegraph |language=en}}</ref> In 1951 Sandler was appointed consultant chemical pathologist at [[Queen Charlotte's and Chelsea Hospital|Queen Charlotte’s Hospital]].<ref name=":0" /> In 1959, he suggested a link between [[Depression (differential diagnoses)|depression]] and [[monoamine]] deficiency in the brain, which led to the development of [[antidepressant]]s. Sandler was Professor of Chemical Pathology at the [[University of London]] from 1973 to 1991, and Fellow Emeritus of the [[American College of Neuropsychopharmacology]]<ref>{{Cite journal |last1=Oxenkrug |first1=Gregory |last2=Healy |first2=David |date=December 2014 |title=Merton Sandler |journal=Neuropsychopharmacology |language=en |volume=39 |issue=13 |pages=3126–3127 |doi=10.1038/npp.2014.248 |issn=1740-634X|pmc=4229587 }}</ref>

==Private life==
Sandler married Lorna Grenby in 1961 and they had four children. He was an active [[Freemason]] initiated in 1954 in the In Arduis Fidelis Lodge (London), and two years later in the [[Holy Royal Arch]]. He belonged to several [[Masonic lodge|lodges]] and chapters,<ref name=chapter>{{cite web |url = https://rshi4.weebly.com/history.html |title = History of the Chapter |publisher = Royal Somerset House & Inverness Chapter |access-date = 13 January 2018 }}</ref> and held office in the [[United Grand Lodge of England]].<ref name=YearBook>{{cite book |title=United Grand Lodge of England: Masonic Year Book (edition 2013-2014) |edition= 2013-2014|chapter= Grand Officers Alphabetically Arranged |location= London|publisher= UGLE|publication-date= 2013|page= 253}}</ref>
