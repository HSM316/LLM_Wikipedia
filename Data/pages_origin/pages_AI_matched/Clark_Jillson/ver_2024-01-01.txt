'''Clark Jillson''' was an American politician who served as the 18th [[List of mayors of Worcester, Massachusetts|Mayor]] of [[Worcester, Massachusetts]], from 1873 to 1874.

Clark Jillson was born in [[Windham, Vermont]], on April 11, 1825.<ref name= grave>{{cite web |title=Clark Jillson |url=https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/111364462/clark-jillson |website=Find A Grave |accessdate=28 March 2019}}</ref> He was brought up on his father's farm in Vermont, also helping him in his blacksmith shop.<ref name =Rice>{{cite book |last1=Rice |first1=Franklin P. |title=The Worcester of Eighteen Hundred and Ninety-Eight |url=https://archive.org/details/worcestereighte00ricegoog |date=1899 |publisher=F.S. Blanchard & Company |location=Worcester, Massachusetts |pages=665}}</ref> He came to Worcester in 1845, taking a job with Howe & Goddard at seventy-five cents a day. Over the next eight years, he worked for various machinists across the city, including [[Loring Coes|L. and A.G. Coes & Co.]] He also wrote for newspapers and magazines, and was elected president of the Young Men's Rhetorical Society in 1853.<ref name= book>{{cite book |last1=Nutt |first1=Charles |title=History of Worcester and its People |date=1919 |publisher=Lewis Historical Publishing Company |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=1XRKAAAAYAAJ&q=clark+jillson+worcester&pg=PA167 |accessdate=28 March 2019 |language=en}}</ref>

In 1873, Jillson was elected Mayor of Worcester and served three terms.<ref>{{cite web |title=Worcester Mayors |url=https://www.mywpl.org/worcester-mayors |website=Worcester Public Library |accessdate=28 March 2019}}</ref> He was the first mayor to use the veto power, and his administration saw the first reduction of city debt in thirteen years.<ref name= book/><ref name= Rice/>

Jillson was a founding member of the Sons and Daughters of Vermont, and served as president of [[Worcester Historical Museum|Worcester Society of Antiquity]].<ref name= book/><ref name= Rice/>

Jillson died in Worcester on June 5, 1894, and was buried in [[Rural Cemetery (Worcester, Massachusetts)|Rural Cemetery]].<ref name= grave/>
