'''Percy Skipworth Duff''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|MBE}} (1922 – 2011)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/announcements/deaths/deaths/9433408.percy_duff/|title=percy duff|website=The Westmorland Gazette|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> was the [[treasurer]] of [[Kendal]]<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=http://www.kendaltowncouncil.gov.uk|title=Kendal Town Council - Home - Old Market Town, Lake District - Kendal, Cumbria|website=Kendal Town Council|accessdate=4 October 2018}}</ref> [[Borough Council]] from 1967, until April 1974 when he became deputy treasurer of the new [[South Lakeland District Council]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.southlakeland.gov.uk|title=South Lakeland District Council home page|website=Southlakeland.gov.uk|accessdate=4 October 2018}}</ref> holding the position until his retirement in 1982. He was the last surviving Honorary Citizen of Kendal,<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/news/6945826.town-honours-remarkable-citizen/|title=Town Honours Remarkable Citizen.|website=The Westmorland Gazette|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/news/9445327.memorial-service-for-kendals-last-surviving-honorary-citizen-percy-duff/|title=Memorial service for Kendal's last surviving Honorary Citizen, Percy Duff|website=The Westmorland Gazette|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> an accolade he was said to value more than his MBE.

As his son Michael Duff said at his funeral "Percy Duff was a lucky man. He was lucky to survive [[scarlet fever]] as a child; he was lucky to survive in the Western Desert in 1943 when the man standing next to him was killed by a [[Mortar (weapon)|mortar]] and he wasn’t; he was lucky to be happily married; he was lucky to suffer from no illness in a long life and he was lucky to live in Kendal for all that time - a place that he loved."<ref name=":2" />

Percy Duff was involved in many clubs and societies some of which were: 
* The [[Westmorland Motor Club]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.westmorlandmotorclub.co.uk|title=Welcome to the updated WESTMORLAND MOTOR CLUB WEBSITE|website=Westmorlandmotorclub.co.uk|accessdate=4 October 2018}}</ref> in which he was President and who organise [[Motorcycle trials|Motorcycle Trials]] and [[Hillclimbing|Hill Climb Racing]]. Percy organised the first the [[Barbon Hillclimb]] for motorcycles in 1960 and was involved in the event for 50 years; the event was latterly referred to as "The Percy Duff Barbon Hill Climb"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cwherald.com/a/archive/50th-percy-duff-barbon-hill-climb.353187.html|title=50th Percy Duff Barbon Hill Climb|website=www.cwherald.com|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref>
* The [[Alfred Wainwright Society]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wainwright.org.uk|title=The Wainwright Society :: for lovers of the fells and the Lake District|website=Wainwright.org.uk|accessdate=4 October 2018}}</ref> Percy was articled to [[Alfred Wainwright]],<ref name=":1" /> who he succeeded as [[treasurer]] of [[Kendal]]<ref name=":0" /> [[Borough Council]].
* The [[Stephenson Locomotive Society]]<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.stephensonloco.org.uk/ |title=SLS Home page |access-date=2019-05-24 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181113173712/http://stephensonloco.org.uk/ |archive-date=2018-11-13 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
*[[Auto-Cycle Union|Northern Centre Auto-Cycle Union]] where he was vice-life president.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/news/9429751.death-of-percy-duff-former-town-treasurer-and-kendals-last-surviving-honorary-citizen/|title=Death of Percy Duff: former town treasurer and Kendal's last surviving Honorary Citizen|website=The Westmorland Gazette|language=en|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref>

He was appointed MBE in the [[1986 New Year Honours]] for his voluntary work with The Mayor Of Kendal's Fund For The Aged And Infirm<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://beta.charitycommission.gov.uk/charity-details/?subid=0&regid=223344|title=Charity Details|website=beta.charitycommission.gov.uk|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> where his careful management over 31 years enabled the charity to improve and increase the number of affordable retirement properties.<ref name=":3" />
