A foreign national wishing to enter '''[[Papua New Guinea]]''' must obtain a [[visa (document)|visa]] in one of the [[List of diplomatic missions of Papua New Guinea|PNG diplomatic missions]], unless they are a citizen of one of the countries eligible for visa on arrival or eVisa. All visitors must hold a passport valid for 6 months.

==Electronic visa (e-Visa)==
Citizens of the following countries can obtain an e-Visa valid for 30 or 60 days.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://evisa.ica.gov.pg/evisa/account/Apply | title=eVisa}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.tourmag.com/Papouasie-Nouvelle-Guinee-introduction-d-un-visa-electronique_a96068.html | title=Papouasie-Nouvelle-Guinée : Introduction d'un visa électronique}}</ref><br/>
Applications lodged via the eVisa portal will attract a fee of US$50.<br/>
This visa is valid for a single entry and expressly prohibits the holder from engaging in paid work.

==APEC Business Travel Card==
Holders of passports issued by the following countries who possess an [[APEC Business Travel Card]] (ABTC) containing a "PNG" code on the reverse side, which indicates that it is valid for travel to Papua New Guinea, can enter Papua New Guinea without a visa for business trips for up to 60 days.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.timaticweb.com/cgi-bin/tim_website_client.cgi?FullText=1&COUNTRY=PG&SECTION=VI&SUBSECTION=00&user=KLMB2C&subuser=KLMB2C | title=Archived copy | access-date=2014-03-15 |archive-date=2014-03-14 | archive-url=https://archive.today/20140314210644/https://www.timaticweb.com/cgi-bin/tim_website_client.cgi?FullText=1&COUNTRY=PG&SECTION=VI&SUBSECTION=00&user=KLMB2C&subuser=KLMB2C | url-status=dead }}</ref>
