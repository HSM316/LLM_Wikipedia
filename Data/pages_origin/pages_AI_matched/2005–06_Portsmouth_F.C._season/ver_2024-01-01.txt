During the [[2005–06 in English football|2005–06 season]], Portsmouth competed in the [[FA Premier League]]. It was Portsmouth's third consecutive season in English football's top-flight.

==Season summary==
Manager [[Alain Perrin]] was sacked in November with Portsmouth struggling in the relegation zone. [[Joe Jordan]] was appointed caretaker manager after the sacking of Perrin. <ref>{{cite news|title=Portsmouth dismiss manager Perrin|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/p/portsmouth/4455700.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=24 November 2005|access-date=28 September 2013}}</ref> He was replaced by [[Harry Redknapp]], who had walked out of arch-rivals [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]] after being refused permission by the club to talk to Portsmouth about the managerial vacancy.<ref>{{cite news|title=Redknapp goes back to Portsmouth|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/4506132.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=7 December 2005|access-date=28 September 2013}}</ref> For a while it looked as if Redknapp's efforts were in vain, with Portsmouth eight points adrift of safety at the end of February. However, by the end of March that had been cut to just three (with a game in hand over seventeenth-placed [[West Bromwich Albion F.C.|West Bromwich Albion]]) and eventually Portsmouth managed to secure survival with a 2–1 win at [[Wigan Athletic F.C.|Wigan Athletic]] to send West Brom and [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]] down.

==Kit==
Portsmouth signed a deal with [[Jako]] to produce the club's kit. [[Oki Electric Industry|Oki]] became the kit sponsors. The strip was manufactured by Pompey Sport, but after the kit deal with Jako, they were rebadged with its logo. The home shirt was atypical for Portsmouth, as it had a large white space under the collar on the rear where players' names were printed. The away was red and gold, whilst the third was white and navy. The white strip was only worn three times, away at [[Manchester United]], [[Aston Villa]] and [[West Ham United]] respectively.
