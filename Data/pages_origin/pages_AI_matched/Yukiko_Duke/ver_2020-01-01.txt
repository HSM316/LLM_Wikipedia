'''Yukiko Anette Torun Maria Duke Bergman''', (born 19 January 1966) is a [[Swedes|Swedish]] translator, journalist, editor and presenter.<ref name="stockholm.se">{{cite web|url=https://biblioteket.stockholm.se/kalender/yukiko-duke-om-l%C3%A4sandet-som-last-lust-och-arbetsliv|title=Yukiko Duke om läsandet som last, lust och (arbets)liv|publisher=|accessdate=10 March 2015}}</ref> Duke is the daughter of Christer Duke and Eiko Duke-Soei, and she specializes on [[Japan]]ese and Asian society and culture in her work.<ref name="stockholm.se"/> She has during several years been a journalist and news reporter for Japanese television, and in later years she has presented the shows ''Kvällsöppet'' and ''Röda rummet'' which are broadcast on [[SVT]].<ref name="stockholm.se"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.svt.se/nyheter/varlden/japansk-mentalitet-beharskning-ar-en-dygd|title=Japansk mentalitet: "Behärskning är en dygd"|work=svt.se|accessdate=10 March 2015}}</ref> She is also a literature reviewer for the SVT morning show Gomorron Sverige since 1993.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.svt.se/gomorron-sverige/recensioner/ |title=Recensioner - SVT.se |accessdate=10 March 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150228103957/http://www.svt.se/gomorron-sverige/recensioner/ |archivedate=February 28, 2015}}</ref> She has worked as a columnist for the paper Tidningen Vi and Ica Kuriren for [[ICA AB|ICA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.norstedts.se/Forfattare/Alfabetiskt/D/Yukiko-Duke/|title=Yukiko Duke|accessdate=10 March 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110701044113/http://www.norstedts.se/forfattare/Alfabetiskt/D/Yukiko-Duke/|archivedate=1 July 2011}}</ref> In 2011 she became an editor for the literature paper Vi läser.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.journalisten.se/folk/?c=pa_nya_jobb|title=Nytt om folk - Journalisten|publisher=|accessdate=10 March 2015}}</ref>

Duke has translated several Japanese books into Swedish, amongst them [[Haruki Murakami]]'s novel ''[[Norwegian Wood (novel)|Norwegian Wood]]''. In 2001, her and Eiko Duke's book, ''Mikaku, den japanska kokboken'', was nominated for the Augustprize.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.augustpriset.se/%C3%A5rtal/2001|title=2001|publisher=|accessdate=10 March 2015}}</ref>
