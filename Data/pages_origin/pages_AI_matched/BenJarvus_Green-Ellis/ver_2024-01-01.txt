'''BenJarvus Jeremy Green-Ellis''' (born July 2, 1985) is an American former professional [[American football|football]] player who was a [[running back]] in the [[National Football League]] (NFL) for the [[New England Patriots]] and [[Cincinnati Bengals]]. He played [[college football]] for the [[Indiana Hoosiers football|Indiana Hoosiers]] and [[Ole Miss Rebels football|Ole Miss Rebels]]. Green-Ellis was signed by the Patriots as an [[undrafted free agent]] in 2008.

Because of his segmented name, Green-Ellis is nicknamed "'''the Law Firm'''". He never lost a [[fumble]] with the Patriots.<ref>{{Cite web |last=MacPherson |first=Brian |date=February 4, 2012 |title=After four years without fumbling, Green-Ellis has "ultimate" trust of Patriots coaches |url=http://blogs.providencejournal.com/sports/patriots/2012/02/after-four-years-without-fumbling-green-ellis-has-ultimate-trust.html |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130201054345/http://blogs.providencejournal.com/sports/patriots/2012/02/after-four-years-without-fumbling-green-ellis-has-ultimate-trust.html |archive-date=February 1, 2013 |access-date=August 21, 2012 |website=[[The Providence Journal]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Graziano |first=Dan |date=September 23, 2012 |title=Rapid Reaction: Bengals 38, Redskins 31 |url=https://www.espn.com/blog/nflnation/post/_/id/63750/rapid-reaction-bengals-38-redskins-31 |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref>

==Early years==
Green-Ellis was born in [[New Orleans, Louisiana]] to Latonia Green and [[Ronald "Slim" Williams|Ronald Ellis]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Shaughnessy |first=Dan |date=November 10, 2008 |title=Green-Ellis now the name of the game |url=http://www.boston.com/sports/football/patriots/articles/2008/11/10/green_ellis_now_the_name_of_the_game/?page=full |access-date=November 10, 2008 |website=Boston.com}}</ref> He attended [[St. Augustine High School (New Orleans)|St. Augustine High School]] in New Orleans, and was a student and a [[Letterman (sports)|letterman]] in [[High school football|football]] and [[Track and field|track]] for the Purple Knights athletic teams. He was named as an All-City selection and as an All-Metro selection, and the ''[[Clarion Herald]]'' and the ''[[New Orleans Times-Picayune]]'' named him as an All-District selection. Also a standout track athlete, he lettered twice in [[track & field]], where he was an All-District selection and an All-Region selection.

==College career==
Before transferring to Mississippi,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Wilson |first=Doug |date=January 27, 2005 |title=Green-Ellis leaving Indiana for Ole Miss |url=https://www.heraldtimesonline.com/story/news/2005/01/27/green-ellis-leaving-indiana-for-ole-miss/48303063/ |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=The Herald-Times |language=en-US}}</ref> Green-Ellis was a productive running back for [[Indiana Hoosiers football|Indiana University]] in the 2003 and 2004 seasons. Green-Ellis transferred when then-coach [[Gerry DiNardo]] was fired and replaced by [[Terry Hoeppner]] before the 2005 season. As a freshman in 2003, Green-Ellis had a breakout year with 938 yards and seven touchdowns on 225 carries.<ref>{{Cite web |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis 2003 Game Log |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/cfb/players/benjarvus-green-ellis-1/gamelog/2003 |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=College Football at Sports-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> As a sophomore in 2004, Green-Ellis was a part of a two running back rotation with [[Chris Taylor (American football)|Chris Taylor]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=2004 Indiana Hoosiers Stats |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/cfb/schools/indiana/2004.html |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=College Football at Sports-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> Still, he led the team with 794 yards rushing on 231 carries and scored five touchdowns.<ref>{{Cite web |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis 2004 Game Log |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/cfb/players/benjarvus-green-ellis-1/gamelog/2004 |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=College Football at Sports-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref>

At [[Ole Miss Rebels football|Ole Miss]], Green-Ellis was only the second running back in the school's history to record back-to-back 1,000-yard seasons.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Borges |first=Ron |date=September 7, 2011 |title=Not easy being BenJarvus Green-Ellis |url=https://www.bostonherald.com/2011/09/07/not-easy-being-benjarvus-green-ellis/ |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Boston Herald |language=en-US}}</ref> As a junior in 2006, Green-Ellis posted 1,000 yards and seven touchdowns on a school record of 234 carries.<ref>{{Cite web |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis 2006 Game Log |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/cfb/players/benjarvus-green-ellis-1/gamelog/2006 |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=College Football at Sports-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> During his senior season in 2007, he recorded 1,137 yards and six touchdowns.<ref>{{Cite web |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis 2007 Game Log |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/cfb/players/benjarvus-green-ellis-1/gamelog/2007 |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=College Football at Sports-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> He was named as an All-[[Southeastern Conference|SEC]] first-team selection by the [[Associated Press]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=December 5, 2006 |title=2006 Associated Press All-SEC football team|agency=Associated Press |url=https://www.espn.com/espn/wire?section=ncf&id=2686391 |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref>

===New England Patriots===

====2008 season====
Green-Ellis was signed by the Patriots as an undrafted free agent on May 1, 2008.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Dunne |first=Tyler |date=January 30, 2012 |title=Super Bowl's undrafted players no longer overlooked |url=http://www.jsonline.com/sports/packers/doing-it-their-way-8m40eaj-138373269.html |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=JSOnline.com |language=en}}</ref> He was waived by the team on August 30, 2008, and signed to the team's practice squad on September 1, 2008.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Battista |first=Judy |date=November 11, 2008 |title=Patriots Lose Stars but Get Job Done |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/11/11/sports/football/11patriots.html |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=New York Times |language=en}}</ref> He was promoted to the active roster on October 11.<ref>{{Cite web |date=October 11, 2008 |title=Patriots sign RB BenJarvus Green-Ellis to active roster |url=https://www.patriots.com/news/patriots-sign-rb-benjarvus-green-ellis-to-active-roster-154651 |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Patriots.com |language=en-US}}</ref>

Green-Ellis made his [[National Football League|NFL]] debut on October 12, 2008, recording one catch for nine yards against the [[2008 San Diego Chargers season|San Diego Chargers]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=New England Patriots at San Diego Chargers - October 12th, 2008 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/200810120sdg.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> October 20, 2008, the same day that the Patriots placed fellow running back [[Laurence Maroney]] on injured reserve, Green-Ellis had his first NFL start in the Patriots' [[2008 New England Patriots season#Week 7: vs. Denver Broncos|41–7 victory]] against the [[2008 Denver Broncos season|Denver Broncos]] on [[Monday Night Football]]. Green-Ellis had 65 yards on 13 rushes in the second half, and scored his first touchdown on a 1-yard run, entering the end zone untouched.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Denver Broncos at New England Patriots - October 20th, 2008 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/200810200nwe.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.projo.com/patriots/content/projo_20081021_green_ellis.133e7edf2.html|title=Green-Ellis has a productive debut in Patriots backfield|work=[[The Providence Journal]]|first=Robert|last=Lee|date=October 21, 2008|access-date=November 17, 2008| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20081024080546/http://www.projo.com/patriots/content/projo_20081021_green_ellis.133e7edf2.html| archive-date= October 24, 2008 | url-status= live}}</ref> During [[NBC Sunday Night Football|Sunday Night Football]]'s November 2, 2008, broadcast, [[Al Michaels]] and [[John Madden]] noted that his Patriots teammates had nicknamed Green-Ellis "Law Firm," on account of his multi-part name.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Powell |first=Brian |date=November 3, 2008 |title=SNF Live-Blog: Patriots @ Colts, 1st Half |url=https://awfulannouncing.com/2008-articles/snf-live-blog-patriots-colts-1st-half.html |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Awful Announcing |language=en-US}}</ref>
[[File:BenJarvus-Green-Ellis 8-28-09 Patriots-vs-Redskins.jpg|thumb|left|"The Law Firm" in his second season with the Patriots]]
In Week 10, against the [[2008 Buffalo Bills season|Buffalo Bills]], Green-Ellis had his first 100-yard game in the NFL (with 105 yards), and scored a touchdown for the fourth consecutive game, giving him a touchdown in every NFL game he had started at the time.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Buffalo Bills at New England Patriots - November 9th, 2008 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/200811090nwe.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis 2008 Game Log |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/players/G/GreeBe00/gamelog/2008/ |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> The one-yard touchdown run capped a 92-yard drive whose 19 plays tied a franchise record for most plays on one drive.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.patriots.com/news/index.cfm?ac=latestnewsdetail&pid=35042&pcid=47|title=Patriots vs. Bills game notes|work=[[New England Patriots]]|date=November 9, 2008|access-date=August 10, 2009}}{{dead link|date=July 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

====2009 season====
Green-Ellis played in 12 games in the 2009 season. He had 26 carries for 114 rushing yards.<ref>{{Cite web |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis 2009 Game Log |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/players/G/GreeBe00/gamelog/2009/ |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref>

====2010 season====

Following the team's trade of [[Laurence Maroney]] and early-season injuries to [[Fred Taylor (American football)|Fred Taylor]] and [[Kevin Faulk]],<ref>{{Cite web |last=Reiss |first=Mike |date=September 14, 2010 |title=Patriots trade running back Maroney to Denver |url=https://www.espn.com/nfl/news/story?id=5572724 |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=September 29, 2010 |title=Patriots Getting Thin at Running Back As Fred Taylor Misses Practice With Toe Injury |url=https://nesn.com/2010/09/patriots-getting-thin-at-running-back-as-fred-taylor-misses-practice-with-toe-injury/ |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=NESN.com |language=en-US}}</ref> Green-Ellis saw an increased role in the Patriots offense. In Week 3, he rushed for 98 yards on 16 carries (6.1 yard average) and a touchdown in a win over the [[2010 Buffalo Bills season|Buffalo Bills]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Buffalo Bills at New England Patriots - September 26th, 2010 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201009260nwe.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> He made his first start of the season the next week, rushing for 76 yards on 16 carries and adding another touchdown against the [[2010 Miami Dolphins season|Miami Dolphins]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=New England Patriots at Miami Dolphins - October 4th, 2010 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201010040mia.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> In Week 8, against the [[2010 Minnesota Vikings season|Minnesota Vikings]], he had 17 carries for 112 yards and two touchdowns in the 28–18 victory.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Minnesota Vikings at New England Patriots - October 31st, 2010 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201010310nwe.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> On [[NFL on Thanksgiving Day|Thanksgiving]], Week 12, against the [[2010 Detroit Lions season|Detroit Lions]], Green-Ellis ran for two touchdowns in a Patriots win.<ref>{{Cite web |title=New England Patriots at Detroit Lions - November 25th, 2010 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201011250det.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> He added another two touchdowns in a 45–3 Patriots win over the [[2010 New York Jets season|New York Jets]] on ''[[Monday Night Football]]'' in Week 13.<ref>{{Cite web |title=New York Jets at New England Patriots - December 6th, 2010 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201012060nwe.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> In Week 17 against the Dolphins, Green-Ellis eclipsed the 1,000-yard rushing plateau on the season, becoming the first Patriots running back to rush for 1,000 yards since [[Corey Dillon]] in [[2004 New England Patriots season|2004]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=New England Patriots Single-Season Rushing Leaders |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/teams/nwe/single-season-rushing.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref>

Green-Ellis finished the 2010 season with 1,008 rushing yards on 229 carries (4.4 yard average), and 13 rushing touchdowns.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/players/G/GreeBe00/gamelog/2010/ |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis 2010 Game Log |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en |access-date=December 7, 2017}}</ref>

====2011 season====
During the 2011 season, Green-Ellis split playing time with several other running backs, primarily [[Danny Woodhead]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=2011 New England Patriots Rosters, Stats, Schedule, Team Draftees, Injury Reports |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/teams/nwe/2011.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> In Week 5, a 30–21 victory over the [[2011 New York Jets season|New York Jets]], he had 27 carries for 136 yards and two touchdowns.<ref>{{Cite web |title=New York Jets at New England Patriots – October 9th, 2011 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201110090nwe.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> At the end of the 2011 season, Green-Ellis and the Patriots appeared in [[Super Bowl XLVI]]. He started the game and had ten carries for 44 yards and two receptions for 15 yards, but the Patriots lost 21–17 to the [[2011 New York Giants season|New York Giants]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201202050nwe.htm |title=Super Bowl XLVI – New York Giants vs. New England Patriots – February 5th, 2012 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en |access-date=December 6, 2017}}</ref> He rushed for 667 yards from 181 attempts with 11 touchdowns for the AFC Champion Patriots, with a yards per carry average of 3.7. He also had nine receptions for 159 yards.<ref>{{Cite web |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis 2011 Game Log |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/players/G/GreeBe00/gamelog/2011/ |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref>

===Cincinnati Bengals===
On March 21, 2012, Green-Ellis signed a three-year contract with the [[Cincinnati Bengals]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=March 22, 2012 |title=Green-Ellis signs three-year deal with Bengals|agency=Associated Press |url=https://www.espn.com/nfl/story/_/id/7723790/2012-nfl-free-agency-benjarvus-green-ellis-signs-three-year-deal-cincinnati-bengals |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=ESPN.com |language=en}}</ref> On September 23, 2012, in a game against [[2012 Washington Redskins season|Washington]], Green-Ellis recorded his first career fumble, ending an impressive record of 589 touches without a fumble.<ref>{{Cite web |date=September 27, 2012 |title=Green-Ellis hopes his first career fumble was his last |url=https://www.nbcsports.com/nfl/profootballtalk/rumor-mill/news/green-ellis-hopes-his-first-career-fumble-was-his-last |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=NBC Sports |language=en-US}}</ref> In the following game, a 27–10 win against the [[2012 Jacksonville Jaguars season|Jacksonville Jaguars]], Green-Ellis fumbled two more times, but did not have another fumble the rest of the regular season.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Cincinnati Bengals at Jacksonville Jaguars – September 30th, 2012 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201209300jax.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> He had four games going over 100 rushing yards in a five-game span from Weeks 11–15. Despite playing in only fifteen games, he gained 1,000 rushing yards in a season for the second time in his career.<ref>{{Cite web |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis 2012 Game Log |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/players/G/GreeBe00/gamelog/2012/ |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=[[Pro-Football-Reference.com]]}}</ref>

Green-Ellis was paired with rookie [[Giovani Bernard]] and as a result of Bernard's effectiveness both rushing and receiving, his snaps declined in the 2013 season.<ref>{{Cite web |title=2013 Cincinnati Bengals Rosters, Stats, Schedule, Team Draftees, Injury Reports |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/teams/cin/2013.htm |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> In the 2013 season, Green-Ellis had 220 carries for 756 rushing yards and seven rushing touchdowns.<ref>{{Cite web |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis 2013 Game Log |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/players/G/GreeBe00/gamelog/2013/ |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> On August 29, 2014, he was released by the Bengals, who added [[Jeremy Hill]] in the draft.<ref>{{cite web|work=ESPN.com|url=http://espn.go.com/nfl/story/_/id/11431868/rb-benjarvus-green-ellis-cut-cincinnati-bengals|title=Bengals cut BenJarvus Green-Ellis|date=August 29, 2014|first=Coley|last=Harvey|access-date=May 1, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Alper |first=Josh |date=August 13, 2015 |title=BenJarvus Green-Ellis says "phone's been ringing," looking for right team |url=https://www.nbcsports.com/nfl/profootballtalk/rumor-mill/news/benjarvus-green-ellis-says-phones-been-ringing-looking-for-right-team |access-date=August 8, 2023 |website=NBC Sports |language=en-US}}</ref>
