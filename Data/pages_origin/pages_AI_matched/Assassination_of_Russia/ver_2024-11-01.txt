'''''Assassination of Russia''''' ({{lang-ru|Покушение на Россию}}) is a 2002 documentary film that describes the September 1999 [[Russian apartment bombings]] as a terrorism act committed by [[Federal Security Service (Russia)|Russian state security services]].<ref name="dissident">[[Alexander Goldfarb (author)|Alex Goldfarb]] and Marina Litvinenko. "[[Death of a Dissident|Death of a Dissident: The Poisoning of Alexander Litvinenko and the Return of the KGB.]]" Free Press, New York, 2007. {{ISBN|978-1-4165-5165-2}}, pages 249-252</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kommersant.com/p313379/r_1/Boris_Berezovsky_organized_Assassination_of_Russia/|title=Boris Berezovsky organized "Assassination of Russia"|website=www.kommersant.com|access-date=2010-02-20|archive-date=2016-03-24|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160324163032/http://kommersant.com/p313379/r_1/Boris_Berezovsky_organized_Assassination_of_Russia/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The film was created in 2001 by French producers [[:fr:Jean-Charles Deniau|Jean-Charles Deniau]] and Charles Gazelle. [[Yuri Felshtinsky]] and [[Alexander Litvinenko]] worked as consultants for the film; the film was made on the basis of their book ''[[Blowing Up Russia: Terror from Within]]''.

The French producers initially worked for Russian TV company [[NTV (Russia)|NTV]] to expand their program "Sugar of Ryazan" and later to [[TV-6 (Russia)|TV-6]]. When TV-6 channel was closed by Russian authorities, the film was 70% completed. The remaining funds were provided by [[Boris Berezovsky (businessman)|Boris Berezovsky]].<ref name="dissident"/><ref name="David Levy">{{Cite journal |title=A chapter from David Levy's work in progress "From Chaguanas to Jerusalem: Tribal Conflict in the Global Mosaic" |first=David |last= Levy |journal=The Montréal Review |date=April 2012 |url=http://www.themontrealreview.com/2009/Post-Soviet-Russia-Chechens-KGB-Khodorkovsky.php }}</ref>

Leader of party ''Liberal Russia'', [[Sergei Yushenkov]] flew to the premiere on March 5, 2002, in London, to announce that his party was going to distribute copies of the film around Russia to demonstrate "how the secret services deceived Russian citizens".

This film was broadcast by the main TV channels of [[Estonia]], [[Latvia]], and [[Lithuania]].<ref name="dissident"/>  Russian [[State Duma]] Deputy [[Yuli Rybakov]] brought a hundred copies to St. Petersburg, but they were confiscated at customs in violation of his [[parliamentary immunity]]. No TV station in Russia was able to show the film. Nevertheless, tens of thousands of copies were sold in Russia in 2002.<ref name="dissident"/> Sergei Yushenkov presented the film at the [[Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars]] in 2002, decrying lack of civilian control over the Russian armed forces including the secret services.<ref>[https://www.wilsoncenter.org/event/assassination-russia-film-screening-and-panel-discussion "Assassination of Russia"- Film Screening and Panel Discussion], [[Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars]], April 24, 2002</ref> A staffer for the [[U.S. Senate Foreign Relations Committee]] said, "We just cannot go out and say that the president of Russia is a mass murderer. But it is important that we know it."<ref name="dissident"/>
