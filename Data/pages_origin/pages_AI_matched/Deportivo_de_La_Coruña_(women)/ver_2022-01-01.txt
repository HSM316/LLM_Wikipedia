'''Real Club Deportivo de La Coruña Femenino''' is the [[Women's association football|women's]] [[Association football|football]] section of [[Deportivo de La Coruña]], club based in the city of [[A Coruña]] ([[Galicia (Spain)|Galicia]], Spain), that currently plays in [[Segunda División Pro (women)|Segunda División Pro]]. Currently also receives the name of '''Deportivo ABANCA''' for sponsorship reasons.

==History==
===Karbo Deportivo (1983–1988)===
[[File:Karbo Deportivo A Coruña.svg|thumb|100px|Logo of former Karbo CF before becoming Karbo Deportivo]]
Deportivo entered in the women's football in the winter of 1983-84 after absorbing ''Karbo C.F.'' that changed its name to '''Karbo Deportivo'''<ref>{{Cite journal |newspaper=[[La Voz de Galicia]] |publication-place=A Coruña |date=26 November 1983 |page=47 |title=Deportes. Fútbol femenino |trans-title=Sports. Women's football |language=es }}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal |newspaper=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]] |publication-place=Madrid |date=5 January 1984 |page=10 |title=El Coruña ha absorbido al club Karbo |trans-title=Coruña has absorbed Karbo |language=es }}</ref> completely integrated into the structure of [[Deportivo de La Coruña]], also using the colors and shield of [[Deportivo de La Coruña|Deportivo]]. The team won the first official women's football competitions in Spain (the current [[Copa de la Reina de Fútbol|Copa de la Reina]], called ''Spanish Championship'' before the foundation of the [[Primera División (women)|women's football league]]) until 1985.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rcdeportivo.es/noticia/la-xunta-apoya-al-olivo-de-vigo-de-futbol-femenino-omite-al-depor-y-se-olvida-del-karbo-deportivo|publisher=Deportivo La Coruña|language=es|title=La Xunta apoya al Olivo de Vigo de fútbol femenino, omite al Dépor y se olvida del Karbo Deportivo|date=19 May 2011|access-date=27 June 2017}}</ref>

The section was dissolved in 1988 due to the economic problems that the R.C. Deportivo, plunged into a suffocating debt and with the men's team on the verge of relegation to [[Segunda División B]], as well as an increase in expenses for the increasing professionalization of women's football, a year before [[Primera División (women)|Superliga]] was created.

===2016–present: recovery of the women's section===
On 16 March 2016, Deportivo announced the recovery of the women's club section.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rcdeportivo.es/en/en/asnosas|title=#ASNOSAS &#124; Página Oficial del R.C. Deportivo de La Coruña|website=#ASNOSAS &#124; Página Oficial del R.C. Deportivo de La Coruña}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.marca.com/futbol/futbol-femenino/2016/03/16/56e94e6fca4741c1418b45f4.html|work=[[Marca (newspaper)|Marca]]|language=es|title=El Deportivo de la Coruña femenino ya es una realidad|date=16 March 2016|access-date=27 June 2017}}</ref> The new club started playing its first season in [[Segunda División (women)|Segunda División]], after an agreement with local team Orzán SD Deportivo to occupy his place forming a stronger Galician team, and in its debut as ''Deportivo Femenino'' ended as runner-up of the Group 1. In the 2017/18 season the team was renamed ''Deportivo ABANCA'' after an agreement with the bank for 4 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.laopinioncoruna.es/deportivo/2016/12/29/equipo-femenino-deportivo-pasara-llamarse/1138717.html|title=Deportivo Abanca, nuevo nombre para las blanquiazules|first=La Opinión de A.|last=Coruña|website=www.laopinioncoruna.es}}</ref>

After three years, on 19 May 2019, Deportivo achieved promotion to [[Primera División (women)|Primera División]] for the first time ever.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.laliga.es/noticias/el-rc-deportivo-abanca-nuevo-equipo-de-la-liga-iberdrola|publisher=La Liga|language=es|title=El RC Deportivo Abanca asciende a la Liga Iberdrola|date=19 May 2019}}</ref>

Their first season in the [[Primera División (women)|Primera División]] was cut short on 8 May 2020, due to the [[Royal Spanish Football Federation|RFEF]] choosing to suspend non-professional football during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. With this cancellation, they finished the season with an unprecedented fourth place.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rcdeportivo.es/noticia/fin-de-la-temporada-para-el-deportivo-abanca-depor-abanca-b-fabril-y-juvenil-a |title=Fin de la temporada para el Deportivo ABANCA, Dépor ABANCA B, Fabril y Juvenil A |website=rcdeportivo.es |publisher=Deportivo de La Coruña |date=8 May 2020 |access-date=10 May 2020}}</ref> In the 2021-22 season, Depor finished 15th was relegated to the [[Segunda División Pro (women)|Segunda División Pro]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://futbolfemenino.rfef.es/es/primera-iberdrola/clasificacion/|title=Clasificación Futbol femenino. Primera Iberdrola|website=futbolfemenino.rfef.es}}</ref>
