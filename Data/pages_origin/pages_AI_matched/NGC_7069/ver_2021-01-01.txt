'''NGC 7069''' is a [[Lenticular galaxy]] located about 400 million [[light-year]]s away in the [[constellation]] of [[Aquarius (constellation)|Aquarius]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dso-browser.com/deep-sky/8260/ngc-7069/galaxy|title=Galaxy NGC 7069 - Galaxy in Aquarius Constellation|archive-date=2017-06-10|access-date=2017-06-10}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://ned.ipac.caltech.edu/cgi-bin/objsearch?objname=NGC%207069&extend=no&hconst=73&omegam=0.27&omegav=0.73&corr_z=1&out_csys=Equatorial&out_equinox=J2000.0&obj_sort=RA%20or%20Longitude&of=pre_text&zv_breaker=30000.0&list_limit=5&img_stamp=YES|title=Your NED Search Results|website=ned.ipac.caltech.edu|access-date=2017-07-10}}</ref> NGC 7069 is also classified as a [[Low-ionization nuclear emission-line region|LINER]] galaxy.<ref>{{Cite simbad|title=NGC 7069|access-date=2017-07-10}}</ref> NGC 7069 was discovered by astronomer [[Albert Marth]] on October 12, 1863.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://cseligman.com/text/atlas/ngc70a.htm#7051|title=New General Catalog Objects: NGC 7050 - 7099|website=cseligman.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-07-01}}</ref>
