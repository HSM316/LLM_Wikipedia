'''SN 1917A''' is a supernova event in the [[Fireworks Galaxy]] (NGC 6946),<ref name=seds /> positioned {{val|37|ul=arcsecond}} west and {{val|105|u=arcsecond}} south of the galactic core.<ref name=Barbon_et_al_2008/> Discovered by American optician [[George Willis Ritchey]] on 19 July 1917, it reached a peak [[visual magnitude]] of 13.6.<ref name=Barbon_et_al_2008/> Based on a poor quality<ref name=Baade1938/> 
photographic spectrum taken at least a month after peak light by [[Francis G. Pease|F. G. Pease]] and Ritchey, it was identified as a [[type II supernova|type II]] core-collapse supernova.<ref name=Fesen_Weil_2020/><ref name=Schlegel1994/>

A 2018 analysis of the surrounding stellar population by B. F. Williams suggests the progenitor star was most likely 13{{±|13|1}}&nbsp;million years old with 15{{±|1|5}} times the [[mass of the Sun]] ({{solar mass}}).<ref name=Williams_et_al_2018/> B. Koplitz and associates in 2021 inferred a progenitor mass estimate of {{val|9.3|16.7|0.7|u=Solar mass}}.<ref name=Koplitz_et_al_2021/> A 2020 search for light echoes from the supernova was unsuccessful.<ref name=Radica_et_al_2020/>
