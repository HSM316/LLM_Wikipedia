'''Lisa McGee''' (born {{Birth based on age as of date|38|2018|11|19|noage=1}}<ref name="bbc"/>) is a [[People of Northern Ireland|Northern Irish]] playwright and screenwriter. McGee is the creator and writer of ''[[Derry Girls]]'', a comedy series that began airing on Channel 4 in the UK in January 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.channel4.com/info/press/news/casting-announced-for-new-channel-4-comedy-derry-girls|title=Casting announced for new Channel 4 comedy Derry Girls  - Channel 4 - Info - Press|website=www.channel4.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.radiotimes.com/news/tv/2019-04-09/derry-girls-writer-lisa-mcgee-series-3-james-erin/|title=Derry Girls writer Lisa McGee breaks down the series 2 finale, James and Erin's budding romance and what might happen next|last=Allen|first=Ben|date=9 April 2019|website=Radio Times|accessdate=10 April 2019}}</ref> In 2018, she was listed as one of [[100 Women (BBC)|BBC's 100 Women]].<ref name="bbc">{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-46225037|title=BBC 100 Women 2018: Who is on the list?|date=19 November 2018|work=BBC News|access-date=21 November 2018|language=en-GB}}</ref>

==Biography==
McGee was born in [[Derry]], [[Northern Ireland]], and studied Drama at [[The Queen's University of Belfast]].<ref name='BelfastTelegraph Dec17'>{{Cite web|date=20 December 2017|title=Derry Girls could become TV hit - if viewers can understand them|url=https://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/entertainment/news/derry-girls-could-become-tv-hit-if-viewers-can-understand-them-36423218.html|website=Belfast Telegraph Digital|accessdate=6 April 2018}}</ref> She was writer on attachment with the [[Royal National Theatre]] in London in 2006. Her plays include ''Jump'', ''The Heights'', ''Nineteen Ninety Two'', and ''Girls and Dolls'', for which she won the Stewart Parker Trust New Playwright Bursary 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nickhernbooks.co.uk/girls-and-dolls|title=Nick Hern Books - Girls and Dolls By Lisa McGee|website=Nick Hern Books}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tinderbox.org.uk/about-us/major-achievements/|title=Major Achievements - Tinderbox Theatre|publisher=|access-date=4 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180105180338/http://www.tinderbox.org.uk/about-us/major-achievements/|archive-date=5 January 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

McGee's television credits include ''The Things I Haven't Told You'' for [[BBC Three]],<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=65luFLpUJCg</ref> the Irish television series ''[[Raw (TV series)|Raw]]'' which she created for [[RTÃ‰]],<ref>https://web.archive.org/web/20090116071432/http://www.rte.ie/tv/raw/</ref> time as a writer for three series of the [[BAFTA]]-nominated [[Being Human (UK TV series)|''Being Human'']] for the [[BBC]], the [[Channel 4]] sitcom [[London Irish (TV series)|''London Irish'']], which she created, writing for the [[Golden Globe]]-nominated drama series ''[[The White Queen (miniseries)|The White Queen]]'' for [[BBC 1]], and ''[[Indian Summers]]'' for Channel 4. Her stage play ''Jump'' has been adapted into a [[Jump (2012 film)|film]].

==Personal life==
She is married to actor [[Tobias Beer]].<ref name='BelfastTelegraph Dec17' />
