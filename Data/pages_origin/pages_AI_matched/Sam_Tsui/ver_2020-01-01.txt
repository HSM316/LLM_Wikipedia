'''Samuel Tsui''' (born May 2, 1989<ref name="facebook" />) is an American singer/songwriter, video producer and actor. He rose to fame as an [[List of YouTube personalities|Internet celebrity]] and is known for covering songs by [[Pop music|popular]] artists like [[Adele]], [[Britney Spears]], [[Lady Gaga]], [[Taylor Swift]], [[Justin Timberlake]], [[Bruno Mars]] and lot more, as well as some original songs, [[List of musical medleys|medleys]], and [[Mashup (music)|mashups]], along with his frequent collaborations with fellow [[YouTuber]] [[Kurt Hugo Schneider]]. As of August 25, 2018, Tsui had reached 3 million subscribers.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.youtube.com/user/TheSamTsui|title = Sam Tsui on YouTube (TheSamTsui)}}</ref>

==Life and career==

===1989–2008: Early life===
Sam Tsui was born on May 2, 1989.<ref name="[[Last.fm]]">{{Cite web|url = http://www.last.fm/music/Sam+Tsui|title = Sam Tsui on last.fm}}</ref> His father is of [[Cantonese people|Cantonese]] descent and his mother is of European descent and hails from [[Iowa]].<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://newmediarockstars.com/2012/11/sam-tsui-singer/2/|title = <nowiki>Sam Tsui | Singer & Songwriter (Interview)</nowiki>|date = 2012-11-13|accessdate = 2014-11-11|website = New Media Rockstars|last = Van|first = Alan}}</ref> He can speak some Cantonese.<ref>{{Cite web|url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Ozs385T6h0|title = At 9:15, Sam Tsui busts out his Cantonese|date=2014-07-04|publisher=[[Young Post]]|accessdate=2015-01-08}}</ref> Tsui has a brother, Kent Kevin. Tsui grew up in [[Blue Bell, Pennsylvania]], a street away from [[Kurt Hugo Schneider|Kurt Schneider]], his producer and accompanist, with whom he attended [[Wissahickon High School]].<ref name="duke">{{cite web|url=http://www.yale.edu/dukesmen/rosterpages/rostersamt.html|title=The Duke's Men of Yale: Sam Tsui|publisher=[[Yale University]]|accessdate=2014-11-11|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20131227002716/http://www.yale.edu/dukesmen/rosterpages/rostersamt.html|archivedate = 2013-12-27|url-status = dead}}</ref><ref name="ABS">{{cite web|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/09/12/10/sam-tsuis-one-man-choir-hits-youtube|title=Sam Tsui's one-man choir hits YouTube|date=2010-09-12|publisher=[[ABS-CBN News and Current Affairs]]|accessdate=2010-09-14}}</ref><ref name="shkboom">{{cite web|url=http://www.sh-k-boom.com/thecovers.shtml|title=Sam Tsui & Kurt Schneider: The Covers|publisher=[[Sh-K-Boom Records]]|accessdate=2009-09-14|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100217041831/http://www.sh-k-boom.com/thecovers.shtml|archivedate=2010-02-17}}</ref> He was active in high school musicals, including ''[[Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat]]'', ''[[Urinetown]]'', ''[[Titanic (musical)|Titanic]]'', and ''[[Miss Saigon]]''. Tsui attended [[Yale University]], where he was a member of [[Davenport College]] and [[The Duke's Men of Yale]], an all-male [[a cappella|acappella]] group. He also majored in classical Greek at Yale University.

===Trinity Church Southport===
While studying at Yale University, Tsui continued to develop his skills as a paid soloist at Trinity Church Southport, an Episcopal church. In a recent interview with Southport-native Quinn Barry, Tsui stated that then-choirmaster Alan Murchie, well-renowned within Christian circles for his stellar work in the arts, taught him how to love music and inspired him to follow his dreams.{{citation needed|reason=Sounds great! Proof please?|date=September 2015}}

===2009–2010: Career Beginnings===
Tsui appeared on ''The [[ABC World News Tonight|ABC World News]]'' on October 28, 2009, singing [[The Jackson 5]]'s "[[I'll Be There (The Jackson 5 song)|I'll Be There]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2009/oct/28/tsui-eet-sounds-on-abcs-world-news-tonight/|title=Tsui-eet sounds on ABC's World News Tonight|date=2009-10-28|work=[[Yale Daily News]]|publisher=The Yale Daily News Publishing Company|accessdate=2010-09-14|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101127160332/http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2009/oct/28/tsui-eet-sounds-on-abcs-world-news-tonight/|archivedate=2010-11-27}}</ref> Tsui also went on to perform on ''[[The Bonnie Hunt Show]]'' in November of the same year, where he was interviewed and performed [[Whitney Houston]]'s "[[I Wanna Dance with Somebody (Who Loves Me)]]". He also appeared on ''[[It's On with Alexa Chung]]'', singing [[Journey (band)|Journey]]'s "[[Don't Stop Believin']]" for the cast of American television series ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2009/nov/12/cross-campus-111209/|title=Cross Campus: 11.12.09|date=2009-11-12|work=[[Yale Daily News]]|publisher=The Yale Daily News Publishing Company|accessdate=2010-09-14|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120924063330/http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2009/nov/12/cross-campus-111209/|archivedate=2012-09-24}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://yaleherald.com/thebullblog/sam-tsui-upstages-the-entire-cast-of-glee/|title=Sam Tsui upstages the entire cast of Glee|author=Joe|date=2009-11-11|work=[[The Yale Herald]]|publisher=[[Yale University]]|accessdate=2010-09-14|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100913231644/http://yaleherald.com/thebullblog/sam-tsui-upstages-the-entire-cast-of-glee/|archivedate=2010-09-13}}</ref>

Tsui also worked on his own project in 2009, where he and [[Kurt Hugo Schneider|Kurt Schneider]] produced a series of episodes of ''College Musical'', a comedy web series parodying the [[High School Musical (film series)|''High School Musical'' film series]], with original songs composed by Schneider.<ref name="redefines" /> Four episodes were released, with Tsui playing the lead role of Cooper. Following the popularity of the series, it was announced that a film (released on September 3, 2014, 8pm Pacific Time), would be made based on the series.<ref name="redefines" /><ref name="Channel" /> The episodes garnered millions of views on YouTube.

In 2010, Tsui released his first cover album, ''The Covers''. The album, released on February 9, 2010, features covers of hits from Michael Jackson, Journey, Beyoncé, Jason Mraz, and Lady Gaga among other artists. On February 22, 2010, Tsui appeared on ''[[The Oprah Winfrey Show]]'' with his producer, Kurt Schneider.<ref name="Oprah" /> The two were interviewed, and Tsui performed his medley of Michael Jackson songs. On December 2, 2010, Tsui gave an interview on ''[[The Ellen DeGeneres Show]]'' and performed a mashup of [[Katy Perry]]'s "[[Firework (song)|Firework]]" and [[Bruno Mars]]' "[[Grenade (song)|Grenade]]" with Schneider.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2010/dec/02/tsui-11-and-schneider-10-ellen/|title=Tsui '11 and Schneider '10 on 'Ellen'|date=2010-12-02|first=Esther|last=Zuckerman|work=[[Yale Daily News]]|publisher=The Yale Daily News Publishing Company|accessdate=2010-12-02|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110222145129/http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2010/dec/02/tsui-11-and-schneider-10-ellen/|archivedate=2011-02-22}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://ellen.warnerbros.com/2010/12/youtube_sensation_sam_tsui_performs_1202.php |title=YouTube Sensation Sam Tsui Performs! |date=December 2, 2010 |publisher=Ellen.WarnerBros.com. [[Warner Bros.]] |accessdate=2010-12-02 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101206031602/http://ellen.warnerbros.com/2010/12/youtube_sensation_sam_tsui_performs_1202.php |archivedate=December 6, 2010 }}</ref> In December 2010, Tsui collaborated with fellow YouTube singer [[Christina Grimmie]], singing "[[Just a Dream (Nelly song)|Just a Dream]]", which is the most viewed video on the [[YouTube]] channel "KurtHugoSchneider", with more than 100 million views.<ref name="Channel" />

===2011–2012: YouTube and rise in popularity===
In 2011, Tsui debuted his own YouTube channel, apart from his collaboration with Kurt Schneider. On February 3, 2011, Tsui was featured on [[Britney Spears]]' official website, along with his cover of "[[Hold It Against Me]]".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.twitter.com/SamuelTsui/statuses/33295678578892800|title=My "Hold it Against Me" cover was just posted on @britneyspears official website!! Ahhh! :-D|publisher=[[Twitter]] |date=2011-02-04 |accessdate=2011-02-06}}</ref> In March 2011, Tsui, and like from his song "Start Again", were also mentioned in the novel ''[[Seeker (Andy Frankham-Allen novel)|Seeker]]'' by Andy Frankham-Allen published in print format by Hirst Publishing, and in all digital formats by Untreed Reads Publishing.<ref>{{cite web|title=Interview with Andy Frankham-Allen|url=http://accidental-author.blogspot.com/2011/03/process-interview-1-untreed-reads.html|website = The Accidental Author|date = 2011-03-26|accessdate = 2014-11-11}}</ref> In November 2011, he made a mashup with "[[It Will Rain]]" by [[Bruno Mars]], and "[[Set Fire to the Rain]]" by [[Adele]].

In 2011, Tsui was a behind-the-scenes host for the third season of NBC's a capella competition show, ''[[The Sing-Off]]''. He recorded a "Judges Medley" that included Sara Bareilles' "Love Song", Ben Folds' "Gone" and Shawn Stockman's (Boyz II Men) "On Bended Knee". The video is free to view on both NBC's website and Tsui's independent YouTube channel, TheSamTsui.

Tsui graduated from Yale ''[[Latin honours|magna cum laude]]'' with a major in [[classical Greece]] in 2011. Tsui is proficient in the [[C++]] programming language.<ref name="duke" /><ref name="redefines">{{cite web|url=http://yaleherald.com/featured/kurt-schneider-redefines-formula-for-youtube-success/|title=Kurt Schneider re(de)fines formula for YouTube success|date=2010-01-29|work=[[The Yale Herald]]|publisher=[[Yale University]]|accessdate=2010-09-14|last=Yager|first=Sarah|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100905153053/http://yaleherald.com/featured/kurt-schneider-redefines-formula-for-youtube-success/|archivedate=2010-09-05}}</ref><ref name="Oprah">{{cite web|url=http://www.oprah.com/oprahshow/Incredible-Feats/6|title=Incredible Feats|work=[[The Oprah Winfrey Show]]|publisher=[[Harpo Productions]]|accessdate=2010-09-14|date = 2010-02-22}}</ref> Tsui was mentioned three times by [[Tom Hanks]] when he addressed the class of Yale 2011.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tom Hanks Addresses Yale Class of 2011|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=baIlinqoExQ&feature=player_detailpage#t=127s}}</ref> Tsui was also featured in the 2010 Yale Admissions video. The video, entitled "That's Why I Chose Yale",<ref>{{cite web|title=Video Gives Musical Answer to the Question: ‘Why Choose Yale?’|url=http://news.yale.edu/2010/01/27/video-gives-musical-answer-question-why-choose-yale|website = news.yale.edu|publisher = [[Yale University]]|date = 2010-01-27|accessdate = 2014-11-11}}</ref> reveals information about the college with students and admission officers breaking out into song across the campus. In 2017, he co-starred in a new admissions video entitled "That's Why I Toured Yale".<ref>{{cite web|title=Admissions to debut new video: update on the ‘That’s Why I Chose Yale’ cast|url=https://news.yale.edu/2017/12/04/admissions-debut-new-video-update-thats-why-i-chose-yale-cast|website = news.yale.edu|publisher=[[Yale University]]|date=2017-12-04|accessdate = 2018-02-23}}</ref>

Tsui has collaborated with fellow musician [[Kurt Hugo Schneider]] since high school, when the pair recorded music with an [[electronic keyboard]] and [[GarageBand]]. Both attended Yale University, where a musical partnership developed. As of April 2017, the [[YouTube]] channel "KurtHugoSchneider" has garnered over 2.1&nbsp;billion total views and over 8&nbsp;million subscribers.<ref name="Channel">{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/KurtHugoSchneider|title=KurtHugoSchneider's Channel|last=Schneider|first=Kurt Hugo|publisher=[[YouTube]]|accessdate=2010-12-02}}</ref><ref>[https://www.youtube.com/user/KurtHugoSchneider?feature=watch Kurt Hugo Schneider]. YouTube. Retrieved on 2013-03-31.</ref> His videos have been described by ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' as a combination of ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]'' and ''[[Star Wars: Episode II – Attack of the Clones|Attack of the Clones]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,1938731,00.html|title=YouTube Effect: Making Money from Viral Videos|date=2009-11-23|first=Dan|last=Fletcher|work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|publisher=[[Time Inc]]|accessdate=2010-12-02}}</ref>

===2013–present: ''Make It Up'' and future projects===
Tsui released his first full-length original album in May 2013 titled ''Make It Up''. In summer 2013, Tsui and Schneider toured across Canada and the US with [[Alex Goot]], Against the Current, Landon Austin, Luke Conard, and King the Kid.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.gootmusic.com/tour |title=Tour Dates – A Summer with Alex Goot and Friends |date=2013-05-15 |accessdate=2014-11-11 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141105111450/http://www.gootmusic.com/tour/ |archivedate=November 5, 2014 }}</ref> In September 2013, Tsui and Schneider performed in Singapore and Hong Kong.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.kurthugoschneider.com/tour/ |title=KHS Tour |date=2013-09-26 |accessdate=2014-11-11 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141012032405/http://www.kurthugoschneider.com/tour/ |archivedate=October 12, 2014 }}</ref> Tsui has so far released music videos for "Make It Up", "Grey Area", "Shadow", "Bring Me the Night", "Worth It" , "Wherever You Are" and "Open Up The Sky".

Tsui continues to make covers of popular music on his YouTube channel, also doing collaborations with other YouTube singers. On September 9, 2013, Sam wrote and recorded a duet with Elle Winter entitled "Unsinkable (Music Is Medicine)". The proceeds of the single go towards [[Childhood cancer|pediatric cancer]] research as a part of the Music Is Medicine's Donate a Song project. On October 3, 2013, Sam uploaded a photo via [[Instagram]],<ref>{{Cite web |url= http://instagram.com/p/e-tbAjO6M5/ |title= Instagram (thesamtsui) |accessdate= 2014-11-11}}</ref> posing alongside [[Jason Mraz]] and [[Hunter Hayes]]. He announced that he shot a video with the two artists and would be releasing it soon. It was later revealed that he has collaborated with [[Tessa Violet]], [[Echosmith]], TJ Smith, [[Tyler Ward]], Brandyn Burnette, Brian Landau, [[Kina Grannis]] and [[Peter Hollens]] on a YouTube mash-up of their song, "[[Everybody's Got Somebody but Me]]".

Tsui's vocals were featured in a promotional video for [[Coca-Cola]] that premiered on January 14, 2014. In 2013, [[Kurt Hugo Schneider]] and Coca-Cola teamed up to create music videos featuring creative covers of two 2011 hits namely [[Calvin Harris]]' "[[Feel So Close]]" and [[Of Monsters and Men]]'s "[[Little Talks]]" for a campaign called "The Sounds of AHH". The commercial edits of both premiered on the inaugural episode of [[American Idol (season 13)|season 13]] of ''[[American Idol]]'' on January 14, 2014, on [[Fox Broadcasting Company|FOX]] with "Feel So Close" featuring the vocals of Tsui whereas Schneider makes music playing only Coca-Cola bottles, glasses and cans.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.coca-colacompany.com/stories/behind-the-bottles-5-questions-with-kurt-hugo-schneider|title=Behind the Bottles: 5 Questions With Kurt Hugo Schneider|work=The Coca-Cola Company}}</ref> Tsui shares in co-playing on the bottles alongside Schneider. In summer 2014, Tsui and Schneider returned to Asia, performing in Kuala Lumpur, Manila, Singapore, Hong Kong, and Taipei.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kurthugoschneider.com/tour/|title=Kurt Hugo Schneider – Tour|work=kurthugoschneider.com}}</ref> They were also scheduled to perform in Beijing and Shanghai, but those shows did not push through.

In 2016, Tsui starred in an episode of [[Bones (TV series)|''Bones'']] as collegiate acappella singing group member Jake Eisenberg.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.spoilertv.com/2016/04/bones-episode-1116-strike-in-chord.html|title=Bones - Episode 11.16 - The Strike in the Chord - Featurette, Promo, Sneak Peeks, Promotional Photos & Press Release *Updated*|date=May 19, 2016|accessdate=June 15, 2016|work=SpoilerTV}}</ref>
