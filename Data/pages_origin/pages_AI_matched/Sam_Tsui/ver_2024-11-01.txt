'''Samuel Tsui''' (born May 2, 1989<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Woooo! 21 Years old! Thanks everyone for your birthday wishes! :-)|url=https://twitter.com/SamuelTsui/status/13268316589|last=Tsui|first=Sam|date=2010-05-02|website=Twitter|language=en|access-date=2020-05-10}}</ref>) is an American singer, songwriter and video producer. He rose to fame as an [[List of YouTube personalities|internet celebrity]] known for doing [[Song cover|covers]] and musical medleys of songs by [[Pop music|pop]] artists.<ref name="redefines">{{cite web|title=Kurt Schneider re(de)fines formula for YouTube success|url=http://yaleherald.com/featured/kurt-schneider-redefines-formula-for-youtube-success/|last=Yager|first=Sarah|date=2010-01-29|work=[[The Yale Herald]]|publisher=[[Yale University]]|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100905153053/http://yaleherald.com/featured/kurt-schneider-redefines-formula-for-youtube-success/|archive-date=2010-09-05|access-date=2010-09-14}}</ref> He has since released original songs and expanded to [[Mashup (music)|mashups]].

Tsui frequently collaborates with [[YouTuber]] and childhood friend [[Kurt Hugo Schneider]]. Their one-man [[a cappella]] videos have been described by ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' as a combination of ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]'' and ''[[Star Wars: Episode II – Attack of the Clones|Attack of the Clones]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Fletcher|first=Dan|date=2009-11-23|title=YouTube Effect: Making Money from Viral Videos|magazine=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|publisher=[[Time Inc]]|url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,1938731,00.html|url-status=dead|access-date=2010-12-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091117090212/http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,1938731,00.html|archive-date=November 17, 2009}}</ref>

As of June, 2021, Tsui had received over 3.18 million subscribers.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sam Tsui on YouTube (TheSamTsui)|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/TheSamTsui|website=YouTube|access-date=May 11, 2020}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Tsui was born on May 2, 1989.<ref name=":0" /> His father was from [[British Hong Kong|Hong Kong]] and of Cantonese descent, while his mother is an American from Iowa.<ref>{{Cite web|title=<nowiki>Sam Tsui | Singer & Songwriter (Interview)</nowiki>|url=http://newmediarockstars.com/2012/11/sam-tsui-singer/2/|last=Van|first=Alan|date=2012-11-13|website=New Media Rockstars|access-date=2014-11-11}}</ref> He has one brother.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Aca-scuse me? Aca-Tsui!|url=https://yaledailynews.com/blog/2015/03/06/aca-scuse-me-aca-tsui/|date=March 6, 2015|website=Yale Daily news|language=en|access-date=2020-05-10|last3=2015}}</ref> He grew up in [[Blue Bell, Pennsylvania]], a street away from Kurt Schneider, his producer and accompanist, with whom he attended [[Wissahickon High School]].<ref name="duke">{{cite web|url=http://www.yale.edu/dukesmen/rosterpages/rostersamt.html|title=The Duke's Men of Yale: Sam Tsui|publisher=[[Yale University]]|access-date=2014-11-11|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20131227002716/http://www.yale.edu/dukesmen/rosterpages/rostersamt.html|archive-date = 2013-12-27|url-status = dead}}</ref><ref name="ABS">{{cite web|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/entertainment/09/12/10/sam-tsuis-one-man-choir-hits-youtube|title=Sam Tsui's one-man choir hits YouTube|date=2010-09-12|publisher=[[ABS-CBN News and Current Affairs]]|access-date=2010-09-14}}</ref><ref name="shkboom">{{cite web|url=http://www.sh-k-boom.com/thecovers.shtml|title=Sam Tsui & Kurt Schneider: The Covers|publisher=[[Sh-K-Boom Records]]|access-date=2009-09-14|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100217041831/http://www.sh-k-boom.com/thecovers.shtml|archive-date=2010-02-17}}</ref> Tsui attended weekend [[Chinese school]] as a child and can speak some Cantonese.<ref>{{Cite web|title=At 9:15, Sam Tsui busts out his Cantonese|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Ozs385T6h0|date=July 4, 2014|website=YouTube|publisher=[[Young Post]]|access-date=2015-01-08}}</ref>

Tsui attended [[Yale University]], where he was a member of [[Davenport College]] and [[The Duke's Men of Yale]], an all-male [[a cappella|acappella]] group.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://dukesmen.com/index.php?id=3#16|title=Meet The Duke's Men|website=The Duke's Men|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100329080032/http://dukesmen.com/index.php?id=3#16|archive-date=March 29, 2010|access-date=February 25, 2020}}</ref> He graduated from Yale with a major in Classics (Greek) in 2011.<ref name="duke" />

== Career ==

===2009–2010: Career beginnings===
Tsui appeared on ''[[ABC World News Tonight|ABC World News]]'' on October 28, 2009, singing [[The Jackson 5]]'s "[[I'll Be There (The Jackson 5 song)|I'll Be There]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2009/oct/28/tsui-eet-sounds-on-abcs-world-news-tonight/|title=Tsui-eet sounds on ABC's World News Tonight|date=2009-10-28|work=[[Yale Daily News]]|publisher=The Yale Daily News Publishing Company|access-date=2010-09-14|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101127160332/http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2009/oct/28/tsui-eet-sounds-on-abcs-world-news-tonight/|archive-date=2010-11-27}}</ref> He went on to perform on ''[[The Bonnie Hunt Show]]'' in November of the same year, where he was interviewed and performed [[Whitney Houston]]'s "[[I Wanna Dance with Somebody (Who Loves Me)]]". He also appeared on ''[[It's On with Alexa Chung]]'', singing [[Journey (band)|Journey]]'s "[[Don't Stop Believin']]" for the cast of American television series ''[[Glee (TV series)|Glee]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2009/nov/12/cross-campus-111209/|title=Cross Campus: 11.12.09|date=2009-11-12|work=[[Yale Daily News]]|publisher=The Yale Daily News Publishing Company|access-date=2010-09-14|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120924063330/http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2009/nov/12/cross-campus-111209/|archive-date=2012-09-24}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://yaleherald.com/thebullblog/sam-tsui-upstages-the-entire-cast-of-glee/|title=Sam Tsui upstages the entire cast of Glee|author=Joe|date=2009-11-11|work=[[The Yale Herald]]|publisher=[[Yale University]]|access-date=2010-09-14|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100913231644/http://yaleherald.com/thebullblog/sam-tsui-upstages-the-entire-cast-of-glee/|archive-date=2010-09-13}}</ref>

[[Kurt Hugo Schneider|Kurt Schneider]] produced the musical [[web series]] ''College Musical'', a parody of the [[High School Musical (film series)|''High School Musical'' film series]], in fall of 2008.<ref name="redefines" /> Four episodes were released, with Tsui playing the lead role of Cooper. Following the popularity of the series, it was announced that a film would be made based on the series. ''College Musical: The Movie'' premiered online in September 2014.<ref name=":02">{{Cite magazine|title='Girls' meets 'Glee'? Watch the trailer for 'College Musical' with Allison Williams|url=https://ew.com/article/2014/09/02/college-musical-trailer-allison-williams/|last=Perlman|first=Jake|date=September 2, 2014|magazine=Entertainment Weekly|language=EN|access-date=2020-05-11}}</ref>

In 2010, Tsui released his first cover album, ''The Covers''. The album, released on February 9, 2010, features covers of hits from Michael Jackson, Journey, Beyoncé, Jason Mraz, and Lady Gaga among other artists. On February 22, 2010, Tsui appeared on ''[[The Oprah Winfrey Show]]'' with his producer Kurt Schneider.<ref name="Oprah">{{cite web|title=Incredible Feats|url=http://www.oprah.com/oprahshow/Incredible-Feats/6|date=2010-02-22|work=[[The Oprah Winfrey Show]]|publisher=[[Harpo Productions]]|access-date=2010-09-14}}</ref> The two were interviewed, and Tsui performed his medley of Michael Jackson songs. In November 2010, Tsui collaborated with fellow YouTube singer [[Christina Grimmie]], singing "[[Just a Dream (Nelly song)|Just a Dream]]", which is the most viewed video on the [[YouTube]] channel "KurtHugoSchneider", with more than 200 million views.<ref name="Channel">{{cite web|title=KurtHugoSchneider's Channel|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/KurtHugoSchneider|last=Schneider|first=Kurt Hugo|publisher=[[YouTube]]|access-date=2010-12-02}}</ref> On December 2, 2010, Tsui gave an interview on ''[[The Ellen DeGeneres Show]]'' and performed a mashup of [[Katy Perry]]'s "[[Firework (song)|Firework]]" and [[Bruno Mars]]' "[[Grenade (song)|Grenade]]" with Schneider.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2010/dec/02/tsui-11-and-schneider-10-ellen/|title=Tsui '11 and Schneider '10 on 'Ellen'|date=2010-12-02|first=Esther|last=Zuckerman|work=[[Yale Daily News]]|publisher=The Yale Daily News Publishing Company|access-date=2010-12-02|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110222145129/http://www.yaledailynews.com/news/2010/dec/02/tsui-11-and-schneider-10-ellen/|archive-date=2011-02-22}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://ellen.warnerbros.com/2010/12/youtube_sensation_sam_tsui_performs_1202.php |title=YouTube Sensation Sam Tsui Performs! |date=December 2, 2010 |publisher=Ellen.WarnerBros.com. [[Warner Bros.]] |access-date=2010-12-02 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101206031602/http://ellen.warnerbros.com/2010/12/youtube_sensation_sam_tsui_performs_1202.php |archive-date=December 6, 2010 }}</ref>

===2011–2012: YouTube and rise in popularity===
In 2011, Tsui debuted his own YouTube channel, apart from his collaboration with Kurt Schneider. On February 3, 2011, Tsui was featured on [[Britney Spears]]' official website, along with his cover of "[[Hold It Against Me]]".<ref>{{cite tweet |url=https://www.twitter.com/SamuelTsui/statuses/33295678578892800|title=My "Hold it Against Me" cover was just posted on @britneyspears official website!! Ahhh! :-D|user=SamuelTsui|last=Tsui|first=Sam|number=33295678578892800|date=2011-02-03 |access-date=2011-02-06}}</ref>

In March 2011, lyrics from Tsui's song "Start Again" was featured  in the novel ''Seeker'' by [[Andy Frankham-Allen]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Interview with Andy Frankham-Allen|url=http://accidental-author.blogspot.com/2011/03/process-interview-1-untreed-reads.html|website = The Accidental Author|date = 2011-03-26|access-date = 2014-11-11}}</ref>

Tsui was mentioned three times by [[Tom Hanks]] when he addressed the Yale class of 2011.<ref>{{cite web|title=Tom Hanks Addresses Yale Class of 2011|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=baIlinqoExQ&feature=player_detailpage#t=127s|last=Hanks|first=Tom|date=May 22, 2011|website=YouTube|publisher=Yale University}}</ref> Tsui was also featured in the 2010 Yale Admissions video. The video, entitled "That's Why I Chose Yale",<ref>{{cite web|title=Video Gives Musical Answer to the Question: 'Why Choose Yale?'|url=http://news.yale.edu/2010/01/27/video-gives-musical-answer-question-why-choose-yale|date=2010-01-27|website=news.yale.edu|publisher=[[Yale University]]|access-date=2014-11-11}}</ref> reveals information about the college with students and admission officers breaking out into song across the campus. In 2017, he co-starred in a new admissions video entitled "That's Why I Toured Yale".<ref>{{cite web|title=Admissions to debut new video: update on the 'That's Why I Chose Yale' cast|url=https://news.yale.edu/2017/12/04/admissions-debut-new-video-update-thats-why-i-chose-yale-cast|date=2017-12-04|website=news.yale.edu|publisher=[[Yale University]]|access-date=2018-02-23}}</ref>

In fall 2011, Tsui was a digital correspondent for the third season of NBC's a capella competition show, ''[[The Sing-Off]]''.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title='The Sing-Off' season 3 preview with Sam Tsui|url=https://ew.com/article/2011/09/16/the-sing-off-season-3-preview-sam-tsui/|last=Smith|first=Grady|date=September 16, 2011|magazine=Entertainment Weekly|language=EN|access-date=2020-05-11}}</ref> He recorded a "Judges Medley" that included Sara Bareilles' "Love Song", Ben Folds' "Gone" and Shawn Stockman's (Boyz II Men) "On Bended Knee".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Tsui '11 sings for 'Sing-Off'|url=https://yaledailynews.com/blog/2011/09/19/tsui-11-sings-for-sing-off/|last=Zuckerman|first=Esther|date=September 19, 2011|website=yaledailynews.com|language=en|access-date=2020-05-11}}</ref>

===2013–present: Album, tours, future projects===
Tsui released his first full-length original album, ''Make It Up,'' in May 2013 after a raising more than $64,000 through the crowdfunding site [[Kickstarter]]. The project was produced by Kurt Schneider.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sam Tsui Talks Release Of His First Original Album 'Make It Up' [INTERVIEW]|url=https://newmediarockstars.com/2013/05/sam-tsui-talks-release-of-his-first-original-album-make-it-up-interview/|last=Ed|first=Carrasco|date=May 10, 2013|website=NewMediaRockstars|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-10}}</ref>

In summer 2013, Tsui and Schneider toured across Canada and the US with [[Alex Goot]], Against the Current, Landon Austin, Luke Conard, and King the Kid.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.gootmusic.com/tour |title=Tour Dates – A Summer with Alex Goot and Friends |date=2013-05-15 |access-date=2014-11-11 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141105111450/http://www.gootmusic.com/tour/ |archive-date=November 5, 2014 }}</ref>

Tsui continues to make covers of popular music on his YouTube channel while also collaborating with other YouTubers. In September 2013, Sam published a duet with [[Elle Winter]] entitled "Unsinkable (Music Is Medicine)" online. The proceeds of the single went towards [[Childhood cancer|pediatric cancer]] research as a part of the Music Is Medicine's Donate a Song project.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Baltimore woman gleeful about making music to help sick children|url=https://www.baltimoresun.com/features/bs-gl-goodworks-vmas-20131025-story.html|last=Nitkin|first=Karen|date=October 27, 2013|website=Baltimore Sun|access-date=2020-05-10}}</ref>

Tsui's vocals were featured in a promotional video for [[Coca-Cola]] that premiered on January 14, 2014. In 2013, Kurt Hugo Schneider and Coca-Cola teamed up to create music videos featuring creative covers of two 2011 hits namely [[Calvin Harris]]' "[[Feel So Close]]" and [[Of Monsters and Men]]'s "[[Little Talks]]" for a campaign called "The Sounds of AHH". The commercial edits of both premiered on the inaugural episode of [[American Idol season 13|season 13]] of ''[[American Idol]]'' on January 14, 2014, on [[Fox Broadcasting Company|FOX]] with "Feel So Close" featuring the vocals of Tsui whereas Schneider makes music playing only Coca-Cola bottles, glasses and cans.<ref>{{cite web|title=Behind the Bottles: 5 Questions With Kurt Hugo Schneider|url=http://www.coca-colacompany.com/stories/behind-the-bottles-5-questions-with-kurt-hugo-schneider|last=Moye|first=Jay|date=August 14, 2013|work=The Coca-Cola Company|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130818020909/http://www.coca-colacompany.com/stories/behind-the-bottles-5-questions-with-kurt-hugo-schneider|archive-date=August 18, 2013}}</ref>

In 2016, Tsui starred in an episode of [[Bones (TV series)|''Bones'']] as collegiate acappella singing group member Jake Eisenberg.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Pentatonix, Sam Tsui, and Bruin Harmony Combine Forces For This Performance On Fox's "Bones"|url=http://www.collegeaca.com/content/pentatonix-sam-tsui-and-bruin-harmony-combine-forces-for-this-performance-on-foxs-bones|date=June 30, 2016|website=College A Cappella|language=en|access-date=2020-05-07|archive-date=2020-08-06|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200806151855/https://www.collegeaca.com/content/pentatonix-sam-tsui-and-bruin-harmony-combine-forces-for-this-performance-on-foxs-bones|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Personal life==
On April 15, 2016, Tsui posted a [[coming out]] video on his YouTube channel in which he announced that he is [[homosexuality|gay]] and in a long-term relationship with fellow musician and collaborator Casey Breves.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Celebrities Coming Out in the Internet Age|url=https://www.thenewcivilrightsmovement.com/2016/06/celebrities_coming_out_in_the_internet_age/|last=Summers|first=Claude|date=June 4, 2016|website=The New Civil Rights Movement|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-07}}</ref> They met while they were both at Yale (Tsui a freshman and Breves a junior) and were both part of an a cappella group on campus.<ref>{{Citation|last=TheSamTsui|title=VLOG: The Proposal Story!!! ft. Casey Breves|date=2017-04-11|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=908Fg2SG4Eg|access-date=2017-04-12}}</ref> The couple got married on April 16, 2016. They have adopted a daughter, named Elaia. <ref>{{Cite web|title=YouTube singer Sam Tsui comes out and reveals surprise wedding|url=https://www.pinknews.co.uk/2016/04/18/youtube-singer-sam-tsui-comes-out-and-reveals-surprise-wedding/|last=Duffy|first=Nick|date=April 18, 2016|website=PinkNews|language=en-GB|access-date=2020-05-07}}</ref>
