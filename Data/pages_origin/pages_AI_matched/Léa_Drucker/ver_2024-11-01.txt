'''Léa Drucker''' (born 23 January 1972) is a French actress. In the English speaking world of television, she is notable for playing Catherine Durand in 3 seasons (24 episodes) of the French/British television series ''[[War of the Worlds (2019 TV series)|War of the Worlds]]'' (2019–2022).

==Early life==
Born in [[Caen]], [[Normandy]], she is the niece of television presenter [[Michel Drucker]], and of ex-president of [[M6 (TV channel)|M6]] [[Jean Drucker]]. Her father Jacques is a medical doctor, and her mother, Martine, an English teacher. She is the cousin of the journalist [[Marie Drucker]], daughter of Jean Drucker. Her paternal grandfather was [[Jewish]]. She is in a relationship with [[Julien Rambaldi]] and they have a daughter.<ref name="belle">{{cite news| title = Léa Drucker, maman comblée : La belle actrice a accouché ! | publisher = Pure People| url = http://www.purepeople.com/article/lea-drucker-maman-comblee-la-belle-actrice-a-accouche_a146878/1 | date = 
| accessdate = 21 January 2017}}</ref> She starred in the film ''Les meilleurs amis du monde'', which Rambaldi was directing at the time.<ref name="buff">{{cite web |url=https://glamourbuff.com/relationship/lea-drucker-married-husband/ |title= Is Léa Drucker Married to Julien Rambaldi? Their Relationship and Children Details |date= February 27, 2023}}</ref>

She studied acting at the École de la rue Blanche, the École Nationale Supérieure des Arts et Techniques du Théâtre (ENSATT) in Paris.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-10-31 |title=Léa Drucker : "Ce que j'aime dans les comédiens, c'est quand j'ai l'impression que ça ne joue plus" |url=https://www.radiofrance.fr/franceculture/podcasts/affaires-culturelles/lea-drucker-est-l-invitee-d-affaires-culturelles-2022690 |access-date=2024-02-22 |website=[[France Culture]] |language=fr}}</ref>

==Career==
Her early career encompassed both classical theatre pieces like ''[[Le Misanthrope]]'', and contemporary plays such as ''Blanc'' by Emmanuelle Marie.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lecoinducinephage.com/?p=581 |title=FRAGMENTS D'UN DICTIONNAIRE AMOUREUX : LÉA DRUCKER |accessdate=July 21, 2011|language=fr}}</ref>

She has been twice nominated for the [[Molière Award]] for Best Female Revelation, in 2001 for her role in ''Danny et la grande bleue'' and in 2004 for ''84 Charing Cross Road''.<ref name="belle"/> In 2019, she won the [[César Award for Best Actress]] for ''[[Custody (2017 film)|Custody]].''<ref name="César2019">{{Cite web |last=Keslassy |first=Elsa |date=2019-02-22 |title=Cesar Awards: Xavier Legrand's 'Custody' Wins Best Film |url=https://variety.com/2019/film/global/xavier-legrands-custody-wins-best-film-at-44th-cesar-awards-1203146421/ |access-date=2024-02-22 |website=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2019, she ventured into English-speaking television, starring as Catherine Durand in 3 seasons (24 episodes) of the [[StudioCanal]] French/British television series ''[[War of the Worlds (2019 TV series)|War of the Worlds]]'' (2019–2022), alongside [[Gabriel Byrne]] and [[Daisy Edgar-Jones]].<ref name="war">{{cite web |url= https://www.studiocanal.com/title/war-of-the-worlds-season-1-2019/ |title= War of the Worls - Season 1 |work= studiocanal.com |accessdate= January 18, 2024 }}</ref> She gave birth to a daughter with her civil partner, the director and screenwriter Julien Rimbaldi.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.closermag.fr/people/lea-drucker-maman-a-42-ans-ses-confidences-sur-martha-sa-fille-et-son-fantasme-de-mere-1687559#item=1 | title=LÃ©a Drucker maman Ã 42 ans : Ses confidences sur Martha, sa fille, et son "fantasme de mÃ¨re" | date=27 January 2023 }}</ref>
