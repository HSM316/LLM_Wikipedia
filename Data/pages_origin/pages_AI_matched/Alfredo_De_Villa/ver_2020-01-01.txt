'''Alfredo De Villa''' is a Mexican director.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://labandafilms.com/|title=Home|website=La Banda Films|accessdate=Feb 16, 2019}}</ref> He is best known for directing award winning films such as ''[[Adrift in Manhattan]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.moviefone.com/movie/adrift-in-manhattan/28593/main/|title=Adrift in Manhattan (2007) - Movie|website=www.moviefone.com|accessdate=Feb 16, 2019}}</ref> which was nominated for the Grand Jury Prize at the [[Sundance Film Festival]] in 2007, and ''[[Washington Heights (film)|Washington Heights]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2003/05/09/movies/film-in-review-washington-heights.html|title=FILM IN REVIEW; 'Washington Heights'|first=Stephen|last=Holden|date=May 9, 2003|accessdate=Feb 16, 2019|via=NYTimes.com}}</ref> which won a special mention for directing and acting at the [[Tribeca Film Festival]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.fandango.com/washingtonheights_v263760/summary|title=fandango.com|accessdate=Feb 16, 2019}}</ref>

== Early life ==
De Villa was born in Puebla, Mexico. He earned his [[Bachelor of Arts]] from the [[University of Miami]] and his [[Master of Fine Arts]] from the film division of [[Columbia University]], with an emphasis in directing.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.filmbug.com/db/346732|title=Alfredo De Villa - Filmbug|website=www.filmbug.com|accessdate=Feb 16, 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
De Villa's two short films,'' Joe’s Egg'' and ''Neto’s Run'' were both awarded the Best Latino Director Award from the ''[[Directors Guild of America]]'' in 1995 and 1999, he is the only filmmaker to be awarded this honor twice.<ref name="auto">[http://www.filmmakermagazine.com/news/2007/09/alfredo-de-villa-adrift-in-manhattan/ filmmakermagazine.com]</ref> De Villa has gone on to direct many acclaimed feature films.

==Personal life==
De Villa currently resides in [[Los Angeles, California]].<ref name="auto"/>
