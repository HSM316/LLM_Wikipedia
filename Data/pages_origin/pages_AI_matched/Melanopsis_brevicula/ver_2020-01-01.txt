'''''Melanopsis brevicula''''' is a small species of [[Gastropoda|gastropod]] endemic to small streams near [[Agourai]], [[Morocco]]. It is distinctive due to its minute size, flattened sculpture, low spire, and small aperture. It is known from a single location 10&nbsp;km<sup>2</sup> in area(Oued Ain Maarouf)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gbif.org/es/species/5782594|title=Melanopsis brevicula Pallary, 1918|website=www.gbif.org|language=es|access-date=2019-06-06}}</ref> which has been well surveyed, and found to be threatened by increasing human population, droughts of increasing extremity, water diversion, and pastoralization. Shell collecting presents a minor threat to populations. The species has been classified as [[Critically endangered]] by the [[IUCN]].<ref name=iucn/>
