'''Ilya Silchukov''', could also be spelled '''Ilya Silchukou''', ({{lang-be|Ілля Сільчукоў}}; {{Lang-ru|Илья Сильчуков}}; born 1982) is a Belarusian operatic baritone. He debuted his career in Tchaikovsky's ''[[Eugene Onegin (opera)|Eugene Onegin]]'' at the [[National Opera and Ballet of Belarus]] in 2005.  Ever since his debut he has shared his appearance at several European opera houses. His academic and vocal achievements had shown his preparedness and the alertness of a true opera singer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bolshoitheatre-belarus.by/en/baritones-9|title=Baritones|publisher=National Academic Bolshoi Opera and Ballet Theatre of the Republic of Belarus|accessdate=7 April 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140407095000/http://bolshoitheatre-belarus.by/en/baritones-9|archive-date=7 April 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Ilya Silchukov was born and grew up in [[Minsk]], Belarus. In 1988, at the age of six he began studies of music.  In 2000, he graduated with honors from the State Musical Lyceum of the [[Belarusian State Academy of Music]]. As his base education he studied choir conducting. Starting from 9th grade, Silchukov began attending voice performance classes of baritone [[Sergey Kostin]].  In 2002, he graduated from [[Minsk State Musical College name after Mikhail Glinka|Minsk State Musical College n.a. M. Glinka]] classes of professor [[Adam Murzich]] majoring in Vocal Performance.  In 2005, Ilya Silchukov earned a [[Bachelor of Fine Arts|BFA]] and in 2007 [[Master of Fine Arts|MD]] in Voice Performance at the Belarusian State Academy of Music under the tutelage of docents/associate professors [[Petr Riedeger]] and [[Larisa Shymanovitch]].  In 2009, Silchukov participated in Tremplin Jeunes Chanteurs project of [[Opéra d'Avignon]]. In 2011, he was selected for Young Singers Program of the [[Salzburg Festival]] where he worked with [[Christa Ludwig]], [[Piotr Beczała]], [[Michael Schade]], [[Simon Keenlyside]], [[Marc Minkowski]] and [[Ivor Bolton]].<ref>{{cite web| title=Salzburger Festspiele| url=https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Young_Singers_Project#Teilnehmer_2011}}</ref>{{Circular reference|date=April 2018}}

== Early career ==
In 2005 Silchukov debuted his career in ''[[Eugene Onegin (opera)|Eugene Onegin]]'' by [[Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky]] entering the ensemble of the [[National Opera and Ballet of Belarus]] in Minsk.  Silchukov's International debut was in 2009 at Parnu International Opera Festival as Athanael in J. Massenett's "Thais" with Veronika Dzhioeva (Russia), Staged by Mai Murdmaa and directed by Erki Pehk.<ref>{{cite web| title=PromFest| url=http://www.promfest.ee/opera-productions/thais}}</ref>  The next years brought performances in many important opera houses including [[Teatro dell'Opera di Roma]], [[Oper Frankfurt]], [[Israeli Opera]], [[Théâtre Royal de la Monnaie]], [[Grand Theatre, Warsaw]], [[Teatro Lirico Giuseppe Verdi]], [[Opéra National de Montpellier]], [[Estonian National Opera]], [[Slovak National Theater]], [[Janáček Theatre]], [[Vorarlberger Landestheater]], Opera i Filharmonia Podlaska, [[Moldova National Opera Ballet]], [[National Theatre Brno]], [[Perm Opera and Ballet Theatre]], Bruckner Festival, and others.

==Professional career==
In 2019 Ilya Silchukov performed a duet with Elena Salo at the Opening Ceremony of 2nd [[2019 European Games]] hold in Minsk, Belarus: Among [[Anna Netrebko]], [[Yusif Eyvazov]], [[Igor Krutoy]] and [[Dimash Kudaibergen]].

== Awards and achievements ==
* In 2004 Ilya Silchukov was first awarded with ''Silver Medal of [[Union of Theatre Workers of the Russian Federation]]''.<ref>[[:ru:Союз театральных деятелей Российской Федерации|Union of Theatre Workers of the Russian Federation]]</ref>{{Circular reference|date=April 2018}}
* In 2008, 2009 and 2010 Ilya Silchukov was awarded grand prizes of the ''Special Fund of the President of the Republic of Belarus'' for Support of the Talented Youth.
* In 2015 Silchukov was given a, governed by the Law of the Republic of Belarus, State award – ''[[Medal Of Francysk Skaryna]]'' for contribution to the development of national culture and art.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.belta.by/culture/view/iljja-silchukov-zanjal-tretje-mesto-v-rossijskom-shou-bolshaja-opera-60128-2014/|title=Илья Сильчуков занял третье место в российском шоу "Большая опера"|date=November 30, 2014|website=www.belta.by}}</ref>
* In 2015 Silchukov received the ''International Music Award BelBrand''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://en.belbrandaward.com/ilya-silchukov|title=Ilya Silchukov|website=en.belbrandaward.com}}</ref>
