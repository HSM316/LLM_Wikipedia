"'''Flowers Become Screens'''" is the debut single by the Canadian [[electronic music]] group [[Delerium]] featuring singer [[Kristy Thirsk]] from their 1994 album ''[[Semantic Spaces]]''. The B-side of the single is the track "Incantation" from the same album. In some territories, "Incantation" was used as the A-side of the single instead of "Flowers Become Screens".<ref name=youtube>{{cite web|title=Delerium - Flowers Become Screens|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZsT-Bdx-Dek|publisher=youtube.com|accessdate=4 January 2014}}</ref><ref name=disc>{{cite web|title=Deleriumâ€“ Flowers Become Screens|url=http://www.discogs.com/Delerium-Flowers-Become-Screens/release/187565|publisher=discogs.com|accessdate=4 January 2014}}</ref><ref name=fm>{{cite web|title=Flowers Become Screens (4:21)|url=http://www.last.fm/music/Delerium/_/Flowers+Become+Screens|publisher=last.fm|accessdate=4 January 2014}}</ref>

"Flowers Becomes Screens" was remixed by [[Deepsky]] in 2000 as a bonus track on the album ''[[Poem (album)|Poem]]''.<ref name=delerium>{{cite web|title=Poem|url=http://www.delerium.ca/index.php/discography/singles-eps/35-discography/382-poem|publisher=delerium.ca|accessdate=4 January 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140104210802/http://www.delerium.ca/index.php/discography/singles-eps/35-discography/382-poem|archive-date=4 January 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Both songs have a music video directed by [[William Morrison (director)|William Morrison]] and Paddy Gillen.
