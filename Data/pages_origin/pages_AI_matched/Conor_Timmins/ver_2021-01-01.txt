'''Conor Timmins''' (born September 18, 1998) is a [[Canada|Canadian]] professional [[ice hockey]] [[defenceman]] currently playing<!-- with the [[Colorado Eagles]] in the [[American Hockey League]] (AHL)--> as a prospect for the [[Colorado Avalanche]] in the [[National Hockey League]] (NHL).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eliteprospects.com/player/267584/conor-timmins|title=Conor Timmins|website=Elite Prospects | date = October 7, 2019 | accessdate = October 7, 2019}}</ref>

==Playing career==
===Junior===
Timmins played minor midget with the Southern Tier Admirals of the [[South-Central Triple A Hockey League]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Alumni (Southern Tier Admirals AAA) |url=https://southerntieradmirals.ca/Pages/5217/Alumni/ |publisher=Southern Tier Admirals AAA |accessdate=6 October 2019}}</ref> He was selected in the 4th round (79th overall) of the 2014 [[Ontario Hockey League|OHL]] Priority Selection by the [[Sault Ste. Marie Greyhounds]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Conor Timmins commits to Greyhounds |url=https://ontariohockeyleague.com/conor-timmins-commits-to-greyhounds/ |accessdate=6 October 2019 |publisher=Ontario Hockey League |date=23 July 2015}}</ref> Timmins started his first season of junior hockey with the [[Thorold Blackhawks]] of the [[Greater Ontario Junior Hockey League]] before being traded to the [[St. Catharines Falcons]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Potrecz |first1=Bill |title=Timmins signs with Greyhounds |url=https://www.pressreader.com/canada/the-niagara-falls-review/20150814/281913066844192 |accessdate=6 October 2019 |work=Niagara Falls Review |date=14 August 2015}}</ref> He debuted for the Greyhounds in the 2015–16 season, scoring 13 points in 60 games. 

In the 2016–17 season, Timmins scored 61 points (7 goals and 54 assists) and played in the [[CHL/NHL Top Prospects Game]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Timmins, Frost named to 2017 Sherwin-Williams CHL/NHL Top Prospects Game Rosters |url=https://soogreyhounds.com/article/timmins-frost-named-to-2017-sherwin-williams-chlnhl-top-prospects-game-rosters |accessdate=6 October 2019 |work=Canadian Hockey League |date=14 December 2016}}</ref> Going into the [[2017 NHL Entry Draft]], Timmins was ranked the 18th best North American skater by NHL Central Scouting. <ref>{{cite web |title=2017 Draft Profile: Conor Timmins |url=https://www.nhl.com/avalanche/2017draft/conor-timmins |publisher=National Hockey League |accessdate=6 October 2019}}</ref> He was taken by the [[Colorado Avalanche]] with the first selection of the second round (32nd overall).<ref>{{cite news |last1=Knabenbauer |first1=Ron |title=Avalanche Picks Conor Timmins No. 32 Overall at 2017 NHL Draft |url=https://www.nhl.com/avalanche/news/avalanche-selects-conor-timmins-at-no-32/c-290116316 |accessdate=6 October 2019 |work=NHL.com |date=June 24, 2017}}</ref> Timmins returned to the Greyhounds for the 2017–18 season, scoring 41 points (8 goals, 33 assists) in 36 games. He signed a three-year, entry-level contract with the Avalanche on March 2, 2018.<ref name="Timmins Contract">{{cite news |title=Avalanche Signs Timmins to Entry-Level Contract |url=https://www.nhl.com/avalanche/news/avalanche-signs-conor-timmins/c-296539536 |accessdate=6 October 2019 |work=NHL.com}}</ref>

===Professional===
Timmins suffered a concussion at the end of his final season in junior hockey, and he continued to have symptoms that kept him from playing in any games for the Avalanche or their [[American Hockey League|AHL]] affiliate, the [[Colorado Eagles]] in the 2018–19 season.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Dater |first1=Adrian |title=BSN exclusive: Conor Timmins to be shut down for rest of season |url=https://bsndenver.com/bsn-exclusive-conor-timmins-to-be-shut-down-for-rest-of-season/ |accessdate=6 October 2019 |work=BSN Denver |date=20 March 2019}}</ref> 

On October 1, 2019, the Avalanche announced that Timmins had made the opening-night roster.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Parmele |first1=Callie |title=Timmins Earns a Spot on Avalanche Opening-Night Roster |url=https://www.nhl.com/avalanche/news/conor-timmins-earns-spot-on-2019-avalanche-opening-night-roster/c-309665642 |accessdate=6 October 2019 |work=NHL.com |date=2 October 2019}}</ref> He made his NHL debut on the Avalanche's third defensive pairing in a 5–3 season opening victory over the [[Calgary Flames]] on October 3, 2019.<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.nhl.com/avalanche/news/colorado-defeats-calgary-in-season-opener/c-309716504 | title = Colorado defeats Calgary in season opener | work = NHL.com | date = October 3, 2019 | accessdate = October 3, 2019}}</ref> After his second game with the Avalanche, Timmins was re-assigned to the Colorado Eagles on October 7, 2019.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Chambers |first1=Mike| url = https://www.denverpost.com/2019/10/07/ian-cole-avalanche-return-nears-conor-timmins-eagles/ | title = Ian Cole nears return, Avalanche sends Conor Timmins to AHL Eagles | publisher = [[Denver Post]] | date = October 7, 2019 | accessdate = October 7, 2019}}</ref>

Timmins was selected to [[Canada men's national junior ice hockey team|Canada's under-20 team]] for the [[2018 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships|2018 World Junior Championships]] in [[Buffalo, New York]], winning gold. He scored 5 points in the tournament, including an assist on the game-winning goal in the gold-medal game. He also lead the tournament in plus/minus at +15.<ref name = "Timmins Contract"/>
