'''Conotton Creek''' ({{IPAc-en|k|ɒ|ˈ|n|ɒ|t|ən}} {{respell|kon|OT|ən}}) is a tributary of the [[Tuscarawas River]], 38.7 miles (62.3&nbsp;km) long, in eastern [[Ohio]] in the [[United States]].  Via the Tuscarawas, [[Muskingum River|Muskingum]] and [[Ohio River|Ohio]] Rivers, it is part of the [[drainage basin|watershed]] of the [[Mississippi River]], draining an area of 286 square miles (741&nbsp;km²) in [[Carroll County, Ohio|Carroll]], [[Harrison County, Ohio|Harrison]] and [[Tuscarawas County, Ohio]].  The source is at 1240 feet and the mouth is at 874 feet.<ref name=ODNR>{{cite web |url=  https://soilandwater.ohiodnr.gov/portals/soilwater/pdf/stream/GAZETTEER_OF_OHIO_STREAMS.pdf#page=81| title= GAZETTEER_OF_OHIO_STREAMS|accessdate=2010-01-07 |author= Ohio Department of Natural Resources}}</ref> Dover Dam, downstream on the Tuscarawas river, is normally dry, but can impound a reservoir on Conotton and tributaries to a pool elevation of 916 feet for downstream flood control by the [[United States Army Corps of Engineers|Corp of Engineers]].  From its source in eastern [[Harrison County, Ohio]] the creek flows west northwest through [[Jewett, Ohio|Jewitt]], [[Scio, Ohio|Scio]], [[Conotton, Ohio|Conotton]], [[Bowerston, Ohio|Bowerston]], [[Leesville, Ohio|Leesville]], [[Sherrodsville, Ohio|Sherrodsville]], [[New Cumberland, Ohio|New Cumberland]] and [[Somerdale, Ohio|Somerdale]] before reaching its mouth in central [[Tuscarawas County, Ohio]]. The [[Wheeling and Lake Erie Railway (1990)|Wheeling and Lake Erie Railway]] is situated in the valley.  [[Ohio State Route 151]] runs in the valley from near the source to Bowerston.  [[Ohio State Route 212]] run along the valley from Bowerston to the mouth.  The [[Conotton Creek Trail]] runs from Bowerston to Jewett.

The stream was declared navigable by the Ohio legislature in 1808, but the act was repealed in 1816, probably to allow the construction of mill dams.<ref name=Tusc669>{{cite book |title=The History of Tuscarawas County |last=Mansfield |first=J.B. |year=1884 |publisher=Warner, Beers & Co. |page=669 |url=https://books.google.com/?id=5DguAAAAYAAJ&printsec=frontcover&dq=history+tuscarawas+county&cd=1#v=onepage&q=&f=false}}</ref>

==Variant names==

According to the [[Geographic Names Information System]], Conotton Creek has also been known historically as:<ref name=GNIS>{{cite web |url={{Gnis3|1070721}} |title=Geographic Names Information System entry for Conotton Creek| accessdate=2010-01-07}}</ref>
*Connotten Creek
*Connotton  Creek
*Conolton  Creek
*Conoten Creek
*Gutgatsink Creek
*Kannotten  Creek
*Kennottenhead Creek
*Knottenhead Creek
*One Leg Creek

Conotton Creek was known in the 19th century as One Leg Creek, named for an [[Native Americans in the United States|Indian]] who lived near its mouth.<ref name=Tusc669/><ref>[http://www.carrollcountyohio.com/history/townships/Monroe/MonroeTownship.pdf#page=2 History of Monroe Township, Carroll County, p.2-3]</ref><ref>{{cite book |title=Historical Collections of Ohio, The Ohio Centennial Edition |last=Howe |first=Henry |year=1907 |publisher=The State of Ohio |page=363 |url=https://books.google.com/?id=OXIOAAAAIAAJ&printsec=frontcover&dq=henry+howe&cd=2#v=onepage&q=&f=false}}</ref>
