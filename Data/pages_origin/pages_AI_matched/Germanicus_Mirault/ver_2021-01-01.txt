'''Germanicus Mirault''' was a [[French people|French]] [[surgeon]] who pioneered [[cleft lip]] [[surgery]].

==Early life==
Germanicus Mirault was born in Angers on either 29 February or 1 March 1796 (1796 was a leap year).<ref name=Dupoirieux>{{cite journal |pmid=8927924 |year=1996 |last1=Dupoirieux |first1=L |last2=Penneau |first2=M |title=The contribution of Germanicus Mirault to maxillofacial surgery in general and to surgery of the cleft lip in particular |volume=97 |issue=4 |pages=195–201 |journal=Revue de Stomatologie et de Chirurgie Maxillo-faciale}}</ref><ref name=Ivy>{{cite journal |last1=Mirault |first1=Germanicus |year=1976 |title=Commentary By Dr. Robert H. Ivy |journal=Plastic & Reconstructive Surgery |volume=57 |issue=4 |pages=506–7 |doi=10.1097/00006534-197604000-00017}}</ref><ref name =ANM>{{Citation | url   = http://www.academie-medecine.fr/detailMembre.cfm?idRub=19&idLigne=128006 | title = Mirault Germanicus | work  = Académie Nationale de Médecine Members | publisher  = [[Académie Nationale de Médecine]] | accessdate = October 7, 2013
}}</ref> His father and grandfather were also surgeons, with his father specialising in [[ophthalmology]].<ref name=Ivy />

==Education==
Mirault first began studying medicine under his father in 1814.<ref name=Ivy /> Moving to Paris to continue his studies, he presented his thesis in 1823 on [[keratitis]].<ref name=Ivy /><ref name=GM3>{{Cite book | last1  = Mirault | first1 = Germanicus | last2 =  Faculté de médecine de Paris | name-list-style = amp | title     = Dissertation sur l'anatomie et l'inflammation de la cornée transparente | publisher = Imprimerie de Didot le Jeune | place = Paris, France | year = 1823}}</ref>

==Medical career==
Together with [[Joseph-François Malgaigne]], Mirault introduced [[Flap (surgery)|flap transposition]] for cleft-lip closure.<ref name=Millard>{{Cite book | last1  = Millard Jr | first1 = Ralph | title  = Cleft Craft: the Evolution of its Surgery; vol 1 | publisher = Little, Brown & Co | place = Boston, MA, USA | year = 1976}}</ref><ref name=GM1>Mirault G. Deux lettres sur l'operation du bec-delievre. J Chir. 1844;2:257.</ref> In 1935, this work was championed by Victor Veau, who stated "Mirault is the genius of cleft lip surgery".<ref name=Veau>Veau V. Bec de Lievre. Hypothese sur la malformation initiale. Ann Anat Pathol Paris. 1935;12:389.</ref> He made other key contributions to [[maxillofacial]] surgical procedures, including the first [[Ligature (medicine)|ligature]] of a human [[lingual artery]] in 1833 and pioneering a method of temporary occlusion of the eyelids when correcting a post-burn [[ectropion]], which is still in use today.<ref name=Dupoirieux /><ref name=GM2>{{Cite book | last1  = Mirault | first1 = Germanicus | title  = De l'occlusion chirurgicale temporaire des paupières dans le traitement de l'ectropion cicatriciel [Texte imprimé] | publisher = Imprimerie de P. Lachèse, Belleuvre et Dolbeau | place = Paris, France | year = 1871}}</ref>

==Awards==
Mirault was awarded by the [[Académie des Sciences]] in 1869 for his work on eyelid surgery.<ref name=Dupoirieux />

==Death==
Mirault died on 19 January 1879 in Angers.<ref name=ANM />
