'''Betty W. Liu''' ({{lang-zh|刘文思}}) is the Executive Vice Chairman of the [[New York Stock Exchange]] and the Chief Experience Officer of [[Intercontinental Exchange]]. Liu joined the NYSE Group after ICE acquired Radiate, the ed-tech startup she founded. Liu is a former news anchor for [[Bloomberg Television]], a subsidiary of [[Bloomberg L.P.]] A business journalist,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bloombergradio.com/hosts/betty-liu/|title=Betty Liu {{!}} Bloomberg Radio|work=Bloomberg Radio|access-date=2017-02-26|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170227062614/https://www.bloombergradio.com/hosts/betty-liu/|archive-date=2017-02-27|url-status=dead}}</ref> Liu regularly interviewed influential business, political and media leaders including Warren Buffett, [[Elon Musk]], and Arianna Huffington.

Liu was the founder and [[CEO]] of ed-tech startup Radiate, based in New York City.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://radiateinc.com/|title=Radiate {{!}} Home|website=radiateinc.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-01-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190116224351/https://radiateinc.com/|archive-date=2019-01-16|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Early life==
Liu was born in Hong Kong, moved to the United States when she was three years old, and from age twelve was raised in [[Philadelphia]]. Liu attended [[Central High School (Philadelphia)|Central High School]].

== Education ==
Liu graduated magna cum laude in 1995 from the [[University of Pennsylvania]] with a degree in English.

==Career==
Liu started her career in financial journalism as a Hong Kong-based regional correspondent and youngest Taiwan Bureau Chief for [[Dow Jones Newswires]].<ref name="Greppi">{{cite web |url=http://www.tvweek.com/news/2008/11/betty_liu_host_of_bloomberg_tv.php |last=Greppi |first=Michele |title=TV Week: Betty Liu, Host of Bloomberg TV's 'Starting Bell' |work=TV Week |accessdate=2009-07-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090621052839/http://www.tvweek.com/news/2008/11/betty_liu_host_of_bloomberg_tv.php |archive-date=2009-06-21 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="Betty Liu">{{cite web|url=http://www.ftpress.com/authors/bio.aspx?a=495A9D9D-124A-4A32-80EB-7F08D07143DC|title=Betty Liu |work=Financial Times Press |accessdate=2009-07-29}}</ref>

After she left Dow Jones, she worked for several years as the Atlanta Bureau Chief for the ''[[Financial Times]]'',<ref name="Betty Liu" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mediabistro.com/tvnewser/morning_media_menu/liu_says_despite_less_bad_news_people_are_still_worried_116298.asp|title=Liu Says Despite 'Less Bad News', 'People are Still Worried'|work=TV Newser|accessdate=2009-07-29|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100603161258/http://www.mediabistro.com/tvnewser/morning_media_menu/liu_says_despite_less_bad_news_people_are_still_worried_116298.asp|archivedate=2010-06-03}}</ref> where she broke stories on top corporate and political leaders such as [[Coca-Cola]] ex-chief executive [[Douglas Daft]], former [[Home Depot]] CEO [[Bob Nardelli]], and former U.S. President [[Jimmy Carter]].

Returning to Asia as an anchor and correspondent for [[CNBC Asia]],<ref name="Betty Liu" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-147107820.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121026084224/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-147107820.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=2012-10-26|title=CNBC Asia has added Betty Liu (Hong Kong) and Sri Jegarajah (Singapore) |work=Television Asia |accessdate=2009-07-29}}</ref> Liu covered the daily market action in the Greater China region for all of CNBC's morning shows, including for CNBC's ''[[Squawk Box]]''.<ref name="Greppi" />

Over the course of her career, she has also written for ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'' and ''[[Far Eastern Economic Review]]''

From 2007 to 2018, she worked for [[Bloomberg Television]], formerly anchoring "In the Loop with Betty Liu" from its inception in 2007 until the show's cancelation in June 2015,<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.bloomberg.com/live/schedule-shows | work=Bloomberg | title=Schedule-shows - Bloomberg Business}}</ref> which was replaced with "Market Movers".  Liu then anchored the morning program "Daybreak Asia" out of NYC and was an Editor-at-Large at Bloomberg.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bloombergradio.com/hosts/betty-liu/|title=Betty Liu {{!}} Bloomberg Radio|work=Bloomberg Radio|access-date=2018-01-02|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180103072717/https://www.bloombergradio.com/hosts/betty-liu/|archive-date=2018-01-03|url-status=dead}}</ref>  She also hosted "In the Loop, At the Half" on Bloomberg Radio.

In 2017 Liu founded RadiateInc.com, a subscription website offering business micro-lessons from the top CEOs.

Liu became [[High Point University]]'s Media Entrepreneur in Residence in October 2017.

Radiate Inc was acquired by [[Intercontinental Exchange]] in 2018, and Liu became Executive Vice Chairman of ICE.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-06-05|title=Intercontinental Exchange Names Betty Liu Executive Vice Chairman of the New York Stock Exchange; NYSE to Acquire Radiate, Inc.|url=https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20180605005932/en/Intercontinental-Exchange-Names-Betty-Liu-Executive-Vice-Chairman-of-the-New-York-Stock-Exchange-NYSE-to-Acquire-Radiate-Inc.|access-date=2022-02-16|website=www.businesswire.com|language=en}}</ref>

In 2021, Liu founded [https://www.dandzmedia.com/about/ D and Z Media Acquisition Corp], a SPAC that focuses on the emerging subscription economy.

==Awards==
In 1997, she received a Dow Jones Newswires Award for her coverage of the Asian financial crisis.{{citation needed|date=January 2021}}

Her coverage while at FT of the biggest [[Fortune 500]] companies based in the South (Coca-Cola, Home Depot, [[United Parcel Service|UPS]], [[FedEx]]) earned her a spot on TJFR's "Top 30 business journalists under 30 list"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newsbios.com/30under30/Past%20Classes.htm |title=Past NewsBios 30 Under 30 Award Winners |work=NewsBios |accessdate=2009-07-29 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131023061508/http://www.newsbios.com/30under30/Past%20Classes.htm |archivedate=2013-10-23 }}</ref> three years in a row (2000–2002).

On October 27, 2011, Betty Liu became the first female and Asian student to be inducted into Central High School's Alumni Hall of Fame.<ref name="CHSHOF">[http://centralhighalumni.com/about/alumni-hall-of-fame/ Central High School Hall of Fame], The Associated Alumni of The Central High School of Philadelphia, Central High School, Philadelphia.</ref>

In 2012, Bloomberg TV ran an advertising campaign incorrectly touting Liu as "Pulitzer Prize-nominated".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theatlantic.com/business/archive/2012/06/journalists-please-stop-saying-youre-pulitzer-prize-nominated/326547/ |title=Journalists, Please Stop Saying You Were 'Pulitzer Prize-Nominated' |first=Alexander |last=Abad-Santos |publisher=The Atlantic Wire |date=2012-06-26 |accessdate=2019-04-24}}</ref> When contacted by msnbc.com, Bloomberg TV acknowledged the error and said it would correct the ads. The same claim of a Pulitzer nomination was made by the publisher of her biography, ''Age Smart: Discovering the Fountain of Youth at Midlife and Beyond''.{{citation needed|date=January 2018}}

== Personal life ==
Liu has been married twice, first to Ohio neurologist Benjamin L. Walter (also a Central High School graduate, whom she married on September 14, 2002 and divorced in 2006<ref name="CNBCFix: CNBC Star Profiles">[http://www.cnbcfix.com/stars.html CNBCFix: "CNBC Star Profiles"]</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.judicialview.com/State-Cases/ohio/Civil-Procedure/Walter-v-Liu/10/25104 |title=Judicial View: Case View: "Walter v Liu" |access-date=2011-12-26 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111121021515/http://www.judicialview.com/State-Cases/ohio/Civil-Procedure/Walter-v-Liu/10/25104 |archive-date=2011-11-21 |url-status=dead }}</ref>) and then to William Browning,<ref name="CNBCFix: CNBC Star Profiles"/> an Australian news executive whom she met in Hong Kong. They divorced in 2012.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.parkplacemag.com/03/2011/balancing-act/ |title="Balancing Act", Park Place Magazine, March 2011 |access-date=2011-12-26 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120426072417/http://www.parkplacemag.com/03/2011/balancing-act/ |archive-date=2012-04-26 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Liu is a mother of twin boys, Dylan and Zachary, who were born July 21, 2004, by her first husband. They live in [[Millburn, New Jersey]].<ref name="NJ">Anderson, Susan. [http://www.nj.com/insidejersey/index.ssf/2010/09/in_the_loop_with_betty_liu.html "In the loop with Betty Liu"], ''[[The Star-Ledger]]'', September 21, 2010</ref>

Liu is fluent in Mandarin, and speaks some Cantonese.
