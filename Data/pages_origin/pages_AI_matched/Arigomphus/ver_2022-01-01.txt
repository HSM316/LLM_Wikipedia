'''''Arigomphus''''' is a [[genus]] of [[dragonfly|dragonflies]] of the [[Gomphidae]] family.
This group is commonly called the '''pond clubtails'''. The species are fairly plain and only the males have the club-shaped [[abdomen]].<ref name="DDW">{{cite book|last=Paulson|first=Dennis R.|title=Dragonflies and Damselflies of the West|year=2009|publisher=Princeton University Press|isbn=0-691-12281-4}}</ref> Unlike other gomphids, they may emerge from artificial ponds.<ref name="abbott">{{Cite book
 |title=Dragonflies and Damselflies of Texas and the South-Central United States
 |last=Abbott |first=John C. |year=2005
 |publisher=[[Princeton University Press]]
 |isbn=0-691-11364-5 |page=179}}</ref> 

The genus is confined to North America.<ref name="DDW"/> It contains the following species:<ref>{{World Odonata List}}</ref><ref name="NAO">{{cite web|title=North American Odonata|url=http://www.pugetsound.edu/academics/academic-resources/slater-museum/biodiversity-resources/dragonflies/north-american-odonata/|publisher=University of Puget Sound|accessdate=5 August 2010|year=2009| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100711213624/http://www.pugetsound.edu/academics/academic-resources/slater-museum/biodiversity-resources/dragonflies/north-american-odonata/| archivedate= 11 July 2010 | url-status= live}}</ref>
*''[[Arigomphus cornutus]]'' {{small|(Tough, 1900)}} – horned clubtail
*''[[Arigomphus furcifer]]'' {{small|(Hagen in Selys, 1878)}} – lilypad clubtail
*''[[Arigomphus lentulus]]'' {{small|(Needham, 1902)}} – stillwater clubtail
*''[[Arigomphus maxwelli]]'' {{small|(Ferguson, 1950)}} – bayou clubtail
*''[[Arigomphus pallidus]]'' {{small|(Rambur, 1842)}} – gray-green clubtail
*''[[Arigomphus submedianus]]'' {{small|(Williamson, 1914)}} – jade clubtail
*''[[Arigomphus villosipes]]'' {{small|(Selys, 1854)}} – unicorn clubtail
