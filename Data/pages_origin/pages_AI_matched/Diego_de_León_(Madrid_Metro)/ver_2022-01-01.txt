'''Diego de León''' is a station on [[Line 4 (Madrid Metro)|Line 4]], [[Line 5 (Madrid Metro)|Line 5]], and [[Line 6 (Madrid Metro)|Line 6]] of the [[Madrid Metro]], located at the intersections of Francisco Silvela, Diego de León, and Conde de Peñalver streets in the [[Salamanca (Madrid)|Salamanca district]] of [[Madrid]]. It is in Zone A.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metromadrid.es/en/linea/linea-4#estacion-410|title=Línea 4|publisher=Metro de Madrid|language=en|access-date=21 March 2020|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.metromadrid.es/en/linea/linea-5#estacion-410|title=Línea 5|publisher=Metro de Madrid|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=6 September 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.metromadrid.es/en/linea/linea-6-circular#estacion-410|title=Línea 6 Circular|publisher=Metro de Madrid|language=en|url-status=live|access-date=5 September 2019}}</ref>

The station is named after Diego de León street, which in turn is named after the 19th-century Spanish military and political figure [[Diego de León, 1st Count of Belascoáin|Diego de León]].

== History ==
The station was inaugurated on 17 September 1932 as part of a branch line of [[Line 2 (Madrid Metro)|Line 2]] that ran from Diego de León to [[Goya (Madrid Metro)|Goya]], where it connected to the rest of Line 2. The branch was considered to be part of Line 2 until 1958, when it was transferred to Line 4.

In 1970, Line 5 was extended to Diego de León. The Line 5 platforms under Juan Bravo street were inaugurated on 26 February, and service began on 2 March.<ref>{{cite web|title=Portada del ABC del día 26 February 1970|trans-title=''ABC'' front page 26 February 1970|url=http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1970/02/26/001.html|access-date=17 July 2014|website=ABC|date=26 February 1970|language=Spanish}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Viaje inaugural|trans-title=Inaugural trip|url=http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1970/02/27/007.html|access-date=17 July 2014|website=ABC|date=27 February 1970|language=Spanish}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=El Ministro de Obras Públicas inaugura la nueva línea del Metro Callao-Ventas|trans-title=Minister of Public Works inaugurates new Metro line Callao-Ventas|url=http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1970/02/27/045.html|access-date=17 July 2014|website=ABC|date=27 February 1970|language=Spanish}}</ref> On 26 March 1973, Diego de León ceased to be a terminus station for Line 4 when the line was extended to [[Alfonso XIII (Madrid Metro)|Alfonso XIII]]. On 10 October 1979, the first stretch of Line 6 from [[Pacífico (Madrid Metro)|Pacífico]] to [[Cuatro Caminos (Madrid Metro)|Cuatro Caminos]] was inaugurated, including a stop at Diego de León.<ref>{{cite web|website=El País|title=El Rey inauguró la nueva línea de Metro entre Pacífico y Cuatro Caminos|language=Spanish|trans-title=King inaugurates new Metro line between Pacífico and Cuatro Caminos|url=http://elpais.com/diario/1979/10/11/madrid/308492658_850215.html|access-date=7 March 2014|date=11 October 1979}}</ref>
