'''''Cleistanthus collinus'''''<ref name = "C132"/> is a plant species first described by [[William Roxburgh|Roxburgh]], with its current name after [[George Bentham|Bentham]] and [[Joseph Dalton Hooker|Hooker]]; it is included in the family [[Phyllanthaceae]].<ref name = "COL"/><ref name = "source"/>  The [[IUCN]] categorizes this species as vulnerable.<ref name = "iucn"/>  No subspecies are listed in the [[Catalogue of Life]].<ref name = "COL"/>

==Properties==
''Cleistanthus collinus'' (Karra) contains a plant poison also called ''oduvan'' ([[Tamil language|Tamil]]), ''kadise'' ([[Kannada language|Kannada]]), ''Vadisaku'' ([[Telugu language|Telugu]]), ''Oduku'' ([[Malayalam]]) and Gaja Madara ([[Sinhala language|Sinhala]]) . Ingestion of its leaves or a decoction of its leaves causes [[hypokalemia]] ([[kaliuresis]] and [[cardiac arrhythmia]]s),<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Thomas | first1 = K | last2 = Dayal | first2 = AK | last3 = Narasimhan | first3 = Alka G | last4 = Seshadri | first4 = MS | last5 = Cherian | first5 = AM | last6 = Kanakasabapathi | first6 = Molly B | year = 1991 | title = Metabolic and Cardiac effects of Cleistanthus Collinus poisoning | journal = J Assoc Physicians India | volume = 39 | issue = 4| pages = 312–314 | pmid = 1938816 }}</ref> [[metabolic acidosis]], [[hypotension]] and [[Hypoxia (medical)|hypoxia]]<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Subrahmanyam | first1 = DK | last2 = Mooney | first2 = T | last3 = Raveendran | first3 = R | last4 = Zachariah | first4 = B. A | date = Nov 2003 | title = Clinical and laboratory profile of Cleistanthus collinus poisoning | journal = J Assoc Physicians India | volume = 51 | pages = 1052–4 | pmid = 15260387 }}</ref> probably due to [[distal renal tubular acidosis]], [[ARDS]] and toxin induced [[vasodilatation]] respectively.<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Eswarappa | first1 = S. | last2 = Chakraborty | first2 = A R | last3 = Palatty | first3 = B U | last4 = Vasnik | first4 = M | year = 2003 | title = Cleistanthus Collinus Poisoning: Case Reports and Review of the Literature | journal = Clinical Toxicology | volume = 41 | issue = 4| pages = 369–72 | doi=10.1081/clt-120022005| pmid = 12870879 | s2cid = 20225328 }}</ref><ref>Benjamin SPE, M Edwin Fernando, JJ Jayanth, Preetha B; Cleistanthus collinus poisoning. ''J Assoc Physicians India'' 2006 Sep; 54:742-44</ref><ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Nampoothiri | first1 = K | last2 = Chrispal | first2 = A | last3 = Begum | first3 = A | last4 = Jasmine | first4 = S | last5 = Gopinath | first5 = KG | last6 = Zachariah | first6 = A | date = Mar 2010 | title = A clinical study of renal tubular dysfunction in Cleistanthus collinus (Oduvanthalai) poisoning | journal = Clin Toxicol | volume = 48 | issue = 3| pages = 193–7 | doi=10.3109/15563651003641786| pmc = 2875161 | pmid=20397801}}</ref> Hypokalemia and acidosis probably also induces [[rhabdomyolysis]] resulting in [[Myoglobinuria|myoglobinuric]] [[kidney failure]] and [[neuromuscular]] weakness.<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Eswarappa | first1 = Benjamin SPE | date = Jan 2007 | title = Renal failure and neuromuscular weakness in Cleistanthus collinus poisoning | journal = J Assoc Physicians India | volume = 55 | pages = 85–86 | pmid = 17447300 }}</ref> Its effects are probably mediated by injury to the distal renal tubules, [[pulmonary]] [[epithelium]] and peripheral blood vessels due to [[glutathione]] depletion<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Sarathchandra | first1 = G | last2 = Balakrishnamoorthy | first2 = P | title = Acute toxicity of Cleistanthus collinus, an indigenous poisonous plant in Cavia procellus | journal = Journal of Environmental Biology | volume = 1998 | pages = 145–8 }}</ref> (animal studies have shown benefit with [[acetylcysteine|''N''-acetylcysteine]]).<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Annapoorani | first1 = KS | last2 = Damodaran | first2 = C | last3 = Chandrasekharan | first3 = P | year = 1986 | title = A promising antidote to Cleistanthus collinus poisoning | journal = J Sci Soc Ind | volume = 2 | pages = 3–6 }}</ref>
