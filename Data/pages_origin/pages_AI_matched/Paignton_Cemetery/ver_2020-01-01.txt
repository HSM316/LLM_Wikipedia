'''Paignton Cemetery''' is a burial ground situated within the town of [[Paignton]], [[Devon]], [[England]]. Notable people with memorials within the cemetery include the scientist [[Oliver Heaviside]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/13533147/oliver-heaviside|title=Oliver Heaviside (1850-1925)|website=Find A Grave|access-date=2018-12-14}}</ref> and [[Victoria Cross]] recipient [[William Harry Thomas Sylvester]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/8111454/william-harry_thomas-sylvester|title=William Harry Thomas Sylvester (1831-1920)|website=Find A Grave|access-date=2018-12-14}}</ref>

== Commonwealth War Graves ==
The cemetery contains 40 burials associated with the First World War and 43 graves from the Second World War.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cwgc.org/find-a-cemetery/cemetery/37444/PAIGNTON%20CEMETERY|title=Paignton Cemetery|website=www.cwgc.org|access-date=2018-12-14}}</ref> Just within the Ailescombe Road entrance there is a [[Cross of Sacrifice]] of the [[Commonwealth War Graves Commission]] erected to commemorate the dead of the First World War.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.iwm.org.uk/memorials/item/memorial/45924|title=Paignton Cemetery - Cwgc Cross|website=Imperial War Museums|language=en|access-date=2018-12-14}}</ref>

== The Torquay and Paignton Hebrew Congregation Cemetery ==
There is a section of the cemetery devoted to Jewish burials situated off Ailescombe Road.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iajgsjewishcemeteryproject.org/england/paignton-torbay-devon.html|title=PAIGNTON, Torbay, Devon: {{!}} england - International Jewish Cemetery Project|website=www.iajgsjewishcemeteryproject.org|access-date=2018-12-14}}</ref> There are approximately 90 people interred in this section.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jewishgen.org/jcr-uk/community/paignton/PaigntonCemetery.htm|title=JCR-UK: Paignton Jewish Cemetery, Torbay, Devon, UK|website=www.jewishgen.org|access-date=2018-12-14}}</ref>
