The '''2018 Australian Labor Party National Conference''' was the 48th triennial [[Australian Labor Party National Conference|National Conference]] of the [[Australian Labor Party]]. It took place in [[Adelaide]] from Sunday 16 to Tuesday 18 December 2018.<ref name=48Conf>[https://lab18.org.au/ Labor 48th National Conference]</ref> Over 2,000 people attended.<ref name=48Conf/>

==Scheduling of the conference==
The conference was originally scheduled for 26–28 July. However, on 24 May, the Liberal [[Speaker of the Australian House of Representatives|Speaker]] of the [[Australian House of Representatives|House of Representatives]], [[Tony Smith (Victorian politician)|Tony Smith]], announced that five by-elections — four were caused by the [[2017-18 Australian parliamentary eligibility crisis]] — would be held on 28 July. Labor accused the Speaker of being "disgraceful and sneaky" over the timing, and announced that it would postpone the conference to enable the party to campaign.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2018-05-24/by-elections-date-set-mayo-longman-braddon-perth-fremantle/9758576|title= Date set for by-elections in Perth, Fremantle, Mayo, Longman and Braddon|last=Yaxley|first=Louise|work=ABC Online|date=24 May 2018|accessdate=23 September 2018}}</ref> A week later, the new dates were announced.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2018/may/31/labor-reschedules-national-conference-for-december-after-super-saturday-clash|title= Labor reschedules national conference for December after Super Saturday clash |last=Murphy|first=Katharine |work=The Guardian (Australia)|date=31 May 2018|accessdate=23 September 2018}}</ref>
