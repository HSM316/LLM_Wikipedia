'''ERHC''', is a publicly traded American corporation with oil and gas assets in [[Sub-Saharan Africa]].<ref name=worldoil>{{cite news |title=ERHC Energy completes farm-out to CEPSA |date=February 11, 2014 |publisher=World Oil News |url=http://www.worldoil.com/ERHC-Energy-completes-farm-out-to-CEPSA.html |accessdate=July 19, 2014}}</ref><ref name=vanguard>{{cite news |title=ERHC gets presidential nod for Chadian oil blocks |date=May 6, 2014 |publisher=Vanguard |url=http://www.vanguardngr.com/2014/05/erhc-gets-presidential-nod-chadian-oil-blocks/ |accessdate=July 19, 2014}}</ref> The company has oil and gas exploration interests in the [[Kenya|Republic of Kenya]], [[Chad]] and [[São Tomé and Príncipe]]. Oil and gas explorations are also located in the Principe Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) and the Nigeria-São Tomé and Príncipe Joint Development Zone (JDZ).<ref name=worldoil />

==History==
ERHC, founded in 1986 in Colorado as the ''Environmental Remediation Holding Corporation'', notably completed an airborne Full Tensor Gravity (FTG) survey of Block 11A in northwestern Kenya in January 2014.<ref name=worldoil2>{{cite news |title=ERHC Energy completes FTG survey of Kenya block |date=January 7, 2014 |publisher=World Oil |url=http://www.worldoil.com/ERHC-Energy-completes-FTG-survey-of-Kenya-block.html |accessdate=July 19, 2014}}</ref> Bell Geospace flew the FTG on ERHC's behalf and acquired a total of 14,943.8 line km.<ref name=worldoil2 /> The results confirmed that the Lotikipi basin extends into Block 11A and provided the company’s exploration team with new information to aid in the identification of the most prospective areas in the area.<ref name=epmag>{{cite news |title=ERHC Completes Airborne FTG Survey in Kenya |date=January 6, 2014 |publisher=Exploration & Production |url=http://www.epmag.com/Exploration-Geology-Geophysics/ERHC-Completes-Airborne-FTG-Survey-Kenya_127671 |accessdate=July 19, 2014}}</ref>

That month ERHC contracted BGP Inc., a Chinese geophysical service company, to its northern Kenya exploration block and identify the best possible drilling spots.<ref name=dailynation>{{cite news |title=Plenty oil jobs coming; where are local experts? |author=Pauline Kairu |date=May 9, 2014 |publisher=Daily Nation |url=http://mobile.nation.co.ke/lifestyle/Plenty-oil-jobs-coming-local-experts/-/1950774/2308600/-/format/xhtml/-/uff0be/-/index.html |accessdate=July 19, 2014}}</ref><ref name=upstreamonline>{{cite news |title=ERHC hires BGP for Kenya 2D shoot |author=Bill Lehane |date=January 14, 2014 |publisher=Upstream |url=http://www.upstreamonline.com/live/article1348994.ece |accessdate=July 19, 2014}}</ref> The company set aside $40 million for the venture.<ref name=dailynation />

In February 2014, ERHC completed its [[farmout agreement]] with [[CEPSA]], a Spanish oil and gas company.<ref name=worldoil /> Following the agreement, CEPSA acquired a 55 percent stake in Block 11A.<ref name=worldoil /><ref name=oilandgastechnology>{{cite news |title=Cepsa-led joint venutre launches survey of onshore Kenyan block |author=Paddy Harris |date=April 23, 2014 |publisher=Oil & Gas Technology |url=http://www.oilandgastechnology.net/upstream-news/cepsa-led-joint-venture-launches-survey-onshore-kenyan-block |accessdate=July 19, 2014}}</ref> ERHC retained 35 percent of interest in Block 11A.<ref name=thestar>{{cite news |title=Exploration paces up for oil, gas in Kenya |author=James Waithaka |date=April 26, 2014 |work=The Star |url=http://www.the-star.co.ke/news/article-164702/exploration-paces-oil-gas-kenya |accessdate=July 19, 2014}}</ref>

Chadian President [[Idriss Déby]] gave ERHC his approval to retain oil exploration block BDS 2008 and voluntary relinquishment of the Manga and Chari-Ouest III Blocks in May 2014.<ref name=petroafrica>{{cite news |title=ERHC Receives Approval for BDS 2008 Retention in Chad |date=April 29, 2014 |publisher=Petroleum Africa |url=http://www.petroleumafrica.com/erhc-receives-approval-for-bds-2008-retention-in-chad/ |accessdate=July 19, 2014}}</ref> The company holds 100% of the interest in block BDS 2008 in southern [[Chad]] which encompasses 41800 square km.<ref name=vanguard /> After the approval, ERHC began using a series of convertible notes to fund a magnetic/gravity survey and a 2D [[seismology|seismic]] acquisition program.<ref name=petroafrica />

In 2015, ERHC partnered with Cepsa to further exploration in Kenya on Block 11A.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theunion.com.ng/energy/erhc-upbeat-with-exploration-in-africa/|title=ERHC Upbeat With Exploration In Africa|date=February 23, 2015|author=Sopuruchi Onwuka|accessdate=July 20, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150722040435/http://theunion.com.ng/energy/erhc-upbeat-with-exploration-in-africa/|archive-date=July 22, 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> That January, the company announced that it was continuing its exploration program in Kenya.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.businessdailyafrica.com/Swala-mulls-over-putting-Kisumu-oil-block-on-sale/-/539552/2597586/-/item/1/-/ecjr1jz/-/index.html|title=Swala mulls over putting Kisumu oil block on sale|date=January 21, 2015|accessdate=July 20, 2015}}</ref> ERHC discovered the Tarach-1 prospect-drilling site for the first exploration well in June 2015. 13 drillable prospects were identified in the basin.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.journaltranscript.com/2015/06/erhc-energy-inc-otcmktserhe-updates-shareholders-on-recent-developments/|title=ERHC Energy Inc. (OTCMKTS:ERHE) Updates Shareholders On Recent Developments|date=June 10, 2015|author=Kyle Lan|accessdate=July 20, 2015}}</ref>
