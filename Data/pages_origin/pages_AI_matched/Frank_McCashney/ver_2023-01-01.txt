'''Francis James "Frank" McCashney''' (9 August 1890 – 19 July 1951) was an [[Australian rules football]]er who played with {{AFL Ric}} in the [[Victorian Football League (1897–1989)|Victorian Football League]] (VFL).<ref name=encyc>{{cite book |title=The Encyclopedia of AFL Footballers: every AFL/VFL player since 1897 |last=Holmesby|first=Russell|last2=Main|first2=Jim |year=2014 |edition=10th |isbn=978-1-921496-32-5 |publisher=BAS Publishing |location=Seaford, Victoria}}</ref>

McCashney was the sixth of ten children born to Henry William McCashney and Mary Anne Robson. McCashney's family owned a sawmill at Trentham and he commenced his football career playing with the Trentham club.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/archive/news/maryborough-castlemaine-football-league/story-fn5bye9r-1225851788779?nk=cc8fc59f8be055b074d85de2d07b0d05 | title=Maryborough Castlemaine Football League |date=9 April 2010 |work=Herald Sun}}</ref> He made his debut for {{AFL Ric}} in 1909 and became a prominent player in their side during the pre-war period. He was a great stab kick who could send long driving left foot kicks downfield and in 1913 he represented Victoria against South Australia. Two of his brothers, [[John McCashney (footballer, born 1884)|John McCashney]] ([[Sydney Swans|South Melbourne]]) and [[Jim McCashney]] ({{AFL Haw}}), also played VFL football.

He married Daisy Ella Smith in 1916 and they had three children. They lived in Richmond and he worked as a mechanic after his football career. Frank McCashney died of [[cancer]] in 1951.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article58568826 |title=ALEXANDRA CLUB CHATTER. |newspaper=[[Alexandra, Eildon, Marysville Standard]] |location=Victoria |date=27 July 1951 |page=2 |via=National Library of Australia}}</ref>
