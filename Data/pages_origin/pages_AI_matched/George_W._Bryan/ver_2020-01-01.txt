'''George W. Bryan''' (born April 5, 1946) is an American businessman, former Chief Executive Officer of Sara Lee Foods, Senior VP of [[Sara Lee Corporation]], and founder of [[Old Waverly Golf Club]]. 

==Biography==
George Bryan was born in [[West Point, Mississippi]] in 1946. He graduated from West Point High School, where he then attended [[Mississippi State University]] in nearby [[Starkville, Mississippi|Starkville]]. Bryan completed his undergraduate degree in 1968 with a degree in Business Administration.<ref name="msstate2000">{{cite web|url=http://www.msstate.edu/newsroom/article/2000/02/george-bryan-mississippi-states-alumnus-year/ |title=George Bryan is Mississippi State's alumnus of the year &#124; Mississippi State University |website=Msstate.edu |date= |accessdate=2015-12-04}}</ref> 
===Bryan Foods===
After graduation Bryan began working for [[Bryan Foods]], his family's meat products manufacturing business based in West Point. Shortly after he began working, Bryan Foods was acquired by [[Sara Lee Corporation]]. After working in cost accounting, he became production manager and vice president of sales before being named president of Bryan Foods in 1974.<ref name="msstate2000"/>
===Sara Lee Corporation===
Bryan further advanced to the role of Senior Vice President of Sara Lee Corporation in 1983 and moved to the company's meat group offices in West Tennessee. Under Bryan's guidance Sara Lee's meat division produced more than $3.5 billion in annual sales. He continued working until retirement as Senior Vice President of [[Sara Lee Corporation]] and CEO of Sara Lee Foods.<ref name="equilar1">{{cite web|url=http://people.equilar.com/bio/george-bryan-regions-financial/salary/49878#.VmCPD3arSM8 |title=George W. Bryan - Executive Bio, Compensation History, and Contacts - Equilar Atlas |website=People.equilar.com |date=2015-01-23 |accessdate=2015-12-04}}</ref>
===Old Waverly Golf Club===
In 1988, George, his wife Marcia, and 29 founding members opened [[Old Waverly Golf Club]]. The course was designed by Jerry Pate and Bob Cupp. Old Waverly was well received throughout the country, being named a Top New Course in the United States by [[Golf Digest]]. Old Waverly continued to garner national acclaim until Bryan sought to host a major tournament. Old Waverly hosted the [[1999 U.S. Women's Open Golf Championship]] to nearly 130,000 attendees. The attendance is still the second highest in the history of the [[United States Women's Open Championship (golf)|U.S. Women's Open]]. The tournament is considered the most prestigious sporting event hosted in the state of Mississippi.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://msbusiness.com/2013/04/old-waverly-has-had-significant-economic-impact-since-it-opened/ |title=25 YEARS â€” Old Waverly has had significant economic impact since it opened - Mississippi Business Journal |website=Msbusiness.com |date= |accessdate=2015-12-04}}</ref>

Old Waverly has regularly been named the top golf course in Mississippi and a Top 100 course in the United States by [[Golf Digest]], [[Golfweek]], and other publications.

===Other endeavors===
Bryan has served in various advisory roles on the Board of Directors of numerous organizations, including:
* [[Regions Financial Corporation]]
* [[American Meat Institute]]
* [[Union Planters|Union Planters National Bank]], [[Memphis, Tennessee]]
* Danek Group, [[Memphis, Tennessee]]
* [[Mississippi State University]] Development Foundation
* Buckeye Technologies, Inc., [[Memphis, Tennessee]]

In addition, Bryan has developed residential and commercial real estate in Mississippi, Tennessee and Utah.<ref name="equilar1"/>
