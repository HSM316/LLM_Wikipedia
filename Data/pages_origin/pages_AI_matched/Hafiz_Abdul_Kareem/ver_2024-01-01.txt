'''Hafiz Abdul Kareem''' ({{Lang-ur|{{Nastaliq|حافظ عبدالکریم}}}}; born 1 January 1960) is a [[Pakistani]] politician who served as [[Ministry of Communications (Pakistan)|Minister for Communications]], in [[Abbasi cabinet]] from August 2017 to May 2018. He has been a member of the [[Senate of Pakistan]] since March 2018. Previously he had been a Member of the [[National Assembly of Pakistan]] from June 2013 to 2018.

==Early life and education==
He was born on 1 January 1960.

He has completed [[matriculation]] level education.<ref>{{cite web|title=MP Profile|url=http://openparliament.pk/mp-profile/?memberId=274|website=Open Parliament Pakistan|publisher=FAFEN|access-date=28 February 2018|date=26 January 2016|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180228223650/http://openparliament.pk/mp-profile/?memberId=274|archive-date=28 February 2018}}</ref>

==Political career==

He ran for the seat of the [[National Assembly of Pakistan]] as a candidate of [[Pakistan Muslim League (N)]] (PML-N) from [[NA-172 (Dera Ghazi Khan-II)|Constituency NA-172 (Dera Ghazi Khan-II)]] in [[2008 Pakistani general election]], but was unsuccessful.<ref>{{cite news|title=Results of two seats in D.G. Khan withheld|url=https://www.dawn.com/news/290155|access-date=8 April 2017|work=DAWN.COM|date=20 February 2008|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170408171314/https://www.dawn.com/news/290155|archive-date=8 April 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Money and tribes talk in NA-172 electioneering|url=https://www.dawn.com/news/941945|access-date=8 April 2017|work=DAWN.COM|date=30 November 2010|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170408171206/https://www.dawn.com/news/941945|archive-date=8 April 2017}}</ref><ref name="dawn/2may2013">{{cite news|title=Traditional rivals face off|url=https://www.dawn.com/news/795081/traditional-rivals-face-off|access-date=8 April 2017|work=DAWN.COM|date=2 May 2013|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170408171518/https://www.dawn.com/news/795081/traditional-rivals-face-off|archive-date=8 April 2017}}</ref> He received 41,894 votes and lost the seat to [[Farooq Leghari]].<ref>{{cite web|title=2008 election result|url=https://www.ecp.gov.pk/Documents/General%20Elections%202008/Report,%20General%20Election%202008,%20Vol-II.pdf|publisher=ECP|access-date=12 March 2018|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180105060230/https://ecp.gov.pk/Documents/General%20Elections%202008/Report,%20General%20Election%202008,%20Vol-II.pdf|archive-date=5 January 2018}}</ref>

He ran for the seat of the [[National Assembly]]  as a candidate of PML-N from Constituency NA-172 (Dera Ghazi Khan-II) in by-election held in March 2011, but was unsuccessful.<ref>{{cite news|title=Awais Leghari leading in NA-172 by-polls|url=https://www.dawn.com/news/616817|access-date=28 February 2018|work=DAWN.COM|date=29 March 2011|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180228223656/https://www.dawn.com/news/616817|archive-date=28 February 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Awais Leghari wins NA-172 by-election|url=https://nation.com.pk/30-Mar-2011/awais-leghari-wins-na172-byelection|access-date=28 February 2018|work=The Nation|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180312170229/https://nation.com.pk/30-Mar-2011/awais-leghari-wins-na172-byelection|archive-date=12 March 2018}}</ref> He received 41,894 votes and lost the seat to [[Awais Leghari]].<ref>{{cite web|title=2002 election result|url=https://www.ecp.gov.pk/ge/ge2002vol2.pdf|publisher=ECP|access-date=12 May 2018|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180126141507/https://www.ecp.gov.pk/ge/ge2002vol2.pdf|archive-date=26 January 2018}}</ref>

He was elected to the [[National Assembly]]  as a candidate of PML-N from Constituency NA-172 (Dera Ghazi Khan-II) in [[2013 Pakistani general election]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Friends or foes?|url=http://www.thefridaytimes.com/beta3/tft/article.php?issue=20130524&page=4|access-date=12 March 2018|work=The Friday Times|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130711050921/http://www.thefridaytimes.com/beta3/tft/article.php?issue=20130524&page=4|archive-date=11 July 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> He received 49,230 votes and defeated [[Jamal Leghari]].<ref>{{cite web|title=2013 election result|url=https://ecp.gov.pk/Documents/General%20Elections%202013%20report/Election%20Report%202013%20Volume-II.pdf|publisher=ECP|access-date=12 March 2018|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180201140612/https://ecp.gov.pk/Documents/General%20Elections%202013%20report/Election%20Report%202013%20Volume-II.pdf|archive-date=1 February 2018}}</ref>
