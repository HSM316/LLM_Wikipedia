'''Tes Lake''' is one of the largest lakes in [[Bengkulu|Bengkulu Province]], Indonesia. It is the main [[Hydroelectricity|hydroelectric]] supplier for nearly all of Bengkulu Province. The lake covers an area of approximately 750 [[hectare]]s.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Tes Lake - 4 Things to Know Before Visiting |url=https://www.travalour.com/attraction/5308-tes-lake#about |access-date=4 December 2023 |website=travalour.com}}</ref>

[[File:COLLECTIE TROPENMUSEUM Het meer van Tas TMnr 60014434.jpg|thumb|1926 photo of the lake]]

== Area ==
Tes Lake covers an area of 3,230 [[Hectare|hectares]]. <ref>{{Citation |title=Decree of Forestry Minister No. 385/Kpts-II/1985 |publication-date=December 27, 1985}}</ref>

==Flora==
Because of its hot and humid climate, crops such as [[Coffee production in Indonesia|coffee]] are mainly grown. Other florae that grow around the area are [[Shorea|Meranti]], [[Dyera costulata]], [[Cinnamomum porrectum|Kayu Gadis]], [[Alstonia scholaris|Pulai]], [[Melaleuca]], along with other plants that also grow elsewhere in Bengkulu Province.

==Location==
Tes Lake lies in the south of [[Lebong Regency]], [[Bengkulu]]. The nearby settlements include Karang Anyar, Kutai Donok (Central Village), Talang Ratu Village, and Tes Village.

=== Topography ===
Its elevation is {{convert|600|m|ft|0}} to {{convert|900|m|ft|0}} above sea level.

==Geology and climate==
Based on information from an exploration conducted in 1964, the main types of soil found in Tes Lake are regosol, andosol, latosol, and podzol (brown podzol soil).

Due to its high number of wet seasons, Tes Lake experiences an average annual rainfall intensity of 2,700-3,500mm. According to classifications from Schmidt and Ferguson, the area is classified as type A, with a Q value of approximately 0.9% - 7.7%. {{sfn|Usman|Suhartoyo|2021|p=2}}
