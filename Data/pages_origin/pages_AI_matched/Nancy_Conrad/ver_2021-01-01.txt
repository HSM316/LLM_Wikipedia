'''Nancy (Crane) Conrad''' (born September 25, 1942) is a teacher, author, publisher, entrepreneur, and public speaker. She is the wife of late astronaut [[Pete Conrad]] and Founder and Chair of the Conrad Foundation.

==Education and Early Career==
Nancy Conrad was born on September 25, 1942 in Denver, Colorado where she became a high school English teacher at George Washington High School. She later began work in the publishing industry as Associate Publisher for International Business Woman magazine. While at this publication, Conrad interviewed many notable women including [[Betty Ford]], [[Kay Koplovitz]], [[Mary Kay Ash]] and [[Caroline Rose Hunt]]. She also served as a contributing writer for the articles “ “A Century on Wheels: The Great American Race”<ref name="AmericanRace">{{cite journal|last1=Conrad|first1=Nancy|last2=D.C.|first2=Agle|title=The Great American Race|journal=A Century on Wheels|date=1993|pages=133–138|publisher=Rococo International|location=Largo, FL|language=English|format=Magazine}}</ref>  and “100 Years of Moving Pictures.”<ref name="100Movies">{{cite journal|last1=Conrad|first1=Nancy|title=Star Trek's Great Vision|journal=100 Years of Moving Pictures|date=1995|pages=164–169|publisher=Faircourt International|location=Tampa, FL|language=English|format=Magazine|issn=1359-5725}}</ref>

==Career==
In 1996, Nancy Conrad founded four companies with her husband [[Pete Conrad]] to make space travel more accessible to all individuals. These companies included Universal Space Network (USN), Universal Spacelines, Universal Spaceware, and Rocket Development Company. She was the Director of Communications for Universal Space Network which is now SSC Space US, Inc.<ref>{{cite web|title=SSC Space US|url=http://www.sscspace.com/sscspaceus|website=SSC Space US|publisher=Satellite Management Services Division|accessdate=29 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Mattingly replaces Conrad at Universal Space Network|url=http://aviationweek.com/awin/mattingly-replaces-conrad-universal-space-network|website=Aviation Weekly|publisher=Aerospace Daily|accessdate=29 June 2016}}</ref> Nancy also founded Universal Spaceworks, an astronaut licensing company representing 20 astronauts who worked in NASA. Universal Spaceworks produced educational products based on the exploration of space including the book, Heroes of Space, published in 1999 by Intervisual Books.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Agle|first1=D. C.|title=Heroes of Space: A Three-Dimensional Tribute to 40 Years of Space Exploration|date=1999|publisher=Intervisual Books|location=Santa Monica, CA|isbn=9781581170542|url=https://books.google.com/books/about/Heroes_of_Space.html?id=DaILAAAACAAJ|accessdate=29 June 2016|language=en}}</ref> Under Nancy Conrad’s leadership, Universal Spaceworks collaborated with Bandai America, Saban Entertainment’s toy licensee for the “Power Rangers in Space,” in 1998 to create a line of action figures called “Heroes of Space.” The line portrayed former astronauts of America’s space program including moonwalker [[Pete Conrad]] from [[Apollo 12]], [[Charles Duke]] from [[Apollo 16]], and [[Alan Bean]] from [[Apollo 12]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Archerd|first1=Army|title=Acad hosts Holocaust pic, speakers|url=https://variety.com/1998/voices/columns/acad-hosts-holocaust-pic-speakers-1117863240/|website=Variety|publisher=Variety Media, LLC|accessdate=29 June 2016|date=1998-04-16}}</ref>

Nancy Conrad co-developed ''One Giant Leap for Mankind'', a publication chosen by NASA as the official book for the 25th anniversary of the first lunar landing. She interviewed 11 Apollo Program astronauts for this publication.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Yehling|first1=Robert|title=Shades of Green|date=2006|publisher=Koboca Publishing|location=Tampa, FL|isbn=978-0-9744997-6-5|page=115}}</ref> She also co-founded Pepper Pike Graphix, an educational comic book publishing company that produced ''Moonshot: The Flight of Apollo 11'', used by filmmaker and actor [[Tom Hanks]] as a source of research for the 1998 [[HBO]] miniseries, ''[[From the Earth to the Moon (miniseries)|From the Earth to the Moon]]''
. Conrad co-authored her husband’s posthumous biography, ''Rocketman: Astronaut Pete Conrad's Incredible Ride to the Moon and Beyond'', with ''[[Space Cowboys]]'' screenwriter, [[Howard Klausner]]. ''Rocketman'' was published by Penguin Books USA in 2005.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Conrad|first1=Nancy|last2=Klausner|first2=Howard|title=Rocket Man|url=https://archive.org/details/rocketmanastrona00conr|url-access=registration|date=2005|publisher=New American Library|location=New York, New York|isbn=978-1-101-09953-7}}</ref>

Following Pete Conrad’s death after a motorcycle accident in 1999,<ref>{{cite news|last1=Wren|first1=Christopher|title=Pete Conrad, 69, the Third Man to Walk on the Moon, Dies After a Motorcycle Crash|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1999/07/10/us/pete-conrad-69-the-third-man-to-walk-on-the-moon-dies-after-a-motorcycle-crash.html?pagewanted=all|accessdate=29 June 2016|work=The New York Times|date=July 10, 1999}}</ref> Nancy Conrad shared Pete’s story with global audiences and in the [[Discovery Channel]] documentary ''[[Surfing the Healthcare Tsunami|Surfing the Healthcare Tsunami: Bring Your Best Board]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Surfing the Healthcare Tsunami: Bring Your Best Board|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2151254/|website=Internet Movie Database|publisher=IMDB|accessdate=29 June 2016}}</ref> When Nancy Conrad learned that her husband’s death was preventable, she co-founded the Community Emergency Healthcare Initiative through the Texas Medical Institute of Technology, to assist small and rural hospital emergency services.<ref name="JPSDisclosure">{{cite journal|last1=Sheridan|first1=Sue|last2=Conrad|first2=Nancy|last3=King|first3=Sorrel|last4=Dingman|first4=Jennifer|last5=Denham|first5=Charles|title=Disclosure Through Our Eyes|journal=Journal of Patient Safety|date=March 2008|volume=4|issue=1|pages=18–26|doi=10.1097/PTS.0b013e31816543cc}}</ref> The Pete Conrad Global Patient Safety Award was established by Nancy Conrad, which is awarded to extraordinary health care leaders and institutions that bring life saving solutions into frontline healthcare use.<ref name="JPSDisclosure" /> Some past winners of the award include Sorrel King, Susan Sheridan, Heather Foster, [[Gregory H. Botz]], [[William H. Adcox]], David Beshk, Perry Bechtle III, Charles Denham III, C. R. Denham II,<ref>{{cite web |title=Recent Recipients |url=https://peteconradaward.com/2018winners/ |website=Pete Conrad Global Patient Safety Award}}</ref> [[Donald Berwick]], Carolyn M. Clancy, Janet Corrigan, Peter Angood, Suzanne Delbanco, James Bagian, J. Michael Henderson, David Classen, Steven Swensen, David Hunt, Dan Ford, Arlene Salamendra, Becky Martins, Jennifer Dingman, Mary Foley, and Patti O'Regan.<ref>{{cite web |title=Prior Recipients |url=https://peteconradaward.com/prior-winners/ |website=Pete Conrad Global Patient Safety Award}}</ref>

In 2008, Nancy Conrad founded the Conrad Foundation to engage high school students across the globe to use STEM (science, technology, engineering and math), innovation and entrepreneurship to develop technologies and solutions to real-world needs.<ref>{{cite web|title=Merritt Island High To Participate In 2016 Innovation Challenge At Kennedy Space Center|url=http://spacecoastdaily.com/2015/10/merritt-island-high-to-participate-in-2016-innovation-challenge-at-kennedy-space-center/|website=Space Coast Daily|publisher=Maverick Multimedia|accessdate=29 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Teens head to NASA|url=http://www.couriermail.com.au/questnews/southwest/st-johns-anglican-college-sends-students-to-nasa/news-story/4ad64ae066d976c3f863c4369c44fc8a|accessdate=31 January 2017|publisher=The Courier Mail}}</ref> The organization’s flagship program, the Conrad Spirit of Innovation Challenge, is a competition challenging students to combine education, innovation, and entrepreneurship to create commercially viable products that address issues in sustainability.<ref>{{cite web|title=CONRAD SPIRIT OF INNOVATION - HEALTH & NUTRITION - 2016 FINAL ROUND|url=https://www.innovationportal.org/opportunities/34|website=Innovation Portal|publisher=Project Lead the Way|accessdate=29 June 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Stone|first1=Amanda|title=Science Fair 2016: Meet the Next Generation of America's Innovators|url=https://www.whitehouse.gov/blog/2016/04/08/science-fair-2016-meet-next-generation-americas-innovators|website=The White House|publisher=The White House|accessdate=29 June 2016|date=2016-04-08}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Nally|first1=Alicia|title=St John's Anglican College sends students to NASA|url=http://www.couriermail.com.au/questnews/southwest/st-johns-anglican-college-sends-students-to-nasa/news-story/4ad64ae066d976c3f863c4369c44fc8a|website=Quest Community Newspapers|publisher=Quest Newspapers|accessdate=29 June 2016|date=2016-04-07}}</ref>

Conrad is a published author and Editorial Advisory Board Member of the Journal of Patient Safety<ref>{{cite web|title=Renowned Experts Join Editorial Board of Journal of Patient Safety|url=http://wolterskluwer.com/company/newsroom/news/health/2012/08/renowned-experts-join-editorial-board-of-journal-of-patient-safety.html|website=Wolters Kluwer|publisher=Wolters Kluwer|accessdate=29 June 2016}}</ref> and a member of the Patient Engagement Board of the Global Patient Safety Team. She serves on the [[National Academy of Sciences|President’s Circle of the National Academies of Sciences]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Nancy E. Conrad|url=http://nas-sites.org/PC/2015/04/21/nancy-e-conrad/|website=The President's Circle|publisher=The National Academies of Sciences|accessdate=29 June 2016}}</ref> Explorer’s Club, and the [[Cosmos Club]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Heussner|first1=Ki Mae|title=The 12 Moonwalkers: Where Are They Now?|url=https://abcnews.go.com/Technology/Apollo11MoonLanding/12-moonwalkers-now-apollo-11-anniversary/story?id=8094239|accessdate=29 June 2016|work=ABC News|publisher=ABC|date=July 16, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Schmidt|first1=Klaus|title=This Week on the Space Show|url=http://spacefellowship.com/news/art26637/this-week-on-the-space-show.html|website=Space Fellowship|accessdate=29 June 2016}}</ref> Conrad has testified before the U.S. House of Representatives Committee on Science, Space and Technology discussing how the Conrad Foundation uses partnership and mentorship to improve STEM education.<ref>{{cite news|title=Nancy Conrad, Founder and Chairman, Conrad Foundation Written Testimony, September 13, 2011 U.S. House of Representatives Committee on Science, Space, and Technology|url=https://science.house.gov/sites/republicans.science.house.gov/files/documents/hearings/091311_Conrad.pdf|accessdate=29 June 2016|work=U.S. House of Representatives Committee on Science, Space, and Technology|publisher=U.S. House of Representatives Committee on Science, Space, and Technology|date=September 13, 2011}}</ref> Conrad also serves on the Advisory Board of the B612 Foundation. She is a founding partner of the Global Patient Safety team advisory Board. Conrad served as  a co-chair of the ''U.S. News & World Report'' STEMS Solutions 2012. She has been affiliated with the STEM Education Coalition, STEMconnector, the Center for the Study of the Presidency and Congress, and the Presidential Scholars. Conrad is currently a member of the [http://nsba.biz/ National Small Business Association (NSBA)] Leadership Council.

==Awards==
Conrad is a recipient of the Thomas Jefferson High School 2011 Tommy Award for her contributions to STEM education. In 2012, U.S. News & World Report and STEMconnector named Conrad one of 100 Top Women Leaders in STEM and inducted her into the 100 Top Women Leaders in STEM Hall of Fame.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Fraser|first1=Edie|title=100 Women Leaders in STEM|url=http://www.stemconnector.org/sites/default/files/100-Women-Leaders-in-STEM-WEB.pdf|website=STEMconnector|publisher=STEMconnector|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160404052849/http://www.stemconnector.org/sites/default/files/100-Women-Leaders-in-STEM-WEB.pdf|archivedate=2016-04-04}}</ref>
