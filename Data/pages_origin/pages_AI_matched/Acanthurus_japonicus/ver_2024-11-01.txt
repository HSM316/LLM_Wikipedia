'''''Acanthurus japonicus''''', the '''Japan surgeonfish''', '''white-faced surgeonfish''', '''gold rim tang''', '''powder brown tang''' and '''white-nose surgeonfish''', is a species of marine [[ray-finned fish]] belonging to the [[Family (biology)|family]] [[Acanthuridae]], the surgeonfishes, unicornfishes or tangs. This fish is found in the Western Pacific Ocean.

==Taxonomy==
''Acanthurus japonicus'' was first formally [[Species description|described]] as ''Hepatus aliala japonicus'' in 1931 by the Soviet ichthyologist [[Peter Yulevich Schmidt]] with its [[Type locality (biology)|type locality]] given as Kominato on [[Amami Ōshima|Amami-Oshima]] in the [[Ryukyu Islands]] of southern Japan.<ref name = CofF>{{Cof genus|genus=Acanthurus|access-date=15 September 2023}}</ref> The genus ''[[Acanthurus]]'' is one of two genera in the tribe [[Acanthurini]] which is one of three tribes in the [[subfamily]] [[Acanthurinae]] which is one of two subfamilies in the family Acanthuridae.<ref name = Nelson5>{{cite book  |title=Fishes of the World |edition=5th |author1=J. S. Nelson |author2=T. C. Grande |author3=M. V. H. Wilson |year=2016 |pages= 497–502 |publisher=Wiley |ISBN= 978-1-118-34233-6 }}</ref>

==Description==
''Acanthurus japonicus'' has a laterally compressed oval body with long based dorsal and anal fins.<ref name = Monaco>{{cite web |url=https://www.monaconatureencyclopedia.com/acanthurus-japonicus/?lang=en |title=''Acanthurus japonicus'' |access-date=15 September 2023 |author=Giuseppe Mazza |publisher=Monaco Nature Encyclopedia}}</ref> 9 spines and between 28 and 31 soft rays support the [[dorsal fin]] while the [[anal fin]] is supported by 3 spines and between 26 and 29 soft rays.<ref name = Fishbase/> The colour of this fish varies with the emotional state of the fish and may be brown, diffused with blue through to almost yellow but its most notable feature is a wide white band extending from the lower part of the orbit to the upper lip. There is a pink band along the soft-rayed part of the dorsal fin.<ref name = Monaco/> The [[caudal peduncle]] is black with a yellow base. The [[caudal fin]] is truncate<ref name = Fishbase/> and the [[pectoral fin]]s have yellow bases.<ref name = Monaco/> This species has a maximum published [[total length]] {{cvt|21|cm}}.<ref name = Fishbase/>

==Distribution and habitat==
''Acanthurus japonicus'' is found in the Western PacificOcean from southern Japan south to [[Sulawesi]] in Indonesia.<ref name = iucn/> This species occurs in small [[Shoaling and schooling|schools]] at depths between {{cvt|1|and|20|m}} in clear water on lagoon and seaward reefs.<ref name = Fishbase/>
