'''DreamWorks Channel''', commonly known as '''DreamWorks''', is a [[pay television]] network owned and operated by the [[NBCUniversal International Networks]] division of [[NBCUniversal]], a subsidiary of [[Comcast]]. First launched in [[Southeast Asia]] on 1 August 2015, it expanded to the rest of Asia as well as Europe, Africa and Oceania. Its programming is mainly sourced from [[DreamWorks Animation]].

Its counterpart in the United States is [[Universal Kids]], which launched on 9 September 2017 replacing Sprout TV, with the rights to DreamWorks Animation's programming in the country also handled by [[Netflix]] and other local networks such as DreamWorks' sister service [[Peacock (streaming service)|Peacock]].

== History ==
=== As a standalone TV Channel ===
[[DreamWorks Animation]] announced plans to launch its own TV channel on 9 December 2014 starting in Asia, rolling out over 19 countries in the second half of 2015. [[Singapore]]-based broadcaster [[HBO Asia]] was contracted to handle marketing, sales and technical services for the channel in Southeast Asia.<ref name="wsj">{{cite web|last=Schwartzel|first=Erich|title=DreamWorks Wants to Start Television Channels in Asia|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/dreamworks-wants-to-start-television-channels-in-asia-1418174876|access-date=22 April 2016|website=[[Wall Street Journal]]|date=9 December 2014|archive-date=22 March 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180322034402/https://www.wsj.com/articles/dreamworks-wants-to-start-television-channels-in-asia-1418174876|url-status=live}}</ref> The studio's first foray into television channel operations in any part of the world was represented with the launch of its first dedicated channel on 1 August 2015 in [[Thailand]] through a joint venture through with [[CTH (company)|CTH]].<ref name="vty">{{cite web|last=Frater|first=Patrick|title=DreamWorks Animation Launches TV Channel In Thailand|url=https://variety.com/2015/tv/asia/dreamworks-animation-launches-tv-channel-in-thailand-1201551924/|access-date=22 April 2016|work=[[variety (magazine)|Variety]]|publisher=[[Penske Media Corporation]]|date=28 July 2015|archive-date=27 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171227122207/https://variety.com/2015/tv/asia/dreamworks-animation-launches-tv-channel-in-thailand-1201551924/|url-status=live}}</ref>

A Middle East and North Africa feed of the channel launched on 1 August 2016 in partnership with the [[beIN Channels Network]].<ref name="ksn0">{{cite news|last=Foster|first=Elizabeth|date=29 July 2016|title=DreamWorks channel heads to MENA|url=http://kidscreen.com/2016/07/29/dreamworks-channel-heads-to-mena/|access-date=29 July 2016|work=Kidscreen|publisher=[[Brunico Communications]]|archive-date=10 December 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171210072241/http://kidscreen.com/2016/07/29/dreamworks-channel-heads-to-mena/|url-status=live}}</ref>

The channel was placed under the NBCU International executive vice president of Lifestyle and Kids, Duccio Donati, in August 2017, following the NBCU/Comcast takeover of DreamWorks Animation. This paired the channel with E! international channels and NBCU Lifestyle Channel content.<ref>{{cite web|title=NBCU EVP Duccio Donati Takes on DreamWorks Channel|url=http://www.animationmagazine.net/people/nbcu-evp-duccio-donati-takes-on-dreamworks-channel/|website=[[Animation Magazine]]|date=3 August 2017|access-date=4 August 2017|archive-date=4 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170804214548/http://www.animationmagazine.net/people/nbcu-evp-duccio-donati-takes-on-dreamworks-channel/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Duccio Donati tapped for new kids role at NBCUniversal|url=http://kidscreen.com/2017/08/04/duccio-donati-tapped-for-new-kids-role-at-nbcuniversal/|work=Kidscreen|publisher=[[Brunico Communications]]|date=4 August 2017|access-date=4 August 2017|archive-date=4 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170804220600/http://kidscreen.com/2017/08/04/duccio-donati-tapped-for-new-kids-role-at-nbcuniversal/|url-status=live }}</ref> On 1 January 2018, NBCUniversal International Networks took over HBO Asia's role with DreamWorks Channel in Southeast Asia.<ref>{{cite web|last=Frater|first=Patrick|title=NBCUniversal to Take Full Control of DreamWorks Channel in Asia|url=https://variety.com/2017/tv/asia/nbcuniversal-full-control-of-dreamworks-channel-in-asia-1202623340/|access-date=21 April 2020|work=[[variety (magazine)|Variety]]|publisher=[[Penske Media Corporation]]|date=27 November 2017|archive-date=4 August 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200804131549/https://variety.com/2017/tv/asia/nbcuniversal-full-control-of-dreamworks-channel-in-asia-1202623340/|url-status=live }}</ref>

The channel launched on [[KPN]] in the Netherlands on 23 July 2019.<ref name="nl">{{cite web|title=KPN is launching DreamWorks channel in The Netherlands|url=https://www.broadbandtvnews.com/2019/07/22/kpn-is-launching-dreamworks-channel-in-the-netherlands/|work=BroadbandTVNews.com|author=Robert Briel|date=22 July 2019|access-date=24 July 2019|archive-date=24 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190724113523/https://www.broadbandtvnews.com/2019/07/22/kpn-is-launching-dreamworks-channel-in-the-netherlands/|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Programming==
The content on the channel mainly consists of productions from the libraries of [[DreamWorks Animation Television]] and [[DreamWorks Classics]] including some of the former's content originally produced for [[Netflix]];<ref name="wsj"/> films and specials from DreamWorks Animation, Illumination and sometimes from third parties only broadcasts in the international versions.<ref name="thr">{{cite web|last=Bond|first=Paul|title=DreamWorks Animation to Launch TV Channel in Asia|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/dreamworks-animation-launch-tv-channel-755631|access-date=22 April 2016|website=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|date=9 December 2014|archive-date=29 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180629023717/https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/dreamworks-animation-launch-tv-channel-755631|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Programming blocks===
*'''''DreamWorks Junior''''' - This block is composed of shows targeting preschool-age children every morning and is a direct competitor to [[Paramount Global]]'s [[Nick Jr. Channel]], [[The Walt Disney Company]]'s [[Disney Junior]] and [[Warner Bros. Discovery]]'s [[Cartoonito]].<ref name="vty" /> Programs currently seen in this block include ''[[Guess with Jess]]'', ''[[Raa Raa the Noisy Lion]]'' and ''[[3-2-1 Penguins!]]''.
