'''Frederick Martin''' (died 1932) was an English [[association football|footballer]] who played as an [[inside right]] for [[South Kirkby Colliery F.C.|South Kirkby]], [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]], [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] and [[Raith Rovers F.C.|Raith Rovers]]

==Playing career==
Martin began his football career with South Kirkby<ref name="History">{{cite web|title=South Kirkby Colliery (Almost) Complete History|url=http://www.southkirkbycolliery.co.uk/player-a-z|publisher=Matthew Thomas|accessdate=31 March 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180715040026/http://www.southkirkbycolliery.co.uk/player-a-z|archive-date=15 July 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> before being signed by Barnsley in May 1909.<ref>{{cite news|title=Player's Movements|publisher=Leeds Mercury|date=3 May 1909|page=7}}</ref> He played at either inside right or centre forward for Barnsley.<ref>{{cite news|title=Mexborough and South Kirkby Players For Barnsley|publisher=Sheffield Daily Telegraph|date=3 May 1909|page=12}}</ref> After three seasons at Oakwell he next moved to Sunderland in February 1913<ref>{{cite news|title=Big Crowd Will See Dundee|publisher=Dundee Courier|date=15 February 1913|page=7}}</ref> and then to Raith Rovers.<ref>{{cite news|title=Raith Rovers New Centre|publisher=Dundee Evening Telegraph|date=13 February 1913|page=5}}</ref> By April Martin found himself in the final of the Scottish Cup with Raith but ended up on the losing side.<ref>{{cite news|title=Raith Rovers Fall At Last Hurdle|publisher=Dundee Courier|date=14 April 1914|page=6}}</ref> In 1914 Martin scored two goals for Raith Rovers in a Scottish Cup win over the then mighty Hearts, where he broke a tooth in doing so.<ref>{{cite news|title=What Martin Told The Rovers|publisher=Dundee Evening Telegraph|date=10 February 1914|page=5}}</ref> His exploits for Raith led to him being heavily linked to a return to Sunderland or a transfer to Hearts or Celtic.<ref>{{cite news|title=Hearts Celtic and Sunderland|publisher=Dundee Courier|date=25 February 1914|page=7}}</ref>

Martin died in January 1932 at the age of 43.<ref>{{cite news|title=Death of Fred Martin|newspaper=Yorkshire Post and Leeds Intelligencer|date=8 January 1932|page=15}}</ref>
