'''Nick Broad''' was an English football nutritionist and worked for some of Britain's biggest football clubs including [[Blackburn Rovers F.C.|Blackburn Rovers]], [[Birmingham City F.C.|Birmingham City]], [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea Football Club]] and in Paris, he worked for [[Paris St-Germain]].<ref>{{cite news|title=PSG staff member Nick Broad killed in car accident|url=http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/soccer/news/20130119/psg-nick-broad-killed.ap/index.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130710025513/http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/soccer/news/20130119/psg-nick-broad-killed.ap/index.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=10 July 2013|publisher=SI.com|accessdate=25 January 2013|date=19 January 2013}}</ref> Broad was a close friend of former Chelsea manager, [[Carlo Ancelotti]]. He graduated from [[Aberdeen University]]. Aged 38, he died on 19 January 2013 of an accidental traffic collision.<ref>{{cite web|title=Plymouth man who trained some of top footballers has died in crash|url=http://www.thisisplymouth.co.uk/Crash-claims-life-inspirational-brilliant-Nick/story-17928202-detail/story.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130505064501/http://www.thisisplymouth.co.uk/Crash-claims-life-inspirational-brilliant-Nick/story-17928202-detail/story.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=5 May 2013|publisher=Plymouth Herald|accessdate=25 January 2013}}</ref>
