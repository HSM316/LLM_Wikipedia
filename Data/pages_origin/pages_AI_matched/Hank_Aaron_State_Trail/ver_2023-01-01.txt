The '''Hank Aaron State Trail''' is a {{Convert|15.2|mi|km|adj=on}}<ref>{{Cite book |title=Rail-Trails: Michigan & Wisconsin |publisher=Wilderness Press |year=2017 |isbn=9780899978734 |location=Birmingham, AL |pages=44 |language=en |author-link=Rails-to-Trails Conservancy}}</ref> [[rail trail]] in [[Milwaukee County, Wisconsin]].

The trail is named for former [[Milwaukee Braves]] and [[Milwaukee Brewers]] [[right fielder]] [[Hank Aaron]],<ref name=dnr>{{cite web|url=http://dnr.wi.gov/topic/parks/name/hankaaron/ |title=Hank Aaron State Trail |publisher=Wisconsin DNR |access-date=August 15, 2016}}</ref> and was built on a former roadbed of the [[Chicago, Milwaukee, St. Paul and Pacific Railway]].

==History==
Following a recommendation by then-mayor [[John Norquist]]'s Bicycle Task Force to develop an east-west trail through the Menomonee Valley, the trail opened in 2000 with a length of only a third of a mile, near [[American Family Field]], though a segment between 13th Street and 25th Street was added shortly after.<ref name="Maher">{{Cite book |last=Maher |first=Rothenbueler |title=Milwaukee County's Oak Leaf Trail: A History |publisher=[[Arcadia Publishing]] |year=2019 |isbn=9781467140683 |location=Charleston, SC |language=en}}</ref>{{Rp|61}} In August 2006, the trail stretched from [[Lakeshore State Park]] in the east to American Family Field in the west.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Ceremony to mark expansion of Hank Aaron State Trail |url=https://dnr.wi.gov/news/Weekly/article/?id=95 |access-date=2022-12-19 |date=2006-08-08 |last=Cook |first=Melissa |website=Wisconsin DNR |language=en}}</ref> In 2010, a four-mile westward extension to 94th Place was completed, setting the total trail length to ten miles.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Hank Aaron State Trail Valley Passage and Extension |url=https://thesigmagroup.com/hank-aaron-state-trail-valley-passage-extension/ |access-date=2022-12-19 |website=The Sigma Group |language=en-US}}</ref> In November 2011, the trail was expanded from 94th Place to Underwood Parkway, though this section was composed of compacted gravel.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Hank Aaron State Trail extension ready for finishing touches |url=https://archive.jsonline.com/blogs/lifestyle/133370153.html |access-date=2022-12-19 |date=2011-11-07 |last=Held |first=Tom |website=Milwaukee Journal Sentinel |language=en}}</ref> In 2012, a one-mile spur connecting to [[Mitchell Park Horticultural Conservatory]] was added.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2012-08-30 |title=Ground broken on new segment of Hank Aaron State Trail |url=https://www.fox6now.com/news/ground-broken-on-new-segment-of-hank-aaron-state-trail |access-date=2022-12-20 |website=FOX 6 Now Milwaukee |language=en-US}}</ref> In 2014, a ramp and stairway were added to the trail near 6th Street to allow easy access to street level.<ref name="faq">{{Cite web |title=Frequently Asked Questions |url=https://www.hankaaronstatetrail.org/frequently-asked-questions |access-date=2022-12-20 |website=Friends of Hank Aaron State Trail |language=en-US}}</ref> In 2018, the compacted gravel section of the trail west of 94th Place was paved with the completion of the reconstruction of the [[Zoo Interchange]].<ref name="faq" />

In 2020, the trail was designated part of [[U.S. Bicycle Route 30]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Wisconsin bicycle route recognized by national organization |url=https://www.hngnews.com/lodi_enterprise/news/regional/wisconsin-bicycle-route-recognized-by-national-organization/article_1101efdd-299d-5b87-ad94-4aa991cf6b71.html |date=2020-08-29 |access-date=2022-12-19 |website=Hometown News LP |language=en}}</ref>

==Route==
The trail begins on the shore of [[Lake Michigan]] in [[Lakeshore State Park]] ({{coord|43.03723|-87.89749|format=dms|type:landmark_region:US-WI|display=inline|name=Hank Aaron State Trail, eastern trailhead}}). The trail travels west through Milwaukee, passing by the [[Harley-Davidson Museum]], [[Potawatomi Hotel & Casino]], [[American Family Field]], and the [[Wisconsin State Fair Park]]. The trail ends at the [[Oak Leaf Trail]] in [[Wauwatosa, Wisconsin]] ({{coord|43.03605|-88.06658|format=dms|type:landmark_region:US-WI|display=inline|name=Hank Aaron State Trail, western trailhead}}).<ref name="map">{{cite web|url=https://www.hankaaronstatetrail.org/s/HASTMAP2021_Final_022621.pdf |title=Hank Aaron State Trail Map |publisher=Friends of Hank Aaron State Trail |access-date=December 18, 2022}}</ref>

==Access==
The trail is open to walkers, joggers, bicyclists, and in-line skaters.<ref name=dnr/> Much of the trail is accessible to people with disabilities.<ref name=dnr/>
