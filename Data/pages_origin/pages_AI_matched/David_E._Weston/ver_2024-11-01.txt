'''David E. Weston''' (19 January 1929 – 16 November 2001) was an English [[physicist]], who worked at the [[Admiralty Research Establishment]]. During his early career he worked with [[Albert Beaumont Wood|A B Wood]], and is best known for his contributions to [[underwater acoustics]]. He published more than 65 papers, including 32 in the [[Journal of the Acoustical Society of America]], and was awarded the [[Rayleigh Medal]] by the [[Institute of Acoustics (United Kingdom)|Institute of Acoustics]] in 1970 and the [[ASA Silver Medal|Helmholtz-Rayleigh Interdisciplinary Silver Medal]] by the [[Acoustical Society of America]] in 1998.<ref>{{Cite journal|date=1999-01-01|title=Acoustical News—USA|url=https://asa.scitation.org/doi/10.1121/1.4734351|journal=The Journal of the Acoustical Society of America|volume=105|issue=1|pages=1–5|doi=10.1121/1.4734351|issn=0001-4966|doi-access=free}}</ref> Weston was president of the UK Institute of Acoustics between 1982 and 1984.<ref>{{cite journal |last=Ainslie |first=Michael A. |title=Obituaries: David E. Weston 1929-2001 |journal=J. Acoust. Soc. Am. |volume=110 |issue=2 |pages=647 |doi=10.1121/1.1385896}}</ref>
