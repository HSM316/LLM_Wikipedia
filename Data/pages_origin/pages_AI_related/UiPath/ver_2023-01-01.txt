'''UiPath''' is a global [[software]] company that makes [[robotic process automation]] (RPA) software. It was founded in [[Bucharest]], Romania, by [[Daniel Dines]] and Marius Tîrcă. Its headquarters are in [[New York City]]. The company's software monitors user activity to automate repetitive front and back office tasks, including those performed using other business software such as [[customer relationship management]] or [[enterprise resource planning]] (ERP) software.

In December 2020, the company filed confidentially for an initial public offering, and became public on April 21, 2021.

== History ==
UiPath was founded in 2005 in [[Bucharest, Romania]] as DeskOver, by Romanian entrepreneurs [[Daniel Dines]] and Marius Tîrcă.<ref name=business1>{{cite web|url=http://business-review.eu/news/the-story-of-uipath-how-it-became-romanias-first-unicorn-164248 |title=The story of UiPath – How did it become Romania's first unicorn? |website=Business Review |date=September 4, 2018 |access-date=March 3, 2021}}</ref>

In 2013, the company released the first UiPath Desktop Automation product line, which gave companies RPA tools to automate manual and repetitive back office tasks.<ref name=business1/>

In 2015, the company changed its name to UiPath.<ref name=business1/> Also in 2015, after receiving seed funding from [[Accel Partners]] and earlier investors, the company also opened offices in [[London]], [[New York City]], [[Bangalore]], [[Paris]], [[Singapore]], [[Washington, D.C.]], and [[Tokyo]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.romania-insider.com/romanian-tech-startup-uipath-raises-usd-30-mln-develop-intelligent-software-robots/|title=Romanian tech startup UiPath raises USD 30 mln to develop intelligent software robots|website=Romania-Insider.com|access-date=March 9, 2018}}</ref><ref name=business1/>

By April 2016, the company had released its Front Office and Back Office Server suites, and also released its Studio Community Edition. Within six months, the company had 10,000 active members, and more than 250 enterprise customers.<ref name=business1/>

In 2017, UiPath reported 590 employees and moved its headquarters to New York to be closer to its international customer base.<ref name=":1">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/reuters/2018/03/06/technology/06reuters-europe-venturecapital-romania.html|title=Romanian Firm Emerges as Leader in Automating Business Tasks|date=March 6, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180306184325/https://www.nytimes.com/reuters/2018/03/06/technology/06reuters-europe-venturecapital-romania.html|archive-date=March 6, 2018|url-status=dead|agency=Reuters}}</ref>

In June 2019, research firm [[Gartner]] announced that from 2017 to 2018 UiPath moved from #5 to #1 in RPA market share.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2019-06-24-gartner-says-worldwide-robotic-process-automation-sof|title= Gartner Says Worldwide Robotic Process Automation Software Market Grew 63% in 2018|website=www.gartner.com|date=June 24, 2019|access-date = March 3, 2021}}</ref> In September, UiPath was ranked #3 on the Forbes Cloud 100.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/cloud100/|title=Forbes Cloud 100|website=www.forbes.com|access-date=11 September 2019}}</ref> The company was the featured cover story of the September 30, 2019 Forbes print edition with UiPath CEO Daniel Dines called "Boss of the Bots."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://forbes.com/cloud100/|title= From Communism To Coding: How Daniel Dines Of $7 Billion UiPath Became The First Bot Billionaire |website=www.forbes.com|access-date=11 September 2019}}</ref>

In October 2019, UiPath announced the acquisition of Ukrainian process [[documentation]] company StepShot and Dutch [[process mining]] company ProcessGold.<ref name="venture2">{{cite web|url =https://venturebeat.com/2019/10/15/uipath-acquires-processgold-and-stepshot-to-help-businesses-identify-rpa-opportunities/|title=UiPath acquires ProcessGold and StepShot to help businesses identify RPA opportunities|website=VentureBeat|date=October 15, 2019|access-date=March 3, 2021}}</ref> Also in October, the company announced UiPath Explorer, a new product using technology from the acquired companies; a robot communication tool called UiPath Apps; a low code robot programming tool called UiPath StudioX; an embedded analytics tool called UiPath Insights; and UiPath Connect, a tool that allowed every employee to find new processes to automate.<ref name="venture2" /><ref name="silicon1">{{cite web|url=https://siliconangle.com/2019/10/15/uipath-debuts-new-tools-find-automate-business-processes/ |title=UiPath announces new tools and acquisitions to automate more business processes |website=Silicon Angle |date=October 15, 2019 |access-date=March 3, 2021}}</ref>

In November of the same year, the company reported it had 5,000 customers worldwide, and a developer community of more than 500,000 people.<ref name="strategy1">{{cite web|url=https://www.strategy-business.com/article/Making-automation-easy|title=PwC Strategy+Business: Automation Made Easy|website=Strategy + Business|date=November 20, 2019|access-date=March 3, 2021}}</ref> In November, the company was ranked first in the Deloitte Technology Fast 500.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www2.deloitte.com/us/en/pages/technology-media-and-telecommunications/articles/fast500-winners.html|title=2019 Technology Fast 500 winners|website=www.deloitte.com|access-date=November 6, 2019}}</ref>

In April 2020, UiPath was named the top tech company and #2 overall in the Financial Times FT1000 ranking of Americas' fastest growing companies 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/americas-fastest-growing-companies-2020|title=2020 FT1000 winners|website=www.ft.com|date=April 6, 2020|access-date=6 April 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/b0a5e02e-7412-11ea-ad98-044200cb277f|title=FT ranking: the Americas' fastest-growing companies |last=Kelly |first=Maxine |date=2020-04-06 |website=www.ft.com|language=en-GB|access-date=2020-04-17}}</ref> In June, UiPath was named to the CNBC 2020 Disruptor 50 ranking.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2020/06/16/uipath-disruptor-50.html|title=2020 CNBC Disruptor 50|website=www.cnbc.com|access-date=13 June 2020}}</ref> In September, UiPath was ranked #3 on the Forbes Cloud 100 list for the second consecutive year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/cloud100/#37c358225f94|title="Forbes Cloud 100 2020"|website=Forbes|language=en-US}}</ref> On December 17, Bloomberg reported that UiPath filed confidentially for an initial public offering (IPO).<ref name=bloomberg1>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-12-17/uipath-is-said-to-be-close-to-filing-confidentially-for-ipo|title="UiPath Says It Filed Confidentially for IPO, Could Be Valued at More Than $20 Billion"|newspaper=Bloomberg.com|date=December 17, 2020|language=en-US}}</ref>

In March 2021, UiPath acquired [[Cloud Elements]], an interconnectivity platform to advance API-based automation capabilities.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Busvine |first=Douglas |title=IPO contender UiPath extends automation offering with Cloud Elements buy |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-cloudelements-m-a-uipath/ipo-contender-uipath-extends-automation-offering-with-cloud-elements-buy-idUSKBN2BF2A1 |date=March 23, 2021 |website=[[Reuters]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Deutscher |first=Maria |title=UiPath acquires Cloud Elements to advance API-based automation in the enterprise |url=https://siliconangle.com/2021/03/23/uipath-acquires-cloud-elements-advance-api-based-automation-enterprise/ |date=March 23, 2021 |website=SiliconANGLE}}</ref>

In April 2021, UiPath raised $1.3 billion in an initial public offering on the [[New York Stock Exchange]] in one of the largest US software IPOs in history.<ref name=CNBC>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2021/04/21/uipath-rises-17percent-in-nyse-debut-after-one-of-top-software-ipos-ever.html |title=UiPath rises 23% in NYSE debut? |website=CNBC |date=April 27, 2021 |access-date=April 27, 2021}}</ref>

In July 2022, UiPath acquired Re:infer, a [[natural language processing]] developer, for an undisclosed sum.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/automation-tech-company-uipath-acquires-ai-startup-re-infer-11659393146 |title=Automation Tech Company UiPath Acquires AI Startup Re:infer |date=August 1, 2022 |work=[[The Wall Street Journal]] |last=Loten |first=Angus |url-access=limited}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=UiPath acquires London-based NLP startup Re:infer |url=https://techcrunch.com/2022/08/01/uipath-acquires-london-based-nlp-startup-reinfer/ |date=August 1, 2022 |website=TechCrunch |last=Sawers |first=Paul}}</ref>

==Products==
UiPath develops software to automate repetitive digital tasks normally performed by people. The technology combines emulating how humans read computer screens (AI computer vision), together with [[API]]s, and gives users access to pre-built automation components that can be combined to automate routine processes.<ref name=strategy1/> Its earlier products simplified tasks performed using other business software such as CRM or ERP systems, in internal and back-office areas like accounting, human resources paperwork, and claims processing.<ref name=venture1>{{cite web|url= https://venturebeat.com/2019/06/22/uipath-rpa-and-ai-will-be-commoditized-productivity-tools-within-5-years/ |title=UiPath: RPA and AI will be commoditized productivity tools within 5 years |website=Venturebeat |date=June 22, 2019 |access-date=March 3, 2021}}</ref><ref name="tech1">{{Cite web|date=April 30, 2019|title=UiPath nabs $568M at a $7B valuation to bring robotic process automation to the front office|url=https://techcrunch.com/2019/04/30/uipath-rpa-series-d/|access-date=March 2, 2021|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US}}</ref> Newer applications of the company's software include coordinating with artificial intelligence systems to simplify repetitive front office tasks including customer management.<ref name=tech1/> UiPath's built-in OCR engines from Google, Microsoft, and ABBYY can read information from a screen or scanned document.{{citation needed|date=February 2022}}

UiPath's main product is the UiPath Automation Platform.<ref name="tech4">{{cite web|date=February 1, 2021|title=Robotic process automation platform UiPath raises $750M at $35B valuation|url=https://techcrunch.com/2021/02/01/robotic-process-automation-platform-uipath-raises-750m-at-35b-valuation/|access-date=March 3, 2021|website=Techcrunch}}</ref> The platform combines a family of low-code visual [[integrated development environment]] (IDE) products called Studio for process creation, with client-side agents called Robots that execute those processes. The processes are deployed, monitored and managed remotely with a central management tool called Orchestrator.<ref name=infoworld1>{{Cite web|url=https://www.infoworld.com/article/3393444/review-uipath-aces-robotic-process-automation.html|title=Review: UiPath aces robotic process automation|website=Infoworld|language=en-US|date=May 6, 2019|access-date = March 3, 2021}}</ref>

Additional available software includes:

*UIPath AI Center (previously AI Fabric) - orchestrates and inserts AI into business processes.<ref name=venture1/>{{citation needed|date=March 2021}}
*UiPath Action Center - a communication tool for instances where robots need to connect with humans for direction, described by the company as “human in the loop.”<ref name=silicon1/>{{citation needed|date=March 2021}}
*UiPath Apps - a low-code application development platform.
*UiPath Assistant - a launchpad for automations that a company provides to its computer workers. It sits on the computer desktop and gives people easy access to selected automations that help them with their tasks.<ref name=computer1>{{cite web|url=https://www.computerweekly.com/blog/CW-Developer-Network/UiPath-robots-calmly-hyper-over-hyperautomation |title=UiPath robots 'calmly hyper' over hyperautomation |website=Computer Weekly |date=May 13, 2020 |access-date=March 2, 2021}}</ref>
*UiPath Automation Hub (previously UiPath Connect Enterprise) - a tool allows every employee at an organization to help find, suggest and track new processes to automate.<ref name=silicon1/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://business-review.eu/tech/uipath-extends-rpa-market-leadership-readies-for-hyperautomation-208170 |title=UiPath Extends RPA Market Leadership, Readies for Hyperautomation |website=Business Review |date=February 21, 2021 |access-date=March 3, 2021}}</ref>
*UiPath Document Understanding - software that extracts, interprets and processes data from PDFs, images, handwriting and other communication media.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.information-age.com/deloitte-uipath-partner-deliver-intelligent-document-processing-123489324/ |title=Deloitte and UiPath partner to deliver Intelligent Document Processing |website=Information Age |date=May 4, 2020 |access-date=March 3, 2021}}</ref>
*UiPath Insights - a modular data dashboard that provides analytics for UiPath's automated processes.<ref name=silicon1/>
*UiPath Process Mining (previously ProcessGold) - a tool that uncovers new automation opportunities by analyzing application logs.<ref name=computer1/>
*UiPath StudioX - a platform with simplified coding that allows workers to build their own robots to simplify their own work.<ref name=silicon1/>
*UiPath Test Suite - automates and centralizes testing to ensure the quality of every automation and application before they go live.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://aithority.com/robots/automation/uipath-now-certified-for-sap-solution-manager-test-automation/ |title=UiPath Now Certified for SAP Solution Manager Test Automation |website=AiThority |date=August 5, 2020 |access-date=March 3, 2021}}</ref>

UiPath's software products are available as native software or using the [[Software as a Service]] (SaaS) model.<ref name=venture2/><ref name=infoworld1/>

UiPath also hosts the UiPath Academy, to provide job training and certification in the field of Robotic Process Automation.<ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.cxoinsightme.com/news/uipath-extends-its-online-rpa-academy-and-certification/|title=UiPath extends its online RPA academy and certification| last1=Lunden |first1=Ingrid |website=CXO Insight Middle East|date=April 3, 2020|access-date=March 3, 2021}}</ref>
