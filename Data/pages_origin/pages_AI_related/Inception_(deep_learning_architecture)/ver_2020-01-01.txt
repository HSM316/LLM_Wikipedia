Inceptionv3<ref>{{cite book |last1=Tang |title=Intelligent Mobile Projects with TensorFlow |date=May 2018 |publisher=Packt Publishing |isbn=9781788834544 |pages=Chapter 2 |url=https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/intelligent-mobile-projects/9781788834544/}}</ref> is the name of a [[Dataset (machine learning)|data set]] sometimes used in a [[Convolutional_neural_network]] for assisting in [[Image_analysis]] and [[Object_detection]], and got its start as a module for [https://www.mathworks.com/help/deeplearning/ref/googlenet.html GoogLeNet]. Just as [[ImageNet]] can be thought of as a database of classified visual objects, Inception helps classification of objects<ref>{{cite book |last1=Karim and Zaccone |title=Deep Learning with TensorFlow |date=March 2018 |publisher=Packt Publishing |isbn=9781788831109 |pages=Chapter 4 |url=https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/deep-learning-with/9781788831109/}}</ref> in the world of [[Computer_vision]]. One such use is in Life Sciences, where it aids in the research of Leakumia<ref>{{cite web |last1=Milton-Barker |first1=Adam |title=Inception V3 Deep Convolutional Architecture For Classifying Acute Myeloid/Lymphoblastic Leukemia |url=https://software.intel.com/en-us/articles/inception-v3-deep-convolutional-architecture-for-classifying-acute-myeloidlymphoblastic |website=intel.com |publisher=Intel |accessdate=2 February 2019}}</ref>. As referenced at [[DeepDream]] it was "codenamed 'Inception' after the film of the same name".
