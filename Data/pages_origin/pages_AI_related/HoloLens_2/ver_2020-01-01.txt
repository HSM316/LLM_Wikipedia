'''Microsoft HoloLens 2''' is a pair of [[mixed reality]] [[smartglasses]] developed and manufactured by [[Microsoft]]. It is the successor to the pioneering [[Microsoft HoloLens]]. On February 24, 2019 the Hololens 2 enterprise edition debuted as the first variant of the device, followed by a developer edition that was announced on May 2, 2019. It was subsequently released in limited numbers on November 7, 2019. 

== History ==
HoloLens 2 was announced by [[Alex Kipman]] on February 24, 2019 at [[Mobile World Congress|MWC]] in Barcelona, Spain.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mashable.com/article/microsoft-hololens-2-mwc-2019 |title=Microsoft Hololens 2 ushers in the next generation of augmented reality|publisher=[[mashable.com]]|date=February 24, 2019|accessdate=February 25, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://edition.cnn.com/2019/02/24/tech/microsoft-hololens-2/index.html|title=Microsoft's new $3,500 HoloLens 2 headset means business|publisher=[[cnn.com]]|date=February 24, 2019|accessdate=February 25, 2019}}</ref> You can see the HoloLens 2 on-stage demo from [[Mobile World Congress|MWC]] 2019 [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uIHPPtPBgHk&t=1s here]. On May 7, 2019 the HoloLens 2 was shown off again at [[Microsoft Build]]. There, it showcased an application created with [[Unreal Engine]], which you can see [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r0ubiU3PRHw here].<ref>{{Citation|title=Apollo 11 HoloLens 2 Demo {{!}} Microsoft Build 2019 {{!}} Unreal Engine|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r0ubiU3PRHw|language=en|access-date=2019-08-07}}</ref>

On August 20, 2019, at [[Hot Chips|Hot Chips 31]] Microsoft presented their Holographic Processing Unit (HPU) 2.0 custom design for the Hololens 2.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.anandtech.com/show/14775/hot-chips-31-live-blogs-microsoft-hololens-20-silicon|title=Hot Chips 31 Live Blogs: Microsoft Hololens 2.0 Silicon|last=Cutress|first=Dr Ian|website=www.anandtech.com|access-date=2019-08-21}}</ref>

* 7x SIMD Fixed Point (SFP) for 2D processing
* 6x Floating Vector Processor (FVP) for 3D processing
* >1 TOP of programmable compute
* 125Mb [[Shadow random access memory|SRAM]]
* 79mm<sup>2</sup> die size and 2 Billion transistors
* [[TSMC]] 16FF+ process
* [[PCIe]] 2.0 x1 at 100 MB/s bandwidth to Snapdragon 850

On August 29, 2019, at the World Artificial Intelligence Conference in Shanghai, [[Microsoft]]'s Executive Vice President, Harry Shum, revealed that HoloLens 2 will go on sale sometime in September 2019.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-china-tech-microsoft-idUSKCN1VJ07Z|title=Microsoft says new augmented reality headset to go on sale in...|date=2019-08-29|work=Reuters|access-date=2019-08-29|language=en}}</ref> The product started shipping on November 7, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theverge.com/2019/11/7/20946589/microsoft-hololens-2-mixed-reality-headset-preorder-shipping-price-upgrade|title=The Microsoft HoloLens 2 ships today for $3,500|last=Robertson|first=Adi|date=2019-11-07|website=The Verge|language=en|access-date=2019-11-19}}</ref>

== Improvements over the previous model ==
Microsoft highlighted three main improvements made to the device: immersiveness, ergonomics and business friendliness.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/hololens/buy|title=HoloLens 2 pricing and preorder|publisher=[[microsoft.com]]|date=|accessdate=February 25, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=New HoloLens 2 gives Microsoft the edge in the next generation of computing |url=https://news.microsoft.com/innovation-stories/hololens-2/ |website=Innovation Stories at Microsoft |publisher=Microsoft |accessdate=25 February 2019}}</ref>

HoloLens 2 has a diagonal [[Field of view (image processing)|field of view]] of 52 degrees, improving over the 34 degree field of view of the first edition of HoloLens, while keeping a resolution of 47 pixels per degree. <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/akipman/status/1100069645661495298|title=No secrets... - The first HoloLens had a 34-degree diagonal FOV; the new headset’s field of view … 52-degree diagonal field of view. More than 2x is area and most of the growth is vertical (no longer 16:9)|last=Kipman|first=Alex|date=2019-02-25|website=@akipman|language=en|access-date=2019-03-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wired.com/story/microsoft-hololens-2-headset/|title=The HoloLens 2 Puts a Full-Fledged Computer on Your Face|last=Goode|first=Lauren|date=2019-02-24|work=Wired|access-date=2019-03-01|issn=1059-1028}}</ref>

Holographic Processing Unit (HPU) 2.0 improvements compared to the HPU 1.0<ref name=":0" />:

* 1.7x compute
* 2x effective DRAM bandwidth
* Improved Hologram stability
* New hardware accelerated workloads such as eye tracking, fully articulated hand tracking, semantic labeling, spatial audio and JBL filter

== HoloLens 2 Emulation ==
The HoloLens 2 Emulator was made available to developers on April 17, 2019. <ref>{{Cite web|url=http://dotnetbyexample.blogspot.com/2019/04/first-look-at-hololens-2-emulator.html|title=First look at the HoloLens 2 emulator|last=Schaik|first=Joost Van|date=2019-04-17|website=Dotnet by Example|access-date=2019-08-07}}</ref> This [[emulator]] allows developers to create applications for the HoloLens 2 before the device ships. For more technical information on the HoloLens 2 Emulator, see the [https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/mixed-reality/using-the-hololens-emulator Microsoft Documentation].
