'''Element AI''' is an [[artificial intelligence]] company based in [[Montreal]], [[Quebec]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=http://fortune.com/longform/element-ai-startup-tech/|title=Can This Startup Break Big Tech’s Hold on A.I.?|last=|first=|date=|work=[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wired.com/2016/10/ai-pioneer-yoshua-bengio-launching-element-ai-deep-learning-incubator/|title=AI Pioneer Yoshua Bengio Is Launching Element.AI, a Deep-Learning Incubator|last=Metz|first=Cade|date=October 26, 2016|work=[[Wired (magazine)|Wired]]|access-date=February 1, 2019}}</ref>

== History ==
Element AI was founded in October 2016 by Jean-François Gagné and co-founders [[Yoshua Bengio]], Anne Martel, Nicolas Chapados, and Philippe Beaudoin, along with Jean-Sébastien Cournoyer of Montreal venture capital fund Real Ventures.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://montrealgazette.com/business/montreals-element-ai-gets-record-102-million-boost-from-u-s-investors|title=Montreal's Element AI gets record $102 million boost from U.S. investors|last=Marotte|first=Bertrand|date=June 14, 2017|work=[[Montreal Gazette]]|access-date=February 1, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.elementai.com/|title=Global AI Software Provider - Element AI|website=Element AI|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref> In 2017, the company raised US$102 million from US investors in a [[series A round]] led by the San Francisco venture fund Data Collective and [[Microsoft Ventures]].<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/cio/2017/06/14/element-ai-raises-102-million-as-new-learning-model-sparks-interest/|title=Element AI Raises $102 Million as New Learning Model Sparks Interest|last=|first=|date=|work=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://techcrunch.com/2017/06/14/element-ai-a-platform-for-companies-to-build-ai-solutions-raises-102m/|title=Element AI, a platform for companies to build AI solutions, raises $102M|last=|first=|date=|work=[[TechCrunch]]|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://business.financialpost.com/entrepreneur/revolution-ai-everyone-wants-in-to-montreals-deep-learning-hub|title=Revolution AI: Why everyone wants in to Montreal's deep-learning hub|last=|first=|date=|work=[[Postmedia Network|Financial Post]]|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref>

In December 2018, the [[Government of Canada]] gave a loan of up to CAD$5 million to Element AI with the goal of creating new jobs.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://betakit.com/element-ai-receives-5-million-loan-from-canadian-government/|title=Element AI receives $5 million loan from the Canadian government|last=|first=|date=|work=BetaKit|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref> They later hosted a conference on the effects of AI, with [[Justin Trudeau]] and [[Mounir Mahjoubi]] establishing a global panel to study the effects of AI.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wired.com/story/canada-france-plan-global-panel-study-ai/|title=Canada, France Plan Global Panel to Study the Effects of AI|last=|first=|date=|work=|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref> In December 2018, Element AI also partnered with [[Amnesty International]] and released a study measuring [[online abuse]] against women in politics and journalism on [[Twitter]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://montrealgazette.com/business/local-business/element-ai-partners-with-amnesty-international-to-study-online-abuse|title=Element AI partners with Amnesty International to study online abuse|last=|first=|date=|work=[[Montreal Gazette]]|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wired.com/story/amnesty-report-twitter-abuse-women/|title=Twitter Is Indeed Toxic for Women, Amnesty Report Says|last=|first=|date=|work=[[Wired (magazine)|Wired]]|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref> 

Element AI has partnerships with [[GIC Private Limited|GIC]], [[Singapore Management University]], and the [[Port of Montreal]], among others.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://betakit.com/element-ai-partnering-with-singapore-companies-to-accelerate-product-development/|title=Element AI partnering with Singapore companies to accelerate product development|last=|first=|date=|work=BetaKit|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=https://montrealgazette.com/business/element-ai-preparing-to-launch-its-first-products|title=Element AI preparing to launch its first products|last=|first=|date=|work=[[Montreal Gazette]]|access-date=January 27, 2019}}</ref> At the Port of Montreal, Element AI is working to predict how long trucks will wait to drop off or pick up goods at the port.<ref name=":2" />

The company's first standalone product Underwriting Partner, an AI-assisted insurance underwriting workflow software, was released in September 2019.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://betakit.com/element-ai-announces-first-product-for-the-insurance-market/|title=Element AI announces first product for the insurance market|last=Hotchkiss|first=Caitlin|date=September 23, 2019|work=Betakit|access-date=December 6, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.elementai.com/industries/insurance|title=Element AI for Insurance|last=|first=|date=|website=Element AI|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191206210233/https:///|archive-date=December 6, 2019|access-date=December 6, 2019}}</ref>In December 2019, they released Knowledge Scout, a platform for data set management in manufacturing companies.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://betakit.com/element-ai-launches-newest-standalone-product-turning-data-into-actionable-insights/|title=Element AI launches newest standalone product, turning data into actionable insights|last=Kirkwood|first=Isabelle|date=December 3, 2019|work=Betakit|access-date=December 6, 2019}}</ref>
