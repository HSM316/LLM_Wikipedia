'''Libby Heaney''' is a British artist and lecturer at the [[Royal College of Art]]. She works on the impact of future technologies and is a resident of [[Somerset House]] Studios. Her work has been featured in the [[Victoria and Albert Museum]], [[Tate Modern]] and the [[Science Gallery]].<ref>{{Official website}}</ref><ref>{{Twitter}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Heaney is from [[Tamworth, Staffordshire]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.skyartsart50.tv/art-50-commissioned-projects/the-britbot-by-libby-heaney/|title=Sky Arts Art 50 {{!}} 'The Britbot' by Libby Heaney|last=elwyn.co|website=Sky Arts Art 50|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> She joined the [[Royal Air Force Air Cadets]] at the age of 13 and was intent on becoming a pilot.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/interactions/archive/INTMay08webTotal.pdf|title=Reaching for blue skies|author=Anon|date=2008|website=iop.org|publisher=Institute of Physics|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> She was awarded a Flying Scholarship at the age of 17, and has flown by herself in a [[Cessna]].<ref name=":1" /> Heaney completed A-Levels in Art, Physics, Maths and German.<ref name=":1" /> She studied physics at [[Imperial College London]], graduating in 2005 with first class honours.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://artsandculture.google.com/exhibit/HwKScJ9ttjDjIQ|title=Somerset House Studios: Libby Heaney - Google Arts & Culture|website=artsandculture.google.com|publisher=Google Cultural Institute|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/student/postgraduate/why-cant-we-get-physical-478699.html|title=Why can't we get physical?|date=2006-05-18|website=independent.co.uk|publisher=The Independent|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> She spent a year in [[Freiberg]] at the [[Albert Ludwig's University of Freiburg|Albert Ludwig University]], modelling the decoherence of trapped ions.<ref name=":1" /> She spent time using physics in an industrial setting, modelling flow systems for a tidal power spin-out company.<ref name=":1" /> Heaney was inspired by her lectures on [[quantum mechanics]] to pursue postgraduate study, moving to the [[University of Leeds]] to work on [[quantum information]] with [[Vlatko Vedral]].<ref name=":1" /> She completed her [[PhD]] thesis on mode entanglement in ultra-cold atomic gases,<ref name=PhD-491808>{{cite thesis|url=https://copac.jisc.ac.uk/id/18160316?style=html|degree=PhD|publisher=University of Leeds|title=Entanglement of non-interacting Bose gases|first= Libby|last=Heaney|date=2008|id={{EThOS|uk.bl.ethos.491808}}|website=jisc.ac.uk|oclc=828574910}}</ref> working at the [[National University of Singapore]].<ref>{{Cite work|last=Heaney|first=Libby|title=Teleportation of a quantum state of a spatial mode with a single massive particle|date=2010|journal=Theory of Quantum Computation, Communication, and Cryptography|volume=6519|series=Lecture Notes in Computer Science|language=en|pages=175–186|doi=10.1007/978-3-642-18073-6_15|arxiv=1011.3743|isbn=9783642180729|s2cid=38098849}}</ref> She worked for the Ogden Trust as [[teaching fellow]] between 2006 and 2007, working with local schools to communicate the excitement of physics research.<ref name=":1" /> Heaney was awarded the 2008 [[Institute of Physics]] Very Early Career Woman in Physics Award (now [[Jocelyn Bell Burnell Medal and Prize]]).<ref name=":1" />

== Career ==
Heaney was made an [[Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council]] [[postdoctoral]] fellow at the [[University of Oxford]] to pursue her own research on ultra-cold gases within Dieter Jaksch's research group.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.physics.ox.ac.uk/groups/qubit/Libby/index.html|title=Dieter Jaksch Theory Group|website=physics.ox.ac.uk|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> She studied [[Bell inequalities]] for systems restricted by the [[particle number]] [[superselection]] rule<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Heaney|first1=Libby|last2=Lee|first2=Seung-Woo|last3=Jaksch|first3=Dieter|date=2010|title=Bell inequality for pairs of particle-number-superselection-rule restricted states|journal=Physical Review A|volume=82|issue=4|pages=042116|doi=10.1103/PhysRevA.82.042116|arxiv=1006.2759|bibcode=2010PhRvA..82d2116H|s2cid=118627059}}</ref> and mode-entanglement in relation to quantum communication.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Heaney|first1=Libby|last2=Vedral|first2=Vlatko|date=2009|title=Natural mode entanglement as a resource for quantum communication|journal=Physical Review Letters|volume=103|issue=20|pages=200502|doi=10.1103/PhysRevLett.103.200502|pmid=20365969|arxiv=0907.5404|bibcode=2009PhRvL.103t0502H|s2cid=30995739}}</ref> In 2011 she joined the [[National University of Singapore]], where she worked for two years in theoretical quantum physics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.somersethouse.org.uk/residents/libby-heaney|title=Libby Heaney|date=2017-03-06|website=somersethouse.org.uk|publisher=Somerset House|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://benasque.org/2009qi/cgi-bin/participants.pl|title=Centro de Ciencias de Benasque Pedro Pascual|website=benasque.org|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://groups.oist.jp/qsu/fy2012-visitors|title=FY2012 Visitors {{!}} OIST Groups|website=groups.oist.jp|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref>

In 2013 Heaney returned to the UK and completed a master's degree at the [[University of the Arts London]]. She studied arts and science at [[Central Saint Martins]] and graduated in 2015.<ref name=":0" /> Heaney has since been employed as a lecturer at the [[Royal College of Art]], teaching Information Experience Design.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.rca.ac.uk/more/staff/dr-libby-heaney/|title=Dr Libby Heaney|website=rca.ac.uk|publisher=Royal College of Art|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> Her graduate work was featured in the [[Affordable Art Fair]] in 2015. She exhibited digital prints of computer codes and was selected as one of the top artists to look out for by [[Christie's]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theresident.co.uk/london-culture-events/arts-music-london/multiplied-2015-top-5-artists/|title=5 artists to look at for at Multiplied 2015|access-date=2018-12-28|website=theresident.co.uk}}</ref> She explored the links between quantum information, quantum physics, linguistics, digital art and design.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://issuu.com/al3xjw/docs/pp_2015awards_brochure_final|title=The Picton Art Prize Catalogue featuring 'Celestial' {{!}} Designed by Everything In Between|website=issuu.com|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> Her artwork was featured in the 2016 [[Tate Modern]] Internet Yami-ichi, an exhibition of internet culture at [[Tate Modern]].<ref name=":4">{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/2016/may/22/internet-yami-ichi-tate-modern|title=Edward Snowdomes and edible internet cookies: welcome to Yami-ichi|last=Sayej|first=Nadja|date=2016-05-22|website=theguardian.com|publisher=The Guardian|access-date=2018-12-28|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> She created a personalised Tarot service that offered advice from [[Match.com]].<ref name=":4" /> Her work ''Sensory Apparatus'', a collaborative project was on show in [[Valletta]] in 2016.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=https://www.um.edu.mt/think/sensory-apparatus/|title=Sensory Apparatus|publisher= Think Magazine|language=en-GB|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> The show was inspired by [[panopticism]] and the [[Quantum Zeno effect]].<ref name=":5" /> She is an artist in residence at [[Somerset House]].<ref>{{Citation|last=British Council Spain|title=Libby Heaney & Alan Warburton at Sónar+D|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BccI_AG6jWI|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref>

=== Tinderbot ===
In 2016 she created a ''Tinderbot'', which presented [[Tinder (app)|Tinder]] conversations between AI bots programmed using ''[[Lady Chatterley's Lover]]''.<ref name=":2" /> The Tinderbot was covered by [[BBC News]], [[TheJournal.ie]] and the ''[[Irish Examiner]]'' and was exhibited at [[The Lowry]] in 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-41995722|title=When Lady Chatterley joined Tinder|last=Youngs|first=Ian|date=2017-11-16|publisher=[[BBC News]]|website=bbc.com|access-date=2018-12-28|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.irishexaminer.com/lifestyle/artsfilmtv/new-exhibition-addresses-the-blurring-of-the-lines-between-humanity-and-the-digital-world-442890.html|title=New exhibition addresses the blurring of the lines between humanity and the digital world|first=Jonathan|last=deBurca Butler|date=2017-02-14|website=irishexaminer.com|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thejournal.ie/science-gallery-exhibits-3230398-Feb2017/|title=Lady Chatterley's Tinderbot: Modern dating at Dublin's Science Gallery|last=Murray|first=Sean|website=TheJournal.ie|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thelowry.com/exhibitions/humansbeingdigital|title={{!}} The Lowry|website=thelowry.com|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> She has collaborated with the [[University of Bristol]] Centre for Quantum Photonics.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|url=http://www.iop.org/careers/working-life/articles/page_69426.html|title=Once a physicist: Libby Heaney|website=iop.org|publisher=[[Institute of Physics]]|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref>

=== Quantum Breathing ===
Heaney used [[virtual reality]] in ''Quantum Breathing'', an insight into quantum mechanics that integrated computer games and art.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.routesnorth.com/event/quantum-breathing-aarhus/|title=Quantum Breathing, Aarhus|last=North|first=Routes|website=routesnorth.com|publisher=Routes North|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> She collaborated with physicists at the [[Aarhus University]] and included the narratives of [[Haruki Murakami]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://scitech.au.dk/en/about-science-and-technology/current-affairs/news/show/artikel/journey-to-an-unknown-world/|title=Journey to an unknown world|last=157485@au.dk|website=scitech.au.dk|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://scitech.au.dk/en/about-science-and-technology/current-affairs/events/show-event/artikel/udstilling-quantum-breathing/|title=Exhibition: Quantum Breathing|last=157485@au.dk|website=scitech.au.dk|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> The creation was exhibited in the Non-space Gallery in Denmark as part of the [[Aarhus]] 2017 EU capital of culture celebrations.<ref name=":6" />

=== Britbot ===
In 2017 Heaney was commissioned by [[Sky Arts]] and the [[Barbican Centre]] to design the ''Britbot'', an [[internet bot]] built using [[artificial intelligence]] and the citizenship book ''Life in the UK: a guide for new residents.''<ref name=":7">{{Cite web|url=https://www.britbot.org/|title=Britbot|website=britbot.org|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> The book, a manual for the citizenship test, has been described by Heaney as being "largely a white male privileged version of British history and culture".<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/tabithagoldstaub/2018/09/24/machine-dreams-art-and-artificial-intelligence/|title=Machine Dreams: Art And Artificial Intelligence|last=Goldstaub|first=Tabitha|work=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> The bot created a test for the public, learnt from their responses and assesses how British they are.<ref name=":0" /> She was awarded an [[Arts Council England]] grant to widen participation of the Britbot to social media.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.britbot.org/about|title=About - Britbot|website=britbot.org|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> She ran a workshop on ''Quantum Britishness'' at the [[Goethe-Institut]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.meetup.com/Creative-AI/events/256141475/|title=Creative AI meetup #22: Quantum Britishness and Computational Spectatorship|website=Meetup.com|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref>

Heaney's artwork was featured in [[Tabitha Goldstaub]]'s festival of [[Artificial intelligence]] ''CogX.''<ref name=":8" /> Heaney has exhibited her chatterbots at the [[Victoria and Albert Museum]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://digitransglasgow.github.io/ArtificiallyIntelligent/contributions/15_Noisy.html|title=noisy chatterbots|website=github.io|publisher=ArtificiallyIntelligent|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref> She has created quantum physics inspired artwork for [[Science Gallery]] and the [[Victoria and Albert Museum]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dublin.sciencegallery.com/fake/?page=quantum-fake|title=Fake - Science Gallery|website=dublin.sciencegallery.com|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vam.ac.uk/event/dQLoj98D/art-science-and-quantum-computing|title=V&A · Art, Science and Quantum Computing|website=vam.ac.uk|publisher=Victoria and Albert Museum|language=en|access-date=2018-12-28}}</ref>
