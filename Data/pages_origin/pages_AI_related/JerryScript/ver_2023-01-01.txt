'''JerryScript''' is an ultra-lightweight [[JavaScript]] engine for the [[Internet of things]]. It is capable of executing [[ECMAScript|ECMAScript 5.1]] source code on devices with less than 64 KB of memory.

The engine was open sourced on [[GitHub]] in June 2015. JerryScript is licensed under the [[Apache License|Apache License 2.0]]. In October 2016 the [[Linux Foundation#JS Foundation|JS Foundation]] was formed and JerryScript is one of the initial projects.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://js.foundation/announcements/2016/10/17/Linux-Foundation-Unites-JavaScript-Community-Open-Web-Development/|title = OpenJS Foundation}}</ref>

==Key characteristics of JerryScript==
* Full [[ECMAScript|ECMAScript 5.1]] standard compliance
* 170K binary size when compiled for [[ARM architecture|ARM]] [[ARM architecture#Thumb-2|Thumb-2]]
* Heavily optimized for low memory consumption
* Written in [[C99]] for maximum portability
* Snapshot support for precompiling [[JavaScript]] source code to byte code
* Mature [[C (programming language)|C]] [[Application programming interface|API]], easy to embed in applications
** Projects such as IoT.js are built on top of JerryScript

== Support ==
