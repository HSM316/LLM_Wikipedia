'''Valencell''' is a US-based biometric technology company that develops [[Biometrics|biometric]] sensor technology for [[Wearable computer|wearables]] and [[hearables]], and provides its patent-protected technology to consumer electronics manufacturers in various industries.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wirelessdesignmag.com/blog/2014/12/finally-wearable-works|title=Finally, A Wearable that Works|date=11 December 2014|publisher=}} {{self-published inline|date=August 2016}}</ref> Valencell's PerformTek technology enables wearables and hearables to continuously measure biometrics such as heart rate, blood oxygen levels, [[blood pressure]], [[heart rate variability]], respiration rate,VO<sub>2</sub> and other parameters for health and fitness assessment.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Leboeuf |first1=Steven Francis |last2=Aumer |first2=Michael E. |last3=Kraus |first3=William E. |last4=Johnson |first4=Johanna L. |last5=Duscha |first5=Brian |title=Earbud-Based Sensor for the Assessment of Energy Expenditure, HR, and V˙O2max |journal=Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise |volume=46 |issue=5 |pages=1046–52 |year=2014 |pmid=24743110 |pmc=3996514 |doi=10.1249/MSS.0000000000000183 }}</ref> The technology can be integrated into products designed by consumer electronics manufacturers, mobile device and accessory makers, medical device, sports and fitness, and hearing aid companies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mhealthspot.com/2014/11/interview-valencells-cofounder-president-dr-steven-leboeuf/|title=Interview with Valencell's Dr. Steven LeBoeuf|date=27 November 2014|publisher=}}</ref> Valencell has more than 60 patents granted,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://valencell.com/patents/|title=Valencell - Virtual Patent Marking|publisher=}}</ref> plus more than 100 patents pending. More than 35 companies utilize Valencell's technology and intellectual property, which has been commercialized in wearable devices made by companies including [[Bose Corporation|Bose]], [[Suunto]], [[LG]], [[Jabra (headset)|Jabra]], [[Samsung]], and [[Sony]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.computerworld.com/article/3020236/internet-of-things/consumer-products-at-ces-arent-the-half-of-it.html|title=Consumer products at CES aren’t the half of it|first=Matt|last=Hamblen|publisher=}}</ref>

== History ==
Valencell was co-founded by a team of scientists and engineers - President Dr. Steven LeBoeuf, VP Engineering Dr. Jesse Tucker and VP Product Management Dr. Mike Aumer, and has been augmented further with R&D talent since the company was formed in 2006.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://valencell.com/about-us|title=Valencell - About|publisher=}}{{psc|date=August 2016}}</ref> Since inception, the company has won more than $3 million in government grants and has raised more than $35 million in venture funding from investor firms such as WSJ Joshua Fund, GII Tech, and TDF Ventures. In June 2018, Valencell raised $10.5M from Sonion and existing investors.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mobihealthnews.com/content/valencells-partnership-earphone-manufacturer-leads-105m-raise|title=Valencell's partnership with earphone manufacturer leads to $10.5M raise|date=14 June 2018|publisher=}}</ref>

== Biometrics Lab ==
In September 2014, Valencell opened the Valencell Biometrics Lab, an exercise science lab designed to test biometric wearables and hearables in their intended use cases.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bizjournals.com/triangle/blog/techflash/2014/09/raleigh-valencell-wearable-tech-north-hills.html |title=Raleigh valencell wearable tech north hills|date=2014 |website=www.bizjournals.com}}</ref> The Biometrics Lab tests more than 400 different devices from various manufacturers each year and analyzes over one million biometric data points on a monthly basis. Volunteers test wearable devices in 25 distinctive testing protocols ranging from hard intervals to easy lifestyle efforts.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.slowtwitch.com/Products/A_day_in_the_life_of_Valencell_5620.html|title=A day in the life of Valencell|first=|last=Slowtwitch.com|publisher=}}</ref>
