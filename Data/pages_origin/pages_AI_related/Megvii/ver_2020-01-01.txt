'''Megvii''' ({{zh|s=旷视|p=Kuàngshì}}) is a Chinese [[technology company]]<ref name="BC">{{cite news |last= Qingqing |first=Chen |date=August 12, 2017 |title=Beijing-based tech start-up has a big edge on facial recognition |url=http://www.globaltimes.cn/content/1060728.shtml |work=[[Global Times]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref> that designs [[image recognition]] and [[deep-learning]] software.<ref name="AB">{{cite news |last=Sun |first=Yiting |date=August 11, 2017 |title=Meet the Company That’s Using Face Recognition to Reshape China’s Tech Scene |url=https://www.technologyreview.com/s/608598/when-a-face-is-worth-a-billion-dollars/ |work=[[MIT Technology Review]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref> Based in [[Beijing]], the company develops [[artificial intelligence]] (AI) technology for businesses and for the public sector.<ref name="AT">{{cite news |last=Dai |first=Sarah |date=June 5, 2019 |title=AI unicorn Megvii not behind app used for surveillance in Xinjiang, says human rights group |url=https://www.scmp.com/tech/start-ups/article/3013229/ai-unicorn-megvii-not-behind-app-used-surveillance-xinjiang-says |work=[[South China Morning Post]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref> In 2019, the company was valued at USD $4 billion.<ref name="BG">{{cite news |last=Dai |first=Sarah |date=May 29, 2019 |title=Rising Chinese AI star Megvii gets caught in the US-China tech war |url=https://www.scmp.com/tech/article/3012103/rising-chinese-ai-star-megvii-gets-caught-us-china-tech-war#l5LgYPG1g4Q0eTVw.99 |work=[[South China Morning Post]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref> Megvii is the largest provider of third-party authentication software in the world,<ref name="BG"/> and its product, '''Face++''', is the world’s largest open-source [[computer vision]] platform.<ref name="CF">{{cite news |last=Barrett |first=Eamon |date=October 28, 2018 |title=In China, Facial Recognition Tech Is Watching You |url=https://fortune.com/2018/10/28/in-china-facial-recognition-tech-is-watching-you/ |work=[[Fortune (magazine)|Fortune]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref> 
 
==History== 

The company was founded in Beijing<ref name="BC"/> with Megvii standing for "mega vision."<ref name="AA">{{cite news|date=September 9, 2017 |title=Ever better and cheaper, face-recognition technology is spreading |url=http://www.startribune.com/the-first-billion-dollar-startup-in-facial-recognition-has-emerged-and-it-s-in-china/444050653/ |work=[[The Economist]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref> The company's core product, Face++, launched in 2012 as the first online facial recognition platform in China.<ref name="AF">{{cite news |last=Feifel |first=Fan |date=January 12, 2017 |title=Megvii gives a digital meaning to face-reading |url=http://www.chinadaily.com.cn/business/tech/2017-01/12/content_27931630.htm |work=[[China Daily]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref> In 2015 Megvii created Brain++, a deep-learning engine to help train its algorithms.<ref name="AB"/> 
 
Megvii raised $100 million in 2016,<ref name="AA"/><ref name="BC"/> $460 million in 2017<ref name="AC">{{cite news |last=Jacob |first=Harrison  |date=July 8, 2018 |title=Inside the creepy and impressive startup funded by the Chinese government that is developing AI that can recognize anyone, anywhere |url=https://www.businessinsider.com/china-facial-recognition-tech-company-megvii-faceplusplus-2018-5 |work=[[Business Insider]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref> and $750 million in May 2019.<ref name="AR"/>
 
In 2017, Megvii marketed authentication and computational photography functions to smart phone companies and mobile application developers, then smart logistics. Megvii’s AI-empowered products include personal [[Internet of things|IoT]], city IoT and [[supply chain]] IoT.<ref>[http://www.thestandard.com.hk/section-news.php?id=211441&fc=1 (Chen, Avery) "AI unicorn plans HK$3.9b stock sale ", ''The Standard'', September 9, 2019.]</ref><ref name="AU">{{cite news |last=Bergen |first=Mark |date=May 24, 2019 |title=Trump's Latest China Target Includes a Rising Star in AI |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-05-24/trump-s-latest-china-target-includes-a-rising-star-in-ai |work=[[Bloomberg L.P.|Bloomberg]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref> In 2017 and 2018,<ref name="AU"/> Megvii beat [[Google]], [[Facebook]], and [[Microsoft]] in tests of [[image recognition]] at the [[International Conference on Computer Vision]].<ref name="AG">{{cite news |last=Yang |first=Yuan |date=November 1, 2017 |title=China pours millions into facial recognition start-up Face++ |url=https://www.ft.com/content/4d008d46-bed2-11e7-b8a3-38a6e068f464 |work=[[Financial Times]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref>

In May 2019, the [[Human Rights Watch]] reported finding Face++ code in the [[Big_data#China|Integrated Joint Operations Platform]] (IJOP), a police surveillance app used to collect data on the [[Uyghur people|Uyghur]] community in [[Xinjiang]].<ref name="AR">{{cite news |last=Liao |first=Rita |date=May 8, 2019 |title=Alibaba-backed facial recognition startup Megvii raises $750 million |url=https://techcrunch.com/2019/05/08/megvii-750-million/ |work=[[TechCrunch]]|access-date= August 28, 2019}}</ref> Shortly after the report, the [[Trump administration]] reportedly considered adding Megvii to a [[blacklist]] of [[US-China trade war|banned Chinese entities]].<ref name="AU"/>  Human Rights Watch released a correction to its report in June 2019 stating that Megvii did not appear to have collaborated on IJOP, and that the Face++ code in the app was inoperable.<ref name="AT"/>

In June 2019, Megvii had 2,349 employees,<ref name=Caxin>[https://www.caixinglobal.com/2019-08-29/risks-mount-for-chinese-ai-firm-as-it-heads-for-ipo-101456610.html (Li, Isabelle and Zhanqi, Ye) "Risks Mount for Chinese AI Firm as It Heads for IPO", ''Caxin Global'', September 9, 2019.]</ref> and was valued at over $4 billion,<ref name="AT"/> as the "world’s biggest provider of third-party authentication software",<ref name="BG"/> with 339 corporate clients in 112 cities in China.<ref name=Caxin/> The Chinese government employs Megvii software.<ref name="AU"/>
