'''BodyMedia''' was a medical and consumer technology company headquartered in [[Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania]].  Incorporated in 1999, BodyMedia developed wearable body monitoring systems. In April 2013, BodyMedia was acquired by Jawbone for an estimated $100 million.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jawbone Acquires BodyMedia For Over $100 Million To Give It An Edge In Wearable Health Tracking|url=https://techcrunch.com/2013/04/30/jawbone-will-acquire-bodymedia-for-over-100-million-to-give-it-an-edge-in-wearable-health-tracking/|publisher=[[TechCrunch]]|date=30 April 2014}}</ref>

==History==

===Wearable device===
BodyMedia staff published some of the first research on wearability of devices in 1998<ref>{{cite web|title=Design For Wearability|url=http://gtubicomp.pbworks.com/f/gemperle-wearability.pdf}}</ref> and detecting activity context using accelerometers in 1999<ref>{{cite book|chapter=Wearable sensor badge and sensor jacket for context awareness|doi=10.1109/ISWC.1999.806681 |isbn=0-7695-0428-0 |title=Digest of Papers. Third International Symposium on Wearable Computers |year=1999 |last1=Farringdon |first1=J. |last2=Moore |first2=A.J. |last3=Tilbury |first3=N. |last4=Church |first4=J. |last5=Biemond |first5=P.D. |pages=107–113 |s2cid=12251548 }}</ref> at the 2nd and 3rd IEEE sponsored International Symposium on Wearable Computers. The BodyMedia informatics group made available a large anonymised human physiology data set for the 2004 International Conference on Machine Learning, running a Machine Learning Challenge.<ref>{{cite web|title=ICML competition and workshop on Physiological Data Modeling|url=http://www.mail-archive.com/uai@cs.orst.edu/msg02915.html}}</ref> They published about their very large data set and data modeling methodology at the Innovative Applications of Artificial Intelligence conference in 2011 winning the IAAI Deployed Application award.<ref>{{cite web|title=Machine Learning and Sensor Fusion for Estimating Continuous Energy Expenditure|url=http://www.aaai.org/ocs/index.php/IAAI/IAAI-11/paper/view/3533/4018|access-date=2012-04-27|archive-date=2016-03-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304113552/http://www.aaai.org/ocs/index.php/IAAI/IAAI-11/paper/view/3533/4018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Clinical history===
Between 2001 and 2005, BodyMedia provided healthcare professionals with metabolic assessment and [[behavioral therapy]] products for the treatment of [[obesity]], [[diabetes]], and [[Cardiovascular disease|CVD]]. BodyMedia have been used in hundreds of clinical studies.<ref>{{cite web|title=BodyMedia Bibliography List|url=http://www.bodymedia.com/Professionals/Bibliography|publisher=BodyMedia|accessdate=12 April 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120421130119/http://www.bodymedia.com/Professionals/Bibliography|archive-date=21 April 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> The U.S. [[Food and Drug Administration]] (FDA) classifies BodyMedia as a [[Medical device#Class II: General controls with special controls|Class II Medical Device]].{{Citation needed|date=August 2022}}

BodyMedia was demonstrated to be accurate in comparison to the [[doubly labeled water]] method for measuring energy expenditure per day.<ref>{{cite journal|title=Accuracy of armband monitors for measuring daily energy expenditure in healthy adults| pmid=20386334 | doi=10.1249/MSS.0b013e3181e0b3ff | volume=42| issue=11 | journal=Med Sci Sports Exerc| pages=2134–40 | last1 = Johannsen | first1 = DL | last2 = Calabro | first2 = MA | last3 = Stewart | first3 = J | last4 = Franke | first4 = W | last5 = Rood | first5 = JC | last6 = Welk | first6 = GJ | year=2010| doi-access = free }}</ref> The mean absolute-percent difference per-person per-day in Kcalories was less than 10%.{{Citation needed|date=August 2022}} It was also shown that the standard WHO GPAQ survey underestimates physical activity bouts of at least 10 minutes.<ref name="Laeremans">{{cite journal

===Consumer products===
BodyMedia sold a consumer software product for [[armband]] hardware starting in 2001.<ref>{{Cite web |date=January 8, 2001 |title=BodyMedia device shown at Cisco model home |url=https://www.bizjournals.com/pittsburgh/stories/2001/01/08/daily4.html |access-date=2022-07-29 |website=www.bizjournals.com}}</ref> A consumer web site using BodyMedia armbands was branded; run in the US by "Dot Fit".{{Citation needed|date=August 2022}} In the UK a consumer web site is branded and run by "Ki Performance".{{Citation needed|date=August 2022}}
