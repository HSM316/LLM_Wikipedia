[[File:Mauro Martino presenting Forma Fluens at Ars Electronica Center.jpg|300px|thumb|Mauro Martino presenting ''Forma Fluens'' at ''Ars Electronica Center'', Linz, Austria, May 2019. Photo by Florian Voggeneder]]
'''Mauro Martino''' is an Italian artist, designer and researcher.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mamartino.com/information.html|title=Mauro Martino. About|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> He is the founder and director of the Visual Artificial Intelligence Lab at [[IBM Research]], and Professor of Practice at [[Northeastern University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://camd.northeastern.edu/faculty/mauro-martino/|title=Northeastern University, CAMD Art&Design|access-date=17 March 2019}}</ref>

== Career ==
He graduated from [[Polytechnic University of Milan]], and was a research affiliate with the [[MIT Senseable City Lab|Senseable City Lab]] at [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology|MIT]]. Mauro was formerly an Assistant Research Professor at Northeastern University working with [[Albert-László Barabási|Albert-Laszlo Barabasi]] at ''Center for Complex Network Research'' and with ''David Lazer'' and Fellows at ''The Institute for Quantitative Social Science (IQSS)'' at [[Harvard University]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Martino|first=Mauro|title=Mauro Martino|url=https://www.mamartino.com/|access-date=2020-11-15|website=Mauro Martino Lab}}</ref>
[[File:Mauro Martino presenting Wonder Net at VISAP, Berlin, Germany, October 2018.jpg|thumb|300x300px|Mauro Martino presenting ''Wonder Net'' at VISAP, Berlin, Germany, October 2018. Photo by Bon Adriel Aseniero]]
His works have been published in "The Best American Infographics" in the 2015<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|title=The Best American Infographics 2015|last=Cook|first=Gareth|publisher=Houghton Mifflin Harcourt|year=2015|isbn=978-0544542709|pages=150–151}}</ref> and 2016 editions<ref name=":1">{{Cite book|title=The Best American Infographics 2016|last=Cook|first=Gareth|publisher=Houghton Mifflin Harcourt|year=2016|isbn=978-0544556386|pages=36–37}}</ref> and have been shown at international festivals and exhibitions including [[Ars Electronica]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ars.electronica.art/origin/2011/07/27/sensing-place-placing-sense-symposium/|title=Ars Electronica 2011. Sensing Place / Placing Sense – Symposium|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> [[RIXC]] Art Science Festival,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://rixc.org/en/exhibitions/2015/open-fields-rixc-art-science-festival-2016-exhibition/|title=OPEN FIELDS. RIXC Art Science Festival 2016 Exhibition|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> Global Exchange at [[Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts|Lincoln Center]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://lincolncenterglobalexchange.org/conference/2015|title=Lincoln Center Global Exchange 2015|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> TEDx Cambridge THRIVE, TEDx Riga,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://2016.tedxriga.com/schedule/mauro-martino/|title=TEDxRiga. Mauro Martino|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> and the [[Serpentine Gallery]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.edge.org/event/edge-serpentine-gallery-maps-for-the-21st-century|title=Edge-Serpentine Gallery-MAPS FOR THE 21ST CENTURY|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> His work is in the permanent collection at [[Ars Electronica Center]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ars.electronica.art/center/en/formafluens/|title=Ars Electronica. Understanding AI|access-date=June 20, 2019}}</ref> In 2017, Martino and his team received the [[National Science Foundation]]'s award for Best Scientific Video for the project ''Network Earth''.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nsf.gov/news/special_reports/scivis/vizzies_winners_2017.jsp|title=Vizzies Visualization Challenge|access-date=17 March 2019}}</ref> In 2019, Martino and Luca Stornaiuolo won the 2019 [[Webby Award|Webby]] People's Voice Award in the category NetArt for the project ''AI Portraits.''<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.webbyawards.com/winners/2019/websites/general-websites/netart/ai-portraits/|title=Webby People's Voice Award 2019 - NetArt|access-date=June 20, 2019}}</ref>

The project ''150 Years of Nature'' won multiple awards such as [[Fast Company]] - Innovation by Design Awards Best Data Design 2020,<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|title=Innovation by Design Awards - Best Data Design|url=https://www.fastcompany.com/90547935/data-design-innovation-by-design-2020|access-date=September 21, 2021}}</ref> [[Webby Awards|Webby Award]] 2020, Webby People's Voice Award 2020.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|title=The Webby Awards. 150 years of Nature|url=https://winners.webbyawards.com/2020/websites/general-websites/netart/122366/150-years-of-nature|access-date=September 21, 2021}}</ref> This project, along with other works created in collaboration with Barabási Lab (e.g., Wonder Net, A Century of Physics, Data Sculpture in Bronze, Control, Resilience, Success in Science, Fake News), was shown at the "Barabási Lab. Hidden Patterns" exhibitions at [[ZKM Center for Art and Media Karlsruhe|ZKM Center for Art and Media]]<ref name=":6">{{Cite web|title=ZKM. BarabásiLab. Hidden Patterns|url=https://zkm.de/en/exhibition/2021/05/barabasilab-hidden-patterns|access-date=September 23, 2021}}</ref> and Ludwig Museum - Museum of Contemporary Art, Budapest.<ref name=":7">{{Cite web|title=Ludwig Museum. == Notable works ==

*''Strolling Cities'' <ref>{{Cite web|title=Fast Company: These locations may look eerily familiar, but none actually exist|url=https://www.fastcompany.com/90640772/these-locations-may-look-eerily-familiar-but-none-actually-exist|access-date=September 21, 2021}}</ref>  is an interactive [[Artificial intelligence|AI]] art project created in collaboration with [[Polytechnic University of Milan|Politecnico di Milano]] and exhibited in the Italian Pavilion at the 2021 [[Venice Biennale]].<ref name=":8">{{Cite web|title=Interni. Strolling Cities: AI, with poetry|date=9 June 2021 |url=https://www.internimagazine.com/features/interviews/strolling-cities-mauro-martino/|access-date=September 21, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Mashable.com: 2 million real-life photos were turned into AI cities...but the cities don't exist| website=[[Mashable]] | date=4 June 2021 |url=https://mashable.com/video/strolling-cities-ai-mit?europe=true|access-date=September 22, 2021}}</ref> ''Strolling Cities'' is the first project that "unites generative AI, human voice, poetry and urban landscape."<ref name=":8" /> The neural network model was trained on "millions of photos taken during the recent lockdowns (’20/’21) that show the urban space as an unfiltered landscape of walls, streets, and buildings."<ref name=":9">{{Cite web|title=Strolling Cities|url=https://strollingcities.com|access-date=September 22, 2021}}</ref> One of the main characteristics of this project is that Italian cities appear empty, without their population. The project makes it possible to "manipulate the urban landscape with the voice, using any kind of vocal input, from simple utterances to more complex phrases."<ref name=":9" /> The video installations used in the exhibit featured 9 Italian cities: [[Bergamo]], [[Bologna]], [[Catania]], [[Como]], [[Florence]], [[Genoa]], [[Milan]], [[Palermo]], [[Rome]] and [[Venice]].<ref name=":9" /> The authors whose poems appeared in the installation included [[Alda Merini]], [[Giulia Niccolai]], [[Stefano Benni]], [[Giorgio Caproni]], [[Cesare Pavese]], [[Goffredo Parise]], and [[Valerio Magrelli]].<ref name=":9" /> 
*''150 Years of Nature'' is a data visualization project created in collaboration with Barabási Lab and dedicated to the 150th Anniversary of [[Nature (journal)|Nature]].  It includes an interactive tool, "data movie" and the cover showing the co-citation network of [[Nature (journal)|Nature]] publications throughout its history.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nature.com. 150 Years of Nature|url=https://www.nature.com/immersive/d42859-019-00121-0/index.html|access-date=September 22, 2021}}</ref> ''150 Years of Nature'' was the Webby Award Winner in 2020;<ref name=":5" /> it was awarded the Best Data Design at Fast Company - Innovation by Design Awards.<ref name=":4" /> The project is exhibited as part of the "Barabási Lab. Hidden Patterns" exhibitions at [[ZKM Center for Art and Media Karlsruhe|ZKM Center for Art and Media]]<ref name=":6" /> and Ludwig Museum - Museum of Contemporary Art, Budapest.<ref name=":7" /> 
*''AI Portraits'' <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://aiportraits.com|title=AI Portraits|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> is a research project that uses [[artificial neural network]] to reconstruct a portrait of a person. The AI system was trained on a dataset that included millions of photos of actors and actresses.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mashable.com/video/ai-portraits-celebrity-doppelganger/#PxK05Uie8kq0|title=Mashable.com. AI Portraits uses code to turn you into a celebrity|website=[[Mashable]] |date=15 November 2018 |access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> 

* ''Wonder Net'' <ref>{{Cite web|url=http://netwonder.net|title=WonderNet|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> was developed in collaboration with [[Albert-László Barabási]] at the Center for Complex Network Research<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.northeastern.edu/research/centers/center-for-complex-network-research-ccnr/|title=Center for Complex Network Research (CCNR)|access-date=17 March 2019}}</ref> at Northeastern University. Wonder Net includes 8 data sculptures which represent 8 different "data-stories" (e.g., art network, flavor network, fake news network, etc.). It was presented at the IEEE VIS 2018 Arts Program in Berlin.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vimeo.com/305763966|title=IEEE VIS 2018 Arts Program|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> 

* ''Forma Fluens'' <ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.formafluens.io/index.html#second|title=Forma Fluens|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> uses the world’s largest doodle data set by [[Google]] [[Quick, Draw!|Quick Draw]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://flowingdata.com/2017/06/16/looking-for-culture-expression-in-50-million-doodles/|title=Flowing Data. Looking for cultural expression in 50 million doodles|date=16 June 2017 |access-date=17 March 2019}}</ref> This project was presented at ''123 DATA'' design exhibition in Paris.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://123data.paris|title=123 DATA|access-date=17 March 2019}}</ref>
* ''Charting Culture'' maps cultural mobility, tracking the births and deaths of notable individuals, from 600 BC to the present day.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://flowingdata.com/2014/08/04/cultural-history-via-where-notable-people-died/|title=Flowing Data. Cultural history via where notable people died|date=4 August 2014 |access-date=17 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://www.nature.com/news/humanity-s-cultural-history-captured-in-5-minute-film-1.15650|title=Nature.com. Humanity's cultural history captured in 5-minute film|journal=Nature |date=31 July 2014 |doi=10.1038/nature.2014.15650 |access-date=17 March 2019 |last1=Abbott |first1=Alison |s2cid=163277505 }}</ref> ''Charting Culture'' is one of the most viewed videos of the [[Nature (journal)|Nature]] Video channel on YouTube with over 1.3 million views. This project is part of the ''[[Places & Spaces: Mapping Science]]'' exhibit.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://scimaps.org/mapdetail/charting_culture_186|title=Places & Spaces: Mapping Science|access-date=17 March 2019}}</ref> It was also featured in "The Best American Infographics 2015".<ref name=":0" />
* ''News Explorer'' is a web application providing new interface for news analysis and discovery.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.visualcomplexity.com/vc/project.cfm?id=993|title=Visual Complexity. Watson News Explorer|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref>
* ''Network Earth''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fastcompany.com/3056999/how-were-pushing-natures-network-architecture-to-a-catastrophic-crash|title=Fast Company. We're Pushing Nature's Network Architecture To A Catastrophic Crash|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> won the 2017 Best Scientific Video award of the National Science Foundation. ''Network Earth'' explores nature's resilience and interconnections between all life on Earth. It accompanied a research paper published in [[Nature (journal)|Nature]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nature.com/nature/videoarchive/network_earth/index.html|title=Nature. Universal resilience patterns in complex networks|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref>
* ''Rise of Partisanship'' shows the party [[Polarization (politics)|polarization]] of the [[United States House of Representatives|House of Representatives]] through time.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/wonk/wp/2015/04/23/a-stunning-visualization-of-our-divided-congress/?amp;utm_term=.a8773a78927c&noredirect=on|title=The Washington Post. A stunning visualization of our divided Congress|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]] |access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/animation-rise-partisanship-congress-house-representatives-60-years-2016-4|title=Business Insider. This 60-second animation shows how divided Congress has become over the last 60 years|website=[[Business Insider]] |access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref> This project was included in "The Best American Infographics 2016".<ref name=":1" />
* ''Redrawing the map of Great Britain from a network of human interactions'' explored a new approach to regional delineation, based on analyzing networks of billions of individual human transactions.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://senseable.mit.edu/network/network&society2.html|title=Senseable City Lab MIT|access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref>
