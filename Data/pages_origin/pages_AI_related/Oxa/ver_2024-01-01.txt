'''Oxa''' (formerly Oxbotica) is an [[autonomous vehicle]] software company, headquartered in [[Oxfordshire|Oxfordshire, England]], and founded by [[Paul Newman (engineer)|Paul Newman]] and Ingmar Posner.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ori.ox.ac.uk/ori-people/paul-newman/|title=Paul Newman|last=Newman|first=Paul|website=Oxford Robotics Institute|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref>

== History ==

In 2013, Newman and Posner led the RobotCar UK project as part of [[Oxford University]]'s [[Department of Engineering Science, University of Oxford|Department of Engineering Science]] Mobile Robotics Group.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ox.ac.uk/research/research-in-conversation/making-sense-numbers/paul-newman |title=Professor Paul Newman {{!}} University of Oxford |website=www.ox.ac.uk|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref> RobotCar became the first [[Vehicular automation|autonomous vehicle]] on UK roads.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://ori.ox.ac.uk/application/robotcar/ |title=RobotCar |website=Oxford Robotics Institute |access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref>

In 2014, the pair used the newly developed technology to found Oxbotica.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.oxbotica.com/about-us/ |title=About Us |website=Oxbotica |access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref>

Oxbotica has raised over $18 million to date and is backed by the [[IP Group]], Parkwalk Advisors and [[Axa XL|AXA XL]].<ref>{{Cite press release |date=2018-09-19 |title=Oxbotica raises Â£14m to accelerate deployment of self-driving vehicles - Oxa |url=https://oxa.tech/news-and-insights/oxbotica-raises-14m-to-accelerate-deployment-of-self-driving-vehicles/ |access-date=2023-08-30 |publisher=Oxa }}</ref> In 2018, Uber's former Head of Business EMEA Fraser Robinson was appointed to the board of directors.<ref>{{Cite news |last=|first=|date=2018-10-09|title=Former Uber Exec Joins Board of Self-Driving Startup|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-10-09/former-uber-executive-joins-board-of-oxford-self-driving-startup |access-date=2019-08-02 |work=Bloomberg.com}}</ref>

In May 2019, Ozgur Tohumcu replaced Dr Graeme Smith as Oxbotica's CEO.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Smith |first=Adrian |date=2019-05-20 |title=UK Autonomous Driving Group Oxbotica Announces New CEO |url=https://www.autofutures.tv/2019/05/20/oxbotica-announces-new-ceo/ |access-date=2021-01-06 |website=Auto Futures}}</ref> Also in 2019, the company opened an office in Toronto, Canada.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ingram |first1=Katie |title=Oxbotica expanding Toronto team to tackle North America's autonomous vehicle software market |url=https://electricautonomy.ca/2023/02/15/oxbotica-autonomous-software-toronto-operations/ |website=Electric Autonomy |access-date=15 Feb 2023}}</ref>

In January 2021, Oxbotica announced it had raised $47 million in a Series B round.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Oxbotica raises $47M to deploy its autonomous vehicle software in industrial applications |url=https://ca.style.yahoo.com/oxbotica-raises-47m-deploy-autonomous-002240071.html |access-date=2021-01-06 |website=ca.style.yahoo.com }}</ref>

In August 2021, the company achieved a safety landmark as the first company to have its autonomy safety case assessed by BSI ([[BSI Group|British Standards Institution]]) against the requirements of the UK Code of Practice 2019, PAS 1881:2020 and PAS 1883:2020, certifying the safety conformity of its autonomous vehicle trials and testing.<ref>{{cite web |last1=O'Halloran |first1=Joe |title=Project Endeavour concludes with autonomous vehicle passenger rides in London |url=https://www.computerweekly.com/news/252505239/Project-Endeavour-concludes-with-autonomous-vehicle-passenger-rides-in-London |website=Computer Weekly |access-date=12 Aug 2021}}</ref> The assessment was completed as part of Project Endeavour, the UK's first multi-city demonstration of autonomous vehicle services and capability.<ref>{{cite web |title=Driving standards for autonomous vehicles with Project Endeavour |url=https://www.bsigroup.com/en-GB/blog/automotive-blog/driving-standards-for-autonomous-vehicles-with-project-endeavour/ |website=www.bsigroup.com/ |access-date=1 March 2021}}</ref>

In December 2021, Gavin Jackson was named CEO.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Spencer |first1=Ben |title=Oxbotica names Gavin Jackson CEO |url=https://www.itsinternational.com/its4/news/oxbotica-names-gavin-jackson-ceo |website=ITS International |access-date=8 December 2021}}</ref>

In January 2023, the company raised $140 million in a Series C round.<ref>{{cite news |title=Oxbotica raises $140 mln to deploy self-driving commercial vehicles |url=https://www.reuters.com/markets/deals/oxbotica-raises-140-mln-deploy-self-driving-commercial-vehicles-2023-01-11/ |work=[[Reuters]] |date=11 January 2023 |access-date=11 January 2023 |last1=Carey |first1=Nick }}</ref>

In May 2023, the company changed its name to Oxa.<ref>{{cite web |title=Oxbotica Unveils New Brand, as Company Scales Globally |url=https://ca.finance.yahoo.com/news/oxbotica-unveils-brand-company-scales-070000615.html |website=Yahoo Finance |access-date=30 May 2023}}</ref>

== Technology ==
Oxbotica's full stack, end-to-end Universal Autonomy software is both vehicle and platform-agnostic, with no dependence on external infrastructure such as [[Global Positioning System|GPS]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sciencebusiness.net/network-news/innovate-uk-oxbotica-develops-ai-based-brain-self-driving-cars|title=Innovate UK: Oxbotica develops AI-based brain for self driving cars|website=Science{{!}}Business|language=en|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref> It can be deployed in any environment and on any terrain.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.globalminingreview.com/finance-business/24052019/oxbotica-appoints-new-ceo-marks-next-stage-of-international-growth/|title=Oxbotica appoints new CEO; marks next stage of international growth|website=Global Mining Review|date=24 May 2019 |language=en|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref> In addition to underground uses, the technology is also useful in natural canyons and forests, where GPS signals are weak or non-existent, but also in "urban canyons" - cities with tall buildings that obstruct GPS signals for proper navigation.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Shah|first=Sooraj|date=2020-07-14|title=How Autonomous Cars Are Overcoming GPS Signal Loss With Radar|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/soorajshah/2020/07/14/how-autonomous-cars-are-overcoming-gps-signal-loss-with-radar/|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-07-14|website=Forbes|language=en}}</ref>

== Public deployments ==

The [[LUTZ Pathfinder]] pod had its first public demonstration in February 2015 in [[Milton Keynes]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ori.ox.ac.uk/projects/lutz-self-driving-pods/|title=LUTZ Pathfinder|last=rosemary|website=Oxford Robotics Institute|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref> The Government-funded project was designed to ensure that autonomous vehicles could comply with the [[Highway Code]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2016/oct/11/self-driving-car-first-uk-test-milton-keynes-driverless-lutz-pathfinder|title=Self-driving car tested for first time in UK in Milton Keynes|last=Davies|first=Rob|date=2016-10-11|work=The Guardian|access-date=2019-08-02|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> The pod featured autonomous control software from Oxbotica, including 19 sensors, cameras, radar and [[Lidar]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ts.catapult.org.uk/innovation-centre/cav/cav-projects-at-the-tsc/self-driving-pods/|title=Driverless pods|website=Catapult|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref>

As part of the GATEway Project in 2017, Oxbotica trialled seven autonomous shuttle buses in [[Greenwich]], navigating a two-mile riverside path near London's [[The O2 Arena|O2 Arena]] on a route also used by pedestrians and cyclists.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sustainable-business/2017/apr/13/driverless-cars-will-make-our-roads-safer-says-oxbotica-co-founder|title=Driverless cars will make our roads safer, says Oxbotica co-founder|last=Balch|first=Oliver|date=2017-04-13|work=The Guardian|access-date=2019-08-02|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> Oxbotica ran the UK's first trial of autonomous grocery deliveries with British online supermarket [[Ocado]] in London as the next evolution of the GATEway Project.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.engadget.com/2017/06/29/cargopod-ocado-oxbotica-driverless-delivery-van/|title=Ocado's driverless delivery van is a glimpse of the future|website=Engadget|language=en|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref>

In 2018, Oxbotica deployed its [[autonomous vehicle]] software at London's [[Gatwick Airport]], which subsequently became the first airport in the world to trial an autonomous shuttle service.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.zdnet.com/article/gatwick-airport-to-trial-british-self-driving-car-system-from-oxbotica/|title=Gatwick Airport to trial British self-driving car system from Oxbotica|last=Schofield|first=Jack|website=ZDNet|language=en|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref> The electric-powered autonomous vehicles transported staff via airside roads between the airport's North and South terminals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mediacentre.gatwickairport.com/press-releases/2018/18_03_16_autonomous_vehicles.aspx|title=Gatwick to be first airport in the world to trial autonomous vehicles to shuttle staff across the airfield|website=www.mediacentre.gatwickairport.com|language=en|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref> An airside trial of Oxbotica's technology was then successfully completed at Heathrow Airport in partnership with IAG Cargo, the first airside trial of an autonomous vehicle at a UK airport.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://interestingengineering.com/heathrow-airport-conducts-first-trail-of-newest-autonomous-airside-vehicles|title=Heathrow Airport Conducts First Trail of Newest Autonomous Airside Vehicles|date=2018-03-29|website=interestingengineering.com|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref> The Oxbotica-designed CargoPod ran autonomously along a cargo route around the airside perimeter for three weeks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lloydsloadinglist.com/freight-directory/adviceandinsight/IAG-Cargo-trials-UK%E2%80%99s-first-autonomous-airport-vehicle/71653.htm#.XUMELpNKiYU|title=IAG Cargo trials UK's first autonomous airport vehicle - Lloyd's Loading List|website=www.lloydsloadinglist.com|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref>

As part of the UK Centre for Connected and Autonomous Vehicles-funded DRIVEN project, Oxbotica is developing and deploying a fleet of [[Ford Motor Company|Ford]] Fusion autonomous vehicles running in both London and Oxford on public roads, and in conjunction with its consortium partners, running real-time insurance.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/case-studies/oxbotica-ai-firm-develops-brain-for-autonomous-vehicles|title=Oxbotica: AI firm develops 'brain' for autonomous vehicles|website=GOV.UK|language=en|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref> [[Axa XL|AXA XL]] is partnering with Oxbotica on the development of smart insurance products using Oxbotica's autonomy technology to improve road safety.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://axaxl.com/media/xl-catlin-signs-landmark-agreement-with-oxbotica|title=XL Catlin Signs Landmark Agreement With Oxbotica|access-date=2 August 2019}}</ref>

In 2018, Oxbotica announced a partnership with London taxi firm [[Addison Lee]] to develop and deploy autonomous taxis in the city of London by 2021.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2018/oct/22/self-driving-cars-london-addison-lee-oxbotica-huge-leap|title=Addison Lee aims to deploy self-driving cars in London by 2021|last=correspondent|first=Gwyn Topham Transport|date=2018-10-22|work=The Guardian|access-date=2019-08-02|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> A 3D street mapping exercise is already being conducted in London's [[Canary Wharf]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.addisonlee.com/addlib/addison-lee-group-oxbotica-begin-mapping-canary-wharf-autonomous-vehicle-services/|title=Addison Lee Group and Oxbotica begin mapping Canary Wharf for autonomous vehicle services|website=Addison Lee|language=en-GB|access-date=2019-08-02}}</ref>

In 2019, Oxbotica deployed a fleet of their autonomous technology within [[Ford Mondeo]] cars on public roads in [[Stratford, London]] to test their use in city environments.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2019/oct/03/driverless-cars-in-new-london-trial-in-complex-urban-environment|title='It's going to be a revolution': driverless cars in new London trial|last=correspondent|first=Gwyn Topham Transport|date=2019-10-03|work=The Guardian|access-date=2019-11-05|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077}}</ref> This Â£13.2m project is in collaboration with The DRIVEN Project to develop self-driving cars.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://trl.co.uk/news/news/driven-project-demonstrates-how-driverless-cars-can-operate-safely-london|title=DRIVEN project demonstrates how driverless cars can operate safely in London|date=2019-09-30|website=TRL|language=en|access-date=2019-11-05}}</ref>
