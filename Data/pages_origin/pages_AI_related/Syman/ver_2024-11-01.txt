SYMAN is an [[artificial intelligence]] technology that uses data from [[social media]] profiles to identify trends in the job market.<ref name=techcrunch>{{cite web |url=https://techcrunch.com/2013/05/08/identified-looks-to-solve-social-medias-dirty-data-problem-for-recruiters-with-help-from-former-linkedin-data-gurus/ |title=Identified Looks To Solve Social Media's Dirty Data Problem For Recruiters With Help From Former LinkedIn Data Gurus |last=Emerson |first=Rip |publisher=Tech Crunch |date=May 8, 2013 |accessdate=September 27, 2013}}</ref><ref name=identified>{{cite web |url=http://blog.identified.com/2013/05/introducing-syman-technology.html |title=Introducing SYMAN Technology |last=Wallace |first=Brendan |date=May 8, 2013 |accessdate=September 27, 2013}}</ref><ref name=startup>{{cite web |url=http://www.startuptip.com/2013/05/09/identifieds-artificial-intelligence-technology-syman-to-turn-social-media-data-into-actionable-insights/ |title=SYMAN to turn social media data into actionable insights |date=May 9, 2013 |accessdate=September 27, 2013}}</ref> SYMAN is designed to organize actionable data for products and services including recruiting, [[human capital]] management, CRM, and marketing.<ref name=techcrunch /><ref name = journal>{{cite web | url = http://digitaljournal.com/article/352135 | title = SYMAN search makes all search methods obsolete? It just might| date = 13 June 2013| publisher = Digital Journal | accessdate = 27 September 2013 }}</ref>

SYMAN was developed with a $21 million series B financing round secured by [[Identified (company)|Identified]], which was led by VantagePoint Capital Partners and Capricorn Investment Group.<ref name=techcrunch /><ref name=techcrunch2>{{cite web |url=https://techcrunch.com/2012/06/05/identified-series-b/ |title=Gamifying The Job Search: Identified Hits 10M Users, Nabs $21M In Series B Funding |last=Emerson |first=Rip |date=June 5, 2012 |accessdate=September 27, 2013}}</ref>
