'''Valve Index''' is a [[virtual reality headset]] created and manufactured by [[Valve Corporation|Valve]]. Announced on April 30, 2019, the headset released on June 28, 2019.<ref name="ars-reveal"/><ref>{{cite web |last1=Higham |first1=Michael |title=Valve Index Impressions - A Necessary But Incremental Step For PC VR |url=https://www.gamespot.com/articles/valve-index-impressions-a-necessary-but-incrementa/1100-6467377/ |website=GameSpot |accessdate=9 June 2019}}</ref> The headset uses a 1440×1600 [[LCD]] panel for each eye for a combined resolution of 2880×1600. The panels are full [[RGB]] and can operate at refresh rates of 80&nbsp;Hz, 90&nbsp;Hz, 120&nbsp;Hz, or a currently experimental 144&nbsp;Hz mode.<ref name="indexspecs-official"/> The headset uses an improved version of Valve's Lighthouse tracking system.<ref name="ars-reveal"/> The headset is intended to be used with the Valve Index Controllers, known during development as the Knuckles Controllers,<ref name="roadtovr-leak"/> but is also backwards compatible with the [[HTC Vive]] and HTC Vive Pro controllers.<ref name="index-storepage"/> Valve Index supports both the [[Windows]] and [[Linux]] operating systems.<ref name="index-storepage"/><ref name="tomshardware-linuxsupport"/>
