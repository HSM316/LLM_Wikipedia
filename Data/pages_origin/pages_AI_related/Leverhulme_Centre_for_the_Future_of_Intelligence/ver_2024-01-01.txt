The '''Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence''' ('''CFI''') is an interdisciplinary research centre within the [[University of Cambridge]] that studies [[artificial intelligence]].<ref name="camresnews">{{cite web|url=http://www.cam.ac.uk/research/news/the-future-of-intelligence-cambridge-university-launches-new-centre-to-study-ai-and-the-future-of|title=The future of intelligence: Cambridge University launches new centre to study AI and the future of humanity|date=3 December 2015|publisher=University of Cambridge|access-date=4 December 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cambridge-news.co.uk/Cambridge-University-launches-10-million-AI/story-28287166-detail/story.html|title=Cambridge University launches £10 million AI research centre, to 'move away from science fiction'|author=Care, Adam|publisher=[[Cambridge News]]|access-date=4 December 2015}}</ref> It is funded by the [[Leverhulme Trust]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://lcfi.ac.uk/about/|title=About|website=lcfi.ac.uk|access-date=2019-07-16}}</ref>

The Centre brings together academics from the fields of computer science, philosophy, social science and others. The centre works with the [[Oxford Martin School]] at the University of Oxford, [[Imperial College London]], and the [[University of California, Berkeley]]<ref name="camresnews" /> and has a memorandum of understanding with the [[Coral Bell School of Asia Pacific Affairs]] at the [[Australian National University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://bellschool.anu.edu.au/news-events/stories/6455/bell-school-signs-mou-cambridge-university-ai-centre|title=Bell School signs MoU with Cambridge University AI centre|date=October 5, 2018|website=Coral Bell School of Asia Pacific Affairs, ANU}}</ref>

== Programmes ==
The CFI research is structured in a series of programmes and research exercises. The topics of the programmes range from algorithmic transparency to exploring the implications of AI for democracy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://lcfi.ac.uk/projects/|title=Programmes|website=lcfi.ac.uk|access-date=2019-07-16}}</ref>

* AI: Futures and Responsibility
* AI: Trust and Society
* Kinds of Intelligence
* AI: Narrative and Justice
* Philosophy and Ethics of AI

In July 2019, Leverhulme released the Animal-AI Olympics competition, featuring tests ordinarily used to test [[animal intelligence]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Whipple |first1=Tom |title=Forget chess, build a robot that can outwit a chicken |url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/forget-chess-build-a-robot-that-can-outwit-a-chicken-xq379kh2c |accessdate=17 July 2019 |work=The Times |date=10 April 2019 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=AIs go up against animals in an epic competition to test intelligence |url=https://www.newscientist.com/article/2197791-ais-go-up-against-animals-in-an-epic-competition-to-test-intelligence/ |accessdate=17 July 2019 |work=[[New Scientist]] |date=2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Crosby |first1=Matthew |title=Animal-AI Olympics |url=http://animalaiolympics.com/ |website=animalaiolympics.com}}</ref>
