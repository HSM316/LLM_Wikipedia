'''Behavior informatics''' ('''BI''') is the informatics of behaviors so as to obtain behavior intelligence and behavior insights.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Cao|first=Longbing|date=|year=2010|title=In-depth Behavior Understanding and Use: the Behavior Informatics Approach|url=|journal=Information Science|volume=180|issue=17|pages=3067–3085|doi=10.1016/j.ins.2010.03.025|pmid=|access-date=}}</ref>

Different from applied behavior analysis <ref>{{Cite book|title=Handbook of Applied Behavior Analysis|last=Fisher|first=Wayne W.|last2=Piazza|first2=Cathleen C.|last3=Roane|first3=Henry S. (eds.)|publisher=The Guilford Press|year=2011|isbn=|location=|pages=}}</ref> from the psychological perspective, BI builds computational theories, systems and tools to qualitatively and quantitatively model, represent, analyze, and manage behaviors of individuals, groups and/or organizations [https://www.computer.org/csdl/mags/ex/2014/04/mex2014040062.pdf].

BI is built on classic study of behavioral science,<ref>{{Cite book|title=Applied Statistics for the Behavioral Sciences: Applying Statistical Concepts|last=Hinkle|first=D.E.|last2=Wiersma|first2=W.|last3=Jurs|first3=S.G.|publisher=Wadsworth Publishing|year=2002|isbn=|location=|pages=}}</ref> including [[behavior modeling]], [[applied behavior analysis]], [[behavior analysis]], [[behavioral economics]], and [[organizational behavior]]. Typical BI tasks consist of individual and group behavior formation, representation,<ref name=":1">{{Cite journal|last=Wang|first=Can|date=|year=2015|title=Formalization and Verification of Group Behavior Interactions|url=|journal=IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems|volume=45|issue=8|pages=1109–1124|doi=|pmid=|access-date=|display-authors=etal}}</ref> computational modeling,<ref>{{Cite book|title=Computational Modeling of Behavior in Organizations: The Third Scientific Discipline|last=Ilgen|first=D.R.|last2=Hulin.|first2=C.L. (Eds.)|publisher=American Psychological Association|year=2000|isbn=|location=|pages=}}</ref> analysis,<ref>{{Cite book|title=Behavior Analysis and Learning|last=Pierce|first=W.D.|last2=Cheney|first2=C.D.|publisher=Psychology Press|year=2008|isbn=|location=|pages=}}</ref> learning,<ref>{{Cite book|title=Human Behavior Learning and Transfer|last=Xu|first=Y.S.|last2=Lee|first2=K.C.|publisher=CRC Press|year=2005|isbn=|location=|pages=}}</ref> simulation,<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|title=Behavioral Modeling and Simulation: From Individuals to Societies|last=Zacharias|first=G.L.|last2=MacMillan|first2=J. (Eds.)|publisher=National Academies Press|year=2008|isbn=|location=|pages=}}</ref> and understanding of behavior impact, utility, non-occurring behaviors etc. for behavior intervention and management.

== Behavior analytics ==
Behavior informatics covers ''behavior analytics'' which focuses on analysis and learning of behavioral data.

== Behavior ==
From an informa[[Informatics|t]]<nowiki/>ics perspective, a [[behavior]] consists of four key elements: actors (behavioral subjects and objects), operations (actions, activities) and interactions (relationships), and their properties. A behavior can be represented as a behavior vector, all behaviors of an actor or an actor group can be represented as behavior sequences and multi-dimensional behavior matrix.
