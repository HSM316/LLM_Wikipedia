The '''International Conference on Learning Representations''' ('''ICLR''')  is a [[machine learning]] [[Academic conference|conference]] held every spring. The conference includes invited talks as well as oral and poster presentations of refereed papers. Since its inception in 2013, ICLR has employed an [[open peer review]] process to referee paper submissions (based on models proposed by [[Yann LeCun]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://yann.lecun.com/ex/pamphlets/publishing-models.html|title=Proposal for A New Publishing Model in Computer Science|website=yann.lecun.com}}</ref>). In 2019, there were 1591 paper submissions, of which 500 accepted with poster presentations (31%) and 24 with oral presentations (1.5%).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://openreview.net/group?id=ICLR.cc/2019/Conference|title=ICLR 2019 Conference|website=openreview.net}}</ref>. In 2021, there were 2997 paper submissions, of which 860 were accepted (29%).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://openreview.net/group?id=ICLR.cc/2021/Conference|title=ICLR 2021 Conference|website=openreview.net}}</ref>.
Along with [[ICML]] and [[NeurIPS]], ICLR is one of the three major [[machine learning]] and [[artificial intelligence]] conferences, and has the highest impact of the three.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-07|title=Artificial Intelligence - Google Scholar Metrics|url=https://scholar.google.es/citations?view_op=top_venues&hl=en&vq=eng_artificialintelligence|access-date=2020-10-07|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201007212857/https://scholar.google.es/citations?view_op=top_venues&hl=en&vq=eng_artificialintelligence|archive-date=2020-10-07}}</ref>
