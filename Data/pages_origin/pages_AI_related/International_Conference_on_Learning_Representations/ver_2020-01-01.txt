The '''International Conference on Learning Representations''' ('''ICLR''')  is a [[machine learning]] [[Academic conference|conference]] held every Spring.  The conference includes invited talks as well as oral and poster presentations of refereed papers. The first ICLR was held in [[Scottsdale, Arizona]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://deeplearning.net/2012/12/13/first-international-conference-on-learning-representationsiclr-2013/|title=First International Conference on Learning Representations(ICLR 2013) « Deep Learning|website=deeplearning.net}}</ref> Since its inception in 2013, ICLR has employed an [[open peer review]] process to referee paper submissions (based on models proposed by [[Yann LeCun]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://yann.lecun.com/ex/pamphlets/publishing-models.html|title=Proposal for A New Publishing Model in Computer Science|website=yann.lecun.com}}</ref>). In 2019, there were 1591 paper submissions, of which 500 accepted with poster presentations (31%) and 24 with oral presentations (1.5%).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://openreview.net/group?id=ICLR.cc/2019/Conference|title=ICLR 2019 Conference|website=openreview.net}}</ref>
