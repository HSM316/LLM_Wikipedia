'''Nexiona Connectocrats''' is a [[Spain|Spanish]] [[software company]] that develops [[Internet of things|Internet of Things]] (IoT) solutions. Jaume Rey, a technology entrepreneur,  founded  M2M Cloud Factory in 2011 and renamed it Nexiona Connectocrats in 2016. Nexiona is based in  [[Barcelona]] and helps integrate hardware and software to build private IoT platforms on the [[Cloud computing|Cloud]] or on site.<ref>{{cite news |title= Conectar el Támesis al internet de las cosas  |url= http://www.elmundo.es/economia/2015/01/26/54c29853e2704e1f618b45e4.html   |work= [[El Mundo (Spain)]] |accessdate= |date= }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=  Una empresa española avanza en la monitorización y control de IoT  |url= http://www.pcworld.es/business-ti/una-empresa-espanola-avanza-en-la-monitorizacion-y-control-de-iot   |work= [[PC World |PC World (Spain)]] |accessdate= |date= 11 February 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title= Crean un programa que controla el estado de máquinas y objetos desde Internet   |url=  http://www.finanzas.com/noticias/empresas/20150210/crean-programa-controla-estado-2867339.html   |work= [[Inversión y Finanzas]]   |accessdate= |date= 10 February 2015 }}</ref>

==History==

In 2011, Rey, the company CEO, founded M2M Cloud Factory dedicated to the remote maintenance via the Internet of [[Internet of things|connected devices]].<ref>{{cite news |title=  Tech Data vertreibt ab sofort IoT-Lösungen von Nexiona  |url=  https://www.it-business.de/tech-data-vertreibt-ab-sofort-iot-loesungen-von-nexiona-a-634525/   |work= IT Business (Germany) |accessdate= |date=17 August 2017 }}</ref> Then, the company developed the MIIMETIQ technology as the core of all its current products with a focus on [[industry 4.0]], [[building automation]], [[eHealth|e-health]] and the [[retail]] industry.<ref>{{cite news |title=  Crean un programa que controla el estado de máquinas y objetos desde Internet  |url= http://www.lavanguardia.com/tecnologia/20150210/54427074069/crean-un-programa-que-controla-el-estado-de-maquinas-y-objetos-desde-internet.html    |work= [[La Vanguardia]] Technologia  |accessdate= |date= 10 February 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title= MIIMETIQ, un programa que controla el estado de cualquier objeto cotidiano desde Internet    |url= received a grant of €1,000,000 from [[Framework Programmes for Research and Technological Development#Horizon 2020|Horizon 2020]], a [[European Union]] research and innovation programme for [[Small In 2016, the company decided to adopt the name Nexiona Connectocrats and expand its operations to [[Oxford]], [[United Kingdom|U.K.]] and [[Munich]], [[Germany]].
