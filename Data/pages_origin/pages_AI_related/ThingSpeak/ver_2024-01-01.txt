'''ThingSpeak''' is an [[open-source software|open-source]] software written in [[Ruby (programming language)|Ruby]] which allows users to communicate with [[Internet of Things|internet enabled devices]].<ref>{{citation
|title=github README file
|date=8 October 2021
|url=https://github.com/iobridge/thingspeak/blob/master/README.textile
}}</ref> It facilitates data access, retrieval and logging of data by providing an [[API]] to both the devices and [[social network]] websites.
ThingSpeak was originally launched by [[ioBridge]] in 2010 as a service in support of IoT applications.<ref>{{citation
|title=ioBridge Blog Post
|date=19 December 2014
|url=http://blog.iobridge.com/2014/12/thingspeak-plus-third-party-hardware-an-alternative-to-iobridge-hardware/
}}</ref>

ThingSpeak has integrated support from the numerical computing software [[MATLAB]] from [[MathWorks]],<ref>{{citation
|title=MATLAB support for ThingSpeak
|url=http://www.mathworks.com/hardware-support/thingspeak.html
}}</ref> allowing ThingSpeak users to analyze and visualize uploaded data using MATLAB without requiring the purchase of a MATLAB license from MathWorks.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mathworks.com/products/thingspeak.html|title = ThingSpeak}}</ref>

ThingSpeak has been the subject of articles in specialized "[[Maker culture|Maker]]" websites like [[Instructables]],<ref>{{citation

}}</ref> 
[[The Code Project|Codeproject]],<ref>{{citation
|title=An Introduction to ThingSpeak
|date=23 November 2014
|url=http://www.codeproject.com/Articles/845538/An-Introduction-to-ThingSpeak
}}</ref> and [[Channel 9 (Microsoft)|Channel 9]].<ref>{{citation
