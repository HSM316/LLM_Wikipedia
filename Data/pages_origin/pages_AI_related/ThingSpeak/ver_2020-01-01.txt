According to its developers, "'''ThingSpeak''' is an [[open-source software|open-source]] [[Internet of Things]] (IoT) application and [[API]] to store and retrieve data from things using the [[HTTP]] and MQTT protocol over the Internet or via a Local Area Network. ThingSpeak enables the creation of sensor logging applications, location tracking applications, and a social network of things with status updates".<ref>{{citation
|title=github README file
|url=https://github.com/iobridge/thingspeak/blob/master/README.textile
}}</ref>

ThingSpeak was originally launched by ioBridge in 2010 as a service in support of IoT applications.<ref>{{citation
|title=ioBridge Blog Post
|url=http://blog.iobridge.com/2014/12/thingspeak-plus-third-party-hardware-an-alternative-to-iobridge-hardware/
}}</ref>

ThingSpeak has integrated support from the numerical computing software [[MATLAB]] from [[MathWorks]],<ref>{{citation
|title=MATLAB support for ThingSpeak
|url=http://www.mathworks.com/hardware-support/thingspeak.html
}}</ref> allowing ThingSpeak users to analyze and visualize uploaded data using Matlab without requiring the purchase of a Matlab license from Mathworks.

ThingSpeak has a close relationship with [[Mathworks]], Inc. In fact, all of the ThingSpeak documentation is incorporated into the Mathworks' Matlab documentation [https://www.mathworks.com/help/thingspeak/ site] and even enabling registered Mathworks user accounts as valid login credentials on the ThingSpeak website.<ref>{{citation |title=Blog post by ioBridge President of Software, Hans Scharler |url=http://community.thingspeak.com/forum/general/mathworks-login/#p2033 |access-date=21 Mar 2016}}</ref>  The terms of service<ref>{{citation |title=ThingSpeak.com Terms of Service |url=https://thingspeak.com/pages/terms |access-date=21 Mar 2016}}</ref> and privacy policy<ref>{{citation |title=ThingSpeak.com Privacy Policy |url=https://thingspeak.com/pages/privacy |access-date=21 Mar 2016}}</ref> of ThingSpeak.com are between the agreeing user and Mathworks, Inc.
