'''Notion''' is a [[note-taking]] and [[Collaborative software|collaboration]] application with [[markdown]] support that also integrates tasks, wikis, and databases. The company describes the app as an all-in-one workspace for note-taking, project management and task management.<ref>{{cite web |url = https://androidcommunity.com/notion-goes-where-evernote-dares-not-what-you-need-to-know-20180608/ |title = Notion goes where Evernote dares not: what you need to know |date = 8 June 2018 |work = [[Android Community]]}}</ref> In addition to cross-platform apps, it can be accessed via most [[web browser]]s.<ref>{{cite web |url = https://www.wsj.com/articles/the-only-app-you-need-for-work-life-productivity-1521640800 |title = The Only App You Need for Work-Life Productivity  |date = 21 March 2018 |work = [[The Wall Street Journal]]}}</ref> The company is headquartered in San Francisco, California.

==History==

Notion Labs Inc is a startup based in San Francisco, founded in 2016. Two of the main investors are [[Josh Kopelman]], an American entrepreneur and venture capitalist, and [[Ram Shriram]], a founding board member and one of the first investors in Google.

In June 2018, an official app for Android was released.<ref>{{cite web |url = https://www.theverge.com/2018/6/7/17434754/notion-android-app-notes-productivity-review |title = Notion brings its powerful note-taking app to Android |date = 7 June 2018 |work = [[The Verge]]}}</ref>

In July 2019, the company raised a $10M Series A.<ref>https://news.crunchbase.com/news/notions-new-round-and-the-optimism-of-hot-saas-valuations/</ref>
