The '''Toyota Partner Robots''' are a series of [[humanoid robot]]s developed by [[Toyota]]. They debuted playing music on [[drums]] and [[trumpets]] at the [[Expo 2005|2005 World EXPO]] in [[Aichi, Japan]].
There are 5 robots in all, most of which have different movement systems. The 5 robots are: '''Version 1''' (bipedal robot), '''Version 2''' ([[Segway PT|Segway]]-like wheels), '''Version 3''' (Segway-like wheels), '''Version 4''' (unique wire system) and the '''i-Foot''' (mountable with 2 legs).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.communistrobot.com/robots/47 |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2009-02-23 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090225202117/http://www.communistrobot.com/robots/47 |archivedate=2009-02-25 }}</ref><ref>http://www.toyota.co.jp/en/special/robot/</ref>
In July 2009, Toyota released a video of the running and standing skills of their partner robot. The robot reaches 7&nbsp;km/hour, however walking and running can only be achieved on flat surfaces.<ref>[http://smart-machines.blogspot.com/2009/07/toyotas-running-humanoid-robot.html Omleiding<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> In 2017, Toyota released its third-generation of humanoid robots, T-HR3, which will be used in [[Spaceflight|space travel]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/toyota-unveils-third-generation-humanoid-robot-t-hr3-300559692.html|title=Toyota Unveils Third Generation Humanoid Robot T-HR3|last=Corporation|first=Toyota Motor|website=www.prnewswire.com|language=en|access-date=2017-11-25}}</ref>
