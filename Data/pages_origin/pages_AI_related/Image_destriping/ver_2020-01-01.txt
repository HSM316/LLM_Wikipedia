{{short description|Image processing task}}
'''Image destriping''' is the process of removing stripes or streaks from images and videos. These artifacts plague a range of fields in scientific imaging including [[atomic force microscopy]],<ref name=Chen2011>{{cite journal |last=Chen |first=S. W. |last2=Pellequer |first2=J. L. |date=2011 |title=DeStripe: frequency-based algorithm for removing stripe noises from AFM images. |journal= BMC Structural Biology |volume=11 |pages=1–10}}</ref> light sheet [[fluorescence microscopy]],<ref name=Liang2016>{{cite journal |last=Liang |first=X. |last2=Zang |first2=Y. |last3=Dong |first3=D. | last4=Zhang |first4=L.|last5=Fang |first5=M. |last6=Arranz |first6=A. |last7=Ripoll |first7=J. |last8=Hui |first8=H. |last9=Tian |first9=J. |date=2016 |title=Stripe artifact elimination based on nonsubsampled contourlet transform for light sheet fluorescence microscopy. |journal= Journal of Biomedical Optics |volume=21 |pages=106005–106010 |doi=10.1117/1.jbo.21.10.106005}}</ref> and planetary [[satellite imaging]].<ref name=Rakwatin2007>{{cite journal |last=Rakwatin |first=P. |last2=Takeuchi |first2=W. |last3=Yasuoka |first3=Y. |date=2007 |title=Stripe Noise Reduction in MODIS Data by Combining Histogram Matching With Facet Filter. |journal= IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing |volume=45 |pages=1844–1856 |doi=10.1109/tgrs.2007.895841}}</ref>

The most common [[image processing]] techniques to reduce stripe artifacts is with [[Fourier analysis|Fourier]] filtering.<ref name=Chen2003>{{cite journal |last=Chen |first=J. |last2=Shao |first2=Y |last3=Guo |first3=H. |last4=Wang |first4=W. |last5=Zhu |first5=B. |date=2003 |title=Destriping CMODIS data by power filtering. |journal=IEEE Trans Geosci Remote Sens |volume=41 |pages=2119–2124 |doi=10.1109/tgrs.2003.817206}}</ref> Unfortunately, filtering methods risk altering or suppressing useful image data. Methods developed for multiple-sensor imaging systems in planetary satellites use statistical-based methods to match signal distribution across multiple sensors.<ref name=Gadallah2010>{{cite journal |last=Gadallah |first=F.L. |last2=Csillag |first2=F |last3=Smith |first3=E.J.M. |date=2010 |title=Destriping multisensor imagery with moment matching. |journal= Int J Remote Sens |volume=21 |pages=2505–2511}}</ref> More recently, a new class of approaches leverage compressed sensing, to regularize an optimization problem, and recover stripe free images.<ref name=Fitschen2017>{{cite journal |last=Fitschen |first=J.H. |last2=Ma |first2=J |last3=Schuff |first3=S. |date=2017 |title=Removal of curtaining effects by a variational model with directional forward differences. |journal= Comput Vis Image Underst |volume=155 |pages=24–32 |doi=10.1016/j.cviu.2016.12.008}}</ref><ref name=Hovden2019>{{cite journal |last=Schwartz |first=J. |last2=Jiang |first2=Y |last3=Bassim |first3=N. |last4=Hovden |first4=R. |date=2019 |title=Removing Stripes, Scratches, and Curtaining with Nonrecoverable Compressed Sensing. |journal=Microscopy and Microanalysis}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last=Bouali|first=Marouan|last2=Ladjal|first2=Saïd|date=August 2011|title=Toward Optimal Destriping of MODIS Data Using a Unidirectional Variational Model|url=https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/5752841/|journal=IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing|volume=49|issue=8|pages=2924–2935|doi=10.1109/TGRS.2011.2119399|issn=0196-2892}}</ref>  In many cases, these destriped images have little to no artifacts, even at low signal to noise ratios.<ref name=Hovden2019/>
