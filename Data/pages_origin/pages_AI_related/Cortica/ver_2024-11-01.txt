==Company Founding==

Cortica was founded in 2007 by Igal Raichelgauz, Karina Odinaev and Yehoshua Zeevi.{{cn|date=August 2021}} Together, the founders developed the company’s core technology while at [[Technion – Israel Institute of Technology]]. By combining discoveries in neuroscience with developments in computer programming, the team created technology that possesses the ability to interpret large amounts of visual data with increased accuracy.<ref>{{cite web|title=Power Pitch: Cortica|url=https://www.cnbc.com/id/100588739|website=CNBC.com|date=26 March 2013 |accessdate=31 March 2016}}</ref> This technology, called [[Image2Text]], is based on the founders’ work in digitally replicating cortical neural networks’ ability to identify complex patterns within massive quantities of ambiguous and noisy data.<ref>{{cite book|doi=10.1007/978-3-540-36266-1_12|title = Applications of Soft Computing|volume = 36|pages = 123–131|series = Advances in Intelligent and Soft Computing|year = 2006|last1 = Odinaev|first1 = Karina|last2 = Raichelgauz|first2 = Igal|last3 = Zeevi|first3 = Yehoshua Y.| chapter=Mapping of Natural Patterns by Liquid Architectures Implementing Neural Cliques |isbn = 978-3-540-29123-7}}</ref>

==Research and Technology==

In 2006, Founders Raichelgauz, Odinaev, and Zeevi shared their findings with the 28th IEEE EMBS Annual International Conference in New York in a paper titled, “Natural Signal Classification by Neural Cliques and Phase-Locked Attractors”.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Raichelgauz|first1=Igal|title=2006 International Conference of the IEEE Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society |chapter=Natural Signal Classification by Neural Cliques and Phase-Locked Attractors|chapter-url=http://webee.technion.ac.il/people/zeevi/conferences/94-04030634.pdf|website=technion.ac.il|year=2006 |volume=Suppl |pages=6693–6697 |doi=10.1109/IEMBS.2006.260923 |pmid=17959488 |isbn=1-4244-0032-5 |s2cid=5394070 |accessdate=7 April 2016}}</ref>

That same year, the team also published “Cliques in Neural Ensembles as Perception Carriers"<ref>{{cite conference |last1=Odinaev |first1=K |last2=Raichelgauz |first2=I |last3=Zeevi |first3=YY |book-title=The 2006 IEEE International Joint Conference on Neural Network Proceedings|pages=285–292|publisher=IEEE |doi=10.1109/IJCNN.2006.246693|year=2006|isbn=978-0-7803-9490-2|title=Cliques in Neural Ensembles as Perception Carriers}}</ref>

CB Insights recently identified Cortica as the number one patent holder among AI companies.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cbinsights.com/research/top-artificial-intelligence-startup-patent-holders/|title=Cortica, Numenta Hold Top Patents In Artificial Intelligence|date=2017-04-27|work=CB Insights Research|access-date=2017-09-07|language=en-US}}</ref>

Cortica is researching to develop a machine-learning driving system which can identify objects and pedestrians. Connecting to it, [[Elon Musk]] has been rumored to partner with Cortica for his electric car company, [[Tesla, Inc.|Tesla]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.jpost.com/Jpost-Tech/Billionaire-Elon-Musk-visits-Israel-to-scour-tech-party-in-Jerusalem-hike-Masada-546592|title=Billionaire Elon Musk visits Israel to scour tech, party in Jerusalem, hike Masada|work=The Jerusalem Post {{!}} JPost.com|access-date=2018-03-21}}</ref> However, Tesla denies it stating that Musk did not discuss a collaboration with [[artificial intelligence]] firm Cortica.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-tesla-israel-cortica/elon-musk-in-talks-with-israeli-ai-vision-firm-cortica-report-idUSKBN1GW1BF|title=Tesla denies Musk in talks with Israeli AI vision firm Cortica|date=2018-03-20|work=Reuters|accessdate=2018-03-21}}</ref>

==Funding==

Cortica raised $7 million in its Series A funding round, announced in August 2012. Investors included [[Horizons Ventures]] (the investment firm of [[Hong Kong]] billionaire [[Li Ka-Shing]]), and [[Ynon Kreiz]], the former chairman and CEO of the [[Endemol|Endemol Group]].<ref name=techcrunch2012>{{cite news|url=https://techcrunch.com/2012/08/02/cortica-li-ka-shing/|title = In-Image Ad Startup Cortica Raises $7M From Li Ka-Shing's Horizons Ventures And Ynon Kreiz|last = Ha|first = Anthony|date = August 2, 2012|accessdate = February 11, 2014|work = [[TechCrunch]]}}</ref><ref name=venturebeat2012>{{cite news|url=https://venturebeat.com/2012/08/02/cortica-gets-7m-to-bestow-computers-with-the-power-of-sight/|title = Cortica gets $7M to endow computers with the power of sight|last = Grant|first = Rebecca|date = August 2, 2012|accessdate = February 11, 2014|work = [[VentureBeat]]}}</ref>

In May 2013, it was announced that Cortica had raised $1.5 million from Russian firm [[Mail.ru Group]].<ref name=venturebeatmay2013>{{cite news|url=https://venturebeat.com/2013/05/28/cortica-funding-mail-ru-group/|title = Image recognition startup Cortica nabs $1.5M from Russian tech leader Mail.Ru|last = Ludwig|first = Sean|date = May 28, 2013|accessdate = February 11, 2014|work = [[VentureBeat]]}}</ref><ref name=tnw>{{cite web|url=https://thenextweb.com/insider/2013/05/28/israel-based-cortica-raises-1-5m-from-mail-ru-to-fund-its-image2text-visual-search-technology/|title = Israel-based Cortica raises $1.5M from Mail.Ru to fund its Image2Text visual search technology|last = Yeung|first = Ken|publisher = The Next Web|date = May 28, 2013|accessdate = February 11, 2014}}</ref> It later transpired that this was a part of Cortica's Series B funding round for $6.4 million, announced in June 2013. The round was led by Horizons Ventures, with participation from the Russian firm [[Mail.ru Group]] and other angel investors.<ref name=techcrunch2013>{{cite news|url=https://techcrunch.com/2013/06/19/cortica-series-b/|title = Cortica Raises $6.4M To Improve Visual Search And In-Image Ads|date = June 19, 2013|accessdate = February 11, 2014|work = [[TechCrunch]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://venturebeat.com/2013/06/19/fabulous-funding-wednesday-from-gay-social-network-to-billion-dollar-design-store/|title = FABulous funding Wednesday: From gay social network to billion-dollar design store|last = Koetsier|first = John|date = June 19, 2013|accessdate = February 11, 2014|work = [[VentureBeat]]}}</ref>
In its fourth funding round, Cortica has raised $20 million, bringing the total investments to $38 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.geektime.com/2014/03/10/cortica-raises-20m-in-fourth-round-funding/|title = Cortica raises $20M in fourth round funding|last = Henn|first = Idan|date = March 10, 2014|accessdate = April 24, 2014|publisher = geektime}}</ref> According to a report from The Israeli lead Daily economic newspaper, [[TheMarker]], the fourth round was led by a strategic Chinese investor who will probably help the company expand into the Asian market.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.haaretz.com/business/daily-roundup/.premium-1.578951/|title = Cortica raises $20m from Chinese investors|last = Hirschauge|first = Orr|date = March 11, 2014|accessdate = April 24, 2014 |newspaper=Haaretz}}</ref>
