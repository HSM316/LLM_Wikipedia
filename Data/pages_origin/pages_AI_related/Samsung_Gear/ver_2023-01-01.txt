'''Samsung Gear''' is a line of [[wearable computing]] devices produced by [[Samsung Electronics]]. The first device in the series, the [[Samsung Galaxy Gear]] smartwatch, was announced in 2013.<ref name="engadget-galaxygearunveil">{{cite web|title=Samsung unveils Galaxy Gear smartwatch with 1.63-inch AMOLED touchscreen, built-in camera, 70 apps|url=https://www.engadget.com/2013/09/04/samsung-unveils-galaxy-gear/|work=Engadget|access-date=9 December 2013}}</ref> Since then, the line has expanded to include fitness bands and earbuds, as well as more smartwatches.

In February 2019, Samsung re-branded smartwatches, fitness bands, and earbuds under the [[Samsung Galaxy#Wearables|Samsung Galaxy]] brand.<ref>{{cite web |title=Samsung Announces New Galaxy Watch Active, Galaxy Fit, and Galaxy Buds |url=https://www.droid-life.com/2019/02/20/samsung-galaxy-wearables-2019/ |website=Droid Life |publisher=Droid Life|date=2019-02-20 }}</ref> Samsung shipped approximately 31 million wearable devices in 2019,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.idc.com/getdoc.jsp?containerId=prUS46122120|title=Shipments of Wearable Devices Reach 118.9 Million Units in the Fourth Quarter and 336.5 Million for 2019, According to IDC|website=IDC: The premier global market intelligence company|access-date=2020-04-09|archive-date=2021-01-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210114120031/https://www.idc.com/getdoc.jsp?containerId=prUS46122120|url-status=dead}}</ref> including 7.4 million true wireless earbuds.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.androidcentral.com/samsung-comes-distant-third-place-tws-headphone-sales-2019|title=Samsung's true wireless headphone sales in 2019 couldn't compete with Apple|date=2020-01-15|website=Android Central|access-date=2020-04-08}}</ref>
