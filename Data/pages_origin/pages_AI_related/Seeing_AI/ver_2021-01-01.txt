}}'''Seeing AI''' is an [[artificial intelligence]] application developed by [[Microsoft]] for [[iOS]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/seeing-ai-talking-camera-for-the-blind/id999062298|title=Seeing AI on the App Store|website=App Store|language=en-US|access-date=2018-05-20}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Novet |first1=Jordan |title=Microsoft has a new app that tells the visually impaired what's in front of them |url=https://www.cnbc.com/2017/07/12/microsoft-launches-seeing-ai-app-for-ios.html |website=CNBC |accessdate=6 June 2019}}</ref> Seeing AI uses the device camera to identify people and objects, and then the app audibly describes those objects for people with visual impairment.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Shah |first1=Saqib |title=Microsoft's Seeing AI app for the blind now reads handwriting |url=https://www.engadget.com/2017/12/14/microsoft-seeing-ai-app-update/ |website=Endgadget |accessdate=6 June 2019}}</ref>

== Capabilities ==

Seeing AI is primarily used to describe short text, documents, products, people, currency scenery, colors, handwriting and light.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bishop |first1=Todd |title=Microsoft's new AI app describes the world for the visually impaired — now available on iPhone |url=https://www.geekwire.com/2017/microsofts-new-ai-app-describes-world-visually-impaired-now-available-iphone/ |website=Geekwire |accessdate=6 June 2019}}</ref> The app can scan a barcode to describe a product<ref>{{cite web |title=Microsoft's 'Seeing AI' app helps vision-impaired users "see" the world through words |url=https://digiaccessorg.wordpress.com/2019/04/14/microsofts-seeing-ai-app-helps-vision-impaired-users-see-the-world-through-words/ |website=DigiAccess |accessdate=6 June 2019}}</ref> and uses sounds to assist the user in focusing on the barcode.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tracy |first1=Phillip |title=How Microsoft's new app for the blind and visually impaired holds up |url=https://www.dailydot.com/debug/microsoft-seeing-ai-app/ |website=The Daily Dot |accessdate=6 June 2019}}</ref> When the app describes people, it attempts to estimate the person's age, gender, and emotional status.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kelley |first1=Steven |title=Seeing AI: Artificial Intelligence for Blind and Visually Impaired Users |url=https://www.visionaware.org/info/everyday-living/helpful-products/using-apps/seeing-ai-app/1235 |website=Vision Aware |accessdate=6 June 2019}}</ref> Additionally, in a test run by German journalists in December 2019, ''Seeing AI'' apparently used some sort of [[Facial recognition system]] to identify people on photographs by name.<ref>[https://www.faz.net/aktuell/technik-motor/digital/microsoft-app-seeing-ai-16520240.html "Der Blindenhund kennt auch die Namen"] [[Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung]] 5 December 2019</ref>

Some functions are performed on the device, however more complex functions such as describing a scene or recognizing handwriting require an [[Cloud computing|Internet connection]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Vincent |first1=James |title=Microsoft's new iPhone app narrates the world for blind people |url=https://www.theverge.com/2017/7/12/15958174/microsoft-ai-seeing-app-blind-ios |website=The Verge |accessdate=6 June 2019}}</ref>

In December 2017, Seeing AI introduced the ability for [[currency]] recognition for [[United States dollar|US]] and [[Canadian dollar]], [[Pound sterling|British pounds]] and [[Euro]]s.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://blogs.msdn.microsoft.com/accessibility/2017/12/13/seeing-ai-new-features/|title=Updated with currency and color recognition, Seeing AI is available in 35 countries|work=Microsoft Accessibility Blog|access-date=2018-05-20|language=en-US}}</ref>

In December 2019, Seeing AI added support for five more languages, Dutch, French, German, Japanese, Spanish.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://news.microsoft.com/features/bonjour-bienvenidos-seeing-ai-expands-to-5-new-languages/|title=Bonjour! ¡Bienvenidos! Seeing AI expands to 5 new languages|work=Microsoft Features|accessdate=2019-12-05|language=en-AU}}</ref>

Seeing AI is available in 70 countries such as Brazil, Argentina, Australia, Canada, Egypt, Albania, Bhutan, etc. 

Supported on iPhone 5C, 5S and later best performance with iPhone 6S, SE and later models
