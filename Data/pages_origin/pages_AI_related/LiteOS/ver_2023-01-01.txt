'''Huawei LiteOS''' was a lightweight [[real-time operating system]] (RTOS) developed by [[Huawei]].<ref name=Huawei-LiteOS-overview>{{Cite web|url=https://Developer.Huawei.com/ict/en/site-iot/article/liteos-overview|title=Huawei LiteOS: Concept and Value|website=Developer.Huawei.com|publisher=[[Huawei|Huawei Technologies Co., Ltd.]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180912165703/https://developer.huawei.com/ict/en/site-iot/article/liteos-overview|archive-date=12 September 2018|url-status=dead|access-date=12 September 2018}}</ref> It is an [[Open-source software|open source]], [[POSIX]] compliant operating system for [[Internet of things]] (IoT) devices, released under a three-clause [[BSD licenses|BSD license]].<ref name=BetaNews>{{Cite web|url=https://BetaNews.com/2015/05/20/huaweis-liteos-internet-of-things/|title=Huawei's LiteOS Internet of Things operating system is a minuscule 10KB|website=BetaNews.com|publisher=[[BetaNews|BetaNews, Inc.]]|date=20 May 2015|access-date=12 February 2021|archive-url=https://web.Archive.org/web/20150524013504/https://BetaNews.com/2015/05/20/huaweis-liteos-internet-of-things/|archive-date=24 May 2015|url-status=live}}</ref> [[Microcontroller]]s of different architectures such as ARM (M0/3/4/7, A7/17/53, ARM9/11), x86, and RISC-V are supported by the project.  Huawei LiteOS is part of Huawei's '1+8+N' [[Internet of Things]] solution, and has been featured in a number of open source [[Software development kit|development kits]] and industry offerings.<ref>{{Cite web|last=July 2020|first=Naushad K. Cherrayil 09|title=Huawei's "1+8+N" strategy will be a big success in China as it has no competitors|url=https://www.techradar.com/sg/news/huaweis-18n-strategy-will-be-a-big-success-in-china-as-it-has-no-competitors|access-date=2021-06-17|website=TechRadar|language=en}}</ref>

[[Smartwatch]]es by Huawei and its former [[Honor (brand)|Honor brand]] run LiteOS.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.TheVerge.com/circuitbreaker/2019/9/19/20873598/huawei-watch-gt-2-liteos-fitness-price-date-specs|title=Huawei Watch GT 2 runs LiteOS and lasts up to two weeks|first=Thomas|last=Ricker|date=19 September 2019|website=www.TheVerge.com|publisher=[[The Verge]]|access-date=3 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.XDA-Developers.com/honor-magic-watch-2-review/|title=The Honor Magic Watch 2 is a great wearable, but LiteOS is too light|website=www.XDA-Developers.com|publisher=[[XDA Developers]]|date=17 December 2019|access-date=3 February 2021}}</ref> LiteOS has since been incorporated into the IoT-oriented [[HarmonyOS]] with open source [[OpenHarmony]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=Amadeo|first=Ron|date=2021-02-02|title=Huawei's HarmonyOS: "Fake it till you make it" meets OS development|url=https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2021/02/harmonyos-hands-on-huaweis-android-killer-is-just-android/|access-date=2021-02-14|website=Ars Technica|language=en-US}}</ref>

==History==
On 20 May [[:Category:2015 in computing|2015]], at the Huawei Network Conference, [[Huawei]] proposed the '1+2+1' Internet of Things solution and release the IoT operating system named Huawei LiteOS.<ref name=BetaNews/><ref name=Huawei-LiteOS-overview/>

==Key features==
*Lightweight, small kernel; <10&nbsp;[[kilobyte]]s (kB)<ref name=BetaNews/><ref name=Huawei-LiteOS-overview/>
*Energy efficient
*Fast startup within milliseconds
*Support [[Narrowband IoT|NB-IoT]], [[Wi-Fi]], [[Ethernet]], [[Bluetooth Low Energy|BLE]], [[Zigbee]], and other different IoT protocols
*Support access to different cloud platforms
