'''IMeasureU''' ('''IMU''') is a [[wearable technology]] company. They make [[inertial measurement unit]] that analyze running movements and other body movements in elite sports.<ref>[http://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/news/article.cfm?c_id=3&objectid=11284537 Small business: Commercialising innovation - Mark Finch, I Measure U] NZ Herald</ref> They combine the sensor data with computational models to accurately model human body movement. IMeasureU has worked extensively with [[Athletics Australia]] helping their elite runners.<ref>[http://www.nbr.co.nz/article/i-measure-u-launch-kickstarter-campaign-consumer-running-app-bd-167362 I Measure U to launch Kickstarter campaign for consumer running app] NBR</ref>

In July 2017 the company was acquired by Vicon, an English company specializing in [[motion capture]], with the plan to integrate Vicon's camera systems with IMeasureU's sensors.<ref>[http://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/news/article.cfm?c_id=3&objectid=11894925 Kiwi tech helps sports superstars recover] NZ Herald 2</ref>

On 23 June 2015 IMeasureU launched an [[Indiegogo]] campaign seeking to raise US$200,000 to develop a consumer solution that reduces the risk of running-related injuries.<ref>[https://www.indiegogo.com/projects/imu-run-reducing-your-risk-of-running-injury IMU-Run: Reducing Your Risk of Running Injury] indiegogo</ref><ref>[http://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/news/article.cfm?c_id=3&objectid=11414897 Your Business: Wearable tech - Mark Finch, I Measure U] NZ Herald</ref>
