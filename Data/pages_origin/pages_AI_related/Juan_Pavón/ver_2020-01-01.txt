'''Juan Pavón''' (born 19 November 1962) is a Spanish [[computer scientist]], full professor of the [[Complutense University of Madrid]] (UCM). He is a pioneer researcher in the field of [[Software agent|Software Agents]], co-creator of the [[Foundation for Intelligent Physical Agents|FIPA]] MESSAGE and [[INGENIAS]] methodologies, and founder and director of the research group ''GRASIA: GRoup of Agent-based, Social and Interdisciplinary Applications'' at [[Complutense University of Madrid|UCM]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sistedes.es/grupo-investigacion/grupo-investigacion-aplicaciones-sociales-e-interdisciplinares-basadas-agentes-grasia|title=Grupo de investigación en Aplicaciones Sociales e Interdisciplinares basadas en Agentes (GRASIA). Grupos de Investigación|website=Sistedes|language=es|access-date=2019-12-21}}</ref><ref name=grasiagroup>{{Cite web
  | last = Complutense University of Madrid
  | title = UCM GRASIA Research Group
  | url= http://grasia.fdi.ucm.es/
  | accessdate = 25 December 2015}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://eshorizonte2020.es/expressions-of-interests/juan-pavon|title=Juan Pavón / Expressions of interest of Spanish institutions / Horizonte2020|website=eshorizonte2020.es|access-date=2019-12-21}}</ref> He is known for his work in the field of [[Artificial intelligence|Artificial Intelligence]], specifically in [[agent-oriented software engineering]].<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Zambonelli | first1 = Franco | last2 = Omicini | first2 = Andrea | year = 2004 | title = Challenges and research directions in agent-oriented software engineering | url = | journal = Autonomous Agents and Multi-Agent Systems | volume = 9 | issue = 3| pages = 253–283 | doi=10.1023/b:agnt.0000038028.66672.1e| citeseerx = 10.1.1.146.2928 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cernuzzi | first1 = Luca | last2 = Cossentino | first2 = Massimo | last3 = Zambonelli | first3 = Franco | year = 2005 | title = Process models for agent-based development | url = | journal = Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence | volume = 18 | issue = 2| pages = 205–222 | doi=10.1016/j.engappai.2004.11.015}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.xataka.com/robotica-e-ia/los-academicos-nos-dicen-la-verdad-sobre-el-estado-del-hype-de-la-inteligencia-artificial|title=Los académicos nos dicen la verdad sobre el estado del hype de la Inteligencia Artificial|last=Pastor|first=Javier|date=2015-12-15|website=Xataka|language=es|access-date=2019-12-21}}</ref>

== Biography ==
===Education===
Pavón belongs to the first Spanish generation to get official studies in [[Computer Science]], during the 1980s.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://portal.uned.es/pls/portal/docs/PAGE/UNED_MAIN/LAUNIVERSIDAD/VICERRECTORADOS/EDUPERMANENTE/CONVOCATORIAS%20DE%20PLAZAS/CURRICULUM%20COMISIONES%20134/CV%20PLAZA%20134.48.PDF|title=Juan Pavón CV for Official Commission at UNED|last=|first=|date=2018|website=UNED (National University of Distance Education)|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=21 December 2019}}</ref> He studied [[Computer Science]] at the [[Technical University of Madrid]], graduating in 1985. In 1988, he obtained his PhD in this area with the [[Thesis (academic document)|thesis]]: "Synthesis of communication protocols from service specifications".<ref name="pavon1988synthesis">{{cite thesis

===Career===
He joined the Alcatel R&D department as a systems engineer. There, he worked on the development of software [[Component-based software engineering|component-based]] [[Software architecture|architectures]] for [[Distributed computing|distributed systems]], and its applications for multimedia services over [[broadband networks]] and new generation mobile phones. During the 10 years he worked for the company, he spent periods in Alcatel centers outside Spain, such as France ([[Lannion]] and [[Vélizy]]) and Belgium ([[Namur (city)|Namur]] and [[Antwerp]]).<ref name=":2" /> In this period, he worked several years in the labs of [[Bellcore]] in [[Red Bank, New Jersey]] (USA),<ref name=":3" /> working in the [[TINA-C]] Core Team, and helping to produce [[Software architectural model|architectural models]] for telecommunication services. He then returned to the academic world, as an [[associate professor|Associate Professor]] at the Computer Science School of the Complutense University of Madrid (1997). By then, he researched [[Multi-Agent System]]s within the [[Eurescom]] project P815 "Communications Management Process Integration Using Software Agents" (1999) working with [[Telefónica]] R+D.<ref name="p815">[http://archive.eurescom.eu/public/projects/P800-series/P815/default.asp Eurescom P815 "Communications Management Process Integration Using Software Agents"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304034324/http://archive.eurescom.eu/public/projects/P800-series/P815/default.asp |date= 4 March 2016 }}</ref> His work in several projects combines [[software engineering]] practices and MAS. In the Eurescom P907 "Methodology for Engineering Systems of Software Agents", he co-created the MESSAGE [[Software development methodology|methodology]], currently part of the main [[Foundation for Intelligent Physical Agents|FIPA]]<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=http://www.fipa.org/resources/methodologies.html|title=Methodologies|website=www.fipa.org|access-date=2019-12-21}}</ref> software agent methodologies.<ref name="p907">[http://archive.eurescom.eu/public/projects/P900-series/P907/default.asp Eurescom P907 "Methodology for Engineering Systems of Software Agents"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303213424/http://archive.eurescom.eu/public/projects/P900-series/P907/default.asp|date=3 March 2016}}</ref><ref name=":2" />

In 2000 he established the research group GRASIA<ref name=grasiagroup /> for the research in [[Software agent|Software Agents]] and [[Artificial Intelligence]] in the [[Complutense University of Madrid]].<ref name="grasiagroup" /> He also held several management positions in the university, serving as Vice Dean for four years (1998–2002).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fdi.ucm.es/extranjeros/tour/es/acercade.htm |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2013-01-20 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090717064105/http://www.fdi.ucm.es/extranjeros/tour/es/acercade.htm |archivedate=17 July 2009 }}</ref> Nowadays, he is [[full professor]] at the [[Universidad Complutense Madrid]].

==Work==
===Software Agents Research===
Pavón work has focused on [[Software agent|Software Agents]] and [[Agent-based model|Agent-based simulation]].<ref>{{cite book|last1=Mas|first1=Ana|title=Agentes software y sistemas multiagente: conceptos, arquitecturas y aplicaciones|date=2005|publisher=Prentice Hall|isbn=978-84-205-4367-3}}</ref> His work on [[Software agent|Software Agents]] started at the end of the 1990s, after they emerge as a new paradigm.<ref>Jorge J. Gómez-Sanz, Juan Pavón, Francisco J. Garijo: Intelligent Interface Agents Behavior Modeling. MICAI 2000: 598–609</ref> His first project in the area was funded by [[Telefonica]] R+D, "Communications Management Process Integration Using Software Agents" (1999). Later he attracted funding for research projects from the 5th [[Framework programme|European Framework Programme]] (PSI3, DEMOS) and from Euroscom (P815, P907), all considered early applications of agents to software engineering processes.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.es/books?id=YKH1BwAAQBAJ&dq=PSI3:+Personalized+Service+Integration+Using+Software+Agents|title=Agent-Oriented Software Engineering VI: 6th International Workshop, AOSE 2005, Utrecht, The Netherlands, July 25, 2005. === INGENIAS ===
His main contribution combining agent concepts in software engineering is the [[INGENIAS]] methodology and toolkit. INGENIAS (Engineering for Software Agents) is a complete [[Software framework|framework]] for the analysis, design and implementation of [[multi-agent systems]] (MAS).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sites.google.com/site/pauloalceu/tecnologias/ingenias|title=INGENIAS Methodology|website=sites.google.com|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-21}}</ref><ref name="pavon2003agentOriented">{{cite book|title=Agent oriented software engineering with INGENIAS|last=Pavón|first=Juan|last2=Gómez-Sanz|first2=Jorge|journal=Multi-Agent Systems and Applications III|publisher=Springer Berlin Heidelberg|year=2003|isbn=978-3-540-40450-7|series=Lecture Notes in Computer Science|volume=2691|pages=394–403|doi=10.1007/3-540-45023-8_38|issn=0302-9743}}</ref>

As a result of the research in these years, Jorge J. Gómez-Sanz published in 2002 his PhD thesis "A Methodology for the Development of Multi-Agent Systems" (in Spanish) advised by Francisco Garijo and Juan Pavón. === Agent-based social simulation ===
The level of maturity reached by the INGENIAS framework and related tools, mainly INGENME, in its application to Software Agents, allowed the GRASIA group to consider its application to other domains. [[Agent-based model|Agent-based simulation]] has been one with an immediate application. As in [[Software agent|Software Agents]], agent-based simulation (ABS) relies on the concept of agent, in this case as the basic block to build computational simulations. The conceptual similarities between the concept of agent in both disciplines, and the suitability of models to work with simulations, made of agent-based simulation a sensible extension for INGENIAS work.<ref name="fuentes2012metamodels" /><ref name=":5" />

This line of research started in GRASIA with a direct application of the works in INGENIAS with software agents to ABS for the PhD thesis of Candelaria Sansores "A Methodology for the Study of Artificial Societies" (2007), advised by Pavonl.<ref name="sansores2007metodologia">{{cite thesis
| type = PhD
| first = Candelaria
| last = Sansores
| title = Metodología para el estudio de sociedades artificiales
| publisher = Universidad Complutense de Madrid
| year = 2007
| language = Spanish
}}</ref> This work pointed out the suitability of applying the MDE approach of INGENIAS to this field, but also the problems of researchers without a background on [[Software Engineering]] to use INGENIAS. These first attempts were oriented to simulations intended to verify behavioral principles described by well-known laws, but not to explore simulations based on the use of big amounts of raw data. This [[Data-driven programming|data-driven]] [[agent-based social simulation]] was the subject of the interdisciplinary PhD thesis of [[Samer Hassan]] "Towards a Data Driven Approach for Agent Based Modelling: Simulating Spanish Postmodernization", also advised by Juan Pavón.<ref name="hassan2010towards">{{cite thesis

}}</ref>

The use of INGENIAS for ABS is based on the adoption of MDE to build simulations.<ref name=fuentes2012metamodels /> This approach was developed and validated by GRASIA in different domains, e.g., urbanism and group work.<ref name=hassan2010towards /><ref name="martinez2010herramienta">{{cite thesis
