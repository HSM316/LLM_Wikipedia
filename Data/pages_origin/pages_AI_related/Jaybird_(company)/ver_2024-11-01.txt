'''Jaybird''' is a [[Utah]]-based consumer electronics company owned by [[Logitech]]. The company designs and manufactures [[headphones]] and wearable [[activity trackers]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Jaybird|url=http://www.inc.com/profile/jaybird|publisher=INC}}</ref> The company is mainly known for its line of wireless [[Bluetooth]] sports headsets. Jaybird was founded in 2006 by [[Australians|Australian]] entrepreneur Judd Armstrong.<ref name="malakye_interview">{{cite web|title=Judd Armstrong, Founder and CEO of JayBird |url=https://www.malakye.com/feature/6727/Judd-Armstrong-Founder-and-CEO-of-JayBird |publisher=Malakye |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160302084338/https://www.malakye.com/feature/6727/Judd-Armstrong-Founder-and-CEO-of-JayBird |archivedate=2016-03-02 }}</ref>

==Company history==
Jaybird was founded by Australian entrepreneur Judd Armstrong in 2006.<ref name="malakye_interview" /> Armstrong was disappointed with the performance of headphones available at the time, and wanted to create a product for himself that would be sweat-resistant and non-restrictive for physical activity.<ref>{{cite web|title=The Wireless Powerhouse: JayBird Headphones |url=http://thebestrunningheadphones.com/blog/jaybird-headphones |publisher=The Best Running Headphones |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160302151552/http://thebestrunningheadphones.com/blog/jaybird-headphones |archivedate=2016-03-02 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Devlin|first1=Jone|title=The Wireless Powerhouse: JayBird Headphones|url=http://digital.macdirectory.com/i/867-tune-in/55|publisher=Mac Directory}}</ref> Armstrong placed a few of his prototype products online for sale to recoup production cost.<ref name="malakye_interview" /> After receiving positive feedback from the public, he founded JayBirdGear [[Limited liability company|LLC]] in [[Salt Lake City, Utah]] in 2006.<ref>{{cite web|title=Jaybird|url=http://store.treoo.com/shop-by-brand/jaybird.html|publisher=Treoo}}</ref>

The company's first Bluetooth headset, the JB100 Freedom, was launched in late 2007.<ref>{{cite web|last1=France|first1=Jasmine|title=JayBird JB-100 Bluetooth Stereo Headset review|url=https://www.cnet.com/products/jaybird-jb-100-bluetooth-stereo-headset/|publisher=CNET}}</ref>
In 2014, the company was listed for the first time amongst the 5 top-selling premium headphones along with [[Beats Electronics|Beats]], [[Bose Corporation|Bose]], [[Sennheiser]], and [[Sony]], according to market research company [[NPD Group]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Johnston|first1=Lisa|title=Beats Still Tops In Headphones|url=http://www.twice.com/beats-still-tops-headphones/56044|publisher=Twice|access-date=2016-02-22|archive-date=2016-03-03|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303015138/http://www.twice.com/beats-still-tops-headphones/56044|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Morris|first1=Chris|title=50 Cent opens up on a big Beats Electronics regret|date=18 March 2015|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2015/03/18/50-cent-opens-up-on-a-big-beats-electronics-regret.html|publisher=CNBC}}</ref>

On April 12, 2016, [[Logitech]] acquired the company for $50 million.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://blog.logitech.com/2016/04/12/music-just-got-better/|title=Music Just Got Better - Blog PROD|date=2016-04-12|work=Blog PROD|access-date=2017-09-21|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.engadget.com/2016/04/13/logitech-jaybird/|title=Logitech buys Jaybird for $50 million|work=Engadget|access-date=2017-09-21|language=en-US}}</ref>

In August 2019, Jaybird released the first generation of Vista wireless Bluetooth earbuds. In June 2021, Jaybird released the next iteration, Vista 2.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-05-09 |title=Jaybird Vista 3 |url=https://reviews-technology.com/jaybird-vista-3/ |access-date=2023-09-07 |website=reviews-technology.com |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Reception==
The company's X2 headset model released in 2015 has been included in Best Headphones lists by [[technology]] and gadget publishers such as [[Engadget]],<ref>{{cite web|last1=Dragan|first1=Lauren|title=The best exercise headphones|date=20 July 2023 |url=https://www.engadget.com/2015/04/24/the-best-exercise-headphones/|publisher=EnGadget}}</ref> [[TechCrunch]],<ref>{{cite web|last1=Matney|first1=Lucas|title=The 13 Best Sounding Headphones For Everyone On Your Nice List|url=https://techcrunch.com/gallery/the-12-best-sounding-headphones-for-everyone-on-your-nice-list/|publisher=TechCrunch}}</ref> and [[PC Magazine]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Greenwald|first1=Will|title=The Best Headphones for Running|url=https://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,2817,2410143,00.asp|publisher=PC Mag}}</ref> Tim Gideon, writing for ''PC Magazine'', rated the headset 4 out of 5 stars, and praised it for its "high quality audio performance," and for being "ideal for athletes who need a very secure fit for intense training."<ref name="pcmag_gideon">{{cite web|last1=Gideon|first1=Tim|title=Jaybird X2|url=https://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,2817,2490095,00.asp|publisher=PC Mag}}</ref> Jeff Dunn from [[Business Insider]] included the X2 in his list of "best headphones for working out." Dunn commented, "For the best sound from an exercise-oriented headphone that isn’t outrageously overpriced, go with the Jaybird X2." Dunn warned that finding the best fit for each individual user could take time as the headset is shipped with equipment for the user to configure to their ear size.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Dunn|first1=Jeff|title=These are the best headphones for working out|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/best-exercise-headphones-running-working-out-buying-guide-2015-10|publisher=Business Insider}}</ref>
