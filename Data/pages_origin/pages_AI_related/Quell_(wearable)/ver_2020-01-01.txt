'''Quell''' is a wearable device, manufactured by Neurometrix, that claims to offer relief from chronic pain without the use of drugs.<ref name=medgadget>{{ cite web | publisher = www.medgadget.com | title = Hands-On With Quell Wearable Pain Relief Device
 | author = Scott Jung | date = August 17, 2015 | url = http://www.medgadget.com/2015/08/hands-on-with-quell-wearable-pain-relief-device.html | accessdate = 2015-12-25 }}</ref>  Quell is an FDA approved band worn on the calf and uses [[Transcutaneous Electrical Nerve Stimulation]]  (TENS) technology. <ref name=techtimes>{{ cite web | publisher = Techtimes | title = Quell Promises Pain Relief with New FDA-Approved Gadget
 | author = James Geddes | date = January 9, 2015 | url = http://www.techtimes.com/articles/25629/20150109/ces-2015-quell-promises-pain-relief-with-new-fda-approved-gadget.htm | accessdate = 2015-12-25 }}</ref> Neurometrix reference a small study, funded by the company, to claim efficacy in chronic pain; however, evidence is currently limited<ref name=fnt>{{ cite web | publisher = Fibromyalgia News Today| title = NeuroMetrix Reports Positive Preliminary Results on Quell Wearable Pain Relief Device for Chronic Pain
 | author = Anna Tan | date = August 20, 2015 | url = http://fibromyalgianewstoday.com/2015/08/20/neurometrix-reports-positive-preliminary-results-on-quell-wearable-pain-relief-device-for-chronic-pain/ | accessdate = 2015-12-25 }}</ref>
