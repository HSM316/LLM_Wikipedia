'''Daon''' (pronounced 'day-on') is a [[biometrics]] and identity assurance software company founded in 2000 by Irish entrepreneur [[Dermot Desmond]]. According to the company, the word Daon stems from an "Old Irish word for human".<ref name="about"/> Daon has its US headquarters in Fairfax, Virginia. It also has operations in the [[International Financial Services Centre, Dublin|International Financial Services Center]] (IFSC) in [[Dublin]], Ireland.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.irishtimes.com/business/technology/dermot-desmond-s-daon-to-supply-kiosks-at-dublin-airport-1.2414568 | work = Irish Times | title = Dermot Desmond's Daon to supply kiosks at Dublin Airport | date = 3 November 2015 | accessdate = 13 September 2024 }}</ref> The company has regional and sales offices in Serbia and Australia.{{fact|date=September 2024}}

== History ==
Founded in 2000,<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.independent.ie/business/irish/bush-aide-joins-board-of-irish-biometric-firm/26391755.html | work = Irish Independent | title = Bush aide joins board of Irish biometric firm | date = 29 April 2006 | accessdate = 13 September 2024 | quote = the Irish company [..] was founded in 2000}}</ref> the company was originally based in Dublin, Ireland.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.independent.ie/news/desmonds-daon-gets-us-army-contract/26715045.html | work = Irish Independent | title = Desmond's Daon gets US army contract | date = 20 March 2011 | accessdate = 13 September 2024 | quote = The company, which is headquartered in Dublin, is one of the world leaders in biometric scanning technology }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.irishexaminer.com/business/arid-10059324.html | work = Irish Examiner | title = Daon to take on Japanese market | date = 19 November 2004 | accessdate = 13 September 2024 | quote = Dublin-based Daon [...] }}</ref>

As of 2006, the company's board included former [[United States Secretary of Homeland Security]], [[Tom Ridge]].<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.nytimes.com/2006/04/28/washington/trade-group-confirms-deal-with-tiny-firm-for-id-cards.html | work = New York Times | title = Trade Group Confirms Deal With Tiny Firm for ID Cards | accessdate = 13 September 2024 | date = 28 April 2006 }}</ref> In 2006, Daon and the [[American Association of Airport Executives]] (AAAE) entered into a joint venture to create the Security Biometric Clearing Network (SBCN) to provide services for [[Biometrics|biometric identity management]], including enrollment, [[Background check|background checking]], secure biometric and biographic information storage, and card issuance.<ref>{{Cite web |date=November 12, 2007 |title=Traveler Biometrics Is Here - |url=https://securitytoday.com/Articles/2007/11/12/Traveler-Biometrics-Here.aspx |access-date=2024-04-07 |website=Security Today |language=en-US}}</ref>

In August 2008, Daon acquired software assets and associated staff for physical security interconnectivity from Enterprise Air, a New York-based provider of solutions for the physical security market.<ref>{{cite press release |date=2008-08-21|title=Daon Acquires Physical Security Interconnect (PSI) Offering from Enterprise Air|url=http://findbiometrics.com/daon-acquires-physical-security-interconnect-psi-offering-from-enterprise-air/|work=Daon Press Release|access-date=2008-10-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131219023631/http://findbiometrics.com/daon-acquires-physical-security-interconnect-psi-offering-from-enterprise-air/|archive-date=2013-12-19}}</ref>

As of 2015, Daon's customers included [[Atom Bank]] in the UK.<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.irishtimes.com/business/technology/atomic-banking-deal-for-daon-1.2470170 | work = Irish Times | title = Atomic banking deal for Daon | date = 18 December 2015 | accessdate = 13 September 2024 }}</ref> In 2017, Daon launched the IdentityX for use by [[Visa Inc.|Visa]], [[Mastercard]], [[USAA]], and [[BNP Paribas]] for security in digital banking.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Chifuri |first=Hiromitsu |date=2018-10-19 |title=Ireland and Japan collaborate on biometric innovation |url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/country-report/2018/10/19/global-media-post/ireland-report-2018/ireland-japan-collaborate-biometric-innovation/ |access-date=2024-04-07 |website=The Japan Times |language=en-US}}</ref> In July 2019, Daon announced the update of its IdentityX Digital Onboarding 2.0.<ref>{{Cite press release |date=2019-07-23|title=Daon Launches Next-Generation Digital Onboarding|url=https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20190723005092/en/Daon-Launches-Next-Generation-Digital-Onboarding|access-date=2020-02-06|website=www.businesswire.com|language=en}}</ref>

In response to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], and need to verify vaccination information prior to travel, Daon launched the VeriFLY app. In 2022, the app was used by [[Carnival Cruise Line|Carnival Cruise Lines]] and [[American Airlines]] to digitally process passenger health information and complete the vaccination verification process.<ref>{{Cite web |title=American Airlines trials Verifly Covid-19 travel requirements app |url=https://www.businesstraveller.com/business-travel/2020/11/16/american-airlines-trials-verifly-travel-requirements-app/ |access-date=2024-04-07 |website=Business Traveller |language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Gaffey |first=Mike |date=2022-03-08 |title=Carnival to use health app to speed up embarkations |url=https://www.vieravoice.com/senior-life/news/carnival-to-use-health-app-to-speed-up-embarkations/article_c928942c-9f1f-11ec-b7ee-a3f84065db7d.html |access-date=2024-04-07 |website=Viera Voice |language=en}}</ref>

Daon, which is reported to be majority owned by a company in the Cayman Islands,<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.irishtimes.com/business/technology/dermot-desmond-s-daon-will-know-all-about-us-yet-we-know-nothing-of-it-1.3270030 | work = Irish Times | title = Dermot Desmond's Daon will know all about us, yet we know nothing of it | date = 27 October 2017 | accessdate = 13 September 2024 }}</ref> declared after-tax profits of approximately â‚¬220,000 in 2023.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.irishtimes.com/business/2023/11/11/dermot-desmond-biometrics-company-daon-sees-profits-dip/ | work = Irish Times | title = Dermot Desmond biometrics company Daon sees profits dip | accessdate = 13 September 2024 | date = 11 November 2023 }}</ref>

== Products ==
Daon's products include IdentityX (a biometric mobile authentication platform), DaonEngine, DaonEnroll, DaonAnalytics, and Credential Connect.{{Citation needed|date=December 2021}} Daon serves multiple markets including mobile authentication, border management, civil ID, traveler ID, employee credentialing, and [[access control]].{{fact|date=September 2024}} Their software is used at various airports and international borders<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.accenture.com/Global/Services/Alliances/Daon.htm |title=Daon |website=www.accenture.com |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060212062046/http://www.accenture.com/Global/Services/Alliances/Daon.htm |archive-date=2006-02-12}}</ref>{{Primary source inline|date=September 2024}} and supports U.S. [[Registered Traveler]] airports.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://findbiometrics.com/interview-with-tom-grissen-ceo-daon-inc/ |title=Interview with Tom Grissen, CEO, Daon Inc. {{!}} findBIOMETRICS |website=findbiometrics.com |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131219021916/http://findbiometrics.com/interview-with-tom-grissen-ceo-daon-inc/ |archive-date=2013-12-19}}</ref>{{Primary source inline|reason=Source is an interview/statement from company CEO. A primary source. A non-primary source would be preferred for this type of assertion.|date=September 2024}}

The company's IdentityX product platform was designed to authenticate users on a mobile device using biometrics instead of a password. IdentityX uses several methods, including facial recognition, voice recognition and fingerprint, to prove a user is who they claim to be. As of 2015, IdentityX was one of several products certified by the [[FIDO Alliance]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://fidoalliance.org/certification/fido-certified/|title=FIDO Certified |website=FIDO Alliance| archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20150714212405/https://fidoalliance.org/certification/fido-certified/ | archivedate = 14 July 2015 }}</ref> Also in 2015, Daon's IdentityX won NACUSO's "Next Big Idea" competition<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nacuso.org/2015/04/28/identityx-wins-nacusos-next-big-idea-competition/|title = IdentityX Wins NACUSO's Next Big Idea Competition - NACUSO | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20160304114945/https://www.nacuso.org/2015/04/28/identityx-wins-nacusos-next-big-idea-competition/ | archivedate = 4 March 2016 | website = nacuso.org }}</ref> and a "Best in Show" award from the CUNA Technology Council.<ref>{{Cite press release |url=http://www.cuna.org/Stay-Informed/Press-Room/Press-Releases/2015-Press-Releases/CUNA-Technology-Council-Announces-Daon-as-2015-Best-in-Show-Award-winner/ |title=CUNA: CUNA Technology Council Announces Daon as 2015 Best in Show Award winner |access-date=2015-07-20 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923211936/http://www.cuna.org/Stay-Informed/Press-Room/Press-Releases/2015-Press-Releases/CUNA-Technology-Council-Announces-Daon-as-2015-Best-in-Show-Award-winner/ |archive-date=2015-09-23 }}</ref>
