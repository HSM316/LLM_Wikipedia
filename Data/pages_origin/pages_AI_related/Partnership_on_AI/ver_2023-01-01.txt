'''Partnership on AI''' (full name '''Partnership on Artificial Intelligence to Benefit People and Society''') is a nonprofit coalition committed to the responsible use of [[artificial intelligence]]. It researches [[best practices]] for [[artificial intelligence]] systems and to educate the public about AI.  Publicly announced September 28, 2016, its founding members are [[Amazon.com|Amazon]], [[Facebook]], [[Google]], [[DeepMind]], [[Microsoft]], and [[IBM]], with interim co-chairs [[Eric Horvitz]] of [[Microsoft Research]] and [[Mustafa Suleyman]] of DeepMind.<ref>{{cite news|last=Hern |first=Alex |url=https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2016/sep/28/google-facebook-amazon-ibm-microsoft-partnership-on-ai-tech-firms |title='Partnership on AI' formed by Google, DeepMind, Facebook, Amazon, IBM and Microsoft &#124; Technology |publisher=The Guardian |date=2016-09-28 |accessdate=2016-09-29}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Waters |first=Richard |url=https://www.ft.com/content/c2c739d0-8661-11e6-8897-2359a58ac7a5 |title=AI is ‘Next Big Thing’ to worry about |publisher=Financial Times |date=2016-09-29 |accessdate=2016-09-29}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Bindi |first=Tas |url=http://www.zdnet.com/article/amazon-google-facebook-ibm-and-microsoft-form-ai-non-profit/ |title=Amazon, Google, Facebook, IBM, and Microsoft form AI non-profit |publisher=ZDNet |date=2016-09-29 |accessdate=2016-09-29}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Rubin |first1=Ben Fox |last2=Cheng |first2=Roger |url=https://www.cnet.com/news/the-ai-super-friends-assemble-the-359-ep-115/ |title=The AI Super Friends assemble! (The 3:59, Ep. 115) |publisher=CNET |date=2016-09-29 |accessdate=2016-09-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160930185512/https://www.cnet.com/news/the-ai-super-friends-assemble-the-359-ep-115/ |archive-date=2016-09-30 |url-status=dead }}</ref> [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] joined the consortium as a founding member in January 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=Partnership on AI Update|url=https://www.partnershiponai.org/2017/01/partnership-ai-update/|website=Partnership on AI|accessdate=26 May 2017}}</ref> More than 100 partners from academia, civil society, industry, and nonprofits are member organizations in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.partnershiponai.org/new-partners-to-bolster-perspective-for-responsible-ai/ |title=New Partners To Bolster Perspective For Responsible AI}}</ref>

In January 2017, [[Apple Inc.|Apple]] head of advanced development for [[Siri]], [[Tom Gruber]], joined the Partnership on AI's board.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/aarontilley/2017/01/27/why-apple-joined-rivals-amazon-google-microsoft-in-ai-partnership/#257a251b3042|title=Why Apple Joined Rivals Amazon, Google, Microsoft In AI Partnership|last=Tilley|first=Aaron|newspaper=Forbes |date=January 27, 2017 |access-date=2017-02-02}}</ref> In October 2017, [[Terah Lyons]] joined the Partnership on AI as the organization's founding executive director.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.partnershiponai.org/partnership-on-ai-announces-executive-director-terah-lyons-and-welcomes-new-partners/|title=Partnership on AI Announces Executive Director Terah Lyons and Welcomes New Partners - The Partnership on AI|date=2017-10-17|work=The Partnership on AI|access-date=2018-09-10|language=en-US}}</ref> Lyons brought to the organization her expertise in technology governance, with a specific focus in machine intelligence, AI, and robotics policy, having formerly served as Policy Advisor to the United States Chief Technology Officer [[Megan Smith]]. Lyons was succeeded by Partnership on AI board member Rebecca Finlay as interim executive director. Finlay was named CEO of Partnership on AI on October 26, 2021.

In October 2018, [[Baidu]] became the first Chinese firm to join the Partnership.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/10/17/baidu-becomes-the-first-chinese-firm-to-join-us-led-ai-body.html|title=Baidu becomes the first Chinese firm to join US-led A.I. body|last=Taylor|first=Chloe|date=2018-10-17|work=CNBC|access-date=2018-10-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181103192645/https://www.cnbc.com/2018/10/17/baidu-becomes-the-first-chinese-firm-to-join-us-led-ai-body.html|archive-date=November 3, 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>
