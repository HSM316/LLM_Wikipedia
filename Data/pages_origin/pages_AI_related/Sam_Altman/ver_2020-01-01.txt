'''Samuel H.''' "'''Sam'''" '''Altman''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|ɔː|l|t|m|ə|n}}; born April 22, 1985) is an American [[entrepreneur]], [[investor]], [[programmer]], and [[blog]]ger.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://blog.samaltman.com |title=Sam Altman |work=SamAltman.com |accessdate=December 15, 2015}}</ref> He is the chairman of [[Y Combinator (company)|Y Combinator]] and co-chairman of [[OpenAI]].<ref>{{cite web |first=Paul |last=Graham |url=http://blog.ycombinator.com/sam-altman-for-president |title=Sam Altman for President |work=Y Combinator |date=April 24, 2014 |accessdate=March 20, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title=OpenAI | url=https://openai.com/blog/introducing-openai/ | accessdate=March 20, 2017}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Altman grew up in [[St. Louis, Missouri]]; his mother was a dermatologist. He received his first computer at the age of 8.<ref name="esquire.com">{{cite web |first=Tom |last=Junod |title=How Venture Capitalists Find Opportunities in the Future |url=http://www.esquire.com/news-politics/interviews/a30763/sam-altman-interview-2014/ |work=[[Esquire (magazine)|Esquire]] |date=December 18, 2014 |accessdate=December 15, 2015}}</ref> He was raised Jewish.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2016/10/10/sam-altmans-manifest-destiny|title=Sam Altman’s Manifest Destiny|website=NewYorker.com|publisher=The New Yorker|accessdate=17 May 2017}}</ref> He attended [[John Burroughs School]] for high school and studied computer science at [[Stanford University]] until dropping out in 2005.<ref>{{cite web |title=People |url=http://ycombinator.com/people.html |work=[[Y Combinator (company)|Y Combinator]] |accessdate=December 15, 2015}}</ref> While studying at Stanford, he worked in the [[Stanford Artificial Intelligence Lab|AI Lab]].<ref name="crunchbase.com">{{Cite web|url=https://www.crunchbase.com/person/sam-altman#section-overview|title=Sam Altman - President, YC Group @ Y Combinator {{!}} Crunchbase|website=Crunchbase|language=en|access-date=2018-02-20}}</ref> In 2017, he received an honorary degree from the [[University of Waterloo]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://uwaterloo.ca/registrar/spring-2017-honorary-and-award-recipients | title=Spring 2017 honorary and award recipients | publisher=University of Waterloo | work=Registrar's Office | accessdate=July 7, 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
===Loopt===
In 2005, at age 19,<ref>{{cite web |first=Jason |last=Ankeny |title=Meet Y Combinator's Bold Whiz Kid Boss |url=http://www.entrepreneur.com/article/244508 |date=April 25, 2015 |accessdate=December 15, 2015}}</ref> Altman [[entrepreneur|co-founded]] and became [[chief executive officer|CEO]] of [[Loopt]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.loopt.com/about/company/executives |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120216022416/http://www.loopt.com/about/company/executives/ |archive-date=February 16, 2012 |title=Executives |accessdate=December 15, 2015 |work=[[Loopt]]}}</ref> a location-based [[social networking service|social networking]] [[mobile software|mobile application]]. After raising more than $30M in venture capital, Loopt was shut down in 2012 after failing to get traction. It was acquired by the [[Green Dot Corporation]] for $43.4 million.<ref>{{cite news | title=Startup Loopt Lands with Green Dot | date=March 9, 2012 | work=[[Wall Street Journal]] | url =https://blogs.wsj.com/digits/2012/03/09/startup-loopt-lands-with-green-dot/| accessdate = March 13, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://mastersofscale.com/sam-altman-why-customer-love-is-all-you-need/|title=Sam Altman: Why customer love is all you need {{!}} Masters of Scale podcast|work=WaitWhat|access-date=2018-03-20|language=en-US}}</ref>

===Y Combinator===
Altman began as a part-time partner at [[Y Combinator]] in 2011.<ref name="crunchbase.com"/><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://social.techcrunch.com/2019/03/08/y-combinator-president-sam-altman-is-stepping-down-amid-a-series-of-changes-at-the-accelerator/|title=Y Combinator president Sam Altman is stepping down amid a series of changes at the accelerator|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-18}}</ref> In February 2014, Altman was named president of Y Combinator by its cofounder Paul Graham.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://social.techcrunch.com/2015/11/06/garry-tan-says-goodbye-to-y-combinator/|title=Garry Tan Says Goodbye to Y Combinator|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-18}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |first=Paul |last=Graham |url=http://blog.ycombinator.com/sam-altman-for-president |title=Sam Altman for President |work=Y Combinator |date=April 24, 2014 |accessdate=December 15, 2015}}</ref> His first batch of investments included Loopt. In a 2014 blog post, Altman said that the total valuation of Y Combinator companies had surpassed $65 billion, including well-known companies like [[Airbnb]], [[Dropbox (service)|Dropbox]], [[Zenefits]] and [[Stripe (company)|Stripe]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://blog.ycombinator.com/yc-stats |accessdate=December 19, 2015|title=YC Stats | publisher=Y Combinator | date=August 26, 2015}}</ref>  In September 2016, Altman announced that he would become president of YC Group, which includes Y Combinator and other units.<ref>{{cite web|first=Sam|last=Altman|url=https://blog.ycombinator.com/yc-changes|title=YC Changes |work=Y Combinator |accessdate=November 7, 2016}}</ref>

Altman has said that he hopes to expand Y Combinator to fund 1,000 new companies per year. He has also tried to expand the types of companies funded by YC, especially 'hard technology' companies.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.fastcompany.com/3044282/the-y-combinator-chronicles/california-dreamin |accessdate=July 22, 2015|title=Y Combinator President Sam Altman is Dreaming Big| website=Fast Company | date=April 16, 2015}}</ref>

In October 2015, Altman announced YC Continuity, a $700 million growth-stage [[equity fund]] that invests in YC companies.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://blog.ycombinator.com/yc-continuity-fund |accessdate=December 19, 2015|title=YC Continuity | publisher=Y Combinator | date=October 15, 2015}}<br>- {{cite news|url=https://venturebeat.com/2015/10/15/y-combinator-raises-700m-to-keep-funding-yc-startups-as-they-mature/ |accessdate=December 19, 2015|title=YC Continuity | website=VentureBeat | date=October 15, 2015}}</ref> Also in October 2015, Altman announced Y Combinator Research, a non-profit research lab, and donated $10 million to the group.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://blog.ycombinator.com/yc-research | accessdate=February 19, 2016 | title=YC Research | publisher=Y Combinator | date=October 7, 2015}}</ref> YC Research has thus far announced research on basic income, the future of computing, education, and building new cities.<ref>{{cite web|title=Y Combinator Research|url=https://ycr.org/|website=YC Research|accessdate=3 August 2016}}</ref>

Altman was named the top investor under 30 by ''[[Forbes]]'' in 2015,<ref>{{cite news | title= Forbes' 30 Under 30 2015: Venture Capital | date=January 5, 2015| url =https://www.forbes.com/30under30/#/venture-capital | work =[[Forbes]] | accessdate = February 28, 2015}}</ref> one of the "Best Young Entrepreneurs in Technology" by ''[[BusinessWeek]]'' in 2008<ref>{{cite news | title= Tech’s Best Young Entrepreneurs | date=April 18, 2008 | url =http://images.businessweek.com/ss/07/03/0326_tech_entrepreneurs/source/9.htm | work =[[BusinessWeek]] | accessdate = April 19, 2009}}</ref> and listed as one of the five most interesting startup founders between 1979 and 2009 by his colleague [[Paul Graham (computer programmer)|Paul Graham]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.paulgraham.com/5founders.html |title=Five Founders |accessdate=April 19, 2009|authorlink=Paul Graham (computer programmer)|last=Graham |first=Paul |date=April 2009 }}</ref>

In March 2019, YC announced Altman's transitioning into a Chairman position to focus more on [[OpenAI]].<ref name=":0" />

===Angel investing===
He is a personal investor in many companies, including [[Airbnb]], [[Stripe (company)|Stripe]], [[Reddit]], [[Asana (software)|Asana]], [[Pinterest]], [[Teespring]], [[Zenefits]], FarmLogs, [[Shoptiques]], [[Instacart]], [[Optimizely]], [[Verbling]], [[Soylent (drink)|Soylent]], Reserve, Vicarious, Clever, Notable PDF<ref name="Angel List">{{cite web|last1=Altman|first1=Sam|title=Angel List|url=https://angel.co/sama/syndicate|website=Angel List|publisher=|accessdate=April 20, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.yahoo.com/tech/s/notable-pdf-built-annotation-tool-130003639.html|title=How Notable PDF built an annotation tool for PDFs on the web|last=|first=|date=2017-04-05|website=|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170405210802/https://www.yahoo.com/tech/notable-pdf-built-annotation-tool-130003639.html|archive-date=2017-04-05|access-date=2018-03-26}}</ref> and [[Change.org]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Change.org - Investors {{!}} CrunchBase|url=https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/change-org/investors|website=CrunchBase|accessdate=3 August 2016}}</ref>

He was the CEO of [[Reddit]] for eight days in 2014 after CEO [[Yishan Wong]] resigned.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://blog.samaltman.com/a-new-team-at-reddit |accessdate=February 13, 2015|title=A New Team At Reddit | publisher=Sam Altman | date=November 13, 2014}}</ref> As part of his investment, he developed a new way for the community to own part of the company. He announced the return of Steve Huffman as CEO on July 10, 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reddit.com/comments/3cucye/ |title=An Old Team At Reddit |accessdate=July 13, 2015 |publisher=[[reddit]] }}</ref>

===Nuclear energy===
He is chairman of the board for [[Helion Energy|Helion]] and Oklo, two nuclear energy companies. He has said that nuclear energy is one of the most important areas of technological development.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.samaltman.com/energy/ | title=Energy |accessdate=July 13, 2015 |publisher=Sam Altman }}</ref>

===OpenAI===
Altman and [[Elon Musk]] were the co-chairmen of [[OpenAI]]. OpenAI is a for profit research company whose goal is to advance digital intelligence in a way that is most likely to benefit humanity as a whole, rather than cause harm.<ref>{{cite web | title=OpenAI | url=https://openai.com/blog/introducing-openai/ | accessdate=December 15, 2015}}</ref>

==Teaching==
In fall 2014, Altman taught a class at [[Stanford University]] called "[http://startupclass.samaltman.com/ How To Start a Startup]". Guest lecturers included [[Dustin Moskovitz]], [[Paul Graham (computer programmer)|Paul Graham]], [[Peter Thiel]], [[Adora Cheung]], Alex Schultz, [[Marc Andreessen]], [[Ron Conway]], Carolynn Levy, Kirsty Nathoo, [[Ben Horowitz]], [[Brian Chesky]], [[Alfred Lin]], [[Aaron Levie]], [[Reid Hoffman]], Kevin Hale, [[Ben Silbermann]] and [[Emmett Shear]]. He currently teaches Y Combinator Startup School, a 10-week course featuring guests including [[Stewart Butterfield]], [[Adam D'Angelo]], and Dustin Moskovitz.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.startupschool.org/|website=Startup School |title= STARTUP SCHOOL BY Y COMBINATOR | access-date= April 25, 2017}}</ref>

==Writing==
Altman occasionally posts to his blog, mostly about startups and technology.<ref>http://blog.samaltman.com</ref>

He also wrote Startup Playbook, a distillation of advice that Y Combinator gives to its startups.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.amazon.com/Startup-Playbook-Sam-Altman-ebook/dp/B0181ZPASC|title=Startup Playbook|last=Altman|first=Sam|others=Gregory Koberger|language=English}}</ref>
