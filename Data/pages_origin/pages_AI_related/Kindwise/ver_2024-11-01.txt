== Features & Tools ==
FlowerChecker offers multiple products.

The '''FlowerChecker app''' was launched in April 2014 for Android and later that year for iOS. <ref name=":6" /><ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> It enables users to submit photographs of the object they want to identify.<ref name=":6" /><ref name=":0" />The photographs are then examined by an international team of experts in [[botany]] and [[horticulture]].<ref name=":6" /><ref name=":0" />

'''Plant.id''' is a machine learning-based plant identification API launched in 2018,<ref name=":7">{{cite web |date=7 May 2018 |title=Tvůrci FlowerCheckeru spouštějí Shazam pro kytky. Plant.id staví na AI a má velké plány |trans-title=The creators of FlowerChecker are launching Shazam for plants. Plant.id is based on AI and has big plans |url=https://tyinternety.cz/startupy/tvurci-flowercheckeru-spousteji-shazam-pro-kytky-plant-id-stavi-na-ai-a-ma-velke-plany/ |accessdate=14 November 2020 |website=tyinternety.cz |language=cs |archive-date=12 May 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180512112326/https://tyinternety.cz/startupy/tvurci-flowercheckeru-spousteji-shazam-pro-kytky-plant-id-stavi-na-ai-a-ma-velke-plany/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> with the plant disease identification API, plant.health, released in April 2022.<ref name=":9" /> The plant.id API is suitable for integration into other software, such as mobile apps<ref name=":5">{{cite journal |last1=Jones |first1=Hamlyn G |date=2020 |title=What plant is that? Tests of automated image recognition apps for plant identification on plants from the British flora |url= |journal=AoB Plants |volume=12 |issue=6 |pages=plaa052 |doi=10.1093/aobpla/plaa052 |issn=2041-2851 |pmc=7640754 |pmid=33173573}}</ref> or urban trees from [[Remote sensing|remote-sensing]] imagery.<ref name=":2">{{Cite journal |last1=Velasquez-Camacho |first1=Luisa |last2=Merontausta |first2=Esko |last3=Etxegarai |first3=Maddi |last4=de-Miguel |first4=Sergio |date=2024-04-01 |title=Assessing urban forest biodiversity through automatic taxonomic identification of street trees from citizen science applications and remote-sensing imagery |journal=International Journal of Applied Earth Observation and Geoinformation |volume=128 |pages=103735 |doi=10.1016/j.jag.2024.103735 |issn=1569-8432|doi-access=free }}</ref>

Other products include insect.id,<ref>{{Cite web |title=insect.id AI Insect Identification API by kindwise |url=https://www.kindwise.com/insect-id |access-date=2024-09-02 |website=www.kindwise.com |language=en}}</ref> mushroom.id<ref>{{Cite web |title=mushroom.id AI Mushroom Identification API by kindwise |url=https://www.kindwise.com/mushroom-id |access-date=2024-09-02 |website=www.kindwise.com |language=en}}</ref> and crop.health are machine learning-based identification APIs for the identification of insects, fungi and economically important plants,<ref>{{Cite web |title=crop.health AI Crop Disease Identification API by kindwise |url=https://www.kindwise.com/crop-health |access-date=2024-09-02 |website=www.kindwise.com |language=en}}</ref> respectively, and include also online public demos.

== Recognition ==
In 2019, FlowerChecker won the Idea of the Year award in the AI Awards organized by the Confederation of Industry of the Czech Republic.<ref name=":8">{{cite web |date=13 May 2019 |title=V soutěži AI Awards uspěli tvůrci aplikací na určování rostlin nebo na rozpoznávání únosců po hlase |trans-title=Authors of applications for plant identification or voice recognition of abductors were successful at the AI Awards |url=https://byznys.ihned.cz/c1-66563320-v-soutezi-ai-awards-uspeli-tvurci-aplikaci-na-urcovani-rostlin-nebo-na-rozpoznavani-unoscu-po-hlase |accessdate=14 November 2020 |website=ihned.cz |language=cs}}</ref> In 2020, an academic study comparing ten free automated image recognition apps showed that plant.id's performance excelled in most of the parameters studied.<ref name=":5" /> In an independent study comparing different image-based species recognition models and their suitability for recognizing invasive alien species, the plant.id achieved the highest accuracy compared to other tools.<ref name=":10">{{cite journal |last1=Jakuschona |first1=Nick |last2=Niers |first2=Tom |last3=Stenkamp |first3=Jan |last4=Bartoschek |first4=Thomas |last5=Schade |first5=Sven |date=18 January 2022 |title=Evaluating image-based species recognition models suitable for citizen science application to support European invasive alien species policy |url=https://op.europa.eu/en/publication-detail/-/publication/91584805-79a1-11ec-9136-01aa75ed71a1/language-en |journal=European Commission, Joint Research Centre |doi=10.2760/97305 |isbn=9789276467212 |access-date=27 June 2022}}</ref> In a subsequent study, plant.id was utilized to evaluate urban forest biodiversity using [[Remote sensing|remote-sensing]] imagery, achieving the highest accuracy in tree species identification among compared methods.<ref name=":2" />

== Research activities ==
Flowerchecker cooperates with the Nature Conservation Agency of the Czech Republic  on a biodiversity mapping project.<ref name=":9" />

FlowerChecker plans to adapt its services to participate in the control of invasive species. In 2022, the company entered a consortium to develop a weeder capable of in-row weed detection and removal.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Přijaté návrhy projektů do veřejné soutěže 6. veřejná soutěž programu TREND, PP1 |url=https://www.tacr.cz/dokumenty/prijate-navrhy-projektu-do-verejne-souteze-6-verejna-soutez-programu-trend-pp1 |access-date=2024-03-11 |website=Technologická agentura ČR |language=cs-CZ}}</ref>
