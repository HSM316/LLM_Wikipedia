'''FlowerChecker app''' was launched in April 2014 on Android and later in the same year on iOS.<ref name=":6" /><ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> It requires users to send photographs of the object they want to identify.<ref name=":6" /><ref name=":0" /> Thereafter, the photographs are sent to an international team of [[botany]] and [[horticulture]] experts for examination.<ref name=":6" /><ref name=":0" />

'''Plant.id''' is a machine learning-based plant identification API launched in 2018.<ref name=":7" /> Plant.id API is suitable for incorporation into other software and can be used for other applications. <ref name=":5" /> It can be also used for non-profit objectives, such as for environmental research or for students.<ref name=":7" /> High identification accuracy can be achieved by combining several images of different plant organs. <ref name=":5">{{cite journal |last1=Jones |first1=Hamlyn G |title=What plant is that? Tests of automated image recognition apps for plant identification on plants from the British flora |journal=AoB PLANTS |date=23 September 2020 |volume=12 |issue=6 |pages=plaa052 |doi=10.1093/aobpla/plaa052 |pmid=33173573 |pmc=7640754 |url=https://doi.org/10.1093/aobpla/plaa052 |accessdate=2020-10-10 |issn=2041-2851}}</ref>
