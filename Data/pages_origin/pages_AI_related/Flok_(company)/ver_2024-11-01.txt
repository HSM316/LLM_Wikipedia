==Background==
Flok was founded in 2011 by Ido Gaver and Eran Kirshenboim and has offices in [[Tel Aviv]], [[Israel]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Zimmerman|first1=Eilene|title=LoyalBlocks Simplifies Mobile App Creation for Small Merchants|url=http://boss.blogs.nytimes.com/2014/09/16/loyalblocks-simplifies-mobile-app-creation-for-small-merchants/?_r=1|accessdate=26 July 2016|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=16 September 2014 }}</ref> In May 2013, Flok secured a $9 million Series A Round from General Catalyst Partners with participation from Founder Collective and existing investor Gemini Israel Ventures.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thenextweb.com/insider/2013/05/23/nyc-based-loyalblocks-raises-9m-for-its-automated-mobile-loyalty-program/#gref|title=LoyalBlocks Nabs $9M for Its Automated Mobile Loyalty Program|date=23 May 2013}}</ref> In total, Flok has raised over $18 million in [[venture capital]] in three rounds.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Taylor|first1=Colleen|title=LoyalBlocks Lands $9 Million Led By General Catalyst To Scale Out Its Mobile-Based Loyalty Program|date=23 May 2013 |url=https://techcrunch.com/2013/05/23/loyalblocks-9-million-funding/|publisher=[[TechCrunch]]|accessdate=26 July 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Your next loyalty 'punch card' will be driven by AI|date=7 April 2016|url=https://venturebeat.com/2016/04/07/your-next-loyalty-punch-card-will-be-driven-by-ai|publisher=[[VentureBeat]]|accessdate=26 July 2016}}</ref>

In May 2014, Flok announced a self-service loyalty platform for SMBs to build their own programs with beacon integration. At that time, approximately 40,000 businesses were using the service.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mediapost.com/publications/article/225373/loyalblocks-adds-beacon-to-self-serve-loyalty-plat.html|title=LoyalBlocks Adds Beacon to Self-Serve Loyalty Platform for SMBS}}</ref> In 2016, Flok released a turnkey chatbot service for local businesses,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://venturebeat.com/2016/07/26/flok-uses-bluetooth-and-chatbots-to-lure-people-inside-thousands-of-small-businesses/|title=Flok uses Bluetooth and chatbots to lure people inside thousands of small businesses|date=26 July 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://marketingland.com/flok-wants-bring-chatbots-small-business-masses-186056|title = Flok wants to bring chatbots to the small business masses|date = 27 July 2016}}</ref> and was featured in [[AdWeek]] for developing the first '''weed bot''' chatbot for a [[California]] [[cannabis]] business.<ref>{{cite web|title=This California Marijuana Dispensary Is Using Bots to Understand Customers' Preferences|date=24 June 2016 |url=http://www.adweek.com/news/technology/california-marijuana-dispensary-using-bots-understand-customers-preferences-172238|publisher=[[Adweek]]|accessdate=26 July 2016}}</ref>

==Services==
Flok offered an eponymous customer-facing app<ref>{{cite web|title=flok - get rewarded on the App Store|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/flok-get-rewarded/id376560037?mt=8|publisher=[[App Store (iOS)]]|accessdate=26 July 2016}}</ref> that consumers use to receive rewards and deals from partner businesses,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/startup-loyalblocks-helps-small-businesses-zap-deals-rewards-customers-smartphones-article-1.1154135|title = Startup LoyalBlocks helps small businesses zap deals and rewards to customers' smartphones| website=[[New York Daily News]] | date=7 September 2012 }}</ref> and a Flok business app for merchants to manage the platform.<ref>{{cite web|title=flok business on the App Store|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/flok-business/id1102450236?mt=8|publisher=[[App Store (iOS)]]|accessdate=26 July 2016}}</ref>
