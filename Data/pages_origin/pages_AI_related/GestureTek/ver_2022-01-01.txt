'''GestureTek''' is an American-based interactive technology company headquartered in [[Silicon Valley]], California, with offices in Toronto and [[Ottawa]], Ontario and Asia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gesturetek.com/ |title=Gesture Recognition & Computer Vision Control Technology & Motion Sensing Systems for Presentation & Entertainment |publisher=Gesturetek.com |accessdate=October 20, 2011}}</ref>

==Founding==

Founded in 1986 by Canadians Vincent John Vincent<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.vjvincent.com/ |title=Vincent John Vincent |publisher=Vjvincent.com |accessdate=October 20, 2011}}</ref> and Francis MacDougall,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.techthink.org/speaker08_MacDougall.asp |title=ATIS TechThink |publisher=Techthink.org |accessdate=October 20, 2011}}</ref> this privately held company develops and licenses [[gesture recognition]] software based on [[computer vision]] techniques. The partners invented video gesture control in 1986 and received their base patent in 1996 for the GestPoint video gesture control system. GestPoint technology is a camera-enabled [[video tracking]] software system that translates hand and body movement into computer control.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2009/TECH/ptech/04/23/cnet.touch.screens/ |accessdate=May 7, 2009 |title=Putting our arms around the future of touch |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090426212731/http://edition.cnn.com/2009/TECH/ptech/04/23/cnet.touch.screens/ |archivedate=April 26, 2009 }}</ref>  The system enables users to navigate and control interactive multi-media and menu-based content, engage in virtual reality game play, experience [[Immersion (virtual reality)|immersion]] in an [[augmented reality]] environment or interact with a consumer device (such a television, mobile phone or set top box) without using touch-based peripherals.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.engadget.com/2009/01/05/gesturetek-brings-3d-and-gestures-together-for-remote-control/ |title=GestureTek brings 3D and gestures together for remote control |publisher=Engadget |date=January 5, 2009 |accessdate=October 20, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.crunchgear.com/2008/11/12/watch-out-surface-gesturetek-is-straight-frontin/ |title=Watch out, Surface; GestureTek is straight frontin' &#124; TechCrunch |publisher=Crunchgear.com |date=November 12, 2008 |accessdate=October 20, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://mobileworldcongress.mediaroom.com/index.php?s=43&item=361 |title=GestureTek Mobile is Overall Winner at the 2008 Mobile Innovation Global Awards at the GSMA's Mobile World Congress in Barcelona – Feb 13, 2008 |publisher=Mobileworldcongress.mediaroom.com |date=February 13, 2008 |accessdate=October 20, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111007091208/http://mobileworldcongress.mediaroom.com/index.php?s=43&item=361 |archivedate=October 7, 2011 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> Similar companies include gesture recognition specialist LM3LABS based in [[Tokyo|Tokyo, Japan]].

==Technology==
GestureTek's gesture interface applications include multi-touch and 3D camera tracking.  GestureTek's [[multi-touch]] technology powers the multi-touch table in Melbourne's [[Eureka Tower]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aboutmicrosoftsurface.com/microsoft-surface-versus-gesturetek-illuminate-table.php |title=Microsoft Surface versus GestureTek's Illuminate Table |publisher=Aboutmicrosoftsurface.com |accessdate=October 20, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111009040633/http://www.aboutmicrosoftsurface.com/microsoft-surface-versus-gesturetek-illuminate-table.php |archivedate=October 9, 2011 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>  A GestureTek multi-touch table with [[object recognition]] is found at the New York City Visitors Center.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://svconline.com/corporateav/features/new_york_information_center_409/ |title=The New York Center Information Center Installation |publisher=Svconline.com |accessdate=October 20, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110928084701/http://svconline.com/corporateav/features/new_york_information_center_409/ |archivedate=September 28, 2011 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> Telefónica has a [[multi-touch]] window with technology from GestureTek.<ref>{{cite web|author=Chris Morrison |url=https://venturebeat.com/2007/12/12/gesturetek-receives-investment-from-telefonica/ |title=GestureTek receives investment from Telefonica &#124; VentureBeat |publisher=Deals.venturebeat.com |date=December 12, 2007 |accessdate=October 20, 2011}}</ref> GestureTek's 3D tracking technology is used in a [[3D television]] prototype from Hitachi and various digital signage and display solutions based on 3D interaction.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.lcdtvreviews.org.uk/lcd-news/hitachi-gesturetek-collaboration-to-realise-bare-hand-tv-remote-control/ |title=LCD TV |publisher=Lcdtvreviews.org.uk |accessdate=October 20, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111009040538/http://www.lcdtvreviews.org.uk/lcd-news/hitachi-gesturetek-collaboration-to-realise-bare-hand-tv-remote-control/ |archivedate=October 9, 2011 |df=mdy-all }}</ref>
