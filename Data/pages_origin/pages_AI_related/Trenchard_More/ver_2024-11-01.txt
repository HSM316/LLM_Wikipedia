{{Short description|Mathematician and Computer Scientist (1930-2019)}}
'''Trenchard More''' (1930&nbsp;– 2019) was a mathematician and computer scientist who worked at IBM's [[Thomas J. Watson Research Center]] and [[Cambridge Scientific Center]] after teaching at [[MIT]] and [[Yale University|Yale]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.legacy.com/us/obituaries/wickedlocal-hingham/name/trenchard-more-obituary?id=8414518|title=Trenchard More Obituary (1930 - 2019)|website=Legacy.com}}</ref> 

He was also a full professor for two years at the [[Technical University of Denmark]].

He participated in the 1956 [[Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence]].<ref>William Bechtel, George Graham [https://books.google.com/books?id=eBw7dVcsTnMC&dq=Trenchard+More+dartmouth+conference&pg=PA13 (://books.google)] ''A companion to cognitive science'' [Retrieved 2011-10-25]</ref><ref>[http://www.dartmouth.edu/~vox/0607/0724/ai50.html .dartmouth.edu/~vox] [Retrieved 2011-10-25] © 2011 Trustees of Dartmouth College (showing photograph of the Professor)</ref><ref>[[Rudolf Seising]] [https://books.google.com/books?id=rdYRdlM2dAQC&dq=professors+of+Dartmouth+Trenchard+More&pg=PA161 (Google eBook)] Fuzzification of systems: ''the genesis of fuzzy set theory and its initial applications'' - developments up to the 1970s  [Retrieved 2011-10-25]</ref> At the 50th year meeting of the Dartmouth Conference with [[Marvin Minsky]], [[Ray Solomonoff]], [[Geoffrey Hinton]] and [[Simon Osindero]] he presented  ''The Future of Network Models'' and also gave a lecture entitled ''Routes to the Summit''.<ref>artintelligence on Dec 22, 2010,''slideshare.net'' (4.) and (6.), Dartmouth College [https://archive.today/20120911064106/http://www.slideshare.net/artintelligence/dartmouth-college-artificial-intelligence-conference slideshare.net/artintelligence] © 2011 SlideShare Inc.
