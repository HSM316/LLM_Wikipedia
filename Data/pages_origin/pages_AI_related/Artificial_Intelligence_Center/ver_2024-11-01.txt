The '''Artificial Intelligence Center''' is a laboratory in the Information and Computing Sciences Division of [[SRI International]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sri.com/about/organization/information-computing-sciences|title=Information and Computing Sciences|publisher=SRI International|accessdate=2013-07-01|archive-date=2013-07-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130702205323/http://www.sri.com/about/organization/information-computing-sciences|url-status=dead}}</ref> It was founded in 1966 by [[Charles Rosen (scientist)|Charles Rosen]] and studies [[artificial intelligence]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ai.sri.com/about/|title=About the AI Center|publisher=Artificial Intelligence Center|accessdate=2011-11-28}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Buchanan|first=Wyatt|date=2002-12-20|url=http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?file=/chronicle/archive/2002/12/20/BA88655.DTL|title=Charles Rosen -- expert on robots, co-founder of winery|work=[[San Francisco Chronicle]]|accessdate=2007-10-23}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |author= Nils J. Nilsson |authorlink= Nils J. Nilsson|title= Introduction to the COMTEX Microfiche Edition of the SRI Artificial Intelligence Center Technical Notes |url= http://www.aaai.org/ojs/index.php/aimagazine/article/download/424/360 |page= 49 |work= AI Magazine |volume= 5 |number= 1 |year= 1984 }}</ref> 

One of their early projects was [[Shakey the Robot]], the first general-purpose mobile [[robot]]. More recently, the center funded early development of [[CALO]] and [[Siri (software)|Siri]]. The center has also provided the military with various technology.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.sri.com/about/organization/information-computing-sciences/aic |title=SRI International about page |access-date=2017-03-02 |archive-date=2019-10-03 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191003104946/https://www.sri.com/about/organization/information-computing-sciences/aic |url-status=dead }}</ref>
