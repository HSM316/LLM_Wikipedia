'''Mila''' (originally ''Montreal Institute for Learning Algorithms'') is a research institute in [[Montreal]], [[Quebec]]. Since 2017, Mila is the result of a partnership between the [[Université de Montréal]] and [[McGill University]] with [[École Polytechnique de Montréal]] and [[HEC Montréal]].  Mila has been founded by [[Yoshua Bengio]], a [[Turing award]] winner and one of the founders of the field of [[deep learning]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.acm.org/media-center/2019/march/turing-award-2018|title=Fathers of the Deep Learning revolution receive 2018 ACM A.M. Turing Award|website=www.acm.org|language=en|access-date=2019-07-17}}</ref>.

Mila is one of the premier centers for AI in the world<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://globalnews.ca/news/4899040/massive-new-centre-for-ai-research-opens-in-montreal/|title=Massive new centre for AI research opens in Montreal - Montreal {{!}} Globalnews.ca|date=2019-01-28|website=globalnews.ca|language=en|access-date=2019-07-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aitrends.com/ai-in-canada/executive-interview-yoshua-bengio-of-mila-university-of-montreal/|title=Executive Interview: Yoshua Bengio of MILA, University of Montreal|last=Desmond|first=John|date=2018-06-22|website=AI Trends|language=en|access-date=2019-07-17}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ibm.com/blogs/insights-on-business/ibmix/montreal-became-worlds-leading-ai-deep-learning-hub/|title=How Montreal became the world’s leading AI and deep learning hub|date=2017-05-09|website=IBM iX|language=en-US|access-date=2019-07-17}}</ref>. Besides Bengio, its faculty members include [[Joëlle Pineau]] and [[Doina Precup]]. Some of its graduates include [[Hugo Larochelle]] and [[Ian Goodfellow]].

Mila is both a research and educational institution, providing [[Postdoctoral researcher|postdoc]] and [[internship]] positions, and granting [[PhD]] and [[Master's degree|Master's]] degrees in [[machine learning]].
