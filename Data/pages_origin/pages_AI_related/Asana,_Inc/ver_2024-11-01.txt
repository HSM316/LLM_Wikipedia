'''Asana, Inc.''' ({{IPAc-en|ə|'|s|ɑː|n|ə}} or {{IPAc-en|'|ɑː|s|ə|n|ə}}), is an American software company based in [[San Francisco]] whose flagship '''Asana''' service is a web and mobile "work management"<ref>{{cite web |title=Gartner Glossary Work Management |url=https://www.gartner.com/en/information-technology/glossary/work-management |access-date=2022-09-01 |website=gartner.com}}</ref> platform designed to help teams organize, track, and manage their work.<ref name="asana_features">{{cite web |title=Understand Asana's core features |url=https://asana.com/features |accessdate=2022-10-10 |website=asana.com}}</ref> Asana, Inc. was founded in 2008 by [[Dustin Moskovitz]] and [[Justin Rosenstein]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |last=Bercovici |first=Jeff |date=May 23, 2018 |title=How Asana's Founders Built a $900 Million Business on Buddhist Principles |url=https://www.inc.com/magazine/201806/jeff-bercovici/asana-best-workplaces-2018.html |access-date=February 10, 2022 |website=Inc.com |language=en-US}}</ref> The product launched commercially in April 2012.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Guynn |first=Jessica |date=April 24, 2012 |title=Asana, software start-up from Facebook vets, launches paid version |language=en-US |work=Los Angeles Times |url=https://www.latimes.com/business/la-xpm-2012-apr-24-la-fi-tn-asana-software-startup-from-facebook-veterans-launches-paid-version-20120424-story.html |access-date=May 18, 2018 |issn=0458-3035}}</ref> In September 2020, the company was valued at $5.5 billion following its direct listing.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Tse |first=Bycrystal |date=October 1, 2020 |title=Asana valued at $5.5 billion after direct listing debut |url=https://fortune.com/2020/09/30/asana-valued-at-5-5-billion-direct-listing/ |access-date=January 26, 2022 |website=Fortune |language=en-US}}</ref>

== History ==
The co-founders met at [[Facebook]], where Moskovitz, Facebook's co-founder and vice president of engineering, and his colleague Rosenstein created a productivity tool called Tasks. In 2008, the co-founders left Facebook to start Asana.<ref name=":3" />  Asana officially launched for free out of beta in November 2011<ref name=":4">{{Cite web |last=Lunden |first=Ingrid |date=30 March 2016 |title=Task management app Asana raises $50M at a $600M valuation led by YC's Sam Altman |url=https://techcrunch.com/2016/03/30/task-management-app-asana-raises-50m-at-a-600m-valuation-led-by-ycs-sam-altman/ |access-date=May 18, 2018 |website=techcrunch.com |language=en-US}}</ref> and commercially in April 2012.<ref>{{Cite web |date=July 23, 2012 |title=Asana raises $28M to make the world a more collaborative place |url=https://venturebeat.com/2012/07/23/asana-funding/ |access-date=February 10, 2022 |website=VentureBeat |language=en-US}}</ref> 

In 2014, Asana launched its native [[iOS]] app and in January 2015, Asana released its native Android app.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Konrad |first=Alex |title=Asana Finally Launches A Native iOS App After Facebook Cofounder Bet Wrong On Web-Based Apps |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/alexkonrad/2014/07/29/asana-finally-launches-a-native-ios-app/ |access-date=2022-11-23 |website=Forbes |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Alba |first=Davey|author-link=Davey Alba |title=Asana's New Android App Shows Design Matters in the Business World |language=en-US |magazine=Wired |url=https://www.wired.com/2015/01/asana-android-app/ |access-date=2022-11-23 |issn=1059-1028}}</ref> In 2016, Asana raised $50 million in Series C financing led by [[Sam Altman]], President of [[Y Combinator]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=March 31, 2016|title=Asana Links Up With $50M Series C for Collaboration Tools|language=en-US|work=Wall Street Journal|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/asana-links-up-with-50m-series-c-for-collaboration-tools-1459425428|access-date=February 10, 2022|issn=0099-9660}}</ref> In 2017, Asana integrated with [[Gmail]], and launched its app in French and German.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Finnegan |first=Matthew |date=2017-11-02 |title=What is Asana? Task management tracking made easy |url=https://www.computerworld.com/article/3235710/what-is-asana-task-management-tracking-made-easy.html |access-date=2022-11-29 |website=Computerworld |language=en}}</ref>

By January 2018, more than 35,000 paying customers were using Asana, including CityFibre,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Trueman|first=Charlotte|date=2021-05-07|title=How Asana helped CityFibre's marketing team boost productivity|url=https://www.computerworld.com/article/3617989/how-asana-helped-cityfibres-marketing-team-boost-productivity.html|access-date=2022-02-17|website=Computerworld|language=en}}</ref> [[AB-InBev]], [[Viessmann]], [[eBay]], [[Uber]], [[Overstock.com]], [[Navy Federal Credit Union]], [[Icelandair Group|Icelandair]], and [[IBM]]. That same year, the company raised $75 million in Series D funding led by Generation Investment Management, a firm backed by [[Al Gore]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Carson|first=Biz|title=Facebook Cofounder's Productivity Startup Asana Hits Growth Spurt, Snags $1.5 Billion Valuation|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/bizcarson/2018/11/29/dustin-moskovitz-asana-funding-valuation/|access-date=2022-02-25|website=Forbes|language=en}}</ref> In November 2018, Asana raised another $50 million in funding in Series E to invest in international and product expansion.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Asana, a work management platform, nabs $50M growth round at a $1.5B valuation|url=https://techcrunch.com/2018/11/29/asana-a-work-management-platform-nabs-50m-growth-round-at-a-1-5b-valuation/|access-date=2022-02-25|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US}}</ref>

In March 2018, Hiten Shah wrote about the history of Asana as "Asana’s Rise to a $1.5 Billion Valuation".<ref>{{Cite web | first = Hiten | last = Shaw | title=Asana's Rise to a $1.5 Billion Valuation |url=https://nira.com/asana-history/ |accessdate=2022-10-04 |website=nira.com| date = June 3, 2019 }}</ref>

In September 2020, Asana went public on the [[New York Stock Exchange]] via a [[direct public offering]]. In August 2021, Asana dual listed on the [[Long-Term Stock Exchange]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Driebusch|first=Corrie|date=June 24, 2021|title=WSJ News Exclusive {{!}} Twilio, Asana to List on Long-Term Stock Exchange as ESG Push Continues|language=en-US|work=Wall Street Journal|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/twilio-asana-to-list-on-long-term-stock-exchange-as-esg-push-continues-11624565701|access-date=February 10, 2022|issn=0099-9660}}</ref>

By December 2021, Asana's customer count increased to 114,000 with two million paid seats globally and 739 of these were spending $50,000 or more on an annualized basis.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Condon|first=Stephanie|title=Asana beats Q3 estimates, surpasses $100m in quarterly sales|url=https://www.zdnet.com/article/asana-beats-q3-estimates-surpassing-100m-in-sales/|access-date=2022-02-18|website=ZDNet|language=en}}</ref>

== Software ==
Asana is a [[Software as a service|software-as-a-service]] platform designed for team collaboration and work management. Teams can create projects, assign [[Task (project management)|tasks]], set deadlines, and communicate directly within Asana. It also includes reporting tools, file attachments, calendars, and goal tracking.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Weir |first=Melanie |title=A guide to Asana, the workplace management tool that helps streamline communication across teams |url=https://www.businessinsider.com/guides/tech/what-is-asana |access-date=2023-01-24 |website=Business Insider |language=en-US}}</ref> 

In 2022, Asana released features for team organization; this included My Goals, Automatic Progress Updates, and integrations for [[Google Workspace]] and [[Figma (software)|Figma]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-06-07 |title=Asana launches new tools to help distributed teams stay aligned on goals |url=https://diginomica.com/asana-tools-distributed-teams-aligned-goals |access-date=2023-02-03 |website=diginomica.com |language=en}}</ref>

=== API and integrations ===
In April 2012, Asana released its [[API]] to third-party developers. Asana's open API provided a means to read and input information and create programmed automation within the app.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last=Constine |first=Josh |date=2012-04-21 |title=Twitter and LinkedIn Manage Tasks With Asana, New API Means Robots Can Too |url=https://techcrunch.com/2012/04/21/asana-stats/ |access-date=2022-12-02 |website=TechCrunch |language=en-US}}</ref> The Asana API is a RESTful interface, allowing users to update and access much of their data on the platform.<ref name=":0" />

In April 2021, Asana launched Asana Partners, which allows for cross-platform integration with its project management software.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Brooks |first=Steve |date=2021-04-16 |title=Asana launches globalisation features and new partner program |url=https://www.enterprisetimes.co.uk/2021/04/16/asana-launches-globalisation-features-and-new-partner-program/ |access-date=2022-11-08 |website=Enterprise Times |language=en-GB}}</ref> 

In July 2021, Asana launched an app for [[Zoom (software)|Zoom]]. The Asana app can be opened within the video-conferencing software Zoom.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Rayome |first=Alison DeNisco |title=Zoom: Use apps like Asana and Dropbox and schedule virtual events now |url=https://www.cnet.com/tech/services-and-software/zoom-use-apps-like-asana-and-dropbox-and-schedule-virtual-events-now/ |access-date=2023-02-10 |website=CNET |language=en}}</ref>

In 2023, Asana launched product capabilities focused on generative artificial intelligence. <ref>{{cite web | url=https://itbrief.com.au/story/asana-launches-ai-product-capabilities-and-guiding-principles | title=Asana launches AI product capabilities and guiding principles }}</ref>

== Reception ==
Asana twice received a 4.5 out of 5 from ''[[PCMag|PC Magazine]]''. In 2017, it was an Editors' Choice and was called "one of the best collaboration and productivity apps for teams" and went on to note its "thoughtful design, fluid interactive elements, and generous member allotment."<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,2817,2408011,00.asp|title=Asana|website=PCMAG|access-date=February 3, 2018}}</ref> In 2020, it received a Best of the Year award, remarking that it "is one of the best apps for managing tasks, workflows, and—yes—certain kinds of projects" despite not being a full-scale project management platform.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Asana Review|url=https://www.pcmag.com/reviews/asana|access-date=February 16, 2022|website=PCMAG|language=en}}</ref> Asana was named as one of the Best Workplaces for Parents in the US by Great Place to Work in 2020 and 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Great Place to Work's Best Workplaces for Parents 2021|url=https://www.greatplacetowork.com/best-workplaces/parents/2021|access-date=February 16, 2022|website=Great Place to Work|language=en-us}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Great Place to Work's Best Workplaces for Parents 2020|url=https://www.greatplacetowork.com/best-workplaces/parents/2020|access-date=December 22, 2020|website=Great Place to Work}}</ref>

In 2021, Inc. named Asana to its annual Best Workplace list and one of the Best Led companies.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Meet the Top 250 Best-Led Companies in America |url=https://www.inc.com/best-led-companies/2021 |access-date=2022-03-04 |website=Inc.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Introducing the Best Workplaces of 2021 |url=https://www.inc.com/best-workplaces/2021 |access-date=2022-03-04 |website=Inc.com |language=en}}</ref> ''[[Fast Company]]'' named Asana the #15 most innovative companies and added the company to its Brands That Matter list.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Brands That Matter – Applications |url=https://www.fastcompany.com/brands-that-matter/2021 |access-date=2022-03-11 |website=Fast Company |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Parrish |first=Cayleigh |date=2021-03-09 |title=The World's Most Innovative Companies 2021 |url=https://www.fastcompany.com/90603436/the-worlds-most-innovative-companies-2021 |access-date=2022-03-11 |website=Fast Company |language=en-US}}</ref>
