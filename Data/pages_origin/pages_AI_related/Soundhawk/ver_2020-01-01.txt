'''Soundhawk''' is an American [[corporation]] headquartered in [[Cupertino, California]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk Corporation  &#124; CrunchBase|url=http://www.crunchbase.com/organization/soundhawk-corporation|website=[[TechCrunch#CrunchBase|CrunchBase]]|publisher=[[TechCrunch]] |date=8 July 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref> that introduced a Smart Listening System in June 2014.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk’s Newly Launched Earpiece and App Will Help You Hear Better|url=http://www.morningnewsusa.com/soundhawks-newly-launched-earpiece-and-app-will-help-you-hear-better-2310671.html|publisher=Morning News USA|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref> The Smart Listening System is a set of products designed to help people hear, communicate and connect with greater clarity in noisy situations.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk Smart Hearing Aid Combines Fashion, Function|url=https://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,2817,2459974,00.asp|publisher=[[PC Magazine]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref> It comes with three pieces of hardware: the Scoop (for the ear), wireless microphone and charging case.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk Launches App-Enabled Wearable to Enhance Hearing|url=http://mashable.com/2014/06/24/soundhawk-listening-wearable/|publisher=[[Mashable]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref> The pieces are unified by a mobile application that includes over one million auditory profiles users can personalize to their environment.<ref name="Bloomberg Businessweek 2013-12-12">{{cite web|title=Silicon Valley's Hearing Aide |url=http://www.businessweek.com/articles/2013-12-12/soundhawk-hearing-aid-by-rodney-perkins-runs-on-bluetooth-and-an-app|publisher=[[Bloomberg Businessweek]]|date=12 December 2013|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref>

== Leadership ==
Soundhawk was founded by [[Rodney Perkins]], [[Stanford University School of Medicine]], [[Otologist]]. He is also the founder of [[ReSound]] and the California Ear Institute at [[Stanford University]] <ref name="Bloomberg Businessweek 2013-12-12"/> Executive leadership at Soundhawk includes Dr. Rodney Perkins as CEO; Drew Dundas, formerly director of Audiology and Assistant Professor of Otolaryngology at UCSF as President and Chief Technology Officer; Steve Manser former engineering leader at [[Apple, Inc.]], [[Palm, Inc.]], and [[Hewlett-Packard]], as Chief Operations Officer and Vice President of Engineering.  The Soundhawk product was built by a group of former hardware and software engineers from [[Palm, Inc.]], [[Apple, Inc.]], [[Hewlett-Packard]], and [[Amazon.com]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Rodney Perkins, M.D. Executive Profile |url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/person.asp?personId=1497405&privcapId=137552040|publisher=[[Bloomberg Businessweek]] |date=9 July 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref>

== Strategic partners ==
Soundhawk is backed by True Ventures,<ref>{{cite web|title=Welcoming Soundhawk|url=http://www.trueventures.com/2013/12/13/welcoming-soundhawk-2/|publisher=True Ventures|date=13 December 2013|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref> [[Foxconn Technology Group]] and other [[Silicon Valley]] entrepreneurs.<ref>{{cite web|title=Foxconn Invests $5.5M in ‘Smart Listening’ Hardware Startup Soundhawk |url=https://blogs.wsj.com/venturecapital/2014/06/24/foxconn-invests-5-5m-in-smart-listening-hardware-startup-soundhawk/|publisher=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref>

== Suspension of operation ==
Soundhawk has suspended operation as per their official website, which as of October 1, 2016 reads "down for maintenance, check back soon". As of March 2018, support pages are no longer available as the domain name connections have expired; nor can it be found through the Apple Store. Amazon.com also lists the device, but says it's currently not available, and they don't know when it will be.

== Product ==

=== Smart Listening System ===
The Smart Listening System blends consumer technology and hearing science to help people hear, communicate and connect.<ref name="Bloomberg Businessweek 2013-12-12"/> Soundhawk includes three pieces of hardware: the Scoop, the Wireless Mic and the Charging Case.<ref name="Mashable">{{cite web|title=Soundhawk Launches App-Enabled Wearable to Enhance Hearing|url=http://mashable.com/2014/06/24/soundhawk-listening-wearable/|publisher=[[Mashable]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref> Unifying the System is a mobile app with patented technology that personalizes Soundhawk to the user and their environments.<ref name="PC Magazine">{{cite web|title=Soundhawk Smart Hearing Aid Combines Fashion, Function |url=https://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,2817,2459974,00.asp|publisher=[[PC Magazine]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref>

==== Soundhawk App ====
The Soundhawk app is used to set up and personalize the system and is available for download to iOS and Android.<ref name="The Next Web">{{cite web |title=Soundhawk is a ‘smart hearing aid’ that cuts through annoying background noise to help you hear more clearly |url=https://thenextweb.com/gadgets/2014/06/24/soundhawk-smart-hearing-aid-cuts-annoying-background-noise-help-hear-clearly/|publisher=The Next Web |date=30 December 2013|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref> The app's patented technology enables many unique hearing profiles to be personalized to the acoustics of different environments.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk hearing amplifier is a 'wearable with a purpose' |url=http://www.cnet.com/news/soundhawk-hearing-amplifier-is-a-wearable-with-purpose/|publisher=[[CNET]]|date=30 December 2013|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref>

==== Scoop ====
The Scoop is a wearable device for the ear that connects with all components of the Smart Listening System™.<ref name="Wired">{{cite web|title=A Sleek New Hearing Aid That Solves a Nagging Problem|url=https://www.wired.com/2014/06/a-sleek-new-hearing-aid-that-solves-a-nagging-problem/|publisher=[[Wired (website)|Wired]]|date=30 December 2013|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref> It uses adaptive audio processing to enhance specific sound frequencies and elevate what the user intends to hear while reducing background noise.<ref name="PC Magazine"/> Included are four different colored ear tips for fit personalization.<ref>{{cite web|title=SoundHawk Unveils A Wearable For Your Listening Pleasure, Partners With FoxConn |url=https://techcrunch.com/2014/06/24/soundhawk-unveils-wearable-for-your-listening-pleasure-partners-with-foxconn/|publisher=[[Techcrunch]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref> The Scoop connects wirelessly to users' phones or tablets via [[Bluetooth]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Soundhawk's earpiece lets you pick exactly what you want to hear|url=https://www.theverge.com/2014/6/24/5835910/soundhawks-earpiece-lets-you-pick-exactly-what-you-want-to-hear/|publisher=[[The Verge]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref>

==== Wireless Mic ====
The Wireless Mic is a wireless microphone used to hear a specific sound source in a variety of environments.<ref name="The Next Web"/> It picks up the intended sound and delivers it to the Scoop.<ref name="Wired"/> The Wireless Mic comes with a clip for attaching it to clothing or placing it on a table.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk's earpiece lets you pick exactly what you want to hear|url=https://www.theverge.com/2014/6/24/5835910/soundhawks-earpiece-lets-you-pick-exactly-what-you-want-to-hear|publisher=[[The Verge]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref>

==== Charging Case ====
The Charging Case is a base for the Scoop and Wireless Mic that protects and charges the system.<ref name="PC Magazine"/> Depending on use, the Charging Case can recharge the Scoop and Wireless Mic up to two additional times.<ref name="Mashable"/>

==Functionality==
Soundhawk can be used to help people participate, connect, and actively listen in any environment. Soundhawk can be used in everyday situations like talking on the phone, dining in noisy restaurants, watching TV or attending business meetings and social gatherings.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk is a wearable for your ear that fine-tunes all the world's noises|url=http://www.techhive.com/article/2366924/soundhawk-is-a-wearable-for-your-ear-that-fine-tunes-all-the-worlds-noises.html/|publisher=[[Techhhive]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=29 September 2014}}</ref> There are three primary ways that Soundhawk can be used:
* Active listening: The Scoop’s dual microphones selectively amplify specific sound frequencies to enhance the user's ability to understand speech while reducing background noise.<ref name="The Next Web"/> Soundhawk can be personalized to unique environments while indoors, outdoors, dining or driving, or for various people the user talks to.<ref>{{cite web|title=Having Trouble Hearing? There's An App For That|url=http://www.smithsonianmag.com/ist/?next=/innovation/Having-Trouble-Hearing-Theres-An-App-For-That-180951902/|publisher=[[Smithsonian Magazine]]|date=30 June 2014|accessdate=29 September 2014}}</ref>
* Cutting through noise: Placing the Wireless Mic near a sound source isolates that specific sound in noisy environments or from a distance.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gadgets to make you a superhuman |url=http://money.cnn.com/2014/09/23/technology/innovationnation/superhuman-wearables/ |publisher=[[CNNMoney]]|date=23 September 2014|accessdate=29 September 2014}}</ref>
* Hands-free communications: Users can place or receive a phone call, talk hands-free on a smartphone or access Siri® and [[Google Now]] directly from the Scoop.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk debuts smart hearing device for those wanting better clarity
|url=http://www.techtimes.com/articles/9122/20140624/soundhawk-smart-hearing-capability.htm/|publisher=[[TechTimes]]|date=24 September 2014|accessdate=29 September 2014}}</ref>
