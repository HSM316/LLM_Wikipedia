'''Soundhawk''' was an American [[corporation]] headquartered in [[Cupertino, California]]. 

In June 2014 Soundhawk introduced a "Smart Listening System", a [[hearing aid]] designed for noisy situations.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk's Newly Launched Earpiece and App Will Help You Hear Better|url=http://www.morningnewsusa.com/soundhawks-newly-launched-earpiece-and-app-will-help-you-hear-better-2310671.html|publisher=Morning News USA|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk Smart Hearing Aid Combines Fashion, Function|url=https://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,2817,2459974,00.asp|publisher=[[PC Magazine]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref> The Soundhawk product came with three pieces of hardware: an earphone called a "Scoop", a wireless microphone, and a charging case.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk Launches App-Enabled Wearable to Enhance Hearing|url=http://mashable.com/2014/06/24/soundhawk-listening-wearable/|publisher=[[Mashable]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref> The product line used mobile applications and could be personalized to different environments.<ref name="Bloomberg Businessweek 2013-12-12">{{cite web|title=Silicon Valley's Hearing Aide |url=http://www.businessweek.com/articles/2013-12-12/soundhawk-hearing-aid-by-rodney-perkins-runs-on-bluetooth-and-an-app|publisher=[[Bloomberg Businessweek]]|date=12 December 2013|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref>

The company has been defunct since around 2016.{{cn|date=October 2022}}

== Company history ==
Soundhawk was founded by [[Rodney Perkins]], [[Stanford University School of Medicine]], [[Otologist]]. He is also the founder of ReSound and the California Ear Institute at [[Stanford University]]<ref name="Bloomberg Businessweek 2013-12-12"/> Executive leadership at Soundhawk includes Perkins as CEO; Drew Dundas, formerly director of Audiology and Assistant Professor of Otolaryngology at UCSF as President and Chief Technology Officer; Steve Manser former engineering leader at [[Apple, Inc.]], [[Palm, Inc.]], and [[Hewlett-Packard]], as Chief Operations Officer and Vice President of Engineering.  The Soundhawk product was built by a group of former hardware and software engineers from [[Palm, Inc.]], [[Apple, Inc.]], [[Hewlett-Packard]], and [[Amazon.com]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Rodney Perkins, M.D. Executive Profile |url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/person.asp?personId=1497405&privcapId=137552040|publisher=[[Bloomberg Businessweek]] |date=9 July 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref>

Soundhawk is backed by True Ventures,<ref>{{cite web|title=Welcoming Soundhawk|url=http://www.trueventures.com/2013/12/13/welcoming-soundhawk-2/|publisher=True Ventures|date=13 December 2013|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref> [[Foxconn Technology Group]] and other [[Silicon Valley]] entrepreneurs.<ref>{{cite web|title=Foxconn Invests $5.5M in 'Smart Listening' Hardware Startup Soundhawk |url=https://blogs.wsj.com/venturecapital/2014/06/24/foxconn-invests-5-5m-in-smart-listening-hardware-startup-soundhawk/|publisher=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=13 August 2014}}</ref>

Soundhawk has suspended operation as per their official website, which as of October 1, 2016 reads "down for maintenance, check back soon". As of March 2018, support pages are no longer available as the domain name connections have expired; nor can it be found through the Apple Store. Amazon.com also lists the device, but says it's currently not available, and they don't know when it will be.{{cn|date=October 2022}}

== Product ==

Soundhawk's Smart Listening System included three pieces of hardware: the Scoop, the Wireless Mic and the Charging Case.<ref name="Mashable">{{cite web|title=Soundhawk Launches App-Enabled Wearable to Enhance Hearing|url=http://mashable.com/2014/06/24/soundhawk-listening-wearable/|publisher=[[Mashable]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref> This system was controlled via a mobile app.<ref name="PC Magazine">{{cite web|title=Soundhawk Smart Hearing Aid Combines Fashion, Function |url=https://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,2817,2459974,00.asp|publisher=[[PC Magazine]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref>

The Soundhawk app was used to set up and personalize the system and was available for download to iOS and Android.<ref name="The Next Web">{{cite web |title=Soundhawk is a 'smart hearing aid' that cuts through annoying background noise to help you hear more clearly |url=https://thenextweb.com/gadgets/2014/06/24/soundhawk-smart-hearing-aid-cuts-annoying-background-noise-help-hear-clearly/|publisher=The Next Web |date=30 December 2013|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref> The app enabled individual hearing profiles to be created for different acoustic environments.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk hearing amplifier is a 'wearable with a purpose' |url=http://www.cnet.com/news/soundhawk-hearing-amplifier-is-a-wearable-with-purpose/|publisher=[[CNET]]|date=30 December 2013|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref>

The Scoop was an earphone that used adaptive audio processing to enhance specific sound frequencies while reducing background noise.<ref name="PC Magazine"/> The Scoop connects to users' phones or tablets via [[Bluetooth]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Soundhawk's earpiece lets you pick exactly what you want to hear|url=https://www.theverge.com/2014/6/24/5835910/soundhawks-earpiece-lets-you-pick-exactly-what-you-want-to-hear/|publisher=[[The Verge]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref>

The system's wireless mic was used to identify specific sounds in different environments to facilitate [[noise cancellation]]. The mic could be clipped to clothing, or placed on a table.<ref>{{cite web|title=Soundhawk's earpiece lets you pick exactly what you want to hear|url=https://www.theverge.com/2014/6/24/5835910/soundhawks-earpiece-lets-you-pick-exactly-what-you-want-to-hear|publisher=[[The Verge]]|date=24 June 2014|accessdate=20 September 2014}}</ref>

The product also came with a charging case,<ref name="PC Magazine"/><ref name="Mashable"/> and could also be used for phone calls or other smartphone functions such as [[virtual assistant]]s.
