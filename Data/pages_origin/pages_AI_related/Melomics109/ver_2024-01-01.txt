'''Melomics109''' is a [[computer cluster]] located at [[University of Málaga|Universidad de Málaga]] used to create digital music. It is part of the [[Spanish Supercomputing Network]], and has been designed to increase the computational power provided by [[Iamus (computer)|Iamus]].<ref>{{cite news | title= Artificial music: The computers that create melodies|publisher= BBC Future|last= Ball |first= Philip| date =8 August 2014| url =http://www.bbc.com/future/story/20140808-music-like-never-heard-before/}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | title= Die Mozart-Maschine| journal= Die Tageszeitung (Taz)| date =13 January 2013| url =http://www.taz.de/Computer-komponiert-klassische-Musik/!108691/| last1= Lehmann| first1= Christian}}</ref> Powered by [[Melomics]]' technology, the composing module of Melomics109 is able to create and synthesize music in a variety of [[musical styles]]. This music has been made freely accessible to everyone.<ref>{{cite news | title= La revolución musical de una máquina| newspaper= El Pais| date =8 December 2013| url =http://ccaa.elpais.com/ccaa/2013/12/06/andalucia/1386362952_545600.html}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal| title= Francisco J. Vico and the First Computer-Musicians| journal= IDG Connect| date= 7 July 2014| url= http://www.idgconnect.com/abstract/8505/francisco-j-vico-first-computer-musicians| access-date= 22 July 2014| archive-date= 25 June 2016| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20160625021540/http://www.idgconnect.com/abstract/8505/francisco-j-vico-first-computer-musicians| url-status= dead}}</ref> The cluster consists of three cabinets with customized front panels.

==Album==
''[[0music (album)|0music]]'' is the first album composed and interpreted by Melomics109. It was launched in July 2014,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://melomics.com/@melomics109/0music/ |title=0music |access-date=2014-08-01 |archive-date=2014-08-11 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140811101055/http://melomics.com/@melomics109/0music/ |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | title= Presentación de 0music| journal= University of Malaga Press| date =19 July 2014| url =http://www.uma.es/sala-de-prensa/noticias/presentacion-de-0music-segundo-disco-compuesto-y-grabajo-con-la-tecnologia-melonics/}}</ref> and was released in audio (MP3) and editable format (MIDI), under CC0 (public domain) licensing.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://melomics.uma.es/search?q=melomics109 |title=repository |access-date=2019-01-17 |archive-date=2019-01-19 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190119174402/http://melomics.uma.es/search?q=melomics109 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
