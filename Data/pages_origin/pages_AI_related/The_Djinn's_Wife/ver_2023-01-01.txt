'''"The Djinn's Wife"''' is a 2006 [[science fiction]] short story by British writer [[Ian McDonald (British author)|Ian McDonald]]. It was first published in ''[[Asimov's Science Fiction]]''.

==Synopsis==
Esha is a dancer and A.J. Rao is an [[artificial intelligence]] embodied in a swarm of [[nanobot]]s. They get married, but their relationship quickly turns sour.

==Reception==

"The Djinn's Wife" won the 2007 [[Hugo Award for Best Novelette]]<ref name=Hugo>[http://www.thehugoawards.org/hugo-history/2007-hugo-awards/ 2007 Hugo Awards], at TheHugoAwards.org; retrieved August 20, 2018</ref> and the 2007 [[BSFA Award]] for Best Short Fiction.<ref name=BSFA>[https://bsfa.co.uk/bsfa-awards/ BSFA Awards] at BSFA.co.uk; retrieved August 20, 2018</ref>

At ''[[Tangent Online]]'', Carole Ann Moleti called it "sumptuous" and "ethereal", noting that it met all the criteria to be considered an example of [[paranormal romance]].<ref name=Tangent>[https://www.tangentonline.com/articles-columnsmenu-284/1111-hugo-and-i-go-meandering-thoughts-on-a-few-that-made-the-list-and-some-that-didnt Hugo and I Go: Meandering Thoughts on a Few That Made the List, and Some That Didn't ], by Carole Ann Moleti, at ''[[Tangent Online]]''; published July 23, 2007; retrieved August 20, 2018</ref> At ''[[SF Signal]]'', however, John DeNardo considered it a "lackluster reading experience".<ref name=DeNardo>[https://www.sfsignal.com/archives/2009/04/review_cyberabad_days_by_ian_mcdonald/ REVIEW: Cyberabad Days by Ian McDonald], by John DeNardo, at ''[[SF Signal]]''; published April 7, 2009; retrieved August 20, 2018</ref>
