'''Robot Monk Xian'er''' ({{zh|c=贤二机器僧}}) is a [[humanoid robot]] based on the [[cartoon]] [[Fictional character|character]] [[Xian'er]]. It was developed by a team of monks, volunteers and [[AI]] experts from [[Beijing Longquan Monastery]] in [[Beijing]], China.
<ref>{{cite web |url=http://digi.163.com/16/0407/08/BK1LFHDB00162OUT.html |title="科研最强寺庙"龙泉寺研发呆萌机器僧 |author= |date=2016-04-07 |work= |publisher=网易 |accessdate=2016-11-13}}</ref> He can follow human instructions to make body movements, read scriptures and play Buddhist music. He can chat and respond to people's emotional and spiritual questions with Buddhist wisdom. As a [[chatbot]], '''Robot Monk Xian'er''' is available on certain public platforms including [[WeChat]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/world/china-watch/technology/wechat-robotic-monk/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171001165131/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/world/china-watch/technology/wechat-robotic-monk/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=1 October 2017 |title=WeChat robotic monk is now chatting in English |author= |date= |work= |publisher=The Telegraph |accessdate=27 January 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/apr/26/robot-monk-to-spread-buddhist-wisdom-to-the-digital-generation |title=Robot monk to spread Buddhist wisdom to the digital generation |author= |date= |work= |publisher=The Guardians |accessdate=27 January 2018}}</ref> and Facebook. Over the years, master [[Xuecheng (monk)|Xuecheng]], the abbot of Beijing Longquan Monastery, replied to thousands of questions on [[Sina Weibo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://weibo.com/xuecheng |title=学诚法师 新浪微博 |author= |date= |work= |publisher= |accessdate=27 January 2018}}</ref> These questions and their answers become the data source of the chatbot.
