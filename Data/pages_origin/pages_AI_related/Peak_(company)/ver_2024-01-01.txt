'''Peak''' is an [[artificial intelligence]] company headquartered in [[Manchester]], [[United Kingdom|UK]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Chapman |first=Stephen |url=https://www.prolificnorth.co.uk/news/digital/2018/02/artificial-intelligence-company-takes-top-floor-£8m-manchester-development |title=Artificial Intelligence company takes top floor of £8m Manchester development |work=Prolific North |date=22 February 2018}}</ref> It was founded in 2015<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bcs.org/articles-opinion-and-research/what-is-decision-intelligence-and-where-has-it-come-from/|title=What is DI: Interview with Richard Potter (Peak)|date=22 October 2015|publisher=BCS}}</ref> and has additional offices in [[Jaipur]], [[India]], and [[New York City]], United States. It is known for its artificial intelligence platform, a [[Software as a Service|SaaS]] platform that allows data scientists to build AI workflows, invoke them on ingested data and expose the results via [[Application programming interface|APIs]] and/or built-in web applications, as well as abstracting the underlying [[Cloud computing|cloud infrastructure]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://venturebeat.com/2020/04/23/peak-ai-raises-12-million-to-bolster-enterprise-ai-adoption/|title=Peak.AI raises $12 million to bolster enterprise AI adoption|first=Kyle|last=Wiggers|date=2020-04-23|website=VentureBeat|access-date=2020-05-04}}</ref>

== History ==

The company was founded by CEO Richard Potter, David Leitch and Atul Sharma.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/business/business-news/data-analytics-company-formed-pub-13607095|title=Data analytics company formed in pub secures £2.5m funding|first=Lucy|last=Roue|date=12 September 2017|publisher=}}</ref> In 2015, Peak was one of the winners of the Tech North Northern Stars competition.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://technorthhq.com/business/northern-stars-grand-final/|title=Northern Stars Grand Final 201|date=4 December 2015|publisher=Tech Nation}}</ref> In 2017, it secured £2.5 million in [[Series A round|Series A capital funding]] from [[MMC Ventures]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://medium.com/mmc-writes/mmcs-investment-in-peak-57e11e345819|title=MMC's investment in Peak|last=Coker|first=Jon|date=2017-09-11|website=MMC writes|access-date=2018-04-09}}</ref> for investment in [[machine learning]] and [[artificial intelligence]] technologies<ref>{{cite web |last=Austin |first=Simon |url=https://www.prolificnorth.co.uk/digital/2017/09/manchester-data-analytics-firm-peak-secures-£25m-funding |title=Manchester data analytics firm Peak secures £2.5m funding |work=Prolific North |date=11 September 2017}}</ref> and was named as one of the top five startups in Manchester by [[Wired (magazine)|''Wired'' magazine]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.wired.co.uk/article/startup-city-manchester|title=Startup Cities: Manchester|last=Volpicelli|first=Gian|magazine=Wired|access-date=2018-04-09}}</ref> In 2018, it was chosen to work as part of [[Arsenal F.C.]]'s Innovation Lab<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.prolificnorth.co.uk/news/digital/2018/01/peak-signed-arsenal-innovation-lab|title=Peak signed up for Arsenal Innovation Lab|date=17 January 2018|publisher=}}</ref> and it was one of the 37 fastest growing technology companies in the UK selected to join the [[East London Tech City|Tech City UK]] Upscale programme.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.techcityuk.com/blog/2018/01/37-of-the-uks-fastest-growing-tech-companies-join-upscale-2018/|title=37 of the UK's fastest growing Tech Companies join Upscale 2018|date=2018-01-25|work=Tech City UK|access-date=2018-04-09|language=en-US}}</ref>

In April 2020, the company raised $12 million in extended series A funding, required for sustaining its growth, commercial expansion, and R&D efforts.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://venturebeat.com/2020/04/23/peak-ai-raises-12-million-to-bolster-enterprise-ai-adoption/|title=Peak.AI raises $12 million to bolster enterprise AI adoption|first=Kyle|last=Wiggers|date=2020-04-23|website=VentureBeat|access-date=2020-05-04}}</ref> In February 2021, Peak announced a $21 million Series B funding round – led by investors Oxx, Praetura Ventures, MMC Ventures and Arete – to further make AI accessible to non-tech companies.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2021/02/17/peak-ai-nabs-21m-for-a-platform-to-help-non-tech-companies-make-ai-based-decisions/|title=Peak AI nabs $21M for a platform to help non-tech companies make AI-based decisions|first=Ingrid|last=Lunden|date=2021-02-18|website=TechCrunch|access-date=2021-02-17}}</ref> In August 2021, Peak announced a $75 million Series C funding round, led by [[SoftBank Vision Fund|SoftBank Vision Fund 2]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Peak raises $75M for a platform that helps non-tech companies build AI applications|url=https://social.techcrunch.com/2021/08/31/peak-raises-75m-for-a-platform-that-helps-non-tech-companies-build-ai-applications/|access-date=2021-11-03|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US}}</ref>
