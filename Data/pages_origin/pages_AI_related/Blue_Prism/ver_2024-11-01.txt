'''Blue Prism''' is the trading name of the '''Blue Prism Group plc''', a British multinational [[Software company|software corporation]] that pioneered and makes enterprise [[robotic process automation]] (RPA) software that provides a digital workforce designed to automate complex, end-to-end operational activities. In March 2022, Blue Prism was acquired by [[SS&C Technologies]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=SS&C Completes Acquisition of Blue Prism Group PLC |url=https://investor.ssctech.com/investor-relations/press-releases/press-release-details/2022/SSC-Completes-Acquisition-of-Blue-Prism-Group-PLC/default.aspx |access-date=2023-01-19 |website=investor.ssctech.com |language=en-US}}</ref>

Blue Prism's headquarters are at 2 Cinnamon Park Crab Lane [[Warrington]] WA2 0XP, UK with regional offices in the U.S. and Australia.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/relieved-shareholders-see-blue-prism-group-in-new-light-xbl3wbtl6|title=Relieved shareholders see Blue Prism Group in new light|last=Howard|first=Tom|newspaper=[[The Times]] |date=22 November 2019|access-date=22 November 2019|language=en|issn=0140-0460}}</ref><ref>{{citation|url=https://beta.companieshouse.gov.uk/company/09759493|title=Blue Prism Group PLC|author=Companies House|date=n.d.|access-date=27 January 2020|language=en}}</ref> The company was listed on the [[London Stock Exchange]] AIM market until its acquisition.<ref name=":0" />

==History==
===Formation===
Blue Prism was founded in 2001 by a group of [[process automation]] experts to develop technology that could be used to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of organisations.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.builtinaustin.com/2019/01/24/blue-prism-130-million-funding|title=Blue Prism is raising $130M to fuel new technologies|website=Built In Austin|language=en|access-date=27 March 2019}}</ref> Initially their focus was on the [[back office]] where they recognised an enormous unfulfilled need for automation. The company was co-founded by Alastair Bathgate and David Moss to provide a new approach that today is known as robotic process automation, or RPA.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2019/01/24/blue-prism-looks-to-partners-to-expand-robotic-process-automation-with-ai/|title=Blue Prism looks to partners to expand robotic process automation with AI|website=TechCrunch|date=24 January 2019 |language=en-US|access-date=27 March 2019}}</ref>

In 2003, Blue Prism's first commercial product, Automate, was launched. In 2005, the second version of Automate was released with features for large scale processing. [[Co-operative Financial Services]] began using Blue Prism software in 2005 to automate manual processes in customer services.<ref name="cw-extends">
{{cite news |last=Ashford |first=Warwick |date=29 August 2007 |title=Co-operative bank extends automation with Blue Prism |url=http://www.computerweekly.com/news/2240082707/Co-operative-bank-extends-automation-with-Blue-Prism | work=[[Computer Weekly]] |access-date=14 September 2017}}</ref>

===Robotic Process Automation===
[[Robotic process automation]] (RPA) is the application of technology that provides organizations with a digital workforce that follows rule-based business processes and interacts with the organizations' systems in the same way that existing users currently do.<ref name="LSEBook">{{cite book |title=Service Automation: Robots and The Future of Work|publisher=London School of Economics|isbn=978-0956414564 |author=Professors Leslie Willcocks and Mary Lacity|year=2016 }}</ref><ref name="TimesBPO">{{Citation|title=Times BPO Supplement|publisher=Raconteur|url=http://np.netpublicator.com/netpublication/n34908574|access-date=6 February 2017|archive-date=24 October 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191024121534/http://np.netpublicator.com/netpublication/n34908574|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="TechChangingBPO">{{Citation |title=How is technology changing BPO? |url=https://www.computerweekly.com/feature/How-technology-is-changing-BPO |publisher=Computer Weekly}}{{Dead link|date=October 2019|bot=InternetArchiveBot|fix-attempted=yes}}</ref><ref name="HfSThreat">{{Citation|title=Robotic Automation Emerges as a Threat to Traditional Low Cost Outsourcing|publisher=HfS Research|url=http://www.hfsresearch.com/Robotic-Automation-as-Threat-to-Traditional-Low-Cost-Outsourcing|access-date=6 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150921062911/http://www.hfsresearch.com/Robotic-Automation-as-Threat-to-Traditional-Low-Cost-Outsourcing|archive-date=21 September 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="ChappelERPA">{{Citation|title=Understanding Enterprise RPA: The Blue Prism Example|publisher=David Chappel & Associates|url=http://www.davidchappell.com/writing/white_papers/Understanding_Enterprise_RPA--The_Blue_Prism_Example_v1.0.pdf}}</ref><ref name="LSENewApproach">{{Citation|title=A new approach to automating services|publisher=London School of Economics|url=http://eprints.lse.ac.uk/68135/1/Willcocks_New%20approach_2016.pdf}}</ref><ref name="HFSUnderlings">{{Citation|title=Welcoming our robotic security underlings|date=19 February 2016 |publisher=HFS Research|url=https://www.hfsresearch.com/pointsofview/welcoming-our-robotic-security-underlings}}</ref> Blue Prism has been credited for coining the term "Robotic Process Automation."<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/alexkonrad/2019/09/11/from-communism-to-coding-how--daniel-dines-of-7-billion-uipath-became-the-first-bot-billionaire/|title=From Communism To Coding: How Daniel Dines Of $7 Billion UiPath Became The First Bot Billionaire|last=Konrad|first=Alex|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=6 December 2019}}</ref>

by the nutrition company, Fonterra, to fix quantity mismatches in planning software SAP;<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fonterra.com/nz/en/our-stories/articles/meet-fonterras-newest-team-member-enid.html|title=Meet Fonterra's newest team member – Enid|website=Fonterra|access-date=17 April 2019}}</ref> and by Milaha for the entry, processing and transfer of data.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.seatrade-maritime.com/news/middle-east-africa/milaha-forges-ahead-with-automation-system-for-back-office.html|title=Milaha forges ahead with automation system for back office|last=Wee|first=Vincent|website=www.seatrade-maritime.com|date=29 May 2018 |language=en|access-date=17 April 2019}}</ref> The independent market research company [[Forrester Research]] identified Blue Prism as one of three companies that is considered a leader in the robotic process automation field both in terms of their market presence as well as the quality of their offering in a 2017 study.<ref name="forrester-wave-robotic-process-automation">{{Citation|title=The Forrester Wave™: Robotic Process Automation, Q1 2017|url=https://www.edgeverve.com/wpcontent/uploads/2017/02/forrester-wave-robotic-process-automation.pdf|publisher=forrester.com}}{{Dead link|date=October 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

An October 2018 study by Grand View Research, Inc. stated that the key companies in the RPA market included: Automation Anywhere, Inc.; Blue Prism Group PLC; UIPath; Be Informed B.V.; OpenSpan; and Jacada, Inc.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.grandviewresearch.com/press-release/global-robotic-process-automation-rpa-market|title=Robotic Process Automation (RPA) Market Worth $3.11 Billion by 2025|website=www.grandviewresearch.com|language=en|access-date=19 March 2019}}</ref> In 2019, Gartner released its 'Magic Quadrant' for RPA, and Blue Prism was one of the leaders in the market.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://enterprisetalk.com/featured/gartner-released-its-first-magic-quadrant-for-rpa-with-three-leaders/|title=Gartner Released Its First Magic Quadrant for RPA with Three Leaders|last=Ramnani|first=Meeta|date=16 July 2019|website=EnterpriseTalk|language=en-US|access-date=20 September 2019}}</ref>

===Initial public offering===

By November 2016, it had offices in [[Chicago]] and [[Miami]], as well as the [[United Kingdom]].<ref name=ft-coop>{{cite news |last1=Bounds |first1=Andy |last2=Tighe |first2=Chris |date=23 November 2016 |title=What businesses want from the Autumn Statement |url=https://www.ft.com/content/101e8858-b0a8-11e6-9c37-5787335499a0 | work=[[Financial Times]] |location=United Kingdom |access-date=14 September 2017}}</ref>

On 6 January 2017 Blue Prism announced it would open new offices in [[Austin, Texas]], while remaining based in [[London]]. At the time, it employed 86 people worldwide.<ref name=biz-theis>

It included a new AI engine, an updated marketplace for extensions, and a new lab for in-house AI innovation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2019/01/24/blue-prism-to-issue-130m-in-stock-to-raise-new-funds/|title=Blue Prism to issue $130M in stock to raise new funds|website=TechCrunch|date=24 January 2019 |language=en-US|access-date=24 April 2019}}</ref>

===Acquisition===
In September 2021, it was announced that [[Vista Equity Partners]] plans to acquire Blue Prism for £1.095 billion ($1.5 billion). Vista intends to merge Blue Prism into [[TIBCO Software]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2021/09/28/rpa-industry-consolidation-continues-with-sale-of-blue-prism-to-vista-for-1-095b/|title=RPA industry consolidation continues with sale of Blue Prism to Vista for £1.095B|date=28 September 2021|website=Tech Crunch}}</ref> Vista is said to be considering “potentially material headcount reductions” of 8 to 10 per cent of the approximately 4,750 employees of the merged companies.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Bradshaw|first=Tim|date=2021-09-28|title=Blue Prism agrees £1.1bn takeover by Vista|work=Financial Times|url=https://www.ft.com/content/43bebb35-e616-4bff-801d-9f4396ece5d6|access-date=2021-10-01}}</ref>

The original takeover deal was first announced in December 2021, when a bidding war between SS&C and Vista Equity partners drove up the price.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.uktech.news/ai/blue-prism-takeover-delisting-20220222|title=Software firm Blue Prism edges closer to London delisting after US takeover|date=22 February 2022|website=UK Tech News}}</ref> Blue Prism shareholders ultimately voted in favor of SS&C. On March 16, 2022 [[SS&C Technologies]] announced it had completed its acquisition of Blue Prism for approximately $1.6 billion (£1.25 billion).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://investor.ssctech.com/investor-relations/press-releases/press-release-details/2022/SSC-Completes-Acquisition-of-Blue-Prism-Group-PLC/default.aspx|title=SS&C Completes Acquisition of Blue Prism Group PLC|date=16 March 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Vaslie |first=Zachary |date=March 16, 2022 |title=SS&C completes $1.6B Blue Prism acquisition |url=https://www.hartfordbusiness.com/article/ssc-completes-16b-blue-prism-acquisition |website=Hartford Business Journal}}</ref>

==Blue Prism technology==
Blue Prism is built on the [[Microsoft .NET Framework]]. It automates any application and supports any platform ([[mainframe]], [[Microsoft Windows|Windows]], [[Windows Presentation Foundation|WPF]], [[Java (programming language)|Java]], web, etc.)<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://searchhealthit.techtarget.com/feature/Blue-Prism-brings-RPA-technology-to-healthcare|title=Blue Prism brings RPA technology to healthcare|website=SearchHealthIT|language=en|access-date=24 April 2019}}</ref> presented in a variety of ways (terminal emulator, thick client, thin client, web browser, Citrix and web services).{{cn|date=March 2024}} All of the process coding is automated on the back end, allowing even non-technical users to automate a process by dragging components into an interface.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2019/01/24/blue-prism-looks-to-partners-to-expand-robotic-process-automation-with-ai/|title=Blue Prism looks to partners to expand robotic process automation with AI|website=TechCrunch|date=24 January 2019 |language=en-US|access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref>

In 2016, Blue Prism received one of the top honors at the AIconics Awards, named as The Best Enterprise Application of AI.<ref name="AIConics">{{Citation|title=Blue Prism: Best Enterprise Application of AI|publisher=aibusiness.org|url=http://aibusiness.org/aiconics-award-winners-blue-prism-best-enterprise-application-of-ai-2/}}</ref><ref name="AIConics2">{{Citation|title=London 2016 AIConics Winners|publisher=theaiconics.com|url=http://theaiconics.com/2016-winners/}}</ref>  In 2017, the company was named one of MIT Tech Review's 50 Smartest Companies and was the winner of the UK Tech Awards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://finance.yahoo.com/news/mashreq-bank-selects-blue-prism-140201928.html|title=Mashreq Bank Selects Blue Prism to Drive Innovation Across All Banking Functions|website=finance.yahoo.com|date=14 February 2018 |language=en-US|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref>

In 2019, Blue Prism announced its idea for connected-RPA. Connected-RPA is the offering of an automation platform with AI and cognitive features built in.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=https://www.information-age.com/blue-prism-rpa-123478555/|title=Blue Prism launches connected-RPA to transform the market|date=25 January 2019|website=Information Age|language=en|access-date=2 May 2019}}</ref> It includes features like a Digital Exchange, with online access to drag and drop AI, machine learning, and cognitive and disruptive technologies; a web-based tool that reduces the time to prepare for a RPA deployment; and an online community for sharing knowledge and best practices.<ref name=":4" /> The Digital Exchange gives customers and partners the ability to create and share tools that can be used with Blue Prism's software.  To encourage innovation, Blue Prism has an AI engine for building connectors to advanced AI tools from Amazon, Google, IBM, and other AI platforms.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2019/01/24/blue-prism-looks-to-partners-to-expand-robotic-process-automation-with-ai/|title=Blue Prism looks to partners to expand robotic process automation with AI|website=TechCrunch|date=24 January 2019 |language=en-US|access-date=8 May 2019}}</ref>

In the summer of 2019, Blue Prism acquired Thoughtonomy for $100 million.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Blue Prism acquires UK's Thoughtonomy for up to $100M to expand its RPA platform with more AI|url=https://techcrunch.com/2019/06/18/blue-prism-acquires-uks-thoughtonomy-for-up-to-100m-to-expand-its-rpa-platform-with-more-ai/|access-date=30 July 2020|website=TechCrunch|date=19 June 2019 |language=en-US}}</ref> Thoughtonomy was later renamed Blue Prism Cloud. The acquisition provided Blue Prism with Cloud capabilities, allowing them to offer Automation as a Service over the cloud.

==The digital workforce==
Blue Prism's digital workforce is built, managed and owned by the user or customer, spanning operations and technology, adhering to an enterprise-wide robotic operating model. It is code-free<ref name="HBR">{{Citation|title=What knowledge workers stand to gain from automation|journal=Harvard Business Review|date=19 June 2015 |url=https://hbr.org/2015/06/what-knowledge-workers-stand-to-gain-from-automation|last1=Lacity |first1=Mary C. |last2=Willcocks |first2=Leslie }}</ref> and can automate any software in a non-invasive way. The digital workforce can be applied to automate processes in any department where clerical or administrative work is performed across an organisation.<ref name="TEDxDavidMoss">{{Citation|title=White Collar Robots: The Virtual Workforce|date=28 January 2016 |publisher=TEDx Talks|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1SximAg9t4w}}</ref>

==Business and markets==
Blue Prism has been deployed in a number of industries including banking, finance and insurance, consumer package goods, legal services, public sector, professional services, healthcare and utilities.

Its RPA software has been purchased by companies such as [[Coca-Cola]], [[Pfizer]], [[Prudential plc|Prudential]], [[Sony]], and [[Walgreens]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nanalyze.com/2018/05/robotic-process-automation-blue-prism/|title=Robotic Process Automation From Blue Prism|date=1 May 2018|website=Nanalyze|language=en-US|access-date=23 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://finance.yahoo.com/news/mashreq-bank-selects-blue-prism-140201928.html|title=Mashreq Bank Selects Blue Prism to Drive Innovation Across All Banking Functions|website=finance.yahoo.com|date=14 February 2018 |language=en-US|access-date=23 May 2019}}</ref>

In 2018, the UK water company United Utilities purchased robots from Blue Prism in order to use RPA to streamline its processes and increase efficiency.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=https://utilityweek.co.uk/united-utilities-uses-robots-revolutionise-service/|title=United Utilities uses robots to 'revolutionise' service|date=9 February 2018|website=Utility Week|language=en-US|access-date=23 May 2019}}</ref> The robots monitor signals and alerts on the water network and automatically inform engineers of any issues.<ref name=":5" />

The company IEG4 worked with Blue Prism to improve the handling of benefits claims with more efficient data processing.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ukauthority.com/articles/using-robots-to-automate-the-repetitive/|title=Using robots to automate the repetitive|website=UKAuthority|language=en|access-date=31 May 2019}}</ref>

The finance company Fannie Mae used an RPA platform from Blue Prism to automate a review and notification process within its mortgage operations area.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://searchcio.techtarget.com/feature/Readying-your-IT-shop-for-RPA-software|title=Readying your IT shop for RPA software|website=SearchCIO|language=en|access-date=31 May 2019}}</ref> Another finance institution, Mashreq, worked with Blue Prism to automate multiple functions, including banking operations, compliance, customer care and help desk operations.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://finance.yahoo.com/news/mashreq-bank-selects-blue-prism-140201928.html|title=Mashreq Bank Selects Blue Prism to Drive Innovation Across All Banking Functions|website=finance.yahoo.com|date=14 February 2018 |language=en-US|access-date=4 June 2019}}</ref>  In the healthcare industry, Ascension Health has used Blue Prism's RPA technology for back-office functions, and to manage the licensure and certification of clinicians.<ref name=":2" />

In 2021, Blue Prism expanded its partnership with [[ABBYY FineReader|ABBYY]], integrating their process and task mining capabilities.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Gray|first=Catherine|date=2021-10-07|title=Blue Prism announces partnership with ABBYY|url=https://technologymagazine.com/ai-and-machine-learning/blue-prism-announces-partnership-abbyy|access-date=2022-02-08|website=technologymagazine.com|language=en}}</ref>
