The '''Mexican International Conference on Artificial Intelligence''' ('''MICAI''') is the name of an annual [[Meeting|conference]] covering all areas of [[Artificial Intelligence]] (AI), held in [[Mexico]]. The first MICAI conference was held in 2000. The conference is attended every year by about two hundred of AI researchers and PhD students and 500−1000 local graduate students.

== Overview ==
MICAI is a high-level peer-reviewed international conference covering all areas of Artificial Intelligence. All editions of MICAI have been published in Springer [[Springer Science+Business Media|Springer]] [[Lecture Notes in Artificial Intelligence|LNAI]] (N 1793, 2313, 2972, 3789, 4293, 4827, 5317, 5845, 6437–6438). Recent MICAI events (2006, 2007, 2008, 2009, and 2010) received over 300 submissions from over 40 countries each. The conference's scientific program includes keynote lectures, paper presentations, tutorials, panels, posters, and workshops. MICAI is organized by the [[Mexican Society for Artificial Intelligence]] (SMIA) in cooperation with various national institutions.
