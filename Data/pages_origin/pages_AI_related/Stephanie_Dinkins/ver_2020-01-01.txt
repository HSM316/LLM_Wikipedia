'''Stephanie Dinkins''' is a [[New York City|New York]] based transmedia artist who creates platforms for dialog about [[Artificial intelligence|artificial intelligence (AI)]] as it intersects race, gender, aging, and our future histories.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stephaniedinkins.com/|title=STEPHANIE DINKINS|website=STEPHANIE DINKINS|language=en|access-date=2019-12-09}}</ref>

Stephanie Dinkins works as a Professor of [[Interactive media|Interactive Media]] at [[Stony Brook University]] and is a 2017 A Blade of Grass Fellow 2016 Artist-in-Residence at NEW INC. Her aim is to “Create a unique culturally attuned AI entity in collaboration with coders, engineers and in close consultation with local communities of color that reflects and is empowered to work toward the goals of its community. “<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.eyebeam.org/introducing-stephanie-dinkins/|title=Meet Stephanie Dinkins|date=2017-11-29|website=Eyebeam|access-date=2019-12-09}}</ref>

Dinkins’ work is exhibited internationally at a broad spectrum of community, private and institutional venues, including [[Herning Museum of Contemporary Art|Herning Kunstmuseum]]; [[Spelman College Museum of Fine Art|Spellman College]]; Wave Hill; Studio Museum; Spedition Bremen; and the corner of Putnam and Malcolm X Blvd, Bedford Stuyvesant, Brooklyn.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.conversationprojectnyc.com/blog/2018/12/21/a-conversation-with-stephanie-dinkins|title=A Conversation With Stephanie Dinkins|website=Conversation Project NYC|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-09}}</ref>
