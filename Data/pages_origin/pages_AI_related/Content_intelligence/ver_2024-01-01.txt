'''Content intelligence''' is a strategy that uses [[artificial intelligence]] systems and [[software]] to process content data into reliable insights about the effectiveness of a businessâ€™ content.

== Working principles of content intelligence software ==

The AI works within a certain framework to edit the behavioral analysis of customers and clients. It should be able to process a large amount of data in order to give the content strategist an idea about:
* Content trends and efficacy<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ama.org/partners/content/Pages/what-is-content-intelligence.aspx|title=What Is Content Intelligence?|website=www.ama.org|language=en-us|access-date=2018-05-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612141915/https://www.ama.org/partners/content/Pages/what-is-content-intelligence.aspx|archive-date=2018-06-12|url-status=dead}}</ref>
* How clients react to the content ([[sentiment analysis]])<ref>{{cite web|url=https://go.forrester.com/blogs/17-03-24-introducing_content_intelligence/|title=Introducing Content Intelligence|website=www.forrester.com|date=24 March 2017 |language=en-us}}</ref>
* The voice and style of the content<ref>{{cite web|url=https://qordoba.com/resources/content-intelligence/|title=An Introduction to Content Intelligence|website=www.qordoba.com|language=en-us}}</ref>
The use of content intelligence is therefore connected to the science of [[big data]] and [[artificial intelligence]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.curata.com/blog/content-intelligence-infographic/|title=Content Intelligence Explained in Five Minutes [Infographic]|website=www.curata.com|date=28 August 2017 |language=en-us}}</ref>

Content intelligence is often viewed as an asset for creating and maintaining high-quality content for targeted audiences.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.curata.com/blog/content-intelligence/|title=Content Intelligence: The New Frontier of Content Marketing Technology|website=www.curata.com|date=4 May 2017 |language=en-us}}</ref>

Some ways for companies to achieve content intelligence include implementing or integrating AI into their business' [[content management system]], [[customer relationship management]], or [[digital asset management]] technologies.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.contentintelligence.net/en/ci/content-intelligence-supports-digital-transformation|title=Where Content Intelligence is positioned in your company|website=www.contentintelligence.net|language=en-us}}</ref> A semantic engine can also be a part of content intelligence software to automatically classify content according to topic or the tags a platform assigns.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thron.com/en/blog/thron-content-intelligence|title=Content Intelligence: Big Data According to Thron|website=www.thron.com|language=en-us}}</ref>

Content intelligence software is often sought by marketers, content strategists, [[user experience design|UX]] writers, and product managers.
