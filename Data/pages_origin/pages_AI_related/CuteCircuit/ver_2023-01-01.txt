'''CuteCircuit''' ({{IPAc-en|ˌ|k|j|uː|t|ˈ|s|ɜːr|k|ɪ|t}} {{Respell|KYOOT|SUR|kit}}<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=slVXG79clMA|title=Innovation and Wearable Technology Fashion - CuteCircuit|work=YouTube|date=September 4, 2020|accessdate=2 July 2022}}</ref>) is a fashion company based in London founded in 2004 by Ryan Genz and Francesca Rosella. CuteCircuit designs [[wearable technology]] and interactive fashion.<ref>{{Cite web |url= http://thecreatorsproject.com/blog/designing-tech-couture-for-katy-perry-a-qa-with-cutecircuit |title= A Q&A With CuteCircuit |access-date= 26 April 2012 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20121010233814/http://thecreatorsproject.com/blog/designing-tech-couture-for-katy-perry-a-qa-with-cutecircuit |archive-date= 10 October 2012 |url-status= dead }}</ref>

All CuteCircuit garments are designed by Francesca Rosella and Ryan Genz.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://interactionivrea.org/en/people/alumni/2003/index.asp|title=Interaction Design Institute Ivrea}}</ref>

CuteCircuit was the first fashion company offering smart textile-based garments that create an emotional experience for their wearers using smart textiles and micro electronics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tedxsilkroad.com/?p=845|title=Pioneers in Wearable Technology}}</ref> With the launch of the first collection in 2004, design critic [[John Thackara]] referred to Francesca Rosella as "The Madonna of wearable computing".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://designobserver.com/feature/new-dark-ages-november-2004/21708|title=Design Observer, Smart Dressers}}</ref>

The transformational creations from CuteCircuit have been cited as being an inspiration and precursor to the work of other avant-garde designers such as the Hussein Chalayan.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.maisondexceptions.com/hussein-chalayan-lurgence-du-mouvement/|title=L'urgence du Mouvement |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171212052849/http://www.maisondexceptions.com/hussein-chalayan-lurgence-du-mouvement/ |archive-date=12 December 2017 |url-status=dead}}</ref> The garments have been worn by celebrities including [[Irina Shayk]], [[Fergie (singer)|Fergie]], [[Katy Perry]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/kittyknowles/2018/03/29/cutecircuits-fantastic-fashion-tech-world-firsts/|title=Meet CuteCircuit: The British Fashion Tech Brand Loved By Fergie And Katy Perry|last=Knowles|first=Kitty|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2020-03-19}}</ref>

==Collections==

===Projects===

'''Kinetic Dress'''<br />The Kinetic Dress designed by CuteCircuit in 2004. It represents an interaction between garment and wearer's activities and mood; it lights up and changes its patterns following the person's movement .<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.we-make-money-not-art.com/archives/2004/11/cute-circuit-an.php|title=Kinetic and Camaleon Garments}}</ref>

'''Hug Shirt'''<br />The Hug Shirt, a T-shirt that recreates the sensation of touch, warmth and emotion of a hug from the distant one using Bluetooth and sensors technology.

The Hug Shirt was awarded as one of the Best Inventions of The Year by ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine in 2006<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,1939342_1939424_1939709,00.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100501160901/http://www.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,1939342_1939424_1939709,00.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=1 May 2010|title=Best Inventions of 2006 | magazine=[[Time (magazine)|Time]] |date=13 November 2006}}</ref> and also awarded with the First Prize at Ciberart Conference in [[Bilbao]], Spain.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ciberartfestival.net/premiados.pdf|title=Premiados Ciberart|access-date=2012-04-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090105174329/http://www.ciberartfestival.net/premiados.pdf|archive-date=2009-01-05|url-status=dead}}</ref>

'''M Dress'''<br />In 2008 CuteCircuit designed the M Dress that accepts a standard SIM card and allows to make and receive calls anytime, everywhere, without having to carry a cellular phone.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/technology/news/7972656/Little-black-dress-thats-also-a-phone.html|title=The Telegraph"Little black dress that's also a phone" | work=The Daily Telegraph|first=Heidi|last=Blake|date=31 August 2010|location=London}}</ref>

'''Galaxy Dress'''<br />It is the world's largest wearable LED display (24,000 full colour LEDs). The Galaxy Dress is the center piece of the "Fast Forward: Inventing the Future" exhibit at the Museum of Science and Industry in Chicago.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wired.com/gadgetlab/2009/11/led-dress/|title=WIRED"Designer Duo Create Dress With 24,000 LEDs" | magazine=Wired|first=Priya|last=Ganapati|date=12 November 2009}}</ref>

'''TshirtOS'''<br />Designed by CuteCircuit in partnership with Ballantine's, tshirtOS is the world's first wearable, sharable, programmable T-shirt, that can be programmed by an iOS app to show images and texts, play music, take photos and share them with everybody.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.ecouterre.com/meet-tshirtos-a-programmable-t-shirt-that-displays-your-tweets/|title=Ecouterre - Wearable Technology |date=13 August 2012}}</ref>

CuteCircuit designs dresses and costumes for international artist special performances or tours. Such as, [[Katy Perry]]'s catsuit for her performance in American Idol,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://createdigitalmotion.com/2011/04/wearables-cutecircuit-turns-katy-perry-into-interactive-digital-scenography-fashion-visualists/|title=Wearables: CuteCircuit Turns Katy Perry into Interactive Digital Scenography; Fashion Visualists}}</ref> U2 leather jackets for their [[U2 360 Tour]], Laura Pausini four and a half meters skirt for her [[Inedito World Tour]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.talk2myshirt.com/blog/archives/5728|title=The world's longest illuminated skirt|access-date=2012-04-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151117020958/http://www.talk2myshirt.com/blog/archives/5728|archive-date=2015-11-17|url-status=dead}}</ref> or Azerbaijani representative for [[Eurovision Song Contest 2010]] [[Safura Alizadeh]] dress.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.talk2myshirt.com/blog/archives/4300|title=Safura|access-date=2012-04-26|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151117015736/http://www.talk2myshirt.com/blog/archives/4300|archive-date=2015-11-17|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Prêt-à-Porter and Haute Couture===
CuteCircuit product line is formed by the [[Prêt-à-Porter]] Collection, the [[Haute Couture]] Collection and special tailored pieces for private customers and celebrities.

The Prêt-à-Porter Collection includes fashionable pieces made of laser cut reflective materials, 3-D digital print and smart textiles. They have successfully redesigned their iconic haute couture pieces for the prêt-à-porter market; for example, the K Dress inspired by [[Katy Perry]]'s dress for [[Met Gala]] 2010<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/spy/celebrity-photos/2010/05/04/costume-institute-gala-2010/gallery#/image/1|title=Vogue: "See who shined on the red carpet at the Costume Institute Gala"}}</ref> was the first interactive fashion garment to be featured in [[Selfridges]] womenswear department.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.graziadaily.co.uk/fashion/archive/2010/09/02/yay-selfridges-are-selling-katy-perry-light-up-dresses.htm|title=YAY! Selfridges Are Selling Katy Perry Light Up Dresses!}}</ref>

More recently CuteCircuit has launched the World's First Couture Twitter Dress, an evening dress worn for the first time by [[Nicole Scherzinger]] which is able to receive and show [[Twitter|tweets]] sent in real time by fans.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/11/02/twitter-dress-nicole-scherzinger-photos_n_2064299.html|work=The Huffington Post |title=Twitter Dress | first=Ellie|last=Krupnick|date=2 November 2012}}</ref>
