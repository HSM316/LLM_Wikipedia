'''Multi-objective reinforcement learning''' (MORL) is a form of [[reinforcement learning]] concerned with conflicting alternatives. It is distinct from [[multi-objective optimization]] in that it is concerned with agents acting in environments.<ref>{{cite book |title=Multiple Attribute Decision Making: Methods and Applications |edition=1st |first=Gwo-Hshiung |last=Tzeng |first2=Jih-Jeng |last2=Huang |date=2011 |isbn=9781439861578}}</ref>
