'''Evergage''' was a [[cloud-based]] [[software]] that allowed users to collect, analyze, and respond to user behavior on their websites and web applications in real-time.<ref name="WebInno">{{Cite news |title=WebInnovators: Apptegic |first=David |last=Beisel |website=Webinno |url=http://webinnovatorsgroup.com/2012/06/22/webinnovators-apptegic |date=June 22, 2012 |accessdate=January 4, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130419043917/http://webinnovatorsgroup.com/2012/06/22/webinnovators-apptegic |archive-date=April 19, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2020, Evergage was acquired by [[Salesforce]]<ref>{{Cite press release|url=https://www.evergage.com/blog/evergage-has-joined-the-salesforce-family/|title=Evergage Has Joined the Salesforce Family |last=Wirth |first=Karl |date=2020-02-03|publisher=Evergage |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20200204100802/https://www.evergage.com/blog/evergage-has-joined-the-salesforce-family/ |archivedate=4 February 2020|access-date=3 May 2023}}<br>- {{Cite news |first=Dom |last=Nicastro |date=7 February 2020|url=https://www.cmswire.com/customer-experience/salesforce-acquires-evergage-cdp-industry-reached-1b-in-2019-and-more-cx-news/|title=Salesforce Acquires Evergage, CDP Industry Reached $1B in 2019 and More CX News|website=CMSWire |access-date=3 May 2023}}</ref> and was rebranded as Interaction Studio.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://help.salesforce.com/s/articleView?id=sf.mc_isev_interaction_studio.htm&type=5|title=Interaction Studio (Formerly Evergage)}}{{dead link|date=May 2023}}</ref> The company positions itself as a "real-time personalization and customer data platform (CDP)".<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://cop.evidon.com/companies/evergage |title=Evergage |publisher=Evidon |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20200204170439/https://cop.evidon.com/companies/evergage |archivedate=4 February 2020 |access-date=3 May 2023}}</ref>

The company was founded as Apptegic in 2010 by Karl Wirth and Greg Hinkle, who met while working at [[Red Hat]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Apptegic, deciphering how customers interact with Web-based apps, attracts angel funding from Netezza founder Jit Saxena |first=Scott |last=Kirsner |url=http://www.boston.com/business/technology/innoeco/2011/06/apptegic_deciphering_how_custo.html |date=June 9, 2011 |work=The Boston Globe |accessdate=3 May 2023}}</ref> In May 2012, Evergage was a finalist in the ''[[TechCrunch]]'' Disrupt Startup Battlefield and, shortly thereafter, launched its cloud-based service.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Apptegic Uses Big Data Analysis To Help Companies Retain And Upsell Their Customers |first=Ryan |last=Lawler |url=https://techcrunch.com/2012/05/22/apptegic/ |date=May 22, 2012 |accessdate=December 11, 2012}}<br>- {{Cite news |title=Behind the Web Hosting Decision of a Boston Startup: Apptegic |first=Nicole |last=Henderson |website=Web Host Industry Review |url=http://www.thewhir.com/web-hosting-news/behind-the-web-hosting-decision-of-a-boston-startup-apptegic |date=June 29, 2012 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120704191756/http://www.thewhir.com/web-hosting-news/behind-the-web-hosting-decision-of-a-boston-startup-apptegic |archivedate=4 July 2012 |accessdate=3 May 2023}}</ref> In August 2013, the company changed its name to Evergage.<ref>{{cite news|last=Brooke|first=Eliza|title=Evergage, The Disrupt Finalist Formerly Known As Apptegic, Launches A Freemium B2B Service For Improving Website Click-Throughs|url=https://techcrunch.com/2013/08/08/evergage-the-disrupt-finalist-formerly-known-as-apptegic-launches-a-freemium-b2b-service-for-improving-website-click-throughs/ |website=TechCrunch|date=August 8, 2013}}</ref>

In 2015, Evergage expanded its capabilities to include A/B and multivariate testing,<ref>{{cite news |first=Glenn |last=Taylor |title=Evergage Launches Multivariate Testing Functionalities |url=http://www.retailtouchpoints.com/features/solution-spotlight/evergage-launches-multivariate-testing-functionalities |publisher=Retail Touchpoints |date=December 8, 2015}}</ref> as well as automated product and content recommendations driven by [[machine learning]]. The platform also added personalization support for mobile apps<ref>{{cite news|title=New 'Evergage For Mobile Apps' Solution Automatically Captures Mobile App User Behavioral Data For Omnichannel Personalization|url=http://www.innovativeretailtechnologies.com/doc/new-evergage-data-for-omnichannel-personalization-0001|publisher=Innovative Retail Technologies|date=September 23, 2015 |url-access=subscription}}</ref> and, in 2016, open-time email personalization,<ref>{{cite news |last=Anderson |first=Steve|title=Evergage for Email brings Personalization, Relevance to Email Marketing|url=http://www.madmarketer.com/topics/news/articles/421508-evergage-email-brings-personalization-relevance-email-marketing.htm |website=Mad Marketer|date=May 25, 2016 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160527032447/http://www.madmarketer.com/topics/news/articles/421508-evergage-email-brings-personalization-relevance-email-marketing.htm |archivedate=27 May 2016 |accessdate=3 May 2023}}</ref> and triggered email in 2017.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.destinationcrm.com/Articles/ReadArticle.aspx?ArticleID=120507 |title=Evergage Launches Personalized Triggered Email Capabilities |website=Destination CRM  |date=14 September 2017 |access-date=3 May 2023}}</ref> Evergage acquired the e-commerce and email personalization provider MyBuys in 2018.{{cn|date=November 2021}}
