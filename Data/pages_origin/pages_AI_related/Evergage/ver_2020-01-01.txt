'''Evergage''' is a [[cloud-based]] [[software]] that allows users to collect, analyze, and respond to user behavior on their websites and web applications in real-time.<ref name="WebInno">{{Cite news |title=WebInnovators: Apptegic |first=David |last=Beisel |url=http://webinnovatorsgroup.com/2012/06/22/webinnovators-apptegic |date=June 22, 2012 |accessdate=January 4, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130419043917/http://webinnovatorsgroup.com/2012/06/22/webinnovators-apptegic |archive-date=April 19, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

The company was founded as Apptegic in 2010 by Karl Wirth and Greg Hinkle, who met while working at [[Red Hat]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=Apptegic, deciphering how customers interact with Web-based apps, attracts angel funding from Netezza founder Jit Saxena |first=Scott |last=Kirsner |url=http://www.boston.com/business/technology/innoeco/2011/06/apptegic_deciphering_how_custo.html |date=June 9, 2011 |accessdate=December 11, 2012 |work=The Boston Globe}}</ref> In May 2012, Evergage was a finalist in the [[TechCrunch]] Disrupt Startup Battlefield and shortly thereafter launched its cloud-based service.<ref name="techcrunch">{{Cite news |title=Apptegic Uses Big Data Analysis To Help Companies Retain And Upsell Their Customers |first=Ryan |last=Lawler |url=https://techcrunch.com/2012/05/22/apptegic/ |date=May 22, 2012 |accessdate=December 11, 2012}}</ref><ref name="Web Host Industry Review">{{Cite news |title=Behind the Web Hosting Decision of a Boston Startup: Apptegic |first=Nicole |last=Henderson |url=http://www.thewhir.com/web-hosting-news/behind-the-web-hosting-decision-of-a-boston-startup-apptegic |date=June 29, 2012 |accessdate=December 26, 2012}}</ref><ref name="Web Host Industry Review"/>   In August, 2013, the company changed its name to Evergage.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Brooke|first1=Eliza|title=Evergage, The Disrupt Finalist Formerly Known As Apptegic, Launches A Freemium B2B Service For Improving Website Click-Throughs|url=https://techcrunch.com/2013/08/08/evergage-the-disrupt-finalist-formerly-known-as-apptegic-launches-a-freemium-b2b-service-for-improving-website-click-throughs/|publisher=TechCrunch|date=August 8, 2013}}</ref>

In 2015 Evergage expanded its capabilities to include A/B and multivariate testing<ref>{{cite news|title=Evergage Launches Multivariate Testing Functionalities|url=http://www.retailtouchpoints.com/features/solution-spotlight/evergage-launches-multivariate-testing-functionalities|publisher=Retail Touchpoints|date=December 8, 2015}}</ref> as well as automated product and content recommendations driven by [[machine learning]][http://www.websitemagazine.com/content/blogs/posts/archive/2015/08/12/getting-personal-with-recommendations.aspx]. The platform also added personalization support for mobile apps<ref>{{cite news|title=New â€˜Evergage For Mobile Apps' Solution Automatically Captures Mobile App User Behavioral Data For Omnichannel Personalization|url=http://www.innovativeretailtechnologies.com/doc/new-evergage-data-for-omnichannel-personalization-0001|publisher=Innovative Retail Technologies|date=September 23, 2015}}</ref> and, in 2016, open-time email.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Anderson|first1=Steve|title=Evergage for Email brings Personalization, Relevance to Email Marketin|url=http://www.madmarketer.com/topics/news/articles/421508-evergage-email-brings-personalization-relevance-email-marketing.htm|publisher=Mad Marketer|date=May 25, 2016}}</ref>

==Funding==
Evergage raised $10 million led by Santa Monica-based Arrowroot Capital in Series C funding in August 2017 and $10 million in Series B funding in January 2016;<ref>{{cite web|title=Evergage|url=https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/evergage#/entity|publisher=TechCrunch|accessdate=January 12, 2016}}</ref> $4M in Series A funding led by G20 Ventures in May, 2014;<ref>{{cite web|title=Evergage|url=https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/evergage#/entity|website=Crunchbase|accessdate=May 1, 2014}}</ref>  and $2M in seed funding in May, 2012 from Point Judith Capital, [[Advanced Technology Ventures]], Jit Saxena, and other angel investors.<ref name="techcrunch" />
