'''Repl.it''' is a San Francisco based start-up and an [[Online integrated development environment|online IDE]] ([[integrated development environment]])<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2018/03/15/repl-it-lets-you-program-in-your-browser/|title=Repl.it lets you program in your browser|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-05}}</ref> supporting 40 programming languages<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.businessinsider.com/replit-online-learn-to-code-2019-1|title=This free online tool makes it so easy to learn how to code, kids are using it to build websites, games, and even apps to help with their math homework|last=Chan|first=Rosalie|date=2019-02-24|website=Business Insider|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref>. Repl.it allows users write code and build apps using a browser, without having to install any software on their devices<ref name=":0" />.

The company has received funding from [[Y Combinator]], [[Andreessen Horowitz]], [[Bloomberg Beta]], and others.<ref name="cnbc">https://www.cnbc.com/2018/10/22/andreessen-horowitz-leads-4point5-million-seed-round-in-replit.html</ref> Its name comes from "[[read–eval–print loop]]." The company has over 500,000 weekly active developers<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://techcrunch.com/2018/03/15/repl-it-lets-you-program-in-your-browser/|title=Repl.it lets you program in your browser|website=TechCrunch|language=en-US|access-date=2019-06-02|date=2018-10-22}}</ref> and over 2 million users.
