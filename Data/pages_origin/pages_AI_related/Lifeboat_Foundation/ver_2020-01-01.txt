The '''Lifeboat Foundation''' is a nonprofit organization in [[Reno, Nevada]], dedicated to the prevention of [[global catastrophic risk]].<ref name="exmachina">{{cite news|last1=Bilton|first1=Nick|title=Ava of ‘Ex Machina’ Is Just Sci-Fi (for Now)|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/05/21/style/ava-of-ex-machina-is-just-sci-fi-for-now.html|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[The New York Times]]|date=May 20, 2015}}</ref><ref name="newsmax">{{cite news|last1=Navrozov|first1=Lev|title=Eric Klien's Lifeboat Foundation|url=http://www.newsmax.com/Pre-2008/Eric-Klien-s-Lifeboat-Foundation/2006/06/29/id/686766/|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Newsmax]]|date=June 29, 2006}}</ref> Technology journalist [[Ashlee Vance]] describes Lifeboat as "a nonprofit that seeks to protect people from some seriously catastrophic technology-related events".<ref name="vance" />

==Organization==
Lifeboat was founded by [[online dating service]] entrepreneur<ref name="bizinsider">{{cite news|last1=Rainey|first1=Sarah|title=This 'King of Love' runs the world's largest dating agency|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/this-king-of-love-runs-the-worlds-largest-dating-agency-2015-2|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Business Insider]]|date=February 11, 2015}}</ref> Eric Klien, who continues to run Lifeboat as president and chairman of the board of directors.<ref name="klienbio">{{cite web|last1=Klien|first1=Eric|title=Eric Klien|url=https://lifeboat.com/ex/bios.eric.klien|website=Lifeboat Foundation|publisher=Lifeboat Foundation|accessdate=5 April 2016}}</ref> Lifeboat is run out of Klien's home in [[Minden, Nevada]], a suburb of [[Reno]]. The organization has raised over $500,000 in total donations from individuals and corporate [[matching funds]] programs, most of which went to "supporting conferences and publishing papers".<ref name="bloomberg">{{cite news|last1=Vance|first1=Ashlee|title=Guardians of the Apocalypse|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2011-12-15/guardians-of-the-apocalypse|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Bloomberg Businessweek]]|date=December 15, 2011}}</ref> Writer and advisory board member [[Sonia Arrison]] describes the group as "basically a Web site that raises money for various things".<ref name="vance" />

In 2007, the Lifeboat Foundation absorbed an organization called the "Alliance to Rescue Civilization", which aimed to establish a disaster-proof record of [[human civilization]] on the Moon.<ref name="wsj">{{cite news|last1=Burrows|first1=William E.|title=Colonize the Moon|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB117038898506395928|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|date=February 2, 2007}}</ref>

Lifeboat has tried to raise more money by accepting donations in [[Bitcoin]], a [[cryptocurrency]]. According to [[Fast Company (magazine)|Fast Company]], Lifeboat raised $72,000 in Bitcoin donations and pledges, and sought to use Bitcoin to protect itself against events such as the [[2012–13 Cypriot financial crisis]].<ref name="fastcompany">{{cite news|last1=Ungerleider|first1=Neal|title=Funding A Non-Profit With... Bitcoins?|url=http://www.fastcompany.com/3007738/tech-forecast/funding-non-profit-bitcoins|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Fast Company (magazine)|Fast Company]]|date=April 2, 2013}}</ref> Lifeboat aimed to use some of the funds raised to fix Bitcoin-related bugs.<ref name="forbes">{{cite news|last1=Hicks|first1=Jennifer|title=Safello Enables Charitable Donations With Bitcoins|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/jenniferhicks/2014/08/17/safello-enables-charitable-donations-with-bitcoins/#b78ee8d37513|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Forbes Magazine]]|date=August 17, 2014}}</ref>

==Activities==
According to [[Fast Company (magazine)|Fast Company]], Lifeboat runs a number of "programs" to protect Earth against threats such as an [[asteroid impact]], [[grey goo]] from [[molecular nanotechnology]], and unfriendly [[artificial general intelligence]].<ref name="wolfram" /> Lifeboat maintains a list of "dozens and dozens" of catastrophic threats - including the eventual burnout of the [[Sun]] - divided into four main categories of "calamities", "collapse", "dominium", and "betrayal".<ref name="salon">{{cite news|last1=Brin|first1=David|title=How will the world end?|url=http://www.salon.com/2012/06/17/how_will_the_world_end/|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Salon (website)|Salon]]|date=June 17, 2012}}</ref> Journalist Ashlee Vance notes that it's "unclear how far along any of these projects is".<ref name="bloomberg" />

The Lifeboat Foundation also publishes books, such as ''Visions of the Future'',<ref name="hplusmag">{{cite news|last1=Bentham|first1=Harry J.|title=Book Review: Visions of the Future (2015)|url=http://hplusmagazine.com/2016/03/31/book-review-visions-future-2015/|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[H+ Magazine]]|date=March 31, 2016}}</ref> an [[anthology]] of futurist and [[science fiction]] writing reviewed in the ''[[Financial Times]]''.<ref name="fintimes">{{cite news|last1=Cave|first1=Stephen|title=Is predicting the future futile or necessary?|url=http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/2/5815c14a-b2e7-11e5-b147-e5e5bba42e51.html#axzz44wHF23PB|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Financial Times]]|date=January 8, 2016}}</ref>

==Advisory boards==
The Lifeboat Foundation is notable for its extremely large set of [[advisory board]]s, containing thousands of scientists, businessmen, and other individuals across dozens of separate disciplines.<ref name="advisory">{{cite web|title=Lifeboat Foundation Advisory Boards|url=https://lifeboat.com/ex/boards|website=Lifeboat Foundation|publisher=Lifeboat Foundation|accessdate=5 April 2016}}</ref> Owing to their size, the Lifeboat boards meet online, rather than in person.<ref name="vance">{{cite news|last1=Vance|first1=Ashlee|title=The Lifeboat Foundation: Battling Asteroids, Nanobots and A.I.|url=http://bits.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/07/20/the-lifeboat-foundation-battling-asteroids-nanobots-and-a-i/|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[The New York Times]]|date=July 20, 2010}}</ref> The boards contain a number of [[Nobel Prize]] winners,<ref name="advisory" /> four MacArthur genius grant fellows,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.macfound.org/fellows/304/|title=Stuart Alan Kauffman  — MacArthur Foundation|website=www.macfound.org|language=en|access-date=2017-05-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.macfound.org/fellows/482/|title=Geerat J. Vermeij  — MacArthur Foundation|website=www.macfound.org|language=en|access-date=2017-05-18}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.macfound.org/fellows/412/|title=Richard M. Stallman  — MacArthur Foundation|website=www.macfound.org|language=en|access-date=2017-05-18}}</ref> including [[Wolfram Mathematica|Mathematica]] founder [[Stephen Wolfram]],<ref name="wolfram">{{cite news|last1=Ungerleider|first1=Neal|title=How Stephen Wolfram Is Preparing For The Singularity|url=http://www.fastcoexist.com/1678899/how-stephen-wolfram-is-preparing-for-the-singularity|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Fast Company (magazine)|Fast Company]]|date=November 29, 2011}}</ref> anti-Islam political activist [[Pamela Geller]],<ref name="beliefnet">{{cite news|last1=Bentham|first1=Harry J.|title=Islamophobia: does Pamela Geller injure the Lifeboat Foundation’s credibility?|url=http://www.beliefnet.com/columnists/lordre/2015/03/islamophobia-does-pamela-geller-injure-the-lifeboat-foundations-credibility.html|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Beliefnet]]|date=March 12, 2015}}</ref> and formerly hosted two Russian secret agents identified in 2010 as part of the [[Illegals Program]].<ref name="bbcspies">{{cite news|title=Profile: Russian agents' all-American lives|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/10459664|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[BBC News]]|date=July 8, 2010}}</ref>
