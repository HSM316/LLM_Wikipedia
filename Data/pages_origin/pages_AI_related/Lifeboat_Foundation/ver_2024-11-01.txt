The '''Lifeboat Foundation''' is a nonprofit organization <ref>https://apps.irs.gov/app/eos/details/</ref> based in [[Gardnerville, Nevada]], dedicated to the prevention of [[global catastrophic risk]].<ref name="exmachina">{{cite news|last1=Bilton|first1=Nick|title=Ava of 'Ex Machina' Is Just Sci-Fi (for Now)|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/05/21/style/ava-of-ex-machina-is-just-sci-fi-for-now.html|accessdate=5 April 2016|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=May 20, 2015}}</ref> Technology journalist [[Ashlee Vance]] describes Lifeboat as "a nonprofit that seeks to protect people from some seriously catastrophic technology-related events".<ref name="vance">{{cite news|last1=Vance|first1=Ashlee|author-link=Ashlee Vance|date=July 20, 2010|title=The Lifeboat Foundation: Battling Asteroids, Nanobots and A.I.|work=[[The New York Times]]|url=http://bits.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/07/20/the-lifeboat-foundation-battling-asteroids-nanobots-and-a-i/|accessdate=5 April 2016}}</ref> Prominent scholars from Lifeboat Foundation's Advisory Board includes 1986 [[List of Nobel laureates|Nobel Laureate]] in Literature [[Wole Soyinka]], 1993 [[List of Nobel laureates|Nobel Laureate]] in Medicine [[Richard J. Roberts]], 2002 [[Nobel Prize|Nobel Laureate]] in Economic Sciences [[Daniel Kahneman]], and 2007 [[List of Nobel laureates|Nobel Laureate]] in Economic Sciences [[Eric Maskin]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Lifeboat Foundation Advisory Boards |url=https://lifeboat.com/ex/boards |access-date=2023-02-07 |website=lifeboat.com}}</ref>

==Organization==
Lifeboat was founded by [[online dating service]] entrepreneur<ref name="bizinsider">{{cite news|last1=Rainey|first1=Sarah|title=This 'King of Love' runs the world's largest dating agency|url=http://www.businessinsider.com/this-king-of-love-runs-the-worlds-largest-dating-agency-2015-2|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Business Insider]]|date=February 11, 2015}}</ref> Eric Klien, who continues to run Lifeboat as president and chairman of the board of directors.<ref name="klienbio">{{cite web|last1=Klien|first1=Eric|title=Eric Klien|url=https://lifeboat.com/ex/bios.eric.klien|website=Lifeboat Foundation|publisher=Lifeboat Foundation|accessdate=5 April 2016}}</ref> The organization has raised over $500,000 in total donations from individuals and corporate [[matching funds]] programs, most of which went to "supporting conferences and publishing papers".<ref name="bloomberg">{{cite news|last1=Vance|first1=Ashlee|title=Guardians of the Apocalypse|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2011-12-15/guardians-of-the-apocalypse|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Bloomberg Businessweek]]|date=December 15, 2011}}</ref> Writer and advisory board member [[Sonia Arrison]] describes the group as "basically a Website, that raises money for various things".<ref name="vance" />

In 2007, the Lifeboat Foundation absorbed an organization called the "[[Alliance to Rescue Civilization]]", which aimed to establish a disaster-proof record of [[human civilization]] on the Moon.<ref name="wsj">{{cite news|last1=Burrows|first1=William E.|title=Colonize the Moon|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB117038898506395928|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|date=February 2, 2007}}</ref>

Lifeboat has tried to raise more money by accepting donations in [[Bitcoin]], a [[cryptocurrency]]. According to [[Fast Company (magazine)|Fast Company]], Lifeboat raised $72,000 in Bitcoin donations and pledges, and sought to use Bitcoin to protect itself against events such as the [[2012–13 Cypriot financial crisis]].<ref name="fastcompany">{{cite news|last1=Ungerleider|first1=Neal|title=Funding A Non-Profit With... Bitcoins?|url=http://www.fastcompany.com/3007738/tech-forecast/funding-non-profit-bitcoins|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Fast Company (magazine)|Fast Company]]|date=April 2, 2013}}</ref>

The Board of Directors includes Eric Klien, Carl Martinez, [[Philippe van Nedervelde]], Chris K. Haley, Sergio Tarrero.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://lifeboat.com/ex/directors |title=Board of Directors |author=<!--Not stated--> |date= |website= |publisher=Lifeboat Foundation |access-date=May 21, 2020 |quote=}}</ref>

==Activities==
According to [[Fast Company (magazine)|Fast Company]], Lifeboat runs a number of "programs" to protect Earth against threats such as an [[asteroid impact]], [[grey goo]] from [[molecular nanotechnology]], and unfriendly [[artificial general intelligence]].<ref name="wolfram">{{cite news|last1=Ungerleider|first1=Neal|date=November 29, 2011|title=How Stephen Wolfram Is Preparing For The Singularity|publisher=[[Fast Company (magazine)|Fast Company]]|url=http://www.fastcoexist.com/1678899/how-stephen-wolfram-is-preparing-for-the-singularity|accessdate=5 April 2016}}</ref> Lifeboat maintains a list of "dozens and dozens" of catastrophic threats - including the eventual burnout of the [[Sun]] — divided into four main categories of "calamities", "collapse", "dominium", and "betrayal".<ref name="salon">{{cite news|last1=Brin|first1=David|title=How will the world end?|url=http://www.salon.com/2012/06/17/how_will_the_world_end/|accessdate=5 April 2016|work=[[Salon (website)|Salon]]|date=June 17, 2012}}</ref> Journalist Ashlee Vance notes that it's "unclear how far along any of these projects is".<ref name="bloomberg" />

The Lifeboat Foundation also publishes books, such as ''Visions of the Future'',<ref name="hplusmag">{{cite news|last1=Bentham|first1=Harry J.|title=Book Review: Visions of the Future (2015)|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210318113559/http://hplusmagazine.com/2016/03/31/book-review-visions-future-2015/|accessdate=9 October 2024|publisher=[[H+ Magazine]]|date=March 31, 2016}}</ref> an [[anthology]] of futurist and [[science fiction]] writing reviewed in the ''[[Financial Times]]''.<ref name="fintimes">{{cite news|last1=Cave|first1=Stephen|title=Is predicting the future futile or necessary?|url=http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/2/5815c14a-b2e7-11e5-b147-e5e5bba42e51.html#axzz44wHF23PB|accessdate=5 April 2016|publisher=[[Financial Times]]|date=January 8, 2016}}</ref>
