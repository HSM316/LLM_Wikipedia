'''CloudSight, Inc.''' is a [[Los Angeles]], CA-based technology company that specializes in [[image captioning]], and understanding.

==History==

CloudSight was founded in 2012 by Dominik Mazur and Bradford Folkens. It was previously known as Image Searcher, Inc. and then [[CamFind]], Inc., respectively. In 2016, the company was officially rebranded as CloudSight, Inc.<ref>{{cite web|title=ImageSearcher Inc.|url=https://relationshipscience.com/organization/imagesearcher-inc-149488082|website=relationshipscience.com|access-date=2021-11-18}}</ref>

As of August 2022, CloudSight has 15+ granted patents for its technology<ref>{{cite web|last1=Dove|first1=Jackie|title=CamFind Launches CloudSight API to Advance Visual Search|url=https://thenextweb.com/creativity/2015/02/24/camfind-launches-cloudsight-api-for-multiplatform-visual-search-functionality/|website=The Next Web|access-date=21 September 2017|date=24 February 2015}}</ref> and has recognized over 1 billion images.

== Products ==

=== TapTapSee ===

On October 11, 2012, CloudSight released its first [[mobile application]] into the [[App Store (iOS)|AppStore]], TapTapSee.<ref>{{cite web|title=Visual Search App CamFind Passes 1M Downloads, Makes Its API Public|url=https://techcrunch.com/2013/10/31/camfind/|website=techcrunch.com|date=31 October 2013 |access-date=2021-11-18}}</ref>

TapTapSee is a [[mobile camera]] application designed specifically for  [[Blindness|blind]] and [[visually impaired]] iOS and Android users.

The application utilizes the device's camera and [[VoiceOver]] functions to photograph objects and identify them out loud for the user.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sanders|first1=James|title=Virtual reality targeted at the visually-impaired|url=http://vator.tv/news/2017-08-23-virtual-reality-targeted-to-the-visually-impaired|website=Vator|access-date=21 September 2017|date=23 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Morlin-Yron|first1=Sophie|title=The app lending eyes to the blind|date=24 November 2016 |url=http://edition.cnn.com/2016/11/24/africa/be-specular-app-helps-the-blind/index.html|publisher=CNN.com|access-date=21 September 2017}}</ref>

TapTapSee was the 2014 recipient of the Access Award by the [[American Foundation for the Blind]].<ref>{{cite web|title=American Foundation for the Blind Announces 2014 Access Award Winners|url=http://www.afb.org/info/about-us/press-room/american-foundation-for-the-blind-announces-2014-access-award-winners/125|website=AFB|access-date=21 September 2017|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170323230832/http://www.afb.org/info/about-us/press-room/american-foundation-for-the-blind-announces-2014-access-award-winners/125|archive-date=23 March 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> In March 2013, TapTapSee was named App of the Month by the [[Royal National Institute for the Blind]]. At the end of 2013, TapTapSee was elected into the AppleVis iOS Hall of Fame.<ref>{{cite web|title=Seeing eye-phone? App helps blind know what they're looking|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/seeing-eye-phone-app-helps-blind-know-what-theyre-looking-at/|work=CBS News|date=13 March 2014 |access-date=21 September 2017}}</ref>

=== CamFind ===
On April 7, 2013, CloudSight released its second mobile application into the [[Apple Applications Store|AppStore]], [[CamFind]]. The mobile application surpassed 1,000,000 downloads within the first seven months after its release into the [[Apple App Store|Apple AppStore]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Shu|first1=Catherine|title=Visual Search App CamFind Passes 1M Downloads, Makes Its API Public|url=https://techcrunch.com/2013/10/31/camfind/|website=TechCrunch|date=31 October 2013 |access-date=21 September 2017|language=en}}</ref>

[[CamFind]] is a [[visual search engine]] application that utilizes image recognition to photograph, identify, and provide information on any object, at any angle. Its image recognition capabilities are powered by CloudSight API.<ref>{{cite web|title=Visual Search App CamFind Passes 1M Downloads, Makes Its API Public|url=https://techcrunch.com/2013/10/31/camfind/|website=techcrunch.com|date=31 October 2013 |access-date=2021-11-18}}</ref>

[[CamFind]] is available in the [[Apple Applications Store|Apple AppStore]] and [[Google Play Store]] where it received 11,000,000+ downloads as of 2022 combined between Apple Store and Google Play Store. In February 2015, [[CamFind]] was released on [[Google Glass]] via MyGlass.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Takahashi|first1=Dean|title=CamFind app brings accurate visual search to Google Glass|url=https://venturebeat.com/2014/09/24/camfind-app-brings-accurate-visual-search-to-google-glass-exclusive/|website=VentureBeat|date=24 September 2014|access-date=21 September 2017}}</ref>

In April 2015, CloudSight evolved [[CamFind]] a step further by releasing [[social network]] capabilities within the application. The app now features the ability for users to share the items they identify, as well as see the items that others are identifying with [[CamFind]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Dove|first1=Jackie|title=CamFind Mobile Visual Search App Now Has a Social Network|url=https://thenextweb.com/creativity/2015/04/16/camfind-visual-search-app-for-iphone-and-android-now-features-a-social-network/|newspaper=TNW &#124; Creativity|access-date=21 September 2017|date=16 April 2015}}</ref>

=== CloudSight API ===

In September 2013, CloudSight released its CloudSight API to the general public.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Wagner|first1=Janet|title=Developers of CamFind App Release Image Recognition API to General Public|url=https://www.programmableweb.com/news/developers-camfind-app-release-image-recognition-api-to-general-public/brief/2013/09/18|website=ProgrammableWeb|date=18 September 2013|access-date=21 September 2017|language=en}}</ref>

"The CloudSight API employs [[deep learning]], a technology that simulates the [[human brain]], 'learning' from its mistakes over time, and is the same technology powering [[CamFind]]."<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sawers|first1=Paul|title=CamFind raises $5M and launches its image-recognition API for any developer to use|url=https://venturebeat.com/2015/02/24/camfind-raises-5m-and-launches-its-image-recognition-api-for-any-developer-to-use/|website=VentureBeat|date=24 February 2015|access-date=21 September 2017}}</ref>
