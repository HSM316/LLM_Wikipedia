'''Harold Cohen''' (1 May 1928 – 27 April 2016)<ref>[http://elcamencinitas.tributes.com/dignitymemorial/obituary/Harold-Cohen-103521554 Obituary tribute Harold Cohen]</ref> was a British-born artist who was noted as the creator of [[AARON]], a computer program designed to produce art autonomously. His work in the intersection of [[computer]] [[artificial intelligence]] and art attracted a great deal of attention, leading to exhibitions at many museums, including the [[Tate Gallery]] in London, and acquisitions by many others.<ref>[http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/aaron/biography/index.html Profile on webpage about AARON]</ref>

==Early life==
Cohen was born in London, the son of [[Poles|Polish]]-[[Russians|Russian]] parents, and was educated there at the [[Slade School of Fine Art]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/obituaries/2016/05/22/harold-cohen-artist--obituary/ |title=Harold Cohen, artist – obituary |publisher=Telegraph.co.uk |date= |accessdate=12 May 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
Cohen moved to the United States as a visiting lecturer at the [[University of California, San Diego]] in 1968. He was later given the rank of professor and stayed at UC San Diego for nearly three decades, part of the time as chairman of the Visual Arts Department. In addition, he served as director of the [[Center for Research in Computing and the Arts]] at UC San Diego from 1992 to 1998.

After his retirement from UCSD,{{When|date=January 2018}} he continued to work on [[AARON]] and produce new artwork in his studio in [[Encinitas, California|Encinitas]], California.

In 2014, Cohen received the [[ACM SIGGRAPH]] Distinguished Artist Award for Lifetime Achievement award.<ref>Archived at [https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211211/_Xbt8lzWxIQ Ghostarchive]{{cbignore}} and the [https://web.archive.org/web/20180119163037/https://www.youtube.com/watch?t=1224&v=_Xbt8lzWxIQ&utm_content=bufferd1d81&utm_medium=social&utm_source=twitter.com&utm_campaign=buffer Wayback Machine]{{cbignore}}: {{cite AV media| url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Xbt8lzWxIQ| title = ACM SIGGRAPH Awards - Harold Cohen, Distinguished Artist Award for Lifetime Achievement | website=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

Early in 2016 Cohen start a new project with AARON called ''Fingerpainting for the 21st Century''. The artist utilised a touch screen to colour and finish artworks. Before AARON's images would be outputted in physical form before Cohen made alterations.<ref>http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/aaron/publications/8Feb2016Fingerpainting-for-the-21st-Century-with-Figures.pdf</ref>

==Family life==
His partner was the prominent Japanese poet [[Hiromi Itō]].

==AARON==
Cohen's work on AARON began in 1968 at the University of California, San Diego.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Cohen|first1=Harold|title=Toward Diaper-Free Autonomy|url=http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/publications/mcasd.doc|website=aaronshome.com|access-date=29 June 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150701130409/http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/publications/mcasd.doc|archive-date=1 July 2015|df=dmy-all}}</ref>
He initially wrote AARON in [[C (programming language)|the C programming language]] but eventually converted to [[Lisp (programming language)|Lisp]], citing that C was "too inflexible, too inexpressive, to deal with something as conceptually complex as color."<ref>{{cite web|last1=Cohen|first1=Harold|title="A Sorcerer's Apprentice" Talk at the Tate Modern|url=http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/publications/tate-final.doc|website=aaronshome.com|access-date=29 June 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150701110404/http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/publications/tate-final.doc|archive-date=1 July 2015|df=dmy-all}}</ref>
