'''Harold Cohen''' (1 May 1928 – 27 April 2016)<ref>[http://elcamencinitas.tributes.com/dignitymemorial/obituary/Harold-Cohen-103521554 Obituary tribute Harold Cohen]</ref> was a British-born artist who was noted as the creator of [[AARON]],<ref>HÉDER, Mihály. AARON. In: Frana, P L; Klein, M J (Eds.) Encyclopedia of Artificial Intelligence: The Past, Present, and Future of AI. Santa Barbara (CA), USA: ABC-CLIO (2021) 378 p. pp. 1-2.</ref> a computer program designed to produce paintings and drawings autonomously. His work in the intersection of [[computer]] [[artificial intelligence]] and painting lead to exhibitions at many museums, including the [[Tate Gallery]] in London.<ref>[http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/aaron/biography/index.html Profile on webpage about AARON]</ref>

==Early life==
Cohen was born in London, the son of [[Polish people|Polish]]-[[Russians|Russian]] Jewish parents, and was educated there at the [[Slade School of Fine Art]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/obituaries/2016/05/22/harold-cohen-artist--obituary/ |title=Harold Cohen, artist – obituary |publisher=Telegraph.co.uk |date= |accessdate=12 May 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
Cohen represented [[Great Britain]] at the [[Venice Biennale|Venice Biennial]] in 1966.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Center for Human Imagination|url=http://imagination.ucsd.edu/_wp/news/harold-cohen-creating-computational-creativity/|access-date=2022-01-15|website=imagination.ucsd.edu}}</ref> Cohen moved to the United States as a visiting lecturer at the [[University of California, San Diego]] in 1968. He was later given the rank of professor and stayed at UC San Diego for nearly three decades; part of the time as chairman of the Visual Arts Department. 

In addition, he served as director of the [[Center for Research in Computing and the Arts]] at UC San Diego from 1992 to 1998.

Cohen taught at UC San Diego from 1968 to 1994.<ref>{{Cite web|title=UC San Diego Visual Arts Exhibition Honors Art and Computing Pioneer Harold Cohen|url=https://ucsdnews.ucsd.edu/pressrelease/uc_san_diego_visual_arts_exhibition_honors_art_and_computing_pioneer_harold|access-date=2022-01-15|website=ucsdnews.ucsd.edu}}</ref> After his retirement from UCSD, he continued to work on [[AARON]] and produce new artwork in his studio in [[Encinitas, California|Encinitas]], California.

In 2014, Cohen received the [[ACM SIGGRAPH]] Distinguished Artist Award for Lifetime Achievement award.<ref>Archived at [https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211211/_Xbt8lzWxIQ Ghostarchive]{{cbignore}} and the [https://web.archive.org/web/20180119163037/https://www.youtube.com/watch?t=1224&v=_Xbt8lzWxIQ&utm_content=bufferd1d81&utm_medium=social&utm_source=twitter.com&utm_campaign=buffer Wayback Machine]{{cbignore}}: {{cite AV media| url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Xbt8lzWxIQ| title = ACM SIGGRAPH Awards - Harold Cohen, Distinguished Artist Award for Lifetime Achievement | website=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref>

Early in 2016, Cohen started a new project with AARON called ''Fingerpainting for the 21st Century''. In this project, Cohen used a touch screen to digitally colour and finish artworks. In previous AARON projects, images would be outputted in physical form before Cohen made alterations.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/aaron/publications/8Feb2016Fingerpainting-for-the-21st-Century-with-Figures.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=16 January 2018 |archive-date=17 January 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180117131216/http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/aaron/publications/8Feb2016Fingerpainting-for-the-21st-Century-with-Figures.pdf |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==AARON==
Cohen's work on AARON began in 1968 at the University of California, San Diego.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Cohen|first1=Harold|title=Toward Diaper-Free Autonomy|url=http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/publications/mcasd.doc|website=aaronshome.com|access-date=29 June 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150701130409/http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/publications/mcasd.doc|archive-date=1 July 2015|df=dmy-all}}</ref>
He initially wrote AARON in [[C (programming language)|the C programming language]] but eventually converted to [[Lisp (programming language)|Lisp]], citing that C was "too inflexible, too inexpressive, to deal with something as conceptually complex as color."<ref>{{cite web|last1=Cohen|first1=Harold|title="A Sorcerer's Apprentice" Talk at the Tate Modern|url=http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/publications/tate-final.doc|website=aaronshome.com|access-date=29 June 2015|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150701110404/http://www.aaronshome.com/aaron/publications/tate-final.doc|archive-date=1 July 2015|df=dmy-all}}</ref>
