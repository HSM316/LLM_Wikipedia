==History==
Amazfit was established in 2015, along with the release of its first smart band.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.wareable.com/fitness-trackers/xiaomi-backed-amazefit-band-is-a-gorgeous-ceramic-tracker-aimed-at-women-1709|title=Amazefit band is a gorgeous ceramic tracker aimed at women|author=James Stables|date=September 18, 2015 |accessdate=|work=Wareable.com}}</ref> In 2016, the brand's first smartwatch was launched.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.firstpost.com/tech/news-analysis/xiaomi-unveils-amazfit-smartwatch-3687679.html|title=Xiaomi unveils AMAZFIT smartwatch|author=|date=Aug 30, 2016|work=[[Firstpost]]}}</ref> In April 2017,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.wareable.com/fitness-trackers/huami-amazfit-health-band-release-date-price-specs-features-4360|title=Amazfit Health Band is bringing ECG tracking to the party|author=Conor Allison|date=April 26, 2017 |accessdate=|work=Wareable.com}}</ref> it launched the Amazfit Health Band,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bgr.in/news/xiaomi-weloop-hey-s3-huami-amazfit-health-band-fitness-trackers-launched-heres-everything-you-need-to-know-457788/|title=WeLoop Hey S3, an Apple Watch-clone, launched on Xiaomi’s MIJIA platform, priced at $78|author=|date=April 28, 2017|accessdate=|work=[[Boy Genius Report|BGR]]}}</ref> with a built-in [[electrocardiography|ECG]] sensor.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.androidheadlines.com/2017/04/huami-amazfit-health-band-with-ip67-certification-announced.html|title=Huami Amazfit Health Band With IP67 Certification Announced|author=Mihai Matei|date=April 25, 2017|accessdate=|work=Android Headlines}}</ref>

A self-developed<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.xinhuanet.com/tech/2019-03/15/c_1124240018.htm|title=Huami shipped 27.5 million units in 2018|author=|date=2019-03-15|accessdate=|work=[[Xinhua News Agency]]}}</ref> [[chipset]] called the "Huangshan-1" <ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.eetimes.com/wearables-firm-invests-8-million-in-risc-v-startup/|title=Wearables Firm Invests $8 Million in RISC-V Startup|author=Nitin Dahad|date=Mar 1, 2019|work=[[EE Times]]}}</ref> was exhibited at [[Mobile World Congress]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://hk.hkcd.com/content_p/2019-03/02/content_70801.html|title=Amazfit debuts at MWC|author=|date=2019-03-02|accessdate=|work=[[Hong Kong Commercial Daily]]}}</ref> and applied to Amazfit devices in 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.firstpost.com/tech/news-analysis/xiaomis-huami-launches-the-amazfit-verge-health-band-1s-and-a-new-soc-in-china-5212251.html|title=Xiaomi's Huami launches the Amazfit Verge, Health Band 1S and a new SoC in China|author=|date=Sep 19, 2018|accessdate=|work=[[Firstpost]]}}</ref>

In April 2020,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thehindubusinessline.com/companies/covid-19-huami-amazfit-collaborates-with-chinese-research-institutes-on-wearable-joint-lab/article31373635.ece|title=Huami Amazfit collaborates with Chinese research institutes on wearable joint lab|author=Hemani Sheth|date=April 18, 2020|accessdate=|work=[[The Hindu Business Line]]}}</ref> its [[parent company]] partnered with a [[laboratory]] led by [[Zhong Nanshan]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.scmp.com/abacus/tech/article/3079351/china-wants-use-fitness-trackers-monitor-coronavirus-survivors|title=China wants to use fitness trackers to monitor coronavirus survivors |author=|date=10 Apr 2020|accessdate=|work=[[South China Morning Post]]}}</ref> to use wearable devices to track [[respiratory diseases]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://techcrunch.com/2020/05/17/fitbits-rival-amazfit-transparent-reusable-mask/|title=Fitbit’s Chinese rival Amazfit mulls a transparent, self-disinfecting mask|author=John Artman|date=May 18, 2020|accessdate=|work=[[TechCrunch]]}}</ref>

In April 2021, Amazfit collaborated with [[Spartan Race|Spartan Race US]] and became the race's annual official partner for 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chinesetoday.com/qiye/keji/20210513/1620881334.html|website=[[International Daily News]]|date=2021-05-13|title=Amazfit partners with Spartan Race US}}</ref>

In early October 2021, the brand unveiled its new GTR 3 and GTS 3 Series, equipped with the Zepp OS - a smartwatch [[operating system|OS]] designed by Zepp Health.<ref>{{cite web|title=Amazfit GTS 3, GTR 3 & GTR 3 Pro watches debut Zepp OS|url=https://www.techadvisor.com/news/wearable-tech/amazfit-gts-3-gtr-3-pro-3809322/|language=|author=Alex Walker-Todd|date=October 11, 2021|access-date=|work=[[Tech Advisor]]}}</ref>

At the middle of October 2021, Amazfit updated its brand identity with the slogan "Up Your Game".<ref>{{cite web|title=Amazfit Refreshes Image, Launches GTR 3 And GTS 3 Smartwatch Lines|url=https://www.twice.com/product/accessories/digitalhealth/amazfit-refreshes-image-launches-gtr-3-and-gts-3-smartwatch-lines|language=|author=Thomas Waller|date=November 8, 2021|access-date=|work=[[Twice (magazine)|Twice]]}}</ref>

In October 2021, it co-operated with designer Christian Cowan<ref>{{cite web|title=Amazfit Taps Christian Cowan for Latest Watch|url=https://wwd.com/accessories-news/accessory-trends/amazfit-taps-christian-cowan-for-latest-watch-1234979735/|language=|author=Thomas Waller|date=October 20, 2021|access-date=|work=[[Women's Wear Daily]]}}</ref> and [[Copenhagen]]-based brand Heliot Emil, which integrated Amazfit's watches into its [[Paris Fashion Week]] shows.<ref>{{cite web|title=How to elevate your workout with a smartwatch|url=https://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/bc/how-to-elevate-your-workout-with-a-smartwatch|language=|author=|date=3 November 2021|access-date=|work=[[British GQ]]}}</ref>
