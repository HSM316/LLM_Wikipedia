'''OptiRTC''' is an American technology company that has developed a [[software as a service]] platform for civil infrastructure. The OptiRTC platform is a cloud-native platform that integrates sensors, forecasts, and environmental contexts to actively control stormwater infrastructure.<ref>[http://www.optirtc.com/ OptiRTC Platform] Retrieved January 02, 2015</ref><ref>[http://www.wateronline.com/doc/innovations-in-cyber-water-0001?platform=hootsuite 5 Innovations in Cyber-Water] Retrieved August 22, 2016</ref> The OptiRTC platform is built on [[Microsoft Azure]] and uses [[internet of things]] technology to predicatively manage distributed water systems.<ref>[http://newsroom.cisco.com/feature/1451749/The-Internet-of-Everything-Meets-Water Internet of Everything Meets Water] Retrieved December 30, 2014</ref>

==History==
In June 2011, the OptiRTC team partnered with [[ioBridge]] to develop smart city tech based on ioBridge's hardware solution{{solution-inline|date=October 2019}} and OptiRTC's platform.<ref>[http://www.water-technology.net/news/news121648.html Geosyntec, ioBridge to Develop Storm Water Management Technology] Retrieved January 03, 2015</ref>

In November 2013, the OptiRTC team was assigned a patent for "Combined water storage and detention system and method of precipitation harvesting and management" that was co-invented by Marcus Quigley.<ref>[http://www.google.com.ar/patents/US8591147 OptiRTC Patent] Retrieved August 24, 2015</ref>

In November 2014, the New York Economic Development Corporation ([[NYCEDC]]) selected Opti technology as the winner of the first Smart City Expo World Congress.<ref>[http://www.elperiodico.com/es/noticias/fira-de-barcelona/bcn-cabeza-inteligencia-urbana-europa-3681691 NYC EDC Selects Opti Technology] Retrieved August 24, 2015</ref>

In December 2014, OptiRTC was formally incorporated as an independent company through a [[spin-out]] from [http://www.geosyntec.com Geosyntec Consultants].<ref>[http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/optirtc-inc-formed-to-scale-intelligent-water-infrastructure-300011127.html OptiRTC Inc Formed] Retrieved December 30, 2014</ref>

In January 2015, the Water Environment Research Foundation (WERF) published a research paper on High Performance Green Infrastructure, which focused primarily on distributed real-time control of stormwater infrastructure.<ref>[http://www.werf.org/a/ka/Search/ResearchProfile.aspx?ReportId=INFR1R11 WERF Report] Retrieved January 11, 2015</ref>

In January 2016, Opti began working with Particle to provide the communications layer for Opti's products.<ref>[http://www.iotjournal.com/articles/view?14863/ How Opti Leverages the IoT to Improve Stormwater Management] Retrieved August 18, 2016</ref>

In October 2018, Opti was commissioner by Albany’s Department of Water and Water Supply to install its underground smart water management system in Washington Park Lake as well as one of Albany’s constructed wetlands.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Olick|first=Diana|date=2018-10-18|title=Cities are using new cloud technology to fight increasingly expensive and catastrophic flooding|url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/10/18/tech-and-real-estate-turn-to-the-cloud-to-protect-cities-from-floods.html|access-date=2021-02-15|website=CNBC|language=en}}</ref>
