'''Aidoc''' is a Tel Aviv, Israel-based company that creates deep learning-based [[artificial intelligence]] (AI) solutions to support [[radiologist]]s.<ref>{{cite web | last=Blum | first=Brian | last2=Leichman | first2=Abigail Klein | title=AI tool helps radiologists clear dangerous data bottleneck | website=Israel21c | date=11 December 2018 | url=http://www.israel21c.org/ai-tool-helps-radiologists-clear-dangerous-data-bottleneck/ }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title=Aidoc releases complete AI package for treatment of stroke | website=NeuroNews International | date=23 September 2019 | url=https://neuronewsinternational.com/aidoc-ai-stroke/ }}</ref><ref name="yahoo">{{cite web | title=Global Diagnostics Australia Incorporates Artificial Intelligence Into Its Radiology Applications | website=Yahoo Finance | date=August 8, 2019 | url=https://finance.yahoo.com/news/global-diagnostics-australia-incorporates-artificial-010000121.html | access-date=November 22, 2019}}</ref>

==History==
Aidoc was founded in 2016 by Elad Walach (CEO),<ref name="yahoo"/> Michael Braginsky (CTO) and Guy Reiner (VP R&D), who had previously specializing in AI technologies for nearly a decade.

The company raised a total of US$ 40 million in funding, with series B round being led by Squarepeg capital.<ref>{{cite web | title=Aidoc, the AI solution for medical imaging analysis, raises $27M Series B – TechCrunch | website=TechCrunch | date=17 April 2019 | url=http://social.techcrunch.com/2019/04/17/aidoc-b/ }}</ref>

In August 2018, Aidoc became the first company in the world to receive clearance for a direct-to-radiology computer-aided traige ([[Computer-aided simple triage|CADt]])<ref>{{cite web | title=FDA OKs World's First AI Solution for Flagging Pulmonary Embolism | website=Medical Product Outsourcing | date=16 May 2019 | url=https://www.mpo-mag.com/contents/view_breaking-news/2019-05-16/fda-oks-worlds-first-ai-solution-for-flagging-pulmonary-embolism/ }}</ref> and gained FDA clearance for its flagging [[pulmonary embolism]]in chest CTs solution.<ref>{{cite web | title=FDA clears Aidoc AI solution for flagging PE in chest CTs | website=dotmed.com | date=May 20, 2019 | url=https://www.dotmed.com/news/story/47290 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title=August 2018 510(k) Clearances | website=U.S. Food and Drug Administration | date=7 September 2018 | url=http://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/510k-clearances/august-2018-510k-clearances}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title=The meaning of regulatory approval for AI | website=AI Med | date=15 May 2019 | url=https://ai-med.io/the-meaning-of-regulatory-approval-for-ai/ }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | title=Aidoc, Zebra Medical Vision announce 510(k) clearances for AI image analysis software | website=MobiHealthNews | date=June 17, 2019 | url=https://www.mobihealthnews.com/content/north-america/aidoc-zebra-medical-vision-announce-510k-clearances-ai-image-analysis-software }}</ref>

It was named one of TIME magazine’s ‘50 Genius companies’ in 2018,<ref>{{cite web | title=Aidoc Is One of TIME's 50 Genius Companies 2018 | website=Time | date=4 October 2018 | url=https://time.com/collection/genius-companies-2018/5412094/aidoc/ }}</ref> and its founders were listed on Israel's<ref>{{cite web | title=Forbes 30 Under 30 | website=Forbes 30 Under 30 | date=17 January 2019 | url=http://lists.forbes.co.il/2019under30/%d7%90%d7%9c%d7%a2%d7%93-%d7%95%d7%95%d7%9c%d7%9a-%d7%92%d7%99%d7%90-%d7%a8%d7%99%d7%99%d7%a0%d7%a8-%d7%95%d7%9e%d7%99%d7%9b%d7%90%d7%9c-%d7%91%d7%a8%d7%92%d7%99%d7%a0%d7%a1%d7%a7%d7%99/ | language=he }}</ref> and Europe’s Forbes 30 under 30 lists.<ref name="Barton 2019">{{cite web | last=Barton | first=Merrilee | title=Aidoc: Elad Walach, Michael Braginsky, & Guy Reiner | website=Forbes | date=14 August 2019 | url=https://www.forbes.com/pictures/5a61e739a7ea431690113339/2018-30u30-europe-science/ }}</ref>

Aidoc won the ''Aunt Minnie’s'' ‘Best New Radiology Software 2019’ award.<ref>{{cite web | last=Casey | first=Brian | last2=Ridley | first2=Erik L. | last3=Yee | first3=Kate Madden | last4=Forrest | first4=Wayne | title=Winners of 20th annual Minnies are radiology's best | website=AuntMinnie.com | date=22 October 2019 | url=https://www.auntminnie.com/index.aspx?sec=nws&sub=rad&pag=dis&ItemID=126854 }}</ref>

Aidoc’s technology analyses medical images directly after the patient is scanned and notifies the radiologist of cases with suspected critical findings, assisting with prioritization of time-sensitive and potentially life-threatening cases in order to expedite patient care.<ref>{{cite web | title=Evaluation of an AI-Based Detection Software for Acute... : Investigative Radiology | website=LWW | date=22 November 2019 | doi=10.1097/RLI.0000000000000509 | url=https://journals.lww.com/investigativeradiology/Fulltext/2019/01000/Evaluation_of_an_AI_Based_Detection_Software_for.8.aspx | access-date=22 November 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | author=P. Ojeda; M. Zawaideh; M. Mossa-Basha; D. Haynor| website=Spie Digital Library|title=The utility of deep learning: evaluation of a convolutional neural network for detection of intracranial bleeds on non-contrast head computed tomography studies| |url=https://www.spiedigitallibrary.org/conference-proceedings-of-spie/10949/109493J/The-utility-of-deep-learning--evaluation-of-a-convolutional/10.1117/12.2513167.short?SSO=1}}</ref> It has been used by the [[University of Rochester Medical Center]],<ref>{{cite web | title=Nuance’s AI Marketplace Delivers AI at Scale with Industry’s First Workflow-Integrated Market for Diagnostic Imaging Algorithms | website=Nuance Communications | date=26 November 2018 | url=https://www.nuance.com/about-us/newsroom/press-releases/2018/Nuances-AI-Marketplace-Delivers-AI-at-Scale-with-Industrys-First-Workflow-Integrated-Market-for-Diagnostic-Imaging-Algorithms.html}}</ref> [[Global Diagnostics]] Australia,<ref>{{cite web | title=Global Diagnostics Australia incorporates artificial intelligence into its radiology applications | website=Global Diagnostics Australia | date=8 August 2019 | url=https://www.globaldiagnostics.com.au/page/about/latest-news/global-diagnostics-australia-incorporates-artificial-intelligence-into-its-radiology-applications/}}</ref> and other medical centers.
