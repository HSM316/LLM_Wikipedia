'''Simegnew Bekele Aynalem''' ({{lang-am|ስመኘው በቀለ አይናለም}}; 13 September 1964 – 26 July 2018) was an [[Ethiopia|Ethiopian]] [[civil engineer]] who served as chief project manager of the [[Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam]] project as well as three other similar dam projects in Ethiopia.<ref name="AJ">{{cite news |title=Ethiopian engineer of controversial renaissance dam found dead |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2018/07/ethiopian-engineer-controversial-renaissance-dam-dead-180726151150589.html |accessdate=26 July 2018 |work=[[Al Jazeera English]] |date=26 July 2018}}</ref> He was considered the "public face" of the dam project.<ref name="marchers">{{cite news |last1=Maasho |first1=Aaron |title=Ethiopian Nile dam manager found shot dead, crowds call for justice |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-ethiopia-dam/ethiopian-nile-dam-manager-found-shot-dead-crowds-call-for-justice-idUSKBN1KG13H |accessdate=27 July 2018 |work=Reuters |date=26 July 2018}}</ref><ref name="dw">{{cite news |title=Ethiopian Grand Renaissance Dam manager Simegnew Bekele found dead |url=https://www.dw.com/en/ethiopian-grand-renaissance-dam-manager-simegnew-bekele-found-dead/a-44842141 |accessdate=27 July 2018 |work=[[Deutsche Welle]] |publisher=Reuters |date=26 July 2018}}</ref>

==Biography==
Simegnew was born in 1964 in the small town of Maksegnit located in [[Begmeder]] Province. He completed a course at the [[Ethiopian Electric Power Corporation]] (EEPCo) technical training center in 1986. He taught at the institution for a while after graduating with a degree in [[Civil engineering|Civil Engineering]] from [[Addis Ababa University]] in 1997.<ref name="Reporter">{{cite news |last1=Endashaw |first1=Dawit |title=A tragic end to a celebrated engineer |url=https://www.thereporterethiopia.com/article/tragic-end-celebrated-engineer |accessdate=29 July 2018 |work=[[The Reporter (Ethiopia)]] |date=28 July 2018}}</ref> He served as deputy project manager for the [[Gilgel Gibe I Dam|Gilgel Gibe I]] and the project manager for the [[Gilgel Gibe II Power Station|Gilgel Gibe II]] dams.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.zehabesha.com/amharic/archives/93213|title=የኢንጂነር ስመኘው በቀለ አሟሟት እያነጋገረ ነው |trans-title=Regarding the death of Engineer Simegnew Bekele|last=Aregay|first=Daniel|date=2018-07-26|work=Zehabesha Amharic|access-date=2018-07-26|language=am-ET}}</ref> In 2011 he was designated as head of the Ethiopian Renaissance Dam Project Office (GERDPO), since the start of its construction in 2011. In 2015 the ''Tigrai Online'' [[e-magazine]] named him as its person of the year.<ref>{{cite news |title=Tigrai Online person of the year 2015 is Engineer Simegnew Bekele |url=http://www.tigraionline.com/articles/tol-person-of-2015.html |accessdate=26 July 2018 |work=Tigrai Online |date=2 January 2016}}</ref>

==Death==
On the morning of 26 July 2018, Simegnew was found shot dead in his [[Toyota Land Cruiser]] in [[Meskel Square]], [[Addis Ababa]]. Speaking at a press conference, Ethiopian Federal Police  Commissioner Zeinu Jemal said that he had been found with a [[Colt guns|Colt gun]] in his right hand and a bullet wound behind his right ear, however foul play has been suspected.<ref name="dw" /><ref name = "borkena">{{Cite web|url=https://www.borkena.com/2018/07/26/engineer-simegnew-bekele-ethiopian-grand-renaissance-dam-project-manager-and-chief-engineer-assassinated/|title=Engineer Simegnew Bekele,Grand Renaissance Dam Project Manager assassinated|website=www.borkena.com|date=26 July 2018|language=en-US|access-date=2018-07-26}}</ref> Simegnew had been scheduled to give a press conference on the progress of the dam later that day, following comments by Prime Minister [[Abiy Ahmed]] that construction could take up to ten more years, far more than previous projections.<ref name = "borkena" /> He was the public face of the dam being built near the [[Ethiopia–Sudan border|country's western border with Sudan]] that, when completed, would be Africa's largest.

Simegnew's death is the second of a high-profile company official at around that time. In May, gunmen ambushed and killed [[Deep Kamara]], the country manager for Nigeria's [[Dangote Cement]], alongside two others in the Oromia region outside Addis Ababa.<ref name="AJ" /><ref name="EastAfro">{{cite news |title=Engineer of Ethiopia's Grand Renaissance Dam found dead |url=https://www.theeastafrican.co.ke/news/africa/Engineer-of-Ethiopia-Grand-Renaissance-Dam-found-dead/4552902-4682384-9d9ugrz/index.html |accessdate=26 July 2018 |work=[[The East African]] |agency=AFP |date=26 July 2018}}</ref> Simegnew's death sparked protests in Addis Ababa, with marchers demanding justice for what they assumed was his assassination.<ref name="VOA">{{cite news |last1=Maasho |first1=Aaron |title=Hundreds in Ethiopia Protest Apparent Killing of Dam Project Manager |url=https://www.voanews.com/a/hundreds-in-ethiopia-protest-apparent-killing-of-dam-project-manager/4502847.html |accessdate=1 August 2018 |work=[[Voice of America]] |publisher=Reuters |date=July 27, 2018}}</ref><ref name="Africanews">{{cite news |last1=Dahir |first1=Abdi Latif |title=Ethiopia's Nile dam engineer died of bullet wound - Police chief |url=http://www.africanews.com/2018/07/26/ethiopia-s-nile-dam-engineer-died-of-bullet-wound-police-chief/ |accessdate=26 July 2018 |work=Africanews |date=26 July 2018}}</ref> Tens of thousands of people gathered in Meskel Square and along [[Menelik II Avenue]] to witness his funeral procession on 29 July.<ref name="CNN">{{cite news |last1=Marsh |first1=Jenni |title=Police fire tear gas at protesters during state funeral for Ethiopia's Grand Dam engineer |url=https://edition.cnn.com/2018/07/29/africa/bekele-funeral-protest-ethiopia/index.html |accessdate=1 August 2018 |work=[[CNN]] |date=29 March 2018}}</ref> Prime Minister [[Abiy Ahmed]] reacted to the news, saying he was "saddened and utterly shocked" by the engineer's death.<ref name="dw" /> On 7 September, police investigation reports suggested that Simegnew committed suicide by self-inflicted gunshot wounds.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.africanews.com/amp/2018/09/07/ethiopia-s-gerd-project-engineer-committed-suicide-police/|title=Africanews {{!}} Ethiopia's GERD project engineer committed suicide - police|website=Africanews|language=en|access-date=2018-09-07}}</ref> Conspiracy theories relating to his death circulated by some skeptics who promote anti-governmental rhetoric, whether he was killed by government agent or [[Tigray People's Liberation Front|TPLF]]'s recruiter.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Ahmed|first=Hadra|date=2018-09-07|title=Death That Spurred Ethiopian Conspiracy Theories Is Ruled a Suicide|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/09/07/world/africa/ethiopia-dam-engineer-suicide.html|access-date=2020-08-30|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>
