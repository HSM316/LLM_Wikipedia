The '''rental vacancy rate''' is an [[economic indicator]] which measures the percentage of [[rental home]]s that are [[wikt:vacant|vacant]].

==Residential vacancies==
In the [[United States]] the [[Census Bureau]] keeps track  of vacancy rates.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.census.gov/housing/hvs/files/qtr212/q212press.pdf |title=Census data |date=July 27, 2012 |website=www.census.gov |format=PDF}}</ref>
In [[Canada]] [[Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation]] measures vacancy rates of 25 [[metropolitan area]]s and for the 75 largest Metropolitan Statistical Areas (MSAs) across the country <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sellmyhouseeasyfast.com/rental-vacancy-rate-whats-likely-to-come-next/|title=Real Estate and Rental Vacancies{{!}}And What's Likely to Come next|date=March 8, 2018}}</ref>and publishes the findings semi-annually in June and December.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cmhc-schl.gc.ca/en/corp/nero/nere/2012/2012-12-13-0815.cfm |title=Archived copy |access-date=2013-07-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130702233129/http://www.cmhc-schl.gc.ca/en/corp/nero/nere/2012/2012-12-13-0815.cfm |archive-date=2013-07-02 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
