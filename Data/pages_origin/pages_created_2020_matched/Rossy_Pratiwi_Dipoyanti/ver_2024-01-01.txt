'''Rossy Pratiwi Dipoyanti Syechbubakar''' or better known as '''Rossy Pratiwi Dipoyanti''' or '''Rossy Syechbubakar''' (born in [[Bandung]] on 28 June 1972) is an Indonesian [[table tennis]] player who collected 13 gold medals, 8 silver medals and 8 bronze medals during her career throughout the year 1987–2001 at the [[Southeast Asian Games]]. In addition, she also managed to collect 7 gold medals, 7 silver medals, and 9 bronze medals during her career throughout the year 1985–2008 at the [[National Sports Week (Indonesia)|National Sports Week]]. The highest achievement of her career at table tennis was when she was ranked 17th in the [[Table tennis at the 1992 Summer Olympics – Women's singles|women's singles]] and [[Table tennis at the 1992 Summer Olympics – Women's doubles|women's doubles]] with [[Ling Ling Agustin]] at the [[1992 Summer Olympics]] in [[Barcelona]].

At the [[1989 Southeast Asian Games]] in [[Kuala Lumpur]], Malaysia, Rossy, who was playing as an Indonesian table tennis athlete of the women's singles number, chose a walkout in the final against the host athlete on 25 August 1989. It was suspected because of the fraud committed by Goh Kun Tee as a referee from [[Malaysia]] who gave free score to the host athlete. Even though a ball from Rossy's blow grabbed thinly on the lip of the table, but the referee said come out and give a score for opponent Rossy, Leong Mee Wan. Chairman of the All-Indonesia Table Tennis Union at that time, [[Ali Said (Indonesia)|Ali Said]] who was in the arena of the game directly instructed athletes and Indonesian table tennis officials to resign.

After retiring in 2009, throughout the year 2010-2014 Rossy is active as a national table tennis coach. Rossy is entrusted with coaching the Indonesian women's table tennis team at the [[2011 Southeast Asian Games|26th SEA Games of 2011]] in [[Palembang]] and coaching the table tennis national team at the Olympic pre-qualification in [[Bangkok]], Thailand on 4&ndash;5 February 2012.

==Biography==
===Early life===
Rossy Pratiwi Dipoyanti was born in [[Bandung]], [[West Java]] as the first child of six siblings.<ref name=:'Maestro1'>{{harvnb|Maestro Indonesia 2018|loc=seg. 2}}, min. 00:01 – 01:20</ref> She comes from [[Ba 'Alawi sada]] family of [[Hadhrami people|Hadhrami]] [[Arab Indonesians|Arabs]] surnamed Aal bin Syechbubakar ({{lang-ar-at| آل بن شيخ أبو بكر |Aāl bin Shāīkh Abū Bakr}}; {{IPA-ar|ʔaːl bin ʃæjx aːbuː bakr}}), her father was Ali Umar Syechbubakar, while her mother was a [[Sundanese people|Sundanese woman]] named Neni Nurlaeni.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Rossy Sang Srikandi}}

===Personal life===

In 2001, Rossy married a [[Javanese people|Javanese man]] named [[Rany Kristiono]].{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Tenis Meja Adalah}} Rany is a basketball athlete who played in Hadtex Bandung club ([[Garuda Bandung|Garuda Flexi Bandung]] in present day), and last played in [[Satya Wacana Salatiga|Satya Wacana Angsapura]] club.{{sfn|Eurobasket.com, Garuda Flexi Bandung}} Both were meet at the Century Park Hotel Jakarta when Rossy was in the [[1994 Asian Games]] training center which was held at the hotel, while Rany was in the 1994 Basketball Competition and stayed at the same hotel.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Kegebet Cinta}} From his marriage to Rany, Rossy was blessed with 4 daughters, they are Diva Marcella Maharani, Najwa Julianoer Qayrani, Jasmine Aprillia Khirani, dan Nayla Julia Aisyahrani.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Kegebet Cinta}} She did not force her children to play table tennis or basketball, like her eldest son, Diva Marcella Maharani (born on March 26, 2002) who claimed to be more interested in art than sports. Even so, Rossy still hopes that one of his four children will follow her parents footsteps as an athlete.<ref>{{harvnb|Maestro Indonesia 2018|loc=seg. 4}}, min. 02:25 – 06:00</ref>

==Career==
===Early career===

Rossy's love of table tennis began when her father, Ali Umar Syechbubakar played in his yard.<ref name=:'Maestro1' /> Rossy then introduced to the world of table tennis since the second grade of elementary school by her father. She started her playing table tennis career from intercity match. During the fourth grade of elementary school she entered the Triple V club,{{sfn|TimIndonesia.id 2018, Get to Know}} where she then got to know [[:id:Diana Wuisan|Diana Wuisan]], one of Indonesia's legendary table tennis athletes. Diana who saw Rossy potentially big on table tennis then invites her to enter the Sanjaya Gudang Garam Table Tennis Association club in [[Kediri (city)|Kediri, East Java]]. Upon the support of her parents, Rossy then left her birthplace, [[Bandung]] for having to live in Gudang Garam dormitory, Kediri until graduate high school (for about seven years).{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Rossy Sang Srikandi}}

In Kediri, Rossy began to be trained through various competitions, including when she will attend the 2nd Asian Junior Championship in [[Nagoya]], [[Japan]], on 1–6 April 1986. In the tournament, the Indonesian women's team is only ranked sixth, below [[Taiwan]], Japan, [[North Korea]], [[South Korea]] and [[China]].{{sfn|Asian Table Tennis Union, Asian Junior Championships}}

===National Sports Week===
In 1985, when she was 13 years old, Rossy began achievement in various national tournaments, ranging from regional championships to [[National Sports Week (Indonesia)|National Sports Week]]. Throughout her career from 1985 to 2008 in the National Sports Week, Rossy once represented [[East Java]], [[East Kalimantan]], [[West Java]], [[Lampung]], and [[South Sumatra]]. In addition, she also managed to collect 7 gold medals, 7 silver medals, and 9 bronze medals during her career at the National Sports Week.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Tenis Meja Adalah}}

===SEA Games===

Rossy's debut at the [[SEA Games]] began when she was just 15 years old, precisely at the [[1987 Southeast Asian Games]] in [[Jakarta]].<ref>{{harvnb|Maestro Indonesia 2018|loc=seg. 1}}, min. 11:11 – 12:05</ref> While going to the 1987 SEA Games, Rossy and other Indonesian teams first attended the national training camp held by the All-Indonesia Table Tennis Union in [[North Korea]].{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Berguru Tenis Meja}} Under the training of Kang Nung-ha, Rossy is not only trained physically but also mentally.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Tenis Meja Adalah}} As a result, she won two golds from the women's singles and mixed doubles, while the two silver she got from the women's doubles and women's team.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Rossy Sang Srikandi}} During her career throughout 1987–2001 at the Southeast Asian Games, she managed to collect 13 gold medals, 8 silver medals, and 8 bronze medals.{{sfn|TimIndonesia.id 2018, Get to Know}}

At the [[1989 Southeast Asian Games|15th SEA Games in 1989]] in [[Kuala Lumpur]], due to referee cheating who gave free scores to the host athlete, Rossy who was playing as an Indonesian table tennis athlete of the women's singles finally chose a walkout in the final against Leong Mee Wan on 25 August 1989.{{sfn|Rappler 2017, Kisah-kisah walk out}} Chronology of cheating occurred in the second set because act of Goh Kun Tee as Malaysian referee who issued a controversy decision, whereas in the first set of the game went normal even though the match was won by Rossy with thin score, 17–16.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Korban Kecurangan}} The second set runs fiercely, when return ball from Mee Wan falls on her right side, Rossy launches a forehand drive. Ball from Rossy's blow grabbed thinly on the lip of the table, but the referee said come out and give a score for Leong Mee Wan. The Indonesian team manager, RM Nuryanto, immediately protested the controversy decision, but the referee remained on his decision. Despite having won two golds (in the women's singles and women's doubles) and one bronze (in the mixed doubles), Rossy remained disappointed and cried to her coach, [[:id:Diana Wuisan|Diana Wuisan]], because the women's singles number was her flagship.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Korban Kecurangan}}

In the middle of a crowded audience, chairman of the All-Indonesia Table Tennis Union, [[Ali Said (Indonesia)|Ali Said]] who was in the arena of the game immediately instructed that athletes and table tennis officials of Indonesia to resign. With shed a tear he said that Indonesia did not want to be trampled by Malaysia, so they chose to leave the game.{{sfn|Said, 1997|p=76}} Chairman of the [[Olympic Council of Malaysia]] at that time, Hamzah Abu Samah even criticized the walkout action conducted by the Indonesian table tennis team. He considered the action would damage the goal of the SEA Games, namely to increase the spirit of friendship between countries in [[Southeast Asia]].{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Malaysia Sengaja Curangi}}

Yap Yong Yih as honorary referee then reported the incident to the organizing committee of SEA Games. Although in the end Goh Kun Tee changed his decision after discussions with assistant referee Cyril Sen, but that does not mean anything because Rossy and table tennis officials of Indonesia have already left the game. Result, Leong Mee Wan still as the first winner and got a gold medal, while Rossy got a silver medal.{{sfn|Historia.id 2017, Atlet Tenis Meja}}

===Asian Games, Asian Championships, and World Championships===

At the [[1994 Asian Games]] in [[Hiroshima]], Rossy brought the Indonesian table tennis women's team to sixth place.{{sfn|Hiroshima City University, Result of Table tennis}} In addition, Rossy also repeatedly brought Indonesian women's teams into the top 10 at the [[Asian Table Tennis Championships]].{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Rossy Sang Srikandi}} In the [[1987 World Table Tennis Championships]] in [[India]], [[1989 World Table Tennis Championships|1989]] in [[Germany]], [[1991 World Table Tennis Championships|1991]] in [[Japan]], [[1993 World Table Tennis Championships|1993]] in [[Sweden]], [[1995 World Table Tennis Championships|1995]] in [[China]], and [[1999 World Table Tennis Championships|1997]] in [[England]], Indonesia's women's table tennis team was always in Division 2. Then at the [[2000 World Team Table Tennis Championships]] in [[Malaysia]] and [[2001 World Table Tennis Championships|2001]] in Japan, Indonesian table tennis women's team rose to Division 1 and ranked 17–20.<ref>{{harvnb|Maestro Indonesia 2018|loc=seg. 1}}, min. 14:30 – 14:50</ref>

===Summer Olympics===

Throughout her career, Rossy twice represented [[Indonesia]] for the [[Olympics]], namely the [[1992 Summer Olympics]] in [[Barcelona]] and the [[1996 Summer Olympics]] in [[Atlanta]].{{sfn|Olympics, Rossy Pratiwi Dipoyanti}}

At the 1992 Summer Olympics in Barcelona, Rossy appeared in [[Table tennis at the 1992 Summer Olympics – Women's singles|women's singles]] and [[Table tennis at the 1992 Summer Olympics – Women's doubles|women's doubles]] paired with Ling Ling Agustin.{{sfn|BolaSport.com 2017, Mantan Petenis Meja}} Four weeks before the Olympics, Rossy faced obstacles for having to be hospitalized for a week when had an appendectomy. After returning home from the hospital, she then underwent a weeklong recovery at home. Actually doctors advise Rossy to not physical activity first, but the advice was ignored by her. Without the permission of the doctor, Rossy was still doing the exercises for two weeks before the Olympics.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Tenis Meja Adalah}} Nevertheless, Rossy still scent the name of Indonesia, because although she did not bring home medals, but she managed to occupy the world 17th rank for the women's singles number, and occupied the same position for the women's doubles with Ling Ling Agustin.{{sfn|Sports Reference, Rossy Pratiwi Dipoyanti}} While on the women's singles who represented by Ling Ling Agustin and the [[Table tennis at the 1992 Summer Olympics – Men's singles|men's singles]] who represented by Anton Suseno, Indonesia ranked 33rd.{{sfn|Sports Reference, Indonesia Table Tennis}}

At the 1996 Summer Olympics in Atlanta, Rossy appeared in the [[Table tennis at the 1996 Summer Olympics – Women's singles|women's singles]] number and was ranked 49th.{{sfn|Sports Reference, Table Tennis at the 1996 Atlanta}} Despite failing to bring home a medal, Rossy remains proud because trusted to carry the [[1996 Summer Olympics torch relay|1996 Summer Olympics torch]] with runner [[Ethel Hudson (athlete)|Ethel Hudson]]. They bring the torch with a run along the 500 meter route as a representative of Indonesia.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Tenis Meja Adalah}}

==Other activities==

After retiring in 2009, Rossy still wrestle in the world of table tennis.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Tenis Meja Adalah}} Throughout the year 2010–2014 Rossy is active as a national table tennis coach. Rossy is trusted to coaching the Indonesian table tennis women's team at the [[2011 Southeast Asian Games|26th Southeast Asian Games in 2011]] in [[Palembang]]{{sfn|Tribunnews.com 2011, Rossy Pratiwi: Singapura}} and coaching the Olympic prequalification team in [[Bangkok]], [[Thailand]] on 4–5 February 2012.{{sfn|Kompas 2012, Rossy Ditunjuk Melatih}} In addition, Rossy also coached the [[South Sumatra]] table tennis women's team{{sfn|BeritaSatu 2012, Rossy Latih Timnas}} and became a table tennis coach for [[civil servants]] throughout of [[West Java]].<ref>{{harvnb|Maestro Indonesia 2018|loc=seg. 4}}, min. 00:01 – 01:00</ref> At the 11th Asia Pacific Veterans Table Tennis Championships at [[Yaizu, Shizuoka]], Rossy played in women's singles and women's doubles with Ling Ling Agustin,{{sfn|ATTVU, List of Participants}} but lost by [[Taiwan]] in the last eight.{{sfn|BolaSport.com 2017, Mantan Petenis Meja}}

In education world, in 2001 Rossy graduated as a Social Bachelor (S.Sos.) From the department of State Administration, College of Administrative Sciences Bagasasi, [[Bandung]]. He was appointed as a civil servant in [[Musi Banyuasin Regency]], [[South Sumatra]] in 2005. He then settled there while training local table tennis athletes until 2012. In 2012, Rossy and her family moved to the birthplace of her husband in [[Bogor]]. There, Rossy worked at the Department of Sanitation and Garden of Bogor City, then transferred to the Department of Youth and Sports of Bogor City as Section Head of Breeding and Sportsmanship since 2017.{{sfn|Historia.id 2018, Rossy Sang Srikandi}}{{sfn|Dispora Bogor, Data Pejabat}}
