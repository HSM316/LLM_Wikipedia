'''Walter L. "Bud" Carpeneti''' (born December 1, 1945) is an American lawyer and jurist, who served as the [[chief justice]] of the [[Alaska Supreme Court]] from 2009 to 2012. He attended Stanford University and Berkeley School of Law. A Californian, Carpeneti moved to Alaska to clerk for two earlier Alaska Supreme Court justices.

==Early life and education==
Born and raised in [[San Francisco]], [[California]], Carpeneti was the son of [[Superior Courts of California|San Francisco Superior Court]] Judge Walter I. Carpeneti.  After receiving an [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] in [[history]] from [[Stanford University]] in 1967 and a [[Juris Doctor|J.D.]] from the [[UC Berkeley School of Law]] in 1970, the younger Carpeneti became a [[law clerk]] for [[Alaska Supreme Court]] Justices [[John H. Dimond]] and [[Jay Rabinowitz (jurist)|Jay Rabinowitz]].

==Early career==
Returning to San Francisco in 1972 after the clerkships, Carpeneti initially entered [[Practice of law|private practice]] with Melvin Belli before joining in private practice with his father, retired Judge Walter I. Carpeneti, and his brother, Richard Carpeneti.<ref name="official">{{cite web|url=http://www.courts.alaska.gov/ctinfo.htm#sctjst|title=About the Alaska Court System|publisher=[[Alaska Court System]]|accessdate=March 20, 2011}}</ref><ref name="votesmart">{{cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=58804|title=Chief Justice Walter L. Carpeneti|publisher=[[Project Vote Smart]]|accessdate=March 20, 2011}}</ref>

Leaving the family law practice, Carpeneti returned to Alaska in 1974 to join the Alaska [[Public Defender]] Agency as supervising attorney of its [[Juneau, Alaska|Juneau]] office.  He left the Public Defender Agency in 1978 to enter private practice with William T. Council.  During this time, he was a member of the Alaska Judicial Council and of the committee that drafted the Alaska rules of evidence.  He was also the reporter to the Supreme Court's Criminal Pattern Jury Instructions Committee.<ref name="official" /><ref name="votesmart" />

==Alaska judicial service==
On October 15, 1981, [[Governor of Alaska|Governor]] [[Jay Hammond]] appointed Carpeneti to the [[Alaska Superior Court]].  While on the Superior Court, he was a member of the Alaska Commission on Judicial Conduct and of the Three-Judge Sentencing Panel.  He was also chair of the Supreme Court's Criminal Sentencing Practices and Procedures Committee.

In 1998, Governor [[Tony Knowles (politician)|Tony Knowles]], a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]], appointed Carpeneti to the [[Alaska Supreme Court]].  While on the Supreme Court, he chaired its Judicial Education Committee.<ref name="official" />

Carpeneti served as the 16th [[chief justice]] of the [[Alaska Supreme Court]] from 2009 to 2012.<ref name="official" /><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.juneauempire.com/stories/060709/reg_448423780.shtml|title=Carpeneti chosen as new chief justice|work=[[Juneau Empire]]|date=June 7, 2009}}</ref>  During his term as chief justice, he also served as second vice president on the board of directors of the national Conference of Chief Justices.<ref>Press Release, Alaska Court System, June 15, 2012, http://www.courts.alaska.gov/media/pr-carpeneti6-12.pdf.</ref>

==Family life==
In 1969, Carpeneti married Anne Dose, with whom he has four children: Chris, Marianna, Lia, and Bianca.<ref name="official" />
