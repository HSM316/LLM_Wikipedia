The '''mandibular incisive canal''' is a [[Bilateral symmetry|bilaterally paired]] [[bone|bony]] canal within the anterior portion of the [[human mandible|mandible]] that extends from the [[mental foramen]]  (usually) to near the [[ipsilateral]] [[lateral incisor]] teeth.<ref name="Greenstein" />

The [[inferior alveolar nerve]] splits into its two terminal branches within the [[mandibular canal]]: the [[mental nerve]] (which exits the mandible through the [[mental foramen]]), and the [[Inferior alveolar nerve#Incisive branch|incisive nerve]] which represents an anterior continuation of the inferior alveolar nerve and continues to course within the mandible in the mandibular incisive canal. The incisive nerve provides innervation to the mandibular first [[premolar]], [[canine tooth|canine]] and lateral and [[central incisor]]s.<ref name="Greenstein">Greenstein, G; Cavallaro, J; [[Dennis Tarnow|Tarnow, D]]. "Practical Application of Anatomy for the Dental Implant Surgeon," ''J Perio'' 2008;79:1833-1846</ref>  The incisive nerve either terminates as nerve endings within the anterior teeth or adjacent bone, or may join nerve endings that enter through the tiny [[lingual foramen]].{{Citation needed|date=July 2023}}

The incisive canal is typically found within the middle third of the mandible in an [[Commonly used terms of relationship and comparison in dentistry|apico-coronal]] dimension, reaching the midline 18% of the time.<ref>Mraiwa, N. "Presence and course of the incisive canal in the human mandibular interforaminal region: Two-dimensional imaging versus anatomical observation. ''Surg Radiol Anat'' 2003;25:416-423</ref>
