'''I-set domains''' are found in several [[cell adhesion molecule]]s, including vascular ([[VCAM-1|VCAM]]), intercellular ([[Intercellular adhesion molecule|ICAM]]), neural ([[Neural cell adhesion molecule|NCAM]]) and mucosal addressin ([[Addressin|MADCAM]]) cell adhesion molecules, as well as junction adhesion molecules ([[JAM2|JAM]]). I-set domains are also present in several other diverse protein families, including several tyrosine-protein kinase receptors, the hemolymph protein [[hemolin]], the muscle proteins titin, telokin, and twitchin, the neuronal adhesion molecule axonin-1,<ref name="PUB00024043">{{cite journal |vauthors=Sonderegger P, Welte W, Diederichs K, Freigang J, Proba K, Leder L |title=The crystal structure of the ligand binding module of axonin-1/TAG-1 suggests a zipper mechanism for neural cell adhesion |journal=Cell |volume=101 |issue=4 |pages=425–33 |year=2000 |pmid=10830169 |doi=10.1016/S0092-8674(00)80852-1|s2cid=15440572 |url=https://www.zora.uzh.ch/id/eprint/1084/1/Freigang_2000%2C_Cell.pdf }}</ref> and the signalling molecule semaphorin 4D that is involved in axonal guidance, immune function and angiogenesis.<ref name="PUB00027657">{{cite journal |vauthors=Stuart DI, Jones EY, Harlos K, Esnouf RM, Love CA |title=Structure determination of human semaphorin 4D as an example of the use of MAD in non-optimal cases |journal=Acta Crystallogr. D |volume=62 |issue= Pt 1|pages=108–15 |year=2006 |pmid=16369100 |doi=10.1107/S0907444905034992|doi-access=free }}</ref>
