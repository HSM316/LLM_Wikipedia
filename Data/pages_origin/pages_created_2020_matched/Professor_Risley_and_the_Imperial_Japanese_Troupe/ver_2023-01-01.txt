'''''Professor Risley and the Imperial Japanese Troupe''''' is a 2012 nonfiction book written by [[Frederik L. Schodt]], author of ''[[Dreamland Japan: Writings on Modern Manga|Dreamland Japan]]'' and ''[[Manga! Manga! The World of Japanese Comics|Manga! Manga!]]'', and published by [[Stone Bridge Press]]. It traces the history of American acrobat and impresario "Professor" Risley ([[Richard Risley Carlisle]]) and his attempts in the 19th century at bringing the West its first glimpse of Japanese popular entertainment. The [[Risley (circus act)|Risley act]] is also named after Professor Risley.<ref>Stuart Thayer. [http://www.circushistory.org/Thayer/Thayer3o.htm Circus Historical Society] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130624062339/http://www.circushistory.org/Thayer/Thayer3o.htm |date=2013-06-24 }}</ref>

==Reception==
After the book's release in 2012, it gained favorable reviews in the press. Kris Kosaka of ''[[The Japan Times]]'' said, "Schodt takes us all around the world of 19th-century entertainment: the competition, the disdain, the copycats and the triumphs. It's a captivating story about a pioneer in international entertainment."<ref>Kris Kosaka. [http://www.japantimes.co.jp/culture/2012/12/09/books/globe-trotting-acrobat-left-a-mark-on-japan/ The Japan Times: Globe-trotting acrobat left a mark on Japan] ''The Japan Times'', December 9, 2012</ref> And the [[Midwest Book Review]] said it is "an intriguing look at international relations, culture, the circus, and its effects on the modern day," and "a must for anyone seeking an original and offbeat take on history".<ref>[http://www.midwestbookreview.com/lbw/dec_12.htm Midwest Book Review: The World History Shelf]</ref>
