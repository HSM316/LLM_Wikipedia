'''Shon McCarthy''' is the Director/[[Curator]] of three [[Jackson State University]] art galleries. The galleries are JSU's Johnson Hall gallery, College of Liberal Arts gallery, and JSU Downtown galleries. Exhibitions are student centered and assist to "Exposure+Experience=Lifelong Learning"(gallery mission) for student by including student work with the international and national artists' exhibitions. Exhibitions have ranged from painting created by musician [[George Clinton (musician)|George Clinton]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Helsel|first1=Amber|title=Chrissy Walter's Funkadelic Family|url=http://www.jacksonfreepress.com/news/2016/mar/01/family-ties-funk/|publisher=Jackson Free Press|date=March 1, 2016}}</ref> JSU was the first university to exhibit his paintings.  In September 2016, McCarthy collaborated with the [[Philadelphia Museum of Art]] and procured a gifted 25 piece permanent collection of Patrick Kelly (thanks to a donation from Bill and Bjorn Amelan).  Spring of 2017, she collaborated with London's Belgravia Art Gallery to bring art created and signed by [[Nelson Mandela]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Did you know Mandela was an Artist?|url=http://m.belgraviagallery.com/news/did-you-know-mandela-was-also-an-artist/}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Featherstone|first1=Alexis|title=Mandela the Activist; Mandela the Artist|url=http://www.jsumsnews.com/?p=28510|publisher=Jackson State University Communications|date=February 13, 2017}}</ref> McCarthy created JSU's Gallery1 Summer Art Institute and where JSU Department of Art Alumni taught art to 150 K-9 students.<ref>{{cite news|last1=James-Terry|first1=Rachel|title=Jackson State University's Summer Art Institute colors outside dividing lines|url=http://www.jsumsnews.com/?p=31657|publisher=Jackson State University|date=July 27, 2017}}</ref>  For two summers, she supervised and instructed art to over 200 K-9 students this included mulit-medium art to sewing instruction. McCarthy later procured an additional 250 plus pieces from the Patrick Kelly collection in the summer of 2017, it is housed the H.T. Sampson Archives.{{citation needed|date=August 2017}}<ref>{{cite web|title=Patrick Kelly:From MS to NY to Paris and Back|url=http://www.jsums.edu/gallery1arts/a-week-of-fashion-week-a-month-of-exhibitions/|website=Jackson State University's Gallery}}</ref>
