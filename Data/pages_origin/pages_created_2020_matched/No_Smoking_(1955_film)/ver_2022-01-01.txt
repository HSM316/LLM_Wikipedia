'''''No Smoking''''' is a 1955 British [[comedy film]] directed by [[Henry Cass]] and starring Reg Dixon, [[Peter Martyn (actor)|Peter Martyn]], [[Belinda Lee]] and [[Lionel Jeffries]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.comedy.co.uk/film/no_smoking/ |website=British Comedy Guide |accessdate=16 May 2019|title=No Smoking - Film }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2b6b1b4a2f|title=No Smoking (1955)|website=BFI}}</ref> <ref>NO SMOKING (Tempean-Eros)
Picture Show; London Vol. 61, Iss. 1707,  (Dec 17, 1955): 10. </ref>
== Synopsis ==

Reg Bates is a scientist who invents a pill that can cure smokers of their [[nicotine]] addiction. This is revealed by a visiting American, Hal Hurst. Bates faces strong opposition from both the [[tobacco]] industry and the government.

==Production==
It was based on a 1952 TV play.<ref>[https://imdb-webservice.amazon.com/title/tt1291745/ 1952 TV play] at [[IMDb]]</ref> One review said it had "plenty of good family fun."<ref>Specially Written TV Play is Effective
Author: J. Stubbs Walker Date: Monday,  Mar. 17, 1952
Publication: Daily Mail (London, England) Issue: 17415 p2 </ref>

It was made at Alliance Studios, Southall. It was one of only a few movies starring Reg Dixon.<ref>BACKBEAT: 1950: Confidentially - it's our Reg ; BECAUSE THEY DON'T MAKE MUSIC LIKE THEY USED TO!: [First Edition]
Chambers, Peter. Coventry Evening Telegraph; Coventry [Coventry]25 Jan 2005: 16, 17. </ref> It was one of several comedies featuring Belinda Lee.<ref name="bomb">{{cite magazine|first=Stephen|last=Vagg|magazine=Filmink|title=A Tale of Two Blondes: Diana Dors and Belinda Lee|date=September 7, 2020|url=https://www.filmink.com.au/a-tale-of-two-blondes-diana-dors-and-belinda-lee/}}</ref>

==Reception==
''The Monthly Film Bulletin'' said "the action does not fizz; the bubbles never quite come to the surface."<ref>NO SMOKING Monthly Film Bulletin; London Vol. 22, Iss. 252,  (Jan 1, 1955): 181. </ref>
