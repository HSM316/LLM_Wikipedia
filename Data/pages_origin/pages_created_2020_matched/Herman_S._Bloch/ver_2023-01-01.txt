'''Herman Samuel Bloch''' (June 15, 1912 â€“ June 16, 1990) was an [[Americans|American]] [[chemist]] and an [[Invention|inventor]]. Bloch invented the [[catalytic converter]], a device that removes pollutants from automobile exhaust fumes.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/1990/06/19/obituaries/herman-s-bloch-78-chemist-and-inventor.html ''The New York Times'':Obituaries:Herman S. Bloch, 78, Chemist and Inventor, Published: June 19, 1990]</ref> Bloch held more than 270 patents.<ref name="NAP">[http://www.nap.edu/openbook.php?record_id=11522&page=110 National Academies Press, V.87]</ref> He was the deputy director of research of the aerospace company [[AlliedSignal]] Inc, and chairman of the [[Cook County, Illinois|Cook County]] Housing Authority.<ref name="Cook">[https://web.archive.org/web/20160409215900/https://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-4003764.html Herman Bloch, inventor, county housing official]</ref> He received the Chemical Pioneer Award in 1989 from the [[American Institute of Chemists]].<ref>[http://www.theaic.org/award_winners/chem_pioneer.html American Institute of Chemists:Chemical Pioneer Award Winners:]</ref> He received the Ernest J. Houdry Award in Applied Catalysis,<ref name="NAP"/> the [[Eger V. Murphree|E. V. Murphree]] Award in Industrial and Engineering Chemistry in 1974,<ref name="NAP"/> and the Richard J. Kokes Memorial Award and Lectureship from [[Johns Hopkins University]] in 1971.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.nacatsoc.org/awards_winners.asp |title=The North American Catalysis Society (NACS) |access-date=2011-12-16 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120426054608/http://www.nacatsoc.org/awards_winners.asp |archive-date=2012-04-26 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Bloch was elected to the [[National Academy of Sciences]] in 1975.<ref name="NAP"/>

== Career ==
Bloch was born in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]]. His parents were [[Ukrainian-Jewish]]<ref>[[Martin Harry Greenberg]], ''The Jewish lists: physicists and generals, actors and writers, and hundreds of other lists of accomplished Jews'', Schocken Books (1979), p. 113</ref><ref>Abraham Scheinberg & Harry Cohen, ''American Jews: Their Lives and Achievements: A Contemporary Biographical Record'', vol. 2, 
American Jewish Literary Foundation (1958), p. 784</ref> [[Immigration to the United States|immigrants]] Aaron and Esther Bloch.<ref name="NAP"/>
He received his B.A. and Ph.D. in organic chemistry in 1936 from the [[University of Chicago]].
