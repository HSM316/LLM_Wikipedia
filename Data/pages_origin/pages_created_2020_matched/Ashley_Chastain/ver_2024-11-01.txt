'''Ashley Chastain''' is an American, former collegiate [[softball]] [[pitcher]] and current [[head coach]] at [[South Carolina Gamecocks softball|South Carolina]]. She played [[college softball]] and was a pitcher for [[South Carolina Gamecocks softball|South Carolina]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Chastain Named 49ers Head Softball Coach |url=https://charlotte49ers.com/news/2019/6/6/chastain-named-49ers-head-softball-coach.aspx |website=Charlotte49ers.com |date=6 June 2019 |publisher=UNC Charlotte |accessdate=8 June 2019}}</ref>

==Playing career==
Chastain played [[college softball]] at [[South Carolina Gamecocks softball|South Carolina]] for three seasons and finished eleventh in career relief appearances for the Gamecocks. She led the team in starts, innings pitched and ERA during her junior season.

==Coaching career==
===College of Charleston===
On October 22, 2013, Chastain was named assistant coach for the College of Charleston softball program.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ashley Chastain Completes CofC Softball Staff |url=https://cofcsports.com/news/2013/10/22/209289457.aspx |website=CofCSports.com |date=22 October 2013 |publisher=COLLEGE OF CHARLESTON |accessdate=8 June 2019}}</ref>

===Michigan State===
On July 20, 2016, Chastain was named the new pitching coach for the [[Michigan State Spartans softball]] program.<ref>{{cite web |title=Chastain Joins Softball Staff |url=https://msuspartans.com/news/2016/7/20/Chastain_Joins_Softball_Staff.aspx |website=MSUSpartans.com |date=20 July 2016 |publisher=Michigan State Spartan Athletics |accessdate=8 June 2019}}</ref>

===Ole Miss===
On November 1, 2017, Chastain was named the new pitching coach for the [[Ole Miss Rebels softball]] program.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ole Miss Softball Adds Ashley Chastain as Pitching Coach |url=https://olemisssports.com/news/2017/11/1/Ole_Miss_Softball_Adds_Ashley_Chastain_as_Pitching_Coach.aspx |website=OleMissSports.com |publisher=University of Mississippi |accessdate=8 June 2019}}</ref>

===Charlotte===
On June 6, 2019, Chastain was named the new head coach for the [[Charlotte 49ers softball]] program.<ref>{{cite web |title=Chastain Named 49ers Head Softball Coach |url=https://charlotte49ers.com/news/2019/6/6/chastain-named-49ers-head-softball-coach.aspx |website=Charlotte49ers.com |date=6 June 2019 |publisher=UNC Charlotte |accessdate=8 June 2019}}</ref> After leading the team to a 14-13 record in 2020 before the season was cut short due to COVID-19, the 49ers went 31-19 in 2021 (their highest win total since 2016) and won their first-ever Conference USA East Division title.

Ahead of the 2022 season, Charlotte for the first time was picked as preseason favorites to win the Conference USA regular-season title.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://conferenceusa.com/news/2022/2/8/softball-sb-22-preseason-poll-team-announced.aspx | title=SB: 2022 Preseason Poll, Team Announced | date=8 February 2022 }}</ref> On March 2, 2022, the 49ers defeated their highest-ranked opponent in program history, upsetting No. 6 Virginia Tech 5-2.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://charlotte49ers.com/news/2022/3/2/softball-siftball.aspx | title=Niners Post Biggest Win in School History; Topple No. 6 Hokies, 5-2 | date=2 March 2022 }}</ref> She finished her tenure at Charlotte with a 154–96 overall record, a 65–32 conference record and two [[NCAA Division I softball tournament|NCAA tournament]] berths in five seasons.

===South Carolina===
On June 11, 2024, Chastain was named the head coach of the [[South Carolina Gamecocks softball]] program. Chastain is an alumna of the [[University of South Carolina]], having played from 2009 to 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://gamecocksonline.com/news/2024/06/11/ashley-chastain-named-gamecock-softball-head-coach/ |title=Ashley Chastain Named Gamecock Softball Head Coach |website=gamecocksonline.com |date=June 11, 2024 |access-date=June 11, 2024}}</ref>
