'''''There Goes the Neighborhood''''' (originally to be titled '''''Block Party''''') is an American [[prime-time]] [[reality television]] program on [[CBS]]. The show premiered on August 9, 2009, and features eight [[suburban]] [[families]] shut out from the outside world with no television, internet, phones, or contact with anybody outside of their [[neighborhood]].<ref name="premise">{{cite news |author=Andy Dehnart |author-link=Andy Dehnart |title=CBS’ There Goes the Neighborhood encloses families behind 20-foot wall; hosted by Idol’s Matt Rogers |url=http://www.realityblurred.com/realitytv/archives/there_goes_the_neighborhood/2009_Jun_30_announcement |publisher="Reality Blurred" |date=30 June 2009 |accessdate=31 July 2009}}</ref> The families will compete in challenges against each other. Each week, one family will be banished from the neighborhood, thereby eliminating the family from contention for the show's [[USD|$]]250,000 prize fund.<ref name="premise" /> The show's executive producers are [[Jay Bienstock]] and [[Mike Fleiss]].<ref name="pressrelease">{{cite news |title="There goes the neighborhood," A new reality-competition series, premieres Sunday, Aug. 9 on the CBS Television Network |url=http://www.thefutoncritic.com/news.aspx?id=20090629cbs02 |publisher="The Futon Critic" |date=29 June 2009 |accessdate=31 July 2009}}</ref><ref name="conditions">{{cite news |first=Rodney |last=Ho |title=CBS Kennesaw Wall reality show to debut Aug. 9 |url=http://blogs.ajc.com/radio-tv-talk/2009/06/29/the-kennesaw-wall-reality-show-there-goes-the-neighborhood-set-to-debut-on-cbs-august-9/ |publisher=[[Atlanta Journal-Constitution]] |date=29 June 2009 |accessdate=31 July 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090803084655/http://blogs.ajc.com/radio-tv-talk/2009/06/29/the-kennesaw-wall-reality-show-there-goes-the-neighborhood-set-to-debut-on-cbs-august-9/ |archivedate=3 August 2009 }}</ref> The show's presenter is [[Matt Rogers]], a finalist on ''[[American Idol (season 3)|American Idol 3]]''.<ref name="conditions" /><ref name="location">{{cite news |first=Christopher |last=Rochhio |title=CBS to debut walled-off 'There Goes the Neighborhood' on August 9|url=http://www.realitytvworld.com/news/cbs-debut-walled-off-there-goes-neighborhood-on-august-9-9203.php |publisher="Reality TV World" |date=30 June 2009 |accessdate=1 August 2009}}</ref>

== Production ==
The show was recorded in [[Kennesaw, Georgia]], a suburb of [[Atlanta]], specifically on the 2500 block of Fairlawn Downs NW in the Legacy Park community's Annandale Main.<ref name="premise" /><ref name="location" /> For the program, producers built a {{convert|20|ft|m|adj=on|0}} tall wall around the competitors' neighborhood to enforce their isolation from the outside world.<ref name="premise" /> [[Electricity]] and [[Natural gas|gas]] [[utilities]] for most household luxuries were cut off from each family's home.<ref name="conditions" />

Production for the show began in early June 2009. The families being placed into lockdown within the constructed barriers beginning on June 15.<ref name="location" />

== Gameplay ==
The program's gameplay is extremely similar to that of the American version of ''[[Big Brother (US TV series)|Big Brother]]'' (specifically ''[[Big Brother 2 (U.S.)|Big Brother 2]]'').<ref name="premise" /><ref name="conditions" /> In each episode, families will compete in a challenge in order to win rewards and safety from elimination.<ref name="conditions" /> The winner will name two families to face the neighbourhood vote. The families will then vote to force one family to leave the neighborhood for the remainder of the series.<ref name="conditions" /> In the finale, it is presumed that the six eliminated families will return to the neighborhood to vote for the show's winner.<ref name="conditions" />
