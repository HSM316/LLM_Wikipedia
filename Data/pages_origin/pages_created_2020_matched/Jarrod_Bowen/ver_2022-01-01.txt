'''Jarrod Bowen''' (born 20 December 1996) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[Forward (association football)|forward]] for [[Premier League]] club [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]]. He has previously played for [[Hereford United F.C.|Hereford United]] and [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]].

==Early life==
Bowen was born in [[Leominster]], Herefordshire.<ref name=11v11/> Growing up, his idol was [[David Beckham]] and he supported [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jarrod Bowen is the FASTEST player at West Ham?! {{!}} TRUE or FALSE |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nMrt9QouyZg|access-date=2021-01-14|website=YouTube}}</ref>

==Club career==
===Hereford United===
After unsuccessful trials at [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] and [[Cardiff City F.C.|Cardiff City]], Bowen started his career as a scholar at [[Hereford United F.C.|Hereford United]].<ref name=Stock/> At the age of 17, Bowen broke into the Hereford United first team after impressing for the youth team that season,<ref name=Stock>{{cite news |url=https://www.hulldailymail.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/hereford-united-hull-city-jarrod-293147 |title=From Hereford United to Hull City, how Jarrod Bowen's stock has kept on rising |first=Philip |last=Buckingham |website=Hull Live |publisher=Local World |date=8 August 2017 |access-date=24 December 2018}}</ref> making his debut in a 2–0 loss against [[Barnet F.C.|Barnet]] on 22 March 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theathletic.co.uk/2056651/2020/09/11/jarrod-bowen-west-ham-background-hereford-united/|title=Jarrod Bowen: The rise of the Milkybar Kid|publisher=The Athletic|first1=Roshane|last1=Thomas|date=10 September 2020|accessdate=13 February 2021}}</ref> He scored his first career goal on 21 April 2014 in Hereford's 3–2 home victory over [[Alfreton Town F.C.|Alfreton Town]].<ref name=Soccerway/>

===Hull City===
After Hereford were expelled from the [[Football Conference]] in June 2014,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/27786442 |title=Hereford United expelled from Football Conference |publisher=BBC Sport |date=10 June 2014 |access-date=28 August 2017}}</ref> Bowen signed for [[Premier League]] club [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]] in July.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/28216672 |title=Hereford: Jarrod Bowen joins Hull, Billy Murphy goes to Bristol City |publisher=BBC Sport |date=8 July 2014 |access-date=28 August 2017}}</ref> After impressing during pre-season in 2016,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.hullcitytigers.com/hull-city/jarrod-bowen/ |title=Jarrod Bowen |publisher=Hull City A.F.C. |access-date=24 December 2018}}</ref> he made his first-team debut for Hull on 23 August, playing the full 90 minutes in a 3–1 [[EFL Cup]] victory over [[Exeter City F.C.|Exeter City]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/37091226 |title=Exeter City 1–3 Hull City |publisher=BBC Sport |date=23 August 2016 |access-date=28 August 2017}}</ref> On 7 November 2016, Bowen signed a new two-year contract with Hull.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.espn.co.uk/football/hull-city/story/2991676/jarrod-bowen-signs-two-year-extension-with-hull-city |title=Jarrod Bowen signs two-year extension with Hull City |publisher=ESPN |agency=PA Sport |date=7 November 2016 |access-date=24 December 2018}}</ref>

Bowen scored his first goal for Hull on 5 August 2017, away to [[Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa]] in a 1–1 draw.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/a-villa-vs-hull-city/374620 |title=Aston Villa 1–1 Hull City: Jarrod Bowen strike earns Tigers point |publisher=Sky Sports |date=6 August 2017 |access-date=24 December 2018}}</ref> In September 2017, Bowen signed a new contract that tied him to the club until June 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.hullcitytigers.com/bowen-looks-forward-to-continuing-his-development-with-the-tigers/ |title=Bowen looks forward to continuing his development with the Tigers |publisher=Hull City A.F.C. |date=28 September 2017 |access-date=24 December 2018}}</ref>
He scored 15 goals in all competitions during the [[2017–18 Hull City A.F.C. season|2017–18 season]], which saw him finish as Hull's top scorer.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.soccerbase.com/teams/team.sd?team_id=1310&teamTabs=stats&season_id=150 |title=Hull: Squad details: 2017/18 |website=Soccerbase |publisher=Centurycomm |access-date=24 December 2018}}</ref> At the annual end of season awards ceremony on 8 May 2018, Bowen won both the Hull City Supporter's Player of the Year and Players' Player of the Year awards.<ref name=POTY>{{cite web |url=https://www.hullcitytigers.com/bowen-bags-a-brace-at-player-of-the-year-awards-evening/ |title=Bowen bags a brace at Player of the Year awards evening |publisher=Hull City A.F.C. |date=8 May 2018 |access-date=24 December 2018}}</ref>
[[File:Jarrod Bowen 20170819.jpg|thumb|Jarrod Bowen playing for [[Hull City A.F.C.|Hull City]] in 2017]]
Bowen was nominated for the [[EFL Championship Player of the Month]] award for December 2018,<ref>{{cite web |title= Championship Player of the Month: December nominations|url=https://www.efl.com/news/2019/january/championship-player-of-the-month-december-nominations/ |publisher=EFL|access-date=11 January 2019}}</ref> subsequently winning the award in January 2019.<ref name="Hull City pair Jarrod Bowen and Nigel Adkins win Sky Bet Championship December awards">{{cite web |title= Hull City pair Jarrod Bowen and Nigel Adkins win Sky Bet Championship December awards|url=https://www.skysports.com/football/news/11688/11603821/hull-city-pair-jarrod-bowen-and-nigel-adkins-win-sky-bet-championship-december-awards |publisher=Sky Sports|access-date=11 January 2019}}</ref> In March 2019, he was selected as part of the 2018–19 Championship Team of the Season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/football/47710254|title=EFL awards: Che Adams, Teemu Pukki & Billy Sharp on Championship shortlist|date=26 March 2019|access-date=28 March 2019|publisher=BBC Sport}}</ref> He scored 22 goals in all competitions during the [[2018–19 Hull City A.F.C. season|2018–19 season]], which saw him finish as Hull's top scorer again.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.soccerbase.com/teams/team.sd?team_id=1310&teamTabs=stats&season_id=150 |title=Hull: Squad details: 2018/19 |website=Soccerbase |publisher=Centurycomm |access-date=13 May 2019}}</ref> At the annual end of season awards ceremony on 7 May 2019, Bowen won the Player of the Year, Players' Player of the Year and Hull City Supporter's Player of the Year awards.<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.hullcitytigers.com/bowen-picks-up-hat-trick-of-awards/| title = Bowen Picks Up Hat-Trick Of Awards| date = 8 May 2019| publisher = Hull City A.F.C.| access-date = 13 May 2019}}</ref> 

===West Ham United===
On 31 January 2020, Bowen joined [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United]] on a permanent transfer, with the Premier League club paying an initial £18&nbsp;million for the forward, with up to £7&nbsp;million in potential add-ons.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.skysports.com/transfer-centre|title=Transfer Centre|website=Sky Sports|access-date=1 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.whufc.com/news/articles/2020/january/31-january/jarrod-bowen-joins-west-ham-united|title=Jarrod Bowen joins West Ham United|website=West Ham|date=1 February 2020|access-date=1 February 2020}}</ref> On 29 February 2020, in his first start for West Ham, Bowen scored his first goal for the club, in a 3–1 win against [[Southampton F.C.|Southampton]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thenational.ae/sport/football/jarrod-bowen-marks-first-start-with-goal-as-west-ham-ease-past-southampton-1.986269|title=Jarrod Bowen marks first start with goal as West Ham ease past Southampton|website=The National|access-date=21 June 2020}}</ref>

On 27 September 2020, Bowen scored his first goals of the [[2020–21 Premier League]] season, scoring twice in a 4–0 win against [[Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.|Wolverhampton Wanderers]]; West Ham's first league win of the season. On 4 October 2020, a week later, Bowen added to his tally for the season, scoring the third goal in a 3–0 away win against [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]]. During the second half of the season, Bowen scored three goals in three consecutive games, in a 3–3 draw against [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]] and 3–2 wins over Wolves and Leicester, taking his tally up to eight goals for the season.<ref name=Soccerway/>

Bowen scored his first goal in European football during the [[2021–22 UEFA Europa Conference League group stage|2021–22 Europa League]] in a 3–0 win over [[K.R.C. Genk|Genk]] on 21 October 2021.<ref>{{cite web|URL=https://www.whufc.com/news/articles/2021/october/21-october/jarrod-bowen-im-buzzing-my-first-europa-league-goal-west-ham|title= Jarrod Bowen: I'm buzzing with my first Europa League goal for West Ham United}}</ref>
