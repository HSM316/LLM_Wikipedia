'''Archery Association of India''' (AAI) is the national [[Sport governing body|governing body]] of [[archery]] in India. Its headquarters are located in [[New Delhi]], and its current president is [[Arjun Munda]], AAI is a non-profit, government funded organisation affiliated by [[World Archery Federation]] (IAF), [[Asian Archery Federation]] (AAF) and [[Indian Olympic Association]] (IOA) and recognized by [[Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports ( Pratsana) (India)|Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports]] of India.<ref name="aaf">{{cite web|url=http://www.asianarchery.com/organization/association.asp |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130407174110/http://www.asianarchery.com/organization/association.asp |url-status=dead |archive-date=7 April 2013 |title=AAF Member Associations |publisher=[[Asian Archery Federation]]'s official website |accessdate=12 April 2011 }}</ref><ref name="aai"/>

AAI came into existence on 8 August 1973 after archery was reintroduced to the [[Olympic Games]] in [[1972 Summer Olympics|1972]]. It is responsible for organising, promoting and controlling the sport of archery in India.<ref name="aai">{{cite web | url=http://www.indianarchery.info/htdocs/iarhist.htm | title=History of Archery Association of India | publisher=Archery Association of India's official website | accessdate=12 April 2011}}</ref>
