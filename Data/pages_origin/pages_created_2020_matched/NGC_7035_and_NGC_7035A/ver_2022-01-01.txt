'''NGC 7035 and NGC 7035A'''<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dso-browser.com/deep-sky/10715/ngc-7035b/galaxy|title=Galaxy NGC 7035B - Galaxy in Capricornus Constellation · Deep Sky Objects Browser|last=Rojas|first=Sebastián García|website=DSO Browser|language=en|access-date=2017-06-22}}</ref> are a pair of interacting [[lenticular galaxies]] located around 400 to 430 million [[light-year]]s away in the [[constellation]] of [[Capricornus (constellation)|Capricornus]].<ref>{{Cite simbad|title=NGC 7035|access-date=2017-06-22}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dso-browser.com/deep-sky/8222/ngc-7035/galaxy|title=Galaxy NGC 7035 - Galaxy in Capricornus Constellation · Deep Sky Objects Browser|last=Rojas|first=Sebastián García|website=DSO Browser|language=en|access-date=2017-06-22}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ned.ipac.caltech.edu/cgi-bin/objsearch?objname=NGC+7035&extend=no&hconst=73&omegam=0.27&omegav=0.73&corr_z=1&out_csys=Equatorial&out_equinox=J2000.0&obj_sort=RA+or+Longitude&of=pre_text&zv_breaker=30000.0&list_limit=5&img_stamp=YES|title=Your NED Search Results|website=ned.ipac.caltech.edu|access-date=2017-06-22}}</ref> The main galaxy, NGC 7035 was discovered by astronomer Frank Muller in 1886.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://cseligman.com/text/atlas/ngc70.htm|title=New General Catalog Objects: NGC 7000 – 7049|website=cseligman.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-04-15}}</ref>
