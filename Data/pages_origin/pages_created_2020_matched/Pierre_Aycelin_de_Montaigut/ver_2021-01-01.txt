'''Pierre Aycelin de Montaigut''' or '''Montaigu''', '''Montagu''', known as '''Cardinal de Laon''', born between 1320 and 1325 and died 8 November 1388,<ref name=Maillard-Luypaert>{{cite book|last=Maillard-Luypaert|first=Monique|title=Papauté, clercs et laïcs: le diocèse de Cambrai à l'épreuve du grand schisme d'occident (1378-1417)|year=2001|publisher=Publications  des Faculté St Louis|isbn=2-8028-0142-2|page=228|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=8b4fiFYVRuYC&pg=PA297&dq=Pierre+Aycelin+de+Montaigut&hl=cs&ei=YVrRTp3bJc_BtAa2mMiHAg&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=book-preview-link&resnum=2&ved=0CDQQuwUwAQ#v=onepage&q=Pierre%20Aycelin%20de%20Montaigut&f=false|language=fr}}</ref> was a fourteenth-century [[France|French]] cardinal, who was the [[bishop of Nevers]] (1361–1371) and bishop of the [[Ancient Diocese of Laon|Diocese of Laon]] (1371-1386), advisor to the king of [[Charles V of France|Charles V]] and [[Peerage of France|peer]] of France.<ref name="Du Chesne">{{cite web|last=Du Chesne|first=François|title=Histoire de tous les cardinaux françois de naissance : ou qui ont esté promeus au cardinalat, Volume 1|url=http://gallica.bnf.fr/ark:/12148/bpt6k914067.image.f697|publisher=Gallica|accessdate=26 November 2011|language=fr}}</ref>

He took part in the Council of King Charles V, acting as regent during the minority of King [[Charles VI of France]] who ascended to the throne after his uncle's death. He was known then as one of the [[marmousets]]. His autopsy proved that he was poisoned on 8 November 1388,<ref name="Fisquet">{{cite book|last=Honoré Jean Pierre Fisquet|title=La France pontificale (Gallia christiana), histoire chronologique et biographique des archevêques et évêques de tous les diocèses de France depuis l'établissement du christianisme jusqu'à nos jours, divisée en 17 provinces ecclésiastique|year=1864|publisher=Repos|isbn=|page=270|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=9xrd-05C5dwC&pg=PA270&dq=Pierre+Aycelin+de+Montaigut+Charles+VI+1388&hl=cs&ei=rl7RTom-LcPHsgbMu521DA&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=book-preview-link&resnum=2&ved=0CDcQuwUwAQ#v=onepage&q=Pierre%20Aycelin%20de%20Montaigut%20Charles%20VI%201388&f=false |OCLC=421594210}} {{in lang|fr}}</ref> being buried in the abbey church of the [[priory]] of Saint-Martin-des-Champs in [[Paris]].

==Biography==
===Family===
Pierre Aycelin de Montaigut descended from a family of minor nobility originally from [[Auvergne (province)|Auvergne]] who owned land near [[Billom]],<ref name=Kibler>{{cite book|last=Kibler|first=William W.|title=Medieval France (Garland Encyclopedias of the Middle Ages)|year=1995|publisher=Routledge|isbn=0-8240-4444-4|pages=90|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=4qFY1jpF2JAC&pg=PA90&lpg=PA90&dq=Gilles+Aycelin+the+Elder&source=bl&ots=XhA1LFO1b3&sig=5eFsobWQtOeVRMGtR329WLqSeIw&hl=cs&ei=RIPRTuqxK4PO8QPI-InlAw&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=8&sqi=2&ved=0CFQQ6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=Gilles%20Aycelin%20the%20Elder&f=false}}</ref> acquired in 1295 by the house of Aycelin. The last representative, who also owned the manor of [[Châteldon]] (Puy Dome), died in 1427. The coat-of-arms of this house was of sand, three golden torn Lyon langued gules.{{clarify|date=May 2017}}<ref name="Fisquet"/>

Brother of Cardinal [[Gilles Aycelin de Montaigu]],<ref group="n">Both were nephews of [[Gilles I Aycelin de Montaigu]], archbishop of [[Narbonne]] and the archbishop of [[Rouen]].</ref> who was bishop of [[Thérouanne]] and [[Lord Chancellor of France]] between  1357-1358 and again in 1361. His mother Mascaronne de La Tour d'Auvergne was the aunt of Guy de La Tour d'Auvergne, Lord of Olliergues, who married Martha Roger de Beaufort.<ref name="Maillard-Luypaert"/> She was the niece of [[Pope Clement VI]], and his brother was [[Pope Gregory XI]].

===Religious===
Like his uncle, Pierre Aycelin Montaigut was a doctor of [[canon law]]. He was a [[Benedictine]] monk and [[prior]] of the abbey of Saint-Martin des Champs. In 1359 he became chancellor to [[John, Duke of Berry|Jean de Berry]], son of the King of France [[John II of France|Jean II, le Bon]]. In 1361 he was appointed [[Roman Catholic Diocese of Nevers|bishop of Nevers]], succeeding Renaud II de Moulins. In 1368 he became [[ambassador]] to King Charles V with [[Pope Urban V]]. On 8 January 1371 he was promoted to bishop-duke of Laon by [[Pope Gregory XI]].<ref name="Maillard-Luypaert"/>

In 1379 he played a role in the support given by France to the antipope [[Clement VII]].{{elucidate|date=December 2016}} In 1383 he tried to bring the county of [[Flanders]], under the authority of the papacy, to Avignon. Pierre Aycelin Montaigut was created a cardinal by Pope Clement VII of the Avignon Obedience in the Consistory of 23 December 1383.<ref name="H. Fisquet">{{cite book|last=Honoré Jean Pierre Fisquet|title=La France pontificale (Gallia Christiana), Paris, Doyens, etc.|year=1864|publisher=Repos|page=333|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0hnlUlAO6acC&pg=PA333&dq=Pierre+Aycelin+de+Montaigut+1379&hl=cs&ei=Vl3RTtb2E87MtAbzwN28DA&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=book-preview-link&resnum=1&ved=0CC8QuwUwADgU#v=onepage&q=Pierre%20Aycelin%20de%20Montaigut%201379&f=false |OCLC=214933323}} {{in lang|fr}}</ref><ref>Conradus Eubel, ''Hierarchia catholica medii aevi'' Tomus I, editio altera (Monasterii 1913), p. 28 no. 14.</ref> In 1385, he gave up his position as bishop of Laon.<ref name="H. Fisquet"/> On 15 January 1388 the abbot of Saint-Denis, Guy de Monceau, accept the arbitration of the cardinal of Laon in the proceedings against the bishop of Paris, locked in the prison of the abbey.

On 1 November 1388 Charles VI attended the [[All Saints' Day]] mass in Reims. He stopped in [[Champagne, France|Champagne]] after returning from an expedition against the [[Guelders|Duke of Guelders]], an ally of the English. On November 3, a large meeting of the council was held in the episcopal palace of Reims. The cardinal of Laon, Peirre Aycelin Montaigut, former advisor to Charles V, was the one to take the matter to the table.{{elucidate|date=December 2016}} After him, the [[archbishop]] of Reims and his [[warlord]]s{{who|date=December 2016}} decided that the young ruler was capable of governing France.
