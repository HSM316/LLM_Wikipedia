'''Charles Sprague Beightler''' (January 12, 1898-August 19, 1982) served in the [[United States Navy]], commanding vessels during [[World War Two]] in the Pacific, and ultimately achieving the rank of [[rear admiral]].

==Early life==
Beightler was born in Marysville, Ohio, the son of William P. Beightler, President of the Perfect Cigar Company, and Joana Sprague, daughter of [[Franklin B. Sprague]]. His brothers were [[Robert S. Beightler]] and Donald,
father of [[Charles S. Beightler|Dr. Charles S. Beightler]].<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=4F-qtvhO5ewC&pg=PA2&lpg=PA2&dq "Minuteman: The Military Career of General Robert S. Beightler"], John Kennedy Ohl. Lynne Rienner Publishers, 2001. p. 4</ref>
[[File:USS_Electra_(AKA-4)_underway_in_the_1950s.jpg|thumb|left|150px|Beightler commanded the USS Electra in the Pacific Theater from June 1943 to September 1944.]]

==College and military==
In 1916, he enrolled at the [[Ohio State University]] and was a member of the freshmen basketball squad and Alpha Sigma fraternity.<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=nxJMAAAAMAAJ&pg=PA530&lpg=PA530&dq "Caduceus, Volume 31, Issue 5"], Kappa Sigma Fraternity. 1916. p. 530</ref> Later that year he left the university and enrolled at the [[United States Naval Academy]] in [[Annapolis]].<ref>[https://archive.org/details/historyofohiosta42ohio "History of the Ohio state university"], Ohio State University; Mendenhall, Thomas. Ohio State University Press. 1920. p. 35</ref> He would graduate with the class of 1920 having achieved the rank of [[Midshipmen]].<ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=0iBIAQAAIAAJ&pg=RA2-PA60&dq "Annual register of the United States Naval Academy, Annapolis, Md"], United States Naval Academy. 1920. p. 60</ref><ref>[https://books.google.com/books?id=JMkcAQAAIAAJ&pg=PA383&lpg=PA383&dq "Register of Commissioned and Warrant Officers of the United States Navy and Marine Corps and Reserve Officers on Active Duty"], United States. Bureau of Naval Personnel. 1917. p. 173</ref>

By 1924 he was a Lieutenant instructing on the [[USS Farquhar (DD-304)|USS Farquhar]] in [[Florida]],<ref>[https://archive.org/details/navydirectoryoff19242unit "Navy directory : officers of the United States Navy and Marine Corps, also including officers of the U.S. Naval Reserve Force (active), Marine Corps Reserve (active), and foreign officers serving with the Navy"], Washington, D.C. : Bureau of Navigation, Navy Dept. March 1924. Retrieved 13 may 2017</ref> and by 1926 he training at the Great Lakes Station.<ref>[https://www.myheritage.com/research/record-90100-81744017/navy-directory-officers-of-the-united-states-navy-and-marine "Navy Directory : Officers of the United States Navy and Marine Corps, Apr 1926"], United States. Navy Dept. Bureau of Navigation, United States. Marine Corps. April 1926. p. 16.</ref>

==World War Two==
During World War Two, he would command the [[USS Electra (AKA-4)|USS Electra]] from June 23, 1943-September 20, 1944,<ref>[http://www.navsource.org/archives/10/02/02004.htm "USS Electra (AKA-4)"], NavSource Online: Amphibious Photo Archive. Retrieved 13 may 2017</ref><ref>[http://ravey.net/electra/skippers.html "USS ELECTRA AKA-4"], Ravey. Retrieved 13 may 2017</ref> and [[USS Rawlins (APA-226)|USS Rawlins]] from November 11, 1944-April 1, 1946<ref>[http://www.navsource.org/archives/10/03/03226.htm "USS Rawlins (APA-226)"], NavSource Online: Amphibious Photo Archive. Retrieved 13 may 2017</ref> in the [[Pacific Theater of World War II|Pacific Theater]], earning a [[Bronze Star]] for extraordinary achievement.<ref>George Carroll Dyer. ''The Amphibians Came to Conquer: The Story of Admiral Richmond Kelly Turner, Volume 2''. U.S. Department of the Navy, 1972. p. 879</ref><ref name=V>[http://valor.militarytimes.com/recipient.php?recipientid=315016 "Charles Sprague Beightler"], Military Times. Retrieved 13 may 2017</ref>

==Personal life==
He was married to Bonita Pennicke (January 28, 1901-May 31, 1981), a United States Naval Nurse.<ref>[https://www.findagrave.com/cgibin/fg.cgipage=gr&GSln=beightler&GSfn=charles&GSbyrel=all&GSdyrel=all&GScntry=4&GSob=n&GRid=35561379&df=all& "Charles Sprague Beightler"], Find A Grave. April 5. 2009. Retrieved 13 may 2017</ref> He passed on August 19, 1982 in [[El Cajon, California]],<ref name=V /> and is interned at [[Arlington National Cemetery]].<ref>[https://billiongraves.com/grave/Charles-Sprague-Beightler/9676361#/ "Charles Sprague Beightler"], Billion Graves. June-August 2014. Retrieved 13 may 2014</ref>
