'''Kobuleti Managed Reserve''' ({{lang-ka|ქობულეთი}}) is a [[List of protected areas of Georgia|protected area]] in [[Kobuleti Municipality]], [[Adjara]] region of [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]] along the [[Black Sea]] coast in the northern part of the resort town [[Kobuleti]].<ref name="protectedplanet">{{cite web|url=https://www.protectedplanet.net/kobuleti-managed-reserve|title=Kobuleti in Georgia|website=Protected Planet|accessdate=2018-11-02}}</ref>  
''Kobuleti Protected Areas''  were  established in 1998 to preserve  unique wetland ecosystems recognized by the [[Ramsar Convention]].

''Kobuleti Protected Areas'' include '''Kobuleti Managed Reserve''' and [[Kobuleti Strict Nature Reserve]] located on left and right banks of Shavi Ghele (''black creek'') river respectively.<ref name="apa">{{cite web|url=http://apa.gov.ge/en/protected-areas/cattestone/qobuletis-daculi-teritoriebis-administracia|title=Kobuleti Protected Areas Administration - Agency of Protected Areas of Georgia|website=apa.gov.ge|accessdate=2018-11-09}}</ref>

== Geography ==
Reserve has flat surface with minor erosion by the rivers of Shavi Ghele  and Togoni and by a few water drains. It is a[[peat]] [[bog]], consisting mainly of [[peat moss]] with hardly noticeable surface elevations which are called locally ''peat domes''. These minor elevations rise 1–3 m above it surroundings. The bog in this area is composed of a single [[peat]] layer with thickness from 5 m up to 9 m.<ref name="vision">[https://www.econatura.nl/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/National-Part-of-the-Black-Sea-vision-GEORGIA.pdf The Black Sea coastal wetlands vision. Georgia] Black Sea Regional Initiative for the wise use of Coastal Wetlands. March 2008 Batumi, Georgia</ref>

== History and culture ==
Kabuleti is an ancient cultural and trading center with many tourist attractions. 
In [[late antiquity]]  major trading routes where crisscrossing in Kobuleti. [[Christianity]] was introduced here by [[St. Andrew the Apostle]] in the 1st century.

Since the 1950s local population started building sophisticated houses with huge stairs and balconies. The  size of the  house was indicative to well-being of the family and represented major investment. Villages around Kobuleti till present day has examples of traditional [[Adjara|Achara]] and [[Lazeti]] dwellings. In vicinity of Kobuleti there are ruins of castles and bridges from [[early middle ages]].<ref>[http://www.georgianholidays.com/sights/protected-areas/kobuleti-protected-area Kobuleti protected area] Travel Company Georgian Holidays</ref>

== Tourist attraction ==
Kobuleti Managed Reserve arranged trails, so that visitors can carry out educational tours. It is possible to rent skis and walk along [[sphagnum bog]].
