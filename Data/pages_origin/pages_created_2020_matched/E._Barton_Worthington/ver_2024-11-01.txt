'''Edgar Barton Worthington''' (13 January 1905{{snd}}14 October 2001) was a British [[Ecology|ecologist]] and science administrator.

==Biography==
His parents were Edgar and Amy Worthington. His early education was at [[Rugby School]], before he went up to gain a First in [[Zoology]] at [[Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge|Gonville and Cauis College]] at [[University of Cambridge|Cambridge]].<ref name = Papers>{{cite web | url = http://www.bodley.ox.ac.uk/dept/scwmss/wmss/online/blcas/worthington-eb.html | title = Papers of Edgar Barton Worthington 1912-1984 | accessdate = 7 December 2018 | publisher = Bodleian Library, University of Oxford | author1 = Ganzenm√ºller, Katja | author2 = Lowman, Marion  | author3 = McCann, Lucy | year = 2017}}</ref> After university, his work alternated between Britain and Africa. He took part in an African lakes expedition in 1927{{ndash}}31; and in an African research expedition 1934{{ndash}}37, for which he was awarded the [[Mungo Park Medal]] of the [[Royal Scottish Geographical Society]].

He was secretary to and first full-time director of the [[Freshwater Biological Association]] 1937{{ndash}}46. He returned to Africa in the late 1940s as science and development advisor. He was deputy scientific director for the [[Nature Conservancy (UK)|Nature Conservancy]] 1957{{ndash}}65, and scientific director of the [[International Biological Programme]] (IBP) 1964{{ndash}}74. His interests included water biology and international nature conservation, including the environmental impacts of drainage and irrigation. He was appointed [[Commander of the Order of the British Empire|CBE]] in 1977.<ref>{{cite book|title=The New Naturalists|first=Peter|last=Marren|date=1995

In 1930, Worthington married [[Stella Worthington|Stella Johnson]], who had been a member of the earlier expeditions he had undertaken and who shared many of his interests. In 1933, they jointly published the book ''Inland Waters of Africa''. They had three daughters, all of whom followed their parents' scientific inclinations. Stella Worthington died in 1978. Two years later, he married Harriet Stockton. He died on 14 December 2001.<ref name = Papers/>
