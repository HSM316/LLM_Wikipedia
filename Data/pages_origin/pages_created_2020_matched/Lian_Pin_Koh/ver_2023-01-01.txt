'''Lian Pin Koh''' (born 1976 in [[Singapore]]) is a Singaporean conservation scientist. He is the Kwan Im Thong Hood Cho Temple Professor of Conservation, Vice Dean of Research at the [http://www.science.nus.edu.sg/ Faculty of Science], Director of the [http://www.nus.edu.sg/cncs Centre for Nature-based Climate Solutions], and Director of the Tropical Marine Science Institute at the [[National University of Singapore]].

Koh was formerly the [http://researchers.adelaide.edu.au/profile/lianpin.koh Chair of Applied Ecology and Conservation] at the [[University of Adelaide]], and Vice President of Science Partnerships and Innovation at [[Conservation International]]. He was also the Founding Director of [[Conservation Drones|ConservationDrones.org]], a non-profit organisation that seeks to introduce drone technology to conservation scientists and practitioners worldwide.

Koh has received multiple awards including the [[Australian Research Council|Australian Research Council Future Fellowship]] in 2014, the [[Swiss National Science Foundation|Swiss National Science Foundation Professorship]] in 2011, the [[ETH Zurich|ETH]] Fellowship in 2008, and was also named a [[Young Global Leaders|World Economic Forum Young Global Leader]] in 2013.

In 2020, Koh received a prestigious award from the [https://www.nrf.gov.sg/home National Research Foundation of the Singapore Prime Minister's Office] under its [https://www.nrf.gov.sg/programmes/returning-singaporean-scientists-scheme Returning Singaporean Scientists Scheme]. Established in 2013, the Scheme seeks to attract outstanding overseas-based Singaporean research leaders back to Singapore to take up leadership positions in Singapore's autonomous universities and publicly funded research institutes. Koh was the sixth recipient of the award.

On 14 January 2021, Koh was appointed as one of nine [[Nominated Members of Parliament]] (NMP) in the [[14th Parliament of Singapore]], which began on 21 January 2021.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Abdullah |first1=Zhaki |title=Nine new Nominated Members of Parliament to be appointed |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/singapore/new-nmp-nominated-members-of-parliament-13959126 |website=CNA |access-date=19 January 2021 |date=14 January 2021}}</ref>

== Education and Career ==
Koh studied at [[Hwa Chong Institution]] (formerly the Chinese High School and Hwa Chong Junior College) for his pre-tertiary education in Singapore.

He completed his Bachelor of Science (with First Class Honours) and Master of Science degrees at the [[National University of Singapore]] in 2001 and 2003, respectively.

Koh received his PhD from the [https://eeb.princeton.edu/ Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at Princeton University] in 2008.<ref>{{cite web|title=Meet the Team|url=http://scholar.princeton.edu/dwilcove/meet-team|language=en}}</ref>

Following that, he received postdoctoral training at [[ETH Zurich]], and in 2011, was appointed an Assistant Professor by the [[Swiss National Science Foundation]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Swissplantscience: Home|url=https://swissplantscienceweb.ch/nc/research/home/portfolio/koh/|website=swissplantscienceweb.ch|language=en}}</ref>

In 2014, he accepted a position at the [[University of Adelaide]] as Associate Professor of Applied Ecology & Conservation. He was subsequently awarded the Australian Research Council Future Fellowship (Level II)<ref>{{cite web|title=Future Fellowships|url=http://www.arc.gov.au/future-fellowships|website=www.arc.gov.au|language=en|date=27 February 2014}}</ref> and was promoted to full Professor in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|title=Lian Pin Koh {{!}} Researcher Profiles|url=http://researchers.adelaide.edu.au/profile/lianpin.koh|website=researchers.adelaide.edu.au|language=en}}</ref> Koh helped set up and served as Director of two University research centers: the [http://www.adelaide.edu.au/environment/acs/ Centre for Applied Conservation Science], and the [http://www.adelaide.edu.au/environment/uraf/ Unmanned Research Aircraft Facility].

From 2018 to 2020, Koh took a hiatus from academia to join [[Conservation International]].

In 2020, Koh accepted a position as tenured full Professor at his alma mater at the [[National University of Singapore]].

== Research ==
Koh's scientific contributions include the study of [[Coextinction|species co-extinctions]]<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Koh|first1=L. P.|title=Species Coextinctions and the Biodiversity Crisis|journal=Science|date=10 September 2004|volume=305|issue=5690|pages=1632–1634|doi=10.1126/science.1101101|pmid=15361627|s2cid=30713492}}</ref> and modeling the environmental impacts of industrial agriculture across the tropics.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Koh|first1=Lian Pin|last2=Wilcove|first2=David S.|title=Cashing in palm oil for conservation|journal=Nature|date=30 August 2007|volume=448|issue=7157|pages=993–994|doi=10.1038/448993a|pmid=17728739|s2cid=4399026}}</ref>

His research focuses on developing innovative science and science-based decision support tools to reconcile societal needs with environmental protection.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Koh|first1=L. P.|last2=Ghazoul|first2=J.|title=Spatially explicit scenario analysis for reconciling agricultural expansion, forest protection, and carbon conservation in Indonesia|journal=Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences|date=28 May 2010|volume=107|issue=24|pages=11140–11144|doi=10.1073/pnas.1000530107|pmc=2890708|pmid=20511535|doi-access=free}}</ref> He addresses this challenge through field studies<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Koh|first1=Lian Pin|title=Can oil palm plantations be made more hospitable for forest butterflies and birds?|journal=Journal of Applied Ecology|date=August 2008|volume=45|issue=4|pages=1002–1009|doi=10.1111/j.1365-2664.2008.01491.x|doi-access=free}}</ref> and experiments,<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Koh|first1=Lian Pin|title=Birds Defend Oil Palms from Herbivorous Insects|journal=Ecological Applications|date=June 2008|volume=18|issue=4|pages=821–825|doi=10.1890/07-1650.1|pmid=18536244}}</ref> computer simulations and modelling,<ref>{{cite journal|last1=KOH|first1=LIAN PIN|last2=GHAZOUL|first2=JABOURY|title=A Matrix-Calibrated Species-Area Model for Predicting Biodiversity Losses Due to Land-Use Change|journal=Conservation Biology|date=5 March 2010|volume=24|issue=4|pages=994–1001|doi=10.1111/j.1523-1739.2010.01464.x|pmid=20214672}}</ref> as well as by co-opting emerging technologies for use in environmental research and applications.<ref>{{cite web|title=ConservationDrones.org|url=http://conservationdrones.org|website=conservationdrones.org}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|last1=van Andel|first1=Alexander C.|last2=Wich|first2=Serge A.|last3=Boesch|first3=Christophe|last4=Koh|first4=Lian Pin|last5=Robbins|first5=Martha M.|last6=Kelly|first6=Joseph|last7=Kuehl|first7=Hjalmar S.|title=Locating chimpanzee nests and identifying fruiting trees with an unmanned aerial vehicle|journal=American Journal of Primatology|date=October 2015|volume=77|issue=10|pages=1122–1134|doi=10.1002/ajp.22446|pmid=26179423|s2cid=23264358}}</ref>

His more recent research as Director of the [http://www.nus.edu.sg/cncs Centre for Nature-based Climate Solutions] seeks to produce policy-relevant science on nature-based climate solutions – tackling climate change by protecting and better managing natural ecosystems – to address knowledge gaps, build capacity and deliver pragmatic solutions and innovations to inform climate policies, strategies and actions to achieve the Centre’s vision.
