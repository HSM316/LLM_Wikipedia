'''Lian Pin Koh''' (born 1976 in [[Singapore]]) is a conservation biologist based in the United States. He works for [[Conservation International]] as Principal (Vice President), Science Partnerships and Innovation.

Koh is also an [http://researchers.adelaide.edu.au/profile/lianpin.koh Adjunct Professor of Applied Ecology and Conservation] at the [[University of Adelaide]], and Founding Director of [[Conservation Drones|ConservationDrones.org]]. 

Throughout his career, Koh has received multiple awards including the [[Australian Research Council|Australian Research Council Future Fellowship]], the [[Swiss National Science Foundation|Swiss National Science Foundation Professorship]], and was also named a [[List_of_Young_Global_Leaders#2013|World Economic Forum Young Global Leader]] in 2013.

== Career ==
Koh was awarded a Ph.D. in [[Ecology and Evolutionary Biology]] by Princeton University in 2008<ref>{{cite web|title=Meet the Team|url=http://scholar.princeton.edu/dwilcove/meet-team|language=en}}</ref>. Following that, he received Post-Doctoral training at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in Switzerland ([[ETH Zurich]]). In 2011, Koh was appointed an Assistant Professor by the [[Swiss National Science Foundation]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Swissplantscience: Home|url=https://swissplantscienceweb.ch/nc/research/home/portfolio/koh/|website=swissplantscienceweb.ch|language=en}}</ref>

In 2014, he was recruited by the [[University of Adelaide]] in Australia to take on the position of Associate Professor of Applied Ecology & Conservation. He was subsequently awarded the prestigious Australian Research Council Future Fellowship (Level II).<ref>{{cite web|title=Future Fellowships|url=http://www.arc.gov.au/future-fellowships|website=www.arc.gov.au|language=en|date=27 February 2014}}</ref>, and was promoted to full Professor in 2017<ref>{{cite web|title=Lian Pin Koh {{!}} Researcher Profiles|url=http://researchers.adelaide.edu.au/profile/lianpin.koh|website=researchers.adelaide.edu.au|language=en}}</ref>. Koh helped set up and served as Director of two University research centers: the [http://www.adelaide.edu.au/environment/acs/ Centre for Applied Conservation Science], and the [http://www.adelaide.edu.au/environment/uraf/ Unmanned Research Aircraft Facility].

In 2018, Koh took a hiatus from academia to joined [[Conservation International]].

== Research ==
Koh is an applied ecologist whose notable scientific contributions include the study of [[Coextinction|species co-extinctions]]<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Koh|first1=L. P.|title=Species Coextinctions and the Biodiversity Crisis|journal=Science|date=10 September 2004|volume=305|issue=5690|pages=1632–1634|doi=10.1126/science.1101101}}</ref> and modeling the environmental impacts of industrial agriculture across the tropics.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Koh|first1=Lian Pin|last2=Wilcove|first2=David S.|title=Cashing in palm oil for conservation|journal=Nature|date=30 August 2007|volume=448|issue=7157|pages=993–994|doi=10.1038/448993a}}</ref>

His research focuses on developing the Science and Science-based decision support tools to help reconcile society’s growing consumptive needs with environmental protection<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Koh|first1=L. P.|last2=Ghazoul|first2=J.|title=Spatially explicit scenario analysis for reconciling agricultural expansion, forest protection, and carbon conservation in Indonesia|journal=Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences|date=28 May 2010|volume=107|issue=24|pages=11140–11144|doi=10.1073/pnas.1000530107|pmc=2890708|pmid=20511535}}</ref>. He addresses this challenge through field studies<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Koh|first1=Lian Pin|title=Can oil palm plantations be made more hospitable for forest butterflies and birds?|journal=Journal of Applied Ecology|date=August 2008|volume=45|issue=4|pages=1002–1009|doi=10.1111/j.1365-2664.2008.01491.x}}</ref> and experiments<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Koh|first1=Lian Pin|title=BIRDS DEFEND OIL PALMS FROM HERBIVOROUS INSECTS|journal=Ecological Applications|date=June 2008|volume=18|issue=4|pages=821–825|doi=10.1890/07-1650.1}}</ref>, computer simulations and modelling<ref>{{cite journal|last1=KOH|first1=LIAN PIN|last2=GHAZOUL|first2=JABOURY|title=A Matrix-Calibrated Species-Area Model for Predicting Biodiversity Losses Due to Land-Use Change|journal=Conservation Biology|date=5 March 2010|volume=24|issue=4|pages=994–1001|doi=10.1111/j.1523-1739.2010.01464.x}}</ref>, as well as by co-opting emerging technologies for use in environmental research and applications<ref>{{cite web|title=ConservationDrones.org|url=http://conservationdrones.org|website=conservationdrones.org}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|last1=van Andel|first1=Alexander C.|last2=Wich|first2=Serge A.|last3=Boesch|first3=Christophe|last4=Koh|first4=Lian Pin|last5=Robbins|first5=Martha M.|last6=Kelly|first6=Joseph|last7=Kuehl|first7=Hjalmar S.|title=Locating chimpanzee nests and identifying fruiting trees with an unmanned aerial vehicle|journal=American Journal of Primatology|date=October 2015|volume=77|issue=10|pages=1122–1134|doi=10.1002/ajp.22446}}</ref>.

Geographically his research is focused on the developing tropics where population growth is most rapid, the people are poorest, and biodiversity is richest and yet most threatened globally.

Koh has partnered with nongovernmental organisations, policymakers, industry players, social scientists, environmental economists, computer engineers and software developers. He has co-authored scientific publications with more than 100 colleagues from over 20 nations, and from top research institutions, including [[Harvard University]], [[Princeton University]], [[Yale University]], [[Stanford University]], [[Cambridge University]], [[ETH Zurich]], and the [[Chinese Academy of Sciences]].

His research has received over 15,00 citations by his peers (with an h-index of >55)<ref>{{cite web|title=Lian Pin Koh - Google Scholar Citations|url=https://scholar.google.com.sg/citations?hl=en&user=KR3AV20AAAAJ|website=scholar.google.com.sg}}</ref>. Koh is the most highly cited Singaporean ecologist. His work has real and significant impacts in both academia and civil society. He was bestowed the honor of a [[List_of_Young_Global_Leaders#2013|Young Global Leader by the World Economic Forum in 2013]], for “... his record of professional accomplishments, commitment to society and potential to contribute to shaping the future of the world through his inspiring leadership”. Koh joins other leaders across the realms of politics, business, and the arts, including actor [[Leonardo DiCaprio]], Google co-founders [[Larry Page]] and [[Sergei Brin]], and Facebook founder [[Mark Zuckerberg]].

Koh is a pioneer in the use of low-cost drone technology for environmental applications<ref>{{cite web|last1=Koh|first1=Lian Pin|title=A drone's-eye view of conservation|url=https://www.ted.com/talks/lian_pin_koh_a_drone_s_eye_view_of_conservation|language=en}}</ref>. He is Founding Director of [http://ConservationDrones.org ConservationDrones.org], a non-profit organisation that seeks to introduce drone technology to scientists and conservationists worldwide. ConservationDrones.org has received numerous awards and media coverage (>650,000 visits to its website)<ref>{{cite web|title=ConservationDrones.org|url=http://conservationdrones.org|website=conservationdrones.org}}</ref>.

Koh was a speaker at the 2013 TED-Global conference in Edinburgh, where he spoke on the positive use of drones<ref>{{cite web|last1=Koh|first1=Lian Pin|title=A drone's-eye view of conservation|url=https://www.ted.com/talks/lian_pin_koh_a_drone_s_eye_view_of_conservation|language=en}}</ref>. His [[TED talk]] has been viewed over 600,000 times by a global audience.

== Outreach ==
Koh is a regular plenary speaker at numerous international meetings, including the WWF Fuller Symposium in Washington D.C. in 2012<ref>{{cite web|title=Speakers {{!}} Pages {{!}} WWF|url=http://www.worldwildlife.org/pages/speakers|website=World Wildlife Fund|language=en}}</ref>, the Clinton Global Initiative University in Florida in 2013<ref>{{cite web|title=CGI University 2015 - Agenda Day 2|url=https://www.clintonfoundation.org/clinton-global-initiative/meetings/cgi-university/2015/agenda/day-2|website=Clinton Foundation|language=en}}</ref>, and the Intergovernmental Eye on Earth Summit in Abu Dhabi in 2015<ref>{{cite web|title=Eye on Earth Summit 2015 to be held under royal patronage of The President HH Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan {{!}} Eye On Earth|url=http://www.eoesummit.org/blog/news/eye-on-earth-summit-2015-to-be-held-under-royal-patronage-of-the-president-hh-sheikh-khalifa-bin-zayed-al-nahyan/|website=www.eoesummit.org}}</ref>.

He is passionate about the communication of Science to the public, as evidenced by numerous and regular features on his work by international media, including the New York Times<ref>{{cite web|last1=Gorman|first1=James|title=Drones on a Different Mission|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/22/science/drones-on-a-different-mission.html?_r=0|website=The New York Times|date=21 July 2014}}</ref>, Smithsonian Magazine<ref>{{cite web|last1=Siber|first1=Kate|title=The One Use of Drones Everyone Can Agree on, Except for Poachers|url=http://www.smithsonianmag.com/science-nature/one-use-drones-everyone-can-agree-except-poachers-180950078/|website=Smithsonian|language=en}}</ref>, Scientific American<ref>{{cite web|last1=Platt|first1=John R.|title=Eye in the Sky: Drones Help Conserve Sumatran Orangutans and Other Wildlife|url=https://blogs.scientificamerican.com/extinction-countdown/drones-help-conserve-sumatran-orangutans-wildlife/|website=Scientific American Blog Network|language=en}}</ref>, NewScientist<ref>{{cite web|title=Tropical forests axed in favour of palm oil|url=https://www.newscientist.com/article/mg19826582-900-tropical-forests-axed-in-favour-of-palm-oil/|website=New Scientist}}</ref>, the Telegraph<ref>{{cite web|last1=Wood|first1=By Ian|title=Palm oil boycott will not protect rainforests|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/earth/earthnews/3340135/Palm-oil-boycott-will-not-protect-rainforests.html|website=Telegraph.co.uk|language=en}}</ref>, among others.

== Residences ==
Since leaving [[Singapore]] in 2004, Koh has lived in [[Princeton, New Jersey|Princeton]] (New Jersey), [[Zurich]] (Switzerland), [[Adelaide]] (Australia), and [[Seattle]] (Washington).
