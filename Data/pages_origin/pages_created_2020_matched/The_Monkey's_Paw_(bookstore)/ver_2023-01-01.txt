'''The Monkey's Paw''' is an independent used [[Bookselling|bookstore]] in [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]], [[Canada]] known for its eclectic, arcane, and absurd books, and for the [[Biblio-Mat]], a random book vending machine.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2022-03-06 |title=Bloor West’s Monkey’s Paw bookshop specializes in bizarre subjects |language=en-CA |work=The Toronto Star |url=https://www.thestar.com/life/together/places/2022/03/06/bloor-wests-monkeys-paw-bookshop-specializes-in-bizarre-subjects.html |access-date=2022-04-05 |issn=0319-0781}}</ref>[[File:The Biblio-Mat (cropped).jpg|alt=The Biblio-Mat vending machine.|thumb|The Monkey's Paw [[Biblio-Mat]]]]

Owner Stephen Fowler founded The Monkey's Paw in 2006, four years after he moved to Toronto from [[San Francisco]], where he worked in numerous bookstores.<ref name="TS">{{citation |last=Marlow |first=Iain |title=A treasure trove of weirdness |date=February 24, 2009 |url=https://www.thestar.com/news/gta/article/592047--a-treasure-trove-of-weirdness |journal=Toronto Star}}</ref><ref name="TT" /> Fowler opened The Monkey's Paw in order to support his family with another income source.<ref name="TS" /> The inspiration and book selection methods of the store is based on his work in a previous San Francisco bookstore that placed books that were too unique, specific, or unusual for a single-subject category under a miscellaneous section "Floop".<ref name="TS" /> Many, including the owner, consider the store itself a showcase and homage to the printed [[book]], which they believe to be dying [[Medium (communication)|medium]].<ref name="TM">{{citation |last=Maclear |first=Kyo |title=The Future of the Book:The Beautiful Afterlife of Dead Books |date=March 12, 2012 |url=http://www.themillions.com/2012/03/the-beautiful-afterlife-of-dead-books.html |journal=The Millions}}</ref><ref name="TT">{{citation |first=Chris |last=Randle |journal=Toronto Standard |date=June 8, 2012 |title=Text/Book: Obscure Obsessions|url=http://www.torontostandard.com/culture/text-book-obscure-obsessions}}</ref> Due to the notoriety and uniqueness of the bookstore, Fowler was invited to join the [[International League of Antiquarian Booksellers]].<ref name="TM" />

[[The New York Times Style Magazine]] quoted the owner, "It's the store where you'll find the book you didn't know you were looking for."<ref>{{Cite web |last=Rosen |first=Jody |date=2013-03-07 |title=An Oddly Modern Antiquarian Bookshop |url=https://tmagazine.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/03/07/an-oddly-modern-antiquarian-bookshop/ |access-date=2022-04-05 |website=T Magazine |language=en-US}}</ref>
