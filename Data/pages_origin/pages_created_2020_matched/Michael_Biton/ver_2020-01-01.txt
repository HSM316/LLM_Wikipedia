'''Michael Biton''' is an Israeli politician, serving as mayor of [[Yeruham]]. He was born and raised in the town.<ref name=":1">{{Cite news|url=https://www.haaretz.com/opinion/small-town-mayor-can-revive-zionism-1.5410306|title=The Small-town Mayor Who Could Rekindle Zionism|date=18 October 2015|work=Haaretz|access-date=28 October 2018|language=en}}</ref> 
His parents are immigrants from [[Morocco]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=http://in.bgu.ac.il/en/now/Pages/Our-Michael.aspx|title=BGUnow Online - Our Michael|website=in.bgu.ac.il|language=en-US|access-date=29 October 2018}}</ref> He has an M.A. in Organizational Leadership from [[Hebrew University of Jerusalem]], a B.A. in Behavioral Studies and Hebrew Literature from [[Ben-Gurion University of the Negev]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.khforum.org/2014/speakers/Michael_Biton.pdf|title=Michael Biton|last=|first=|date=|website=Keren Hayesod Forum|archive-url=|access-date=28 October 2018}}</ref><ref name=":2" /> He also studied English at [[Yale University]] and worked as an Au Pair in the US.<ref name=":2" /> Formerly a member of [[Kadima]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.israelnationalnews.com/News/Flash.aspx/198526|title=Kadima Wins in Yeruham City Elections - Israel National News|website=Israel National News|language=en|access-date=28 October 2018}}</ref> and [[Israeli Labor Party|Labor]].<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.jewishpress.com/news/israel/jerusalem/daughter-of-shas-party-founder-establishes-new-party/2018/07/30/|title=Daughter of Shas Party Founder Rav Ovadia Establishes New Party|last=Agency|first=TPS / Tazpit News|access-date=29 October 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> Biton has been the Mayor of [[Yeruham]] since his victory in November 2010. He has worked with [[Adina Bar-Shalom]] to form a new political party called Achi Yisraeli to run in the [[Next Israeli legislative election]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.israelnationalnews.com/News/News.aspx/249750|title=Rabbi Ovadiah Yosef's daughter registers new political party|website=Israel National News|language=en|access-date=28 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.jpost.com/Israel-News/Barak-bashes-Benny-Gantz-Gabbay-critics-569124|title=Barak bashes Benny Gantz, Gabbay critics|work=The Jerusalem Post {{!}} JPost.com|access-date=29 October 2018}}</ref> Since becoming mayor, Biton has been credited with Yeruham's turn around from a corrupt government to an up and coming city. <ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.jpost.com/Jerusalem-Report/Mayor-Biton-Social-warrior-450966|title=Mayor Biton: Social warrior|work=The Jerusalem Post {{!}} JPost.com|access-date=28 October 2018}}</ref> He left Achi Yisraeli due to a leadership dispute with Bar-Shalom.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jpost.com/Israel-News/Adina-Bar-Shalom-to-head-nascent-Achi-Israeli-party-575202|title=Adina Bar-Shalom to head nascent Achi Israeli party - Israel News - Jerusalem Post|website=www.jpost.com|access-date=28 December 2018}}</ref> Biton is currently a member of the Knesset with the [[Israel Resilience Party]] as part of the [[Blue and White (political alliance)|Blue and White]] list.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.timesofisrael.com/israel-resilience-party-reveals-top-picks-for-knesset-slate/|title=Israel Resilience party reveals top picks for Knesset slate|last=Bachner|first=Michael|website=www.timesofisrael.com|language=en-US|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref>

Before his political career he was an officer in the [[Israeli Defense Force]] and featured in the documentary "To Be An Officer". After his studies he was head of Yeruham's local community center, managed the Jewish Agency's [[Be'er Seva]] District, and founded the nonprofit Youth of Yeruham.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.haaretz.com/1.5122820|title=Back to the Ballot Box|last=Yagna|first=Yanir|date=8 October 2010|work=Haaretz|access-date=29 October 2018|last2=Zuta|first2=Rutie|language=en}}</ref>

Biton is considered a rising star in Israeli Politics who has spoken at the [[J Street]] conference and met with Palestinian President [[Mahmoud Abbas]].<ref name=":1" /> He won his first election to lead Yeruham's town council by around 45% in 2010, but one his re-election in 2014 by nearly 70%.<ref name=":1" />

He is a well-known leader in the fight against [[Economic inequality|income inequality]] and is a leader in the national "Equality March" which started in Yeruham. <ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nif.org/stories/social-and-economic-justice/equality-march-fairer-budget-allocation/|title=Equality March for Fairer Budget Allocation - New Israel Fund (NIF)|work=New Israel Fund (NIF)|access-date=29 October 2018|language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2014 he signed a landmark agreement to transfer the Camp Ariel Sharon training complex from the jurisdiction of the [[Ramat Negev Regional Council]] to that of Yeruham.<ref name=":2" />

He came in 2nd in the election to be the head of the Israeli branch of the [[Jewish National Fund]]. 238 votes behind the victor, Labor MK [[Danny Atar|Dani Atar]], but 112 votes ahead of the incumbent, Efi Stenzler.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jpost.com/Israel-News/Atar-elected-new-head-of-KKL-427449|title=Atar Elected New Head of KKL|last=Stern Hoffman|first=Gil|date=19 October 2015|website=[[The Jerusalem Post]]|access-date=28 October 2018}}</ref>
