'''Kevin Pauza''' is an American [[physiatrist]] and interventional spine specialist. He is the co-founder of the Texas Spine and Joint Hospital located in [[Tyler, Texas]].<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Swanson |first1=Holly |title=Alumni Profile: Kevin Pauza |journal=Penn State Medicine |date= October 2008 |volume=18 |issue=2 |page=44 |<!--accessdate=17 December 2019-->}}</ref> Pauza developed the Discseel procedure<ref>{{cite journal| title=BACK PAIN: IDET procedure proven effective in randomized study| date=October 21, 2002| page=4| journal=Health and Medicine Week}}</ref> for the treatment of spinal disorders known as the Pauza Disc Treatment,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Massey |first1=Bob |title=New procedure may offer alternative to spine surgery |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130531061236/http:/feelingfit.com/jointandotherpain/topic/11742-8/story |website=Charlotte Sun |accessdate=August 13, 2015}}</ref> which claims to repair re-grow discs without a [[spinal fusion]].<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.nytimes.com/2000/10/24/health/for-back-pain-sufferers-a-technique-under-study-may-offer-hope.html?pagewanted=2| title=For Back Pain Sufferers, a Technique Under Study May Offer Hope| last1=Freeman| first1=Karen| date=October 24, 2000| publisher=New York Times| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref><ref name="cbs">{{cite web| url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/a-new-hope-for-back-pain-sufferers/2/| title=A new hope for back pain sufferers?| date= May 6, 2012| publisher=CBS News| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref> Pauza’s work has been featured on national news outlets<ref>{{cite web |last1=Esquivel |first1=Stephanie |title=East Texas doctors fight to prevent opioid use back pain and spinal disc degeneration.

==Early life==
Kevin Pauza was born on December 15, 1964, in Hershey, Pennsylvania, with twin sister Jill Ann Pauza. He and his three siblings grew up on his parents’ farm. His mother, Gail Ann Pauza,  was a registered nurse, and his father, William Vito Pauza, is a farmer, land conservationist,<ref>{{cite web |title=South Londonderry Township Planning Commission, Current Members |url=http://www.southlondonderry.org/planning-commission.html |website=South Londonderry Township |accessdate=16 December 2019}}</ref> and inventor.

==Career==
Pauza is a physiatrist and a founding partner of the Texas Spine and Joint Hospital in Tyler, Texas.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.newjerseynewsroom.com/healthquest/chronic-back-pain-spinal-fusion-may-be-wrong-surgery |title=Chronic Back Pain? Spinal Fusion May be the Wrong Surgery |last1=Weiss |first1=Frances |date=May 8, 2012 |publisher=New Jersey Newsroom |access-date=August 13, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150703075958/http://www.newjerseynewsroom.com/healthquest/chronic-back-pain-spinal-fusion-may-be-wrong-surgery |archivedate=July 3, 2015 }}</ref> Pauza received degrees from [[University of Pennsylvania]], [[Columbia University College of Physicians and Surgeons]], [[Penn State Milton S. Hershey Medical Center|Penn State College of Medicine]], and [[Lehigh University]].{{CN|date=November 2019}}

Outside of treating patients suffering from chronic lower back pain and degenerative disc disease, Pauza is on the editorial panel of several scientific journals, committees and boards.<ref name="penn">{{cite web| url=http://archive.constantcontact.com/fs059/1103344271496/archive/1105029022742.html| title=Groundbreaking Spinal Treatment Spurs Saudi Interest| date=March 2011| publisher=Penn State College of Medicine| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref> He established the John Dehaan Foundation which annually awards $250,000 for advances in nonsurgical spinal treatment.<ref name="penn"/> In 2008, Penn State College of Medicine recognized Pauza with the Alumni Fellow Award.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://tr22g12.aset.psu.edu/story/182531/2008/10/22/college-medicine-seeks-nominations-alumni-fellow-award| title=College of Medicine seeks nominations for Alumni Fellow Award| date=October 22, 2008| publisher=Pennsylvania State College of Medicine| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref>

==Pauza Disc Treatment==
Pauza developed the Pauza Disc Treatment and Biostat System,<ref name="nih">{{cite web| url=https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/results/NCT01011816| title=Treatment of Symptomatic Lumbar Internal Disc Disruption (IDD) With the Biostat System| publisher=U.S. National Institutes of Health| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref> a minimally invasive procedure that repairs and re-grows discs for the treatment of spinal pain and disorders including degenerative disc disease.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.skyvalleychronicle.com/BREAKING-NEWS/TEXAS-DOCTOR-FINDS-REVOLUTIONARY-TREATMENT-FOR-SEVERE-BACK-PAIN-991883| title=Texas Doctor finds Revolutionary Treatment for Backpain| date=May 6, 2012| publisher=Sky Valley Chronicle| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref> Pauza holds a series of 16 patents for a device and Discseel [[biologic medical product|biologic]] that support the treatment. The procedure involves injecting two components of blood plasma, [[thrombin]] and [[fibrinogen]], which combine to make fibrin, into damaged spinal discs<ref name="feel">{{cite web|url=http://feelingfit.com/jointandotherpain/topic/11742-8/story |title=New procedure may offer alternative to spine surgery |last1=Massey |first1=Bob |publisher=Charlotte Sun |access-date=August 13, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130531061236/http://feelingfit.com/jointandotherpain/topic/11742-8/story |archivedate=May 31, 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=http://thedartmouth.com/2010/04/02/cutting-edge-surgery-aids-student/| title=Cutting-edge surgery aids student| date=April 2, 2010| last=Karpis| first=Paulina| publisher=The Dartmouth| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref> for the treatment of spinal conditions such as [[degenerative disc disease]], [[back pain]] and chronic [[low back pain]], or internal disc disruption.<ref name="nih"/> The Biostat intradiscal fibrin sealant underwent multiple [[Food and Drug Administration]] studies to become an FDA approved Discseel biologic that reduces back pain and regrows degenerated spinal discs.<ref name="nih"/><ref>{{cite journal| title=Intradiscal Injection of Fibrin Sealant for the Treatment of Symptomatic Lumbar Internal Disc Disruption: Results of a Prospective Multicenter Pilot Study with 24-Month Follow-Up| date=January 2014| journal=Pain Medicine| volume=15| issue=1| pages=16–31| doi=10.1111/pme.12249}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://ryortho.iphost6.com/breaking/spinal-restoration-secures-funding-for-fda-process/ |title=Spinal Restoration Secures Funding for FDA Process |last=Eisner |first=Walter |date=October 26, 2012 |publisher=RRY Publications |access-date=August 13, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151223102054/http://ryortho.iphost6.com/breaking/spinal-restoration-secures-funding-for-fda-process/ |archivedate=December 23, 2015 }}</ref> On July 18, 2013, it was announced the results of its Phase III Investigational New Drug study had not met its success criteria, and injections of the Fibrin Sealant were not statistically significantly better than saline injections.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20130718005215/en/Spinal-Restoration-Announces-Disappointing-Phase-III-Study|title=Spinal Restoration, Inc. Announces Disappointing Phase III Study Results for the Biostat® System|date=2013-07-18|website=www.businesswire.com|language=en|access-date=2019-04-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://regenexx.com/blog/new-disc-treatment/|title=New Disc Treatment: DISCSEEL™ to Heal? - Regenexx Blog|date=2016-06-02|website=Regenexx|language=en-US|access-date=2019-10-05}}</ref> The procedure is being challenged by Christopher J. Centeno creator of the Regenexx Stem Cell Spinal Procedure. Centeno suggested that the research and data provided by Pauza is flawed and as of 2016, no real or medically relevant data has been provided to prove the<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://discseel.com/patient-resources/for-physicians.html|title=Early Fibrin Study vs New|website=discseel.com|language=en|access-date=2019-10-05}}</ref> cement biologic fibrin offered in the Discseel Procedure works or helps patients suffering from back and neck pain.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.benzinga.com/pressreleases/18/08/p12151470/discseel-procedure-takes-bold-steps-to-increase-availability-of-proced|title=Discseel™Procedure Takes Bold Steps to Increase Availability of Procedure|website=Benzinga|language=English|access-date=2019-10-05}}</ref>

Pauza served on the advisory board of several medical tech companies. He served as medical advisor overseas for royal families to which he provided treatment.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.cbs19.tv/story/27927846/saudi-arabian-king-abdullah-had-a-strong-link-to-east-texas| title=Saudi Arabian King Abdullah had a strong link to East Texas| last1=Morris| first1=Risa| date=January 23, 2015| publisher=KYTX19| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.kltv.com/story/27927437/late-saudi-king-abdullah-has-strong-connection-in-east-texas| title=Late Saudi King Abdullah has strong connection in East Texas| date=January 23, 2015| publisher=KLTV| access-date=August 13, 2015}}</ref>. <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tylerpaper.com/news/local/local-doctors-reprimanded-by-state-board/article_ab599338-6d2c-5dad-a8fc-c542f21ba082.html|title=2 local doctors reprimanded by state board|last=Reports|first=From Staff|website=TylerPaper.com|language=en|access-date=2019-10-05}}</ref> In 2013 Pauza was reprimanded by the Texas State Medical Board. The Medical Board found that Pauza failed to meet the standard of care, failed to follow the board's guidelines for the treatment of pain, failed to keep adequate medical records and failed to cooperate with board staff.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tylerpaper.com/news/local/tyler-doctor-among-disciplined-by-medical-board/article_86f30778-bb63-5850-bf7c-fdc7c250affd.html|title=Tyler doctor among 40 disciplined by medical board|website=TylerPaper.com|language=en|access-date=2019-10-05}}</ref> Two years later the Board “found Dr. Pauza’s medical records for the patient at issue were inadequate and that Dr. Pauza’s stated rationale for prescribing a medication as a sleep aid and for treating the patient with corticosteroid injections after a five-year treatment hiatus were inadequate.”<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tmb.state.tx.us/dl/547A504C-2D9B-C84D-8E9B-9D8306FA9877|title=Texas Medical Board Discipline Kevin Pauza for Risking Patients Helath|last=Schneider|first=Jarrett|date=September 10, 2018|website=Texas Medical Board|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>
