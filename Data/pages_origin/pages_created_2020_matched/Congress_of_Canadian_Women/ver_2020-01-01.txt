The '''Congress of Canadian Women''' was a [[feminist]] organization created in March 1950 by the merger of several organizations, including the Housewives and Consumers Association. The Congress lobbied for [[women's equality]] including measures such as [[equal pay]] and public [[daycare]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://famouscanadianwomen.com/timeline/timeline1950-1959.htm|title=Famous Canadian Women's Historic Timeline - 1950-1959|website=famouscanadianwomen.com}}</ref> [[Rae Luckock]] was the organization's founding president.<ref>https://www.collectionscanada.gc.ca/women/030001-1335-e.html</ref> Former [[Labor-Progressive Party]] [[House of Commons of Canada|Member of Parliament]] [[Dorise Nielsen]] was also involved in founding the organization.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://books.google.ca/books?id=e_hFKQeC3JgC&pg=PA215&lpg=PA215&dq=%22congress+of+Canadian+women%22+nielsen&source=bl&ots=Xyp_fmAVYV&sig=AJOEh9KSbY5AhfgcZ4vxwCsI6y4&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjXpKrL-Z7ZAhUSSK0KHTVDBaIQ6AEIKDAA#v=onepage&q=%22congress+of+Canadian+women%22+nielsen&f=false|title=A Great Restlessness: The Life and Politics of Dorise Nielsen|first=Faith|last=Johnston|date=11 February 2018|publisher=Univ. of Manitoba Press|via=Google Books}}</ref> The CCW was largely led by women associated with the communist [[Labor-Progressive Party]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://books.google.ca/books?id=0Fk9DgAAQBAJ&pg=PA206&lpg=PA206&dq=%22congress+of+Canadian+women%22+nielsen&source=bl&ots=6BWAA4ehc8&sig=nLxLuS4mncZV9X2ij98ALVqC2Cg&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjXpKrL-Z7ZAhUSSK0KHTVDBaIQ6AEILjAC#v=onepage&q=%22congress+of+Canadian+women%22+&f=false|title=Progressive Heritage: The Evolution of a Politically Radical Literary Tradition in Canada|first=James|last=Doyle|date=1 January 2006|publisher=Wilfrid Laurier Univ. Press|via=Google Books}}</ref> and was also involved with the [[peace movement]] during the [[Cold War]], facilitating meetings between people from the Soviet Union and Canadians, by inviting them to visit Canada.<ref name="CCW President">{{cite news|title=May visitors from Russia|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=viBUAAAAIBAJ&sjid=KjoNAAAAIBAJ&dq=rae%20luckock&pg=5219%2C2936835|accessdate=2012-03-03|newspaper=The Leader-Post|date=1956-01-23|author=Canadian Press|location=Regina, Saskatchewan|page=6}}</ref>
