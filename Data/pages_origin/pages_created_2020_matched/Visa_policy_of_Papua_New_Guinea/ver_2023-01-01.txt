A foreign national wishing to enter '''[[Papua New Guinea]]''' must obtain a [[visa (document)|visa]] in one of the [[List of diplomatic missions in Papua New Guinea|PNG diplomatic missions]], unless they are a citizen of one of the countries eligible for visa on arrival or eVisa. All visitors must hold a passport valid for 6 months.

==Visa on arrival==
Nationals of the following 71 countries and territories may obtain a free visa valid for 60 days (extension is possible for a fee) <ref>[http://www.immigration.gov.pg/images/documents/Publication-Revised%20MSF2.pdf REVISED MIGRATION SERVICE FEES (MSF)]</ref> on arrival at [[Port Moresby Airport|Port Moresby]] or [[Rabaul Airport|Tokua (Rabaul)]]:<ref name=Timatic>{{Timatic Visa Policy|country=PG|access-date=1 April 2017}}</ref><ref name=VOAlist>[http://www.immigration.gov.pg/images/documents/VOA%20Eligible%20Country%20List%20Public%20Notice%2031032014%20v2%201.pdf Visa on Arrival Eligible Country List (as at 27 March 2014)]</ref><ref>On 27 March 2014, the list was expanded with Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Estonia, Hungary, Israel, Latvia, Lithuania, Philippines, Poland, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia and Macao while Australia was removed on 1 March 2014.</ref>

Visa exemption agreement for diplomatic and service passports was signed with {{flag|China}} on 17 November 2018 and it is not yet ratified.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://postcourier.com.pg/png-china-signs-agreements/|title=PNG and China signs agreements|date=17 November 2018}}</ref>

Visa on arrival can also be obtained by citizens of [[China]] who are diplomatic, service, and passport for public affairs holders and ordinary passport holders travelling under organized tour groups sanctioned by the Tourism Promotion Authority.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20180913071036/http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2018-09/13/c_137464952.htm  PNG visa requirements relaxed for some Chinese visitors]</ref>

;History 
The visa on arrival policy was suspended from 30 October to 1 December 2018 due to the [[APEC Papua New Guinea 2018]] summit.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.immigration.gov.pg/images/documents/Media_Realeases/Public_Notices/Media%20Release-Suspension%20of%20VoA.pdf |title=MEDIA RELEASE PNG Immigration suspending visa on arrival to enhance border security for APEC|publisher=Papua New Guinea Immigration and Citizenship Authority|access-date=2018-10-15}}</ref>

In June 2019 it was announced that 20 additional countries from [[Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation|APEC]] region would become eligible for eVisa.<ref>[https://postcourier.com.pg/png-opens-e-visa-20-apec-economies/ PNG opens e-visa to 20 more APEC economies]</ref>

Australian passport holders are not eligible for visa on arrival for business visits.{{citation needed|date=July 2019}}

==APEC Business Travel Card==
