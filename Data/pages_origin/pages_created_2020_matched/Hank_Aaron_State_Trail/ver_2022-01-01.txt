The '''Hank Aaron State Trail''' is a 14-mile [[rail trail]] in [[Milwaukee County, Wisconsin]].

The trail is named for former [[Milwaukee Braves]] and [[Milwaukee Brewers]] [[right fielder]] [[Hank Aaron]], <ref name=dnr>{{cite web|url=http://dnr.wi.gov/topic/parks/name/hankaaron/ |title=Hank Aaron State Trail |publisher=Wisconsin DNR |access-date=August 15, 2016}}</ref> and was built on a former roadbed of the [[Chicago, Milwaukee, St. Paul and Pacific Railway]].

==Route==
The trail begins on the shore of [[Lake Michigan]] in [[Lakeshore State Park]] ({{coord|43.03723|-87.89749|format=dms|type:landmark_region:US-WI|display=inline|name=Hank Aaron State Trail, eastern trailhead}}). The trail travels west through Milwaukee, passing by the [[Harley-Davidson Museum]], [[Potawatomi Hotel & Casino]], [[Miller Park (Milwaukee)|Miller Park]], and the [[Wisconsin State Fair Park]]. The trail ends at the [[Oak Leaf Trail]] in [[Wauwatosa, Wisconsin]] ({{coord|43.03605|-88.06658|format=dms|type:landmark_region:US-WI|display=inline|name=Hank Aaron State Trail, western trailhead}}).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hankaaronstatetrail.org/pdf/2014-HAST-full-map-brochure.pdf |title=Hank Aaron State Trail Map |publisher=Friends of Hank Aaron State Trail |access-date=August 15, 2016}}</ref> The trail is 14 miles long, is paved, and is mostly off road.

View a map of the trail [https://www.hankaaronstatetrail.org/maps here].

==Access==
The trail is open to walkers, joggers, bicyclists, and in-line skaters.<ref name=dnr/> Much of the trail is accessible to people with disabilities.<ref name=dnr/>
