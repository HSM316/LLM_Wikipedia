'''Daniel Gardano Serra''' (born 24 February 1984) is a Brazilian [[auto racing]] driver. He competes full-time in the Brazilian [[Stock Car Pro Series]], driving the No. 29 [[Chevrolet Cruze]] for [[RC Competições|Eurofarma-RC]] and is also a Ferrari Factory Driver. 

He won 3 times the [[Stock Car Brasil]] in the years 2017, 2018 and 2019. He is a two time [[24 Hours of Le Mans]] GTE Pro class winner, having won in [[2017 24 Hours of Le Mans|2017]] with [[Darren Turner]] and [[Jonathan Adam]], while driving for [[Aston Martin Racing]], and again in [[2019 24 Hours of Le Mans|2019]], with [[James Calado]] and [[Alessandro Pier Guidi]], driving for [[AF Corse]].

==Career==

Son of three-time Stock Car champion [[Chico Serra]], began karting at age 14, after receiving authorization from his parents. He competed in [[Formula Renault 2.0 Brazil]] and [[Eurocup Formula Renault 2.0]] (the lack of sponsorship brought him back to Brazil). In [[Copa Chevrolet Montana|Stock Car Light]], he finished in runner-up, opened the doors for Stock Car. His debut in the [[Red Bull]]-[[Amir Nasr Racing|Amir Nasr]], scored pole position and finished the Championship as the eighth place. In 2009, he joined [[Red Bull]]-[[WA Mattheis]]. On 20 September 2009, he won for the first time in Stock Car. After joining [[RC Competições|Eurofarma RC]] for the 2017 season, Daniel won his first Stock Car title, a feat he would repeat in the next season. On international level, he has competed part time on the [[2017 FIA World Endurance Championship]] for [[Aston Martin Racing]], winning the [[2017 24 Hours of Le Mans]]. He additionally raced in three races of the [[2018–19 FIA World Endurance Championship|2018–19 WEC season]] for [[AF Corse]], winning the [[2019 24 Hours of Le Mans]].
