'''William Edward Lyons''' is a philosopher who specializes in [[philosophy of mind]]. Lyons was the head of the Department of Philosophy (1985–1995) and Professor of Moral Philosophy (1985–2004) in the School of Mental and Moral Science, [[Trinity College, Dublin]]. He is now an [[Emeritus Fellow]] of Trinity College Dublin and a member of the [[Royal Irish Academy]]."<ref name="RIA">{{cite web|title=Membership|url=https://www.ria.ie/bios/membership/l|website=Royal Irish Academy|accessdate=24 December 2016}}</ref>  He is also the author of a number of "theatre of thought" dramas.<ref>[https://edinburghuniversitypress.com/william-lyons.html William Lyons] edinburghuniversitypress.com</ref> His play about [[Wittgenstein]], ''The Crooked Roads of Genius''  had its world premiere on 19 April 2011 at the [[Riverside Studios]].<ref name="Bloom">{{Cite web |url=http://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/wittgenstein-9781474218412/ |title=Wittgenstein The Crooked Roads |publisher=Bloomsbury Publishing |access-date=24 December 2016}}</ref> He received his PhD at the [[University of Dundee]].<ref name="RIA"/>

==Theatre of Thought==
Alongside his work in philosophy, Professor Lyons also has an interest in creating &#39;Theatre of Thought&#39; drama. His play about Wittgenstein, &#39;Wittgenstein - The Crooked Roads of Genius&#39; <ref name="Playography-Wittgenstein ">{{Cite web |url=https://www.irishplayography.com/play.aspx?playid=33706 | title=Wittgenstein - The Crooked Roads|access-date=22 July 2022 |publisher=www.irishplayography.com}}</ref> won the open-entry and blind-reviewed START Chapbooks play competition in Ireland in 2009. The Premiere and subsequent performances of &#39;Wittgenstein - The Crooked Roads of Genius&#39;, directed by Nicholas Blackburn, took place at the Riverside Studios, London, 18th April - 8th May 2011.<ref name="Riverside">{{Cite web |url=https://www.acflondon.org/events/wittgenstein-crooked-roads-william-lyons/ | title=Wittgenstein – The Crooked Roads|access-date=22 July 2022}}</ref> The play-text was published by Methuen Drama-Bloomsbury, London, in 2015. A short film of one scene from the play, &quot;The Examination&quot;, was an official selection at the Swedish International Film Festival in July 2016. There is also an avant-garde full film script entitled “The Crooked Roads” which is &quot;under consideration&quot; by a European production company. 
 
His play about Heidegger and Arendt, &#39;The Fir Tree and the Ivy&#39;, won the open-entry and blind-reviewed Eamon Keane Full Length Play award in Ireland in 2006. L&#39;Edera e l&#39;Abete, the Italian translation of William Lyons&#39; play &#39;The Fir Tree and the Ivy&#39;, won the open-entry and blind-reviewed Premio Colline di Torino (Testo Teatrale) in Italy in 2015. The translation is by Dr Alessio Frenda a graduate in Linguistics at TCD. Aided by a grant from The Raymond Williams Foundation in England in 2015, there is now a German translation of &#39;The Fir Tree and the Ivy&#39;, entitled Die Tanne und das Efeu, made by Dr Manfred Welteke, a graduate in philosophy at TCD. 
 
After being selected for work-shopping at the Directors’ Lab at the Lincoln Center Theatre in New York in August 2012, his play &#39;Socrates and his Clouds&#39; was produced by the Greek-Cypriot Meddlers Theatre Company and directed by Melina Theocharidou. <ref name="Playography">{{Cite web |url=https://www.irishplayography.com/play.aspx?playid=33707 | title=Greek-Cypriot Meddlers Theatre Company:Socrates and his Clouds|access-date=22 July 2022}}</ref> The Premiere and subsequent performances of took place at the Jermyn Street Theatre in central London, from 4th - 22nd June 2013. <ref name="oliviosk">{{Cite web |url=http://oliviosk.com/socrates-and-his-clouds-en | title= Socrates and his clouds|publisher=Oberon Books |access-date=22 July 2022}}</ref> The play text was published by Oberon Books - Modern Plays, London, in 2013. {{cite book |last=Lyons |first=William |date=6 March 2013|title=Socrates and his Clouds |url=https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/socrates-and-his-clouds-9781783190065/ |publisher=Bloomsbury Publishing |page= |isbn=9781783190065}}There was a production by DRAMSOC on the campus of University College Dublin in November 2015, and a “staged reading” by The Classics Club of Washington University and St Louis University in Missouri in November 2017. 
 
A short version of his play &#39;All The Hours&#39;, entitled &#39;A Beginning and an end&#39;, about religious faith and especially &quot;a religious vocation&quot;, was shortlisted for the open-entry and blind-reviewed Kenneth Branagh Award for New Drama Drama Writing in London in 2017. The full-length version of the play will be premiered at La Mama Theatre in Melbourne, 21st September to 2nd October 2022, directed by Adam Cass.<ref name="laMama">{{Cite web |url=https://lamama.com.au/whats-on/la-mama-hq-winter-2022/all-the-hours/ | title=All the Hours|access-date=22 July 2022}}</ref> The play text will be published by Australian Scholarly Publishing in Melbourne in August-September 2022.
