'''DreamWorks Channel''' is a [[pay television]] network that is owned and operated by [[NBCUniversal]], a division of [[Comcast]]. It first launched in Southeast Asia.

[[Singapore]]-based broadcaster [[HBO Asia]] was contracted to handle marketing, sales and technical services for the channel in Southeast Asia.<ref name="wsj"/>

The rights for DreamWorks Animation's programming in the United States are handled by [[Netflix]] and other networks.<ref name="thr">{{cite news|last1=Bond|first1=Paul|title=DreamWorks Animation to Launch TV Channel in Asia|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/dreamworks-animation-launch-tv-channel-755631|accessdate=April 22, 2016|work=The Hollywood Reporter|date=December 9, 2014}}</ref> DWA programming, both in new and archive forms, make up the primetime and early day schedules of new sister network [[Universal Kids]], which launched in the United States on 9 September 2017 over the channel space of the current Sprout network.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Steinberg|first1=Brian|title=NBCU’s Sprout Grows Up: Universal Kids to Debut in September |url= https://variety.com/2017/tv/news/sprout-universal-kids-tv-nbcuniversal-1202404505/ |accessdate= May 8, 2017 |work=Variety |date= May 1, 2017 |quote=The Comcast-owned media conglomerate said Monday it would in September rechristen the network as Universal Kids, ...}}</ref><ref name=ksn>{{cite web|url=http://kidscreen.com/2017/05/01/a-new-age-sprout-to-become-universal-kids/|title=A new age: Sprout to become Universal Kids |last= Getzler |first=Wendy |work=Kidscreen |date= May 1, 2017 |accessdate= May 1, 2017 |quote= On September 9, Sprout will rebrand to become Universal Kids, a US network for two- to 11-year-olds that will include preschool content, a lineup of DreamWorks Animation TV series, unscripted entertainment and live-action scripted originals.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.animationmagazine.net/people/nbcu-evp-duccio-donati-takes-on-dreamworks-channel/|title=NBCU EVP Duccio Donati Takes on DreamWorks Channel |work=Animation Magazine |date= August 3, 2017 |accessdate= August 4, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://kidscreen.com/2017/08/04/duccio-donati-tapped-for-new-kids-role-at-nbcuniversal/|title=Duccio Donati tapped for new kids role at NBCUniversal |work=Kidscreen |date= August 4, 2017}}</ref>

==History==
[[DreamWorks Animation]] announced on 9 December 2014 plans to launch its own TV channel starting in Asia rolling out over 19 countries in the second half of 2015.<ref name="wsj">{{cite news|last1=Schwartzel|first1=Erich|title=DreamWorks Wants to Start Television Channels in Asia|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/dreamworks-wants-to-start-television-channels-in-asia-1418174876|accessdate=April 22, 2016|work=Wall Street Journal|date=December 9, 2014}}</ref> DreamWorks Channel launched on 1 August 2015 in [[Thailand]]. The channel represents DWA's first foray into operating its own television channel in any part of the world as a result of a joint venture through CTH.<ref name="vty">{{cite news |last1=Frater |first1=Patrick |title=DreamWorks Animation Launches TV Channel In Thailand|url=https://variety.com/2015/tv/asia/dreamworks-animation-launches-tv-channel-in-thailand-1201551924/|accessdate=April 22, 2016|work=Variety|date=July 28, 2015}}</ref>

The channel launched on [[KPN]] in the [[Netherlands]] on 23 July 2019.<ref name="nl">{{cite web|url=https://www.broadbandtvnews.com/2019/07/22/kpn-is-launching-dreamworks-channel-in-the-netherlands/|title=KPN is launching DreamWorks channel in The Netherlands|work=BroadbandTVNews.com|author=Robert Briel|date=22 July 2019}}</ref>

==Programming==
The content on the channel mainly consists of series produced by DreamWorks Animation SKG and [[DreamWorks Classics]] library.<ref name="wsj"/> However, DWA's films only air in the MENA version.<ref name="thr"/> 

===Programming blocks===
*'''''DreamWorks Junior''''' - The DreamWorks Channel programs shows targeted at preschool-age children daily every morning.<ref name="vty" /> Programs currently seen in this block include ''[[Guess with Jess]]'' and “Raa Raa The Noisy Lion”. The block is a direct competitor of [[Nick Jr.]] and [[Disney Junior]].
