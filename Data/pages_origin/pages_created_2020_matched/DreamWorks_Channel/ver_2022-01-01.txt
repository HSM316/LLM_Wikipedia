'''DreamWorks Channel''' (commonly shortened to '''DreamWorks''') is a [[pay television]] network owned and operated by [[NBCUniversal]], a division of [[Comcast]], that first launched in Southeast Asia on 1 August 2015.

==History==
[[DreamWorks Animation]] announced on 9 December 2014 plans to launch its own TV channel starting in Asia rolling out over 19 countries in the second half of 2015. [[Singapore]]-based broadcaster [[HBO Asia]] was contracted to handle marketing, sales and technical services for the channel in Southeast Asia.<ref name="wsj">{{cite news|last1=Schwartzel|first1=Erich|title=DreamWorks Wants to Start Television Channels in Asia|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/dreamworks-wants-to-start-television-channels-in-asia-1418174876|access-date=April 22, 2016|work=Wall Street Journal|date=December 9, 2014}}</ref> DreamWorks Channel launched on 1 August 2015 in [[Thailand]]. The channel represents DWA's first foray into operating its own television channel in any part of the world as a result of a joint venture through [[CTH (company)|CTH]].<ref name="vty">{{cite news |last1=Frater |first1=Patrick |title=DreamWorks Animation Launches TV Channel In Thailand|url=https://variety.com/2015/tv/asia/dreamworks-animation-launches-tv-channel-in-thailand-1201551924/|access-date=April 22, 2016|work=Variety|date=July 28, 2015}}</ref>

A Middle East and North Africa channel launched on 1 August 2016 in partnership with [[beIN Network]].<ref name="ksn0">{{cite news|title=DreamWorks channel heads to MENA|url=http://kidscreen.com/2016/07/29/dreamworks-channel-heads-to-mena/|access-date=July 29, 2016|work=Kidscreen}}</ref> The channel(s) were placed under NBCUniversal

The channel was placed under the NBCU International executive vice president, lifestyle and kids Duccio Donati in August 2017. This paired the channel with E! international channels and NBCU lifestyle channel content.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.animationmagazine.net/people/nbcu-evp-duccio-donati-takes-on-dreamworks-channel/|title=NBCU EVP Duccio Donati Takes on DreamWorks Channel |work=Animation Magazine |date= August 3, 2017 |access-date= August 4, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://kidscreen.com/2017/08/04/duccio-donati-tapped-for-new-kids-role-at-nbcuniversal/|title=Duccio Donati tapped for new kids role at NBCUniversal |work=Kidscreen |date= August 4, 2017}}</ref> On 1 January 2018, NBCUniversal International Networks took over HBO Asia's role with DreamWorks Channel in Southeast Asia.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Frater |first1=Patrick |title=NBCUniversal to Take Full Control of DreamWorks Channel in Asia |url=https://variety.com/2017/tv/asia/nbcuniversal-full-control-of-dreamworks-channel-in-asia-1202623340/ |access-date=21 April 2020 |work=Variety |date=27 November 2017 |language=en}}</ref>

The channel launched on [[KPN]] in the [[Netherlands]] on 23 July 2019.<ref name="nl">{{cite web|url=https://www.broadbandtvnews.com/2019/07/22/kpn-is-launching-dreamworks-channel-in-the-netherlands/|title=KPN is launching DreamWorks channel in The Netherlands|work=BroadbandTVNews.com|author=Robert Briel|date=22 July 2019}}</ref>

A pop-up version was launched in [[Australia]] via pay television provider [[Foxtel]] from 1 to 30 June 2020, on channel 700 until a full version in 2021.<ref name="au">{{cite news |title=Foxtel launches DreamWorks pop-up channel with NBCUniversal |url=https://mumbrella.com.au/foxtel-launches-dreamworks-pop-up-channel-with-nbcuniversal-629534 |access-date=30 May 2020 |work=Mumbrella |date=27 May 2020}}</ref>

The channel launched in [[Sub-Saharan Africa]] through African pay-television operator [[StarTimes]] on 3 August 2020.<ref name="af">{{cite news|url=https://www.techafricanews.com/2020/08/05/nbcuniversals-dreamworks-channel-launches-in-sub-saharan-africa/|title=NBCUniversal's DreamWorks Channel Launches in Sub-Saharan Africa|access-date=4 September 2020|work=TechAfricaNews.com|date=5 August 2020}}</ref>

The channel launched in [[Spain]] with 2 television providers [[Movistar+]] and [[Orange España|Orange]] on 21 February 2021.<ref name="es">{{cite web|url=https://www.digitaltveurope.com/2021/01/13/nbcuniversal-teams-with-movistar-to-launch-universal-and-dreamworks-channel-in-spain/|title=NBCUniversal teams with Movistar+ to launch Universal+ and DreamWorks channel in Spain|work=DigitalTVEurope.com|author=Stuart Thomson|date=13 January 2021}}</ref>

The channel launched in the Philippines through Filipino-dubbed version via [[Cignal]] on 11 September 2021<ref name="CignalNewChannels-September2021">{{cite web|title=New channels on Cignal starting September 2021|url=https://cignal.tv/article/2683/new-channels-more-awesome|access-date=2 September 2021}}</ref> and [[Sky Cable]] on 15 November 2021.<ref name="skycable dreamworks">{{Cite web |title=DreamWorks on SKYcable |url=http://www.mysky.com.ph/metromanila/updates/1527/2021/11/15/dreamworks-on-skycable |access-date=2021-11-20 |website=Sky Cable}}</ref>

==Programming==
The content on the channel mainly consists of series produced by DreamWorks Animation SKG and [[DreamWorks Classics]] library.<ref name="wsj"/> However, DWA's films only air in the MENA version.<ref name="thr">{{cite news|last1=Bond|first1=Paul|title=DreamWorks Animation to Launch TV Channel in Asia|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/dreamworks-animation-launch-tv-channel-755631|access-date=April 22, 2016|work=The Hollywood Reporter|date=December 9, 2014}}</ref>

===Programming blocks===
*'''''DreamWorks Junior''''' - The DreamWorks Channel programs shows targeted at preschool-age children daily every morning.<ref name="vty" /> Programs currently seen in this block include ''[[Guess with Jess]]'', ''[[Raa Raa the Noisy Lion]]'' and ''[[3-2-1 Penguins!]]''. The block is a direct competitor of [[Nick Jr.]] and [[Disney Junior]].
