'''Bradley Edward Davis''' (born December 29, 1982) is an American former [[professional baseball]] [[catcher]], who played in [[Major League Baseball]] for the [[Florida Marlins]] in 2010.

==Early life==
Davis graduated from [[Capistrano Valley High School]] in [[Mission Viejo, California]] in 2001, where he was named CIF Player of the Year as a senior. He attended [[California State University, Long Beach|Long Beach State University]], where he majored in Communications, and was also named as a Freshman All-American.

==Professional career==
===Florida Marlins===
Davis was drafted by the Marlins in the fifth round of the [[2004 Major League Baseball Draft]].  Davis spent all of 2006 with the Class-A Advanced [[Jupiter Hammerheads]], and he went on to hit .259 with four home runs and 46 RBI in 106 games. He was honored by being named to represent Jupiter in the [[Florida State League]] All-Star Game. He had seven three-hit games and seven three-RBI games, including four games with three hits and three RBI.  His best month was August, when he batted .302 (19x63) with one home run and twelve RBI in twenty-one games.

On July 21, 2010, the Marlins designated [[Nate Robertson]] for assignment and promoted Davis to the major leagues.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://blogs.palmbeachpost.com/marlins/2010/07/21/florida-marlins-designate-nate-robertson-for-assignment-call-up-catcher-brad-davis/ |title=Florida Marlins designate Nate Robertson for assignment; call up catcher Brad Davis |publisher=Blogs.palmbeachpost.com |date=2010-07-21 |accessdate=2010-08-20 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100723143046/http://blogs.palmbeachpost.com/marlins/2010/07/21/florida-marlins-designate-nate-robertson-for-assignment-call-up-catcher-brad-davis/ |archivedate=2010-07-23 }}</ref>  He was optioned back to Triple-A on August 5,<ref>https://www.google.com/hostednews/canadianpress/article/ALeqM5hwuvqyZRVuaC7i-gexIQ7cNMPo3g</ref> but he was recalled on August 20 after [[Ronny Paulino]] was suspended for the use of performance-enhancing drugs.<ref>{{cite news|author=Alden Gonzalez |url=http://mlb.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20100820&content_id=13700366&vkey=news_mlb&fext=.jsp&c_id=mlb |title=Paulino suspended for positive PED test &#124; MLB.com: News |publisher=Mlb.mlb.com |date=2010-08-20 |accessdate=2010-08-20}}</ref>

He was designated for assignment on June 15, 2011.<ref>{{cite web|last=Dierkes|first=Tim|title=Marlins Designate Brad Davis For Assignment|url=http://www.mlbtraderumors.com/2011/06/marlins-designate-brad-davis-for-assignment.html|work=MLBTradeRumors.com|accessdate=15 June 2011}}</ref>

===San Diego Padres===
Davis signed a minor league contract with the [[San Diego Padres]] in 2012 but was released by the end of June after playing 39 games with their triple-a team in Tucson.

===Miami Marlins===
On July 5, 2012, Davis signed a minor league contract with the [[Miami Marlins]] but was released after only 7 games in their minor league system.

===Detroit Tigers===
On July 26, 2012, the [[Detroit Tigers]] signed a minor league contract with Davis.<ref>{{cite web|last=Blade Staff|first=|title=Hens fall to Clippers as woes continue|url=http://www.toledoblade.com/Mud-Hens/2012/07/27/Hens-fall-to-Clippers-as-woes-continue.html|work=toledoBlade.com|accessdate=27 July 2012}}</ref>
