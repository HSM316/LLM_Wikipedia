The '''MCI Group''' is an independently owned global event, [[association management]] and congress management company with headquarters in [[Geneva]], [[Switzerland]].

It offers association, company and brand solutions{{solution-inline|date=March 2019}} through offline and online events, meetings, congresses and conferences. Services include analytics, content, creative design and production, destination consulting, digital, production and [[audio visual]], and sustainability.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mciannualreport2017.com/about-mci/|title=MCI Annual Report 2017 | date=2019-01-20}}</ref>

MCI was founded in 1987 by Roger Tondeur <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mci-group.com/about-us/leadership-teams/roger_tondeur|title=About MCI | date=2019-01-18}}</ref>  and Ursula Wigert in Geneva, Switzerland.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mci-group.com/about-us | title=About MCI | date=2019-01-18}}</ref> Further expansion was swift and more offices were opened in Europe, the Americas, Asia-Pacific, India, the Middle East and Africa. The company now employs more than 2,300 people in 60 offices in 31 countries worldwide.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mciannualreport2017.com/about-mci/|title=MCI Annual Report 2017 | date=2019-01-20}}</ref>

MCI's brand portfolio includes MCI Group; Black Flower Agency; Don't Believe in Style; Dorier Group; Fair Control; Hagen Invent; Logos Public Affairs; MCI Experience; Network Media Partners; ONE CMG; and Ovation Global DMC.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mci-group.com/about-us|title=About MCI | date=2019-01-20}}</ref>

MCI's partners include INCON Global Partnership; Emirates; B-Com PSiDEO; Blue Sky Broadcast; Higher Logic; Expedia Affiliate Network; and Cvent.

MCI currently has 85 clients in the [[Fortune 500]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mciannualreport2015.com/strategy/|title=MCI Annual Report 2015 | date=2019-01-18}}</ref>

== History ==
=== 1987: The founding of MCI ===
Roger Tondeur and Ursula Wigert founded MCI in 1987. It started as a logistics-driven agency with a focus on responding to RFPs and implementing client requests.

=== 1999: Second office opens ===
The company quickly grew in size to become the leading event management company in Geneva, and a second office was opened in Zürich, Switzerland in 1999 to form MCI Zürich.

=== 2000: Acquisitions in France ===
MCI acquired Transit Communications in 2000 and Podiums in 2001.

=== 2002: Acquisition in the UK ===
MCI acquired Status Communications in Petersfield to form MCI UK.

=== 2003: Acquisition of GIC ===
MCI acquired GIC Management to include Association Management and Consulting.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mciannualreport2017.com/wp-content/uploads/16050_MCI_AR_Presentation2017_PR9.pdf | title=MCI Annual Report 2017 | date=2019-01-20}}</ref> MCI also launched its Institutional Division to enhance institutions’ strategic objectives, headed by Robin Lokerman.

=== 2003-4: New offices in Europe ===
In 2003, MCI opened offices in Paris, France; Stuttgart, Germany to form MCI Germany; and Brussels, Belgium to form MCI Benelux. MCI opened offices in Barcelona and Madrid in Spain in 2004. It merged its Paris and Lyon offices in France. MCI launched its Corporate Division in 2004 to support the activities of corporate clients in Europe, headed by Sébastien Tondeur. MCI entered into joint venture with Dorier in Geneva, Switzerland in 2004.

=== 2005: MCI Asia-Pacific ===
MCI announced partnership with Pacific World Singapore, and rebranded MCI Asia-Pacific. MCI also launched its new brand signature, ‘Building Community’ to focus on human capital and human connections. MCI also acquired Congress Partner GmbH, Berlin.

=== 2006: Acquisition of Dorier ===
MCI merged with Dorier.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mciannualreport2017.com/wp-content/uploads/16050_MCI_AR_Presentation2017_PR9.pdf | title=MCI Annual Report 2017 | date=2019-01-18}}</ref> to strengthen its Creative and Staging Production solutions{{solution-inline|date=March 2019}} in Europe. MCI start-up companies include MCI Dubai, MCI Vienna, MCI Prague and MCI Shanghai. MCI also opened an office in central London, UK.

=== 2007: Acquisition of Ovation ===
MCI acquired Ovation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mciannualreport2017.com/wp-content/uploads/16050_MCI_AR_Presentation2017_PR9.pdf | title=MCI Annual Report 2017 | date=2019-01-18}}</ref>  to expand its global destination management (DMC) services to over 13 countries. The company also launched the MCI Institute to support talent development in the group. MCI start-up companies include MCI Abu-Dhabi, MCI Mumbai and MCI Rome.

=== 2008: MCI expands to Latin America ===
MCI opened three new offices in Argentina. It also opened new offices in Bengaluru, Delhi, Hong Kong, and through mergers, Singapore and Mumbai.

=== 2009: MCI opens more offices worldwide ===
MCI opened new offices in Marseille, Spain, São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro in Brazil, Copenhagen, Denmark and Beijing, China.

=== 2010: New CEO ===
MCI remains a family company as in 2010, Roger handed over the reins to his son, Sébastien Tondeur,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mci-group.com/about-us/leadership-teams/sebastien_tondeur | title=About Us | date=2019-01-18}}</ref> who took over as Chief Executive Officer. Roger remains as Chairman, President and Member of the Executive Committee, continuing to play a key role in the strategic direction of the group. In 2017, he was included as one of the 25 most influential people in the meetings industry<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.successfulmeetings.com/Strategy/Case-Studies/25-Most-Influential-People-in-Meetings-2017-The-Game-Changers/ | title=Successful Meetings, Case Studies | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> In 2018, he won the Xaver Lifetime Achievement Award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.incentivetravel.co.uk/news/awards/44331-roger-tondeur-wins-the-2018-xaver-award | title=Incentive Travel & Corporate Meetings News | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> MCI also opened new offices in France, Korea and Australia<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mice.net.au/article/news-the-mci-factor/ | title=Mice Net News | date=2019-01-23}}</ref>

=== 2011: New areas ===
MCI introduced new services in the areas of content capture, hybrid events and community management technologies.

=== 2013: Expansion in Asia-Pacific and the Americas ===
MCI reinforced its creative and consulting services in the Asia-Pacific and Americas region. In 2017, MCI was voted the Best Event agency in Asia-Pacific by CEI Asia's annual readers’ choice awards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.campaignasia.com/article/readers-choice-best-event-agency/441711| title=CEI News Campaign Asia | date=2019-01-23}}</ref>

=== 2015: Merger with Coulter Companies ===
MCI expanded in the United States by merging with Coulter Companies<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mciannualreport2017.com/wp-content/uploads/16050_MCI_AR_Presentation2017_PR9.pdf| title=Annual Report 2017 | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> to form MCI USA. The merger created a new events management division, Strategic Event Management, headed by Shawn Pierce in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.tradeshowexecutive.com/archive/industry-news/shawn-pierce-lead-new-mci-usa-event-unit/| title=Trade Show Executive News | date=2019-01-24}}</ref>

=== 2016: New partnership with Visit Houston ===
MCI announced a two-year partnership with Visit Houston, a destination marketing organisation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.visithoustontexas.com/media/press-releases/post/visit-houston-mci-group-announcement/| title=Visit Houston Press Release | date=2019-01-23}}</ref>

=== 2017: Acquisition of Wyndham Jade ===
MCI acquired Texas-based Wyndham Jade<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tsnn.com/news-blogs/mci-buys-dallasbased-wyndham-jade-shawn-pierce-tapped-lead| title=Trade Show News Network News Blog | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> to strengthen its Meetings, Conventions and Incentives platform in the US<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.internationalmeetingsreview.com/united-states/mci-acquires-wyndham-jade-boost-us-growth-103881| title=International Meetings Review United States | date=2019-01-23}}</ref>

=== 2017: Launch of MCI Experience ===
MCI Experience,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.successfulmeetings.com/News/Products-and-Services/MCI-Group-Launches-Experience-Marketing-Arm/| title=Successful Meetings | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> a new brand of MCI Group, was formed to offer live, digital and virtual "experiences", headed by Kim Myhre. He became head of MCI UK in 2018<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.campaignlive.co.uk/article/kim-myhre-becomes-md-mci-uk/1463948| title=Campaign News Article | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> after Jennifer Jenkins stepped down after 15 years<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.citmagazine.com/article/1421619/jennifer-jenkins-steps-down-mci-uk| title=Sustain Europe | date=2019-01-24}}</ref>

=== 2017: Expansion worldwide ===
MCI Middle East announced a partnership with Zahara Tours in the Sultanate of Oman to form MCI Oman.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://khaleejdailies.blogspot.com/2017/11/mci-middle-east-and-zahara-tours-join.html| title= MCI Middle East and Zahara Tours Join Forces | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> MCI's Black Flower Agency acquired Miami based In-house Ideas<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.successfulmeetings.com/News/Products-and-Services/MCI-Group-Launches-Experience-Marketing-Arm/| title=MCI Group Launches Experience Marketing Arm  | date=2019-01-24}}</ref> to expand in the global luxury market. MCI USA acquired Network Media Partners, a sales management organisation specialising in customised media sales, event planning and management, digital and print design and market services for associations.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.meetings-conventions.com/News/Third-Party/MCI-USA-Acquires-Network-Media-Partners/| title=MCI USA Acquires Network Media Partners | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> MCI opened its capital to EMZ Partners and Indigo Capital.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.all-invest.com/en/mci-opens-its-capital-to-emz-partners-and-indigo-capital/| title=MCI opens its capital to EMZ Partners and Indigo Capital| date=2019-01-23}}</ref>

=== 2018: Acquisition of majority stake in ONE CMG ===
MCI acquired controlling interest in One Communications and Marketing Group (ONE CMG) <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.incentivetravel.co.uk/news/agency-news/45050-mci-group-acquire-majority-stake-in-one-communications-and-marketing-group-in-the-middle-east| title=MCI group acquire majority stake in One Communications and Marketing Group in the Middle East| date=2019-01-23}}</ref> – the holding company of Turret Media, Saluki Media, tmh, ONE PR and ONE Digital – in the Middle East. MCI Canada also acquired Montreal-based events planning firm Conferences, Groups & Incentives.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.citmagazine.com/article/1492603/mci-group-canada-acquires-conferences-groups-incentives-cg-i| title=MCI Group Canada acquires Conferences, Groups & Incentives (CG&I)| date=2019-01-24}}</ref> MCI expanded in Italy by focusing on national and regional conferences and congresses by opening a new office in Florence.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://kongres-magazine.eu/2018/12/mci-group-announced-its-expansion-in-italy-florence/| title=MCI Group announced its expansion in Italy| date=2019-01-24}}</ref>

=== 2019-present ===
Today, MCI employs more than 2,300 people in 60 offices in 31 countries worldwide.

== Corporate Identity ==
=== Corporate Culture ===
The company set up the MCI Institute in 2007 to support talent development of its employees. In 2017, it delivered over 60,000 learning hours to its employees.

MCI was awarded with the Best Small Company title at the FDI National Workplace Wellbeing Awards in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.businessworld.ie/news/National-Workplace-Wellbeing-Awards-Announced-568003.html|title=Business World | date=2019-01-18}}</ref>

===Company Logo===
The flower of MCI represents a sense of welcome, reunion, celebration and attention to detail, symbolising elegance, diplomacy and poetry. The colour black represents sobriety, strength, solidarity, authority and endurance. The centre of the fuchsia remains faithful to its historic origins, highlighting joy, radiance and light.

===Sustainability===
MCI has three priorities in its sustainability strategy: People, Planet and Profit.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sustaineurope.com/mci-inspiring-events-20181101.html|title=Sustain Europe | date=2019-01-18}}</ref> This consists of six key steps – including leadership, objectives, engagement, integration, governance and recognition – using the ISO20121 International Standard for Sustainability Management Systems for the events industry.

In 2008, the company joined the UN Caring for Climate Programme.

MCI has received a number of sustainability awards, including the Meetings & Events Australia's Corporate Social Responsibility Award 2016,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.meetingsevents.com.au/awards2016/2016winners.php|title=2016 MEA Industry Awards | date=2019-01-18}}</ref> the IMEX-GMIC Green Meeting Awards for Symantec's 2014 Vision EMEA Symposium<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.symantec.com/connect/blogs/symantec-wins-icarus-award-outstanding-event-sustainability-work|title=Symantec Official Blog | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> and the Global Business Travel Association Foundation's Project ICARUS Sustainability Gold Medal.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mcisustainability.com/our-performance/awards/|title=MCI Sustainability Report 2017 | date=2019-01-23}}</ref>

In 2016, MCI formed a collaborative partnership with the International Congress and Conventions Association (ICCA), the ICCA's Scandinavian Chapter, European Cities Marketing and the IMEX Group, to launch the first-ever sustainability ranking for event destinations worldwide, the GDS-Index.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.slovenia-convention.com/launch-of-the-new-global-destination-sustainability-index/|title=Bureau News Slovenia Convention | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> It rates cities on convention bureau strategy and initiatives, social sustainability performance, industry supplier support, and environmental strategy and infrastructure. In 2017, the GDS-Index released its second annual ranking of 40 meetings and events cities.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.internationalmeetingsreview.com/americas/americas-sustainability-awards-recognize-innovation-and-improvement-104102|title=Americas International Meetings Review | date=2019-01-23}}</ref>

MCI's sustainability strategy and performance is assessed annually by EcoVadis, who provided the company with a Silver rating<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mcisustainability.com/our-performance/awards/|title=MCI Sustainability Report 2017 | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> in 2017.

In 2018, the company released its 2017 Sustainability Report ‘Fostering Care & Generating Value’,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mcisustainability.com/our-performance/awards/|title=MCI Sustainability Report 2017 | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> focusing on the environmentally friendly and inclusive policies play in the business.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.exhibitionworld.co.uk/2018/06/28/mci-puts-sustainability-first-annual-report/|title=News Europe Exhibition World | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> It highlighted that its employees worldwide volunteered for over 6,300 hours, raising more than 1.4 million Euros through pro bono charity event consulting.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://mcisustainability.com/our-priorities/community/|title=MCI Sustainability | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> MCI reduced its carbon emissions, with over a third of its offices running on sustainable energy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mcisustainability.com/|title=MCI Sustainability Report | date=2019-01-23}}</ref> The company also included its support for non-profit organisation, Cool Earth, which works alongside rainforest communities to halt deforestation and reduce its impact on climate change.

===Headquarters===
MCI's corporate headquarters is located at Rue du Pré-Bouvier in Geneva, Switzerland. The company moved from its former headquarters in Rue de Lyon, Geneva in 2016.
