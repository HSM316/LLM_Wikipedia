'''Celedonio B.''' "'''Don'''" '''Trollano Jr.''' (born January 7, 1992) is a Filipino professional basketball player for the [[San Miguel Beermen]] of the [[Philippine Basketball Association]] (PBA).

==College and amateur career==

Trollano studied at [[Adamson University]] and played for the [[Adamson Falcons]] from 2012 to 2014.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date= |title=Celedonio Trollano Player Profile :: PBA-Online! |url=http://www.pba-online.net/basketball/uaap/profile/Celedonio-Trollano/12/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304040340/http://www.pba-online.net/basketball/uaap/profile/Celedonio-Trollano/12/ |archive-date=March 4, 2016 |access-date=June 23, 2022 }}</ref>  He also suited up for the [[Cagayan Rising Suns]] of the [[PBA Developmental League]].<ref>{{ cite news |last1=Flores |first1=Celest R. |title=Trollano shines in PBA D-League debut as Cagayan beats Cafe France |url=http://sports.inquirer.net/125925/trollano-shines-in-pba-d-league-debut-as-cagayan-beats-cafe-france |newspaper=[[Philippine Daily Inquirer]] |date=October 24, 2013 |access-date=September 29, 2015 }}</ref>

==Professional career==
Trollano was drafted by the [[Rain or Shine Elasto Painters]] with the 15th overall pick in the [[2015 PBA draft]].<ref>{{cite news |title=SUMMARY – 2015 PBA Rookie Draft |url=http://www.interaksyon.com/interaktv/live-results-2015-pba-rookie-draft |work=InterAksyon |date=August 23, 2015 |access-date=September 25, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924102153/http://www.interaksyon.com/interaktv/live-results-2015-pba-rookie-draft |archive-date=September 24, 2015 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

On October 25, 2015, Trollano recorded 8 points, 3 rebounds and 4 assists in a win over the [[Terrafirma Dyip|Columbian Dyip]] in his 2nd career game played. On November 11, 2015, Trollano recorded his first double-digit scoring output as he scored 12 points to go along with 3 rebounds in a win over the Alaska Aces. In the [[2016 PBA Commissioner's Cup|2016 Commissioner's Cup]], which was Trollano's rookie year, he won his first championship.

In 2018, he was traded to the [[TNT Tropang Giga|TnT Katropa]] for [[Norbert Torres]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ramos|first=Gerry|date=March 26, 2018|title=TnT Katropa acquires Don Trollano from Rain or Shine in trade for Norbert Torres|url=https://www.spin.ph/basketball/pba/tnt-katropa-nets-don-trollano-for-norbert-torres-in-another-trade-with-rain-or-shine?ref=article_tag|url-status=live|access-date=January 6, 2022|website=Spin.ph|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220105180905/https://www.spin.ph/basketball/pba/tnt-katropa-nets-don-trollano-for-norbert-torres-in-another-trade-with-rain-or-shine?ref=article_tag |archive-date=January 5, 2022 }}</ref> In Game 3 of the [[2019 PBA Commissioner's Cup Finals|2019 Commissioner's Cup Finals]], he scored a playoff career-high 18 points.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Villanueva|first=Eros|date=August 10, 2019|title=Defensive wiz Don Trollano dials up offense for shorthanded TNT|url=https://www.espn.ph/basketball/story/_/id/27356229/defensive-wiz-don-trollano-dials-offense-shorthanded-tnt|url-status=live|access-date=January 6, 2022|website=ESPN Philippines|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220105180900/https://www.espn.ph/basketball/story/_/id/27356229/defensive-wiz-don-trollano-dials-offense-shorthanded-tnt |archive-date=January 5, 2022 }}</ref> The next conference, Trollano scored a career high 19 points in a win against the Columbian Dyip.<ref>{{Citation|title=One Sports – Best Player: Don Trollano {{!}} PBA Governors' Cup 2019 {{!}} Facebook|url=https://www.facebook.com/OneSportsPHL/videos/best-player-don-trollano-pba-governors-cup-2019/438376570116017/|language=en|access-date=January 5, 2022}}</ref>

During the [[2019 PBA Governors' Cup|2019 Governor's Cup]], he was traded to the [[Blackwater Elite]] along with [[Anthony Semerad]] and a first round draft pick for [[Bobby Ray Parks Jr.|Bobby Ray Parks Jr]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ramos|first=Gerry|date=November 3, 2019|title=Ray Parks now with TNT as PBA approves trade for Trollano, Semerad, draft pick|url=https://www.spin.ph/basketball/pba/bobby-ray-parks-trade-to-tnt-katropa-approved-don-trollano-tony-semerad-to-blackwater-a793-20191103?ref=article_tag|url-status=live|access-date=January 6, 2019|website=Spin.ph|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210116185237/https://www.spin.ph/basketball/pba/bobby-ray-parks-trade-to-tnt-katropa-approved-don-trollano-tony-semerad-to-blackwater-a793-20191103?ref=article_tag |archive-date=January 16, 2021 }}</ref> In 2020, he scored 23 points, 7 rebounds, and 4 assists in a 96–109 loss.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sarmenta|first=Yoyo|date=October 22, 2020|title=Pogoy's outburst keeps TNT unbeaten in Philippine Cup|url=https://www.espn.com/basketball/pba/story/_/id/30166352/pogoy-parks-lead-way-tnt-streaks-fifth-straight-win|url-status=live|access-date=January 6, 2022|website=ESPN.com|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220107035414/https://www.espn.com/basketball/pba/story/_/id/30166352/pogoy-parks-lead-way-tnt-streaks-fifth-straight-win |archive-date=January 7, 2022 }}</ref>

Trollano was on the move again after the [[NLEX Road Warriors]] gave the No. 4 pick to Blackwater for him, [[Maurice Shaw]], [[Roi Sumang]], and the Bossing's 2022 second-round draft pick. The Bossing then transferred the draft selection for [[Simon Enciso]], [[David Semerad]], TNT's 2023 first-round draft pick and 2024 second-round draft pick in the trade.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mikey Williams the draft target as TnT acquires NLEX pick in three-way trade|url=https://www.spin.ph/basketball/pba/mikey-williams-the-target-as-tnt-acquires-nlex-draft-pick-in-three-way-trade-a795-20210311|access-date=January 5, 2022|website=Spin.ph|language=en}}</ref> This reunited him with the coach he won his first championship with, [[Yeng Guiao]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Trollano embraces chance to work anew with primary mentor Guiao|url=https://www.spin.ph/basketball/pba/trollano-embraces-chance-to-work-anew-with-primary-mentor-guiao-a793-20210311|access-date=January 5, 2022|website=Spin.ph|language=en}}</ref>

In the [[2022 PBA Philippine Cup|2022 Philippine Cup]], he hit a game-winning shot against TNT.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Ramos |first=Gerry |date=June 16, 2022 |title=Meet Finals breakout star Don Trollano's multitasking inspiration |url=https://www.spin.ph/life/guide/wags-patricia-palacol-a796-20190903 |access-date=June 23, 2022 |website=Spin.ph |language=en}}</ref> In a win against the [[Meralco Bolts]], he scored 11 of his 19 points in the fourth quarter to help NLEX pull away for the win.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Terrado |first=Reuben |date=June 25, 2022 |title=Trollano takes over as NLEX pulls away vs Meralco in heated battle |url=https://www.spin.ph/basketball/pba/nlex-vs-meralco-don-trollano-jr-quinahan-kevin-alas-pba-philippine-cup-a795-20220625 |access-date=June 25, 2022 |website=Spin.ph |language=en}}</ref> In December 2022, Trollano signed a two-year extension with the team, which came after he scored a career-high 26 points in two different games during the [[2022–23 PBA Commissioner's Cup|Commissioner's Cup]].<ref>{{cite news |date=December 26, 2022 |title=Surprise BPC candidate Don Trollano signs contract extension with NLEX |url=https://www.spin.ph/basketball/pba/surprise-bpc-candidate-don-trollano-signs-extension-deal-with-nlex-a793-20221226 |work=Spin.ph |accessdate=December 29, 2022}}</ref> On March 2, 2023, in a [[2023 PBA Governors' Cup|Governors' Cup]] win over the [[Terrafirma Dyip]], he hit a new career-high with 44 points, and broke the PBA's all-time record for consecutive triples without a miss with nine straight as well.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Sacamos |first=Karlo |date=March 2, 2023 |title=Don Trollano explodes for 44 as NLEX rips Terrafirma |url=https://www.spin.ph/basketball/pba/nlex-vs-terrafirma-pba-governors-cup-don-trollano-a794-20230302 |access-date=March 2, 2023 |website=Spin.ph |language=en}}</ref>

On December 11, 2023, Trollano was traded to the [[San Miguel Beermen]] in a three-team trade involving San Miguel, NLEX, and [[NorthPort Batang Pier]].<ref>{{ cite news |date=December 11, 2023 |title=NorthPort deals Bolick to NLEX, Trollano to SMB |url=https://pba.ph/news/northport-deals-bolick-to-nlex-trollano-to-smb |work=pba.ph  |access-date=December 11, 2023 }}</ref>
