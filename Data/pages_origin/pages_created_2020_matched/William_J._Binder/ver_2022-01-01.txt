'''William J. Binder''' is an American facial plastic and reconstructive surgeon. He is best known for his 1992 discovery of the use of [[Botulinum toxin|Botox]] to alleviate [[chronic migraine]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://time.com/4501839/botox-inection-wrinkles-migraine-depression/ |title=11 Surprising Uses for Botox|website=Time|access-date=2017-05-12}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.latinpost.com/articles/130951/20170106/botox-drug-health-problems-consider-side-effects.htm|title=Botox Is the Drug for All Health Problems-Yet Consider the Side Effects Too |date=2017-01-06|work=Latin Post |access-date=2017-05-12|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2010, [[Food and Drug Administration|FDA]] approved the use of Botox as a treatment to migraine.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.webmd.com/migraines-headaches/news/20101018/fda-approves-botox-to-treat-chronic-migraines#1|title=FDA Approves Botox to Treat Chronic Migraines|work=WebMD|access-date=2017-05-12|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Biography==
Binder holds an M.D. degree from [[University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/british-columbia/20-year-olds-getting-botox-preventative-1.3794558|title=Why more 20-somethings are getting Botox|publisher=}}</ref>
Binder was elected as a member of the Board of Directors of the American Board of Facial Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery, for which he also serves as a National Board Examiner. He is a member of the National Review Committee for the American Academy of Neurology Therapeutics and Technologies Subcommittee on the Assessment of Botulinum Toxin Therapy and also an adjunct reviewer for the Archives of Facial Plastic Surgery Journal.
Binder is board certified by both the American Board of Facial Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery and the American Board of Otolaryngology and Head and Neck Surgery.
Binder published around 70 articles in national medical and surgical journals. He edited three textbooks in Facial Plastic Surgery and Head and Neck Surgery and Otolaryngology. He speaks and lecturers on various aspects of facial plastic surgery, including Rhinoplasty and multi-level concept of facelift surgery.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hollywoodlife.com/pics/real-housewives-of-beverly-hills-plastic-surgery-botox/|title=Photos! See Her Scars Plus Other Stars Who Went Under Knives in 2010!|last=hollywoodlifeintern|date=22 December 2010|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://doctor.webmd.com/doctor/william-binder-md-37678e5e-bc06-45f0-b43b-19ddc58cb26d-appointments|title = Times, Locations & Directions for Dr. William Binder}}</ref> Binder filed 15 U.S. and European patents in the area of Custom Plastic Surgery Implants, Method for Reduction of Migraine Headache Pain, Flexible support wrap, and Anesthesia conduit.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.google.com/search?tbm=pts&hl=en&q=ininventor:%22William+J.+Binder%22|title=ininventor:"William J. In 1992, Binder discovered the use of Botox to alleviate sufferers of chronic migraine headaches.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.latinpost.com/articles/130951/20170106/botox-drug-health-problems-consider-side-effects.htm|title=Botox Is The Drug For All Health Problems-Yet Consider The Side Effects Too|first=Latin|last=Post|date=6 January 2017|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mercado.com.ar/notas/mercado-plus/8023219/botox-mucho-ms-que-cosmtica|title=Botox: mucho más que cosmética|website=www.mercado.com.ar|date=10 January 2017}}</ref>
Binder reported in 2000 that patients who had cosmetic injections around the face reported relief from chronic headache.<ref>{{cite journal | vauthors = Binder WJ, Brin MF, Blitzer A, Schoenrock LD, Pogoda JM | title = Botulinum toxin type A (BOTOX) for treatment of migraine headaches: an open-label study | journal = Otolaryngology–Head and Neck Surgery | volume = 123 | issue = 6 | pages = 669–76 | date = December 2000 | pmid = 11112955 | doi = 10.1067/mhn.2000.110960 | s2cid = 24406607 }}</ref> This was initially thought to be an indirect effect of reduced muscle tension, but it is now known that the toxin inhibits release of peripheral nociceptive neurotransmitters, suppressing the central pain processing systems responsible for [[migraine]] headache.<ref>{{cite journal | vauthors = Jackson JL, Kuriyama A, Hayashino Y | title = Botulinum toxin A for prophylactic treatment of migraine and tension headaches in adults: a meta-analysis | journal = JAMA | volume = 307 | issue = 16 | pages = 1736–45 | date = April 2012 | pmid = 22535858 | doi = 10.1001/jama.2012.505 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | vauthors = Ramachandran R, Yaksh TL | title = Therapeutic use of botulinum toxin in migraine: mechanisms of action | journal = British Journal of Pharmacology | volume = 171 | issue = 18 | pages = 4177–92 | date = September 2014 | pmid = 24819339 | pmc = 4241086 | doi = 10.1111/bph.12763 }}</ref> In 2010, the [[Food and Drug Administration|FDA]] approved intramuscular botulinum toxin injections for prophylactic treatment of chronic [[migraine]] headache.<ref>{{cite news|title=FDA Approves Botox to Treat Chronic Migraines|url=http://www.webmd.com/migraines-headaches/news/20101018/fda-approves-botox-to-treat-chronic-migraines#1|accessdate=12 May 2017|work=WebMD}}</ref>
