'''Edith Hannah Gostick''' (née '''Thomas'''; January 15, 1894 – July 8, 1984) was a provincial level politician from [[Alberta]], Canada. She served as a member of the [[Legislative Assembly of Alberta]] as a representative from the electoral district of [[Calgary (provincial electoral district)|Calgary]] from 1935 to 1940.

==Political career==
Gostick ran for a seat as a [[Social Credit Party of Alberta]] candidate in the Alberta Legislature in the [[1935 Alberta general election]]. She was declared elected on the 18th vote count, taking the fourth seat in the Legislature.<ref name="1935 official">{{cite web|url=http://www.abheritage.ca/abpolitics/administration/maps_choice.php?Year=1935&Constit=Calgary| title=Calgary results 1935 Alberta general election | publisher=Alberta Heritage Community Foundation| accessdate=2009-05-17}}</ref> Gostick ran for re-election in the [[1940 Alberta general election|1940 general election]] but was defeated.<ref name="1940 official">{{cite web|url=http://www.abheritage.ca/abpolitics/administration/maps_choice.php?Year=1940&Constit=Calgary| title=Calgary results 1940 Alberta general election | publisher=Alberta Heritage Community Foundation| accessdate=2009-05-17}}</ref>

Her son [[Ron Gostick]] was a well-known publisher of [[anti-Semitism|anti-Semitic]] and far right literature.
