'''Alain Anthony Pons''' (born 16 September 1995) is a [[Gibraltar]]ian [[association football|football]] [[Midfielder (association football)|midfielder]] who currently plays for [[St Joseph's F.C.|St Joseph's]] and the [[Gibraltar national football team]].

==Club career==
Pons came through the youth ranks at AD Taraguilla in [[Spain]], breaking into the senior team in 2013 before moving to his home country of [[Gibraltar]] to sign for [[Lincoln Red Imps F.C.|Lincoln Red Imps]]. While studying at [[University of Central Lancashire]], he remained contracted to Lincoln, occasionally playing for the first team while also representing UCLan's football team. Returning to Gibraltar after his studies, he signed for [[Gibraltar United F.C.|Gibraltar United]], but this stay would be short lived as he would re-sign for Lincoln in January 2017. Again, he would feature primarily in the reserve teams, appearing on the bench several times but not playing. In August 2019, Pons signed for [[St Joseph's F.C.|St Joseph's]].<ref>[https://twitter.com/StJosephsFCGib/status/1164470676188741632 Pons joins Saints] St Joseph's FC. 22 August 2019. Retrieved 22 August 2019.</ref> On 4 May 2023, Pons extended his contract with [[St Joseph's F.C.|St Joseph's]] until 30 June 2024.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stjosephsfcgib.co.uk/news/breaking--new-deal-for-alain-pons?id=430335|title=Breaking: New deal for Alain Pons|publisher=stjosephsfcgib.co.uk|accessdate=31 July 2023}}</ref>

==International career==
Pons has been capped 5 times for the [[Gibraltar national under-19 football team]], making his debut in 2013. In 2015, he played for a development squad at the [[Men's Football at the 2015 Island Games|2015 Island Games]], scoring once as Gibraltar finished a disappointing 10th place.<ref>[https://jersey2015results.com/competitor.aspx?RegID=33947 Team Details: Gibraltar] 2015 Island Games. Retrieved 7 January 2019.</ref> Having been called up to several senior squads without playing, he finally made his debut as a substitute for the [[Gibraltar national football team|senior team]] on August 31, 2017, in an eventual 9–0 defeat to [[Belgium national football team|Belgium]].
