'''Gerhard Herman Knoop''' (3 November 1920 &ndash; 25 October 2009) was a [[Norwegians|Norwegian]] actor, stage producer, [[theatre director]] and film director. He is mostly known for directing the movies [[Minne om to mandager]] (1964), [[Dei rid mot havet]] (1960) and [[Avskjedsgaven]] (1961).<ref>{{Cite web |title=Gerhard Knoop {{!}} Director |url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0461328/ |access-date=2023-11-21 |website=IMDb |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Early and personal life==
Knoop was born in [[Oslo|Kristiania]] (present-day [[Oslo]]), the son of Herman Waldemar Knoop (1887–1961) and Ellen Caroline Sontum (1897–1962). He made his stage debut in a school revue while studying at the [[gymnasium (school)|gymnasium]]. He took his [[examen artium]] (university entrance exam) in 1939. During [[World War II]] he was involved in [[Norwegian resistance movement|resistance]] work, and had to flee to Sweden in 1942, where he joined the police troops. After the war, he studied at the [[University of Oslo]] and at the [[University of Denver]]. He married Eva Cecilie Julsrud in September 1948 in [[Denver, Colorado|Denver]].<ref name=nbl>{{cite encyclopedia |title=Gerhard H Knoop |encyclopedia=[[Norsk biografisk leksikon]]|first=Merete |last=Skavlan |authorlink=Merete Skavlan |editor=[[Knut Helle|Helle, Knut]]  |publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget |location=Oslo|url=http://www.snl.no/.nbl_biografi/Gerhard_H_Knoop/utdypning |language=Norwegian|accessdate=30 October 2009}}</ref>

==Theatre career==
Knoop was employed at [[Rogaland Teater]] from 1949, first as actor and later as stage producer. Among his productions were adaptions of [[Franz Arnold|Arnold]] and [[Ernst Bach|Bach's]] [[farce]] ''Die spanische Fliege'', and of [[Henrik Ibsen|Ibsen]]'s play ''[[A Doll's House]]'',<ref name=nbl/> and of [[A. M. Willner|Willner]] and [[Heinz Reichert|Reichert]]'s operetta ''[[Das Dreimäderlhaus]]''.<ref name=snl/> He worked as a free-lance producer from 1952, for [[Riksteatret]], the [[Nationaltheatret|National Theatre]], [[Det Norske Teatret]], [[Det Nye Teater]], [[Fjernsynsteatret]] and [[Radioteatret]].<ref name=nbl/> Among his productions were adaptions of [[Samuel Beckett|Beckett]]'s play ''[[Waiting for Godot]]'', [[Anton Chekhov|Chekhov]]'s comedy ''[[Uncle Vanya]]'' and Ibsen's play ''[[Rosmersholm]]''.<ref name=nbl/> He produced Ibsen's play ''[[Hedda Gabler]]'' for the [[Pitlochry Festival Theatre]] in Scotland in 1963.<ref name=nbl/> He has also produced Ibsen's plays ''[[Ghosts (play)|Ghosts]]'' and ''[[The Wild Duck]]'', [[August Strindberg|Strindberg]]'s ''[[Easter (play)|Easter]]'' and ''[[Miss Julie]]'', and [[Eugène Ionesco|Ionesco]]'s ''[[The Chairs]]''.<ref name=snl/>

He headed the [[Norwegian National Academy of Theatre]] from 1964 to 1970.<ref name=snl/> From 1973 to 1984 he was employed at the [[Norwegian Broadcasting Corporation]] as head of [[Radioteatret]].<ref name=snl>{{cite encyclopedia |year=2007 |title=Gerhard Knoop |encyclopedia=[[Store norske leksikon]] |publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget |location=Oslo |url=http://www.snl.no/Gerhard_Knoop |editor=Henriksen, Petter|language=Norwegian | accessdate=7 May 2009 }}</ref> He started the independent theatre group Intimteatret in 1991, together with [[Merete Skavlan]]. He died in Oslo on 25 October 2009.<ref name=nbl/>
