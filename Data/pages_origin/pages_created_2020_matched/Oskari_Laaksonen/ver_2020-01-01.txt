'''Oskari Laaksonen''' (born 2 July 1999) is a Finnish professional [[ice hockey]] [[Defenseman (ice hockey)|defenseman]] who is currently playing with [[Ilves]] of the Finnish [[Liiga]]. He was selected by the [[Buffalo Sabres]] in the third round, 89th overall, of the [[2017 NHL Entry Draft]].

== Playing career ==
Laaksonen as a youth played with Ilves' U16, U18, and U20 teams. After playing for Ilves U20, Laaksonen was drafted 89th overall by the Sabres in the [[2017 NHL Entry Draft]].<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.nhl.com/sabres/news/oskari-laaksonen-sabres-third-round-pick/c-290337418 | title = Meet Oskari Laaksonen, Buffalo's third round pick | website = [[Buffalo Sabres]] | date = 2017-07-10 | accessdate = 2017-07-10}}</ref>

Laaksonen made his Liiga debut during the [[2017-18 Liiga season|2017–18]] season, on 9 January 2018, in a 6-5 defeat to [[HPK]].<ref>{{citeweb| url = http://liiga.fi/ottelut/2017-2018/runkosarja/296/kokoonpanot/ | title = Ilves stopped by HPK | publisher = [[Liiga]] | date = 2018-01-09 | accessdate = 2018-01-09}}</ref> He played out the season to contribute with 4 assists in 21 games.<ref>{{citeweb| url = https://finnprospects.com/player/oskari-laaksonen/ | title = Oskari Laaksonen player biography | website = finnprospects.com | date = 2018-01-09 | accessdate = 2018-01-09}}</ref>

Laaksonen appeared for [[Finland men's national junior ice hockey team|Finland]] in 6 junior international friendly games in 2016, before he was later selected to represent his country at the [[2019 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships|2019 World Junior Championships]] in [[Vancouver, British Columbia]], [[Canada]].<ref>{{citeweb| url = https://www.eliteprospects.com/player/372926/oskari-laaksonen | title = Oskari Laaksonen player profile |  website = eliteprospects.com | date = 2017-06-25| accessdate = 2017-06-25}}</ref> He collected 1 goal in 7 games from the blueline, helping Finland claim the Gold Medal for their 5th title.<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.nhl.com/news/finland-defeats-united-states-to-win-world-junior-championship/c-303601898 | title = Finland defeats United States to win World Junior Championship | website = [[National Hockey League]] | date = 2019-01-06 | accessdate = 2019-01-06}}</ref>
