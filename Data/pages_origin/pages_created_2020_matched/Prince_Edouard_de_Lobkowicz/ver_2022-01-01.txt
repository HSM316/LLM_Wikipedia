'''Prince Edouard de Lobkowicz''' (12 June 1926 – 2 April 2010) was an Austrian-American diplomat and investment banker. A member of the [[House of Lobkowicz]], he served as the [[ambassador]] of the [[Sovereign Military Order of Malta]] to Lebanon.

==Early life==
Lobkowicz was the elder son of Prince Edouard von Lobkowicz (1899–1959) and of his American wife, [[Anita Lihme]] (1903–1976). Since he was born in the [[United States]], he held American citizenship. He was baptised with the names ''Maria Eduard August Joseph Wilhelm Ignatius Patricius Hubertus Kaspar''.<ref name="ghda">''Genealogisches Handbuch des Adels'', Fürstlicher Häuser Band XVIII, 189.</ref>

Lobkowicz was educated at the [[Lycée Saint-Louis-de-Gonzague]] in Paris.<ref name="whoswho">{{cite web | url=https://www.whoswho.fr/decede/biographie-edouard-de-lobkowicz_16435 | title=Biographie Edouard de Lobkowicz | website=Who’s Who in France | accessdate=2016-09-04}}</ref>  From 1944 to 1947, he served in the [[United States Army]]. He then completed his tertiary education at the [[University of Paris]] and at [[Harvard University]].<ref name="whoswho" />

==Banking career==
From 1951 to 1958, Lobkowicz worked at [[Chase Bank|Chase Manhattan Bank]] in New York.<ref name="whoswho" /> In 1960, he became assistant to the director of the investment firm A. L. Stamm, and in 1963, delegate for Europe and the Middle East of the same firm.<ref name="whoswho" /> In 1969, he transferred to Coleman and Co.<ref name="whoswho" /> From 1972 to 1989, he worked for Stralem and Co.<ref name="whoswho" />

==Marriage and family==
[[File:Huwelijk van Prinses Francoise van Bourbon Parma met Prins Eduard van Lobkowicz , Bestanddeelnr 910-9251.jpg|thumb|The couple on their wedding day]]
On 11 December 1959, at [[Besson, Allier]], France, Lobkowicz was married civilly to [[Princess Marie-Françoise of Bourbon-Parma]], eldest daughter of [[Prince Xavier of Bourbon-Parma]] and of his wife, [[Madeleine de Bourbon-Busset]]. A religious wedding followed on 7 January 1960, in [[Notre Dame de Paris]];<ref name="ghda" /> this was the first marriage of a member of the [[House of Bourbon]] at Notre Dame since the 1816 wedding of [[Charles Ferdinand, Duke of Berry]], to [[Princess Caroline of Naples and Sicily]].

Lobkowicz and his wife had four children:<ref name="ghda" />
* Prince ''Edouard''-Xavier Ferdinand Auguste Gaspard (18 October 1960, Paris&nbsp;– 27 April 1984, [[Ivry-sur-Seine]]); murdered.<ref>Eric Pace, "Body of Prince Found Near Paris", ''New York Times'' (8 May 1984), A12.</ref>
* Prince ''Robert'' Emanuel Joseph Michel Benoît Melchior (31 December 1961, Paris&nbsp;– 29 October 1988, Bhannes, [[Lebanon]]); died of a brain tumor.<ref>"Robert de Lobkowicz, Prince, 26", ''New York Times'' (1 November 1988).</ref>
* [[Prince Charles-Henri de Lobkowicz|Prince ''Charles-Henri'' Hugues Xavier Benoît Michel Edouard Joseph Balthazar]] (born 17 May 1964, Paris).
* Princess ''Gabrielle'' Anita Olga Thérèse Lisieux Gaspara (born 11 June 1967, Paris); a member of the [[Little Sisters of the Poor]].

==Sovereign Military Order of Malta==
Lobkowicz was an active member of the Sovereign Military Order of Malta. He entered the order as a Knight of Honour and Devotion, but later became a [[Knight in Obedience]].<ref name="ghda" /> Eventually, he advanced to the degree of a knight grand cross.<ref name="ghda" />

In 1980, Lobkowicz was appointed the Order's ambassador to [[Lebanon]], a position he held until 1990.<ref name="sire">H.J.A. Sire, ''The Order of Malta, A Modern Resurrection'' (London: Third Millennium, 2016), 249-250.</ref> In 1981, he set up the Order's Lebanese Association.<ref>Sire, 250.</ref> In 1987, Lobkowicz and his wife founded the ''Association Malte Liban''.  Under Lobkowicz’s leadership, the Order’s medical centres increased from one to thirteen.<ref>Jean-Pierre Chanial, "Le prince et la princesse Edouard de Lobkowicz: au nom de la Foi”, ''Point de Vue'' (3 mai 1990), 16. Sire, 250.</ref>

Lobkowicz died on Good Friday, 2 April 2010, in Paris. His funeral mass was celebrated in the [[Église Saint-Thomas-d'Aquin (Paris)|Église Saint-Thomas-d'Aquin]].
