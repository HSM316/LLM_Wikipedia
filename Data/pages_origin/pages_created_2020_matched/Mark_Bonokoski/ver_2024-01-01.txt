'''Mark Bonokoski''', <!-- needs cite: Bono among friends,--> is a Canadian conservative newspaper columnist and commentator. In November 2017, he was inducted into the Canadian News Hall of Fame.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://torontosun.com/news/local-news/warmington-honouring-our-bono|title=WARMINGTON: Our Bono inducted into Canadian News Hall of Fame|date=2017-11-22|newspaper=Toronto Sun|access-date=2018-05-11|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180511221123/http://torontosun.com/news/local-news/warmington-honouring-our-bono|archive-date=2018-05-11|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newswire.ca/news-releases/mark-bonokoski-named-to-canadian-news-hall-of-fame-645084903.html|title=Mark Bonokoski named to Canadian News Hall of Fame|website=newswire.ca|language=en|access-date=2018-05-11|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180512043707/https://www.newswire.ca/news-releases/mark-bonokoski-named-to-canadian-news-hall-of-fame-645084903.html|archive-date=2018-05-12|url-status=live}}</ref> He has been an authoritative and in-depth journalist who has covered topics ranging from civil wars in Africa to federal Canadian politics. His Canadian political coverage has been universally{{tone inline|date=December 2020}} respected by his colleagues for his wit and writing abilities.{{by whom|date=December 2022}} Toronto Mayor John Tory once stated "He's a tough columnist but always a fair columnist."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://torontosun.com/news/local-news/warmington-honouring-our-bono|title=WARMINGTON: Our Bono inducted into Canadian News Hall of Fame|date=2017-11-22|newspaper=Toronto Sun|access-date=2018-05-11|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180511221123/http://torontosun.com/news/local-news/warmington-honouring-our-bono|archive-date=2018-05-11|url-status=live}}</ref>

Before returning to newspapers, he was a senior communications advisor and speechwriter for former federal Transport Minister Lisa Raitt. Previously, he was a newspaper editor and broadcaster primarily associated, until 2013, with [[Sun Media]]/[[Quebecor Media|QMI]].

==Biography==
Bonokoski is a graduate of [[Ryerson Polytechnical Institute]]'s journalism programme. He worked as a general assignment reporter with the ''[[Calgary Herald]]'' and then the ''[[Windsor Star]]'' before joining the ''[[Toronto Sun]]'' in 1974.<ref>{{Cite press release |url=https://www.bizjournals.com/prnewswire/press_releases/2017/09/17/C4247 |website=bizjournals.com |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20180511215510/https://www.bizjournals.com/prnewswire/press_releases/2017/09/17/C4247 |archivedate=11 May 2018 |title=Register |url-access=registration}}{{Dead Link|date=September 2023}}</ref> He was promoted to columnist in 1977 and has served in various capacities with the Sun and its parent companies in the ensuing decades. He was Sun Media's European Bureau Chief from 1988 to 1991, based in [[London, England]]. In 1991, he was appointed editor of the ''[[Ottawa Sun]]'' and then became the paper's publisher and CEO in 1997. In 2000, he returned to writing as national affairs columnist for Sun Media and then returned to the ''Toronto Sun'' as a columnist in 2002. In 2010 he was appointed National Editorial Writer for the Sun chain of newspapers.<ref>{{cite web|title=Mark Bonokoski QMI agency|url=http://www.torontosun.com/author/mark-bonokoski|publisher=QMI|access-date=2011-11-27|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111104145343/http://www.torontosun.com/author/mark-bonokoski|archive-date=2011-11-04|url-status=live}}</ref>  In 2011, he also became a regular contributor on the chain's all-news channel, the [[Sun News Network]], appearing regularly as a commentator or substitute host on various programmes.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://nmc-mic.ca/news/people/sun-columnist-mark-bonokoski-named-canadian-news-hall-fame/|title=Sun columnist Mark Bonokoski named to Canadian News Hall of Fame - News Media Canada|date=2017-11-22|work=News Media Canada|access-date=2018-05-11|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180511214823/https://nmc-mic.ca/news/people/sun-columnist-mark-bonokoski-named-canadian-news-hall-fame/|archive-date=2018-05-11|url-status=live}}</ref> In a round of layoffs at Sun Media, Bonokoski's contract was [[Involuntary termination of employment|terminated]] and his final column appeared July 20, 2013.<ref>{{cite news|title=New challenges, adventures await Bono|url=http://www.torontosun.com/2013/07/19/new-challenges-adventures-await-bono|accessdate=July 20, 2013|newspaper=Toronto Sun|date=July 20, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130822115940/http://www.torontosun.com/2013/07/19/new-challenges-adventures-await-bono|archive-date=August 22, 2013|url-status=live}}</ref> Following his forays as a senior political staffer provincially and later federally,Bonokoski subsequently returned to the newspaper business in 2015 as a contract columnist for the Postmedia Network (Sun Media division), largely on national political affairs, writing upwards of four columns a week.

His freelance work has appeared in ''[[Maclean's]]'' and ''[[Reader's Digest]] among others''.

Bonokoski is also a fishing and [[outdoor enthusiast]] and contributes commentaries to Outdoor Journal Radio. He also provided weekly commentaries to the [[Haliburton Broadcasting Group]] network of Moose-FM radio stations in Ontario's [[cottage country]] under their sale to a Western Canadian chain. He has also been an instructor to fourth-year graduating students at Ryerson University's School of Journalism. Bonokoski won the [[National Newspaper Awards|National Newspaper Award]] for column writing in 2004.

Bonokoski has been married to the former Karen Ann Foley since 1984 (they have one child, Erin).
