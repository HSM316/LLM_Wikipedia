'''''The Flaming Forest''''' is a 1926 American [[Silent film|silent]] [[drama film]] directed by [[Reginald Barker]] and starring [[Antonio Moreno]] and [[Renée Adorée]]. The film is based on the novel of the same name by [[James Oliver Curwood]], and was produced by [[Cosmopolitan Productions]] and distributed by [[Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer]].<ref>[http://www.silentera.com/PSFL/data/F/FlamingForest1926.html ''The Flaming Forest'' at silentera.com database]</ref>  A two-color [[Technicolor]] sequence was shot for a climactic blaze sequence featured in the film.<ref>{{cite book|last=Eames|first=John Douglas |title=The MGM Story: The Complete History Of Fifty Roaring Years|year=1988|publisher=Crown Publishers|isbn=0-517-52613-1|page=31}}</ref>

This is a preserved film at the [[Library of Congress]].<ref>[http://lcweb2.loc.gov/diglib/ihas/loc.mbrs.sfdb.1842/default.html The Library of Congress American Silent Feature Film Survival Catalog:''The Flaming Forest'']</ref><ref>''Catalog of Holdings The American Film Institute Collection and The United Artists Collection at The Library of Congress'' p.59  c.1978 by The American Film Institute</ref>

==Plot==
[[North-West Mounted Police]] sergeant David Carrigan (Antonio Moreno) fights Indians and wooes Jeanne-Marie (Renée Adorée).
