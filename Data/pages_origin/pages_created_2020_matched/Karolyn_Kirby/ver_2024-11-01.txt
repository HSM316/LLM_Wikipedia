'''Karolyn Kirby''' (born June 30, 1961) is an American female retired [[beach volleyball]] player. She won the bronze medal at the [[1997 Beach Volleyball World Championships|1997 World Championships]] in [[Los Angeles, California]], partnering with [[Nancy Reno]].<ref name="beachmajors">{{Cite web |url=https://hamburg2019.beachmajors.com/en/2589/the-1997-world-championships |website=Beachmajors.com |author=@barringer_a |date=April 4, 2019 |title=The 1997 World Championships |access-date=August 25, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230825173352/https://hamburg2019.beachmajors.com/en/2589/the-1997-world-championships |archive-date=August 25, 2023 |url-status=live }}</ref> The pair also won the [[1992 Summer Olympics|1992 Olympic tournament]], at which time beach volleyball was a [[demonstration sport]].<ref name="AVP">{{Cite web |url=https://avp.com/news/avp-101-the-basics-of-beach-volleyball/ |website=[[Association of Volleyball Professionals]] |date=July 10, 2019 |title=AVP 101: The Basics Of Beach Volleyball |access-date=August 25, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221204210306/https://avp.com/news/avp-101-the-basics-of-beach-volleyball/ |archive-date=December 4, 2022 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In her career in beach volleyball, Kirby won 67 tournaments and $680,000 in prizes.<ref name="BVD">{{Cite web |url=http://www.bvbinfo.com/player.asp?ID=1207 |title=Karolyn Kirby |website=[[Beach Volleyball Database]] |access-date=August 16, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230611002929/http://www.bvbinfo.com/player.asp?ID=1207 |archive-date=June 11, 2023 |url-status=live }}</ref> 29 of her tournament wins were with partner [[Liz Masakayan]].<ref name="BVD" /> 

Kirby won numerous awards as a beach volleyball player. She was WPVA Most Valuable Player in 1990, 1991, 1993, and 1994.<ref name="BVD" /> She was WPVA Best Offensive Player in 1990 and WPVA Best Hitter in 1992.<ref name="BVD" /> She was WPVA Best Setter in 1991, 1993, 1994, 1995, 1996, and 1997.<ref name="BVD" />

In 2004, Kirby was inducted into the [[International Volleyball Hall of Fame]].<ref name="HoF">{{Cite web |url=https://www.volleyhall.org/karolyn-kirby.html |website=[[International Volleyball Hall of Fame]] |title=Karolyn Kirby |access-date=August 16, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221206111915/https://www.volleyhall.org/karolyn-kirby.html |archive-date=December 6, 2022 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==College==

Kirby was twice an [[AIAW]] volleyball All-American at [[Utah State University]] (USU), leading the Aggies to consecutive top 10 national finishes from 1979 to 1981.<ref name="UtahState" /> Kirby helped USU to a combined record of 106–38 (.736) over the three years that she played for the school, including a second-place finish in the 1979 AIAW Nationals at 35–5.<ref name="UtahState" /> Additionally, Kirby was twice an Intermountain All-Conference selection (1980–81).<ref name="UtahState">{{Cite web |url=https://utahstateaggies.com/honors/hall-of-fame/karolyn-kirby/97 |website=Utah State University Athletics |title=Karolyn Kirby |access-date=August 25, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230816053234/https://utahstateaggies.com/honors/hall-of-fame/karolyn-kirby/97 |archive-date=August 16, 2023 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Kirby was inducted into the Utah State University Hall of Fame in 1995.<ref name="UtahState" />

==National team==

Kirby was briefly on the [[United States women's national volleyball team]] in 1986, and was a teammate of Masakayan.<ref name="SI">{{Cite magazine |url=https://vault.si.com/vault/1993/07/05/liz-masakayan-and-karolyn-kirby |title=Liz Masakayan and Karolyn Kirby |last=Smith |first=Shelley |date=July 5, 1993 |magazine=[[Sports Illustrated]] |publisher=Time |location=New York City |access-date=August 29, 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221207110639/https://vault.si.com/vault/1993/07/05/liz-masakayan-and-karolyn-kirby |archive-date=December 7, 2022 |url-status=live }}</ref>

==Awards==
* Two-time AIAW All-American
* Goodwill Games bronze medal (indoor) 1986
* Utah State University Hall of Fame 1995
* Four-time WPVA Most Valuable Player 1990, 1991, 1993, 1994
* WPVA Best Offensive Player 1990
* WPVA Best Hitter 1992
* Six-time WPVA Best Setter 1991, 1993, 1994, 1995, 1996, 1997
* Goodwill Games gold medal (beach) 1994
* World Championships bronze medal 1997
* International Volleyball Hall of Fame 2004
