'''Daniel Garbade''' (born 1957) is a Painter, illustrator, [[art director]], [[property master]], and publisher. Born in Switzerland from Swiss-Cuban origins, Garbade is the grand-nephew of [[Adrien Lachenal]], great-grandchild of Cuban sculptor [[Fernando Heydrich]], grandson of [[Theodore Garbade]] and cousin of sculptor [[Juan Esnard Heydrich]].

Garbade lived in Spain from 1983–2011, then taking residence in his hometown Zurich, to exhibit at the Peyer Fine Art Gallery in Zurich. In 2016 he returned to Spain, living in [[Mascaraque]], [[Castile-La Mancha|Castile- La Mancha]]<ref>{{Cite book|title=Historia del arte en Castilla-La Mancha en el siglo XX|last=Cadarso|first=Maria Victoria|publisher=Junta de Comunidades Castilla La Mancha|year=2003|isbn=8477882568|location=Toledo|pages=788|via=www.uclm.es}}</ref> and gained the Spanish Citizenship in 2023.

== Work ==
Garbade is one of Spanish [[Expressionism|expressionists]], who turned to [[Pop art]] during the ''Movida [[La Movida Madrileña|madrileña]]''. His stroke is personal and minimalistic. Writer [[Álvaro Pombo|Alvaro Pombo]] described Garbade's work by coining the term "garbatear".<ref>{{Cite book|title=Los dibujos y oleos de Garbade|last=Pombo|first=Alvaro|publisher=Nicolas|year=1988|location=Worldcat|pages=7}}</ref> Garbade's portraits are melancholy yet humorous and aim to show mankind at its core. Nobel Prize winner [[José Saramago]] once said, "Garbade shows us humanity where it unfolds"<ref>{{Cite book|title=Coctel|last=Saramago|first=Jose|publisher=El Wisli|year=1996|isbn=978-84-615-3679-5|location=Biblioteca nacional Spain|pages=11|via=worldcat}}</ref> His most well-known portraits include those of [[Nancy Cunard]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1988/10/27/009.html|title=La pintura suiza como un reloj|last=Luca de Tena|first=Catalina|date=1988-10-27|website=ABC de las artes|publisher=ABC Diario|access-date=2016-11-17}}</ref>[3], [[Juan Carlos I of Spain|King Juan Carlos I]], [[José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero|Rodriguez Zapatero]], [[Kofi Annan]] and [[Pedro Almodóvar|Pedro Almodovar]]. His image of the naked [[Pope Benedict XVI]] attracted attention in 2006 and was removed from his exhibition at the request of the Swiss government.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Nadie dira nunca que esta desnudo|last=Alas|first=Leopoldo|date=2006-10-06|work=El Mundo M2|via=El Mundo}}</ref>

=== Poetry ===
In 1987, he took part in the publication of poems as co-founder of the [[Signos Magazine|Magazine Signos]]

=== Sculpture ===
On behalf of the [[Dadaists]] of the [[Cabaret Voltaire (Zurich)|Cabaret Voltaire Zurich]], he designed the gravestone of [[Mikhail Bakunin]] at the Bremgarten cemetery, [[Bern]]e. The City of Toledo in Spain invited Garbade to exhibit his sculptures at the [[Oratorio de San Felipe de Neri, Toledo|Oratorio de San Felipe Neri]] in 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Daniel Garbade: Selfis del Pasado - Exhibition at Oratorio San Felipe de Neri: Consorcio de Toledo in Toledo |url=https://www.artrabbit.com/events/daniel-garbade-selfis-del-pasado |access-date=2022-10-10 |website=ArtRabbit |language=en}}</ref>

=== South America ===
Garbade has close ties to [[South America]] and [[Cuba]] through his family: Garbade's grandmother, Aída, was from [[Matanzas]]. [[Mexico]] showed his works at the National Museum of Tequila in Jarisco (2021), at the  Museum of the Agave Landscape and Mining (2022) and at the Galerie Kin (1993) in Mexico City. In 2021, Garbade showed his works in the exhibition dedicated to the [[Bicentennial of the Independence of Peru]] at the Casa Museo Mario Vargas Llosa in [[Arequipa]] (2021). In May 2022, [[The Ludwig Foundation of Cuba]] invited the artist to present his works about his origins in Cuba in its Gallery in [[Havana]] and in the Visual Arts Center of Matanzas, the [[Galería Pedro Esquerré|Pedro Esquerré Gallery]], in two parallel exhibitions.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Vest Y Menté en La Habana - Exhibition at Ludwig Foundation of Cuba in Havana |url=https://www.artrabbit.com/events/vest-y-menté-en-la-habana |access-date=2022-06-08 |website=ArtRabbit |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=ArtFacts |title=Galería Pedro Esquerré {{!}} Institution |url=https://artfacts.net/institution/galeria-pedro-esquerre/51311 |access-date=2022-06-08 |website=ArtFacts |language=en}}</ref>

== Film ==
Since 1979, Garbade has worked as a production manager and later as [[Property master]] for advertising and feature films at [[Condor Films]], Barney, Tardio & Melsky, [[Warner Bros.|Warner Bros]], [[TF1 Group|TF1]] and [[The Ladd Company]]. Garbade as had a hand in films such as [[Five Days One Summer|"Five days one summe]]<nowiki/>r" by [[Fred Zinnemann]] and "[[Espion, lève-toi|Espion leve to]]<nowiki/>i" by [[Yves Boisset]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://worldscinema.org/2013/09/yves-boisset-espion-leve-toi-1982/|title=Yves Boisset espion leve toi|website=worldcinema|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160924200546/http://worldscinema.org/2013/09/yves-boisset-espion-leve-toi-1982/|archive-date=2016-09-24|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Engagement ==
An avid fighter for the rights of homosexuals, he worked for the American Foundation for AIDS Research ([[AmfAR, The Foundation for AIDS Research|AmfAR]]) and the {{Interlanguage link|Fundación Triángulo|es|Fundación Triángulo|WD=}} in Madrid, where he illustrated texts in various writings such as ''Orientaciones''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://issuu.com/triangulo/docs/orientaciones2|title=Hacia una reescritura de la historia|last=Ugarte|first=Javier|date=2000|website=Issuu.com|publisher=Fundacion Triangulo|access-date=2016-11-21}}</ref> In the book ''Cosas de casados,'' which was later turned into an exhibition, he published his drawings on the subject of homosexuality in Art together with [[David Hockney]] and [[Tom of Finland]].<ref>{{Cite book|title=Cosas de casados|last=Gimeno|first=Beatriz|publisher=Asociacion Cultural Visible|year=2006|isbn=978-84-611-14542|location=Madrid|pages=37|via=Stanford University Libraries}}</ref> In 2022, Garbade was invited by the Embassy of Switzerland in India to give a lecture on ''The Queer in Art and Real Life'' with Curator [[Alka Pande|Dr.Alka Pande]], [[Saurabh Kirpal]], Advocate and LGBTQ+ activist and Vivek Raj Anand, CEO of Humsafar Trust.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Pritam |date=2022-10-15 |title=The Queer in Art and Real Life at the Embassy of Switzerland |url=https://www.timesstoday.com/2022/10/15/the-queer-in-art-and-real-life-at-the-embassy-of-switzerland/ |access-date=2022-10-28 |website=Timess Today |language=en-US}}</ref>

His wedding (2006) was the first same-sex marriage in Mascaraque, [[Castilla-La Mancha|Castile- La Mancha]], Spain

Garbade's exhibition ''Côctel,'' with contributions from writers such as [[Rafael Alberti]], [[Vicente Molina Foix]], [[José Saramago]], {{Interlanguage link|Luis Antonio de Villena|es|Luis Antonio de Villena|WD=}}, [[Jesús Ferrero]] and [[Leopoldo Alas Minguez|Leopoldo Alas]], discusses tolerance in the Convent of San Ildefonso (Toledo). The exhibition was supported by the Vice-President of [[UNESCO]] and the [[Swiss government|Swiss Government]].<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=rtouAwAAQBAJ&pg=PA26|title=coctel|last=saramago|first=jose|date=1996|publisher=el wisli|isbn=9788461536795|access-date=2016-11-20}}</ref>

Garbade has been a source of inspiration for intercultural cooperation between [[Switzerland]] and [[Spain]] since 1983. He brought the first Swiss gallery to the ARCO Art fair in 1985, exhibiting artists like [[Pablo Runyan]] or Jorge Arxe. He co-operated with [[Werner Bischof]] and [[John Armleder]] at the [[Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofía|Museo Reina Sofia]], [[Círculo de Bellas Artes|Circulo de Bellas Artes]] and other galleries in the Swiss weeks of Madrid in 1988. In 2012 he was invited to the exhibitions Hispano-Suizo<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.swissinfo.ch/spa/arte-de-ida-y-vuelta/33706648|title=Arte ida y vuelta|last=Carrizo Couto|first=Rodrigo|date=2012-10-16|website=swissinfo|publisher=swissinfo|access-date=2016-11-21}}</ref> in Zurich, and in 2014 to Hispano -Suizo Madrid. As curator, he conducted the exhibition Desayuno para Inmigrantes on the immigration of Swiss artists in Spain. In 1989, he helped François Lachenal for the exhibition ''du Greco a Goya'', a homage to the works of art from the [[Museo del Prado]] saved during the Spanish Civil War in Geneva. His grandfather [[Paul Lachenal]] was involved in the safeguard of the paintings as delegate of the International Committee.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5hfuPwx3lq0C&q=paul+lachenal+prado&pg=PA555|title=Arte en tiempos de guerra|last=Bravo|first=Miguel Cabañas|date=2009|publisher=Editorial CSIC - CSIC Press|isbn=9788400089429|language=es}}</ref>
