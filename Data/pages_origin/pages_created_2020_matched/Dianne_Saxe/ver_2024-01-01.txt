'''Dianne Saxe''' (née Shulman) is a Canadian lawyer and politician who was elected to represent [[Ward 11 University—Rosedale]] on [[Toronto City Council]] following the [[2022 Toronto municipal election|2022 municipal election]]. Before entering politics, Saxe practised [[environmental law]] and served as the last [[Environmental Commissioner of Ontario|environmental commissioner of Ontario]] from 2015 to 2019. She was deputy leader of the [[Green Party of Ontario]] (GPO) from 2020 to 2022.

== Legal career ==
=== Education ===
Saxe studied law at [[Osgoode Hall Law School]], earning an [[Bachelor of Laws]] (L.L.B.) in 1974. She was called to the bar in 1976 and earned a Ph.D. in law from Osgoode in 1991.<ref name="SaxeFactsAbout">{{cite web |title=Chronology |url=https://saxefacts.com/dianne-saxe-climate-lawyer/ |access-date=March 8, 2022 |website=Saxe Facts}}</ref>

=== Career ===
Prior to entering the government sector, Saxe worked in private practice with two major law firms and then ran an environmental law boutique firm for 25 years. Her early career focused on the intersection of environmental law and [[corporate liability]], while her more recent practice has centred on [[climate change law|climate change]] and related law. She has published widely on environmental issues. From 1975 to 1989, Saxe practiced law with the [[Government of Ontario]].<ref name="SaxeFactsAbout" /><ref name="starmain">{{cite web |last=Rushowy |first=Kristin |date=October 8, 2021 |title=Political parties are aligning their stars for next year's Ontario election |url=https://www.thestar.com/politics/provincial/2021/10/08/political-parties-are-aligning-their-stars-for-next-years-ontario-election.html |access-date=February 13, 2022 |publisher=The Toronto Star}}</ref>

In 1991, Saxe moved into private practice. She represented the [[Association of Municipalities of Ontario]] (AMO) in their successful $115 million claim against Stewardship Ontario for the cost of Ontario's [[Blue box recycling system|Blue Box]] program in 2014.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://stewardshipontario.ca/news/update-on-blue-box-arbitration/ |title= Update on Blue Box Arbitration | publisher=Stewardship Ontario |access-date=March 8, 2022 |quote=}}</ref>

=== Environmental Commissioner of Ontario ===
Saxe was unanimously named the [[Environmental Commissioner of Ontario|environmental commissioner of Ontario]] in 2015 by the [[Legislative Assembly of Ontario]] for a five year term.<ref name="osgoode"/> The commissioner was an independent officer of the legislature which monitored the ''[[Environmental Bill of Rights]]'', and submitted annual reports on the province's progress on each of [[energy conservation]], [[environmental protection]] and [[climate change]]. She was also permitted to deliver special reports. As commissioner, she delivered 17 reports to the legislature on topics including environmental injustice to First Nations,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ecojustice.ca/environmental-commissioner-report/|title=Environmental Commissioner of Ontario calls upon province to take action in Chemical Valley|date=October 25, 2017|website=Ecojustice}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dragun.ca/environmental-commissioner-of-ontarios-report/|title=Environmental Commissioner of Ontario's Report|date=November 8, 2017|publisher=Dragun Corporation}}</ref> [[electricity]], waste and [[circular economy]], [[endangered species]], [[water pollution]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://globalnews.ca/news/4657993/ontario-environmental-watchdog-sewage/|title=Raw sewage overflowing into waterways at alarming rate: Ontario watchdog|publisher=Global News|date=November 13, 2018|access-date=2022-05-17}}</ref> [[soil health]] and [[Politics of climate change|climate policy]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://saxefacts.com/environmental-policies-in-canada/|title=ECO Reports and Environmental Policies in Canada|first=Dianne|last=Saxe}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.auditor.on.ca/en/content/reporttopics/environment.html  |title=Reports by Topic: Environment |publisher=Office of the Auditor General of Ontario |date=2021 |access-date=May 16, 2022}}</ref>

On November 15, 2018, the [[Progressive Conservative Party of Ontario|Progressive Conservative]] (PC) government announced their intention to abolish the position of environment commissioner, transferring some of its functions to the [[Auditor General of Ontario|auditor general]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/opinion/contributors/2018/11/19/eliminating-ontarios-environmental-commissioner-a-short-sighted-move.html|title=Opinion &#124; Eliminating Ontario's environmental commissioner a short-sighted move|date=November 19, 2018|website=thestar.com|access-date=2022-05-17}}</ref> Saxe had published reports critical of the incoming administration's environmental positions, including the absence of a climate change policy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tvo.org/article/the-environmental-commissioner-shows-why-the-powers-doug-ford-just-eliminated-are-so-important|title=The environmental commissioner shows why the powers Doug Ford just eliminated are so important|website=TVO.org|access-date=2022-05-17|date=November 22, 2018}}</ref> The decision to eliminate independent environmental oversight was widely reported on. More than 200 scientists and researchers sent an open letter to Premier [[Doug Ford]] calling for reconsideration.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://evidencefordemocracy.ca/en/content/scientists-send-letter-ford-urging-him-reconsider-eliminating-office-environmental|title=Scientists' letter to Ford on the Environmental Commissioner of Ontario|date=December 4, 2018|website=Evidence For Democracy}}</ref> Her last report was heavily critical of the Ford government.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ipolitics.ca/2019/03/27/ontario-environment-commissioner-exits-warning-of-frightening-policies/|title=Ontario environment commissioner exits warning of 'frightening' policies|date=March 27, 2019}}</ref> After 25 years, the position of the environmental commissioner of Ontario ceased to exist when the ''Environmental Bill of Rights'' was amended on April 1, 2019.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web |date=July 24, 2014 |title=Law Document English View |url=https://www.ontario.ca/laws/view |website=Ontario.ca}}</ref>

=== Return to private practice ===
After being the Environmental Commissioner of Ontario, Saxe reopened her SaxeFacts environmental law practice focussed on climate issues.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Saxe |first=Dianne |title=Climate Change Toronto and the Climate Crisis |url=https://saxefacts.com/ |website=Saxe Facts}}</ref> She publishes articles and a blog and presents on climate issues.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Saxe |first=Dianne |title=Informative Climate Law Articles |url=https://saxefacts.com/climate-law-articles/}}</ref><ref name="opencanada.org">{{Cite web |date=September 16, 2019 |title=10 principles to guide the transition to a green economy |url=https://opencanada.org/10-principles-to-guide-the-transition-to-a-green-economy/ |website=Open Canada}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Saxe |first=Dianne |date=April 21, 2020 |title=Canada's murky bailout deal for oil and gas will cost us all |url=https://www.nationalobserver.com/2020/04/21/opinion/canadas-murky-bailout-deal-oil-and-gas-will-cost-us-all |website=National Observer}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Saxe |first=Dianne |title=Learn More in our Climate Risk Management Blog |url=https://saxefacts.com/climate-risk-management/}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Saxe |first=Dianne |title=In-Depth and Informative Climate Presentations |url=https://saxefacts.com/climate-presentations/}}</ref> She was a McMurtry Clinical Fellow at [[Osgoode Hall Law School]] from 2019 to 2020,<ref>{{Cite web |title=Meet the McMurtry Fellows |url=https://www.osgoode.yorku.ca/faculty-and-research/fellowships/mcmurtry-visiting-clinical-fellowship-call-applications/meet-the-mcmurtry-fellow/ |website=Osgoode Hall Law School}}</ref> is a senior fellow at [[Massey College]], and an adjunct professor at the [[The University of Toronto School of the Environment|University of Toronto School of the Environment]]. She hosts a podcast called "Green Economy Heroes" features interviews with green business leaders,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Saxe |first=Dianne |title=Green Economy Heroes Climate Podcast |url=https://saxefacts.com/climate-podcast/}}</ref> and has also been a public support to the youth climate strikes movement in Toronto.<ref>{{Cite web |last=ICI.Radio-Canada.ca |first=Zone Environnement- |title=Outiller les jeunes pour la lutte contre les changements climatiques |url=https://ici.radio-canada.ca/nouvelle/1312452/jeunes-lutte-changements-climatiques-greve |access-date=July 25, 2020 |website=Radio-Canada.ca |language=fr-ca}}</ref>

== Political career ==
=== 2022 provincial election ===
Saxe ran for the [[Green Party of Ontario]] in [[University—Rosedale (provincial electoral district)|University—Rosedale]]. Her candidacy was confirmed along with the deputy leader role on November 30, 2020.<ref name="gponom">{{cite web |date=November 16, 2020 |title=Dianne Saxe named Deputy Leader of the Green Party of Ontario |url=https://gpo.ca/2020/11/16/dianne-saxe-named-deputy-leader-of-the-green-party-of-ontario/ |access-date=February 13, 2022 |website= |publisher=Green Party of Ontario}}</ref> She has cited the climate crisis as a primary reason for her political run as well as the well-publicized conflict with Ford over the closure of her office.<ref name=cjn>{{cite web |url=https://thecjn.ca/news/environmental-lawyer-dianne-saxe-credits-jewish-values-and-concern-for-the-planet-for-her-role-in-ontarios-green-party/ |title=Environmental lawyer Dianne Saxe credits Jewish values and concern for the planet for her role in Ontario's Green Party |publisher=Canadian Jewish News |access-date=May 21, 2022 |last=Appel |first=Jeremy |date= April 8, 2022}}</ref><ref name=cbcplatform>{{cite web |url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/toronto/green-party-platform-costed-ontario-election-1.6449152 |title=Green Party campaign platform includes $65B for 'new climate economy,' major health-care promises |publisher=CBC |date= May 11, 2022 |access-date=May 21, 2022|last=Powers |first=Lucas}}</ref> The riding was one of a small number of ridings the Ontario Greens targeted to add to the single-seat caucus of provincial leader [[Mike Schreiner]] in the [[2022 Ontario general election|2022 election]]; Saxe featured prominently in party campaign messaging and her riding was chosen for the platform launch.<ref name=cbcplatform/> Housing policy and [[urban sprawl|sprawl]]—key policy battles in [[Greater Toronto]]—were a focus of Saxe's campaign to curb Ontario emissions.<ref name=cjn/> The official party platform listed mental health, affordable housing, and the climate economy as its primary election pillars.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://gpo.ca/platform/ |title=The Green Plan |publisher=Green Party of Ontario |access-date=May 21, 2022 }}</ref> Saxe came in fourth place, with 6,092 total votes for a share of 15.9 per cent; the riding was won by the incumbent, [[Ontario New Democratic Party|New Democratic Party]] (NDP) [[Member of Provincial Parliament (Canada)|member of Provincial Parliament]] (MPP) [[Jessica Bell (politician)|Jessica Bell]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://newsinteractives.cbc.ca/elections/ontario/2022/results/ |title=Ontario Votes 2022 |publisher=CBC |website=Encyclopedia of Things |access-date=June 12, 2022}}</ref> Saxe nearly tripled the Green vote share achieved in the [[2018 Ontario general election|2018 provincial election]].

=== 2022 municipal election ===
[[Ward 11 University—Rosedale]] was left vacant for the 2022 election when Councillor [[Mike Layton]] announced he would not seek re-election. Saxe announced her candidacy for the [[2022 Toronto municipal election]], concluding on October 24, 2022.<ref name="nominations">{{cite web |date=20 August 2022 |title=List of Candidates & Third Party Advertisers |url=https://www.toronto.ca/city-government/elections/candidate-list/ |access-date=20 August 2022 |website=City of Toronto}}</ref> Saxe stated that she was running as an independent municipally and would resign as deputy leader of the GPO if elected.<ref name="platform">{{cite web |last= |first= |date= |title=Platform |url=https://www.votefordianne.ca/platform |access-date=20 October 2022 |website=Votefordianne |publisher=Dianne Saxe |quote=}}</ref>

Saxe list housing, mobility, climate and sustainability, and a city in good repair as her primary platform planks.<ref name="platform" />

She won with 8,614 votes and 35.37 per cent of the total, a little more than 100 votes more than runner-up Norm Di Pasquale, a Catholic school trustee.<ref>{{cite web |last= |first= |date= |title=2022 Municipal Election Results |url=https://electionresults.toronto.ca/ |access-date=October 26, 2022 |website= |publisher=City of Toronto |quote=}}</ref> She officially took office on November 15, 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |title=2022 Candidates' Guide - Ontario municipal council and school board elections |url=http://www.ontario.ca/document/2022-candidates-guide-ontario-municipal-council-and-school-board-elections |access-date=2022-11-01 |website=ontario.ca |language=en}}</ref>

===City Council===

Saxe's first year on Toronto City Council coincided with the resignation of Mayor [[John Tory]] and the by-election that saw [[Olivia Chow]] assume the mayoralty. Saxe has held a number of committee appointments across both administrations, often relating to her background in environment and law. Chief among her appointments is the powerful Infrastructure and Environment Committee. Under Mayor Chow she has also been appointed to the board of the [[Toronto Transit Commission]] and [[Toronto Hydro]].

Her full list of council roles:

* Infrastructure and Environment Committee
* Toronto and East York Community Council
* Toronto Atmospheric Fund Board of Directors
* Toronto Transit Commission
* National Zero Waste Council Management Board
* Toronto and Region Conservation Authority
* Toronto Hydro Corporation
* 58 Cecil Street Community Centre Board of Management
* William H.(Bill) Bolton Arena Board of Management<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.toronto.ca/city-government/council/members-of-council/councillor-ward-11/ |title= Councillor Dianne Saxe |last= |first= |date= |website= |publisher=City of Toronto |access-date= September 18, 2023 |quote=}}</ref>

== Awards and recognition ==
Saxe is a recipient of numerous awards, including specialist certifications from [[The Law Society of Upper Canada]],<ref name="osgoode">{{cite web|title=Dianne Saxe '74, '91 (PhD) appointed Environmental Commissioner of Ontario|url=http://www.osgoode.yorku.ca/news/dianne-saxe-74-91-phd-appointed-environmental-commissioner-of-ontario-2|website=Osgoode Hall Law School|accessdate=24 March 2016}}</ref> [[Osgoode Hall Law School]] Alumni Gold Key for Achievement <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.osgoode.yorku.ca/alumni/alumni-awards/gold-key/gold-key-award-winners/|title=Alumni Gold Key Recipients|website=Osgoode Hall Law School}}</ref> and a 2020 Law Society Medal for exemplary leadership in environmental law.<ref name="LSOmedals">{{cite web|title= Law Society announces 2020 award recipients |url=https://lso.ca/news-events/news/latest-news-2020/law-society-announces-2020-award-recipients|website=Law Society of Ontario|accessdate=10 June 2020}}</ref> In August 2020, Saxe completed her training to become an En-ROADS Climate Ambassador, a member in the international network that leads climate simulation events developed by Climate Interactiv and the MIT Sloan Sustainability Initiative.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Meet our Ambassadors|url=https://www.climateinteractive.org/tools/en-roads/climate-ambassadors/|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Climate Interactive|language=en-US}}</ref> Saxe is also a trained Climate Reality Leader, having been personally trained by [[Al Gore]] on the climate crisis and its solutions.

Saxe sat on a number of public and private boards, including Draxis Health, Solarshare,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.solarbonds.ca/|title=SolarShare |website=SolarShare|access-date=2022-05-17}}</ref> [[WindShare]] and Evergreen,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.evergreen.ca/|title=Home &#124; Evergreen |website=www.evergreen.ca|access-date=2022-05-17}}</ref> helped to manage the endowment of the [[Ontario Bar Association]] and is recognized as board-ready by Women in Capital Markets.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wcm.ca/women-in-leadership/boards|title=WCM Board Ready Directory|publisher=Women in Capital Markets|access-date=2022-05-17}}</ref> In June 2022, she was awarded an honorary doctorate of [[Environmental Studies]] from the [[University of Waterloo]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://uwaterloo.ca/news/inspiring-leaders-will-receive-honorary-degrees-university |title=Inspiring leaders will receive honorary degrees from University of Waterloo |publisher=University of Waterloo |date=May 31, 2022 |access-date=June 22, 2022}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Saxe is the daughter of [[Morton Shulman]],<ref>{{Cite web |title=Morton Shulman and Saxe family &#124; Ontario Jewish Archives |url=http://search.ontariojewisharchives.org/Permalink/accessions24577 |website=search.ontariojewisharchives.org}}</ref> a physician who served as a NDP MPP in the 1960s and 1970s. She is the mother of [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology|MIT]] neuroscience professor [[Rebecca Saxe]], and [[University of Toronto]] civil engineering professor, Shoshanna Saxe.
