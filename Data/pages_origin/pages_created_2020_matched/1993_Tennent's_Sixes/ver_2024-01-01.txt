The '''1993 [[Tennents' Sixes]]''' was the tenth and final staging of the indoor 6-a-side football tournament. It was held at the [[Scottish Exhibition and Conference Centre]] (SECC) in [[Glasgow]] on 24 and 25 January.

Clubs from the [[1992&ndash;93 Scottish Premier Division|1992-93]] Premier Division season competed except [[Rangers F.C.|Rangers]] and [[Dundee F.C.|Dundee]] and the two group winners and runners-up qualified to the semi-finals and [[Partick Thistle F.C.|Partick Thistle]] became the final Sixes winners beating [[Airdrieonians F.C. (1878)|Airdrieonians]] 4&ndash;3.

== Incidents and events ==
*[[Davie Irons]] of [[Partick Thistle F.C.|Partick Thistle]] was voted Player of the Tournament.<ref>{{ Cite news | url = https://www.britishnewspaperarchive.co.uk/viewer/bl/0003741/19930130/045/0045 | title = MAN OF BATTLE | newspaper = [[Daily Record (Scotland)|Daily Record]] | date = 30 January 1993 | access-date = 9 April 2023 | url-access=subscription | via = [[British Newspaper Archive]] }}</ref>
*[[Darren Jackson]] of [[Hibernian F.C.|Hibs]]' goal against [[Aberdeen F.C.|Aberdeen]] was the 900th goal scored in the tournament's history.<ref>{{ Cite news | url = https://www.britishnewspaperarchive.co.uk/viewer/bl/0000540/19930125/366/0026 | title = Creaney at seven in Sixes as Hearts keep up chase | newspaper = [[The Scotsman]] | date = 25 January 1993 | access-date = 9 April 2023 | url-access=subscription | via = British Newspaper Archive }}</ref><ref>{{ Cite news | url = https://www.britishnewspaperarchive.co.uk/viewer/bl/0000578/19930125/500/0022?browse=false | title = Aberdeen are hit for six | newspaper = [[Aberdeen Press and Journal|Press and Journal]] | date = 25 January 1993 | access-date = 9 April 2023 | url-access=subscription | via = British Newspaper Archive }}</ref>
*[[Tosh McKinlay]] of [[Heart of Midlothian F.C.|Hearts]]' goal against [[Airdrieonians F.C. (1878)|Airdrie]] was the 1000th goal scored in the tournament's history. He and [[Davie Cooper]] (scorer of the 1st ever goal) were later presented with a special quaich each.<ref>{{ Cite news | url = https://www.britishnewspaperarchive.co.uk/viewer/bl/0002391/19930129/036/0036 | title = Fan-tastic! | newspaper = Airdrie & Coatbridge Advertiser | date = 29 January 1993 | access-date = 9 April 2023 | url-access=subscription | via = British Newspaper Archive }}</ref>
* [[Huntly F.C.|Huntly]] ([[Highland League Cup]] holders) played [[Vale of Leithen F.C.|Vale of Leithen]] (from the [[East of Scotland Football League|EoSFL]]) in an [[exhibition match]].<ref>{{ Cite news | url = https://www.britishnewspaperarchive.co.uk/viewer/bl/0000578/19930126/514/0026 | title = Huntly hit by goal barags | newspaper = Press and Journal | date = 30 January 1993 | access-date = 9 April 2023 | url-access=subscription | via = British Newspaper Archive }}</ref>
*[[Hibernian F.C.|Hibs]] exited the group stage in dubious fashion. Referee [[Bill Crombie]] penalized Hibs for time wasting in the [[Celtic F.C.|Celtic]] match, despite Hibs being in possession of the ball with two players still in the opponents half. Celtic scored from it and as a result of that goal [[St Johnstone F.C.|St Johnstone]] qualified at the expense of Hibs because of goal difference.<ref>{{ Cite news | url = https://www.britishnewspaperarchive.co.uk/viewer/BL/0000578/19930126/513/0026?browse=False | title = Partick polish off diamonds in the Sixes | newspaper = Press and Journal | date = 26 January 1993 | access-date = 9 April 2023 | url-access=subscription | via = British Newspaper Archive }}</ref>
*The 1993 edition brought the sponsors ''[[Tennent Caledonian]]'s'' total investment to over £3,000,000 in the history of the tournament.<ref>{{ Cite news | url = https://www.britishnewspaperarchive.co.uk/viewer/BL/0003741/19930118/036/0036?browse=False | title = See the TENNENTS SIXES free! | newspaper = Daily Record | date = 18 January 1993 | access-date = 9 April 2023 | url-access=subscription | via = British Newspaper Archive }}</ref>
