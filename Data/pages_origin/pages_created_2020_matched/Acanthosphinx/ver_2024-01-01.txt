'''''Acanthosphinx''''' is a [[monotypic]] [[moth]] genus in the family [[Sphingidae]] erected by [[Per Olof Christopher Aurivillius]] in 1891. Its only species, '''''Acanthosphinx guessfeldti''''', the '''widow sphinx''', was [[Species description|first described]] by [[Hermann Dewitz]] in 1879.<ref>{{cite web |first=Markku |last=Savela |url=http://ftp.funet.fi/pub/sci/bio/life/insecta/lepidoptera/ditrysia/bombycoidea/sphingidae/smerinthinae/acanthosphinx/ |title=''Acanthosphinx'' Aurivillius, 1891 |website=Lepidoptera and Some Other Life Forms |access-date=November 15, 2017}}</ref> It is known from forests from [[Sierra Leone]] to the [[Congo Basin|Congo]], [[Angola]], [[Zambia]], [[Malawi]], [[Tanzania]] and [[Uganda]].<ref>{{cite journal |last=Carcasson |first=R. H. |author-link=Robert Herbert Carcasson |date=1967 |url=https://www.biodiversitylibrary.org/page/51111621#page/279/mode/1up |title=Revised Catalogue of the African Sphingidae (Lepidoptera) with Descriptions of the East African species |journal=Journal of the East Africa Natural History Society and National Museum |volume=26 |issue=3 |pages=1–173 |via=Biodiversity Heritage Library}}</ref>

The length of each forewing is 57–70&nbsp;mm.

The larvae feed on ''[[Bridelia micrantha]]''.<ref>{{cite web |first1=J. |last1=De Prins |first2=W. |last2=De Prins |date=2017 |url=http://www.afromoths.net/plants_species/show/208 |title=Euphorbiaceae: ''Bridelia micrantha'' (Hochst.) Baill. |website=Afromoths |access-date=November 15, 2017}}</ref>
