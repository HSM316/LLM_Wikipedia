'''Jessica Matten''' born in [[Alberta]], is a Canadian [[First Nations]] actress.<ref>https://m.imdb.com/name/nm3906656/bio?ref_=m_mn_ov_bio</ref>

Matten’s breakthrough came from a short film directed by [[Elle-Máijá Tailfeathers]] which won Best Canadian Action Short at the imagiNative festival in Toronto, called A Red Girl’s Reasoning. It was also the winner of the 2012 Vancouver Crazy8s Competition.<ref>http://postcity.com/Eat-Shop-Do/Do/January-2017/The-game-of-furs/</ref> 

Matten has a recurring role in the [[Discovery Channel]] and [[Netflix]] original production [[Frontier (2016 TV series)]]<ref>https://www.newswire.ca/news-releases/discoverys-original-dramatic-adventure-series-frontier-returns-for-world-premiere-of-season-2-on-october-18-as-series-renewed-for-season-3-646149433.html</ref><ref>https://www.hypable.com/frontier-season-2-women/</ref> Which is set to return for a third series.<ref>https://www.tvinsider.com/654426/jason-momoa-announces-frontier-season-3-instagram-video/</ref>

Matten previously had a recurring role in ATPN and [[Showcase]] serial [[Blackstone (TV series)]].<ref>https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/jennifer-podemski-jessica-matten-board-692778</ref>

She plays a recurring character in [[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|CBC]] drama [[Burden of Truth (TV series)]]<ref>http://www.postcity.com/Eat-Shop-Do/Do/December-2017/Frontiers-Jessica-Matten-favourite-books/</ref>

She is in the forthcoming [[Boom! Studios]] production of [[The Empty Man]].<ref>https://www.bleedingcool.com/2017/02/16/boom-studios-logo-front-empty-man-movie/</ref>

==Personal Life==
Of British, Chinese and Metis-Cree descent. She spent time at the Maskwacis reserve south of Edmonton. Her mother’s maiden name was Wild Horse, she has discussed wanting to help create a positive impression of and for the First Nation people as well as using her position to highlight issues they face and advocating for opportunities within First Nation communities. Matten started the N8V Girls social media campaign and website to raise awareness of the murdered and missing indigenous peoples in Canada.<ref>https://www.n8vgirls.com/</ref><ref>https://tworowtimes.com/arts-and-culture/thru-reddoors-youth-empowerment-summit-returns-look-creative-industries/</ref>

A former dancer, she performed in front of 20,000 people in Sourh Korea. <ref>   
http://www.flare.com/celebrity/entertainment/ashley-callingbull-in-conversation-with-frontier-star-jessica-matten/</ref> <ref>http://www.cowboysindians.com/2018/02/generation-next/</ref>

Matten graduated from the University of Alberta with a degree in Human Ecology

With her mother Jessica runs Lemon Cree, an Aboriginal wellness and fitness company that has helped thousands of Aboriginal people achieve goals in fitness and health.
Jessica runs multiple monthly articles and the magazine “Active Cree” which is distributed monthly to 60,000 people across Northern Ontario. She runs acting and fashion workshops on the First Nation community.
