'''Canjilón Mountain''' is a {{convert|10922|ft|0}}. mountain approximately six miles northeast of the village of Canjilón,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.digitalabiquiu.com/pages/tours/canjilonlakes_t.html |title=Canjilón Lakes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120201104346/http://www.digitalabiquiu.com/pages/tours/canjilonlakes_t.html |archivedate=February 1, 2012 |publisher=Digital Abiquiu |accessdate=February 12, 2014}}</ref> in the [[Carson National Forest]].

The word ''cajilon'' is the term for "deer antler" in [[Spanish in the United States#New Mexico|Northern New Mexican Spanish]].<ref name="Julyan">{{cite book |last=Julyan |first=Robert |title=The place names of New Mexico |year=1998 |location=Albuquerque, NM |publisher=University of New Mexico Press |isbn=978-0826316899 }}<!--|accessdate=February 12, 2014--></ref> The mountain was so named because of its resemblance to an antler.<ref name="Julyan"/> The mountain gave its name to the nearby creek (Canjilon Creek), to the nearby lakes (Canjilon lakes), and to the village of [[Canjilon, New Mexico|Canjilon]].<ref name="Julyan"/>

Canjilón Mountain was the site of a 3.7 magnitude earthquake on June 4, 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ees.nmt.edu/Geop/recentquakes.html  |title=Information on New Mexico Seismic Activity from the New Mexico Tech Seismological Observatory: Earthquake between Chama and Abiqui, NM |publisher=Department of Earth and Environmental Science, New Mexico Institute of Mining and Technology |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080927040943/http://www.ees.nmt.edu/Geop/recentquakes.html|archive-date=27 September 2008 |url-status=dead |df=dmy}}</ref>
