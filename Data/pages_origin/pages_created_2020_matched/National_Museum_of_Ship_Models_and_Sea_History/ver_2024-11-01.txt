The '''National Museum of Ship Models and Sea History''' is a private non-profit museum, located in [[Sadorus, Illinois]]. It features [[ship model]]s from around the world and throughout history.<ref>{{cite web|title=National Museum of Ship Models and Sea History|url=http://www.atlasobscura.com/places/national-museum-of-ship-models-and-sea-history|publisher=Atlas Obscura|accessdate=12 December 2015}}</ref>

Recent exhibits include a 27-foot model of the {{RMS|Queen Mary}} made entirely out of one million toothpicks. The collection includes ship models from the movies ''[[Tora! Tora! Tora!]]'', ''[[Cleopatra (1963 film)|Cleopatra]]'', and ''[[Ben-Hur (1959 film)|Ben-Hur]]'' as well as from the television series ''[[Tugboat Annie Sails Again]].''

The museum is open on Saturdays (11 a.m. - 4:30&nbsp;p.m.) Saturdays from April 1 to November 30; also by appointment, including through the winter holidays.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sadorus museum crammed full of maritime history with models, texts, much more|url=http://www.news-gazette.com/news/local/2012-12-20/sadorus-museum-crammed-full-maritime-history-models-texts-much-more.html|publisher=News Gazette|accessdate=22 December 2016}}</ref>
