'''Elizabeth "Lisa" McGee''' (born 1980)<ref>{{Cite web |title=Profile - Lisa McGee - BBC Sounds |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sounds/play/m0016phd |access-date=2022-05-24 |website=www.bbc.co.uk |language=en-GB}}</ref> is an Irish<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/sunday-life/news/lisa-mcgee-i-had-to-defend-being-irish-41163580.html|title=Lisa McGee: I had to defend being Irish|newspaper=Belfasttelegraph|via=www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk}}</ref> playwright and screenwriter. McGee is the creator and writer of ''[[Derry Girls]]'', a comedy series that began airing on Channel 4 in the UK in January 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.channel4.com/info/press/news/casting-announced-for-new-channel-4-comedy-derry-girls|title=Casting announced for new Channel 4 comedy Derry Girls - |publisher=Channel 4 - Info - Press|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170701230950/http://www.channel4.com/info/press/news/casting-announced-for-new-channel-4-comedy-derry-girls|archive-date=July 1, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.radiotimes.com/news/tv/2019-04-09/derry-girls-writer-lisa-mcgee-series-3-james-erin/|title=Derry Girls writer Lisa McGee breaks down the series 2 finale, James and Erin's budding romance and what might happen next|last=Allen|first=Ben|date=9 April 2019|website=Radio Times|access-date=10 April 2019}}</ref> In 2018, she was listed as one of [[100 Women (BBC)|BBC's 100 Women]].<ref name="bbc">{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-46225037|title=BBC 100 Women 2018: Who is on the list?|date=19 November 2018|work=BBC News|access-date=21 November 2018|language=en-GB}}</ref>

==Career==
She was writer on attachment with the [[Royal National Theatre]] in London in 2006. Her plays include ''Jump'', ''The Heights'', ''Nineteen Ninety Two'', and ''Girls and Dolls'', for which she won the Stewart Parker Trust New Playwright Bursary 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nickhernbooks.co.uk/girls-and-dolls|title=Nick Hern Books - Girls and Dolls By Lisa McGee|website=Nick Hern Books}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tinderbox.org.uk/about-us/major-achievements/|title=Major Achievements - Tinderbox Theatre|publisher=|access-date=4 January 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180105180338/http://www.tinderbox.org.uk/about-us/major-achievements/|archive-date=5 January 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

McGee's television credits include ''The Things I Haven't Told You'' for [[BBC Three]],<ref>{{cite AV media |url-status = live |archive-url = https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211211/65luFLpUJCg |archive-date = 2021-12-11| url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=65luFLpUJCg |title = THE THINGS I HAVEN'T TOLD YOU - TX Trailer |website=[[YouTube]]}}{{cbignore}}</ref> the Irish television series ''[[Raw (TV series)|Raw]]'' which she created for [[RTÉ]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rte.ie/tv/raw/ |title=RTÉ Television - RAW |website=www.rte.ie |access-date=12 January 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090116071432/http://www.rte.ie/tv/raw/ |archive-date=16 January 2009 |url-status=dead}}</ref> time as a writer for three series of the [[BAFTA]]-nominated [[Being Human (UK TV series)|''Being Human'']] for the [[BBC]], the [[Channel 4]] sitcom [[London Irish (TV series)|''London Irish'']], which she created, writing for the [[Golden Globe]]-nominated drama series ''[[The White Queen (miniseries)|The White Queen]]'' for [[BBC 1]], ''[[Indian Summers]]'' for Channel 4, and ''The Deceived'' for [[Channel 5 (British TV channel)|Channel 5]] co-written with her husband Tobias Beer.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Hardy|first=Jane|date=2020-08-05|title=Lisa McGee and husband Tobias Beer on their new television drama The Deceived|url=http://www.irishnews.com/arts/2020/08/05/news/lisa-mcgee-and-husband-tobias-beer-on-their-new-television-drama-the-deceived-2025806/|access-date=2021-01-24|website=The Irish News|language=en}}</ref> Her stage play ''Jump'' has been adapted into a [[Jump (2012 film)|film]].

==Personal life==
McGee was born in [[Derry]], [[Northern Ireland]] to an [[Irish Catholic]] family.<ref>{{Cite news |last=O'Dornan |first=David |date=December 19, 2021 |title=Lisa McGee: I had to defend being Irish |newspaper=[[Belfast Telegraph]] |url=https://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/sunday-life/news/lisa-mcgee-i-had-to-defend-being-irish-41163580.html}}</ref> She studied at [[Thornhill College]] and then studied Drama at [[The Queen's University of Belfast]].<ref name="BelfastTelegraph Dec17">{{Cite news |date=20 December 2017 |title=Derry Girls could become TV hit - if viewers can understand them |newspaper=Belfast Telegraph |url=https://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/entertainment/news/derry-girls-could-become-tv-hit-if-viewers-can-understand-them-36423218.html |access-date=6 April 2018}}</ref> 

She is married to actor [[Tobias Beer]].<ref name="BelfastTelegraph Dec17" />

In December 2022 she received the [[Freedom of the City]] of Derry.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lisa McGee: Derry Girls writer awarded freedom of city |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-63856911 |website=BBC News |access-date=5 December 2022 |date=5 December 2022}}</ref>
