'''Denis Hardy''' (27 January 1936 – 12 May 2016)<ref name=profile>[http://www.assnat.qc.ca/fr/deputes/hardy-denis-3595/biographie.html Profile, Quebec Assembly] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160605042021/http://www.assnat.qc.ca/fr/deputes/hardy-denis-3595/biographie.html |date=2016-06-05 }} {{in lang|fr}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nordinfo.com/Actualites/2016-05-30/article-4543871/Deces-et-funerailles-de-l%26rsquo%3Bex-depute-Denis-Hardy/1|title=Décès et funérailles de l’ex-député Denis Hardy|date=30 May 2016|author=Benoît Bilodeau|work=Nord Info et Voix des Mille-Iles}} {{in lang|fr}}</ref> was a Canadian [[Quebec Liberal Party|Liberal]] Quebec politician.

He was born in [[Sainte-Thérèse, Quebec|Sainte-Thérèse]], [[Quebec]] to Hervé Hardy and Jeanne Lafleur. He studied law and political science at the [[University of Montreal]] and went on to practice law. He then became a professor of law and political science at [[Collège Lionel-Groulx]].<ref name=profile />

He was elected as the member of the [[National Assembly of Quebec]] for [[Terrebonne (provincial electoral district)|Terrebonne]] in 1965 and was defeated for re-election the next year. He was then elected to the same position in 1970 and 1973, serving from 1970 until 1976. He was the body's [[Vice President of the National Assembly of Quebec|Vice President]] from 1970 until 1973.<ref name=profile /><ref>[http://www.assnat.qc.ca/fr/patrimoine/vicepres.html Vice-présidents depuis 1909] {{in lang|fr}}</ref>

Hardy married Rosemère Caron in 1969. After retiring, he held many civic positions and practiced law with the firm of Duquette and Hardy.<ref name=profile />
