"Thu Tran is a writer and producer, known for Food Party (2009)." <ref>https://www.imdb.com/name/nm3494426/bio?ref_=nm_ov_bio_sm</ref>
'''Thu Tran '''is the<ref>https://www.nytimes.com/2009/06/06/arts/television/06food.html</ref> star of the [[Independent Film Channel]] television show [http://www.ifc.com/food-party Food Party] and [https://web.archive.org/web/20130910021645/http://mtvother.com/ MTVother] web series [https://archive.is/20130909053450/http://mtvother.com/mtvothershows/late-night-munchies/ Late Night Munchies]. 
She is of [[Vietnam]]ese descent and was raised in Cleveland, Ohio.<ref>http://splashlife.com/article/30-under-30-futurist-foodies</ref> She now lives in Brooklyn.<ref>https://editorial.rottentomatoes.com/article/five-favorite-films-with-food-partys-thu-tran/</ref> She crafts many of the puppets herself.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://gothamist.com/2008/12/09/thu_tran_food_party.php |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2009-06-03 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090719231014/http://gothamist.com/2008/12/09/thu_tran_food_party.php |archivedate=2009-07-19 }}</ref> She is also a cook.<ref>http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/showtracker/2010/04/food-party-returns-a-qa-with-thu-tran.html</ref> Thu first learned to cook from her parents and then attended the Cleveland Institute for Art. She then moved to New York and created food-themed art installations.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.poortastemag.com/headline/food-party-at-the-farmers-market-with-thu-tran |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2010-10-10 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100731182927/http://www.poortastemag.com/headline/food-party-at-the-farmers-market-with-thu-tran |archivedate=2010-07-31 }}</ref>

Thu was named on Splashlife's list of 30 Under 30: Futuristic Foodies.<ref>http://splashlife.com/article/30-under-30-futurist-foodies</ref>
