"'''Ditty'''" is a song by American rapper [[Paperboy (rapper)|Paperboy]] from his debut studio album ''[[The Nine Yards]]''. It is the opening track on the album and was issued as the album's lead [[single (music)|single]]. The song is primarily based around a sample of [[Zapp (band)|Zapp]]'s "[[Doo Wa Ditty (Blow That Thing)/A Touch of Jazz (Playin' Kinda Ruff Part II)|Doo Wa Ditty (Blow That Thing)]]", but it also contains a sample of [[James Brown]]'s "[[Funky President (People It's Bad)]]".<ref name="whosampled">{{cite web|url=http://www.whosampled.com/Paperboy/Ditty|title=Ditty - Paperboy - WhoSampled|work=[[WhoSampled]]|accessdate=November 18, 2014}}</ref>

"Ditty" is Paperboy's only song to chart on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], peaking at No. 10 in 1993.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/paperboy-mn0000011193/awards|title=Paperboy - Awards - AllMusic|work=[[AllMusic]]|publisher=[[All Media Network]]|accessdate=November 18, 2014}}</ref> It was awarded a [[RIAA certification|platinum]] certification from the [[Recording Industry Association of America]].<ref>{{Cite certification|region=United States|artist=Paperboy|title=Ditty}}</ref> In 1994, the song was nominated for a [[36th Annual Grammy Awards|Grammy Award]] for [[Grammy Award for Best Rap Solo Performance|Best Rap Solo Performance]], but lost to "[[Let Me Ride]]" by [[Dr. Dre]].<ref name="rotn">{{cite web|url=http://www.rockonthenet.com/archive/1994/grammys.htm|title=Rock On The Net: 36th Annual Grammy Awards - 1994|work=Rock on the Net|accessdate=November 18, 2014}}</ref>
