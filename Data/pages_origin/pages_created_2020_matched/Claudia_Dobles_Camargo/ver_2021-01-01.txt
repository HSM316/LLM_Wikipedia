'''Claudia Vanessa Dobles Camargo''' (born November 19, 1980) is a Costa Rican [[architect]] and [[urban planner]]. Dobles, the wife of [[President of Costa Rica|President]] [[Carlos Alvarado Quesada]], has served as the [[First Lady of Costa Rica]] since May 8, 2018.<ref name=scd>{{cite news|title=La sancarleña que en un mes será la Primera Dama del país |url=https://sancarlosdigital.com/la-sancarlena-que-en-un-mes-sera-la-primera-dama-del-pais/ |work=San Carlos Digital |date=2018-04-02 |accessdate=2018-11-24 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20181124143535/https://sancarlosdigital.com/la-sancarlena-que-en-un-mes-sera-la-primera-dama-del-pais/ |archivedate=2018-11-24 |url-status=live}}</ref> She is the first person from [[San Carlos (canton)|San Carlos]] to hold the position of first lady.<ref name=scd/>

==Early life==
Dobles was born on November 19, 1980, in [[Ciudad Quesada]] to Carlos Dobles and María Claudia Camargo.<ref name=scd/> Her father is Costa Rican, while her mother had moved to the country from [[Mexico]].<ref name=scd/> She attended [[kindergarten]] in Mexico, but competed her elementary and [[high school]] studies in [[San José, Costa Rica]].<ref name=scd/> She met her future husband, [[Carlos Alvarado Quesada]], on the [[school bus|bus]] that both took to school.<ref name=scd/>

==Prominent roles==
Dobles played a prominent role in her husband's [[2018 Costa Rican general election|2018 presidential campaign]].<ref name=scd/> In addition to her role as the candidate's wife, Dobles was seen as a key advisor to both Alvarado and the campaign staff.<ref name=scd/>

Dobles is also advising the Costa Rican government's [[Environmental economics|environmental economic]] plan to completely replace the nation's use of [[fossil fuel]]s with [[renewable energy]] sources.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/03/12/climate/costa-rica-climate-change.html|title=Tiny Costa Rica Has a Green New Deal, Too.
