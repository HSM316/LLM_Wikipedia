'''Edward Russell Dalglish''' (1909-2001) was an American Biblical scholar and professor of the Old Testament.

==Education==
Dalglish earned his Bachelors in 1931 from [[Columbia University]], and his Masters in 1946 from [[Union Theological Seminary in the City of New York]], before earning his [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] from Columbia. He also went on to further postgraduate work at [[Harvard University]], [[Episcopal Divinity School|Episcopal Theological Seminary]], [[University of London]], and [[University of Heidelberg]].<ref name="BP">{{cite news|url=http://media.sbhla.org.s3.amazonaws.com/2159,23-Feb-1966.pdf|title=Baylor Expands Religion Faculty Toward PhD Goal|last=Hurt|first=John J.|date=February 23, 1966|work=Baptist Press|accessdate=2009-11-13}}</ref>

==Career==
After pastoring a church for 10 years, Dalglish joined the faculty at [[Gordon Divinity School]] in 1946, where he served as professor of religion until 1952.  While at Gordon, he was the chair of the committee that founded the [[Evangelical Theological Society]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.etsjets.org/files/JETS-PDFs/28/28-5/28-5-pp005-024_JETS.pdf|title=Introduction - The Evangelical Theological Society: Yesterday and Today|last=Wiseman|first=John|accessdate=2009-11-13}}</ref> From 1952 until 1966, Dalglish served as professor of Old Testament and Hebrew at [[Eastern Baptist Theological Seminary]]. During this time, he joined the team that originally translated the New American Standard Bible.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lockman.org/nasb/nasbprin.php|title=New American Standard Bible - Translators of the NASB|accessdate=2009-11-13|archive-date=2006-11-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061118110400/http://www.lockman.org/nasb/nasbprin.php|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 1966, Dalglish joined the faculty at [[Baylor University]],<ref name="BP" /> eventually becoming the director of major library acquisitions for Baylor.

In 2000, Dalglish was the recipient of the "[[Herbert H. Reynolds]] Retired Faculty Award."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bayloralumniassociation.com/content/programs_and_awards/rf_recipients.asp|title=Herbert H. Reynolds Retired Faculty and Administrators Award|publisher=Baylor Alumni Association|accessdate=2009-11-13}}</ref> He was hospitalized that same year<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.baylor.edu/pr/gallery/document/000032.pdf|title=Spotlight|date=November 2000|work=Baylor News|accessdate=2009-11-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060920033504/http://www.baylor.edu/pr/gallery/document/000032.pdf|archive-date=2006-09-20|url-status=dead}}</ref> and died the following year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.baylor.edu/pr/gallery/document/000025.pdf|title=Spotlight|date=September 2001|work=Baylor News|accessdate=2009-11-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060920035922/http://www.baylor.edu/pr/gallery/document/000025.pdf|archive-date=2006-09-20|url-status=dead}}</ref>
