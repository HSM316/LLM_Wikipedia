'''6 Cassiopeiae''' (6 Cas) is a white [[hypergiant]] in the [[constellation]] [[Cassiopeia (constellation)|Cassiopeia]], and a small-amplitude [[variable star]].

==System==
6 Cassiopeiae A is a white A2.5 type hypergiant. It is about 25 times as massive as the [[Sun]] and 200,000 times as luminous.  The star is slightly and erratically variable, an [[Alpha Cygni variable]].  Not all sources consider 6 Cas to be a hypergiant.  It is thought that the "+" in an early A3 Ia+ spectral classification referred to indications of additional spectral features from a possible companion rather than the more modern indication of a hypergiant [[luminosity class]].  However, later publications have given more conventional hypergiant spectral types such as B9Ia<sup>+</sup> and A3Ia<sup>+</sup>.<ref name=skiff2014>{{cite journal |bibcode=2014yCat....1.2023S |title=VizieR Online Data Catalog: Catalogue of Stellar Spectral Classifications (Skiff, 2009- ) |last1=Skiff |first1=B. A. |journal=VizieR On-line Data Catalog |year=2014 }}</ref>

[[File:V566CasLightCurve.png|thumb|left|A [[light curve]] for V566 Cassiopeiae from ''[[Hipparcos]]'' data, plotted as a function of phase assuming the dominant period found by Koen and Eyer (2002)<ref name=koen/><ref name=HipDataAccess/>]]
6 Cas A has a number of close companions, most notably an 8th magnitude O class bright giant at only {{val|1.5|ul="}}. Its spectral type is O9.75 and its absolute magnitude is −5.8.<ref>{{cite journal|bibcode=1990AISAO..29..101B|title=The atmosphere of the supergiant 6 Cas. IV. The spectrum and radial velocities of the visual companion|journal=Astrofiz. Issled. Izv. Spets. Astrofiz. Obs|volume=29|pages=101|last1=Barsukova|first1=E. A.|last2=Chentsov|first2=E. L.|year=1990}}</ref>  Both are considered to be members of the Cassiopeia OB5 [[stellar association]] at a distance of around 8,000 [[light-year]]s,<ref>{{cite journal|bibcode=1994BSAO...38..103B|title=Hypergiant 6 Cas and association Cas OB5|journal=Bulletin of the Special Astrophysical Observatory|volume=38|pages=103|last1=Bartaya|first1=R. A.|last2=Chargeishvili|first2=K. B.|last3=Chentsov|first3=E. L.|last4=Shkhagosheva|first4=Z. U.|year=1994}}</ref> along with several other nearby stars.<ref name=ma2021>{{cite journal |doi=10.1051/0004-6361/202039479 |title=Lucky spectroscopy, an equivalent technique to lucky imaging |year=2021 |last1=Maíz Apellániz |first1=J. |last2=Barbá |first2=R. H. |last3=Fariña |first3=C. |last4=Sota |first4=A. |last5=Pantaleoni González |first5=M. |last6=Holgado |first6=G. |last7=Negueruela |first7=I. |last8=Simón-Díaz |first8=S. |journal=Astronomy & Astrophysics |volume=646 |pages=A11 |s2cid=227151330 |doi-access=free |arxiv=2011.12250 }}</ref>  [[Gaia (spacecraft)|Gaia]] parallaxes of the nearby stars suggest a mean distance to the association of about 9,000 light years.<ref name=ma2021/>

==Variability==
6 Cas A is an α Cyg variable, pulsating erratically between 5.34 and 5.45.  The strongest period detected in one study was 37 days.<ref name=koen>{{cite journal|bibcode= 2002MNRAS.331...45K |title= New periodic variables from the Hipparcos epoch photometry |journal= Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society |volume= 331 |issue= 1 |pages= 45–59 |last1= Koen |first1= Chris |last2= Eyer |first2= Laurent |year= 2002 |doi= 10.1046/j.1365-8711.2002.05150.x |doi-access= free |arxiv = astro-ph/0112194 |s2cid= 10505995 }}</ref>  It has the [[variable star designation]] V566 Cassiopeiae.<ref name=gcvs/>
