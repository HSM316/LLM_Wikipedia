'''Charles T. Gelatka''' (January 28, 1914 – May 23, 2001)<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/players/G/GelaCh20.htm|title=Chuck Gelatka|work=[[Pro-Football-Reference.com]]|accessdate=2019-01-26}}</ref> was an [[American football]] [[End (American football)|end]] who played four seasons with the [[New York Giants]] of the [[National Football League]] (NFL). He was drafted by the New York Giants in the tenth round of the [[1937 NFL draft]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=1937 NFL Draft Listing |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/years/1937/draft.htm |access-date=2023-03-21 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> He played [[college football]] at [[Mississippi State University]] and attended [[Francis W. Parker School (Chicago)|Francis W. Parker School]] in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.profootballarchives.com/gela00200.html | title=CHUCK GELATKA | publisher=profootballarchives.com | accessdate=24 August 2014 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140826120021/http://www.profootballarchives.com/gela00200.html | archivedate=26 August 2014 }}</ref> Gelatka was a member of the New York Giants team that won the [[1938 NFL Championship Game|1938 NFL Championship]]. He served in the [[United States Armed Forces]] during [[World War II]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.profootballhof.com/story/2005/1/1/1173/ | title=FOOTBALL AND AMERICA: WW II Honor Roll | publisher=profootballhof.com | accessdate=24 August 2014}}</ref>
