'''Oluseyi Ajirotutu''' (born June 12, 1987) is a former [[American football]] [[wide receiver]] and [[special teamer]]. He was signed by the [[San Diego Chargers]] as an [[undrafted free agent]] in 2010. He played [[college football]] at [[Fresno State Bulldogs football|Fresno State]]. He is of Nigerian origin.

Ajirotutu has also played for the [[Carolina Panthers]] and [[Philadelphia Eagles]].

==Early years==
Ajirotutu attended [[Oak Ridge High School (El Dorado Hills, California)|Oak Ridge High School]] in [[El Dorado Hills, California]] from 2001 to 2005.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Seyi Ajirotutu, Oak Ridge , Wide Receiver |url=https://247sports.com/Player/Seyi-Ajirotutu-42267/high-school-70091/ |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=247Sports |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Seyi Ajirotutu, 2005 Wide Receiver, Fresno State |url=https://rivals.com/content/athletes/seyi-ajirotutu-52626?view=pv |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=Rivals.com |language=en-US}}</ref> As a junior, Ajirotutu was selected to the First-Team All-League as a defensive back after making 70 tackles and picking off five passes. As a senior during the 2004-2005 season, he caught 77 passes for 1,205 yards and 17 touchdowns, leading all divisions in the Sac-Joaquin section. Ajirotutu had a monster game in the section championship against Del Oro with six receptions for 194 yards and three touchdowns, and at safety he made 15 tackles, eight of them solo. At season's end, he was selected as the Most Valuable Player on the Sacramento Bee All-Metro team after leading Oak Ridge to the Sac-Joaquin Section Division II championship. Additionally, Ajirotutu was selected as the Sierra Conference co-Defensive Player of the Year after tallying 95 tackles, five interceptions and a blocked punt. Ajirotutu was a two-year varsity letter winner and was selected to both the Cal-Hi Sports second-team all-state and the first-team All-NorCal.<ref>{{Cite web|title=seyi ajirotutu ig|url=http://dt-fotografie.nl/m1rro3/seyi-ajirotutu-ig-0850db|access-date=May 27, 2021|website=dt-fotografie.nl}}</ref>

==College career==
Ajirotutu played college football at Fresno State.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Seyi Ajirotutu - Football |url=https://gobulldogs.com/sports/football/roster/seyi-ajirotutu/1319 |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=Fresno State Bulldogs Athletics |language=en}}</ref> In 2005, he [[redshirted]] as a freshman. In 2009, he was selected Second Team All-Western Athletic Conference as voted on by the [[Western Athletic Conference|WAC]] head coaches. Ajirotutu led the Bulldogs with 48 receptions for 677 yards and seven touchdowns.<ref>{{Cite web |title=2009 Fresno State Bulldogs Stats |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/cfb/schools/fresno-state/2009.html |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=College Football at Sports-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref>

==Professional career==

===San Diego Chargers (2010)===
Ajirotutu was not selected in the [[2010 NFL draft]] but joined the team shortly after as an [[undrafted free agent]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=April 24, 2010 |title=Ranking the best undrafted free agents |url=https://www.espn.com/nfl/draft10/insider/news/story?id=5133095 |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=ESPN.com|via=Scouts Inc. |language=en}}</ref> Ajirotutu received playing time due to the many injuries. He had a breakout game during week 9 on November 7, 2010, when he caught 4 catches for 111 yards and two touchdowns in the Chargers win over the [[Houston Texans]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=San Diego Chargers at Houston Texans - November 7th, 2010 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201011070htx.htm |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> He ended the season with 13 catches for 262 yards and two touchdowns.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Seyi Ajirotutu 2010 Game Log |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/players/A/AjirSe00/gamelog/2010/ |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref> He was waived on September 3, 2011.<ref>{{Cite web |date=September 3, 2011 |title=Chargers waive WR Ajirotutu, DL Nwagbuo to reach roster limit |url=https://www.nfl.com/news/chargers-waive-wr-ajirotutu-dl-nwagbuo-to-reach-roster-limit-09000d5d821f1f6b |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=NFL.com |language=en-US}}</ref>

===Carolina Panthers (2011)===
On September 4, 2011, Ajirotutu was claimed off waivers by the [[Carolina Panthers]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Dator |first=James |date=September 4, 2011 |title=Panthers claim WR Seyi Ajirotutu and DT Marcus Harrison, cut five total |url=https://www.catscratchreader.com/2011/9/4/2404606/panthers-claim-wr-seyi-ajirotutu-cut-five-total-nfl-carolina |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=Cat Scratch Reader |language=en}}</ref>

On August 31, 2012, the day directly following their final preseason game in 2012, he was cut by the Panthers.<ref>{{Cite web|title=seyi ajirotutu ig|url=http://dt-fotografie.nl/m1rro3/seyi-ajirotutu-ig-0850db|access-date=May 27, 2021|website=dt-fotografie.nl}}</ref>

===San Diego Chargers (2012−2014)===
On October 29, 2012, Ajirotutu was again signed by the Chargers after [[Richard Goodman (American football)|Richard Goodman]] was put on [[Injured reserve list|injured reserve]] for the remainder of the season.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Gennaro |first=John |date=October 29, 2012 |title=Chargers Sign Seyi Ajirotutu, Goodman to IR |url=https://www.boltsfromtheblue.com/2012/10/29/3573638/san-diego-chargers-sign-seyi-ajirotutu-richard-goodman-to-ir |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=Bolts From The Blue |language=en}}</ref> Ajirotutu would later be placed on injured reserve for the remainder of the [[2012 San Diego Chargers season|2012 season]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=December 5, 2012 |title=Chargers put WR Ajirotutu, center Molk on IR |url=https://www.espn.com/nfl/story/_/id/8712694/san-diego-chargers-put-seyi-ajirotutu-david-molk-ir |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=ESPN.com|agency=Associated Press |language=en}}</ref>

On August 16, 2013, Ajirotutu was re-signed by the [[San Diego Chargers]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.chargers.com/news/article-1/Look-Whos-Back-with-the-Bolts/3b673cca-b312-4096-b5c6-a50958af9654 |title=Look Who's Back with the Bolts... |last=Henne |first=Ricky |date=August 16, 2013 |website=Chargers.com |accessdate=August 17, 2013}}</ref> On November 24, he caught a game-winning touchdown pass with 24 seconds left on the clock against the [[2013 Kansas City Chiefs season|Kansas City Chiefs]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=San Diego Chargers at Kansas City Chiefs - November 24th, 2013 |url=https://www.pro-football-reference.com/boxscores/201311240kan.htm |access-date=March 5, 2024 |website=Pro-Football-Reference.com |language=en}}</ref>

In [[2014 San Diego Chargers season|2014]], he was voted by his teammates as the Chargers' Special Teams Player of the Year<ref name=henne_12292014>{{cite web|last=Henne|first=Ricky|title=2014 Team Player Awards Announced|date=December 29, 2014|work=Chargers.com|url=http://www.chargers.com/news/article-1/2014-Team-Player-Awards-Announced/af1d2738-89af-4a68-ad25-9ab6d5bd9645|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141231232357/http://www.chargers.com/news/article-1/2014-Team-Player-Awards-Announced/af1d2738-89af-4a68-ad25-9ab6d5bd9645|archivedate=December 31, 2014|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Philadelphia Eagles (2015)===
On April 7, 2015, the [[Philadelphia Eagles]] signed Ajirotutu to a one-year deal.

==Personal life==
Seyi married Autumn Ajirotutu in 2012, who appeared on the [[E!]] reality show ''[[WAGS (TV series)|WAGS]]'' for 3 seasons. They are the parents to twin girls Londyn and Laiyah. The couple divorced in 2020 and it was finalized in 2021. His father, Leke, was born in [[Nigeria]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chargers.com/team/players/roster/seyi-ajirotutu|title=Chargers bio|accessdate=December 4, 2017}}</ref>
