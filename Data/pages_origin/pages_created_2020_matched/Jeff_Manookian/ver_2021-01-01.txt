'''Jeff Manookian''' (born November 24, 1953) is an American pianist, composer, and conductor from [[Salt Lake City]], [[Utah]].<ref>[http://www.requiemsurvey.org/composers.php?id=1497 Jeff Manookian], [[Requiem Survey (website)|''Requiem Survey'']].</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.saltlakesymphony.org/bio.php?fname=Jeff&lname=Manookian|title=Salt Lake Symphony Guest Artist: Jeff Manookian|work=Salt Lake Symphony website|publisher=Salt Lake Symphony|accessdate=20 October 2012}}</ref>

Manookian shared 1st prize in the 2017 International Piano Competition of the World Piano Teachers Association.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wpta.info/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/WPC2017Catalogue.pdf#page=10|title=''9th World Piano Conference (catalog)''|access-date=June 21, 2020}}</ref> He has been a recipient of commissions by the Barlow Endowment of [[Brigham Young University]],<ref>{{cite web |title=1999 Annual Report |url=https://barlow.byu.edu/barlow-history/1999-annual-report/ |website=Barlow Endowment for Music Composition |accessdate=21 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=2005 Annual Report |url=https://barlow.byu.edu/barlow-history/2005-annual-report/ |website=Barlow Endowment for Music Composition |accessdate=21 June 2020}}</ref> the Abramyan String Quartet<ref>{{cite news |author1=Edward Reichel |title=Abramyan Quartet to play firsts |url=https://www.deseret.com/2001/4/29/19583540/abramyan-quartet-to-play-firsts |agency=Deseret News |date=April 29, 2001}}</ref> and the [[National Endowment for the Arts]]<ref>{{cite web |title=Salt Lake Symphony Guest Artist: Jeff Manookian |url=http://www.saltlakesymphony.org/bio.php?fname=Jeff&lname=Manookian |website=Salt Lake City Symphony |accessdate=21 June 2020}}</ref>
