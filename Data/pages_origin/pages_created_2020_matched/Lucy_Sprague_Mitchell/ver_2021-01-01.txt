'''Lucy Sprague Mitchell''' (2 July 1878 – 15 October 1967<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.oxfordreference.com/view/10.1093/acref/9780199754663.001.0001/acref-9780199754663|title=Dictionary of Modern American Philosophers - Oxford Reference|year=2005|language=en|doi=10.1093/acref/9780199754663.001.0001|isbn=9780199754663|access-date=2018-02-15}}</ref>)<ref>{{cite web|title=Mitchell, Lucy Sprague|url=http://www.encyclopedia.com/article-1G2-2591306662/mitchell-lucy-sprague-18781967.html|website=Encyclopedia.com|access-date=March 7, 2015}}</ref> was an American educator and the founder of [[Bank Street College of Education]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Chesler|first1=Ellen|title=She Wanted it All, and Got It|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1987/03/22/books/she-wanted-it-all-and-got-it.html|access-date=March 7, 2015|work=New York Times|date=March 22, 1987}}</ref>

Mitchell attended Radcliffe College from 1896 to 1900, graduating with honors in philosophy.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Haller|first=Evelyn|date=1988|title=Lucy Sprague Mitchell: The Making of a Modern Woman (review)|url=https://muse.jhu.edu/article/372206/pdf|journal=Biography|volume=11|issue=4|pages=331–336|doi=10.1353/bio.2010.0616|s2cid=161651043|via=Project MUSE}}</ref> During her time at [[Radcliffe College]], Mitchell lived with [[Alice Freeman Palmer]] and [[George Herbert Palmer]] on Quincy Street in Cambridge, Massachusetts. Because of the college's strict codes of gender segregation at the time, Mitchell had to circumvent the all-male [[Harvard Yard]] in order to reach [[Harvard University]]'s [[Museum of Comparative Zoology]], where she worked in the Radcliffe Zoological Laboratory.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Tonn|first=Jenna|date=2017|title=Extralaboratory Life: Gender Politics and Experimental Biology at Radcliffe College, 1894–1910|journal=Gender & History|volume=29|issue=2|pages=329–358|doi=10.1111/1468-0424.12292}}</ref>

Mitchell was the first dean of women at the [[University of California, Berkeley|University of California at Berkeley]], where she lectured in the English Department and promoted educational and career opportunities for women students from 1903–1912.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Ruyle|first1=Janet|title=Dean Lucy Sprague, the Partheneia, and the Arts|url=http://www.cshe.berkeley.edu/sites/default/files/shared/publications/docs/chron2_excerpt_Partheneia.pdf|publisher=Chronicle of the University of California|access-date=March 8, 2015}}</ref> In 1916, influenced by the work of John Dewey, Mitchell cofounded the Bureau of Educational Experiments (BEE) in New York City to study and develop optimal learning environments for children.<ref>{{cite web|title=A Brief History|url=http://www.bankstreet.edu/discover-bankstreet/what-we-do/history/|website=Bank Street College of Education|access-date=March 8, 2015}}</ref><ref>Winsor, Charlotte B.; Stodt, Martha (1957). [https://educate.bankstreet.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1005&context=books "Teacher Education for a Changing World"] (PDF). ''Bank Street Profile: An Informal Report: 1916-1956.''</ref> The BEE evolved into the Bank Street College of Education.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Nager|first1=Nancy|last2=Shapiro|first2=Edith|date=2007|title=A Progressive Approach to the Education of Teachers: Some Principles from Bank Street College of Education|url=https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED495462.pdf|journal=Occasional Paper Series|volume=18}}</ref>
