'''Chris Wardman''' is a Canadian music producer, musician and songwriter.  Wardman was a founding member of [[Blue Peter (band)|Blue Peter]], and was their lead guitarist and main contributing songwriter. Wardman was also a member of [[Breeding Ground (band)|Breeding Ground]] in the late 1980s.<ref name=Grid>{{cite web|last1=Rolfe|first1=Chris|title=Wha' Happen?: Blue Peter|url=http://www.thegridto.com/blog-post/wha%E2%80%99-happen-blue-peter/|newspaper=[[The Grid (newspaper)|The Grid]]|accessdate=28 July 2014|date=22 April 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140810052544/http://www.thegridto.com/blog-post/wha%E2%80%99-happen-blue-peter/|archive-date=10 August 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> Wardman has been actively producing albums for many Canadian acts, including [[Chalk Circle (Canadian band)|Chalk Circle]],<ref name="CC">{{cite web|title=Album info|url=http://m.billboard.com/album/chalk-circle/the-great-lake/156671#/album/chalk-circle/the-great-lake/156671|work=Chalk Circle – The Great Lake|publisher=Billboard.com|accessdate=10 January 2011}}{{Dead link|date=July 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> [[Leslie Spit Treeo]],<ref name="LST">{{cite web|title=Don't Cry Too Hard|url=http://www.discogs.com/Leslie-Spit-Treeo-Dont-Cry-Too-Hard/release/1722989|work=Leslie Spit Treeo Discography|publisher=Discogs|accessdate=10 January 2011}}</ref> [[Randy Bachman]]<ref name="MM">{{cite web|title=Randy Bachman / Guess Who|url=http://www.maplemusic.com/artists/rba/disc.asp|work=Artist Index|publisher=Maple Music|accessdate=10 January 2011}}</ref> and [[Emm Gryner]]. Wardman produced two albums for [[Art Bergmann]], including ''[[Sexual Roulette]]'' in 1990.<ref name=Renaissance>{{cite book|last1=Barclay|first1=Michael|last2=Jack|first2=Ian A.D.|last3=Schneider|first3=Jason|title=Have Not Been the Same: The CanRock Renaissance, 1985–1995|date=1 January 2011|publisher=ECW Press|isbn=978-1550229929|pages=138|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=xLIiOE_jCpAC&q=Chris+Wardman+guitar&pg=PT138|accessdate=28 July 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Moi, je chante avec Blue Peter|url=http://www.emmgryner.com/emmbassy/?q=node/561|work=Emm's Journal|publisher=EmmGryner.com|accessdate=10 January 2011}}</ref> [[Meryn Cadell]]'s most recent new release, 1997's ''6 Blocks'', was produced by Wardman, for which he hired, among others, former bandmate [[Jason Sniderman]], and he performed on the album as well.<ref name="BB Cadell">{{cite web|last1=LeBlanc|first1=Larry|title=Cadell Ditches 'Kooky' Image; Handsome Boy Bows Radio-Friendly 'Blocks 6'|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Kg8EAAAAMBAJ&q=meryn+cadell+block+6+billboard+chris+wardman&pg=PA50|publisher=Billboard|accessdate=28 July 2014|pages=50|date=10 May 1997}}</ref> [[The Watchmen (band)|The Watchmen]] credit Wardman with discovering them while playing at the [[Horseshoe Tavern]] in Toronto, and he produced their debut album, ''[[McLaren Furnace Room]]'', which was [[List of music recording certifications|certified gold]] in Canada in 1996.<ref name="Watchmen bio">{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://www.the-watchmen.com/bio.html|publisher=The Watchmen official site|accessdate=28 July 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140706082213/http://www.the-watchmen.com/bio.html|archive-date=6 July 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="Watchmen gold">{{cite web|title=Gold/Platinum – The Watchmen|url=https://musiccanada.com/gold-platinum/#!/gp_search=The%20Watchmen|publisher=Music Canada|accessdate=28 July 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140726065233/http://musiccanada.com/gold-platinum/#!/gp_search=The%20Watchmen|archive-date=26 July 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> In his review of the album on Allmusic, Roch Parisien complimented Wardman's mix of the album for how he balanced lead singer [[Daniel Greaves (musician)|Danny Greaves]]' vocals with the music production.<ref name="MFR review">{{cite web|last1=Parisien|first1=Roch|title=The Watchmen, ''McLaren Furnace Room''|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/mclaren-furnace-room-mw0000102671|publisher=Allmusic|accessdate=30 July 2014}}</ref>

Wardman played on tour with Emm Gryner in 2011.<ref name=Grid /><ref name="EGTour">{{cite web|title=Tour Dates |publisher=EmmGryner.com |accessdate=10 January 2011 |url=http://www.emmgryner.com/tour.php |work=Tour |url-status=dead |archivedate=14 May 2011 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110514042328/http://emmgryner.com/tour.php }}</ref>
