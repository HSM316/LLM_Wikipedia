'''Kobuleti Strict Nature Reserve''' ({{lang-ka|ქობულეთის სახელმწიფო ნაკრძალი}}) is a [[Protected areas of Georgia (country)|protected area]] in [[Kobuleti Municipality]], [[Adjara]] region of [[Georgia (country)|Georgia]] along the [[Black Sea]] coast in the northern part of the resort town [[Kobuleti]].<ref name="protectedplanet">{{cite web|url=https://www.protectedplanet.net/kobuleti-strict-nature-reserve|title=Kobuleti in Georgia|website=Protected Planet|access-date=2018-11-02}}</ref>  
''Kobuleti Protected Areas''  were  established in 1998 to preserve  unique wetland ecosystems recognized by the [[Ramsar Convention]]. Because of these wetland ecosystems, ancient forests, and the high biodiversity found within the reserve, it was inscribed on the [[UNESCO]] [[World Heritage List]] as part of the [[Colchic Rainforests and Wetlands]] in 2021.<ref name="UNESCO">{{cite web |title=Colchic Rainforests and Wetlands |url=https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/1616/ |publisher=UNESCO World Heritage Centre |access-date=7 May 2022 |language=en}}</ref> 

''Kobuleti Protected Areas'' include the '''Kobuleti Strict Nature Reserve'''  and [[Kobuleti Managed Reserve]], located on the left and right banks of Shavi Ghele (''black creek'') river respectively.<ref name="apa">{{cite web|url=http://apa.gov.ge/en/protected-areas/cattestone/qobuletis-daculi-teritoriebis-administracia|title=Kobuleti Protected Areas Administration - Agency of Protected Areas of Georgia|website=apa.gov.ge|access-date=2018-11-09}}</ref>
'''Kobuleti Strict Nature Reserve'''  occupies territory bordered by the Togoni river on the north, the [[Kobuleti]]—[[Ozurgeti]] highway on the east, Shavi Ghele river on the south and Kobuleti suburbs on the west.

== Geography ==
Reserve has flat surface with minor erosion by the rivers of Shavi Ghele  and Togoni and by a few water drains. It is [[peat]] [[bog]] which consists mainly of [[peat moss]] with hardly noticeable surface elevations which are called locally ''peat domes''. These minor elevations rise 1–3 m above it surroundings.  Bog in this area is composed of a single [[peat]] layer with thickness from 5 m up to 9 m.<ref name="vision">[https://www.econatura.nl/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/National-Part-of-the-Black-Sea-vision-GEORGIA.pdf The Black Sea coastal wetlands vision. Georgia] Black Sea Regional Initiative for the wise use of Coastal Wetlands. March 2008 Batumi, Georgia</ref>

== Climate ==
The reserve has a humid marine [[subtropical climate]] with high relative humidity and periodic strong winds. Because of the influence of the surrounding mountains, the reserve is exceptionally humid, with annual rainfall often exceeding 1500–2500&nbsp;mm. The primary wet season is during the autumn and winter.<ref name = "nominate">{{cite report |url = https://whc.unesco.org/document/172285 |title = Colchic Rainforests and Wetlands: Nomination Dossier |author=Valerian Mchedlidze |author2=Toma Dekanoidze |author3=Tamar Khakhishvili |author4=Nata Sultanishvili |author5=Lia Salia |author6=Davit Kobakhidze |author7=Khatuna Tsikaluri |author8=Ana Akhalaia |author9=Mariam Tatarashvili |author10=Paata Dvaladze |publisher = Georgian Agency of Protected Areas}}</ref>

== Flora ==
The Kobuleti Strict NatureReserve is very rich in plant species. Many of plants are of [[boreal ecosystem|boreal]] origin and appeared here during the [[Last Glacial Period|Ice Age]] of the [[Quaternary period]]. Most of the area is covered with various [[peat moss]]es, in particular variety of [[sphagnum]] species, such as [[white sphagnum moss]] and Imeretian sedge ([[Molinia litoralis]]), white beak sedge ([[Rhynchospora alba]]), Caucasian beak sedge ([[:sv:Rhynchospora caucasica|Rhynchospora caucasica]]), Great Pond-sedge ([[Carex riparia]]), buckbean ([[Menyanthes trifoliata]]) and carnivorous round-leaved sundew ([[Drosera rotundifolia]]) to name some. Local Colchic plants also grow in peat mires including  Pontic rhododendron ([[Rhododendron ponticum]]), Yellow azalea ([[Rhododendron luteum]]), climber ([[Smilax|Smilax excelsa]])<ref>[https://georgianrecipes.net/tag/smilax-excelsa/ Ekala(ეკალა)] ''Georgian Recipes'' A True Taste of Georgia.</ref> and rare, threatened Royal fern ([[Osmunda regalis]]).

== Fauna ==
Kobuleti Protected Areas provide habitat for migrating, nesting and wintering water birds including [[Gallinago media]], which is on the [[IUCN Red List of Threatened Species]],<ref name=status>{{cite iucn |author=BirdLife International |date=2017 |title=''Gallinago media'' |volume=2017 |page=e.T22693093A111105264 |doi=10.2305/IUCN.UK.2017-1.RLTS.T22693093A111105264.en |access-date=12 November 2021}}</ref> [[Gallinago gallinago]] and many others.<ref name="vision" />

[[Mammals]] are represented by  [[jackal]], Eurasian otter (''[[Lutra lutra]]''), [[badger]], [[nutria]], [[hare]], [[woodmouse]], [[Apodemus|fieldmouse]], et al. [[Eurasian otter]] is included in the Red List of Georgia.

[[Amphibians]] are represented by Caspian turtle (''[[Mauremys caspica]]'') and [[marsh turtle]], [[common toad]], [[European green toad|green toad]], [[common tree frog]], et al.

[[Reptiles]] are represented by [[dice snake]], [[grass snake]], [[slow worm]], European legless lizard (''[[Pseudopus apodus]]''), striped lizard (''[[Lacerta strigata]]''), [[common newt]], et al.

[[Ichthyofauna]] include such species as [[Northern pike]], [[Crucian carp]], [[European perch]] and [[silver eel]].<ref>[http://www.georgianholidays.com/sights/protected-areas/kobuleti-protected-area Kobuleti protected area] Travel Company Georgian Holidays</ref>
