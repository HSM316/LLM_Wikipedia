The '''Offerman Building''' is a historic [[Romanesque Revival architecture|Romanesque Revival]] building in [[Downtown Brooklyn]], [[New York City]].<ref>{{Cite book|title=An Architectural Guidebook to Brooklyn|last=Morrone|first=Francis|publisher=Gibbs Smith|year=2011|isbn=978-1438437699|location=Layton, Utah|pages=12-13}}</ref> Designed by architect Peter J. Lauritzen, the building is eight stories tall, and was originally built to house the S. Wechsler & Brothers department store.<ref>{{Cite landmarks|pages=320-321}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://s-media.nyc.gov/agencies/lpc/lp/2169.pdf|title=Landmarks Preservation Commission Designation Report for the Offerman Building|last=|first=|date=March 15, 2005|website=NYC Landmarks Preservation Commission|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191021231654/http://s-media.nyc.gov/agencies/lpc/lp/2169.pdf|archive-date=October 21, 2019|access-date=October 21, 2019}}</ref> In 2017 it was converted into a 121-unit residential complex.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://ny.curbed.com/2017/7/10/15947668/downtown-brooklyn-offerman-building-historic-rentals|title=Downtown Brooklynâ€™s historic Offerman Building reveals its future as luxury rentals|last=Plitt|first=Amy|date=July 10, 2017|work=Curbed NY|access-date=2019-10-21}}</ref>
