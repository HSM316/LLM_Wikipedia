'''Holbeach Fen''' is a [[The Fens|fenland]] settlement and area in the [[South Holland, England|South Holland]] district of southern [[Lincolnshire]], England. It is situated {{convert|3|mi|km|0}} south from [[Holbeach]] and 3 miles north-west from [[Sutton St James]].

In 1885 ''[[Kelly's Directory|Kelly’s]]'' noted that Holbeach Fen had become an [[Parish|ecclesiastical parish]] in 1867, had an area of {{convert|6,190|acre|km2}} and an 1881 population of 872.

[[John the Baptist|St John]]’s [[chapel of ease]] was erected in 1840 by Robert Eliot of [[Fleet, Lincolnshire|Fleet]] on land donated by the [[Edward St Maur, 11th Duke of Somerset|Duke of Somerset]] [[Knight of the Garter|KG]], and by subscription, particularly from [[John Kaye (bishop)|Bishop Kaye]] of [[Lincoln, England|Lincoln]].<ref name=Kellys>''Kelly's Directory of Lincolnshire with the port of Hull'' 1885, p. 477</ref><ref>Rose, Hugh James; Maitland, Samuel Roffey (1838) [https://books.google.com/books?id=NMhPAAAAMAAJ&pg=PA354&dq=%22Holbeach+Fen%22&hl=en&ei=stupTsz_FdHXsgaDsfimBg&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&redir_esc=y#v=onepage&q=%22Holbeach%20Fen%22&f=false ''The British Magazine, and Monthly Register of Religious and Ecclesiastical Information, Parochial History, and Documents Respecting the State of the Poor Progress of Education''] volume 13, p. 354. Retrieved 28 October 2011</ref> Brick built in [[Early English Period|Early English]] style it was described by ''Pevsner'' in 1964 as consisting of a [[nave]], short [[chancel]], [[lancet window]]s, [[Bell-Cot|bellcote]] and shallow [[Porch#Britain|porch]].<ref name=Pevsner>[[Nikolaus Pevsner|Pevsner, Nikolaus]]; [[John Harris (curator)|Harris, John]]; ''The Buildings of England: Lincolnshire'' pp. 574, 575; Penguin, (1964); revised by Nicholas Antram in 1989, Yale University Press. {{ISBN|0-300-09620-8}}</ref>
