'''Henri Deglane''' (22 June 1902 &ndash; 7 July 1975) was a French [[sport wrestling|wrestler]]. He was an Olympic Champion in [[Greco-Roman wrestling]] and [[AWA World Heavyweight Championship (Boston version)|AWA World Champion]].

In July 1937 in Paris, France, Deglane lost his [[European Heavyweight Championship]] title to American [[Al Pereira]].<ref>http://www.wrestlingdata.com/index.php?befehl=titles&titel=1055</ref>

He was inducted into the [[Wrestling Observer Hall of Fame]] in 2013 for his accomplishments in professional wrestling.<ref>{{cite journal| last = Meltzer | first = Dave | authorlink = Dave Meltzer | date = 6 November 2013 | title = Nov 6 Wrestling Observer Newsletter: 2013 Hall of Fame issue with several new inductees | journal = [[Dave Meltzer#Wrestling Observer Newsletter|Wrestling Observer Newsletter]] | location = [[Campbell, California]] | issn = 1083-9593 | url=http://www.f4wonline.com/component/content/article/110-wrestling-observer-newsletter/33860-nov-6-wrestling-observer-newsletter-2013-hall-of-fame-issue-with-several-new-inductees | page=1}}</ref>

==Olympics==
Deglane competed at the [[1924 Summer Olympics]] in Paris and won a gold medal in [[Greco-Roman wrestling]], the heavyweight class.<ref name=sports-ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/de/henri-deglane-1.html |title=Profile: Henri Deglane |publisher=sports-reference.com |accessdate=4 March 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121020021858/http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/de/henri-deglane-1.html |archivedate=20 October 2012 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>
