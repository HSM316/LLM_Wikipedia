'''Enrique Nicanor''' (born 5 December 1944) is a film and TV producer/director, writer and designer best known for his works for public service broadcasting as Director of [[La 2 (Spanish TV channel)|TVE-2]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://hemeroteca.abc.es/nav/Navigate.exe/hemeroteca/madrid/abc/1983/10/19/071.html|title=ABC (Madrid) - 19/10/1983, p. 71 - ABC.es Hemeroteca|website=hemeroteca.abc.es|language=es|access-date=2018-05-14}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://elpais.com/elpais/2017/01/04/opinion/1483560150_119859.html|title=opinión|last=|first=|date=|website=El País|access-date=2 October 2018}}</ref> the Spanish Public TV and the creation of&nbsp; [[:es:La Gallina Caponata|Caponata]] and Perezgil, the Spanish muppets of Sesame Street ([[Barrio Sésamo]]). He began as a designer and film animation Director in Cuba (1959). Based in Paris (1965) and Spain (1967) he was Board member and President of INPUT-TV,<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=http://input-tv.org/network/board-and-assembly|title=Input TV – Board & Assembly|website=input-tv.org|language=en|access-date=2018-05-14}}</ref> The International Public TV Conference<ref name="documentary.org">{{Cite news|url=https://www.documentary.org/feature/ecletic-public-tv-offerings-input-95|title=Ecletic Public TV Offerings at INPUT '95|date=1995-09-01|work=International Documentary Association|access-date=2018-10-06|language=en}}</ref> and Film trainer at [https://eave.org/ EAVE],<ref name="Enrique Nicanor — Network — EAVE">{{Cite web|url=http://eave.org/network/enrique/|title=Enrique Nicanor — Network — EAVE|website=eave.org|language=en|access-date=2018-05-14}}</ref> [https://eave.org The European Producers' workshop] and the [[European Commission]] Learning Network. Independent producer since 1988.
{{TOC limit|2}}

== Personal background ==
The son of a Galician family of modest landowners, Nicanor's parents emigrated after the Spanish Civil war to New York joining their family there. In 1950 the family&nbsp; established their home in Havana where he studied Fine Arts at The National School of Fine Arts [[Escuela Nacional de Bellas Artes "San Alejandro"]] and Philosophy and History of Arts at the [[University of Havana]]. In 1965 the family left Cuba and he went to Paris to work as animation director and cameraman with producer [[Serge Danot]]. In 1967, [[Adolfo Suárez|Mr. Adolfo Suárez]], Director of the recently created Spanish Public TV ([[RTVE]]), invited E. Nicanor to come back to Spain and join the new institution where he produced and directed films and documentaries till 1983 becoming that year Director of the channel. In 1988 he left the Spanish institution to work internationally as an independent producer/consultor promoting public broadcasting service within institutions such as INPUT-TV,<ref name=":5" /> collaborating as professional film trainer at [https://eave.org/ EAVE] the European Audiovisual workshop and producing for international TV channels.

== President of [http://input-tv.org/home INPUT-TV], The International Public TV Conference ==

* 1983, Elected member of the Board of INPUT-TV, The Public TV International Conference,<ref name=":5"/> invited by the founders of the institution,<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://web.pts.org.tw/~web01/input2006/about_e_1-2.htm|title=INPUT 2006|website=web.pts.org.tw|access-date=2018-10-06}}</ref> Howard Klein<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1986/09/22/arts/howard-klein-to-leave-rockefeller-foundation.html|title=HOWARD KLEIN TO LEAVE ROCKEFELLER FOUNDATION|last=Rockwell|first=John|access-date=2018-10-17|language=en}}</ref> (Rockefeller Foundation), Mike Fentiman<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2017/mar/09/mike-fentiman-obituary|title=Mike Fentiman obituary|last=Bakewell|first=Joan|date=2017-03-09|website=the Guardian|language=en|access-date=2018-10-06}}</ref>(BBC) [[James Day (journalist)|Jim Day]] (WNET-PBS), [[Sergio Borelli]] (RAI) and [[:fr:Michel Anthonioz|Michel Anthonioz]] (ARTE).
* 1987, Hosted and organised the INPUT-TV International Conference held in 1987-Granada, Spain.
* 1995, Hosted and organised the INPUT-TV International Conference held in 1995-San Sebastian,<ref name="documentary.org"/> Spain.
* 1997, Elected President of INPUT-TV He held the Conferences of 1997-Nantes, France, 1998-Stuttgart, Germany, 1999-Fort Worth, USA, 2000-Halifax, Canada and 2001-Cape Town, South Africa.

== European Institutions, [https://eave.org EAVE] and the [[MEDIA Programme]] ==

* 1987, Appointed Commissioner for European Audiovisual policies at the Spanish Ministry of Culture. He organised the “First European Year of Cinema” ceremony at Madrid under the presidency of Mme. [[Simone Veil]] and the [[Juan Carlos I of Spain|King]] and [[Queen Sofía of Spain|Queen]] of Spain.
* Hosted the first international meeting of the Media Programme Initiative at the Barcelona Film Festival’87.
* 1987, Joined the foundational group of EAVE, The European Audiovisual Training Programme together with European producers Raymond Ravar, Eckart Stein, Jacques Bidou, Luciano Gloor and Marcia Lerner, working as Group Leader and representing Spain as National Coordinator. &nbsp;

# EAVE 2001-Seville
# EAVE 2003- A Coruña
# EAVE 2005-Santiago de Compostela

# EAVE 1995 in Lisbon 
# EAVE 1996, in Besançon, Saarbrücken and Torino.
