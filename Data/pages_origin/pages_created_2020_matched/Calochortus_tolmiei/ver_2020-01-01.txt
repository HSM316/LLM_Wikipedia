'''''Calochortus tolmiei''''' is a [[North America]]n species of flowering plant in the [[Liliaceae|lily family]] known by the common names '''Tolmie's star-tulip''' and '''pussy ears'''.<ref name=o/><ref>[https://www.biodiversitylibrary.org/page/404463#page/495/mode/1up Hooker, William Jackson & Arnott, George Arnott Walker 1840. Botany of Captain Beechey's Voyage 398] short description in Latin, longer description in English</ref><ref name=o/> It was discovered by and named for Dr. [[William Fraser Tolmie]].

It is native to the west coast of the [[United States]]: [[Washington (U.S. state)|Washington]], [[Oregon]], and northern [[California]] as far south as [[Santa Cruz County, California|Santa Cruz County]], though it is now apparently [[extinct]] in [[Washington (state)|Washington]]. It is a common member of the flora in several types of habitat.<ref>[http://www.calflora.org/cgi-bin/species_query.cgi?where-calrecnum=1310 Calflora taxon report, University of California @ Berkeley, ''Calochortus tolmiei''  Hook. & Arn. Hairy Star Tulip,  Tolmie star tulip, Tolmie's star tulip, White pussy ears ]</ref>

==Description==
''Calochortus tolmiei'' is a [[Perennial plant|perennial]] [[Herbaceous plant|herb]] producing a slender stem, branched or unbranched, to 40 centimeters in maximum height. There is a [[basal leaf]] up to 40 centimeters long which does not wither at flowering, and generally a smaller leaf farther up the stem.<ref name=o>[http://www.efloras.org/florataxon.aspx?flora_id=1&taxon_id=242101499 Flora of North America, ''Calochortus tolmiei'']</ref>

The [[inflorescence]] is a solitary bloom or a cluster of bell-shaped flowers. Each has white to pale pink or purple [[petal]]s, each up to 2.5 centimeters long, and three narrower [[sepal]]s beneath. The petals are usually very hairy on their inner surfaces, and may be fringed with long hairs as well.<ref name=o/>

The fruit is a winged [[Capsule (fruit)|capsule]] 2 or 3 centimeters long containing several dark brown seeds.<ref name=o/>
