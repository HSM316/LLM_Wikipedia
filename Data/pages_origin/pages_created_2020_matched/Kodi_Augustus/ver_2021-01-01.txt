'''Kodi Jerome Augustus''' (born November 2, 1987) is an American former professional [[basketball]] player. He played [[college basketball]] for [[Mississippi State Bulldogs men's basketball|Mississippi State]].

==High school career==
Augustus originally attended [[Tara High School]] in his hometown of [[Baton Rouge, Louisiana]] before transferring to [[Willowridge High School (Houston)|Willowridge High School]] in [[Houston]], [[Texas]] for his junior season. As a junior in 2005–06, he averaged 16.9 points, 7.8 rebounds and 2.1 blocks per game as he earned first-team All-District 20-5A honors. He was also selected to the 2006 Houston Area Basketball Coaches Association All-Star Game, and was rated seventh among Texas recruits by Rivals.com in 2006.<ref name=bio>{{cite web | url=http://www.hailstate.com/ViewArticle.dbml?ATCLID=1244473 | title=#24 Kodi Augustus | work=HailState.com | accessdate=December 22, 2014}}</ref>

In November 2006, Augustus signed a [[National Letter of Intent]] to play [[college basketball]] for Mississippi State University.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hailstate.com/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=16800&ATCLID=935167 |title=BASKETBALL BULLDOGS SIGN TRIO OF HIGH SCHOOL STANDOUTS |work=HailState.com |date=November 14, 2006 |accessdate=December 22, 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141222105925/http://www.hailstate.com/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=16800&ATCLID=935167 |archivedate=December 22, 2014 }}</ref>

As a senior at [[Maine Central Institute]] in 2006–07, Augustus averaged a team-leading 18.9 points, along with seven rebounds and three blocks per game for coach Dave Campbell. He started all 29 games for the Huskies and recorded six double-doubles during the campaign. His best game of the season came against Lee Academy when he recorded 30 points and 22 rebounds. Following the season, he was rated as the nation's No. 20 post-graduate player by Scout.com.<ref name="bio" />

==College career==
As a freshman at [[Mississippi State Bulldogs men's basketball|Mississippi State]] in 2007–08, Augustus averaged 1.5 points and 1.1 rebounds per game in 11 brief appearances (4.7 mpg). He did not see game action in the last 16 contests as his final appearance for the season came on January 26 against Ole Miss.<ref name="bio" />

As a sophomore in 2008–09, Augustus started the first nine games of the season for the Bulldogs before moving back to a bench role for the rest of the season. He played a further 16 games off the bench while also registering a "DNP" in 11 games. On the season, he averaged 6.2 points and 3.4 rebounds per game, and was second on the team at the free throw line with a 79.2 percent accuracy (42-of-53).<ref name="bio" />

As a junior in 2009–10, Augustus appeared in 34 games with 33 starting assignments. On November 17, 2009, four days after the Bulldogs' season opening 74-88 loss to [[Rider Broncs men's basketball|Rider]], Augustus was suspended for two games after he criticized head coach [[Rick Stansbury]] after the game for his lack of playing time and how he thought the Bulldogs were outcoached by Rider.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://sdnmsu.wordpress.com/2009/11/17/kodi-augustus-suspended-2-games-for-post-game-comments/ | title=Kodi Augustus suspended 2 games for post game comments | work=WordPress.com | date=November 17, 2009 | accessdate=December 22, 2014}}</ref> He returned to face [[Texas–Pan American Broncs men's basketball|Texas–Pan American]] on November 24. He went on to record a season-high 17 points three times and a season-high 16 rebounds against Houston on December 19. On the season, he averaged 8.9 points and 5.4 rebounds per game.<ref name="bio" />

As a senior in 2010–11, Augustus started all 31 games while averaging 12.2 points, 7.2 rebounds, 1.6 assists and 1.0 blocks in 31.5 minutes per game.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.sports-reference.com/cbb/players/kodi-augustus-1.html | title=Kodi Augustus Stats | work=Sports-Reference.com | accessdate=December 22, 2014}}</ref> On November 19, 2010, he scored a career-high 26 points in the 76-74 win over [[Appalachian State Mountaineers men's basketball|Appalachian State]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Smith |first=Danny |url=http://www.starkvilledailynews.com/node/2952 |title=Smith Column: Expect MSU to have more close games |work=StarkvilleDailyNews.com |date=November 20, 2010 |accessdate=December 22, 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141222104814/http://www.starkvilledailynews.com/node/2952 |archivedate=December 22, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://espn.go.com/ncb/recap?gameId=303230344 | title=Mississippi St. 76, Appalachian St. 74 | work=ESPN.com | date=November 19, 2010 | accessdate=December 22, 2014}}</ref>

==Professional career==

===2011–12 season===
In August 2011, Augustus signed with [[Hebraica y Macabi|Hebraica Macabi]] of Uruguay for the 2011–12 season.<ref>{{cite web | last=Banewur | first=Alen | url=https://twitter.com/alenb259/status/108157216254726144 | title=Kodi Augustus, ala-pívot de 2.03 y 23 años... | publisher=Twitter | date=August 29, 2011 | accessdate=December 22, 2014 | language=Spanish}}</ref> In December 2011, he left Hebraica after appearing in 17 games.

On March 16, 2012, Augustus signed with the [[Ballarat Miners]] for the 2012 [[South East Australian Basketball League|SEABL]] season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.seabl.com.au/index.php?id=257&tx_ttnews%5Btt_news%5D=3881&cHash=a62a85423d |title=LATE IMPORT SIGNING FOR BALLARAT MINERS |work=SEABL.com.au |date=March 16, 2012 |accessdate=December 22, 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://archive.is/20141222092556/http://www.seabl.com.au/index.php?id=257&tx_ttnews%5Btt_news%5D=3881&cHash=a62a85423d |archivedate=December 22, 2014 }}</ref> He broke his right arm after appearing in five games and he subsequently returned to the United States for recovery and rehab.<ref>{{cite web | last=Brehaut | first=David | url=http://www.thecourier.com.au/story/64050/miners-kodi-augustus-going-home/ | title=Miners: Kodi Augustus going home | work=TheCourier.com.au | date=May 3, 2012 | accessdate=December 22, 2014}}</ref>

===2012–13 season===
In August 2012, Augustus signed a one-year deal with [[08 Stockholm Human Rights|08 Stockholm]] of the Swedish [[Basketligan]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.eurobasket.com/Sweden/basketball.asp?NewsID=282472 | title=08 Stockholm signs American Kodi Augustus | work=Eurobasket.com | publisher=Sports I.T. | date=August 6, 2012 | accessdate=December 22, 2014}}</ref> He went on to earn the league's Forward of the Year award after averaging 17.1 points and 5.6 rebounds per game.

===2013–14 season===
On August 10, 2013, Augustus signed with [[KK Olimpija|Union Olimpija]] of Slovenia for the 2013–14 season.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.sportando.com/en/cups/adriatic-league/104838/union-olimpija-lubiana-sign-kodi-augustus.html | title=Union Olimpija Lubiana sign Kodi Augustus | work=Sportando.com | date=August 10, 2013 | accessdate=December 22, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.court-side.com/news/blog/kodi-augustus-signs-for-ljubljana/ | title=Kodi Augustus signs for Ljubljana | work=Court-Side.com | date=August 10, 2013 | accessdate=December 22, 2014}}</ref> On December 24, 2013, he parted ways with Olimpija after appearing in nine [[ABA League|Adriatic League]] and eight [[Eurocup Basketball|Eurocup]] games.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.sportando.com/en/europe/slovenia/109626/union-olimpija-and-kodi-augustus-part-ways.html | title=Union Olimpija and Kodi Augustus part ways | work=Sportando.com | date=December 24, 2013 | accessdate=December 22, 2014}}</ref>

On January 6, 2014, Augustus signed with [[LF Basket Norrbotten|LF Basket]] for the rest of the season, returning to the Basketligan for a second stint.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://ttela.se/sport/ttsport/1.2697350-lf-basket-varvar-amerikan | title=LF Basket värvar amerikan | work=ttela.se | date=January 6, 2014 | accessdate=December 22, 2014 | language=Swedish}}</ref>

===2014–15 season===
On November 1, 2014, Augustus was selected by the [[Idaho Stampede]] in the third round of the [[2014 NBA Development League Draft]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/idaho/2014_nba_dleague_draft_result_2014_11_02.html | title=STAMPEDE SELECTS EIGHT PLAYERS IN THE NBA D-LEAGUE DRAFT | work=NBA.com | date=November 1, 2014 | accessdate=December 22, 2014 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150216104914/http://www.nba.com/dleague/idaho/2014_nba_dleague_draft_result_2014_11_02.html | archivedate=February 16, 2015 }}</ref> On November 28, 2014, he was waived by the Stampede after appearing in four games.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/idaho/hearn_activated_2014_11_28.html | title=Stampede Activates Reggie Hearn; Waives Kodi Augustus | work=NBA.com | date=November 28, 2014 | accessdate=December 22, 2014 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150115194411/http://www.nba.com/dleague/idaho/hearn_activated_2014_11_28.html | archivedate=January 15, 2015 }}</ref>

On December 17, 2014, Augustus signed with the [[Ballarat Miners]] for the 2015 SEABL season, returning to the club for a second stint.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://ballaratbasketball.com/augustus-returns-stronger-to-miners/ | title=Augustus returns stronger to Miners | work=BallaratBasketball.com | date=December 17, 2014 | accessdate=December 22, 2014 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141222103756/http://ballaratbasketball.com/augustus-returns-stronger-to-miners/ | archivedate=December 22, 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.thecourier.com.au/story/2772634/miners-regain-import/ | title=Kodi Augustus returns to Ballarat Miners for first time since 2012 injury | work=TheCourier.com.au | date=December 18, 2014 | accessdate=December 22, 2014}}</ref> However, his stint last just half the season, as he was released by the Miners on June 1 after the club felt he was not the right fit for the program.<ref>{{cite web | last=Whelan | first=Melanie | url=http://www.thecourier.com.au/story/3116182/basketball-miners-cut-import-kodi-augustus/ | title=Basketball: Miners cut import Kodi Augustus | work=TheCourier.com.au | date=June 1, 2015 | accessdate=December 7, 2015}}</ref> In 11 games for the Miners in 2015, he averaged 18.5 points, 8.4 rebounds and 1.0 assists per game.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.foxsportspulse.com/team_info.cgi?action=PSTATS&pID=194705192&client=1-3363-36749-345137-4362813 |title=Player statistics for Kodi Augustus |work=FoxSportsPulse.com |accessdate=December 7, 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151207105721/http://www.foxsportspulse.com/team_info.cgi?action=PSTATS&pID=194705192&client=1-3363-36749-345137-4362813 |archivedate=December 7, 2015 }}</ref>

===2015–16 season===
On January 10, 2016, Augustus signed with [[Caballeros de Culiacán]] of Mexico for the 2016 [[Circuito de Baloncesto de la Costa del Pacífico|CIBACOPA]] season.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://twitter.com/AkosVeg/status/686195713521381376 | title=Kodi Augustus signed with Caballeros de... | work=Twitter | date=January 10, 2016 | accessdate=January 10, 2016}}</ref> However, due to a strained left calf, he never made it onto the court with Culiacán.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.lineadirectaportal.com/movil/publicacion.php?id=284112&seccionID=125&genList=1&back=index.php | title=Caballeros se prepara para CIBACOPA 2016 | work=lineadirectaportal.com | date=March 5, 2016 | accessdate=September 26, 2016 | language=Spanish}}</ref>

===2016–17 season===
In August 2016, Augustus signed with [[Atlético Echagüe]] of Argentina for the 2016–17 [[Liga Nacional de Básquet|LNB]] season.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.latinbasket.com/Argentina/news/459398/Echague-inks-Kodi-Augustus,-ex-Culiacan | title=Echague inks Kodi Augustus, ex Culiacan | work=Latinbasket.com | date=August 18, 2016 | accessdate=September 26, 2016}}</ref> He left the team in October after appearing in nine games; he averaged 9.0 points and 2.9 rebounds per game.

==Personal==
Augustus is the son of Troy and Georgiana Carter. He is the cousin of [[Women's National Basketball Association|WNBA]] standout and former LSU All-American, [[Seimone Augustus]].<ref name="bio" />
