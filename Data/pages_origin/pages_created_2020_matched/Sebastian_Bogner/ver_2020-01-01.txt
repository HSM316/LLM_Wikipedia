'''Sebastian Bogner''' (born 17 January 1991) is a German-Swiss [[chess]] [[grandmaster (chess)|grandmaster]]. He represented Germany until [[List of nationality transfers in chess|transferring to Switzerland]] in 2013. He won the [[Swiss Chess Championship]] in 2018.

==Chess career==
Born in 1991, Bogner earned his [[international master]] title in 2005 and his [[grandmaster (chess)|grandmaster]] title in 2009. He competed for Germany at the [[39th Chess Olympiad]].<ref>[http://www.olimpbase.org/2010/2010ger.html 39th Chess Olympiad: Khanty-Mansiysk 2010]</ref> He [[List of nationality transfers in chess|transferred to Switzerland]] in 2013. He competed for Switzerland at the [[42nd Chess Olympiad]].<ref>[http://www.olimpbase.org/2016/2016sui.html 42nd Chess Olympiad: Baku 2016]</ref> In March 2018, he competed in the [[European Individual Chess Championship]]. He placed sixty-eighth,<ref>{{cite web|url =http://chess-results.com/tnr295754.aspx?lan=1&art=1&rd=11&turdet=YES&flag=30|title =European Individual Chess Championship 2018|author =Staff writer(s)|publisher =Chess Results|date =28 March 2018}}</ref> scoring 6½/11 (+4–2=5).<ref>{{cite web|url =http://chess-results.com/tnr295754.aspx?lan=1&art=9&fed=SUI&turdet=YES&flag=30&snr=80|title =European Individual Chess Championship 2018: Bogner Sebastian|author =Staff writer(s)|publisher =Chess Results|date =28 March 2018}}</ref> He won the [[Swiss Chess Championship]] in July 2018.<ref>[http://www.chessdom.com/bogner-wins-swiss-championship-2018/ Bogner wins Swiss Championship 2018]</ref> He is the No. 1 ranked Swiss player as of August 2018, with a rating of 2619.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ratings.fide.com/topfed.phtml?ina=1&country=SUI|title=Federations Ranking – Switzerland|author=Staff writer(s)|publisher=FIDE|date=August 2018}}</ref>
