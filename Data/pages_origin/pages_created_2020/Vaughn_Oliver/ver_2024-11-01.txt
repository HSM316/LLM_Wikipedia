'''Vaughn Richard Oliver''' is a Canadian music producer, sound designer, DJ, and mix engineer.<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite news |title=Vaughn Oliver {{!}} Credits |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/vaughn-oliver-mn0002948340/credits |access-date=23 September 2023 |work=AllMusic |language=en}}</ref> He was also part of the electronic music production and DJ duo  [[Oliver (DJs)]] with Producer, DJ and songwriter Oliver Goldstein.

== Career ==
Vaughn Richard Oliver (also known as U-Tern), originally from Canada, was a hip-hop and scratch DJ in the 1990s. Vaughn Oliver, upon moving to Vancouver, started DJing in clubs and radio shows, while Goldstein played in bands and made beats in the studio. While living separately by distance, Vaughn and Oliver Goldstein communicated by [[Skype]] and produced music by sending each other parts via the internet.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.musicradar.com/news/tech/interview-oliver-talk-studio-synths-and-making-it-big-559404|title=Interview: Oliver talk studio, synths and making it big|work=MusicRadar|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://elektrodaily.com/Exclusive-Interview-Oliver/|title=elektro Exclusive Interview with Oliver|website=Elektro Daily|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://queermeup.com/music/producerdj-duo-oliver-release-chemicals-featuring-singer-songwriter-mndr/|title=Producer/DJ duo Oliver release "Chemicals" featuring singer-songwriter MNDR - QUEER ME UP|date=2017-08-14|work=QUEER ME UP|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref>

Vaughn launched his duo's debut studio album, which titled ''[[Full Circle (Oliver album)|Full Circle]]'', which featured collaborations with [[Chromeo]], [[MNDR]], [[Sam Sparro]], [[De La Soul]] and [[Yelle]], was released via [[Interscope Records]] on August 25, 2017,<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7842022/oliver-full-circle-heart-attack-de-la-soul|title=Oliver Announce Debut Album 'Full Circle,' Share 'Heart Attack' Feat. De La Soul: Listen|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> alongside singles titled "Heart Attack" featuring De La Soul, "Electrify" and "Chemicals".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Yelle/news-104940.html|title="Heterotopia" : Yelle passe en mode disco pour le duo Oliver|website=chartsinfrance.net|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7942064/oliver-full-circle-album-review-interview-stream|title=Oliver Finds Its Groove With Help From De La Soul, Chromeo & More on Debut Album 'Full Circle'|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> The song "Electrify" has received over 1.8 million streams on [[Spotify]] and was included in the official [[soundtrack]] of [[EA Sports]]' [[FIFA 17]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lavendermagazine.com/resources/contests-and-promotions/oliver-full-circle/|title=Oliver – "Full Circle"|website=Lavender Magazine|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref>

In the years before 2014, Vaughn released the extended plays titled ''Dirty Talk'' (2012), ''Mechanical'' (2013) and ''Light Years Away'' (2014).<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Oliver-Releases-New-Album-Full-Circle-Today-20170825|title=Oliver Releases New Album 'Full Circle' Today|author=BWW News Desk|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> In an interview, Vaughn noted his dream collaborations to be [[Prince (musician)|Prince]], [[Quincy Jones]] and [[Daft Punk]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.allaccess.com/an-interview-with-the-producerdj-duo-oliver/|title=An Interview With the Producer/DJ Duo, OLIVER! {{!}} All Access Music|website=music.allaccess.com|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thump.vice.com/en_au/article/gv37d4/oliver-on-daft-punk-comparisons-confessions-about-miley-and-getting-blanked-by-tchami|title=Oliver on Daft Punk Comparisons, Confessions About Miley, and Getting Blanked by Tchami|website=Thump|date=30 September 2014 |access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref>

A remix EP of ''Mechanical'' was released, featuring remixes from [[Tchami]], [[Dillon Francis]], Values and Nom De Strip.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.purplesneakers.com.au/2013/10/oliver-release-mechanical-remixes/|title=Oliver Release 'Mechanical Remixes' {{!}} Purple Sneakers|date=2013-10-26|work=Purple Sneakers|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> Their song "MYB" was featured on Annie Mac's [[BBC Radio 1]] [[Annie Mac (radio show)|show]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.lessthan3.com/news/the-best-dj-duos-groups|title=The Best DJ Duos & Groups|website=The Best DJ Duos & Groups|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> Earlier in 2017, they featured on the Leon Else song "The City Don't Care", which was released via What Are We Doing/Interscope Records.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Leon-Else-Releases-New-Single-The-City-Dont-Care-ft-Oliver-20170313|title=Leon Else Releases New Single 'The City Don't Care' ft. Oliver|author=BWW News Desk|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref>

On October 23, 2019, Vaughn announced on their official Twitter account that no plans were set for any future albums/tours and that they (Goldstein and he) hadn't worked together on music for over a year, citing professional issues. Vaughn intends to sporadically release under the Oliver name, but states that his focus has been on producing and sound engineering for other artists.<ref>{{cite web |title=Official Twitter post on 23 October 2019 |url=https://twitter.com/WeAreOliver/status/1187089328964423681 |website=Twitter |access-date=26 October 2019}}</ref>

After 2019, he made the switch to mainly producing for artists including [[Latto]], [[Kim Petras]], [[Nicki Minaj]], [[Megan Thee Stallion]].

In 2021, Vaughn handled the production of ''Big Energy'', a single by [[Latto]]. "[[Big Energy]]" has gone 2 x platinum and earned Latto her first top 10 hit on the US Billboard Hot 100, peaking at number three in April 2022. “Big Energy” also reached #1 on U.S. Rhythmic chart and #1 on Top 20.

Vaughn also made contributions to 9 Kim Petras songs from the album "Problematique" and 3 songs from the album "Feed the Beast".

Vaughn Oliver has worked with [[Nicki Minaj]], [[Megan Thee Stallion]], [[Katy Perry]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/katy-perry-new-album-kp6-dr-luke-max-martin-1235041835/|title=Katy Perry Reconnected With 'Teenage Dream' Producers Dr. Luke, Max Martin for KP6 |work=Rollingstone|access-date=2024-06-19}}</ref> [[Latto]], [[Kim Petras]], [[Kylie Minogue]], Khalid, Poppy, Slayyyter, Yelle, Remi Wolf, Chromeo, Lu Kala, as a producer and songwriter<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/code/5763169/oliver-remixed-and-ready-listen-to-exclusive-dillon-francis-mixed|title=Oliver, Remixed and Ready: Listen to Exclusive Dillon Francis-Mixed 'Night Is On My Mind'|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> and frequent collaborators include [[Dr. Luke]], Tobias Wincorn, Jesse St John, Malibu Babie, [[LunchMoney Lewis]], [[KBeaZy]], Ryland Blackinton, [[Yung Bae]], Nico Stadi, Blake Straus and [[Chloe Angelides]].

== Splice, Samples and Beat Making ==

Vaughn Oliver<ref>[https://www.meldaproduction.com/about/artists/vaughn_oliver/ Vaughn Oliver]”, “Meldaproduction.com"</ref>  is well established in the producer community through [[Splice (platform)|Splice]],<ref>[https://splice.com/blog/oliver-in-the-charts/ oliver in the charts]", “Splice.com", July 14, 2021</ref> where his Power Tools<ref>[https://www.billboard.com/pro/splice-music-creation-platform-producers-samples-analysis/ How Splice Became the Hottest Platform on the Beat Market
]", “Billboard.com", July 22, 2021</ref>  sample packs have become one of the platform's most downloaded brands.<ref>{{cite web |title=Oliver: Power Tools Sample Pack: Disco Samples |url=https://splice.com/sounds/packs/splice/oliver-power-tools |website=Splice |access-date=25 January 2024 |language=en}}</ref> He has released three main packs with a total of around 3,000 sounds, along with a DECADES series that focuses on the sounds of the '80s and '90s. Vaughn’s Power Tools sample packs<ref>[https://futurefilter.substack.com/p/oliver-and-the-not-so-secret-sound/ Oliver and the Not-so-secret Sound of Modern Pop
]", “Futurefilter.com", March 11, 2024</ref>  sounds have contributed to the making of many tracks,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Shimazu |first1=Harrison |title=Oliver on his most iconic loops, new sounds, and Doja Cat's "Say So" |url=https://splice.com/blog/oliver-in-the-charts/ |website=Blog {{!}} Splice |access-date=25 January 2024 |date=14 July 2021}}</ref> including underground releases and chart-topping hits.<ref>[https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/pop-star-ranking/2024-june/sabrina-carpenter-s-espresso-highlights-the-new-way-music-is-made.html/ sabrina carpenters espresso highlights the new way music is made]", “Bloomberg.com", June, 2024</ref> Vaughn’s samples can be found in songs by artists like [[Doja Cat]], [[Dua Lipa]], [[SZA]], [[Justin Timberlake]], [[Kygo]], [[Sabrina Carpenter]],<ref>[https://www.musicradar.com/news/sabrina-carpenter-espresso-splice-loops/ sabrina carpenter espresso splice loops]", “Musicradar.com", June 6, 2024</ref> [[The Weeknd]], and [[Adele]].
