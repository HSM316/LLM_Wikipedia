'''Vaughn Oliver''' is an American multi-platinum music producer, songwriter and DJ based in [[Los Angeles]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Vaughn Oliver {{!}} Credits |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/vaughn-oliver-mn0002948340/credits |access-date=23 September 2023 |work=AllMusic |language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==
Vaughn Oliver (also known as U-Tern) was a hip-hop and scratch DJ in the 1990s. He collaborated with Oliver Goldstein. Vaughn Oliver, upon moving to Vancouver, started DJing in clubs and radio shows, while Goldstein played in bands and made beats in the studio. 

While living separately by distance, they communicated by [[Skype]] and produced music by sending each other parts via the internet.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.musicradar.com/news/tech/interview-oliver-talk-studio-synths-and-making-it-big-559404|title=Interview: Oliver talk studio, synths and making it big|work=MusicRadar|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://elektrodaily.com/Exclusive-Interview-Oliver/|title=elektro Exclusive Interview with Oliver|website=Elektro Daily|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://queermeup.com/music/producerdj-duo-oliver-release-chemicals-featuring-singer-songwriter-mndr/|title=Producer/DJ duo Oliver release "Chemicals" featuring singer-songwriter MNDR - QUEER ME UP|date=2017-08-14|work=QUEER ME UP|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> 

Vaughn Oliver has also worked with [[Shakira]], [[Britney Spears]], [[Latto]], and [[Kelly Clarkson]] as a producer and songwriter.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/code/5763169/oliver-remixed-and-ready-listen-to-exclusive-dillon-francis-mixed|title=Oliver, Remixed and Ready: Listen to Exclusive Dillon Francis-Mixed 'Night Is On My Mind'|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref>

Vaughn launched his debut studio album, which titled ''[[Full Circle (Oliver album)|Full Circle]]'', which featured collaborations with [[Chromeo]], [[MNDR]], [[Sam Sparro]], [[De La Soul]] and [[Yelle]], was released via [[Interscope Records]] on August 25, 2017,<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7842022/oliver-full-circle-heart-attack-de-la-soul|title=Oliver Announce Debut Album 'Full Circle,' Share 'Heart Attack' Feat. De La Soul: Listen|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> alongside singles titled "Heart Attack" featuring De La Soul, "Electrify" and "Chemicals".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.chartsinfrance.net/Yelle/news-104940.html|title="Heterotopia" : Yelle passe en mode disco pour le duo Oliver|website=chartsinfrance.net|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/dance/7942064/oliver-full-circle-album-review-interview-stream|title=Oliver Finds Its Groove With Help From De La Soul, Chromeo & More on Debut Album 'Full Circle'|work=Billboard|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> The song "Electrify" has received over 1.8 million streams on [[Spotify]] and was included in the official [[soundtrack]] of [[EA Sports]]' [[FIFA 17]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lavendermagazine.com/resources/contests-and-promotions/oliver-full-circle/|title=Oliver – "Full Circle"|website=Lavender Magazine|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> 

In the years before 2014, Vaughn released the extended plays titled ''Dirty Talk'' (2012), ''Mechanical'' (2013) and ''Light Years Away'' (2014).<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Oliver-Releases-New-Album-Full-Circle-Today-20170825|title=Oliver Releases New Album 'Full Circle' Today|author=BWW News Desk|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> In an interview, Vaughn noted his dream collaborations to be [[Prince (musician)|Prince]], [[Quincy Jones]] and [[Daft Punk]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://music.allaccess.com/an-interview-with-the-producerdj-duo-oliver/|title=An Interview With the Producer/DJ Duo, OLIVER! {{!}} All Access Music|website=music.allaccess.com|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thump.vice.com/en_au/article/gv37d4/oliver-on-daft-punk-comparisons-confessions-about-miley-and-getting-blanked-by-tchami|title=Oliver on Daft Punk Comparisons, Confessions About Miley, and Getting Blanked by Tchami|website=Thump|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> 

A remix EP of ''Mechanical'' was released, featuring remixes from Tchami, Dillon Francis, Values and Nom De Strip.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.purplesneakers.com.au/2013/10/oliver-release-mechanical-remixes/|title=Oliver Release 'Mechanical Remixes' {{!}} Purple Sneakers|date=2013-10-26|work=Purple Sneakers|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> Their song "MYB" was featured on Annie Mac's [[BBC Radio 1]] [[Annie Mac (radio show)|show]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.lessthan3.com/news/the-best-dj-duos-groups|title=The Best DJ Duos & Groups|website=The Best DJ Duos & Groups|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref> Earlier in 2017, they featured on the Leon Else song "The City Don't Care", which was released via What Are We Doing/Interscope Records.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Leon-Else-Releases-New-Single-The-City-Dont-Care-ft-Oliver-20170313|title=Leon Else Releases New Single 'The City Don't Care' ft. Oliver|author=BWW News Desk|access-date=2017-09-05}}</ref>

On October 23, 2019, Vaughn announced on their official Twitter account that no plans were set for any future albums/tours and that they (Goldstein and he) hadn't worked together on music for over a year, citing professional issues. Vaughn intends to sporadically release under the Oliver name, but states that his focus has been on producing and sound engineering for other artists.<ref>{{cite web |title=Official Twitter post on 23 October 2019 |url=https://twitter.com/WeAreOliver/status/1187089328964423681 |website=Twitter |access-date=26 October 2019}}</ref>

In 2021, Vaughn handled the production of ''Big Energy'', a single by [[Latto]]. "Big Energy" earned Latto her first top 10 hit on the US Billboard Hot 100, peaking at number three in April 2022.
