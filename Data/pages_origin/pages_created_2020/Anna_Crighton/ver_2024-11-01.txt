'''Dame Anna Louisa de Launey Crighton''' {{post-nominals|country=NZL|DNZM|QSO|JP|size=85%}} (born January 1944) is a New Zealand heritage campaigner and historian, and former local-body politician in [[Christchurch]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://chchplan.ihp.govt.nz/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/3688-Peter-Dyhrberg-Others-Supplementary-evidence-of-Anna-Crighton-9-03-2016.pdf |title=Supplementary statement of evidence of Anna Louisa de Launey Crighton on behalf of submitter 2688: Peter Dyhrberg and others |date=9 March 2016 |access-date=30 December 2021 |archive-date=28 January 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190128005700/http://www.chchplan.ihp.govt.nz/wp-content/uploads/2015/08/3688-Peter-Dyhrberg-Others-Supplementary-evidence-of-Anna-Crighton-9-03-2016.pdf |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Biography==
Crighton was born in Christchurch in January 1944.<ref name="Keeping On"/><ref name="life of Anna">{{cite news |url= https://www.thepress.co.nz/nz-news/350189764/life-anna |title=The life of Anna |date=24 February 2024 |first=Philip |last=Matthews |work=[[The Press]] |access-date=29 February 2024}}</ref> She attended Christchurch East Primary School, before her family moved to [[Dunedin]]. There she attended Pinehill School and [[Otago Girls' High School]], before returning to Christchurch where she went to [[Christchurch Girls’ High School]]. After travelling overseas and a brief unsuccessful marriage, she worked and was a single parent in North Canterbury, before moving back to Christchurch where she completed her education. She earned [[School Certificate (New Zealand)|School Certificate]] and University Entrance by correspondence, before enrolling at the [[University of Canterbury]] with a scholarship as a mature student. Her [[Bachelor of Arts]] degree in history, art history and classics, was followed by a [[Master of Arts]] degree, with a thesis on colonial architecture.<ref name="Keeping On">{{cite news |url=https://ageconcerncan.org.nz/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/Keeping-On-Autumn-2016.pdf |title=A mother of city heritage |date=February 2016 |page=3 |work=Keeping On |publisher=Age Concern |volume=89 |access-date=13 March 2022}}</ref>

In 1979, Crighton was appointed registrar or collections manager at the [[Robert McDougall Art Gallery]], where she remained until 2001. For part of that time, she served as the New Zealand representative on the committee of the American Association of Museum Registrars.<ref name="Keeping On"/> 

In Christchurch, Crighton has been a community board member and chair, and she was a [[Christchurch City Council|Christchurch city councillor]] from 1995 to 2007. She has a long association with Christchurch Heritage Ltd and the Christchurch Heritage Trust, which she founded in 1996.<ref name="Keeping On"/><ref name="Investiture"/> The trust purchased the [[The Octagon, Christchurch|former Trinity Church]] in Worcester Street and saved it from demolition.<ref name="Keeping On"/>

From 2008, Crighton undertook doctoral research through the [[University of Otago]]. She completed her [[PhD]] studies, which had been disrupted by the [[2011 Christchurch earthquake|Christchurch earthquakes]], with her thesis titled ''The selection and presentation culture of the Robert McDougall Art Gallery, Christchurch, New Zealand, 1932–2002''.<ref name="Keeping On"/><ref>{{cite thesis |last=Crighton |first=Anna |year=2012 |type=Doctoral thesis |title=The selection and presentation culture of the Robert McDougall Art Gallery, Christchurch, New Zealand, 1932-2002 |publisher=OUR Archive, University of Otago |hdl=10523/2449}}</ref> Her doctoral thesis led to the book, ''English, Colonial, Modern and Māori: The Changing Faces of the Robert McDougall Art Gallery, Christchurch, New Zealand, 1932–2002'', which was published in 2014.<ref name="Investiture">{{cite web |url= https://gg.govt.nz/image-galleries/8727/media?page=0 |title=Investiture ceremony 6 July 2020 |publisher=Government House |access-date=13 March 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cambridgescholars.com/product/978-1-4438-6694-1 |title=English, Colonial, Modern and Maori: The Changing Faces of the Robert McDougall Art Gallery, Christchurch, New Zealand, 1932–2002 |website=Cambridge Scholars |access-date=13 March 2022}}</ref>

From 2010 to 2014, Crighton was chair of the Canterbury Earthquake Heritage Buildings Fund Trust. Since 2001, she has been a director the Theatre Royal Charitable Foundation, and she has chaired the Christchurch Heritage Awards Charitable Trust since 2009. She served as a board member of the [[Heritage New Zealand|New Zealand Historic Places Trust]] between 2003 and 2010, and she was president of Historic Places Aotearoa between 2010 and 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.otago.ac.nz/alumni/news/anna-crighton.html |title=Otago alumna awarded top honour for heritage |date=29 January 2020 |publisher=University of Otago |access-date=13 March 2022}}</ref> Crighton was an elected member of the [[Canterbury District Health Board]] for 12 years, from 2007 to 2019.<ref name="Investiture"/><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.pressreader.com/new-zealand/the-press/20071015/281569466362647 |title=Protest over unelected chairmen |first=Amanda |last=Morrall |date=15 October 2007 |work=The Press |access-date=13 March 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.pressreader.com/new-zealand/the-press/20191014/281659666802086 |title=Dual roles for some DHB members |first=Tina |last=Law |date=14 October 2019 |work=The Press |access-date=13 March 2022}}</ref>

==Honours==
In the [[2005 Birthday Honours (New Zealand)|2005 Queen's Birthday Honours]], Crighton was appointed a [[Companion of the Queen's Service Order]] for public services.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://dpmc.govt.nz/publications/queens-birthday-honours-list-2005 |title=Queen's Birthday honours list 2005 |date=6 June 2005 |publisher=Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet |access-date=10 August 2020}}</ref> In the [[2020 New Year Honours (New Zealand)|2020 New Year Honours]], she was appointed a [[Dame Companion of the New Zealand Order of Merit]], for services to heritage preservation and governance.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.dpmc.govt.nz/publications/new-year-honours-list-2020 |title=New Year honours list 2020 |date=31 December 2019 |publisher=Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet |access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref>
