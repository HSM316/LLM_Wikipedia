'''Shlok Sharma''' is an Indian director, screenwriter and producer known for his works in [[Hindi cinema]]. His notable works include the 2013 [[anthology]] film [[Shorts (2013 film)|Shorts]], the 2017 feature film [[Haraamkhor]] and the 2018 feature film Zoo.

== Early career ==
Shlok's father was a [[Yoga as exercise|Yoga teacher]] to [[Film director|director]] [[Vishal Bhardwaj]] and [[lyricist]] [[Gulzar]]. After completing his [[Secondary school|higher secondary]] education, Shlok decided not to pursue education further and build a career in film-making. His father recommended him to Bhardwaj who hired him as a [[production assistant]] on his 2005 film, [[The Blue Umbrella (2005 film)|The Blue Umbrella]].<ref name="mans">{{cite web |last1=Ghose |first1=Arnesh |title="All The Characters Are Doing Some Sort Of Haraamkhori" – Shlok Sharma |url=https://www.mansworldindia.com/entertainment/cinema/characters-sort-haraamkhori-shlok-sharma/ |website=Man's World |date=13 January 2017 |publisher=MW.Com India Pvt Ltd |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref> He then went on to work with Bhardwaj on his next film, [[Omkara (2006 film)|Omkara]]. Shlok met [[Anurag Kashyap]] on the sets of the film who hired him as an [[Assistant Director]] for his 2007 film, [[No Smoking (2007 film)|No Smoking]]. He worked with Kashyap on his next feature film [[Dev.D]] before joining as a [[Second unit|Second Unit Director]] on [[Gangs of Wasseypur]]. During the shooting of the film, he went to jail for shooting on [[Track (rail transport)|railway tracks]] without permission.<ref name="jail">{{cite news |title=Did you know? 'Haraamkhor' helmer was jailed during 'Gangs of Wasseypur' shoot |url=https://www.dnaindia.com/entertainment/report-did-you-know-haraamkhor-helmer-was-jailed-during-gangs-of-wasseypur-shoot-2291440 |accessdate=1 January 2020 |agency=PTI |work=[[Daily News and Analysis]] |date=11 January 2017}}</ref>

In 2010, Shlok Sharma released his first short film ''Tubelight Ka Chaand''<ref name="telangana">{{cite news |last1=Nanisetti |first1=Shanti |title=Tubelight Ka Chand is an exceptional short film |url=https://telanganatoday.com/tubelight-ka-chand-exceptional-short-film |accessdate=1 January 2020 |publisher=Telangana Today |date=8 July 2017}}</ref> which won the Best Short Film award at the 2011 Indian Film Festival Stuttgart.<ref name="stuttgart">{{cite web |title=Winners |url=https://indisches-filmfestival.de/en/preistragerinnen/ |website=Indian Film Festival Stuttgart |publisher=Filmbüro Baden-Württemberg |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref> He made his second short film, ''Bombay Mirror'' in 2010 which featured [[Rajkummar Rao]] and [[Vijay Maurya]]<ref name="direct">{{cite web |title=Short films keep me excited |url=http://www.bollywoodirect.com/short-films-keep-excited-interview-shlok-sharma/ |website=Bollywood Direct |date=5 May 2014 |publisher=Bollywood Direct |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref> but the film wasn't released till 2014.<ref name="mirror">{{cite news |title='Bombay Mirror': This 3-minute short film will change your opinion on fanaticism forever |url=https://www.news18.com/news/india/bombay-mirror-this-3-minute-short-film-will-change-your-opinion-on-fanaticism-forever-684891.html |accessdate=1 January 2020 |agency=[[News18 India]] |publisher=IBNLive.com |date=1 May 2014}}</ref>

== Feature films and other projects ==
Shlok made his feature film debut with the anthology film [[Shorts (2013 film)|Shorts]] that was released in 2013. The film consisted of five short films out of which one, Sujata, was directed by him and consisted of a story of a young girl, who is struggling to come out of the clutches of her tormenting cousin brother.<ref name="free press">{{cite news |last1=Bureau |first1=FPJ |title=Shorts : A melange of five unique, well crafted films |url=https://www.freepressjournal.in/entertainment/a-melange-of-five-unique-well-crafted-films |accessdate=1 January 2020 |publisher=The Free Press Journal |date=13 July 2013}}</ref> His second feature film, [[Haraamkhor]] was shot in 2013 and explores the romance between a 14-year-old school student and her teacher. The film premiered at the 15th [[New York Indian Film Festival]] and won the Silver Gateway of India trophy at the 17th [[Mumbai Academy of the Moving Image|MAMI Mumbai Film Festival]] but was denied a certificate by the [[Central Board of Film Certification]].<ref name="livemint">{{cite news |title=Censor board declines to pass Nawazuddin Siddiqui's 'Haraamkhor' |url=https://www.livemint.com/Politics/iSahIRipZY2MKpyoSMY9EN/Censor-board-declines-to-pass-Nawazuddin-Siddiquis-Haraamk.html |accessdate=1 January 2020 |agency=[[Press Trust of India]] |publisher=[[Mint (newspaper)|Live Mint]] |date=19 June 2016}}</ref> The film was subsequently approved by the Film Certification Appellate Tribunal and was released in 2017 to critical acclaim.<ref name="business">{{cite news |title=Was frustrating when 'Haraamkhor' was stuck at Censors: Shlok |url=https://www.business-standard.com/article/pti-stories/was-frustrating-when-haraamkhor-was-stuck-at-censors-shlok-117010700375_1.html |accessdate=1 January 2020 |agency=[[Press Trust of India]] |publisher=[[Business Standard]] |date=7 January 2017}}</ref>
