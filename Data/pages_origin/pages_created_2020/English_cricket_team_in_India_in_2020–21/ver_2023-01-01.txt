The [[England cricket team]] toured India during February and March 2021 to play four [[Test cricket|Test]] matches, three [[One Day International]] (ODI) and five [[Twenty20 International]] (T20I) matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://indianexpress.com/article/sports/cricket/england-tour-india-4-tests-3-odis-5-t20is-sourav-ganguly-7064150/ |title=England will tour India for 4 Tests, 3 ODIs, 5 T20Is: Sourav Ganguly |work=The Indian Express |access-date=24 November 2020}}</ref> The Tests formed part of the inaugural [[2019–2021 ICC World Test Championship]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://icc-static-files.s3.amazonaws.com/ICC/document/2018/06/20/6dc2c8d4-e1a5-4dec-94b4-7121fab3cd7f/ICC_Tours.pdf |title=Men's Future Tours Programme |access-date=11 October 2019 |work=International Cricket Council}}</ref> and the ODI series formed part of the inaugural [[2020–2023 ICC Cricket World Cup Super League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/news/742337 |title=Schedule for inaugural World Test Championship announced |work=International Cricket Council |access-date=11 January 2019}}</ref> In December 2020, the full itinerary was released with three venues hosting the entire tour.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.bcci.tv/articles/2020/news/147524/bcci-ecb-announce-itinerary-for-england-s-tour-of-india-2020-21 | title=BCCI, ECB announce itinerary for England's tour of India 2020-21 | work=Board of Control for Cricket in India | access-date=10 December 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/30487971/england-tour-india-chennai-ahmedabad-host-tests-odis-held-pune |title=England tour of India: Chennai, Ahmedabad to host Tests; ODIs to be held in Pune |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=10 December 2020}}</ref>

England won the first Test by 227 runs,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/series/england-tour-of-india-2020-21-1243364/india-vs-england-1st-test-1243384/match-report |title=James Anderson and Jack Leach consign India to rare home defeat |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=25 February 2021}}</ref> and India won the second Test by 317 runs,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/series/england-tour-of-india-2020-21-1243364/india-vs-england-2nd-test-1243385/match-report |title=Axar Patel five-for seals crushing India win to level series |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=25 February 2021}}</ref> to level the series at 1–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/news/2031569 |title=Axar five-for guides India to victory in Chennai as WTC race heats up |work=International Cricket Council |access-date=25 February 2021}}</ref> The third Test, a [[day/night cricket|day/night]] fixture,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/news/2043630 |title=India win thriller, eliminate England in race to WTC final |work=International Cricket Council |access-date=25 February 2021}}</ref> was completed inside two days, with India winning by ten wickets.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/56197297 |title=India v England: Hosts win astonishing third Test in two days |work=BBC Sport |access-date=25 February 2021}}</ref> The loss meant that England could no longer qualify for the [[2021 ICC World Test Championship Final|final]] of the World Test Championship.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/media-releases/2043586 |title=England out of contention for a place in WTC final |work=International Cricket Council |access-date=25 February 2021}}</ref> India won the fourth and final Test by an innings and 25 runs, taking the series 3–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/56294545 |title=India v England: Axar Patel and Ravichandran Ashwin seal series for hosts |work=BBC Sport |access-date=6 March 2021}}</ref> The series win meant that India joined [[New Zealand national cricket team|New Zealand]] in the final of the World Test Championship.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/sport/cricket/300246462/world-test-championship-india-claim-31-series-win-vs-england-will-face-black-caps-in-final |title=World Test Championship: India claim 3-1 series win vs England, will face Black Caps in final |work=Stuff |access-date=6 March 2021}}</ref>

India won the fourth T20I match by eight runs, to level the series 2–2 with one match to play.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/56446341 |title=India v England: Hosts win fourth T20 by eight runs to set up series decider |work=BBC Sport |access-date=18 March 2021}}</ref> India won the fifth and final T20I by 36 runs, to win the series 3–2.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/56470362 |title=India v England: Virat Kohli and Bhuvneshwar Kumar seal series in Ahmedabad |work=BBC Sport |access-date=20 March 2021}}</ref> India won the first ODI by 66 runs,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thenationalnews.com/sport/cricket/debutants-prasidh-krishna-and-krunal-pandya-lead-india-to-big-win-in-first-odi-against-england-1.1190052 |title=Debutants Prasidh Krishna and Krunal Pandya lead India to big win in first ODI against England |work=The National |access-date=26 March 2021}}</ref> with England winning the second ODI by six wickets to level the series ahead of the final ODI of the tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/56500210 |title=India v England: Jonny Bairstow and Ben Stokes led chase of 338 |work=BBC Sport |access-date=26 March 2021}}</ref> India won the third ODI by seven runs, winning the series 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/56395245 |title=India v England: Sam Curran heroics not enough in Pune |work=BBC Sport |access-date=28 March 2021}}</ref>

==Background==
Originally, England were scheduled to tour India in September and October 2020 to play the ODI and T20I matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thenational.ae/sport/cricket/complete-schedule-of-indian-cricket-team-in-2020-including-the-all-important-tour-of-australia-and-t20-world-cup-1.957676 |title=Complete schedule of Indian cricket team in 2020 including the all-important tour of Australia and T20 World Cup |work=The National |access-date=30 December 2019}}</ref> However, after the 2020 ICC Men's T20 World Cup was [[2021 ICC Men's T20 World Cup|postponed by one year]] due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/media-releases/1733341 |title=Men's T20 World Cup postponement FAQs |work=International Cricket Council |access-date=20 July 2020}}</ref> the [[2020 Indian Premier League]] (IPL) was rescheduled to be played in its place.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/53693968 |title=England's white-ball tour of India postponed until 2021 |work=BBC Sport |access-date=7 August 2020}}</ref> In February 2020, the [[Board of Control for Cricket in India]] (BCCI) announced that they would like to play a [[day/night cricket|day/night]] Test match at the refurbished [[Narendra Modi Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/28714863/india-play-day-night-test-australia-ahmedabad-england |title=India to play day-night Test in Australia, Ahmedabad likely to host pink-ball Test against England |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=16 February 2020}}</ref> The [[England and Wales Cricket Board]] (ECB) confirmed the changes to England's tour in August 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ecb.co.uk/england/men/news/1749739/england-men-s-white-ball-tour-to-india-postponed-until-early-2021 |title=England men's white-ball Tour to India postponed until early 2021 |work=England and Wales Cricket Board |access-date=7 August 2020}}</ref>

On 20 August 2020, [[Sourav Ganguly]], president of the BCCI, said that India would host England to fulfil their [[Future Tours Programme]] (FTP) commitments.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/29715376/sourav-ganguly-commits-india-hosting-england-february-2021 |title=Sourav Ganguly commits to India hosting England in February 2021 |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=22 August 2020}}</ref> In September 2020, the BCCI were also in discussions with the [[Emirates Cricket Board]] with regards to using the United Arab Emirates to host the series, due to the COVID-19 situation.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/29919102/india-vs-england-tests,-ipl-2021-held-uae |title=India v England Tests, IPL 2021 could be held in the UAE |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=19 September 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thenational.ae/sport/cricket/uae-one-step-closer-to-hosting-india-england-series-1.1079858 |title=UAE one step closer to hosting India-England series |work=The National |access-date=19 September 2020}}</ref> Later the same month, Sourav Ganguly reiterated that it was a "priority" to host the matches in India, and that the BCCI were continuing to monitor the COVID-19 situation.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/29993123/sourav-ganguly-priority-host-england-series-india-covid-19-threat |title=Sourav Ganguly: 'Priority' is to host England series in India despite Covid-19 threat |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=28 September 2020}}</ref>

The tour's itinerary originally was scheduled to have five Tests, three ODIs and three T20I matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/29613227/england-white-ball-tour-india-postponed-2021 |title=England white-ball tour of India postponed until 2021 |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=7 August 2020}}</ref> However, in November 2020, Sourav Ganguly confirmed that one of the Test matches had been dropped with two more T20I matches added to the tour, in preparation for the [[2021 ICC Men's T20 World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/30379087/ind-vs-eng-sourav-ganguly-five-t20is,-fewer-tests-england-tour-india-eye-2021-t20-world-cup |title=Ganguly: Five T20Is, fewer Tests in England's tour of India with eye on 2021 T20 World Cup |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=24 November 2020}}</ref> On 1 January 2021, the Government of India announced that up to 50% of fans would be allowed in stadiums for sporting events, and it was expected that the BCCI would also allow fans in the stadiums for the series.<ref>{{cite web|last=Dhyani|first=Kunal|date=2021-01-01|title=IPL 2021: Good news, government to allow 50% fans in the stadium for IPL 2021 and England tour of India|url=https://www.insidesport.co/ipl-2021-good-news-government-to-allow-50-fans-in-the-stadium-for-ipl-2021-and-england-tour-of-india/|access-date=1 January 2021|website=InsideSport|language=en-gb}}</ref> Initially, the BCCI and the [[Tamil Nadu Cricket Association]] (TNCA) took the decision not to allow fans into the stadium for the first two Test matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.crictracker.com/no-spectators-will-be-allowed-in-stadiums-during-england-tour-of-india/ |title=No spectators will be allowed in stadiums during England tour of India |work=CricTracker |access-date=12 January 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/india-vs-england-chennai-tests-set-to-be-played-behind-closed-doors-1248869 |title=First two India-England Tests in Chennai set to be played behind closed doors |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=22 January 2021}}</ref> However, the decision was later reversed, with up to 50% of fans allowed to attend the second Test.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/crowds-allowed-up-to-50-capacity-for-second-india-england-test-1249872 |title=Crowds allowed up to 50% capacity for second India-England Test |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=2 February 2021}}</ref> On 27 January 2021, the England team arrived in [[Chennai]] to begin their quarantine ahead of the first Test.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thehindu.com/sport/cricket/england-cricket-players-arrive-in-chennai-for-tests-versus-india/article33674030.ece |title=England cricket players arrive in Chennai for Tests versus India |work=The Hindu |access-date=27 January 2021}}</ref>

The [[International Cricket Council]] (ICC) confirmed that [[Nitin Menon]] would be one of the on-field umpires for the first two Test matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/ind-vs-eng-2021-no-neutral-umpires-for-india-vs-england-test-series-1249492 |title=No neutral umpires for India vs England Tests; Nitin Menon, Anil Chaudhary, Virender Sharma to officiate in Chennai |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=28 January 2021}}</ref> [[Anil Chaudhary (umpire)|Anil Chaudhary]] and [[Virender Sharma]] were also named as on-field umpires for the first and second Tests respectively, with both of them making their debuts as Test cricket umpires.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cricbuzz.com/cricket-news/115969/nitin-menon-home-umpire-india-vs-england-test-series |title=Anil Chaudhary, Virender Sharma set to debut as umpires in Tests |work=CricBuzz |access-date=28 January 2021}}</ref> The same set of match officials were retained for the third and fourth Test matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cricbuzz.com/cricket-news/116284/icc-announce-same-match-officials-for-remaining-ind-eng-tests-england-cricket-tour-of-india-2021 |title=ICC announce same match officials for remaining IND-ENG Tests |work=CricBuzz |access-date=18 February 2021}}</ref>

Ahead of the first Test, [[Channel 4]] secured the live broadcasting rights for the Test matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/india-v-england-2020-21-channel-4-secure-free-to-air-uk-coverage-of-england-india-test-series-1249970 |title=Channel 4 secure free-to-air UK coverage of England-India Test series |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=3 February 2021}}</ref> The last time the channel screened live Test cricket in the United Kingdom was for the [[2005 Ashes series]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2021/feb/02/channel-4-wins-rights-for-england-test-cricket-tour-of-india |title=Channel 4 wins rights for England's Test cricket tour of India |work=The Guardian |access-date=2 February 2021}}</ref> It was also the first full broadcast of an England overseas Test series on [[terrestrial television]] in the United Kingdom.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/55902859 |title=India v England: Channel 4 to broadcast tour for first live free-to-air Test coverage since 2005 |work=BBC Sport |access-date=3 February 2021}}</ref>

Crowds were allowed to attend the first two T20I matches, with more than 66,000 fans attending each of the two games.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/56400859 |title=India v England: Remaining three T20s in Ahmedabad to be played behind closed doors |work=BBC Sport |access-date=15 March 2021}}</ref> However, after a rise in COVID-19 cases in [[Ahmedabad]], the BCCI and the [[Gujarat Cricket Association]] agreed to play the remaining T20I and ODI matches [[Behind closed doors (sport)|behind closed doors]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/india-vs-england-2021-remaining-t20is-to-be-played-behind-closed-doors-in-ahmedabad-1254967 |title=Remaining India-England T20Is to be played behind closed doors |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=15 March 2021}}</ref>

On 19 January 2021, the BCCI named India's squad for the first two Test matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sportscafe.in/cricket/articles/2021/jan/19/virat-kohli-back-for-first-two-england-tests-hardik-pandya-and-axar-patel-receive-call-ups |title=Virat Kohli back for first two England Tests; Hardik Pandya and Axar Patel receive call-ups |work=Sports Cafe |access-date=19 January 2021}}</ref> They also named [[K. S. Bharat]], [[Abhimanyu Easwaran]], [[Shahbaz Nadeem]], [[Rahul Chahar]] and [[Priyank Panchal]] as standby players, and [[Ankit Rajpoot]], [[Avesh Khan]], [[Sandeep Warrier]], [[Krishnappa Gowtham]] and [[Saurabh Kumar (cricketer)|Saurabh Kumar]] as net bowlers.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bcci.tv/articles/2021/news/148377/india-s-squad-for-first-two-tests-against-england-announced |title=India's squad for first two Tests against England announced |work=Board of Control for Cricket in India |access-date=19 January 2021}}</ref> Two days later, the ECB announced England's squad for the first two Test matches, with [[James Bracey]], [[Mason Crane]], [[Saqib Mahmood]], [[Matt Parkinson (cricketer)|Matt Parkinson]], [[Ollie Robinson]] and [[Amar Virdi]] named as reserve players.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/55747795 |title=India v England: Ben Stokes and Jofra Archer return to Joe Root's squad for first two Tests in Ahmedabad |work=BBC Sport |access-date=21 January 2021}}</ref> [[Ollie Pope]] was not originally named in England's Test squad after sustaining an injury [[Pakistani cricket team in England in 2020|against Pakistan]], but was added to the squad on 3 February 2021, after the England medical team were satisfied he had recovered sufficiently.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ecb.co.uk/news/2010611/pope-added-to-test-squad-for-india-series |title=Ollie Pope added to Test squad for India series |work=England and Wales Cricket Board |access-date=3 February 2021}}</ref> The following day, [[Zak Crawley]] was ruled out of England's squad for the first two Tests with a wrist sprain.<ref>{{cite
