'''James''' "'''J. C.'''" '''Hassenauer''' (born September 15, 1995) is an American professional [[American football|football]] [[Center (gridiron football)|center]]. He played [[college football]] for the [[Alabama Crimson Tide football|Alabama Crimson Tide]], winning national championships in [[2016 College Football Playoff National Championship|2015]] and [[2018 College Football Playoff National Championship|2017]]. Hassenauer has been a member of the [[Atlanta Falcons]], [[Birmingham Iron]], [[Pittsburgh Steelers]], [[New York Giants]], and [[Washington Commanders]].

==Early life==
Hassenauer played at East Ridge High under coach Mike Pendino. As a freshman, Hassenauer was only {{height|ft=5|in=10}} and weighed {{convert|180|lb|kg}}. Following his coach's recommendation, Hasseneur trained with former [[Minnesota Golden Gophers football|Minnesota Gophers]] center [[Ray Hitchcock]]. By his senior year at East Ridge, Hassenauer's measurements expanded to {{height|ft=6|in=3}} and weighed {{convert|300|lb|kg}}.<ref name="Alabama's Hassenauer">[https://rolltide.com/sports/football/roster/j-c-hassenauer/3910 "J.C. Hassenauer"] Profile at Alabama. Retrieved October 13, 2024.</ref><ref name="J.C. Hassenauer">[https://www.mnfootballhub.com/news_article/show/285880-j-c-hassenauer-growing-into-a-player-as-big-as-his-alabama-dream "J.C. Hassenauer"]: "Growing into a player as big as his Alabama dream". Paulsen, Jim, Star Tribune. Retrieved October 13, 2024.</ref> 

Hassenauer earned a letter during his time with the East Ridge Raptors. He also received offers from Minnesota, [[Vanderbilt Commodores football|Vanderbilt]] and Alabama.<ref name="J.C. Hassenauer"/> He verbally accepted a scholarship offer to play center for Alabama in August 2013.  It was reported in February 2014, Hassenauer signed with Alabama, making him the second player from Minnesota to sign with the university since 1925.<ref name="MN Hassenauer">[https://www.mnfootballhub.com/news_article/show/346455--a-is-for-hassenauer-as-east-ridge-lineman-signs " J.C. Hassenauer"]: "'A' is for Hassenauer as East Ridge lineman signs". La Vaque, David, Star Tribune. Retrieved October 13, 2024.</ref>

==College career==
Hassenauer began his freshman season at Alabama in 2014 on the [[Substitution (sport)|bench]] until called to play center against [[Texas A&M Aggies football|Texas A&M]]. Starting his sophomore season as a reserve [[offensive lineman]], Hassenauer joined the Crimson Tide in Game 9 against Texas A&M replacing [[Ryan Kelly (American football)|Ryan Kelly]]. He finished the season playing in only 7 games. 

In 2016, during his junior season, Hassenauer was a backup center, playing in only 9 games with the Crimson Tide. During Hassenauer's senior season in 2017, he held reserve positions as [[Guard (gridiron football)|guard]] and center and started in 3 of 8 games he played. He replaced [[Ross Pierschbacher]] during the second half of the game against [[Mississippi State Bulldogs football|Mississippi State]]. He replaced an injured [[Lester Cotton]], during the second half of the game against [[Clemson Tigers football|Clemson]]. He earned Player of the Week honors for his performance against [[Mercer Bears football|Mercer]]. Hassenauer started as a left guard during the game against [[Auburn Tigers football|Auburn]] before dropping out due to an injury.<ref name="Alabama's Hassenauer"/>   

Hassenauer earned a [[Masters of Science]] in [[Marketing]] from Alabama in 2018.<ref name="Marketing Pro J.C. Hassenauer">[https://www.linkedin.com/in/james-j-c-hassenauer-msm-666aa7b8/ "J. C. Hassenauer"] LinkedIn Profile. Retrieved October 13, 2024.</ref>

===Atlanta Falcons===
After playing four years at [[Alabama Crimson Tide football|Alabama]], Hassenauer was signed by the [[Atlanta Falcons]] as an undrafted free agent on April 30, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web |date=April 30, 2018 |title=Five Former Alabama Football Players Sign as Free Agents Following 2018 NFL Draft |url=https://rolltide.com/news/2018/4/30/five-former-alabama-football-players-sign-as-free-agents-following-2018-nfl-draft.aspx |access-date=December 30, 2019 |website=Rolltide.com}}</ref> He was waived on September 1 and signed to the [[practice squad]] the next day, where he spent most of the season.<ref>{{Cite web |last=McFadden |first=Will |date=September 1, 2018 |title=Falcons 2018 roster: Atlanta announces roster cuts, trims active roster down to 52 |url=https://www.atlantafalcons.com/news/falcons-2018-roster-atlanta-announces-roster-cuts-trims-active-roster-down-to-52 |access-date=December 30, 2019 |website=AtlantaFalcons.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=McFadden |first=Will |date=September 2, 2018 |title=Falcons sign 10 players to practice squad |url=https://www.atlantafalcons.com/news/falcons-sign-10-players-to-practice-squad |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180904121013/https://www.atlantafalcons.com/news/falcons-sign-10-players-to-practice-squad |archive-date=September 4, 2018 |access-date=December 30, 2019 |website=AtlantaFalcons.com}}</ref>

===Birmingham Iron===
After the season ended, Hassenauer signed with the [[Birmingham Iron]] of the [[Alliance of American Football]], where he played eight games.
