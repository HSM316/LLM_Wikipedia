{{Year in Algeria|2020}}

Events from [[2020]] in [[Algeria]].

== Incumbents ==
* [[President of Algeria|President]]: [[Abdelmadjid Tebboune]]
* [[Prime Minister of Algeria|Prime Minister]]: [[Abdelaziz Djerad]]

== Events ==
Ongoing – [[COVID-19 pandemic in Algeria]]

=== January ===
*January 28 – The [[Human Rights Watch]] denounces the arrests of dozens of peaceful dissidents that have occurred since the [[2019 Algerian presidential election|presidential election]] in 2019.<ref>[https://www.hrw.org/news/2020/01/28/algeria-post-election-repression Algeria: Post Election Repression] Human Rights Watch, 28 Jan 2020</ref>
*January 30 – Defense forces announce the arrest of a man planning a suicide attack against peaceful protesters.<ref>[https://af.reuters.com/article/algeriaNews/idAFL8N29Z6G2 Algeria captures man planning suicide attack - defence ministry] Reuters Africa News, 30 Jan 2020</ref>

=== February ===
*February 3 – [[Tunisian President]] [[Kais Saied]] [[Algeria–Tunisia relations|visits Algeria]] in his first trip outside the country.<ref>[https://www.middleeastmonitor.com/20200203-tunisian-president-arrives-in-algeria-on-his-first-trip-abroad/ Tunisian president arrives in Algeria on his first trip abroad] Middle East Monitor, 3 Feb 2020</ref>
*February 14 – Thousands of Algerians march against corruption and military participation in politics.<ref>{{cite web|website=Reuters|title=Algerians keep up protests a year after demonstrations began|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-algeria-protests-anniversary/algerians-keep-up-protests-a-year-after-demonstrations-began-idUSKBN2081RK?il=0|date=Feb 14, 2020}}</ref>
*February 25 – The first confirmed case of [[COVID-19 pandemic in Algeria|COVID-19 is reported in Algeria]]: an Italian man who had arrived on February 17. He was deported back to [[COVID-19 pandemic in Italy|Italy]] on February 28.<ref>[https://africatimes.com/2020/02/25/algerian-health-minister-confirms-first-covid-19-case/ Algerian health minister confirms first COVID-19 case] Africa Times, 25 Feb 2020, retrieved 30 Mar 2020</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-02-28|title=ترحيل المواطن الإيطالي المصاب بكورونا|url=https://www.echoroukonline.com/%d8%aa%d8%b1%d8%ad%d9%8a%d9%84-%d8%a7%d9%84%d9%85%d9%88%d8%a7%d8%b7%d9%86-%d8%a7%d9%84%d8%a5%d9%8a%d8%b7%d8%a7%d9%84%d9%8a-%d8%a7%d9%84%d9%85%d8%b5%d8%a7%d8%a8-%d8%a8%d9%83%d9%88%d8%b1%d9%88%d9%86/|access-date=2020-08-08|website=الشروق أونلاين|language=ar}}</ref>

=== March ===
*March 12 – Schools were locked down due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Algeria|spread of COVID-19]]. Five new cases of COVID-19, including one death, were reported.<ref>[https://www.tsa-algerie.com/coronavirus-premier-deces-en-algerie-deux-nouvelles-wilayas-touchees/ Coronavirus: first death in Algeria, two new wilayas affected] {{in lang|fr}} TSA, 12 Mar 2020, retrieved 30 Mar 2020</ref>

=== May ===
*May 14 – Algeria summons [[2020 in Morocco|Morocco's]] ambassador to [[Algiers]] in protest against when the Moroccan consul in [[Oran]] purportedly referred to [[Algeria–Morocco relations|Algeria as an "enemy country]]."<ref>{{cite web |title=Algeria summons Moroccan ambassador over 'enemy country' remark |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/05/algeria-summons-moroccan-ambassador-enemy-country-remark-200514181909333.html |website=www.aljazeera.com |accessdate=June 3, 2020}}</ref>
*May 20 – Soheib Debaghi, Larbi Tahar, and Boussif Mohamed are sentenced to tough jail terms after [[Facebook]] posts are ruled to be potentially damaging to national interest.<ref>{{cite web |title=Algeria hands tough jail terms to three opposition activists |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/05/algeria-hands-tough-jail-terms-opposition-activists-200520114735326.html |website=www.aljazeera.com |accessdate=June 3, 2020}}</ref>
*May 28 – Algeria recalls its [[Algeria–France relations|ambassador to France]] for consultations after [[French television|French public television]] aired documentaries on the anti-government protest movement, one of which contained shots of young people kissing and drinking alcohol.<ref>{{cite web |title=Algeria recalls envoy to France over public TV documentaries |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/05/algeria-recalls-envoy-france-public-tv-documentaries-200528165827920.html |website=www.aljazeera.com |accessdate=June 3, 2020}}</ref>

=== June ===
*June 2 – Opposition leaders of the [[2019–20 Algerian protests]], Karim Tabbou and Samir Benlarbi, are announced to be freed soon. "[[President of Algeria|President]] [[Abdelmadjid Tebboune]] assured me that he would use his constitutional [[Prerogative writ|prerogative]] to ensure that Tabbou and Benlarbi regain their freedom," Sosiane Djilali of the Jil Jadid party told [[AFP news agency]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Algeria to release two leaders of Hirak protest movement |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/06/algeria-release-leaders-hirak-protest-movement-200602155302721.html |website=www.aljazeera.com |accessdate=June 3, 2020}}</ref>

===October===
*October 1 - President Abdelmadjid Tebboune meets with [[U.S. Secretary of Defense]] [[Mark Esper]] to discuss security issues in [[Libya]] and the [[Sahel]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/idUSKBN26M78I |publisher=[[Reuters]] |title=U.S. defense chief's rare Algeria visit points to Sahel region threats |date=October 1, 2020 |first=Lamine |last=Chikhi |first2=Idrees |last2=Ali |first3=Angus |last3=McDowall |editor-first=Chizu |editor-last=Nomiyama |editor-first2=Daniel |editor-last2=Wallis}}</ref>

===December===
*December 29 – President Abdelmadjid Tebboune returns to Algeria after receiving treatment for [[COVID-19]] in Germany since October.<ref>{{cite news |title=President returns to Algeria, reports recovery from COVID-19 |url=https://apnews.com/article/africa-television-algeria-public-television-algiers-b34d3db094fcd15a300814663ff23dde |access-date=December 29, 2020 |work=AP NEWS |date=29 December 2020 |language=en |quote=I passed through difficult moments. I am happy to be back, thanks God.}}</ref>
