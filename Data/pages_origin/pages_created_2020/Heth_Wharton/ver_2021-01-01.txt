'''Heth Wharton''' (April 18, 1892 - October 28, 1958) was an American architect.<ref name="pcadhethwharton">{{cite web |title=Heth Wharton (Architect) |url=http://pcad.lib.washington.edu/person/1068/ |website=Pacific Coast Architecture Database |publisher=University of Washington |accessdate=January 1, 2020}}</ref> He grew up in Virginia, and he was a draughtsman for Hunt & Chambers.<ref name="latimesdemise">{{cite news |last1=McCoy |first1=Esther |title=Demise of Georgian Architecture. Blame it on the machine. |url=https://www.newspapers.com/image/381058523 |accessdate=January 1, 2020 |work=The Los Angeles Times |date=August 24, 1958|pages=35; 38|via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> With architect [[Ralph A. Vaughn]], he designed Chase Knolls Apartments in [[Sherman Oaks, California|Sherman Oaks]] and Lincoln Place in [[Venice, Los Angeles|Venice]].<ref name="laconservancywharton">{{cite web |title=Heth Wharton |url=https://www.laconservancy.org/architects/heth-wharton |website=Los Angeles Conservancy |accessdate=January 1, 2020}}</ref>

Wharton was involved in a legal dispute with film director [[John Cromwell (director)|John Cromwell]] and actress [[Kay Johnson]] when he tried to overcharge them for a house he designed for them in 1934; the couple won the lawsuit.<ref name="latimeslosessuit">{{cite news |title=Architect Loses Cromwell Suit |url=https://www.newspapers.com/image/380625665/?terms=%22Heth%2BWharton%22 |accessdate=January 1, 2020 |work=The Los Angeles Times |date=May 4, 1934|page=26|via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref><ref name="sfexaminermoviepair">{{cite news |title=Movie Pair Win In Suit by Architect |url=https://www.newspapers.com/image/458076470/?terms=%22Heth%2BWharton%22 |accessdate=January 1, 2020 |work=The San Francisco Examiner |date=May 4, 1934|page=10|via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>
