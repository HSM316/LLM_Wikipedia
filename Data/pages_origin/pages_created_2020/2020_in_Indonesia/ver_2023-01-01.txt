{{Year in Indonesia|2020}}
{{Year article header|2020}}
In Indonesia, the year was marked by the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], which started in March when two people from Jakarta tested positive to the disease. The government responded to the outbreak by enacting large-scale social restrictions (PSBB) throughout much of Indonesia, opting against imposing lockdowns because of economic reasons. The pandemic put the Indonesian economy into recession, the country's first in nearly two decades, with the rupiah free-falling. Multiple international events that were to have been hosted in Indonesia had to be cancelled because of the pandemic.

Students and workers [[Indonesia omnibus law protests|protested in October]] against the government's controversial Omnibus Law, resulting in more than 5,000 arrests. Two ministers, Social Affairs Minister [[Juliari Batubara]] and Marine Affairs and Fisheries Minister [[Edhy Prabowo]], were arrested in late 2020 for alleged corruption in separate cases.

===January===

Dubbed as one of the worst floods in Jakarta history, the massive flood was caused by the highest recorded rainfall in 24 years since record began in Jakarta.<ref>{{cite web|author=Damarjati, Danu|title=Jabodetabek Banjir, Curah Hujan 1 Januari 2020 Tertinggi Selama 24 Tahun|url=https://news.detik.com/berita/d-4843412/jabodetabek-banjir-curah-hujan-1-januari-2020-tertinggi-selama-24-tahun|publisher=Detik|accessdate=2 February 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|title=66 People Have Died in Indonesia's Devastating Floods. Here's What to Know|url=https://time.com/5761097/jakarta-indonesia-floods/|magazine=Time|accessdate=2 February 2020}}</ref>
* January 6 - Indonesia announced that it was mobilizing fishermen and naval vessels to patrol the northern part of the [[Natuna Sea]], following incursions by Chinese vessels.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-indonesia-china/indonesia-deploys-fighter-jets-in-stand-off-with-china-idUSKBN1Z61AM |title=Indonesia deploys fighter jets in stand-off with China |publisher=[[Reuters]] |date=January 7, 2020 |first1=Stanley |last1=Widianto |first2=Agustinus |last2=Beo Da Costa |first3=Wilda |last3=Asmarini |editor-first=Matthew |editor-last=Tostevin |editor2-first=Gareth |editor2-last=Jones}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3045051/chinese-coastguard-ships-sail-tensions-indonesia-build |newspaper=[[South China Morning Post]] |title=Chinese coastguard ships sail as tensions with Indonesia build |first=Kristin |last=Huang |date=January 7, 2020 |quote=Two Chinese coastguard ships left Fiery Cross Reef in the disputed South China Sea and sailed in the direction of the Indonesia-controlled Natuna Regency islands on Monday, amid renewed tensions between Beijing and Jakarta [...] One of the two ships, CCG 35111, put in at Fiery Cross – where China has refuel and resupply facilities – on Saturday |archive-url=https://archive.today/20200109012424/https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3045051/chinese-coastguard-ships-sail-tensions-indonesia-build |archive-date=January 9, 2020 |access-date=January 9, 2020 |url-status=live }}</ref>
* January 10 - The Indonesian Ministry of Forestry and Environment announced the termination of partnership with WWF due to alleged "untrue facts and data" claimed by the WWF.<ref>{{cite web|title=KLHK Putuskan Kerja Sama dengan WWF, Ini Alasannya|url=https://www.liputan6.com/bisnis/read/4166143/klhk-putuskan-kerja-sama-dengan-wwf-ini-alasannya|publisher=Liputan6|accessdate=15 February 2020|date=2020-01-28}}</ref>
* January 14 - Demonstrations took place in front of Jakarta City Hall. Opposition groups demanded the Governor of Jakarta [[Anies Baswedan]] to step down from his position due to the perceived mishandling of the emergency response in the 2020 Jakarta floods while supporters of Anies Baswedan also held two demonstrations to defend the incumbent.<ref>{{cite web|title=Demo Pro & Kontra Anies Sama-Sama Jadikan Banjir Komoditas Politik|url=https://tirto.id/demo-pro-kontra-anies-sama-sama-jadikan-banjir-komoditas-politik-esQX|publisher=Tirto|accessdate=2 February 2020}}</ref>

* January 17 - The leaders of the Indonesian micronation-cum-cult "[[Keraton Agung Sejagat]]" were arrested for public deception.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ditangkap, 'Raja-RAtu' Keraton Agung Sejagat Dijerat Pasal Penipuan|url=https://news.detik.com/berita-jawa-tengah/d-4859279/ditangkap-raja-ratu-keraton-agung-sejagat-dijerat-pasal-penipuan|publisher=Detik|accessdate=2 February 2020}}</ref>

* February 2
** 238 Indonesians were successfully evacuated to Natuna from [[Wuhan]]. The Indonesian government had planned to evacuate Indonesians in Wuhan after a massive [[COVID-19 pandemic in mainland China|outbreak in China]] caused a global health emergency, infecting thousands and killing hundreds of people.<ref>{{cite web|title=245 WNI dari Wuhan Tiba di Indonesia, Ini Tahapan Evakuasi sampai Isolasi|date=2 February 2020 |url=https://www.kompas.com/tren/read/2020/02/02/102259165/245-wni-dari-wuhan-tiba-di-indonesia-ini-tahapan-evakuasi-sampai-isolasi|publisher=Kompas|accessdate=2 February 2020}}</ref>
** Indonesian government temporarily halts the issuance of its visa-free policy and visa on arrival for all tourists from China. All flights from and to Mainland China are banned due to concern on the outbreak, effective by 5 February. Those who had been to Mainland China for 14 days during the outbreak would not be allowed to enter or transit in Indonesia.<ref>{{cite news|title=Virus corona: Pemerintah tidak izinkan semua pendatang dari China masuk dan transit di Indonesia|url=https://www.bbc.com/indonesia/indonesia-51346574|publisher=BBC Indonesia|accessdate=3 February 2020|newspaper=BBC News Indonesia|date=2020-02-02}}</ref>

* February 10 - The wreckage of an Indonesian Air Force military helicopter which had disappeared since June 2019 has been found on the mountainous region in Papua.
