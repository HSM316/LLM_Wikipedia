'''Wimpole Mews''' is a [[mews]] street in [[Marylebone]], [[London]] [[SW postcode area#W1|W1]], [[England]]. It is known for being a key location in the [[Profumo affair]] in the early 1960s.

The street runs north-south, with [[Weymouth Street]] to the north and [[New Cavendish Street]] to the south.<ref>{{cite web| title=Lockie's Topography of London | url=https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=h5EKAQAAMAAJ&pg=PP344 | page=344 | first=John | last=Lockie | website=[[Google Books]] | location=London | date=1810 | accessdate=30 December 2019 }}</ref> To the east is [[Harley Street]] and to the west is [[Wimpole Street]]. It is named after [[Wimpole Hall]] in [[Cambridgeshire]], the seat of [[Edward Harley, 2nd Earl of Oxford and Earl Mortimer]].{{sfn|Fairfield|1983|p=342}}{{sfn|Bebbington|1972|p=348}}

17 Wimpole Mews was the home of the osteopath [[Stephen Ward]] (1912–1963)<ref>{{cite article| url=https://www.historyextra.com/period/20th-century/james-norton-interview-society-osteopath-stephen-ward-profumo-affair/ | title=“Somewhere in between exploitation and empowerment”: James Norton on society osteopath Stephen Ward | first=James | last=Norton | accessdate=30 December 2019 | magazine=[[BBC History]] | publisher=[[BBC]] }}</ref> in the early 1960s. He acted as "landlord" to [[Christine Keeler]] (1942–2017)<ref>{{cite article| url=https://www.radiotimes.com/news/2019-12-29/the-trial-of-christine-keeler-real-life-history-profumo-affair/ | title=Who was Christine Keeler, and how did she know Stephen Ward? | first=Flora | last=Carr | date=29 December 2019 | magazine=[[Radio Times]] }}</ref> and [[Mandy Rice-Davies]] (1944–2014). Keeler had affairs with the politician [[John Profumo]] (1915–2006), [[Secretary of State for War]], and the [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] naval attaché and spy Captain [[Yevgeny Ivanov (spy)|Yevgeny Ivanov]] (1926–1994), resulting in the [[Profumo affair]] scandal in 1963. In December 1962, [[Johnny Edgecombe]] (1932–2010), a former lover of Christine Keeler, fired five shots at the lock of 17 Wimpole Mews using a handgun that Keeler had given him, triggering events that led to the scandal.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2009/nov/14/johnny-edgecombe-profumo-scandal-1962 | title=Haunted by the Profumo scandal | first=Mark | last=Olden | date=14 November 2009 | newspaper=[[The Guardian]] }}</ref>

The experiences of Christine Keeler were dramatised as the six-part television serial ''[[The Trial of Christine Keeler]]'' and broadcast by the [[BBC]] during December 2019 to January 2020, featuring events in Wimpole Mews.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/fashion/people/untold-story-profumo-affair-christine-keeler-ellie-bamber-playing-heroine-behind/ | title=The real story of the Profumo Affair: Ellie Bamber on playing the 'heroine' behind the 1960s sex scandal | first=Caroline | last=Leaper | date=30 December 2019 | newspaper=[[Daily Telegraph]] }}</ref><ref>{{cite article| url=https://www.historyextra.com/period/20th-century/real-history-profumo-affair-christine-keeler-who-what-happened/ | title=Sex, lies and spies: the real history of the Profumo Affair | first=Anna | last=Harris | accessdate=30 December 2019 | magazine=[[BBC History]] | publisher=[[BBC]] }}</ref>
