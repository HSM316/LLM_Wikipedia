'''Heman Lowry''' (September 4, 1778 - January 5, 1848) was a county, state and federal government official in Vermont.  He was a delegate to two state constitutional conventions (1814, 1828).  Lowry was also the longtime sheriff of [[Chittenden County, Vermont|Chittenden County]] (1810-1813, 1815-1827, 1835-1836).  In addition, he served twice as U.S. Marshal for Vermont (1829-1835, 1837-1841).

==Life==
Lowry was born in [[North East, New York]] on September 4, 1778, the son of Thomas Lowry and Phoebe (Benedict) Lowry.{{sfn|''Vermont Historical Gazetteer''|page=602}}{{sfn|''The History of Jericho, Vermont''|page=560}}{{sfn|''Genealogical and Family History of the State of Vermont''|page=15}}  The Lowry family (sometimes spelled Lowrey){{sfn|''The History of Jericho, Vermont''|page=560}} moved to [[Jericho, Vermont]] in 1789, and after completing his education Lowry became a farmer in Jericho.{{sfn|''Vermont Historical Gazetteer''|page=602}}  Active in politics as a [[Democratic-Republican Party|Democratic-Republican]], and later as a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]],{{sfn|''Vermont Historical Gazetteer''|page=602}} in 1809 he became [[High bailiff (Vermont)|high bailiff]] of [[Chittenden County, Vermont|Chittenden County]].{{sfn|"Proceedings of the Legislature"|page=2}}  In 1810 he became sheriff and he served until 1813.{{sfn|''Catalogue of the Principal Officers of Vermont''|pages=91-92}}  He returned to the position in 1815 and served until 1827.{{sfn|''Catalogue of the Principal Officers of Vermont''|pages=92-95}}  He was Jericho's delegate to the state constitutional convention in 1814,{{sfn|''Catalogue of the Principal Officers of Vermont''|page=102}} and the delegate from [[Burlington, Vermont|Burlington]] in 1828.{{sfn|''Catalogue of the Principal Officers of Vermont''|page=103}}

In 1829, Lowry was appointed U.S. Marshal from Vermont, a position he held until 1835.{{sfn|"Heman Lowry Appointed Marshal"|page=3}}  In 1835 and 1836, he again served as sheriff of Chittenden County.{{sfn|''Catalogue of the Principal Officers of Vermont''|page=96}}{{sfn|"Appointment by the President: George W. Barker"|page=2}}  He was reappointed as Marshal in 1837 and he served until 1841.{{sfn|"Appointments by the President: Heman Lowry"|page=2}}{{sfn|"Gen Wm. Barron of Bradford"|page=3}}  He was a delegate to the Democratic state convention in 1841,{{sfn|"Democratic Convention"|page=2}}{{sfn|"Democratic State Convention"|page=2}} and president of the party's Chittenden County convention in 1842.{{sfn|"Democratic County Convention" (1842)|page=3}}  He was also president of the Democratic county convention in July 1843.{{sfn|"Democratic County Convention" (1843)|page=2}}  In November 1843, Lowry was one of several prominent Vermont Democrats who took part in an event at which the guest of honor was former Vice President [[Richard M. Johnson]], who was campaigning for the party's 1844 presidential nomination.{{sfn|"Col. Johnson"|page=2}}  In 1844 he was one of the organizers of the Chittenden County Agricultural Society.{{sfn|"Agricultural Society"|page=2}}

Lowry died in Burlington on January 5, 1848.{{sfn|''Genealogical and Family History of the State of Vermont''|page=15}}  Cemetery records indicate he was buried at Elmwood Cemetery in Burlington.{{sfn|"Vermont Vital Records"}}{{efn|There is also a headstone for Lowry at Lowrey Cemetery in Jericho, which indicates that either he was reinterred at Lowrey Cemetery after initial burial at Elmwood, or that the grave marker at Lowrey Cemetery is a cenotaph.}}

==Family==
In 1800, Lowry married Lucy Lee of Jericho.{{sfn|''Vermont Historical Gazetteer''|page=602}}{{sfn|''Genealogical and Family History of the State of Vermont''|page=16}}  She died in 1801, and in 1803 he married Margaret Campbell, who died in 1849.{{sfn|''Vermont Historical Gazetteer''|page=602}}{{sfn|''Genealogical and Family History of the State of Vermont''|page=16}}  They were the parents of a son, Heman Lowry Jr. (1819-1860).{{sfn|''Genealogical and Family History of the State of Vermont''|page=16}}
