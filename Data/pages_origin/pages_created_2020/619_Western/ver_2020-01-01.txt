'''619 Western''' is a building at 619 Western Avenue in Seattle.

From 1980 to 2011 it hosted an artist community.<ref>{{cite web |last1=April 03 |first1=Anna Callahan on |title=619 Western Avenue |url=https://www.4culture.org/619-western-avenue/ |website=4Culture |date=3 April 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=619 Western Building Artists Take Art Walk Outside |url=https://historicseattle.org/619-western-building-artists-take-art-walk-outside/ |website=[[Historic Seattle]] |date=4 August 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Guay |first1=Abigail |title=Dozens of artists to be evicted from Pioneer Square studios |url=https://www.realchangenews.org/2011/01/17/dozens-artists-be-evicted-pioneer-square-studios |website=www.realchangenews.org |language=en |date=17 January 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Artist colony at 619 Western moved by tunnel project |url=https://www.king5.com/article/news/artist-colony-at-619-western-moved-by-tunnel-project/281-146140862 |website=KING}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Graves |first1=Jen |title=Killing the Colony |url=https://www.thestranger.com/seattle/killing-the-colony/Content?oid=6264461 |website=The Stranger |language=en |date=13 January 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Western Building declared unsafe, artists to move early |url=https://www.seattletimes.com/seattle-news/western-building-declared-unsafe-artists-to-move-early/ |website=The Seattle Times |date=20 July 2011}}</ref>
