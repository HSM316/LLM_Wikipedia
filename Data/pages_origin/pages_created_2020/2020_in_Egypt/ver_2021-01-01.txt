=== January ===
*7 – 11 January – The [[Volleyball at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Men's African qualification|African Qualification Tournament]] for the [[Volleyball at the 2020 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2020 Men's Olympic Volleyball Tournament]] was held in [[Cairo]]. The tournament was won by [[Tunisia men's national volleyball team|Tunisia]] who qualified for the [[2020 Summer Olympics]].<ref name="drawing">{{cite web|date=17 December 2019|title=Drawing of lots for Volleyball Olympic qualifiers done|url=https://www.cavb.org/2019/12/18/drawing-of-lots-for-volleyball-olympic-qualifiers-done/|access-date=29 December 2019|publisher=[[African Volleyball Confederation|CAVB]]}}</ref>
*16 – 26 January – The [[2020 African Men's Handball Championship|African Men's Handball Championship]] was held in [[Tunisia]]. Egypt won their seventh title after defeating [[Tunisia]] 27 to 23 in the final.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-01-26|title=L'Egypte champion d'Afrique 2020|url=http://www.can2020.tn/legypte-champion-dafrique-2020/|access-date=2020-08-31|website=CAN 2020|language=fr-FR}}</ref>
*28 January – 7 February – The [[2020 Africa Futsal Cup of Nations]] was hosted in [[Morocco]] after being postponed due to [[Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on sports|the COVID-19 pandemic]]. [[Morocco]] successfully defended their title after beating Egypt 5–0 in the final.<ref name="hosts">{{cite web|date=29 September 2018|title=Decisions of CAF Executive {{sic|nolink=y|Comm|itee}} - 27 & 28 September 2018|url=http://www.cafonline.com/en-US/NewsCenter/News/NewsDetails?id=uLxLJ117lJ7E68bnx%2fVQWg%3d%3d|publisher=CAF}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Football|first=CAF-Confedération Africaine du|title=Five-star Morocco hammer Egypt to retain title {{!}} Total Futsal Africa Cup of Nations|url=https://www.cafonline.com/total-futsal-africa-cup-of-nations/news/five-star-morocco-hammer-egypt-to-retain-title|access-date=2020-08-28|website=CAFOnline.com|language=en}}</ref>
*31 January – A 12-year-old girl died after a [[Female genital mutilation|genital mutilation surgery]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=In Egypt, 12-year-old girl dies after genital mutilation|url=https://news.yahoo.com/egypt-12-old-girl-dies-125836798.html|access-date=2020-08-31|website=news.yahoo.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== February ===
*1 February – The [[Grand Mufti]] began to rule on the legality of 37 death sentences for terrorism.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Egypt weighs 37 death sentences that include militant leader|url=https://news.yahoo.com/egypt-weighs-37-death-sentences-105519501.html|access-date=2020-08-31|website=news.yahoo.com|language=en-US}}</ref>
*3 February – [[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant|Daesh]] claimed responsibility for a gas pipeline explosion in the [[Sinai Peninsula]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=AFP|title=Islamic State claims purported attack on Egypt-Israel gas pipeline in Sinai|url=https://www.timesofisrael.com/islamic-state-claims-purported-attack-on-egypt-israel-gas-pipeline-in-sinai/|access-date=2020-08-31|website=www.timesofisrael.com|language=en-US}}</ref>
*10 – 13 February – The 2020 [[Africa Continental Team Badminton Championships|All Africa Men's and Women's Team Badminton Championships]] was held in [[Cairo]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Aarhus Awaits the World|work=bwfthomasubercups.bwfbadminton.com|url=https://bwfthomasubercups.bwfbadminton.com/news-single/2020/02/06/aarhus-awaits-the-world/|access-date=10 February 2020}}</ref>
*10 – 16 February – The [[2020 Zed Tennis Open]], the first edition of the tournament to be part of the [[2020 ITF Women's World Tennis Tour]], took place in [[Cairo]].<ref>{{cite web|title=W100 Cairo|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/tournament/w100-cairo/egy/2020/w-itf-egy-06a-2020/overview/|work=ITFtennis.com|publisher=ITF World Tennis Tour}}</ref>
*11 February – The [[Demographics of Egypt|Egyptian population]] reached 100 million.<ref>{{cite web|website=The New Arab|title=Egypt's population reaches record 100 million mark|date=Feb 11, 2020|access-date=Feb 11, 2020|url=https://www.alaraby.co.uk/english/news/2020/2/11/egypts-population-reaches-record-100-million-mark|language=en}}</ref>
*14 February – The [[Egyptian Health Ministry]] confirmed the first case of [[COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt|COVID-19 in the country]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Egypt confirms coronavirus case, the first in Africa|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/02/egypt-confirms-coronavirus-case-africa-200214190840134.html|access-date=2020-08-27|website=www.aljazeera.com}}</ref>
*14 – 16 February – The [[2020 African Badminton Championships]] was held at the [[Cairo Stadium Indoor Halls Complex|Cairo Stadium Hall 2]] in [[Cairo]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The Road to Glory: All Africa Senior Badminton Championships 2020|url=https://badmintonafrica.com/the-road-to-glory-all-africa-senior-badminton-championships-2020-2/|access-date=13 February 2020|website=Badminton Confederation of Africa}}</ref>
*16 February – [[Judiciary of Egypt|Egypt's chief prosecutor]] denied that 28-year-old Egyptian student and human rights activist [[Detention of Patrick Zaki|Patrick George Zaki]] was tortured for criticizing President [[Abdel Fattah el-Sisi|Fattah el-Sissi]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Egypt’s top prosecutor denies activist was tortured|url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/egypts-top-prosecutor-denies-activist-tortured-69015422|access-date=2020-08-31|website=ABC News|language=en}}</ref>
*17 – 23 February – The [[2020 Zed Tennis Open II]] took place in [[Cairo]].<ref>{{cite web|title=W60 Cairo|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/tournament/w60-cairo/egy/2020/w-itf-egy-07a-2020/overview/|work=ITFtennis.com|publisher=ITF World Tennis Tour}}</ref>
*29 February – The country announced that it will use "all means" to defend its interests in a dispute with [[Egypt–Ethiopia relations|Ethiopia]] and [[Egypt–Sudan relations|Sudan]] over a [[Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam|dam]] on the [[Nile|Nile River]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Egypt: We'll use 'all means' to defend Nile interests|url=https://news.yahoo.com/egypt-well-means-defend-nile-190432433.html|access-date=2020-08-31|website=news.yahoo.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== March ===
*8 March – A [[Egypt–Germany relations|60-year-old German citizen]] in [[Hurghada]] died, the first [[COVID-19 pandemic in Germany|German fatality from the virus]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Hamburg|first=Hamburger Abendblatt-|date=10 March 2020|title=Coronavirus: RKI meldet 1139 Fälle – Israel weist alle Touristen aus|url=https://www.abendblatt.de/vermischtes/article228637475/Coronavirus-Corona-News-Live-Ticker-Covid-19-Tote-in-Deutschland-1164-Infektionen.html|website=www.abendblatt.de}}</ref>
*13 March
**After a [[Tourism in Egypt|second Tunisian]] coming back from Egypt tested [[COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt|positive for the virus]], Tunisia officially added the country to a list of outbreak areas. As a result, Tunisia closed [[Egypt–Tunisia relations|its borders with Egypt]] and imposed a quarantine to anyone coming from the country.<ref name="Kapitalis">{{cite web|date=13 March 2020|title=Fakhfakh: "Suspension de toutes les lignes maritimes et réduction importante des dessertes aériennes"|trans-title=Fakhfakh: "Suspension of all shipping lines and significant reduction of air services"|url=http://kapitalis.com/tunisie/2020/03/13/fakhfakh-fermeture-de-toutes-les-lignes-maritimes-et-reduction-importante-des-dessertes-aeriennes/|access-date=14 March 2020|work=kapitalis.com|language=fr}}</ref>
**Twenty people were killed after the [[2020 Middle East storms|strongest storm in the region in 35 to 40 years]] hits Egypt. The storm, nicknamed "the dragon," also lead to the closure of [[Luxor International Airport]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Nearly 20 killed as 'dragon' storm brings widespread flooding to Egypt|url=https://www.middleeasteye.net/news/egypt-weather-dragon-storm-several-killed|access-date=Apr 25, 2020|website=Middle East Eye|language=en}}</ref>
*18 March – Police detained four activists after they protested in front of the [[Cabinet of Egypt|cabinet headquarters]] calling for the release of political prisoners to protect them from the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt|spread of COVID-19]].<ref>{{cite web|date=18 March 2020|title=Coronavirus: Egypt detains novelist Ahdaf Soueif for demanding prisoners' release|url=https://www.middleeasteye.net/news/coronavirus-egypt-detains-activists-demanded-release-prisoners-over-fears|access-date=19 March 2020|work=Middle East Eye}}</ref>
*19 March – The [[Politics of Egypt|minister of aviation]] closed airports and suspended all air travel until 31 March [[COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt|due to COVID-19]].<ref>{{cite web|date=16 March 2020|title=Egypt Shuts Down Airports, Suspends Air Travel: PM|url=https://egyptianstreets.com/2020/03/16/egypt-shuts-down-airports-suspends-air-travel-pm/}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|date=19 March 2020|title=فيروس كورونا: حالة وفاة و46 إصابة جديدة في مصر وتزايد الإصابات في دول عربية|trans-title=Corona Virus: One death, 46 new infections in Egypt, and increasing infections in Arab countries|url=https://www.bbc.com/arabic/middleeast-51963726|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200320110826/https://www.bbc.com/arabic/middleeast-51963726|archive-date=20 March 2020|access-date=21 March 2020|publisher=[[BBC Arabic]]|language=ar}}</ref>
*30 March – Museums and archaeological sites, including the [[Giza pyramid complex|Pyramids]] and the [[Great Sphinx of Giza|Sphinx at Giza]], were closed until at least April 15 in an effort to combat the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt|spread of COVID-19]]. Despite this, authorities still planned to light up the pyramids on 30 March to honor [[Healthcare in Egypt|Egyptian health workers]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prisoners riot in Iran; Netanyahu goes into self-quarantine|url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/jordan-releases-travelers-quarantined-dead-sea-hotels-69867295|access-date=2020-08-31|website=ABC News|language=en}}</ref>

=== April ===
*4 April – The number of cases of [[COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt|COVID-19 in the country]] surpassed 1000.<ref>{{cite news|date=4 April 2020|title=Egypt's coronavirus infection rate exceeds 1,000 on Saturday: Ministry of Health and Population|website=egyptindependent.com|url=https://egyptindependent.com/egypts-coronavirus-infection-rate-exceeds-1000-on-saturday-ministry-of-health-and-population/}}</ref>
*23 April
**The nighttime curfew was shortened by one hour for [[Ramadan]], with the new curfew running from 9 pm to 6 am.<ref>{{cite web |title=Egypt shortens night curfew by an hour for Ramadan |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-egypt/egypt-shortens-night-curfew-by-an-hour-for-ramadan-idUSKCN2252GD |website=Reuters |access-date=Apr 23, 2020 |language=en |date=23 April 2020}}</ref>
**An agreement between the Spanish firm [[Naturgy|Naturgy Energy Group S.A.]], Italian firm [[Eni|Eni S.p.A.]], and the [[Politics of Egypt|Egyptian government]] failed to resolve disputes over a gas plant in [[Damietta]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Eni and Naturgy's Damietta gas plant deal with Egypt falls through |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-egypt-gas-eni-naturgy/eni-and-naturgys-damietta-gas-plant-deal-with-egypt-falls-through-idUSKCN2252KD |website=Reuters |access-date=Apr 23, 2020 |language=en |date=23 April 2020}}</ref>

=== May ===
*3 May – On [[World Press Freedom Day]], [[Amnesty International]] issues a statement claiming that [[Mass media in Egypt|journalism in Egypt]] has effectively become a crime over the past four years.<ref>{{cite web |title=Amnesty reports chilling details of Egypt press crackdown |url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/amnesty-reports-chilling-details-egypt-press-crackdown-70475281 |website=ABC News |access-date=May 4, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>
*8 May – President [[Abdel Fattah el-Sisi|Abdel-Fattah el-Sissi]] expanded his state-of-emergency powers in response to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt|increasing threat of COVID-19]]. As a result of the increased powers, public and private meetings, protests, celebrations and other forms of assembly can now be banned while taxes and utility payments can be postponed. The state of emergency had originally began in April 2017.<ref>{{cite web |title=Egypt’s president expands powers, citing virus outbreak |url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/egypts-president-expands-powers-citing-virus-outbreak-70595331 |website=ABC News |access-date=May 9, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>
*17 May – [[Lina Attalah]], editor-in-chief for the independent news website ''[[Mada Masr]]'', was arrested during an interview.<ref>{{cite web |title=Independent Egyptian media outlet says top editor arrested |url=https://news.yahoo.com/independent-egyptian-media-outlet-says-133648126.html |website=news.yahoo.com |access-date=May 17, 2020}}</ref>

=== June ===
*2 June – Egyptian-American [[Mohamed Soltan]] sued former Prime Minister [[Hazem El Beblawi]] for [[crimes against humanity]]. Soltan had been previously imprisoned in the country for 21 months after protesting against the [[2013 Egyptian coup d'état]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Egypt's ex-PM faces torture allegation in American's lawsuit |url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/egypts-pm-faces-torture-allegations-washington-dc-71009268 |website=ABC News |access-date=June 2, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>
*17 – 20 June – [[Crime in Egypt|Egyptian hackers]] engaged in cyber attacks against [[Ethiopian National Defense Force|Ethiopia's security forces]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Munawer |first1=Qamar |title=Egyptian cyberattack on Ethiopian Security Agency website and some other |url=https://www.easternherald.com/news/egypt-cyber-attack-ethiopia-72854/ |website=The Eastern Herald |access-date=June 27, 2020 |date=23 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Zelalem |first1=Zecharias |title=An Egyptian cyber attack on Ethiopia by hackers is the latest strike over the Grand Dam |url=https://qz.com/africa/1874343/egypt-cyber-attack-on-ethiopia-is-strike-over-the-grand-dam/ |website=Quartz Africa |access-date=June 27, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>
*25 June – The [[International Monetary Fund|IMF]] granted another US$5.2 billion loan to Egypt, bringing the total value of the loans at US$14.8 billion, in order to help the [[Economy of Egypt|country's economy]] through [[COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt|the pandemic]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Egypt eases restrictions despite surge in virus infections|url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/egypt-eases-restrictions-surge-virus-infections-71489334|access-date=2020-08-28|website=ABC News|language=en}}</ref>
*26 June – The country lifted numerous COVID-19 restrictions, allowing for the reopening of cafes, clubs, gyms and theaters following over three months of closure. Mosques and churches were also partially reopened while the nighttime curfew was completely lifted. The moves come despite a [[COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt|continued growth in cases]] and were aimed at reducing the [[COVID-19 recession|economic burden of the pandemic]].<ref name=":0" />
*27 June – Egypt executed [[Terrorism in Egypt|Libyan militant]] Abdel-Rahim al-Mosmari for plotting an attack that killed at least 16 police officers in October 2017.<ref>{{cite web |title=Egypt executes Libyan militant for plotting deadly attack |url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/egypt-executes-libyan-militant-plotting-deadly-attack-71491630 |website=ABC News |access-date=June 27, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

*6 July
**The government arrested [[Healthcare in Egypt|several doctors and other medical workers]] who had criticized the governments handling of [[COVID-19 pandemic in Egypt|the COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Egypt arrests doctors, silences critics over virus outbreak |url=https://news.yahoo.com/egypt-arrests-doctors-silences-critics-061449405.html |website=news.yahoo.com |access-date=July 6, 2020}}</ref>
**Egyptian-American student Mohamed Amashah, age 24, is released from [[Tora Prison|Tora prison]] in [[Cairo]] after 16 months of captivity.<ref>{{cite web |title=American student released after 486 days in Egyptian prison |url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/american-student-released-486-days-egyptian-prison-71639193 |website=ABC News |access-date=July 6, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>
*10 July
**An Egyptian spy is uncovered in a German Press Office. He was looking for information on Egyptian opposition figures in Germany.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-germany-egypt-idUSKBN24B22K|title=Germany says suspected Egyptian spy had no access to sensitive data|date=July 10, 2020|via=www.reuters.com}}</ref>
**The government bans kite-flying and seizes 369 kites in [[Cairo]] and 99 in [[Alexandria]], citing both safety and national security concerns.<ref>{{cite web |title=Egypt grounds kites for 'safety', 'national security' |url=https://news.yahoo.com/egypt-grounds-kites-safety-national-security-160711929.html |website=news.yahoo.com |access-date=July 12, 2020}}</ref>
*13 July – In response to the complaints of at least 100 women of sexual assault, harassment, blackmail, and rape, activists set up @assaultpolice on [[Instagram]] to collect allegations. Many of the anonymous accounts come from students at [[The American University in Cairo]] and the [[American International School in Egypt|American International School]], one of the [[Education in Egypt|country’s most expensive]] private high schools.<ref>{{cite web |title=Accusations of serial assault spark new #MeToo wave in Egypt |url=https://news.yahoo.com/accusations-serial-assault-spark-metoo-062943152.html |website=news.yahoo.com |access-date=July 13, 2020}}</ref>
