'''Aung Shwe Prue Chowdhury''' (1 August 1914 – 8 August 2012) was a Bangladeshi from [[Bandarban District|Bandarban]] belonging to [[Bangladesh Nationalist Party]]. He was the 15th King of [[Bohmong Circle]].<ref name=a/>

==Biography==
Chowdhury was born on 1 August 1914.<ref name=b/> He was elected as a member of the [[East Pakistan Legislative Assembly]] in 1970.<ref name=c/> He was elected as a member of the [[Jatiya Sangsad]] from Chittagong Hill Tracts-2 in 1979.<ref name="1979results">{{cite web |url=http://www.parliament.gov.bd/images/pdf/formermp/2nd.pdf |title=List of 2nd Parliament Members |website=Jatiya Sangsad |format=PDF |language=bn |access-date=13 August 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180904090815/http://www.parliament.gov.bd/images/pdf/formermp/2nd.pdf |archive-date=4 September 2018}}</ref> He was also appointed as the state minister of the [[Ministry of Food (Bangladesh)|Ministry of Food]].<ref name=d>{{cite news |url=https://www.kalerkantho.com/print-edition/2nd-rajdhani/2018/12/19/716114 |script-title=bn:বান্দরবানে বীর বাহাদুর ও সাচিং প্রু টেনশনে |date=19 December 2018 |access-date=1 January 2019 |work=Kaler Kantho |language=bn}}</ref>

Chowdhury was appointed as the King of [[Bohmong Circle]] on 19 November 1998.<ref name=c/> He remained as the King of [[Bohmong Circle]] till his death.{{fact|date=July 2024}}

Chowdhury was married to Abain Prue Chowdhury.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://m.prothomalo.com/bangladesh/article/140401/শো-ক |script-title=bn:শো ক |date=6 February 2014 |newspaper=Prothom Alo |language=bn |access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref> They had six sons and two daughters.<ref name=a/> His son  [[Saching Prue Jerry]] is a former member of the [[Jatiya Sangsad]].<ref name=c/><ref name=d/> His co-sister-in-law [[Ma Mya Ching]] is also a former member of the [[Jatiya Sangsad]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.kalerkantho.com/print-edition/priyo-desh/2017/05/18/498413 |script-title=bn:বিএনপির দুই পক্ষ মুখোমুখি |date=18 May 2017 |access-date=1 January 2019 |work=Kaler Kantho |language=bn}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.prothomalo.com/bangladesh/%E0%A6%95%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%A8%E0%A7%8D%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%8D%E0%A6%B0%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B0-%E0%A6%A6%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%95%E0%A7%87-%E0%A6%A4%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%95%E0%A6%BF%E0%A7%9F%E0%A7%87-%E0%A6%AE%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%AE%E0%A6%BF-%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97%E0%A6%A8%E0%A7%87 |script-title=bn:কেন্দ্রের দিকে তাকিয়ে মামি-ভাগনে |date=22 November 2018 |access-date=1 January 2019 |work=Prothom Alo |language=bn}}</ref>

Chowdury died on 8 August 2012 at the age of 98.<ref name=a>{{cite news |url=https://m.banglanews24.com/national/news/bd/131441.details |script-title=bn:বান্দরবানের বোমাং রাজা অংশৈ প্রু চৌধুরীর মহাপ্রয়াণ |date=8 August 2012 |work=Banglanews24.com |language=bn |access-date=1 January 2020 |archive-date=1 January 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101091825/https://m.banglanews24.com/national/news/bd/131441.details |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=c>{{cite news |url=https://www.kalerkantho.com/print-edition/campus/2012/08/09/276586 |script-title=bn:বোমাং রাজা অং শৈ প্রু চৌধুরী আর নেই |date=9 August 2012 |newspaper=Kaler Kantho |language=bn |access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref>

==Controversy==
Chowdhury collaborated with the [[Pakistan Army]] during the [[Liberation War of Bangladesh]].<ref name=b>{{cite news |url=https://www.jugantor.com/country-news/256243/রাজাকারের-তালিকায়-পাহাড়ের-দুই-রাজা |script-title=bn:রাজাকারের তালিকায় পাহাড়ের দুই রাজা |date=16 December 2019 |work=Jugantor |language=bn |access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.dainikamadershomoy.com/post/232184 |script-title=bn:রাজাকারের তালিকায় পার্বত্য চট্টগ্রামের ২ রাজা |date=17 December 2019 |work=Amader Shomoy |language=bn |access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref> He also took oath as a member of a provincial cabinet during the [[Liberation War of Bangladesh]].<ref name=c/><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.thedailystar.net/september-17-1971-54690 |title=September 17, 1971 |date=12 December 2014 |newspaper=The Daily Star |access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref>
