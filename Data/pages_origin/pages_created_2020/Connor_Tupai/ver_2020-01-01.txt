'''Connor Tupai''' (born 8 December 1999) is a New Zealand born [[rugby union]] player currently playing for [[Northampton Saints]] in [[Premiership Rugby]]. He plays as a [[Scrum-half (rugby union)|Scrum-Half]]. 

Connor is the son of Paul Tupai who played over one thousand rugby union games for club and country across a 28-year playing career.<ref name="Last Survivor">{{cite web |url= https://www.telegraph.co.uk/rugby-union/2018/05/02/paul-tupai-last-survivor-rugbys-move-professionalism-bows-went |title= Paul Tupai ends 28-year career - 'I went from getting a few beers to thousands of dollars' |publisher=telegraph.co.uk |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

==Background==
Tupai was born in [[Rotorua]], New Zealand on 8 December 1999.<ref name="Connor Tupai Player Profile">{{cite web |url= https://www.northamptonsaints.co.uk/person/connor-tupai |title= Connor Tupai Scrum Half |publisher=northamptonsaints.co.uk |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> At the age of 6 he moved to England with his family. His father, Paul Tupai had been playing for [[Bay of Plenty Rugby Union|Bay of Plenty]], stepping down in October 2005.<ref name=" About Us - Union ">{{cite web |url= https://www.boprugby.co.nz/about-us/union/|title= About Us - Union |publisher=boprugby.co.nz |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> Following a tour to England with [[Samoa national rugby union team|Samoa rugby]], he was spotted by [[Budge Pountney]], director of rugby at [[Northampton Saints]] at the time.<ref name=" Paul Tupai, in perpetuity ">{{cite web |url= https://samrobertsrugby.com/2017/09/05/paul-tupai-in-perpetuity/|title= Paul Tupai, in perpetuity |publisher= samrobertsrugby.com |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> Paul Tupai signed for Northampton in December 2005.<ref name="Tupai to join Northampton">{{cite web |url= https://www.espn.co.uk/rugby/story/_/id/15382642/tupai-join-northampton|title= Tupai to join Northampton |publisher= espn.com |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
<br><br>
Connor Tupai began his rugby career playing for his school, [[Northampton School for Boys]]. He was billed as a dynamic utility back during his school playing career, excelling at fly-half and centre.<ref name=" NSB Young Saints Join Senior Academy For 2018/19 Season">{{cite web |url= https://www.nsb.northants.sch.uk/news/?pid=3&nid=1&storyid=1207|title= NSB Young Saints Join Senior Academy For 2018/19 Season|publisher= nsb.northants.sch.uk |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
<br><br>
In 2016, Tupai and the NSB team reached the 2016 [[NatWest Schools Cup]] Vase final at [[Twickenham Stadium]]. Connor scored a game winning drop goal and claimed man of the match.<ref name="NatWest Schools Cup 2015-16">{{cite web |url= https://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/rugbyunion/schools-cup/12191352/NatWest-Schools-Cup-2015-16-Northampton-School-for-Boys-defeat-St-Ambrose-College-to-secure-U18-Vase.html |title= NatWest Schools Cup 2015-16|publisher= telegraph.co.uk |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
<br><br>
He has a close relationship with retired Saints and [[England national rugby union team | England Rugby]] captain, [[Dylan Hartley]]. Hartley and Connor’s father moved to Northampton from Rotorua at a similar time. Tupai took Hartley under his wing who in turn babysat the young Connor Tupai, teaching him ball skills. Their brotherly relationship remains strong today.<ref name=" Paul Tupai, in perpetuity ">{{cite web |url= https://samrobertsrugby.com/2017/09/05/paul-tupai-in-perpetuity/|title= Paul Tupai, in perpetuity |publisher= samrobertsrugby.com |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
==Club career==
===Northampton Saints===
Tupai joined the [[Northampton Saints]] junior academy at a young age and was awarded his first professional contract with the club’s senior academy in 2018.<ref name="Tupai and Sleightholme stay on">{{cite web |url= https://www.northamptonchron.co.uk/sport/tupai-and-sleightholme-stay-on-as-saints-hand-senior-contracts-to-five-young-stars-1-8413532 |title= Tupai and Sleightholme stay on |publisher= northamptonchron.co.uk |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
<br><br>
The young scrum half appeared in his first Northampton senior game in the annual Mobbs Memorial Match against the [[Army Rugby Football Club]] in 2018.<ref name="Army Take On Northampton Saints To Honour WWI Rugby Player">{{cite web |url= https://www.forces.net/sport/army-take-northampton-saints-honour-wwi-rugby-player |title= Army Take On Northampton Saints To Honour WWI Rugby Player|publisher= forces.net|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
<br><br>
He captained a young Northampton team during the [[Premiership Rugby Sevens Series]] in 2019.<ref name=" Tupai to lead young saints side for Premership Rugby 7s">{{cite web |url= https://www.northants-chamber.co.uk/news/tupai-to-lead-young-saints-side-for-premership-rugby-7s |title= Tupai to lead young saints side for Premership Rugby 7s|publisher= northants-chamber.co.uk|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> Hosted at [[Franklin’s Gardens]], the Saints team failed to win a game and did not progress in the competition, ultimately won by [[Saracens F.C. |Saracens]].<ref name="Premiership Rugby 7s Fixtures and Results">{{cite web |url= https://www.premiershiprugby.com/premiership-rugby-7s/#fixtures_&_results |title= Premiership Rugby 7s Fixtures and Results |publisher= premiershiprugby.com|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
<br><br>
Following a steady string of off the bench appearances for Northampton, Tupai started his first [[European Rugby Champions Cup]] game against [[Leinster Rugby|Leinster]] at the age of 20.<ref name=" Saints scrum-half Tupai admits his shock after 'surreal' Champions Cup experience">{{cite web |url= https://www.northamptonchron.co.uk/sport/rugby-union/saints-scrum-half-tupai-admits-his-shock-after-surreal-champions-cup-experience-1-9178513 |title= Saints scrum-half Tupai admits his shock after 'surreal' Champions Cup experience|publisher= northamptonchron.co.uk|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> Northampton lost, 50-21.<ref name="Leinster beat Northampton 50-21 to secure last-eight spot with two pool rounds to spare">{{cite web |url= https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/rugby-union/50783414 |title= Leinster beat Northampton 50-21 to secure last-eight spot with two pool rounds to spare|publisher= bbc.co.uk|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
