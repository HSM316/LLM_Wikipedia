"'''Deep in the Darkness a Starlight is Gleaming'''" is a British Christian [[Epiphany]] hymn by Jan Berry. The hymn was written to reference to the [[Massacre of the Holy Innocents]] following the visit of the [[Biblical Magi]] to the [[Infant Jesus|baby Jesus]].

== History ==
"Deep in the darkness a starlight is gleaming" was written by Jan Berry, a [[Baptist]] and [[United Reformed Church]] minister.<ref name=m>{{cite web |url=https://www.methodist.org.uk/our-faith/worship/singing-the-faith-plus/hymns/deep-in-the-darkness-a-starlight-is-gleaming-stf-625/ |title=Deep in the Darkness a Starlight is Gleaming |publisher=The Methodist Church |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> The hymn came about after a United Reformed Church minister approached her and stated that they could not find an appropriate hymn for Epiphany that covered the massacre of the Holy Innocents by [[King Herod the Great|King Herod]] at the end of the Epiphany timeline.<ref name=m /> This was owning to not many hymns covering the topic aside of a verse in "[[Unto Us Is Born a Son]]".<ref name=m /> Berry copyrighted the hymn in 2011 when it was published in the [[Methodist Church of Great Britain]]'s ''[[Singing the Faith]]'' hymnal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://hymnary.org/text/deep_in_the_darkness_a_starlight_is |title=Deep in the darkness a starlight is gleaming |publisher=Hymnary.org |date= |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> It was originally set to the "Epiphany Hymn" tune, the same one used in the fellow Epiphany hymn "[[Brightest and Best|Brightest and Best of the Sons of the Morning]]".<ref>{{cite book |first=Geoffrey |last=Duncan |title=Shine On, Star of Bethlehem |publisher=Hymns Ancient and Modern Ltd |date=2004 |isbn=1853115886 |page=241}}</ref> However an original hymn tune called "High Point" was created for it by Paul Leddington Wright.<ref name=m /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://hymnary.org/tune/high_point_wright |title=High Point |publisher=Hymnary.org |date= |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref>

== Verse ==
The hymn uses imagery of a long journey that a Christian has to take through life. The second verse references [[Matthew 2:13]]-18 where [[Virgin Mary|Mary]], [[St Joseph|Joseph]] and [[Jesus]] leave [[Bethlehem]] to [[Flight to Egypt|flee to Egypt]] and Herod ordering the slaughter of the Holy Innocents after realising the Magi have not returned to him.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bible.oremus.org/?ql=255421442 |title=Matthew 2: 13-18 |publisher=Bible.oremus.org |date=2019-04-29 |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> Though the hymn does not explicitly mention them, it refers to them generally.<ref name=m /> The verses conclude with though a Christian can go through dark times, there will always be the brightness of God at the end.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.methodist.org.uk/our-faith/worship/singing-the-faith-plus/posts/more-to-say-about-healing/ |title=More to say about healing |publisher=The Methodist Church |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref>

== Usage ==
The hymn has also been used to commemorate final services in closing churches.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.expressandstar.com/news/local-hubs/wolverhampton/2019/09/30/end-of-an-era-as-landmark-wolverhampton-church-holds-final-service/ |title=End of an era as landmark Wolverhampton church holds final service |publisher=Express and Star |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref>
