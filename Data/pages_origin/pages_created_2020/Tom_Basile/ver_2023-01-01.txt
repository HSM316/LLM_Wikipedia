'''Thomas J. Basile''' (born October 22, 1975<ref>{{cite news |date=October 22, 2016 |title=TRUMP begins to ponder LOSING |url=https://www.politico.com/tipsheets/playbook/2016/10/trump-begins-to-ponder-losing-5-states-3-events-today-ash-carter-drops-into-baghdad-house-dems-we-wont-win-the-majority-weekend-reads-bdays-chris-licht-and-stephanie-cutter-216997 |work=Politico }}</ref>) is an American businessman, political commentator, and [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] politician in [[New York (state)|New York]]. He served in the [[Presidency of George W. Bush|George W. Bush administration]] in various capacities, including in [[Iraq]], and was the executive director of the [[New York Republican State Committee|New York Republican Party]] from 2009–2011.

Basile was a councilman in [[Stony Point, New York]], and currently runs the strategic communications firm Empire Solutions. He is the author of the 2017 book ''Tough Sell: Fighting The Media War In Iraq'', writes for [[Fox News|FoxNews.com]] and ''[[Newsmax]]'', and hosts the [[Newsmax TV]] program "America Right Now".

==Early life and education==
Basile grew up in [[Clarkstown, New York]], in [[Rockland County, New York|Rockland County]].<ref name="dailyvoice">{{cite news |date=June 23, 2017 |title=Stony Point Resident Defends Iraqi Occupation In New Book |url=https://dailyvoice.com/new-york/northrockland/news/stony-point-resident-defends-iraqi-occupation-in-new-book/714094/ |work=Daily Voice }}</ref> He attended [[Clarkstown High School South]].<ref>{{cite news |date=May 10, 1993 |title=Student of Life Enjoys Its Diversity |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41135001/the_journal_news/ |work=Rockland Journal-News }}</ref>

Basile graduated from [[Hofstra University]] in 1997, with a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] in political science and history.<ref name="hofstra">{{cite web |url=https://www.hofstra.edu/alumni/aotm/july-2019-basile.html |title=July 2019 Alumna of the Month: Tom Basile |publisher=Hofstra University }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://news.hofstra.edu/2007/12/14/meet-the-2007-alumni-award-winners/ |title=Meet the 2007 Alumni Award Winners |date=December 14, 2007 |work=Hofstra Magazine }}</ref> He then earned a [[Juris Doctor|JD]] from the [[Fordham University School of Law]].<ref name="wh-archive">{{cite web |url=https://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/government/basile-bio.html |title=Thomas J. Basile |publisher=George W. Bush White House Archive }}</ref>

==Career==
In 1997, after graduating from Hofstra, Basile ran as a Republican for Clarkstown Town Supervisor.<ref>{{cite news |date=May 24, 1997 |title=GOP backs 22-year-old in Clarkstown supervisor race |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41184993/ |work=Rockland Journal-News }}</ref> The incumbent Democrat went on to win.<ref>{{cite news |date=November 5, 1997 |title=Incumbents Almost Make Clean Sweep in Clarkstown |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41184876/ |work=Rockland Journal-News }}</ref> From 1997–2000, Basile served in Governor [[George Pataki]]'s administration as Public Relations Director for the [[New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation|New York State Office of Parks Recreation and Historic Preservation]].<ref name="wh-archive"/><ref>{{cite news |date=October 9, 2013 |title=Meet Tom Basile: He's Been Around the World, but Home is where he Hopes to Serve |url=https://www.rocklandtimes.com/2013/10/09/meet-tom-basile-hes-been-around-the-world-but-home-is-where-he-hopes-to-serve/ |work=Rockland County Times }}</ref>

Basile worked on the [[George W. Bush 2000 presidential campaign|2000 Bush–Cheney presidential campaign]] and was a consultant to the [[Republican National Committee]] during the [[2004 United States presidential election|2004 Presidential election]].<ref name="observer">{{cite news |date=October 15, 2009 |title=Basile New GOP Executive Director |url=https://observer.com/2009/10/basile-new-gop-executive-director/ |work=Observer }}</ref>

In the [[Presidency of George W. Bush|George W. Bush administration]], Basile served as Director of Communications for the [[United States Environmental Protection Agency|U.S. Environmental Protection Agency]] (EPA) under [[Christine Todd Whitman]]. From 2003–2004, Basile served in Iraq as senior advisor to the [[Coalition Provisional Authority]] (CPA) under [[Paul Bremer]]. The U.S. Department of Defense subsequently awarded him the Joint Civilian Service Commendation for his service with the CPA. Basile was also the press secretary for the [[second inauguration of George W. Bush]] in January 2005.<ref name="observer"/>

From 2009–2011, Basile was executive director of the [[New York Republican State Committee|New York Republican Party]].<ref name="observer"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nystateofpolitics.com/2011/06/basile-resigns-state-gop-post/ |title=Basile Resigns State GOP Post |date=June 24, 2011 |publisher=State of Politics }}</ref> With David Webb, Basile co-founded TeaParty365, the New York City metropolitan area chapter of the [[Tea Party movement]].<ref>{{cite news |date=December 5, 2011 |title=Tea Party group's co-founder calls Cuomo's tax plan 'smoke and mirrors' |url=https://www.wnyc.org/story/195509-tea-party-group-calls-cuomos-tax-plan-smoke-and-mirrors/ |publisher=WNYC }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=November 21, 2011 |title=OWS Getting Under Tea Party's Skin |url=https://abcnews.go.com/Business/tea-party-response-occupy-wall-st/story?id=14985439 |publisher=ABC }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |date=May 9, 2010 |title=NYC Tea-off |url=https://www.crainsnewyork.com/article/20100509/FREE/305099970/nyc-tea-off |work=Crain's New York }}</ref>

Basile has served as a councilman in [[Stony Point, New York]], since January 2014. He also serves as the Deputy Town Supervisor.<ref>{{cite news |date=May 5, 2018 |title=Larkin endorses Basile as his successor for 39th Senate District |url=https://www.recordonline.com/news/20180505/larkin-endorses-basile-as-his-successor-for-39th-senate-district |work=Times-Herald Record }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.townofstonypoint.org/officials/tom-basile |title=Tom Basile |publisher=Town of Stony Point |access-date=December 27, 2019 }}</ref> Basile and Webb were featured in the 2011 [[Time Person of the Year|''Time'' Person of the Year]] report, "The Protester", as leaders in the Tea Party Movement.<ref>{{cite magazine |date=December 26, 2011 |title=2011 Person of the Year: The Protester |magazine=TIME }}</ref>

In 2018, Basile unsuccessfully ran for a seat in the [[New York State Senate]] to represent the [[New York's 39th State Senate district|39th district]],<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.recordonline.com/news/20180320/rockland-man-waging-only-if-larkin-retires-election-campaign |title=Rockland man waging only-if-Larkin-retires election campaign |date=March 20, 2018 |work=Times Herald-Record }}</ref> defeated by Democrat [[James Skoufis]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.politico.com/states/new-york/albany/story/2018/10/11/four-senate-seats-emerging-as-the-key-battlegrounds-in-november-643387 |title=4 Senate seats emerging as the key battlegrounds in November |date=October 11, 2018 |work=Politico }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.dailyfreeman.com/news/elections/th-ny-senate-district-democratic-assemblyman-skoufis-beats-republican-basile/article_4791fe3c-e1e3-11e8-a188-3727463724d0.html |title=39th NY Senate District: Democratic Assemblyman Skoufis beats Republican Basile to take Larkin's seat |date=November 7, 2018 |work=Daily Freeman }}</ref>

Basile heads the strategic communications firm Empire Solutions, which he founded in 2005 after leaving government. He is the former host of ''Sunday in America'' on [[Sirius XM]] radio, where he continues to contribute. Basile is also an adjunct professor at [[Fordham University]]'s Graduate School of Arts and Sciences.<ref name="hofstra"/>

==Publications and writings==
Basile wrote the 2017 book ''Tough Sell: Fighting The Media War In Iraq'', published by Potomac Books.<ref>{{cite news |date=March 20, 2017 |title=Tough Sell by Tom Basile |url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/tom-basile/tough-sell/ |work=Kirkus Reviews }}</ref> The book's [[foreword]] is by Ambassador [[John Bolton]].<ref>{{cite news |date=May 4, 2017 |title=Basile Pens Book About Iraq War |url=https://www.rocklandtimes.com/2017/05/04/basile-pens-book-about-iraq-war/ |work=Rockland County Times }}</ref> In the book, he relates his firsthand experiences in [[Baghdad]] during the Iraq War and criticizes the media for biased coverage. While he defends the Bush administration's decision to launch the war, he is critical of the communications strategy to defend the policy.<ref name="dailyvoice"/> Reviewing the book in the [[United States Army War College|U.S. Army War College]] journal ''[[Parameters (journal)|Parameters]]'', James P. Farwell wrote, "Basile merits high credit for his patriotic service and his thought-provoking book that provides keen insights into what it takes to make strategic communication in war zones a success and into the obstacles to good strategic communication. ''Tough Sell'' is highly recommended.<ref>{{cite journal |date=Autumn 2017 |title=Irregular Warfare |url=https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/473e/bbaaf53d5f4a0e9da7695fe551f2550549a7.pdf |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191231201741/https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/473e/bbaaf53d5f4a0e9da7695fe551f2550549a7.pdf |url-status=dead |archive-date=2019-12-31 |journal=Parameters |volume=47 |issue=3 |pages=111–112 |s2cid=159283445 }}</ref> 

==Personal life==
Basile resides in Stony Point, New York, with his wife and their three children.<ref>{{cite news |date=May 11, 2018 |title=Stony Point Councilman Tom Basile launches state senate campaign |url=http://www.chroniclenewspaper.com/news/local-news/stony-point-councilman-tom-basile-launches-state-senate-campaign-BRCN20180512180519986 |work=The Chronicle }}</ref> He is a Knight Commander in the [[Catholic Church|Roman Catholic]] [[Order of the Holy Sepulchre]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cny.org/stories/new-knights-ladies-told-to-becomepart-of-the-holy-sepulchre-family,16330? |title=New Knights, Ladies Told to Become Part of the Holy Sepulchre 'Family' |date=October 25, 2017 |publisher=Catholic New York }}</ref>
