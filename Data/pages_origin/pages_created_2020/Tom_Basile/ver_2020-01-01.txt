'''Thomas J. Basile''' (born October 22, 1975<ref>{{cite news |last= |first= |date=October 22, 2016 |title=TRUMP begins to ponder LOSING |url=https://www.politico.com/tipsheets/playbook/2016/10/trump-begins-to-ponder-losing-5-states-3-events-today-ash-carter-drops-into-baghdad-house-dems-we-wont-win-the-majority-weekend-reads-bdays-chris-licht-and-stephanie-cutter-216997 |work=Politico |location= |access-date= }}</ref>) is an American businessman, writer, and [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] politician in [[New York (state)|New York]].

==Early life and education==
Basile grew up in [[Clarkstown, New York]] in [[Rockland County, New York|Rockland County]].<ref name="dailyvoice">{{cite news |last= |first= |date=June 23, 2017 |title=Stony Point Resident Defends Iraqi Occupation In New Book |url=https://dailyvoice.com/new-york/northrockland/news/stony-point-resident-defends-iraqi-occupation-in-new-book/714094/ |work=Daily Voice |location= |access-date= }}</ref> He attended [[Clarkstown High School South]].<ref>{{cite news |last= |first= |date=May 10, 1993 |title=Student of Life Enjoys Its Diversity |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41135001/the_journal_news/ |work=Rockland Journal-News |location= |access-date= }}</ref>

Basile graduated from [[Hofstra University]] in 1997, with a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] in political science and history.<ref name="hofstra">{{cite web |url=https://www.hofstra.edu/alumni/aotm/july-2019-basile.html |title=July 2019 Alumna of the Month: Tom Basile |last= |first= |date= |website= |publisher=Hofstra University |access-date= |quote=}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://news.hofstra.edu/2007/12/14/meet-the-2007-alumni-award-winners/ |title=Meet the 2007 Alumni Award Winners |last= |first= |date=December 14, 2007 |work=Hofstra Magazine |access-date= |quote=}}</ref> He then earned a [[Juris Doctor|JD]] from the [[Fordham University School of Law]].<ref name="wh-archive">{{cite web |url=https://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/government/basile-bio.html |title=Thomas J. Basile |last= |first= |date= |website= |publisher=George W. Bush White House Archive |access-date= |quote=}}</ref>

==Career==
Basile worked on the [[George W. Bush 2000 presidential campaign|2000 Bush–Cheney presidential campaign]] and was a consultant to the [[Republican National Committee]] during the [[2004 United States presidential election|2004 Presidential election]].<ref name="observer">{{cite news |last= |first= |date=October 15, 2009 |title=Basile New GOP Executive Director |url=https://observer.com/2009/10/basile-new-gop-executive-director/ |work=Observer |location= |access-date= }}</ref>

In the [[Presidency of George W. Bush|George W. Bush administration]], Basile served as Director of Communications for the [[United States Environmental Protection Agency|U.S. Environmental Protection Agency]] (EPA) under [[Christine Todd Whitman]]. From 2003–2004, Basile served in Iraq as senior advisor to the [[Coalition Provisional Authority]] (CPA) under [[Paul Bremer]]. The U.S. Department of Defense subsequently awarded him the Joint Civilian Service Commendation Medal for his service with the CPA. Basile was also the press secretary for the [[second inauguration of George W. Bush]] in January 2005.<ref name="observer"/> 

From 2009–2011, Basile was Executive Director of the [[New York Republican State Committee|New York Republican Party]].<ref name="observer"/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nystateofpolitics.com/2011/06/basile-resigns-state-gop-post/ |title=Basile Resigns State GOP Post |last= |first= |date=June 24, 2011 |website= |publisher=State of Politics |access-date= |quote=}}</ref>

Basile has served as a councilman in Stony Point, New York since January 2014. He also serves as the Deputy Town Supervisor.<ref>{{cite news |last= |first= |date=May 5, 2018 |title=Larkin endorses Basile as his successor for 39th Senate District |url=https://www.recordonline.com/news/20180505/larkin-endorses-basile-as-his-successor-for-39th-senate-district |work=Times-Herald Record |location= |access-date= }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.townofstonypoint.org/officials/tom-basile |title=Tom Basile |last= |first= |date= |website= |publisher=Town of Stony Point |access-date=December 27, 2019 |quote=}}</ref>

In 2018, Basile unsuccessfully ran for a seat in the [[New York State Senate]] to represent the [[New York's 39th State Senate district|39th district]],<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.recordonline.com/news/20180320/rockland-man-waging-only-if-larkin-retires-election-campaign |title=Rockland man waging only-if-Larkin-retires election campaign |last= |first= |date=March 20, 2018 |work=Times Herald-Record |access-date= |quote=}}</ref> defeated by Democrat [[James Skoufis]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.politico.com/states/new-york/albany/story/2018/10/11/four-senate-seats-emerging-as-the-key-battlegrounds-in-november-643387 |title=4 Senate seats emerging as the key battlegrounds in November |last= |first= |date=October 11, 2018 |work=Politico |access-date= |quote=}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.dailyfreeman.com/news/elections/th-ny-senate-district-democratic-assemblyman-skoufis-beats-republican-basile/article_4791fe3c-e1e3-11e8-a188-3727463724d0.html |title=39th NY Senate District: Democratic Assemblyman Skoufis beats Republican Basile to take Larkin's seat |last= |first= |date=November 7, 2018 |work=Daily Freeman |access-date= |quote=}}</ref>

==Publications==
Basile wrote the 2017 book ''Tough Sell: Fighting The Media War In Iraq'', published by Potomac Books.<ref name="dailyvoice"/><ref>{{cite news |last= |first= |date=March 20, 2017 |title=Tough Sell by Tom Basile |url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/tom-basile/tough-sell/ |work=Kirkus Reviews |location= |access-date= }}</ref> The book's [[foreword]] is by Ambassador [[John Bolton]].<ref>{{cite news |last= |first= |date=May 4, 2017 |title=Basile Pens Book About Iraq War |url=https://www.rocklandtimes.com/2017/05/04/basile-pens-book-about-iraq-war/ |work=Rockland County Times |location= |access-date= }}</ref> In the book, he relates his firsthand experiences in [[Baghdad]] during the Iraq War and defends the Bush administration's decision to launch the war.<ref>{{cite journal |last1= |first1= |last2= |first2= |date=Autumn 2017 |title=Irregular Warfare |url=https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/473e/bbaaf53d5f4a0e9da7695fe551f2550549a7.pdf |journal=Parameters |volume=47 |issue=3 |pages=111–112 |doi= |access-date= }}</ref>
