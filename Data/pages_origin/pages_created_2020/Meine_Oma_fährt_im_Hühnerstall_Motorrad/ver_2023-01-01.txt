'''''Meine Oma fährt im Hühnerstall Motorrad''''' ("My Grandma Drives Her Motorcycle In The Chicken Coop"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.euronews.com/2019/12/31/german-kids-song-parody-turns-into-death-threats-and-omagate|title='Environmental pig': Song parody sparks controversy in Germany|last=Bauomy|first=Jasmin|date=2019-12-31|website=euronews|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>) is a German humouristic song. It dates back to the 1930s, probably stemming from two older works: the refrain of the Rheinländer dance ''[[Wir versaufen unsrer Oma ihr klein Häuschen]]'', authored by [[Robert Steidl]] in 1922, constitutes most of the melody, while the text is a variation of the [[Foxtrott]] ''Meine Oma fährt Motorrad, ohne Bremse, ohne Licht'' ("my grand-mother rides a motorcycle without brakes nor lighting"), a 1928 work with words by [[Ernst Albert]] and music by [[Erwin Bolt]].

== Origins and variants ==
The [[Deutsches Volksliedarchiv]] (German archives of popular songs) have conducted extensive research into the origin of the song. They describe it as "an instance of absurd humour, as well as a typical example of the songs that developed in parallel to the media musical culture of the 20th century, constantly mutating under their own dynamic." {{refn|Es ist ein Spiegelbild des spielerischen Vergnügens am Absurden und zugleich ein typisches und virulentes Beispiel für jenes Liedgut, das sich parallel zur medial geprägten Musikkultur des 20. Jahrhunderts entwickelt und mit einer gewissen Eigendynamik beständig verändert.<ref name="HKL">{{cite web|url=http://www.liederlexikon.de/lieder/meine_oma_faehrt_im_huehnerstall_motorradd|title=Meine Oma fährt im Hühnerstall Motorrad|author=Eckhard John, Renate Sarr|year=2008}}</ref>|group=Note}} When exactly the two works mixed is not known. The earliest known instance is an incipit in a magazine containing the words of the song in 1935 or 1936.<ref>[[Gerd Eckert]]: ''Schlagertexte wenig verändert.'' In ''[[Der Türmer]]: Deutsche Monatshefte. Die Bergstadt'', Band 38 (1935–36), Teil 2, {{ZDB|501058-5}}, S. 87–89, hier S. 88 ({{Google books|bSEvAAAAMAAJ||page=88}}).</ref> The earliest document held by the Deutschen Volksliedarchiv dates from 1942.<ref name="HKL" />

=== 1958 version ===
In 1958, ''Meine Oma fährt im Hühnerstall Motorrad'' was printed for the first time in a songbook, ''Der Zündschlüssel'', by the Fidula-Verlag printing company, from the recollection of editor Johannes Holzmeister.<ref>[http://www.liederlexikon.de/lieder/meine_oma_faehrt_im_huehnerstall_motorrad/editionb Version von 1958], in ''Der Zündschlüssel''</ref>

=== 1980 version ===
In 1980, [[Fredrik Vahle]] published a 25-stanza version in ''Liederspatz'', where the grandma sits behind the wheel, watches the TV series [[Tagesschau (German TV series)|''Tagesschau'']], and goes to the disco.<ref>[http://www.liederlexikon.de/lieder/meine_oma_faehrt_im_huehnerstall_motorrad/editionc 1980 version] in ''Liederspatz''</ref>

== 2019 WDR parody ==
In 2019, [[Westdeutscher Rundfunk Köln]] (WDR) had a new version sung by the children choir of the Choral Academy of Dortmund, authored by one of the hosts of WDR-2 as a [[caricature]] of intergenerational tensions.<ref>Anna Ernst:[https://www.sueddeutsche.de/medien/wdr-kinderchor-leiter-drohung-1.4739887 ''"Dieses Ausmaß habe ich im Ansatz nicht erwartet"''], sueddeutsche.de 29 December 2019.</ref><ref name="statement">{{cite web|url=https://www.wdr-kinderchor.de/de/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170925081748/http://www.wdr-kinderchor.de/de/|url-status=dead|archive-date=2017-09-25| title=Zeljo Davutovic, Chorleitung WDR Kinderchor Dortmund|access-date=2019-12-29}}</ref> The new version appeared on 27 December 2019 on an online video. The file was removed shortly thereafter, following criticism of the refrain that states "My grand-mother is an old environmental pig" (in original German: ''Meine Oma ist 'ne alte Umweltsau'').<ref name="welt">{{cite news |url=https://www.welt.de/vermischtes/article204618322/Oma-ist-ne-alte-Umweltsau-WDR-Kinderchor-sorgt-fuer-Empoerung.html |title=Und dann ruft Tom Buhrow in der Sondersendung an |work=[[welt.de]] |date=2019-12-28 |access-date=2019-12-28}}</ref> However, the video remains available from YouTube.<ref>{{youTube|er4M785oMzo|WDR 2 Comedy Kinderchor: "Meine Oma ist ne alte Umweltsau" in HD 720p}}</ref> The next day, in a special broadcast on WDR, [[Tom Buhrow]] and the head of programmes [[Jochen Rausch]] apologised without reserve for the video, which he called a "mistake".<ref name="welt"/> The choir director,  [[Zeljo Davutovic]], issued a statement praising the youth movement [[Fridays for Future]], commenting "this is not about grand-mothers, this is about all of us".<ref name="statement"/>

On 29 December, Right-Wing groups started demonstrating in front of the WDR headquarters in protest of the song. Counter-protests by Left-Wing groups soon followed, as well as a police presence. In total, around 100 people were involved.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fr.de/kultur/wdr-kinderchor-lied-klimaschutz-oma-umweltsau-fuehrt-aerger-13372729.html |title="Umweltsau"-Satire: Chorleiter wehrt sich - "Neonazis" pöbeln vorm WDR |date=2019-12-29 |access-date=2019-12-30 |language=de}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.deutschlandfunk.de/streit-um-umweltsau-video-demonstration-vor-dem-wdr-in-koeln.1939.de.html?drn:news_id=1085323 |title=Streit um "Umweltsau"-Video - Demonstration vor dem WDR in Köln |access-date=2019-12-29 |language=}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.derstandard.de/story/2000112758451/warum-ein-kinderlied-ueber-oma-umweltsau-fuer-aufregung-sorgt |title=Warum ein Kinderlied über "Oma Umweltsau" für Aufregung sorgt|newspaper=Der Standard |date=2019-12-29|access-date=2019-12-30 |language=de-AT}}</ref>
