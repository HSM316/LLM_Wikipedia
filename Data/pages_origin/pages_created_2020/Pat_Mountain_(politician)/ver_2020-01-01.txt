'''Patricia Ann Mountain'''<ref name=mep>{{cite web|url=https://www.lewes-eastbourne.gov.uk/_resources/assets/inline/full/0/280158.pdf | title=Statement of Parties and Individual Candidates Nominated - Notice of Poll - 23 May 2019 | accessdate=27 April 2019}}</ref> is a British politician currently serving as the acting leader of the [[UK Independence Party]].
==Career==
Mountain was one of ten UKIP candidates who stood for the [[South East England (European Parliament constituency)|South East England]] constituency in the [[2019 European Parliament election in the United Kingdom]]; none of them were elected.<ref name=mep/> Mountain has also stood for [[Brighton and Hove City Council]] thrice: as the sole UKIP candidate in the 11 July 2013 [[by-election]] at the [[Hanover and Elm Grove]] ward, in [[2015 Brighton and Hove City Council election|2015]] at the [[Hangleton and Knoll]] ward, and in [[2019 Brighton and Hove City Council election|2019]] for the [[North Portslade]] ward; she did not win in any of them.<ref>https://present.brighton-hove.gov.uk/mgElectionAreaResults.aspx?ID=113&RPID=0</ref><ref>https://present.brighton-hove.gov.uk/mgElectionAreaResults.aspx?ID=121</ref><ref name=bh/><ref>https://present.brighton-hove.gov.uk/mgElectionAreaResults.aspx?ID=154</ref>

Mountain had retired by April 2019,<ref name=bh>https://www.brightonandhovenews.org/2019/04/26/north-portslade-candidate-spotlight/</ref> and was a member of the National Executive Committee when she was appointed interim leader on 16 November 2019<ref>[https://twitter.com/UKIP/status/1195813693965111297 UKIP announces new interim leader] Retrieved 20 November 2019</ref> to succeed [[Richard Braine]].<ref name=es>https://www.standard.co.uk/news/politics/ukips-new-interim-leader-patricia-mountain-bungles-her-way-through-car-crash-interview-as-party-a4302456.html</ref> Mountain was the acting leader of UKIP during the [[2019 United Kingdom general election]].<ref>https://www.bbc.com/news/election-2019-50628082</ref>

Mountain's 2 December 2019 interview with [[Sky News]] journalist [[Adam Boulton]], which lasted for eight minutes,<ref name=es/> was described by ''[[The Independent]]'' as "the outstanding TV interview of the year" and by the ''[[Evening Standard]]'' as a "car crash",<ref name=es/>,<ref>https://www.independent.co.uk/voices/politics-2019-summary-funny-brexit-interviews-government-a9263441.html</ref> and there were reports that she was mistaken for the titular character of ''[[Catherine Tate's Nan]]''.<ref name=es/><ref>https://www.indy100.com/article/ukip-leader-pat-mountain-interview-sky-catherine-tate-character-9230511</ref>

Her term as acting leader is set to expire on 31 January 2020.<ref>https://www.indy100.com/article/ukip-interview-pat-mountain-racist-tommy-robinson-general-election-9229116</ref>
