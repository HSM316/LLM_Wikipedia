'''Patricia Ann Mountain'''<ref name=mep>{{cite web|url=https://www.lewes-eastbourne.gov.uk/_resources/assets/inline/full/0/280158.pdf | title=Statement of Parties and Individual Candidates Nominated - Notice of Poll - 23 May 2019 | accessdate=27 April 2019}}</ref> is a British politician who served as the Interim Leader of the [[UK Independence Party]] (UKIP) from 2019 to 2020. Mountain also served as the Deputy Leader of UKIP from July to September 2020.

==Career==
Mountain was one of ten UKIP candidates who stood for the [[South East England (European Parliament constituency)|South East England]] constituency in the [[2019 European Parliament election in the United Kingdom]], which also included [[Piers Wauchope]], another interim leader; none of them was elected.<ref name=mep/> Mountain has also stood for [[Brighton and Hove City Council]] thrice: as the sole UKIP candidate in the 11 July 2013 [[by-election]] at the [[Hanover and Elm Grove]] ward, in [[2015 Brighton and Hove City Council election|2015]] at the [[Hangleton and Knoll]] ward, and in [[2019 Brighton and Hove City Council election|2019]] for the [[North Portslade]] ward; she did not win in any of them.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://present.brighton-hove.gov.uk/mgElectionAreaResults.aspx?ID=113&RPID=0|title=Election results for Hanover & Elm Grove, 11 July 2013|date=11 July 2013|website=present.brighton-hove.gov.uk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://present.brighton-hove.gov.uk/mgElectionAreaResults.aspx?ID=121|title=Election results for Hangleton & Knoll, 7 May 2015|date=7 May 2015|website=present.brighton-hove.gov.uk}}</ref><ref name=bh/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://present.brighton-hove.gov.uk/mgElectionAreaResults.aspx?ID=154|title=Election results for North Portslade, 2 May 2019|date=2 May 2019|website=present.brighton-hove.gov.uk}}</ref>

Mountain had retired by April 2019,<ref name=bh>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brightonandhovenews.org/2019/04/26/north-portslade-candidate-spotlight/|title=North Portslade candidate spotlight|date=26 April 2019}}</ref> and was a member of the National Executive Committee when she was appointed interim leader on 16 November 2019<ref>[https://twitter.com/UKIP/status/1195813693965111297 UKIP announces new interim leader] Retrieved 20 November 2019</ref> to succeed [[Richard Braine (politician)|Richard Braine]].<ref name=es>{{Cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/news/politics/ukips-new-interim-leader-patricia-mountain-bungles-her-way-through-car-crash-interview-as-party-a4302456.html|title=Ukip's new interim leader bungles her interview on manifesto day|date=2 December 2019|website=Evening Standard}}</ref> Mountain was the acting leader of UKIP during the [[2019 United Kingdom general election]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/election-2019-50628082|title=UKIP launches 'Brexit and beyond' manifesto|work=BBC News|date=2 December 2019|via=www.bbc.com}}</ref> where none of the party's forty-four candidates was elected to the House of Commons.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://candidates.democracyclub.org.uk/elections/parl.2019-12-12/|title=Known candidates for each ballot in the UK Parliament elections|publisher=Democracy Club Candidates|accessdate=1 December 2019}}</ref>

On 2 December 2019, Mountain appeared on [[Sky News]] for an interview with journalist [[Adam Boulton]]; it lasted for eight minutes,<ref name=es/> and was described by the ''[[Evening Standard]]'' as a "car crash"<ref name=es/> and by ''[[The Independent]]'' as "the outstanding TV interview of the year... It wasn’t so much of a car crash as a spectacular, [[Evel Knievel]]-style nose-dive into a canyon".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/voices/politics-2019-summary-funny-brexit-interviews-government-a9263441.html|title=Reclining in parliament, melting ice sculptures and that staring contest – the wildest political moments of 2019|date=30 December 2019|website=The Independent}}</ref>

Her term as acting leader was due to expire on 31 January 2020. However, due to the fact that no leadership election had taken place by then, she continued in the role until April.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.indy100.com/article/ukip-interview-pat-mountain-racist-tommy-robinson-general-election-9229116|title=Ukip leader may have just given the worst interview of the general election|date=2 December 2019|website=indy100}}</ref> It was later confirmed in June 2020 that [[Freddy Vachha]] was the new leader of UKIP.

Mountain was appointed as Deputy Leader of UKIP in July 2020, but had left the role by September 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=UKIP|title=UK Independence Party (UKIP)|url=http://www.ukip.org/|access-date=2020-07-04|website=UK Indendence Party (UKIP)|language=en}}</ref>

Mountain was a UKIP candidate for the [[South Scotland]] regional list seat in the [[Scottish Parliament]], during the [[2021 Scottish Parliament election]]; she was unsuccessful in getting elected.
