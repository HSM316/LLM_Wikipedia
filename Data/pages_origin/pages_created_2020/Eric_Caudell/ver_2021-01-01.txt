'''Eric Caudell''' (born January 2, 1967) is an American professional [[stock car racing]] driver. He currently competes part-time in the [[ARCA Menards Series]], driving the No. 7 [[Ford Motor Company|Ford]] for his own team, CCM Racing, as well as the No. 69 [[Toyota]] for Kimmel Racing. He has competed in ARCA for the past five years, including running full-time for one season in [[2017 ARCA Racing Series|2017]], where he finished tenth in points. He also made one Truck Series start in [[2014 NASCAR Camping World Truck Series|2014]] at [[Iowa Speedway|Iowa]].

==Racing career==
[[File:2 Eric Caudell 2017 Road America 100 ARCA.jpg|thumb|left|Caudell's No. 2 car in [[2017 ARCA Racing Series|2017]] for Hixson Motorsports at [[Road America]].]]
[[File:2EricCaudell2019ShoreLunch200.jpg|thumb|left|Caudell's No. 2 car in [[2019 ARCA Menards Series|2019]] for CCM Racing at [[Madison International Speedway|Madison]].]]
Caudell had raced for many years in the United States Super Truck Series until the series shut down.<ref name=Caudell>{{cite web |first=Don |last=Radebaugh |url=https://www.arcaracing.com/articles/2008784-energy-trading-software-consultant-caudell-looking-for-room-for-racing |title=Energy trading software consultant Caudell looking for space to race |website=ARCARacing.com |date=December 19, 2017 |accessdate=December 31, 2019}}</ref>

Caudell made his Truck Series debut in [[2014 NASCAR Camping World Truck Series|2014]], driving the No. 12 for Cefalia Motorsports. He finished 28th.

In [[2017 ARCA Racing Series|2017]], Caudell competed in a full season in the [[ARCA Racing Series]] in the No. 2 for Hixson Motorsports, a team he previously drove part-time for in the series in [[2016 ARCA Racing Series|2016]]. Also, his crew chief that year was Crystal Bates,<ref>{{cite web |first=Don |last=Radebaugh |url=https://www.arcaracing.com/articles/2006010-crew-chief-crystal-ready-with-two-car-team-for-salem |title=Crew chief Crystal Bates ready for Salem |website=ARCARacing.com |date=April 23, 2017 |accessdate=January 20, 2020}}</ref> one of the few female crew chiefs in stock car racing. He ended up finishing tenth in points.

For [[2020 ARCA Menards Series|2020]], it was announced that Caudell and his No. 7 team would be returning to ARCA in ten races: Daytona, Talladega, Chicagoland, Lucas Oil, Elko, Iowa, Springfield, DuQuoin, Bristol, Memphis, and Kansas.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thepitlane.org/ |title=Eric Caudell planning on a partial schedule; looking at two cars |website=ThePitLane.org |date=January 18, 2020 |accessdate=January 20, 2020}}</ref> In addition, Caudell and his team participated in ARCA's Daytona testing in January 2020 leading up to the race there the following month. Caudell and his former Hixson Motorsports teammate Rick Tackman shared his No. 7 car during the test session.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.arcaracing.com/events/1995335-dayton-test#tab_entry_list |title=Daytona Test Entry List |website=ARCARacing.com |accessdate=January 20, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Caudell graduated from the [[University of Oklahoma]], earning a degree in Management Information Systems.<ref name=Caudell>{{cite web |first=Don |last=Radebaugh |url=https://www.arcaracing.com/articles/2008784-energy-trading-software-consultant-caudell-looking-for-room-for-racing |title=Energy trading software consultant Caudell looking for space to race |website=ARCARacing.com |date=December 19, 2017 |accessdate=December 31, 2019}}</ref>

He lives in [[Piedmont, Oklahoma]] and in addition to racing and has worked as an energy trading software consultant for Murphy USA for over twenty years.<ref name=Caudell>{{cite web |first=Don |last=Radebaugh |url=https://www.arcaracing.com/articles/2008784-energy-trading-software-consultant-caudell-looking-for-room-for-racing |title=Energy trading software consultant Caudell looking for space to race |website=ARCARacing.com |date=December 19, 2017 |accessdate=December 31, 2019}}</ref>
