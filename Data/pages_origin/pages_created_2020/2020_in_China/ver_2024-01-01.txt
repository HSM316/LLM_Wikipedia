==Events==
===January===
*January 20 – Chinese Premier [[Li Keqiang]] has urged efforts to prevent and control [[Coronavirus disease 2019]].<ref name="Premier urged2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202001/21/WS5e26556ca31012821727269c.html|title=Chinese premier stresses curbing viral pneumonia epidemic|date=January 21, 2020|website=[[China Daily]]|publisher=[[Xinhua News Agency]]|location=Beijing|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200122124640/https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202001/21/WS5e26556ca31012821727269c.html|archive-date=January 22, 2020|access-date=January 22, 2020}}</ref>
*January 26 – Leading group on the prevention and control of [[Coronavirus disease 2019]] was established, led by Li Keqiang.<ref name="Premier leading">{{Cite web|url=http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2020-01/26/c_138735589.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200126221455/http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2020-01/26/c_138735589.htm|url-status=dead|archive-date=January 26, 2020|title=China to extend Spring Festival holiday to contain COVID-19 pandemic|date=January 26, 2020|website=[[Xinhua News Agency]]|location=Beijing}}</ref> The leading group has decided to extend [[Chinese New Year|Spring Festival]] holiday to contain the COVID-19 pandemic.
*January 27 – Li Keqiang visited [[Wuhan]], center of what became the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], to direct the epidemic prevention work.<ref name="Premier visiting">{{Cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-china-health-premier/chinese-premier-li-visits-wuhan-epicenter-of-virus-outbreak-idUSKBN1ZQ08G|title=Chinese Premier Li visits Wuhan, epicenter of virus outbreak|date=January 27, 2020|website=[[Reuters]]|location=Beijing}}</ref>
*January 28 – The opening of [[Dabie Mountain Regional Medical Centre]] in [[Huangzhou District]], [[Huanggang]], Hubei.
*January 30 – At the [[World Health Organization]]'s second meeting of the Emergency Committee declared the Wuhan outbreak of novel coronavirus a [[Public Health Emergency of International Concern]] (PHEIC).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.who.int/news-room/detail/30-01-2020-statement-on-the-second-meeting-of-the-international-health-regulations-(2005)-emergency-committee-regarding-the-outbreak-of-novel-coronavirus-(2019-ncov)|title=Statement on the second meeting of the International Health Regulations (2005) Emergency Committee regarding the outbreak of novel coronavirus (2019-nCoV)|website=www.who.int|language=en|access-date=January 31, 2020}}</ref>

=== February ===
*February 3 – [[Huoshenshan Hospital]] accepted patients after a speedy construction build period. 
*February 6 – [[Leishenshan Hospital]] passed evaluations and will begin accepting patients soon.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202002/06/WS5e3c15c3a31012821727591f.html|title=2nd new hospital for Wuhan virus patients passes evaluation – Chinadaily.com.cn|last=刘明|website=www.chinadaily.com.cn|access-date=February 7, 2020}}</ref>
*February 10 – An advance [[World Health Organization]] team of medical experts has arrived in China, led by Dr. [[Bruce Aylward]], who coordinated the WHO's [[Ebola virus disease|Ebola]] Response in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jpost.com/Breaking-News/World-Health-Organization-experts-arrive-in-China-for-coronavirus-probe-617118|title=World Health Organization experts arrive in China for coronavirus probe|website=The Jerusalem Post {{!}} JPost.com|access-date=February 11, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.who.int/emergencies/diseases/novel-coronavirus-2019/media-resources/press-briefings|title=Press briefings|website=www.who.int|language=en|access-date=February 11, 2020}}</ref> 
*February 28 – Three time Chinese [[Summer Olympic Games|Olympic]] swimming champion, [[Sun Yang]], has been given an 8-year ban for missing a [[doping in sport|doping]] test in September 2018 by the [[Court of Arbitration for Sport]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/swimming/51670931|title=Sun Yang: Eight-year ban for Chinese Olympic champion|date=February 28, 2020|work=BBC Sport|access-date=February 28, 2020|language=en-GB}}</ref>

=== March ===
[[File:Water salute for Air China 042 from Wuhan with medical team.jpg|thumb|Beijing medical team aiding Hubei returned to Beijing on March 31, 2020, with their flight receiving [[water salute]] at [[Beijing Capital International Airport]]]]
*March 7 – [[Collapse of Xinjia Express Hotel]]
*March 10 – [[Chinese Communist Party]] [[General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party|general secretary]] [[Xi Jinping]] visits [[Wuhan]], the origin of the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], after an unprecedented [[2020 Hubei lockdowns|lockdown]] of the central city of 11 million people.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.xinhuanet.com/politics/leaders/2020-03/11/c_1125693204.htm|title=习近平总书记武汉之行传递战"疫"新信号|author=赵承|editor=顾天成|date=March 11, 2020|work=Xinhua News Agency|access-date=March 11, 2020|language=zh-cn}}</ref>
*March 24 – [[Premier of the People's Republic of China|Chinese Premier]] [[Li Keqiang]] reported that spread of the [[COVID-19 pandemic in mainland China|domestically transmitted epidemic]] has been basically blocked and the [[COVID-19 pandemic]] has been controlled in China.<ref>{{cite news |title= China deploys measures to curb imported COVID-19 cases, rebound in indigenous cases |url= http://english.scio.gov.cn/m/topnews/2020-03/24/content_75852078.htm |work=State Council Information Office of China |date=March 24, 2020}}</ref>
*March 28 – A tailings dam fails at the [[Yichun Luming mine]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://blogs.agu.org/landslideblog/2020/04/02/luming-mine-accident/|title=High resolution Planet Labs imagery of the Luming Mine accident|date=April 2, 2020|access-date=June 26, 2021}}</ref>

=== April ===
* April 4 – Coinciding with the [[Qingming Festival]], a national day of mourning was declared followed by a three-minute silence to honor 3,300+ people who died of [[COVID-19]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-china-52162004|title=China holds three-minute silence for virus victims|date=April 4, 2020|work=BBC News|access-date=April 7, 2020|language=en-GB}}</ref>

=== May ===
* May 9 – Several Indian and Chinese soldiers were injured in a cross-border clash at the [[Nathu La]] crossing. Around one hundred and fifty troops clashed in a "standoff" that included fistfights and stone-throwing.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Indian and Chinese soldiers injured in cross-border fistfight, says Delhi|url=http://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/may/11/indian-and-chinese-soldiers-injured-in-cross-border-fistfight-says-delhi|agency=Agence France-Presse|date=May 11, 2020|website=the Guardian|language=en|access-date=May 12, 2020}}</ref>

=== June ===
* June 26 – Nearly 50 independent United Nations Human Rights experts highlighted their concern on the situation in China. They voiced concern for many actions including but not limited to allegations of forced labor; arbitrary interferences with the right to privacy; restrictive cybersecurity, anti-terrorism, and sedition laws; the retaliation against journalists, medical workers and others speaking out about COVID-19; the repression of ethnic minorities in Xinjiang and Tibet; and the repression of protests and democracy advocacy in the [[Government of Hong Kong|Hong Kong Special Administrative Region]] (SAR). They urged China to "withdraw the draft [[National Security Law (Hong Kong)|national security law]] for Hong Kong".<ref>{{Cite web|title=OHCHR {{!}} UN experts call for decisive measures to protect fundamental freedoms in China|url=https://ohchr.org/EN/NewsEvents/Pages/DisplayNews.aspx?NewsID=26006&LangID=E|access-date=July 1, 2020|website=ohchr.org}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=June 26, 2020|title=Independent UN rights experts call for decisive measures to protect 'fundamental freedoms' in China|url=https://news.un.org/en/story/2020/06/1067312|access-date=July 1, 2020|website=UN News|language=en}}</ref>
