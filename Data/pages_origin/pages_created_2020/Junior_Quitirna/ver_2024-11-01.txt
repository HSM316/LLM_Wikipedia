'''Armando Junior Quitirna''' (born 25 April 2000) is a [[Guinea-Bissau|Bissau-Guinean]] professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays as a [[midfielder]] for {{English football updater|CrawleyT}} club [[Crawley Town F.C.|Crawley Town]].<ref>{{soccerway|624099}}</ref>

==Career==
===Early career===
Quitirna was born in Guinea-Bissau, but moved to [[London]] at the age of eight.{{citation needed|date=May 2023}} He started his youth career with local side [[Hornchurch F.C.|AFC Hornchurch]] where he featured mainly for the Development squad but also featured for the first team as an unused substitute in the [[FA Cup]] and [[Isthmian League]] Division One North, against [[Hertford Town F.C.|Hertford Town]] and [[Witham Town F.C.|Witham Town]] respectively.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.romfordrecorder.co.uk/news/21523547.fa-cup-hertford-town-1-hornchurch-2/|title=FA Cup: Hertford Town 1 Hornchurch 2|website=www.romfordrecorder.co.uk|date=16 September 2017|accessdate=7 May 2023}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.romfordrecorder.co.uk/news/21521789.bostik-north-hornchurch-0-witham-town-1/|title=Bostik North: Hornchurch 0 Witham Town 1|website=www.romfordrecorder.co.uk|date=4 November 2017|accessdate=7 May 2023}}</ref>

===Charlton Athletic===
In 2018 he signed for [[EFL League One]] side [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]] and was placed in the under-23 squad. He failed to make an appearance in his first season with the club as Charlton were promoted to the [[EFL Championship]] through the play-offs, with victory over [[Sunderland A.F.C.|Sunderland]] at [[Wembley Stadium]]. In August 2019, he made his professional debut for the first team when he featured as a substitute in the [[EFL Cup]] first round 0–0 draw with [[Forest Green Rovers F.C.|Forest Green Rovers]], which they eventually lost on penalties.<ref name="2019/20"/> He failed to feature in another matchday squad as Charlton were relegated back to League One after only one season. Following that campaign he was offered a new one-year contract in July 2020 with another one-year option included.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.charltonafc.com/news/view/5efda22caea4a/charlton-confirm-list-of-released-and-retained-players|title=RELEASED AND RETAINED PLAYERS|website=www.cafc.co.uk|date=2 July 2020|accessdate=7 May 2023}}</ref> He made his second and final appearance for the club in September 2020 in a 0–0 draw with [[Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. Under-21s and Academy|Brighton & Hove Albion U21]] in the [[EFL Trophy]].<ref name="2020/21"/> It was announced on 18 May 2021 that he would leave the club at the end of his contract, with Quitirna turning down a new deal in order to pursue first team football.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cafc.co.uk/news/view/609d02c1c601c/squad-update-following-completion-of-the-202021-season|title=Squad update following completion of the 2020/21 season|website=www.cafc.co.uk|date=18 May 2021|accessdate=22 March 2022}}</ref>

===Waterford===
On 9 July 2021, it was announced that had Quitirna made the move to [[League of Ireland Premier Division]] side [[Waterford F.C.|Waterford]] after an approach from chairman, Mitch Cowling, who promoted first team football.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://waterfordfc.ie/|title=Waterford FC|date=9 July 2021|website=Waterford FC}}</ref> On the same day, he made his debut for the club, scoring a penalty in a 1–0 win away to [[Finn Harps F.C.|Finn Harps]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.independent.ie/sport/soccer/league-of-ireland/junior-quitirna-strikes-to-give-waterford-lifeline-at-bottom-of-premier-division-40635541.html|title = Junior Quitirna strikes to give Waterford lifeline at bottom of Premier Division}}</ref> On 30 July 2021, he came off the bench away to [[Drogheda United F.C.|Drogheda United]] in the 75th minute and scored the winning goal eight minutes later in a 2–1 win for his side.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.irishexaminer.com/sport/soccer/arid-40349732.html|title = Super-sub Junior Quitirna seals Waterford win away to Drogheda United|date = 30 July 2021}}</ref> He scored six goals in his first 14 appearances in Waterford's hugely-improved form in the second half of the season. Waterford finished in 9th position which sent them into a relegation play-off with [[League of Ireland First Division|First Division]] side [[University College Dublin A.F.C.|UCD]], which they subsequently lost 2–1.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.the42.ie/ucd-waterford-report-5613512-Nov2021/|title=Joy for UCD as they beat beleagured &#91;sic&#93; Waterford to seal Premier Division status|first=Paul|last=Fennessy|website=The42|date=26 November 2021 }}</ref> He made a total of 20 appearances in all competitions over the season, scoring seven goals.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://int.soccerway.com/players/junior-quitirna/667885/|title=Guinea-Bissau - Junior Quitirna - Profile with news, career statistics and history - Soccerway|website=int.soccerway.com}}</ref> In his second season for the club he made a total of 35 appearances in all competitions, scoring 13 goals as the club finished in second behind Champions [[Cork City F.C.|Cork City]].<ref name="auto"/> The club qualified for the promotion play-offs, beating [[Treaty United F.C.|Treaty United]] in the semi-finals and [[Galway United F.C.|Galway United]] in the final. However, they lost the promotion/relegation play-off to UCD for the second season running. He was named in the League of Ireland First Division Team of the Year.<ref name="Fleetwood"/> On 31 January 2023, it was announced that Quitirna had left the club due to ongoing visa issues and a move to sister club, Fleetwood Town, facilitated a move back to his native UK.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://waterfordfc.ie/update-junior-quitirna/ | title=Update: Junior Quitirna|date=31 January 2023|accessdate=7 May 2023}}</ref>

===Fleetwood Town===
Quitirna joined [[EFL League One|League One]] club [[Fleetwood Town F.C.|Fleetwood Town]] until his visa issues were sorted and was immediately loaned out to [[National League (division)|National League]] club [[Altrincham F.C.|Altrincham]] on an initial one-month deal, following in the footsteps of [[Chris Conn-Clarke]] and [[Barry Baggley]].<ref name="Fleetwood">{{cite web|url=https://www.fleetwoodtownfc.com/news/2023/january/junior-junior-joins-fleetwood-town-from-waterford-fc/|title=Junior Quitirna joins Town and heads to Altrincham on loan|website=www.fleetwoodtownfc.com|date=31 January 2023|accessdate=31 January 2023}}</ref> Quitirna returned to his parent club on 2 March 2023, after making just one appearance for [[Altrincham F.C.|Altrincham]], playing 10 minutes in an [[FA Trophy]] tie away to [[Bracknell Town F.C.|Bracknell Town]], in which he scored the last goal in a 3–1 win.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://altrinchamfc.com/blogs/news/junior-rejoins-fleetwood-after-alty-loan-spell-comes-to-an-end?fbclid=IwAR2xhR23wKrMgxd8T7HkLqALux3bCaJTV0LD-1loHa9aWayiO60BGVneMkk | title=Junior rejoins Fleetwood after Alty loan spell comes to an end }}</ref> Upon his return to Fleetwood, he forced himself into first team contention and scored on his EFL debut on 7 April after replacing [[Daniel Batty]] as a substitute in a 2–1 defeat to [[Cambridge United F.C.|Cambridge United]] at the [[Abbey Stadium]].<ref name="2022/23"/> He made a further four appearances for the Cods as they finished in 13th position.<ref name="2022/23"/>

In January 2024, Quitirna signed a new long-term contract with the club, keeping him at the club until June 2026 with the option for a further year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fleetwoodtownfc.com/news/2024/january/quitirna-contract/|title=Junior Quitirna pens a new long-term deal with Town|website=www.fleetwoodtownfc.com|date=24 January 2024|accessdate=24 January 2024}}</ref>

===Crawley Town===
On 25 July 2024, Quitirna returned to League One following Fleetwood Town's relegation, joining newly promoted [[Crawley Town F.C.|Crawley Town]] for an undisclosed fee on an initial two-year deal.<ref> {{cite web|url=https://www.crawleytownfc.com/news/2024/july/25/reds-secure-the-signing-of-armando-quitirna/|title=REDS SECURE THE SIGNING OF ARMANDO JUNIOR QUITIRNA|website=www.crawleytownfc.com|date=25 July 2024|accessdate=25 July 2024}}</ref> He scored on his debut for the club as Crawley beat [[Blackpool F.C.|Blackpool]] 2–1 in the opening game of the season.<ref>{{Cite web |date=10 August 2024 |title=Crawley 2–1 Blackpool |url=https://www.skysports.com/football/crawley-town-vs-blackpool/report/508795 |access-date=11 August 2024 |website=Sky Sports}}</ref>
