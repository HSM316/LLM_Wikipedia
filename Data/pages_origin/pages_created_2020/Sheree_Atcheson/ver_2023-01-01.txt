'''Sheree Atcheson''' (born 28 February 1991) is a Sri Lankan-born [[People of Northern Ireland|Northern Irish]] computer scientist and Global Director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at [https://peakon.com/ Peakon]. She previously has held roles such as Head of Diversity and Inclusion at [[Monzo (bank)|Monzo]] and Consulting Inclusion Lead at [[Deloitte]]. Atcheson has been recognised by ''[[Computer Weekly]]'' as one of the Most Influential Women in UK Tech. She is the Global Ambassador for [[Women Who Code]].

== Early life and education ==
Atcheson was born in [[Sri Lanka]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.siliconrepublic.com/people/sheree-atcheson-women-who-code-inspirefest-2|title=Sheree Atcheson: 'The tech industry doesn't just have one face'|last=Darmody|first=Jenny|date=2018-07-13|website=Silicon Republic|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> At three weeks old, Atcheson was adopted by a Roman Catholic family in [[County Tyrone]] [[Northern Ireland]]  [[United Kingdom]], where she attended [[St Patrick's Academy, Dungannon]].

As a child she played computer games with her brother and she decided that she wanted to pursue a career in technology.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|publisher=BBC|date=2019|title=BBC - Sheree Atcheson|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/3w5KkQf8gwYpJYhq9TFcwwP/sheree-atcheson}}</ref> Her first job was working at the counter at her local pharmacy.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.womenofinfluence.ca/2019/12/01/meet-sheree-atcheson-one-of-the-uks-top-most-influential-women-in-tech/|title=Meet Sheree Atcheson: One of the UK's Top Most Influential Women...|last=Intern|first=Media|date=2019-12-01|website=Women of Influence|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> She decided to study computer science at [[Queen's University Belfast]].<ref>{{cite web|work=One Young World|date=2019|title=40 under 40: Get to know Sheree Atcheson|url=https://www.oneyoungworld.com/blog/40-under-40-get-know-sheree-atcheson}}</ref> As an undergraduate student, only one in ten of her classmates were women, and Atcheson has worked since then to address this imbalance.<ref>{{cite web|work=Blogs.deloitte.co.uk|date=2019|title=Meet Sheree, one of the UK's most influential women in tech - Deloitte careers blog|url=https://blogs.deloitte.co.uk/careers/2018/05/meet-sheree-one-of-the-uks-most-influential-women-in-tech.html}}</ref>

== Career ==
After graduating, Atcheson joined [[Kainos]] as a software engineer before joining SR Laboratories.<ref>{{cite web|publisher=Kainos|date=2019|title=Kainos: transformational Digital Services and market-leading Digital Platforms|url=https://www.kainos.com/}}</ref> In 2016 Atcheson joined [[Deloitte]], where she worked as a business consultant for strategy and architecture. She was eventually promoted lead for inclusion, and helped to design and implement the Deloitte Inclusion Strategy.<ref>{{Citation|title=Sheree Atcheson, Tech Respect & Inclusion Manager - Deloitte UK, at #DES2019 Digital Enterprise Show|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GuNDpt3-ol4|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> At Deloitte, Atcheson developed and led the Consulting practice Inclusion strategy. In 2019 she was appointed Head of Diversity and Inclusion at [[Monzo (bank)|Monzo]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sifted.eu/articles/sheree-atcheson-monzo/|title=Monzo snags Deloitte consultant as head of diversity|website=Sifted|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://monzo.com/blog/women-in-finance-charter-update|title=Women In Finance Charter Update|website=Monzo|language=en-GB|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> and left this role in June 2020. In August 2020, she was appointed as Global Director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at [https://peakon.com/ Peakon].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Sheree Atcheson Joins Peakon As Its New Director of Diversity, Equity & Inclusion|url=https://peakon.com/press/press-releases/sheree-atcheson-joins-peakon-as-its-new-director-of-diversity-equity-inclusion/|access-date=2020-08-25|website=Peakon|language=en-GB}}</ref> She has written for ''[[Forbes]]'' and ''[[The Guardian]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/shereeatcheson/|title=Sheree Atcheson|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/profile/sheree-atcheson|title=Sheree Atcheson {{!}} The Guardian|website=the Guardian|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> and been featured in the ''[[Evening Standard]]'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/futurelondon/skills/women-who-code-global-ambassador-sheree-atcheson-on-the-importance-of-diversity-and-inclusion-in-a4165881.html|title=Women Who Code's global ambassador Sheree Atcheson on the importance of diversity and inclusion in tech|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> BBC Make It Super Assembly <ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/articles/5HP6vbsQr6n0P8sHzKlzd35/make-it-super-assembly-with-sheree-atcheson|title=Make It: Super Assembly with Sheree Atcheson|last=|first=|date=|website=BBC News|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> and ''Positive News''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.positive.news/science/inspiring-more-women-into-tech-careers/|title=The leader who wants to inspire more women into tech careers|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

=== Advocacy ===
Atcheson was involved with establishing United Kingdom expansion of [[Women Who Code]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Huntley|first=A.|date=2016|title=Women Who Code survey: Women in tech have a hard time with career advancement|work=Fierce CIO|page=1|url=http://search.ebscohost.com/login.aspx?direct=true&db=edb&AN=115196326}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cohaesus.co.uk/diversity-series-qa-sheree-atcheson-women-code/|title=The diversity series: Q&A with Sheree Atcheson at Women Who Code|date=2016-07-20|website=Cohaesus|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.computerweekly.com/photostory/450427464/The-50-Most-Influential-Women-in-UK-Tech-2017/35/35-Sheree-Atcheson-UK-expansion-director-Women-Who-Code|title=35. Sheree Atcheson, UK expansion director, Women Who Code - The 50 Most Influential Women in UK Tech 2017|website=www.computerweekly.com|access-date=2020-01-05}}</ref> Since launching in 2013, Atcheson has taken their membership to over 8,000 members, over 1,000 of which belong to the Belfast branch. She serves as their Global Ambassador.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/futurelondon/skills/women-who-code-global-ambassador-sheree-atcheson-on-the-importance-of-diversity-and-inclusion-in-a4165881.html|title=Sheree Atcheson on the importance of diversity and inclusion in tech|date=2019-06-13|website=Evening Standard|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.womenwhocode.com/about|title=Women Who Code {{!}} About Us|website=Women Who Code|language=en-us|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>

Atcheson established "I am Lanka", a project set up to champion role models from [[Sri Lanka]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eximomarketingstrategy.com/the-strategy-sessions-sheree-atcheson/|title=The Strategy Sessions â€“ Sheree Atcheson|last=|first=|date=2019|publisher=Eximo Marketing Ltd.|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> "I am Lanka"<ref name=":0" /> began when Atcheson was searching for her birth mother, receiving thousands of messages from Sri Lankan people revealing that she had inspired them to share their stories.<ref>{{cite web|work=Iamlanka.org|date=2019|title=I Am Lanka|url=https://iamlanka.org/}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newsfirst.lk/2017/11/03/sheree-atcheson-reunited-birth-mother/|title=Sheree Atcheson to be reunited with her birth mother - Sri Lanka Latest News|date=2017-11-03|website=Sri Lanka News - Newsfirst|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> She delivered a keynote lecture at the 2018 ''InspireFest''.<ref>{{Citation|title=Sheree Atcheson, Women Who Code {{!}} Inspirefest 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ry62OUfjTt4|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>

=== Awards and honours ===
Atcheson was selected by ''Computer Weekly'' as one of the Most Influential Women in UK tech.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.computerweekly.com/news/450421935/Most-influential-women-in-UK-tech-The-2017-longlist|title=Most influential women in UK tech: The 2017 longlist|website=ComputerWeekly.com|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> She was selected by ''WeAreTheCity'' as one of the United Kingdom's Rising Stars.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://risingstars.wearethecity.com/rising-stars-winners-2019/rising-stars-in-diversity-2019/|title=Rising Star Awards Diversity Winners 2019|website=WeAreTheCity Rising Star Awards|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> In 2019 she was awarded [[Queen's University Belfast]] Graduate of the Year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://daro.qub.ac.uk/Graduate-of-the-Year-2019-announced|title=Graduate of the Year 2019 announced - QUB|website=daro.qub.ac.uk|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> and was listed as one of the ''Financial Times'' Top 100 BAME Leaders influencing the Tech sector.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/43f7cece-0086-11ea-be59-e49b2a136b8d|title=100 Bame leaders influencing the tech sector|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> Whilst at Deloitte, she won the Women in Tech Employer of the Year Award in the Women in Tech Employer Awards 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.womenintech.co.uk/employer-awards/winners-2019|title=Women in Tech Employer Awards 2019 - Winners|website=Women in Technology|access-date=2020-01-30}}</ref>
