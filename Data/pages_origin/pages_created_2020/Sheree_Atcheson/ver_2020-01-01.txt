'''Sheree Atcheson''' (born February 28, 1991) is a Sri-Lankan Irish computer scientist and Head of Diversity and Inclusion at [[Monzo (bank)|Monzo]]. Atcheson has been recognised as one of the Most Influential Women in UK Technology. She is the Global Ambassador for [[Women Who Code]].

== Early life and education ==
Atcheson was born in [[Sri Lanka]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.siliconrepublic.com/people/sheree-atcheson-women-who-code-inspirefest-2|title=Sheree Atcheson: ‘The tech industry doesn’t just have one face’|last=Darmody|first=Jenny|date=2018-07-13|website=Silicon Republic|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> At only three weeks old Atcheson was adopted by a family in [[County Tyrone]]. As a child she played computer games with her brother. When her cousin was appointed as a senior engineer at the [[New York Stock Exchange]] in Belfast Atcheson decided that she wanted to pursue a career in technology.<ref name=":0">BBC. (2019). BBC - Sheree Atcheson. [online] Available at: https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/profiles/3w5KkQf8gwYpJYhq9TFcwwP/sheree-atcheson</ref> Her first job was working at the counter at her local pharmacy.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.womenofinfluence.ca/2019/12/01/meet-sheree-atcheson-one-of-the-uks-top-most-influential-women-in-tech/|title=Meet Sheree Atcheson: One of the UK’s Top Most Influential Women...|last=Intern|first=Media|date=2019-12-01|website=Women of Influence|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> She decided to study computer science at [[Queen's University Belfast]].<ref>One Young World. (2019). 40 under 40: Get to know Sheree Atcheson. [online] Available at: https://www.oneyoungworld.com/blog/40-under-40-get-know-sheree-atcheson</ref> As an undergraduate student only one in ten of her classmates were women, and Atcheson has worked since then to address this imbalance.<ref>Blogs.deloitte.co.uk. (2019). Meet Sheree, one of the UK’s most influential women in tech - Deloitte careers blog. [online] Available at: https://blogs.deloitte.co.uk/careers/2018/05/meet-sheree-one-of-the-uks-most-influential-women-in-tech.html</ref>

== Career ==
After graduating Atcheson joined [[Kainos]] as a software engineer before joining SR Laboratories.<ref>Kainos. (2019). Kainos: transformational Digital Services and market-leading Digital Platforms. [online] Available at: https://www.kainos.com/</ref> In 2016 Atcheson joined [[Deloitte]], where she worked as a business consultant for strategy and architecture. She was eventually promoted lead for inclusion, and helped to design and implement the [[Deloitte]] Inclusion Strategy.<ref>{{Citation|title=Sheree Atcheson, Tech Respect & Inclusion Manager - Deloitte UK, at #DES2019 Digital Enterprise Show|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GuNDpt3-ol4|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> At [[Deloitte]] Atcheson coordinated the BrightStart scheme programme, where Deloitte employees study toward degrees while working. In 2019 she was appointed as Head of Diversity and Inclusion at [[Monzo (bank)|Monzo]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sifted.eu/articles/sheree-atcheson-monzo/|title=Monzo snags Deloitte consultant as head of diversity|website=Sifted|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://monzo.com/blog/women-in-finance-charter-update|title=Women In Finance Charter Update|website=Monzo|language=en-GB|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> She has written for [[Forbes]] and [[The Guardian]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/shereeatcheson/|title=Sheree Atcheson|website=Forbes|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/profile/sheree-atcheson|title=Sheree Atcheson {{!}} The Guardian|website=the Guardian|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>

=== Advocacy ===
Atcheson was involved with establishing United Kingdom expansion of [[Women Who Code]].<ref>Huntley, A. (2016) ‘Women Who Code survey: Women in tech have a hard time with career advancement’, Fierce CIO, p. 1. Available at: http://search.ebscohost.com/login.aspx?direct=true&db=edb&AN=115196326</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cohaesus.co.uk/diversity-series-qa-sheree-atcheson-women-code/|title=The diversity series: Q&A with Sheree Atcheson at Women Who Code|date=2016-07-20|website=Cohaesus|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> Since launching in 2013, Atcheson has taken their membership to over 8,000 members, over 1,000 of which belong to the Belfast branch. She currently serves as their Global Ambassador.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.standard.co.uk/futurelondon/skills/women-who-code-global-ambassador-sheree-atcheson-on-the-importance-of-diversity-and-inclusion-in-a4165881.html|title=Sheree Atcheson on the importance of diversity and inclusion in tech|date=2019-06-13|website=Evening Standard|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.womenwhocode.com/about|title=Women Who Code {{!}} About Us|website=Women Who Code|language=en-us|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>

She established ''I am Lanka'' a project set up to champion role models from [[Sri Lanka]].<ref>Anon, (2019). [online] Available at: https://eximomarketingstrategy.com/the-strategy-sessions-sheree-atcheson/</ref>''I am Lanka''<ref name=":0" /> began when Atcheson was searching for her birth mother, inspiring thousands of messages from Sri Lankan people revealing that she had inspired them to share her story.<ref>Iamlanka.org. (2019). I Am Lanka. [online] Available at: https://iamlanka.org/</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newsfirst.lk/2017/11/03/sheree-atcheson-reunited-birth-mother/|title=Sheree Atcheson to be reunited with her birth mother - Sri Lanka Latest News|date=2017-11-03|website=Sri Lanka News - Newsfirst|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> She delivered a keynote lecture at the 2018 ''InspireFest''.<ref>{{Citation|title=Sheree Atcheson, Women Who Code {{!}} Inspirefest 2018|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ry62OUfjTt4|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> 

=== Awards and honours ===
Atcheson was selected as one of the most influential women in the UK tech sector.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.computerweekly.com/news/450421935/Most-influential-women-in-UK-tech-The-2017-longlist|title=Most influential women in UK tech: The 2017 longlist|website=ComputerWeekly.com|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> She was selected by ''WeAreTheCity'' as one of the United Kingdom's Rising Stars.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://risingstars.wearethecity.com/rising-stars-winners-2019/rising-stars-in-diversity-2019/|title=Rising Star Awards Diversity Winners 2019|website=WeAreTheCity Rising Star Awards|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> In 2019 she was selected as [[Queen's University Belfast]] Graduate of the Year.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://daro.qub.ac.uk/Graduate-of-the-Year-2019-announced|title=Graduate of the Year 2019 announced - QUB|website=daro.qub.ac.uk|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>
