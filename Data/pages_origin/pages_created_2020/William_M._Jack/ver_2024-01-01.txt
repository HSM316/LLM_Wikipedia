'''William Maxwell Jack''' (March 5, 1892 â€“ April 14, 1970) was an American politician who served as auditor and secretary of state of Wyoming as a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]].

==Early life==

William Maxwell Jack was born in New York City on March 5, 1892 to Scottish immigrants. In 1894 his family moved back to Scotland and he was raised in Edinburgh where he had to drop out of school at age 12 to help run his family's store. In October 1910 he returned to the United States, his family would remain in Britain with his brother losing his arm during [[The Blitz]], and became a rancher in [[Lusk, Wyoming]] and later married Huldah Teresa on June 1, 1920.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41444431/casper_startribune/ |title=Widow of 'Scotty' Jack Dies |date=31 May 1970 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101045804/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41444431/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=12 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41455690/casper_startribune/ |title=Scotty Jack Brother Loses Arm In Raid |date=30 December 1940 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101144738/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41455690/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=2 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> During World War I he was drafted, but was discharged after training and after returning to Wyoming became active in the oil industry.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41443220/casper_startribune/ |title='Scotty' Jack Dies Tuesday |date=15 April 1970 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101045450/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41443220/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=1 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

==Career==
===Early politics===

In 1922 he unsuccessfully ran for justice of the peace in Lusk. In 1924 he successful ran for one of [[Niobrara County, Wyoming|Niobrara County]]'s two seats in the [[Wyoming House of Representatives|state house]] and was reelected in 1926.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41446371/casper_startribune/ |title=Niobrara County |date=3 December 1924 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101055448/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41446371/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=10 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> In 1928 he moved to Casper after being transferred by an oil company and was elected to the state house again in 1930 and reelected in 1932. In 1933 he was elected as Speaker of the House becoming the first Democratic Speaker in Wyoming's history.

===State Auditor===

On March 30, 1934, he announced that he would run for the Democratic nomination for state auditor and defeated incumbent Auditor Roscoe Alcorn in the general election in the [[1934 Wyoming state elections|Democratic landslide of the year]] when they took control of all five statewide offices and the state legislature.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41446916/casper_startribune/ |title=William M. Jack Enters Race for State Auditor |date=30 March 1934 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101055448/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41446371/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=1 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> In early 1935, Governor [[Leslie A. Miller]] had to temporarily leave Wyoming resulting in Secretary of State [[Lester C. Hunt]] becoming acting governor and further responsibility out of state lead to Senate President [[Nels H. Smith]] becoming acting governor and on March 5, 1935, Jack became acting governor for a few hours.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41447107/casper_startribune/ |title="Scotty" Jack Is Acting Governor for Brief Time |date=4 March 1935 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101062333/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41447107/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=1 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> When the gas chamber in the [[Wyoming State Penitentiary]] was under construction Jack stated that he was against it and supported efforts to prevent its completion along with members of the state legislature, but it was eventually completed.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41447353/casper_startribune/ |title=Wyoming Lethal Gas House May Never Be Placed In Use |date=8 October 1936 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101063316/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41447353/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=12 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

On April 2, 1938, he announced that he would seek reelection and after facing no opposition in the primary defeated C. J. Rogers in the general election. In the [[1938 Wyoming state elections|1938 elections]] the Democrats lost three statewide elections and control of the state legislature, but Jack was the best performing statewide candidate.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41454758/casper_startribune/ |title="Scotty" Jack Declares His Candidacy For Reelection |date=3 April 1938 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101142130/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41454758/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=1 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41455148/casper_startribune/ |title=Miller and Greever |date=9 November 1938 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101143602/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41455148/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=2 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> In 1940 he served as the chairman of the Natrona county delegation to the Wyoming Democratic party's state convention and was later named as temporary chairman.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41455675/casper_startribune/ |title=Scotty Jack Heads County Delegation |date=13 May 1940 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101144839/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41455675/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=4 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

On May 12, 1942 he announced that he would seek reelection to a third term and easily won the primary against Carl A. Johnson and later won in the general election against Everett T. Copenhaver.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41456043/casper_startribune/ |title=Morris Concedes To Mrs. Quealy |date=20 August 1942 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101145652/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41456043/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=1 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41456143/casper_startribune/ |title=1942 State Auditor election results |date=4 November 1942 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101150343/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41456143/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=6 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

In 1944 Secretary of State Mart T. Christensen died and after Governor [[Lester C. Hunt]] rejected Deputy Secretary of State Everett T. Copenhaver for the position he chose to appoint Jack. On October 16, 1944 he resigned as auditor to take up the position of secretary of state.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41456487/casper_startribune/ |title=1942 State Auditor election results |date=17 October 1944 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101151146/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41456487/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=1 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> On January 10,1946 he announced that he would not seek a term in his own right or seek any other political office.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41456713/casper_startribune/ |title='Scotty' Jack Declares Will Not Be Candidate |date=10 January 1946 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101151529/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41456713/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=1 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

==Later life==

After leaving office he returned to the oil industry and became a public relations officer for the Rocky Mountain and Gas Association throughout the 1950s and was appointed as executive vice president in 1950 which he served as until April 1, 1954. On March 3, 1954 he announced that he would seek the Democratic nomination for governor and won the primary, but was defeated in the general election by [[Milward Simpson]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41444846/casper_startribune/ |title='Scotty' Jack Is First Democrat In Ballot Race |date=4 March 1970 |work=Casper Star-Tribune |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101051246/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41444846/casper_startribune/ |archive-date=1 January 2020 |url-status=live |page=1 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> He was later appointed by Simpson to the State Board of Equalization for a six year term. In March 1962 Jack announced that he would mount a primary challenge against Governor [[Jack R. Gage]], but Gage narrowly defeated him by 4,176 votes and went on to be defeated in the general election by Teton county commissioner [[Clifford Hansen]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41270961/casper_morning_star/ |title=Gage Reains Mum On Jack's Entry |date=22 March 1962 |work=Casper Morning Star |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191229161821/https://www.newspapers.com/clip/41270961/casper_morning_star/ |archive-date=29 December 2019 |url-status=live |page=9 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> In 1964 Senator [[Gale W. McGee]] appointed him as regional director of the Small Business Administration in Casper and served until November 1969.

On April 14, 1970 he died at his home in [[Casper, Wyoming]] at age 78.
