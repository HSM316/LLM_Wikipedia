'''''Der Naturforscher''''', The Naturalist,  was a German scientific  publication of the [[Enlightenment] devoted to [[Natural history]] .It was published by J. J. Gebauers Witwe and Joh. Jac. Gebauer at [[Halle]] from 1774 to 1804, one volume in each year. It is usually bound in fifteen volomes octavo.  Indices and registers are  given at ten year intervals enumerating 640 memoirs. Just over 150 plates accompany the text. Many of the illustrations are by [[Johann Stephan Capieux]] and are of a very high standard.  The editors were [[Johann Ernst Immanuel Walch]] (1774-1778) and [[Johann Christian Daniel von Schreber]] (1779-1802). Both were contributors. 

Most of the articles concern aspects of inverebrate zoology mostly
entomology and conchology, a few concern ornithology. 
Notable naturalists contributing include, amongst others [[Johann August Ephraim Goeze]], [[Theodor Gottlieb von Scheven]], [[Josef Aloys Frölich]],  
[[Nikolaus Joseph Brahm]], [[Eugen Johann Christoph Esper]], [[Johann Kaspar Füssli]] 
[[Johann Friedrich Gmelin]]
[[Johann August Ephraim Goeze]]
[[Jean Baptiste Louis d'Audibert de Férussac]],
[[Johann Matthäus Bechstein]] and [[Johann Hermann]]. 

Nissen claims Der Naturforscher to be "the most important 18th century German periodical for the descriptive natural sciences" <ref>Claus Nissen, 1971 ''Die Zoologische Buchillustration.
