'''Norman Denis Botton''' (born 21 January 1954) is a former [[English people|English]] first-class [[cricket]]er.

Born at [[Hammersmith]], Botton attended [[Hertford College, Oxford]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/28/28025/28025.html |title=Player profile: Norman Botton |publisher=CricketArchive |accessdate=2020-01-02}}</ref> While studying at Oxford, Botton played [[first-class cricket]] for [[Oxford University Cricket Club|Oxford University]]. His debut came against [[Leicestershire County Cricket Club|Leicestershire]] at [[University Parks#Cricket ground|Oxford]] in 1974. He played first-class cricket for Oxford until 1975, making fourteen appearances.<ref name="FCM">{{cite web|url=https://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/28/28025/First-Class_Matches.html |title=First-Class Matches played by Norman Botton |publisher=CricketArchive |accessdate=2020-01-02}}</ref> In his fourteen matches, he scored a total of 272 runs at an [[batting average (cricket)|average]] of 12.36 and with a high score of 38 [[not out]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/28/28025/f_Batting_by_Team.html |title=First-class Batting and Fielding For Each Team by Norman Botton |publisher=CricketArchive |accessdate=2020-01-02}}</ref> With his left-arm [[Seam bowling|medium pace]] bowling, he took 11 wickets at a [[bowling average]] of 64.90, with best figures of 2 for 53.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/28/28025/f_Bowling_by_Team.html |title=First-class Bowling For Each Team by Norman Botton |publisher=CricketArchive |accessdate=2020-01-02}}</ref> He also made a single first-class appearance for a combined [[Oxford and Cambridge Universities cricket team]] against the touring [[West Indies cricket team|West Indians]] in 1974.<ref name="FCM"/>

After graduating from Oxford, Botton became a schoolteacher. Prior to his retirement, he was the head of history at [[Monkton Combe School]].<ref name="NEWS">{{cite web|url=https://jri-ltd.com/company-news-item/norman-gets-england-cricket-call-three-years-hip-replacement/ |title=Norman gets England cricket call up three years after hip replacement |date=2016-11-10 |work=www.jri-ltd.com |accessdate=2019-01-02}}</ref> Botton continued to play cricket long after the conclusion of his brief first-class career, featuring for the Somerset Over-50s and Over-60s.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/28/28025/all_teams.html |title=Teams Norman Botton played for |publisher=CricketArchive |accessdate=2020-01-02}}</ref> However, severe [[osteoarthritis]] which restricted his ability to walk made it impossible to play cricket. In 2013, Botton received a [[hip replacement]] and within ten months he was playing cricket once more, resulting in him being selected to play for the England Over-60s on their 2016 tour of Australia.<ref name="NEWS"/>
