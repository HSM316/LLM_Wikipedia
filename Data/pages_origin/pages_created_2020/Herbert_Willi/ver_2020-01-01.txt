'''Herbert Willi''' (born 7 January 1956) is a freelance Austrian composer of classical music, whose orchestral works, concertos and chamber music have been performed internationally and also recorded. Willi composed an opera, ''Schlafes Bruder'', for the [[Opernhaus Zürich]].

== Life ==
Willi was born in [[Bludenz]], [[Vorarlberg]]. He studied music pedagogy and theology at the [[University of Innsbruck]], and simultaneously bassoon and piano at the Innsbruck conservatory.<ref name="Schott" /> From 1983, he studied composition with [[Helmut Eder]] at the Salzburg [[Mozarteum]], then with [[Boguslaw Schaeffer]].<ref name="Doblinger" /> He lives in [[Sankt Anton im Montafon]].<ref name="Schott" />

== Work ==

Willi's work comprises an opera, orchestral works, and chamber music, including compositions for one player. Willi received a commission from the [[Salzburg Festival]] and the [[Cleveland Orchestra]] in 1991 for ''Konzert für Orchester''. The premiere was conducted by [[Christoph von Dohnányi]].<ref name="Schott" /> In 1994/95, the [[Opernhaus Zürich]] commissioned the opera ''Schlafes Bruder'' on the occasion of Austria's millennial, with a [[libretto]] by [[Robert Schneider (writer)|Robert Schneider]], who had written the novel on which it is based, ''[[Schlafes Bruder]]''. It was premiered in 1996.<ref name="Schott" /> In 1997/98, Willi composed ''Begegnung für Orchester'' on a commission by the [[Wiener Philharmoniker]] for the orchestra's 150th anniversary.<ref name="Schott" />

He composed ''Zyklus Montafon'', containing the works ''Eirene'', a trumpet concerto; ''...geraume Zeit...'', a concerto for flute, oboe and orchestra; ''ego eimi'', a clarinet concerto; and ''Äon'', a horn concerto.<ref name="Schott" /> A revised version of ''Schlafes Bruder'' was produced at the [[Stadttheater Klagenfurt]] in March 2008.<ref name="Schott" />

Willi's works were performed in [[Carnegie Hall]] in [[New York City]], [[Tokyo]]<!-- ([[Suntory Hall]], [[Sumida Triphony Hall]])-->, the [[Royal Albert Hall]] in London, and at the [[Philharmonie Berlin]], among others. They were played by the [[Berlin Philharmonic]], [[Wiener Philharmoniker]], [[Cleveland Orchestra]], [[Philadelphia Orchestra]], [[Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra]] and [[New Japan Philharmonic]], conducted by [[Claudio Abbado]], [[Gustavo Dudamel]], [[Manfred Honeck]], [[Riccardo Muti]] and [[Seiji Ozawa]].<ref name="Schott" />

His works were recorded: the [[WERGO]] label published orchestral works including ''Eirene'', ''Räume'', ''Rondino'' after his opera ''Schlafes Bruder'', ''geraume Zeit'' and ''Begegnung'', and the Japanese label [[Camerata Tokyo]] released a CD with chamber music from 1984 to 2005.<ref name="Schott" />

== Awards ==
Willi received scholarships and awards, and was [[composer in residence]]:<ref name="Schott" /> 
* 1985, 1989: Austrian state scholarship for composers<ref name="Harten" />
* 1987: Prize at the competition for string quartets of the [[Wiener Konzerthaus]]gesellschaft (for his ''Streichquartett 1986'' premiered by the [[Arditti Quartet]])
* 1989: {{ill|Erste-Bank-Kompositionspreis|de}}
* 1990: [[Rolf Liebermann|Rolf-Liebermann-Stipendium]] für [[Opera|Opernkompositionen]]<ref name="Harten" />
* 1991: [[Ernst von Siemens Music Prize|Ernst von Siemens Composers' Prize]]<ref name="Harten" />
* 1997: [[Österreichisches Ehrenzeichen für Wissenschaft und Kunst]]<ref name="Harten" />
* 1998: Großes Verdienstzeichen des Landes [[Vorarlberg]]<ref name="Harten" />
* 1992: Composer in residence at the [[Salzburg Festival]]
* 1996–1998: Composer in residence with the [[Camerata Academia]]
* 2002/2003: Composer in residence with the [[Gesellschaft der Musikfreunde]] of the [[Wiener Musikverein]] and the [[Wiener Concert-Verein]]<ref name="Harten" />
* 2007: Composer in residence at the [[Pacific Music Festival]] in [[Sapporo]]
* 2008: Composer in residence at the International Summer Music Academy & Festival in [[Kusatsu (Gunma)|Kusatsu]]
