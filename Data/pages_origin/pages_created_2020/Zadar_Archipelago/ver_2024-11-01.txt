The '''Zadar Archipelago''' ({{langx|hr|Zadarski arhipelag}}, {{langx|it|Archipelago zaratino}}) is a [[archipelago|group of island]]s in the [[Adriatic Sea]], near the [[Croatia]]n city of [[Zadar]].<ref name=Alberi>{{cite book |first= Dario |last= Alberi | title=Dalmazia. Storia, arte, cultura |publisher= Lint Editoriale | location=Trebaseleghe (PD) | year=2008 |  pp=626–628 }}</ref><ref name=Rizzi>{{cite book |first= Alberto | last=Rizzi | title=Guida della Dalmazia |publisher= ed. Italo Svevo | location=Trieste |year= 2010 | volume=I | p=430 }}</ref>

==Important Bird Area==
The northern part of the Zadar Archipelago, including the islands of [[Premuda]], [[Silba]], [[Olib]], [[Škarda]], [[Iž]] and [[Molat]], has been designated an [[Important Bird Area]] (IBA) by [[BirdLife International]] because it supports breeding populations of several species of [[piscivore|fish-eating]] [[seabird]]s.<ref name=bli>{{cite web |url= https://datazone.birdlife.org/site/factsheet/24001 |title=North part of Zadar Archipelago|author=<!--Not stated--> |date=2024|website= BirdLife Data Zone|publisher= BirdLife International|access-date= 2024-08-27}}</ref>
