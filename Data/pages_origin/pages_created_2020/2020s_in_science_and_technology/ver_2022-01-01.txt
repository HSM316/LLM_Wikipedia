==Space==
*[[NASA]]'s [[Mars 2020]] mission, which includes the ''[[Perseverance (rover)|Perseverance]]'' rover, was successfully launched on 30 July 2020 to study the habitability of Mars in preparation for future human missions.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://mars.nasa.gov/mars2020/mission/overview/ |title= Overview – 2020 Mission Plans |website= Mars.nasa.gov |publisher=NASA |accessdate=2015-11-12}}</ref>
*The [[Chang'e 5]] successfully landed on the surface of the moon in 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Myers|first=Steven Lee|last2=Chang|first2=Kenneth|date=2020-12-01|title=China Lands Chang'e-5 Spacecraft on Moon to Gather Lunar Rocks and Soil|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/12/01/science/china-moon-landing.html|access-date=2020-12-09|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>
* 16 September 2021, [[SpaceX]] launched [[Inspiration4]]. It was the first orbital launch of an all-private crew, including first person with a prosthesis who was also the youngest [[Hayley Arceneaux|person]] in space (aged 29). 
* [[NASA]]'s [[Artemis 1]] mission to the Moon is set to launch November 2021 to test the [[Space Launch System]] rocket.
* The [[James Webb Space Telescope]] is scheduled to be launched on 31 October 2021 after being delayed several times.<ref>{{cite news|title=New launch date for James Webb Space Telescope|url=https://www.esa.int/Our_Activities/Space_Science/New_launch_date_for_James_Webb_Space_Telescope|publisher=[[European Space Agency]]|date=June 28, 2018|accessdate=July 15, 2018}}</ref>
* [[India]] hopes to conduct its first [[Indian human spaceflight program|human spaceflight]] in 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.merinews.com/article/indias-first-manned-space-mission-likely-to-be-accomplished-in-2021-now/15911532.shtml|title=India's first manned space mission likely to be accomplished in 2021 now|website=www.merinews.com|access-date=1 January 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171013182345/http://www.merinews.com/article/indias-first-manned-space-mission-likely-to-be-accomplished-in-2021-now/15911532.shtml|archive-date=13 October 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>
* ESA's [[ExoMars]] Rover launches in 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.esa.int/For_Media/Press_Releases/Second_ExoMars_mission_moves_to_next_launch_opportunity_in_2020|title=Second ExoMars mission moves to next launch opportunity in 2020|last=esa|website=European Space Agency}}</ref>
* The first component of the [[Lunar Gateway]], a proposed inhabitable space station to be implemented by multiple international space agencies on the [[Moon]], the Power and Propulsion Element, is set to be delivered by an undetermined commercial launch vehicle in 2022.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.space.com/amp/moon-mars-nasa-2020-budget-proposal.html | work= Space.com | title = Trump's 2020 budget Will Take US from Moon to Mars, Agency Chief Says | date = March 11, 2019 | accessdate = March 22, 2019}}</ref>
* [[ESA]]'s [[Jupiter Icy Moon Explorer]] is scheduled to be launched in 2022.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/science-environment-17917102 |title=Esa selects 1bn-euro Juice probe to Jupiter |author=Jonathan Amos |date=2012-05-02|accessdate= May 13, 2012|work=[[BBC News Online]] |publisher=BBC}}</ref>
* [[SpaceX]] founder and entrepreneur [[Elon Musk]] plans to send the first [[SpaceX Starship|Starship]] to Mars in 2022, paving the way to the [[Colonization of Mars|colonization of the planet]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/science-environment-37486372 |title=Elon Musk outlines Mars colony vision|publisher=BBC|date=September 27, 2016|accessdate=September 28, 2016}}</ref>
* NASA plans to send "the first woman and the next man" to the [[Lunar south pole region]] via [[Artemis 3]] in 2024.
* The [[European Space Agency]] plans to begin mining the [[moon]] for natural resources by 2025.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://edition.cnn.com/2019/01/22/europe/mining-on-moon-trnd/index.html|title=The European Space Agency plans to start mining for natural resources on the moon|first=Lauren|last=Kent|date=January 22, 2019|publisher=CNN|accessdate=January 23, 2019}}</ref>
