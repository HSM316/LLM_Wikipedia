{{See also|Timeline of solar cells#2020}}
* [[QuantumScape]] revealed that it had created the first functioning prototype of a  [[solid-state battery]], promising to massively increase battery capacity.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Did QuantumScape Just Solve a 40-Year-Old Battery Problem?|language=en-us|magazine=Wired|url=https://www.wired.com/story/quantumscape-solid-state-battery/|access-date=2020-12-09|issn=1059-1028}}</ref>
* The Chinese [[Experimental Advanced Superconducting Tokamak]] was turned on for the first time.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-12-07|title=China turns on nuclear-powered 'artificial sun' for first time|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/asia/china-nuclear-fusion-reactor-artificial-sun-chengdu-b1767393.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201207163844/https://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/asia/china-nuclear-fusion-reactor-artificial-sun-chengdu-b1767393.html |archive-date=2020-12-07 |url-access=limited |url-status=live|access-date=2020-12-09|website=The Independent|language=en}}</ref> The [[China Fusion Engineering Test Reactor]] is being planned for commissioning later in the decade.

==Space==
*[[NASA]]'s [[Mars 2020]] mission, which includes the ''[[Perseverance (rover)|Perseverance]]'' rover, was successfully launched on 30 July 2020 to study the habitability of Mars in preparation for future human missions.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://mars.nasa.gov/mars2020/mission/overview/ |title= Overview – 2020 Mission Plans |website= Mars.nasa.gov |publisher=NASA |accessdate=2015-11-12}}</ref>
*The [[Chang'e 5]] successfully landed on the surface of the Moon in 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|last1=Myers|first1=Steven Lee|last2=Chang|first2=Kenneth|date=2020-12-01|title=China Lands Chang'e-5 Spacecraft on Moon to Gather Lunar Rocks and Soil|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/12/01/science/china-moon-landing.html|access-date=2020-12-09|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>
* 16 September 2021, [[SpaceX]] launched [[Inspiration4]]. It was the first orbital launch of an all-private crew, including the first person with a prosthesis who was also the youngest [[Hayley Arceneaux|person]] in space (aged 29). 
* [[NASA]]'s [[Artemis 1]] mission to the Moon launched on 16 November 2022 to test the [[Space Launch System]] rocket.
* The [[James Webb Space Telescope]] was successfully launched on 25 December 2021. On 12 July 2022, the first full-color images captured were released to the public which included ''[[Webb's First Deep Field]] and others''.<ref>{{cite news |date=July 8, 2022 |orig-date=July 8, 2022 |title=First Images of James Webb |work=Ars Technica |publisher=[[Ars Technica]] |url=https://arstechnica.com/science/2022/07/nasa-names-first-five-targets-for-webb-images/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220708195443/https://arstechnica.com/science/2022/07/nasa-names-first-five-targets-for-webb-images/ |archive-date=July 8, 2022 |accessdate=}}</ref>
* [[India]] hopes to conduct its first [[Indian human spaceflight program|human spaceflight]], [[Gaganyaan]] in late 2024.<ref>{{cite web |last=Pillai |first=Soumya |date=22 December 2022 |title=India’s 1st human space flight to be launched in end of 2024 |url=https://www.hindustantimes.com/india-news/indias-1st-human-space-flight-to-be-launched-in-end-of-2024-centre-101671649211273.html |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230103122733/https://www.hindustantimes.com/india-news/indias-1st-human-space-flight-to-be-launched-in-end-of-2024-centre-101671649211273.html |archive-date=3 January 2023 |access-date=11 January 2023 |website=www.hindustantimes.com}}</ref>
* The first component of the [[Lunar Gateway]], a proposed inhabitable space station to be implemented by multiple international space agencies on the [[Moon]], the Power and Propulsion Element, is set to be delivered by a [[SpaceX]] [[Falcon Heavy]] in November 2024.<ref name="nasa-20210709">{{cite press release |url=https://www.nasa.gov/press-release/nasa-northrop-grumman-finalize-moon-outpost-living-quarters-contract |title=NASA, Northrop Grumman Finalize Moon Outpost Living Quarters Contract |work=[[NASA]] |date=9 July 2021 |access-date=9 July 2021}}</ref>
* [[ESA]]'s [[Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer]] was launched on 14 April 2023.<ref>{{cite press release |url=https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Space_Science/Juice/ESA_s_Juice_lifts_off_on_quest_to_discover_secrets_of_Jupiter_s_icy_moons |title=ESA's Juice lifts off on quest to discover secrets of Jupiter's icy moons |work=[[ESA]] |date=2023-04-14|accessdate= November 21, 2023}}</ref>
* [[SpaceX]] founder and entrepreneur [[Elon Musk]] plans to send the first [[SpaceX Starship|Starship]] to Mars in 2029, paving the way to the [[Colonization of Mars|colonization of the planet]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Torchinsky |first1=Rina |title=Elon Musk hints at crewed mission to Mars in 2029 |url=https://www.npr.org/2022/03/17/1087167893/elon-musk-mars-2029 |website=nrp.com |publisher=nrp |access-date=24 January 2023}}</ref>
* NASA plans to send "the first woman and the next man" to the [[Lunar south pole region]] via [[Artemis 3]] in 2025.
* The [[European Space Agency]] plans to begin mining the [[Moon]] for natural resources by 2025.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://edition.cnn.com/2019/01/22/europe/mining-on-moon-trnd/index.html|title=The European Space Agency plans to start mining for natural resources on the moon|first=Lauren|last=Kent|date=January 22, 2019|publisher=CNN|accessdate=January 23, 2019}}</ref>
