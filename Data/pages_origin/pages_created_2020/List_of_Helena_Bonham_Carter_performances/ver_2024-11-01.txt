English actress [[Helena Bonham Carter]] has been acting since the early 1980s. First appearing in the television series ''[[A Pattern of Roses]]'' in 1983 before making her film debut playing Lucy Honeychurch in ''[[A Room with a View (1985 film)|A Room with a View]]'' (1985) and the [[Lady Jane Grey|title character]] in ''[[Lady Jane (1986 film)|Lady Jane]]'' (1986). She was nominated for the [[Academy Award for Best Actress]] for her role as Kate Croy in ''[[The Wings of the Dove (1997 film)|The Wings of the Dove]]'' (1997). For her role as [[Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother|Queen Elizabeth]] in ''[[The King's Speech]]'' (2010), she was nominated for the [[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress]] and won the [[BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role]]. She also won the 2010 [[International Emmy Award for Best Actress]] for her role as the author [[Enid Blyton]] in the television film ''[[Enid (film)|Enid]]'' (2009).

Her other film roles include [[Ophelia]] in ''[[Hamlet (1990 film)|Hamlet]]'' (1990), ''[[Where Angels Fear to Tread (film)|Where Angels Fear to Tread]]'' (1991), ''[[Howards End (film)|Howards End]]'' (1992), [[Elizabeth Lavenza]] in ''[[Mary Shelley's Frankenstein (film)|Mary Shelley's Frankenstein]]'' (1994), [[Woody Allen]]'s ''[[Mighty Aphrodite]]'' (1995), Marla Singer in ''[[Fight Club]]'' (1999), [[Bellatrix Lestrange]] in four of the [[Harry Potter (film series)|''Harry Potter'' films]] (2007–11), [[Skynet (Terminator)|Dr. Serena Kogan / Skynet]] in ''[[Terminator Salvation]]'' (2009), [[Miss Havisham]] in ''[[Great Expectations (2012 film)|Great Expectations]]'' (2012), [[Mme. Thénardier|Madame Thénardier]] in ''[[Les Misérables (2012 film)|Les Misérables]]'' (2012), the [[List of Disney's Cinderella characters#The Fairy Godmother|Fairy Godmother]] in ''[[Cinderella (2015 American film)|Cinderella]]'' (2015) and Rose Weil in ''[[Ocean's 8]]'' (2018).

She has frequently collaborated with director [[Tim Burton]]; in ''[[Planet of the Apes (2001 film)|Planet of the Apes]]'' (2001), ''[[Big Fish]]'' (2003), ''[[Corpse Bride]]'' (2005), ''[[Charlie and the Chocolate Factory (film)|Charlie and the Chocolate Factory]]'' (2005), ''[[Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street (2007 film)|Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street]]'' (2007), ''[[Dark Shadows (film)|Dark Shadows]]'' (2012), and playing the [[Red Queen (Through the Looking-Glass)|Red Queen]] in ''[[Alice in Wonderland (2010 film)|Alice in Wonderland]]'' (2010) and its sequel ''[[Alice Through the Looking Glass (2016 film)|Alice Through the Looking Glass]]'' (2016).

Her other television work includes the television films ''[[Fatal Deception: Mrs. Lee Harvey Oswald]]'' (1993), ''[[Live from Baghdad (film)|Live from Baghdad]]'' (2002), ''[[Toast (film)|Toast]]'' (2010), and ''[[Burton & Taylor]]'' (2013); and television series ''[[Love, Nina]]'' (2016), ''[[The Crown (TV series)|The Crown]]'' as [[Princess Margaret]] (2019–2020) and as [[Noele Gordon]] in the biographical miniseries ''[[Nolly (TV series)|Nolly]]'' (2023).

She has also narrated [[Audiobook|audiobooks]]. In 2011, [[Penguin Audio|Penguin Audios]] released an [[audiobook]] version of [[Anne Frank|Anne Frank's]] [[The Diary of a Young Girl]], narrated by Bonham Carter.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Diary of a Young Girl (Audio Download): Anne Frank, Helena Bonham Carter, Penguin Audio: Amazon.co.uk: Books |url=https://www.amazon.co.uk/Diary-of-a-Young-Girl/dp/B0053YOY64 |access-date=2024-02-10 |website=www.amazon.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book |url=https://www.listening-books.org.uk/book/the-diary-of-a-young-girl/2801 |title=The Diary of A Young Girl Audiobook - Anne Frank - Listening Books |language=en}}</ref>
