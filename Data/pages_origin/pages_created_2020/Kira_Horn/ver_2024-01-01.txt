'''Kira Leonie Horn''' (born 12 February 1995)<ref name="FIH1">{{cite web |url=https://tms.fih.ch/competitions/544/reports/teams |title=Team Details – Germany |publisher=[[International Hockey Federation]] |website=tms.fih.ch |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> is a German [[field hockey]] player, who plays as a midfielder.<ref name="DHB">{{cite web |url=https://web.hockey.de/natio/natio_Damen/news/v5xurz.html |title=DANAS: Reckingers erweiterter Olympiakader steht |publisher=[[Deutscher Hockey-Bund]] |website=web.hockey.de |language=German |accessdate=31 December 2019 |archive-date=1 October 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211001200950/https://web.hockey.de/natio/natio_Damen/news/v5xurz.html |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Career==
===Club hockey===
In the German [[Feldhockey Bundesliga (Women's field hockey)|Bundesliga]], Horn plays club hockey for [[der Club an der Alster|Club an der Alster]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.dcada.de/hockey/1-damen-hockey/ |title=1. Damen Hockey |publisher=[[der Club an der Alster|Club an der Alster]] |website=dcada.de |language=German |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://eurohockey.altiusrt.com/people/11145 |title=Horn Kira |publisher=[[European Hockey Federation]] |website=eurohockey.altiusrt.com |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

===National teams===
====Under–21====
Kira Horn represented the [[Germany women's national under-21 field hockey team|Germany U–21]] side on numerous occasions throughout her junior career. Her most notable performance with the team was at the [[2016 Women's Hockey Junior World Cup|2016 FIH Junior World Cup]] in [[Santiago, Chile]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://tms.fih.ch/people/9762 |title=HORN Kira |publisher=[[International Hockey Federation]] |website=tms.fih.ch |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> During the tournament Horn scored two goals, helping the German team to a fifth place finish.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.teamusa.org/USA-Field-Hockey/EVENTS/2016/November/23/Womens-Hockey-Junior-World-Cup |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160818180515/http://www.teamusa.org/USA-Field-Hockey/EVENTS/2016/November/23/Womens-Hockey-Junior-World-Cup |url-status=dead |archive-date=August 18, 2016 |title=Women's Hockey Junior World Cup 2016 |publisher=[[USA Field Hockey]] |website=teamusa.org |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

====Die Danas====
In 2019, Horn made her debut for the [[Germany women's national field hockey team|German]] national team during the [[2019 Women's FIH Pro League|inaugural tournament]] of the [[Women's FIH Pro League|FIH Pro League]]. The team eventually won a bronze medal at the Grand Final in [[Amsterdam, Netherlands]], Horn's first in German colours.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fihproleague.com/women/players/horn-kira__71700__4873 |title=HORN Kira |publisher=[[Women's FIH Pro League|FIH Pro League]] |website=fihproleague.com |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> Horn represented the team again in August at the [[2019 Women's EuroHockey Nations Championship|EuroHockey Nations Championship]] in [[Antwerp, Belgium]]. At the tournament, Germany finished in second place, winning Horn a silver medal.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.belfiuseurohockey.com/en/news/article.html?Article_ID=867553 |title=BRILLIANT ORANJE PERFORMANCE EARN TENTH WOMEN'S EUROHOCKEY CHAMPIONSHIP TITLE |publisher=[[Women's EuroHockey Nations Championship|Belfius EuroHockey]] |website=belfiuseurohockey.com |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

In December 2019, Horn was named in the preliminary German Olympic squad to train for the [[2020 Summer Olympics]] in [[Tokyo, Japan]].<ref name="DHB"/>
