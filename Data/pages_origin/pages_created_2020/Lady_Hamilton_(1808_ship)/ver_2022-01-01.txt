'''''Lady Hamilton''''' was a Danish vessel taken in prize. She first appeared under British ownership in 1808. She became a transport and then a [[whaler]]. She was wrecked in December 1831.

==Career==
===Transport===
''Lady Hamilton'' was a Danish prize. She first appeared in ''[[Lloyd's Register]]'' (''LR'') in 1808 with Gander, master, Gouch & Co., owners, and trade London transport.<ref name=LR1808>[https://hdl.handle.net/2027/mdp.39015005676377?urlappend=%3Bseq=593 ''LR' (1808), Supple.pages "L", Seq.№L101.]</ref>

===1st whaling voyage (1821–1824)===
Captain J. Keen sailed from England on 25 May 1821, bound for the Pacific. In 1823 and 1824 she was reported at Tahiti and Hawaii. She returned to England on 17 November 1824 with 350 casks of whale oil.<ref name=bswf>[https://whalinghistory.org/bv/voyages/ British Southern Whale Fishery Database – Voyages: ''Lady Hamilton''.]</ref>

===2nd whaling voyage (1824–1828)===
Captain Maughan sailed from England on 6 March 1825. ''Lady Hamilton'' was reported to have visited Honolulu, Tahiti, and Oahu. She returned to England on 29 May 1828 with 2000 barrels or more of whale oil.<ref name=bswf/>

===3rd whaling voyage (1829–Loss)===
The ''Register of Shipping'' for 1832 showed ''Lady Hamilton'' with Maughan, master, changing to Hayward, T.Ward, owner, and trade London–Southern Fishery. She had undergone a through repair in 1829.<ref name=RS1832>[https://hdl.handle.net/2027/mdp.39015024214499?urlappend=%3Bseq=397 ''RS'' (1832), Seq.№L64.]</ref>

Captain Hayword (or Haywood) sailed from England on 12 February 1829. She sailed via the Seychelles Bank to [[Socotra]]. In 1831 she stopped at Mauritius. The whaler ''L'Aigle'' reported on 23 May 1832 to {{ship||Cheviot|1827 ship|2}} that ''Lady Hamilton'' had been lost in the Mozambique Channel. Her crew  had been saved but her cargo of 1800 barrels of whale oil had been lost.<ref name=bswf/>

==Fate==
''Lady Hamilton'' was wrecked on 11 December 1831 in the [[Aldabra Islands]]. Her crew were rescued.<ref name=MC020632>{{Cite news |title=Ship News |newspaper=The Morning Chronicle |date=2 June 1832 |issue=19584}}</ref>
