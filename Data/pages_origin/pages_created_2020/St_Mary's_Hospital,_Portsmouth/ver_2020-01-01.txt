'''St Mary's Hospital''' is a health facility in Milton Road, [[Portsmouth]], [[Hampshire]], England. It is managed by [[Solent NHS Trust]].

==History==
The facility has its origins in the Portsea Island Union Workhouse and Infirmary which was designed by Augutus Livesay and Thomas Ellis Owen and which opened in 1846.<ref name=workhouses>{{cite web|url=http://www.workhouses.org.uk/PortseaIsland/|title=Portsea Island|publisher=Workhouses|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> The facility became St Mary's Hospital in 1930 and joined the [[National Health Service]] in 1948.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/hospitalrecords/details.asp?id=598&page=60|title= St Mary's Hospital, Portsmouth|publisher=National Archives|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> In May 2018 the trust announced plans to refurbish the buildings and turn the site into a health campus.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.portsmouth.co.uk/health/10m-project-to-make-portsmouth-hospital-into-health-hub-1-8493110|title=Â£10m project to make Portsmouth hospital into health hub|publisher=Portsmouth News|date=10 May 2018|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
