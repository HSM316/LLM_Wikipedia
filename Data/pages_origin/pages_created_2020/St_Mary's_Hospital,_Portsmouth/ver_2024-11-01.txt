'''St Mary's Hospital''' is a health facility in Milton Road, [[Portsmouth]], [[Hampshire]], England. It is managed by [[Solent NHS Trust]].

==History==
{{stack|[[File:Front of St Mary's Workhouse - geograph.org.uk - 698802.jpg|thumb|The main block of the old workhouse (now converted into flats)]]}}
The facility has its origins in the Portsea Island Union Workhouse which was designed by [[A. F. Livesay|Augustus Livesay]] and [[Thomas Ellis Owen]] and which opened in 1846.<ref name=workhouses>{{cite web|url=http://www.workhouses.org.uk/PortseaIsland/|title=Portsea Island|publisher=Workhouses|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> A new infirmary block, designed by Charles Bevis, was added in 1896.<ref name=past>{{cite web|url=https://www.pastscape.org.uk/hob.aspx?hob_id=537066&sort=2&type=&rational=a&class1=11|title=Portsea Island Union Workhouse|publisher=Pastscape|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> The infirmary block was extended by the addition of a ward block and a maternity block in 1908.<ref name=past/> Further ward blocks were added in 1911.<ref name=past/>

The facility became St Mary's Hospital in 1930 and joined the [[National Health Service]] in 1948.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/hospitalrecords/details.asp?id=598&page=60|title= St Mary's Hospital, Portsmouth|publisher=National Archives|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> The main block of the old workhouse was converted into flats and re-named "St Mary's House" in 1990.<ref>{{NHLE|desc=St Mary's House including lodge and community centre|num=1387196|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

In May 2018 the trust announced plans to refurbish the buildings and turn the site into a health campus.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.portsmouth.co.uk/health/10m-project-to-make-portsmouth-hospital-into-health-hub-1-8493110|title=Â£10m project to make Portsmouth hospital into health hub|publisher=Portsmouth News|date=10 May 2018|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
