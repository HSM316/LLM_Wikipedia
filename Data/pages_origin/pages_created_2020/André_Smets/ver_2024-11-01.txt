'''André Smets''' (5 July 1943 – 30 December 2019) was a Belgian politician, who served as Mayor of [[Herve]] from 1985 to 2010. He also sat in the [[Chamber of Representatives (Belgium)|Chamber of Representatives]] for the [[Verviers (Chamber of Representatives constituency)|Verviers constituency]] from 1999 to 2003. As a member of parliament he mainly focused on traffic, infrastructure and government-owned companies.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sudinfo.be/id158976/article/2019-12-30/andre-smets-ex-bourgmestre-de-herve-est-decede-durant-la-nuit|title=André Smets, ex-bourgmestre de Herve, est décédé durant la nuit |language=fr |date=2019-12-30 |website=sudinfo.be}}</ref>

In 1970 he was elected to Herve as a municipal councillor for the then PSC (precursor of the current [[Centre démocrate humaniste]]) and from 1977 to 1985 as an alderman. In 1985 he became mayor of the municipality, which he remained until leaving politics at the end of 2010.

Smets was a teacher by profession and from 1975 to 1999 he was head of the Institut Saint-Joseph in Herve.
