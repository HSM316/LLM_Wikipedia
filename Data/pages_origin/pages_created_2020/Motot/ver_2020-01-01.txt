'''Motot''' (also spelled '''Mwot Tot''') is a town in [[Uror County]], [[Bieh State]], [[South Sudan]]. 

== History ==

Motot was one of the Lou [[Nuer people|Nuer]] villages in which the [[Sudan People's Liberation Army|SPLA]] carried out a [[Disarmament of the Lou Nuer|forcible disarmament campaign]] in 2006. The battle of Motot which occurred near Motot was the end of the campaign and ended the [[Nuer White Army]] for several years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://issafrica.org/research/papers/sudan-peoples-liberation-army-disarmament-in-jonglei-and-its-implications|title=Sudan People's Liberation Army: Disarmament in Jonglei and its implications|last=ISSAfrica.org|date=2007-04-06|website=ISS Africa|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> [[UN peacekeepers]] were deployed in Motot after the conflict.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sudantribune.com/spip.php?article16921|title=Fragile disarmament in South Sudan - Sudan Tribune: Plural news and views on Sudan|website=www.sudantribune.com|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

In August 2011, 200 people were injured and more than 300 were killed in Motot and the village of Pieri due to [[Murle people|Murle]] attacks on the Lou Nuer.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sudantribune.com/spip.php?article39939|title=UN, US express concern as 26,000 displaced from Jonglei conflict - Sudan Tribune: Plural news and views on Sudan|website=www.sudantribune.com|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

During the [[South Sudanese Civil War]], the [[United Nations World Food Program]] conducted food airdrops in May 2014 in Motot due to food shortages.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://radiotamazuj.org/en/news/article/south-sudan-crisis-key-humanitarian-updates-3|title=South Sudan crisis: Key humanitarian updates|website=Radio Tamazuj|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> In March 2017, humanitarians left Motot due to fighting in the area.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sudantribune.com/spip.php?article61798|title=South Sudan orders withdrawal of humanitarian agencies from Mayendit - Sudan Tribune: Plural news and views on Sudan|website=www.sudantribune.com|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> On April 23, 2018, the [[SPLA-IO]] claimed government forces attacked its positions in Motot, although the government denied it was in the area.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://radiotamazuj.org/en/news/article/rebels-claim-government-attacked-them-in-akobo-govt-denies-claims|title=Rebels claim clashes with govt in Akobo, govt denies|website=Radio Tamazuj|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> In June 2019 Motot experienced heavy flooding.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://radiotamazuj.org/en/news/article/bieh-government-warns-of-looming-hunger|title=Bieh government warns of looming hunger|website=Radio Tamazuj|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

Motot was part of [[Jonglei State]] until the reorganization of states in 2015, in which Motot became part of [[Eastern Bieh State]]. Eastern Bieh State was later renamed [[Bieh State]].
