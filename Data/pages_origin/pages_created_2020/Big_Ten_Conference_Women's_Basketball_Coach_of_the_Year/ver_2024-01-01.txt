The '''Big Ten Conference Women's Basketball Coach of the Year''' is an annual [[college basketball]] award presented to the top women's basketball coach in the [[Big Ten Conference]]. The winner is selected by the Big Ten media association and conference coaches. The award was first given following the 1983–84 season to [[Tara VanDerveer]] of [[Ohio State Buckeyes women's basketball|Ohio State]]. [[Rene Portland]], [[Jim Foster (basketball)|Jim Foster]], and [[Brenda Frese]] have won the award a record four times each.
