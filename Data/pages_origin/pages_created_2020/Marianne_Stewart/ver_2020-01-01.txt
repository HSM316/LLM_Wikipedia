'''Marianne Stewart''' (born '''Annemarie Schünzel''', January 16, 1922 – November 1, 1992) was a German-American stage, film and television actress.

==Early life==
Stewart was born Annemarie Schünzel in Berlin, Germany on January 16, 1922 to [[Hanne Brinkmann]] and [[Reinhold Schünzel]]. In 1937, she and her father emigrated to the United States,<ref name=ACMB/> where she attended [[Beverly Hills High School]], graduating in 1940.<ref name=YANGTMT3>[http://www.mediafire.com/view/ain25sn07fgw6kc/.jpg/file "Young Actress Nearly Got Too Much Theater"]. ''The New York Herald Tribune''. October 22, 1944. Retrieved December 31, 2019.</ref>

==Career==
On November 1, 1940, Stewart made her uncredited screen debut in MGM's ''[[Escape (1940 film)|Escape]]'',<ref>[http://www.mediafire.com/view/ntrx9mgnfqtejlq/.jpg "Director's Child in Screen Debut"]. ''The Washington Post''. June 26, 1940. Retrieved December 31, 2019.</ref> her first credited appearance coming 2 years later in ''[[Valley of Hunted Men]]''.

Stewart's [[Broadway theater|Broadway]] debut came on October 23, 1944, when she replaced [[Annabella (actress)|Annabella]] in [[Elia Kazan]]'s production of [[Jacobowsky_und_der_Oberst_(opera)|Jacobowsky and the Colonel]], opposite [[Oscar Karlweis]] and [[Louis Calhern]].<ref name=J&TC>Quirk, David. [http://www.mediafire.com/view/kz74iopumk5brpv/.jpg 'Norway' Duo Signed to Tune Berle Show"]. ''New York Daily News''. Oct 23, 1944. Retrieved December 31, 2019.</ref> The following fall, Kazan cast Stewart, along with [[Edmund Gwenn]] and [[Montgomery Clift]], in his staging of ''You Touched Me'', [[Tennessee Williams]]' and [[Donald Windham]]'s adaptation of the [[England,_My_England_and_Other_Stories#You_Touched_Me|same-named D.H. Lawrence short story]].<ref>[http://www.mediafire.com/view/pi4gha3jdc9hsro/.jpg "Photos by European"]. ''Town and Country''. October 1945. Retrieved December 31, 2019</ref> 

Stewart was married to her erstwhile leading man, Louis Calhern, from 1946 to 1955, and later to Wilbur George Dirksing until her death.<ref name="WGD"/><ref name="LATobit"/> Stewart died of cancer on May 10, 1992, in [[Los Angeles, California]], at the age of 70.<ref name="LATobit"/>
