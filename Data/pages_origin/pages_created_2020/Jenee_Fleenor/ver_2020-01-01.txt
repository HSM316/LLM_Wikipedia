'''Jenee Fleenor''' is an American musician. In 2019, she became the first woman named the [[Country Music Association Awards|Country Music Association]]'s [[53rd Annual Country Music Association Awards|Musician of the Year]] and the first fiddle player to win the award in over 20 years.<ref name="Hight">{{cite web |last1=Hight |first1=Jewly |title=The Woman Leading The Fiddle Revival In Country Music |url=https://www.npr.org/2019/12/30/788444301/the-woman-leading-the-fiddle-revival-in-country-music |website=NPR.org |accessdate=31 December 2019 |language=en}}</ref> Fleenor performs with [[Jon Pardi]], [[Blake Shelton]], [[Steven Tyler]], [[Cody Johnson]], [[Rascal Flatts]], and more. She has written songs for many of the same musicians, as well as [[Dolly Parton]], [[Gretchen Wilson]], [[Kathy Mattea]] and more.

==Early life==

Fleenor was born in [[Springdale, Arkansas]].<ref name="GrandOleOpryBio">{{cite web |title=Jenee Fleenor |url=https://www.opry.com/artists/jenee-fleenor/ |website=Grand Ole Opry |accessdate=31 December 2019 |language=en}}</ref> She started learning to play the violin when she was three-years-old. She studied the [[Suzuki method]] of playing. She used to play alongside her parents, with her mother playing the piano and her father, the violin. Fleenor started playing the fiddle, at the age of five, after hearing the song ''[[Faded Love]]'' by [[Bob Wills]]. She became a fiddle player for the band at a local opry house when she was ten. She found influence in the fiddle playing of [[Mark O'Connor]]. She tried to dress like him when she was a girl.<ref name="Hight"/> 

She relocated to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] in 2001 to attend college. She sat in with a bluegrass band during a show and [[Larry Cordle]] asked her a few days later to perform with him. She quit college and became full time musician.<ref name="Hight"/> 

==Career==

Fleenor is a plays fiddle, mandolin and acoustic guitar. She also is a singer-songwriter.<ref name="Hight"/>

Early in her career, she toured with [[Martina McBride]] and [[Terri Clark]]. In the 2010s, she began playing mandolin and acoustic guitar to compensate for low industry demand for fiddle.<ref name="Hight"/> She was named the [[Country Music Association Awards|Country Music Association]]'s Touring Musician of the Year in 2015.<ref name="GrandOleOpryBio"/> She began performing with [[Jon Pardi]], a [[neotraditional country|neotraditional]] singer-songwriter credited with helping to keep traditional instruments in popular country. She performed fiddle on Pardi's 2019 album ''[[Heartache Medication]]'', including the song by the same title.<ref name="Hight"/> 

In 2019, she released her debut album, ''Fiddle & Steel''. That same year, she was nominated for, and awarded, the Country Music Association's Musician of the Year Award, the first for a woman.<ref name="Hight"/> That same year, she was nominated for the [[Academy of Country Music]]'s Speciality Instrument Player of the Year Award, the only woman to ever be nominated for the award.<ref name="GrandOleOpryBio"/> Fleenor currently performs with [[Steven Tyler]] and [[Blake Shelton]].<ref name="Hight"/> She also performs regularly in the house band on ''[[The Voice (American TV series)|The Voice]]''.<ref name="GrandOleOpryBio"/>
