'''Gregg Hymowitz''' (born 1966)<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2014-01-10/hymowitz-raises-600-million-to-back-activist-investors|title=Hymowitz Raises $600 Million to Back Activist Investors|last=Weiss|first=Miles|date=10 January 2014|website=www.bloomberg.com|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> is chairman, chief executive officer and founder of EnTrust Global,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.alpha-week.com/entrust-global-not-only-fund-funds|title=EnTrust Global: Not Only A Fund Of Funds {{!}} AlphaWeek|website=www.alpha-week.com|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> a diversified alternative investment firm.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-hedgefunds-investors-focus-idUSKCN1UP17T|title=Karmic reckoning? Investors in activist hedge funds agitate for change|date=2019-07-30|work=Reuters|access-date=2019-12-31|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hedgeweek.com/2019/03/07/273739/entrustpermal-rebrands-entrust-global|title=EnTrustPermal rebrands as EnTrust Global|last=|first=|date=3 July 2019|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref> He is known as an [[Activist shareholder|activist investor]], approving a $650 million investment in [[Nestlé]] in May 2017.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/the-man-behind-activist-investors-biggest-bets-1500037200|title=The Man Behind Activist Investors' Biggest Bets|last=Benoit|first=Juliet Chung and David|website=WSJ|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Hymowitz is from [[The Bellmores, New York|Bellmore]], New York.<ref name=":1" /> In 1990, he graduated [[Latin honors|cum laude]] from [[Harvard Law School]] with a JD, and received a BA, [[Phi Beta Kappa]], from the [[Binghamton University|State University of New York at Binghamton]] in 1987.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://hls.harvard.edu/event/traphagen-distinguished-alumni-speaker-series-with-gregg-hymowitz-jd90/|title=Traphagen Distinguished Alumni Speaker Series with Gregg Hymowitz JD'90|last=School|first=Harvard Law|website=Harvard Law School|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> He was the 1985 [[Harry S. Truman Scholarship|Harry S. Truman Scholar]] from New York, the 1987 [[Hansard Society|British Hansard Society Scholar]] and the 2004 recipient of the Governor's Committee on Scholastic Achievement Award.<ref name=":2" />

== Career ==
He began his career as an attorney at [[Skadden|Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom]] where he focused on mergers and acquisitions before joining [[Goldman Sachs|Goldman, Sachs & Co]]. as a vice president in 1992. In 1997 he left Goldman to establish Entrust Global (originally EnTrust Capital).<ref name=":0" /> The firm joined The [[Permal Group]] in 2016, assuming the name of EnTrustPermal, and rebranded to EnTrust Global in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.opalesque.com/industry-updates/5590/entrust-permal-rebrands-as-entrust-global.html|title=EnTrust Permal rebrands as EnTrust Global - Opalesque|last=Knab|first=Matthias|website=www.opalesque.com|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> As of February 2020, Legg Mason owned 65% of EntTrust Global. In February 2020, Leg Mason was acquired by Franklin Resources for $4.5 billion. Instead of being incorporated into Franklin Resources as a result of the deal, Hymowitz agreed to make EnTrust Global an independent, private company by buying back Legg Mason's share of EnTrust.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Lombardo|first=Justin Baer and Cara|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/franklin-resources-is-in-talks-to-buy-legg-mason-11582009251|title=WSJ News Exclusive {{!}} Franklin Resources to Buy Legg Mason for $4.5 Billion|date=2020-02-18|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2020-03-15|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newyork.citybizlist.com/article/597112|title=EnTrust Global Announces Reacquisition of Legg Mason's Interest|website=Citybizlist|access-date=2020-03-15}}</ref> He also serves as Chair of EnTrust Global's Investment Committee, Compensation Committee and Financial & Control Committee, and is a member of the Blue Ocean Executive Committee.<ref name=":2" />

EntrustPermal.<ref name="The Wall Street Journal (Deutsche Bank)">{{cite web |last1=Lombardo |first1=Cara |last2=Strasburg |first2=Jenny |title=Activist Hedge Fund Takes $620 Million Stake in |publisher=The Wall Street Journal |accessdate=20 February 2020}}</ref> EnTrust Global has a 25% stake in Alma Capital,<ref>{{Cite Hymowitz has suggested that activist investing helps investors identify the best investment opportunities.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-hedgefunds-investors-focus-idUSKCN1UP17T|title=Karmic reckoning? During the [[COVID-19 pandemic|COVID-19 crisis of 2020]], Hymowitz said that activist investing, so that one has an impact on company decisions, is a good investment strategy for the period of economic dislocation.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-hedgefunds-fundraising-idUSKBN21B3H1|title=U.S. activist investors Land & Buildings, Engaged raise new capital: sources|date=2020-03-24|work=Reuters|access-date=2020-03-26|language=en}}</ref>

== Board membership ==
Hymowitz is on the Advisory Board of [[Quibi]], a streaming video platform that offers short-form, mobile content. He was a member of the Board of Trustees at [[Montefiore Medical Center]] and served two terms as a trustee of the [[Riverdale Country School|Riverdale Country Day School]].<ref name=":2" />
