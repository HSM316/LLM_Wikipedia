'''Gregg S. Hymowitz''' (born November 16, 1965)<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |last=Weiss |first=Miles |date=10 January 2014 |title=Hymowitz Raises $600 Million to Back Activist Investors |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2014-01-10/hymowitz-raises-600-million-to-back-activist-investors |archive-url= |archive-date= |access-date=2019-12-31 |website=www.bloomberg.com}}</ref> is an American billionaire who is the chairman, chief executive officer and founder of EnTrust Global,<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |title=EnTrust Global: Not Only A Fund Of Funds {{!}} AlphaWeek |url=https://www.alpha-week.com/entrust-global-not-only-fund-funds |access-date=2019-12-31 |website=www.alpha-week.com|date=26 March 2019 }}</ref> a diversified alternative investment firm.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2019-07-30 |title=Karmic reckoning? Investors in activist hedge funds agitate for change |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-hedgefunds-investors-focus-idUSKCN1UP17T |access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last= |first= |date=3 July 2019 |title=EnTrustPermal rebrands as EnTrust Global |url=https://www.hedgeweek.com/2019/03/07/273739/entrustpermal-rebrands-entrust-global |archive-url= |archive-date= |access-date=31 December 2019 |website=}}</ref> Hymowitz is an [[activist investor]], known for his bold investment strategy. According to ''[[Forbes]]'', he has an estimated personal net worth of $1.7 billion as of May 2024.<ref name="Forbes Profile" />

== Early life and education ==
Hymowitz is from [[Bellmore, New York]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web |last=Benoit |first=Juliet Chung and David |title=The Man Behind Activist Investors' Biggest Bets |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/the-man-behind-activist-investors-biggest-bets-1500037200 |access-date=2019-12-31 |website=WSJ |language=en-US}}</ref> His father and grandfather owned a sponge manufacturing business which they lost in the 1980s, and this experience inspired him to "make sure [he] always had something to fall back on."<ref name=":7">{{Cite web |title=This Billionaire Investor Takes Advice From Usher |url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/johnhyatt/2024/03/05/this-billionaire-investor-takes-advice-from-usher/ |access-date=2024-05-14 |website=Forbes}}</ref> He received a Bachelor of Arts degree, [[Phi Beta Kappa]], from the [[Binghamton University|State University of New York at Binghamton]] in 1987. He later graduated [[Latin honors|cum laude]] from [[Harvard Law School]] with a [[Juris Doctor]] in 1990.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web |title=Traphagen Distinguished Alumni Speaker Series with Gregg Hymowitz JD'90 |url=https://hls.harvard.edu/event/traphagen-distinguished-alumni-speaker-series-with-gregg-hymowitz-jd90/ |access-date=2019-12-31 |website=Harvard Law School |language=en}}</ref> He was the 1985 [[Harry S. Truman Scholarship|Harry S. Truman Scholar]] from New York, the 1987 [[Hansard Society|British Hansard Society Scholar]] and the 2004 recipient of the Governor's Committee on Scholastic Achievement Award.<ref name=":2" />

== Career ==
=== Early career ===
He began his career as an attorney at [[Skadden|Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom]] where he focused on mergers and acquisitions before joining [[Goldman Sachs|Goldman, Sachs & Co]]. as a vice president in 1992.<ref name=":0" />

=== Entrust Global ===
In 1997, he left Goldman to establish Entrust Global (originally EnTrust Capital).<ref name=":0" /> When founding Entrust, Hymowitz and his colleagues personally handled asset gathering and investment decisions to build a small but strong portfolio.<ref>{{Cite news |date=1997-05-09 |title=Three Goldman Managers Start a Firm |language=en-US |work=The New York Times |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1997/05/09/business/three-goldman-managers-start-a-firm.html |access-date=2023-05-05 |issn=0362-4331}}</ref> By the end of the year, the firm had over $1.1 billion in assets and was continuing to expand.<ref>{{Cite web |last= |first= |date= |title=Entrust Global, Gregg Hymowitz and Michael Horowitz. |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB88257456061806500 |archive-url= |archive-date= |access-date=2021-01-21 |website=www.wsj.com |language=en-US}}</ref> In 2016, [[Legg Mason]] purchased a 65% stake in EnTrust for $400 million, with Hymowitz also retaining a 35% stake.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Staff |first=Verdict |date=2016-01-25 |title=Legg Mason to merge Permal hedge fund platform with EnTrust Capital |url=https://www.privatebankerinternational.com/news/legg-mason-to-merge-permal-group-with-entrust-capital-250116-4792401/ |access-date=2023-05-05 |website=Private Banker International |language=en-US}}</ref> Under Legg Mason, EnTrust was merged with [[Permal Group]], assuming the name of EnTrustPermal.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Legg Mason completes merger of Permal and EnTrust Capital – Baltimore Sun |url=https://www.baltimoresun.com/business/bs-bz-entrustpermal-transaction-20160502-story.html |access-date=2023-05-05 |website=www.baltimoresun.com|date=2 May 2016 }}</ref> The firm rebranded to EnTrust Global in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Knab |first=Matthias |title=EnTrust Permal rebrands as EnTrust Global - Opalesque |url=https://www.opalesque.com/industry-updates/5590/entrust-permal-rebrands-as-entrust-global.html |access-date=2019-12-31 |website=www.opalesque.com}}</ref> As of 2024, it had $18.8 billion in assets under management.<ref name="Forbes Profile" />

In February 2020, Legg Mason was acquired by Franklin Resources for $4.5 billion. Instead of being incorporated into Franklin Resources as a result of the deal, Hymowitz agreed to reacquire EnTrust by buying back Legg Mason's share of EnTrust.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Lombardo |first=Justin Baer and Cara |date=2020-02-18 |title=WSJ News Exclusive {{!}} Franklin Resources to Buy Legg Mason for $4.5 Billion |language=en-US |work=Wall Street Journal |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/franklin-resources-is-in-talks-to-buy-legg-mason-11582009251 |access-date=2020-03-15 |issn=0099-9660}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=EnTrust Global Announces Reacquisition of Legg Mason's Interest |url=https://newyork.citybizlist.com/article/597112 |access-date=2020-03-15 |website=Citybizlist}}</ref> Hymowitz later sold a 20% minority stake in EnTrust to the [[Brunei Investment Agency|Brunei Sovereign Wealth Fund]].<ref name="Forbes Profile">{{Cite web |title=Gregg Hymowitz |url=https://www.forbes.com/profile/gregg-hymowitz/ |access-date=2024-05-12 |website=Forbes |language=en}}</ref>

EnTrust Global and GMF Capital formed EG Acquisitions, a blank-check [[special-purpose acquisition company]]. EG Acquisitions filed for a $250 million [[IPO]] in April 2021.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-04-06 |title=EG Acquisition Corp. Locks Up Founder Shares for 3 Years |url=https://spacinsider.com/2021/04/05/eg-acquisition-corp-3-yr-founder-shares-lockup/ |access-date=2021-06-27 |website=SPACInsider |language=en-US}}</ref> In October 2022, it was announced that EG Acquisitions would merge with [[private jet]] company [[Tommy Sowers#flyExclusive - President and Chief Operating Officer|flyExclusive]], taking the company public with a combined value of $600 million.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Thomas |first=Lauren |title=WSJ News Exclusive {{!}} FlyExclusive to Go Public Through $600 Million SPAC Merger |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/flyexclusive-strikes-spac-deal-to-go-public-at-600-million-valuation-11666009311 |access-date=2022-10-31 |website=WSJ |language=en-US}}</ref>

In 2018, a large portion of the capital Hudson Executive Capital LP acquired for its stake in [[Deutsche Bank]] investment came from Hymowitz's EntrustPermal.<ref name="wsj_Q114686096">{{Cite Q|Q114686096|access-date=2022-10-26}}</ref><ref name="The Wall Street Journal bank exit">{{cite web |last1=Strasburg |first1=Jenny |title=Deutsche Bank to Exit Global Equities, Trading Business |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/deutsche-bank-to-exit-global-equities-trading-business-11562512302?shareToken=st4d78d299920a4414bf48249a3f7742ae |accessdate=20 February 2020 |website=The Wall Street Journal }}</ref> EnTrust Global has a 25% stake in Alma Capital,<ref>{{Cite news |title=DWS sells global hedge fund Ucits arm to alts specialist |language=en |work=Wealth Manager |url=https://citywireselector.com/news/dws-sells-global-hedge-fund-ucits-arm-to-alts-specialist/a1240229 |access-date=2020-03-22}}</ref> and is a co-investor with the US-based activist firm [[Third Point|Third Point LLC]], with a $650 million stake in [[Nestlé]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=2018-11-01 |title=Ex-JPMorgan CFO's activist fund takes stake in Deutsche Bank |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-deutsche-bank-investor-idUSKCN1N64I5 |access-date=2020-03-22}}</ref>

EnTrust Global has also provided capital to Politan Capital Management, through which it invested in healthcare companies [[Centene Corporation]] in 2021,<ref>{{Cite web |last=Chung |first=Cara Lombardo and Juliet |title=WSJ News Exclusive {{!}} Politan Capital Management Has Roughly $900 Million Stake in Centene |url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/politan-capital-management-has-roughly-900-million-stake-in-centene-11635966370 |access-date=2023-05-05 |website=WSJ |language=en-US}}</ref> and [[Masimo]] in 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Brennan |first=Peter J. |date=2022-10-18 |title=Activist Investor Ups Masimo Stake, Pressure |url=https://www.ocbj.com/healthcare/activist-investor-ups-masimo-stake-pressure/ |access-date=2023-05-05 |website=Orange County Business Journal |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Investment style==
Hymowitz is an activist investor, and has commented on activist investing's potential to help investors identify the best investment opportunities.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2019-07-30 |title=Karmic reckoning? Investors in activist hedge funds agitate for change |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-hedgefunds-investors-focus-idUSKCN1UP17T |access-date=2020-03-01}}</ref> He has become known for his use of concentrated investments and "bold" investment strategies. ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'' described him as being "behind some of Wall Street’s biggest activist bets".<ref name=":1" /> During the [[2007–2008 financial crisis]], his investment strategy with EnTrust evolved to incorporate more co-investments and single-idea investments based on dislocations.<ref name=":7" />

In 2013, Hymowitz, as head of EnTrust Capital, raised approximately $600 million for funds controlled by shareholder activists.<ref name="Bloomberg ($600 million)">{{cite news |last1=Weiss |first1=Miles |date=10 January 2014 |title=Hymowitz Raises $600 Million to Back Activist Investors |website=Bloomberg |publisher=Bloomberg |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2014-01-10/hymowitz-raises-600-million-to-back-activist-investors |accessdate=20 February 2020}}</ref> During the [[COVID-19 pandemic|COVID-19 crisis of 2020]], Hymowitz stated that activist investing, by enabling the investor to have an impact on company decisions, is a good investment strategy for periods of economic dislocation.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2020-03-24 |title=U.S. activist investors Land & Buildings, Engaged raise new capital: sources |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-hedgefunds-fundraising-idUSKBN21B3H1 |access-date=2020-03-26}}</ref> Hymowitz has summarized his business philosophy as "evolve or evaporate", a motto he adopted after a conversation with singer [[Usher (musician)|Usher]].<ref name=":1" />

== Personal life ==
Hymowitz was married to Debby Hymowitz, with whom he had three children, until 2007. Hymowitz later married Marcella Guarino Hymowitz in 2013, and they have three children.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2015-09-23 |title=Meet the Prom Queen of Instagram |url=http://www.thecut.com/2015/09/lilli-hymowitz-prom-queen-of-instagram.html |access-date=2021-01-21 |website=The Cut |language=en}}</ref> In 2021, Hymowitz and his wife Marcella purchased five acres of ocean-front property in [[Parrot Cay]] from [[Bruce Willis]] for $6 million.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2021-09-29 |title=Investor makes $6M deal with Bruce Willis for Turks and Caicos estate |url=https://nypost.com/2021/09/29/investor-gregg-hymowitz-buys-from-bruce-willis-in-turks/ |access-date=2021-10-28 |website=New York Post |language=en-US}}</ref>
