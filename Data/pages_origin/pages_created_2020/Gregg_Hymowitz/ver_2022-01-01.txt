'''Gregg S. Hymowitz''' (born November 16, 1965)<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2014-01-10/hymowitz-raises-600-million-to-back-activist-investors|title=Hymowitz Raises $600 Million to Back Activist Investors|last=Weiss|first=Miles|date=10 January 2014|website=www.bloomberg.com|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> is chairman, chief executive officer and founder of EnTrust Global,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.alpha-week.com/entrust-global-not-only-fund-funds|title=EnTrust Global: Not Only A Fund Of Funds {{!}} AlphaWeek|website=www.alpha-week.com|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> a diversified alternative investment firm.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-hedgefunds-investors-focus-idUSKCN1UP17T|title=Karmic reckoning? Investors in activist hedge funds agitate for change|date=2019-07-30|work=Reuters|access-date=2019-12-31|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hedgeweek.com/2019/03/07/273739/entrustpermal-rebrands-entrust-global|title=EnTrustPermal rebrands as EnTrust Global|last=|first=|date=3 July 2019|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref> He is known as an [[Activist shareholder|activist investor]], approving a $650 million investment in [[Nestlé]] in May 2017.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/the-man-behind-activist-investors-biggest-bets-1500037200|title=The Man Behind Activist Investors' Biggest Bets|last=Benoit|first=Juliet Chung and David|website=WSJ|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Hymowitz is from [[The Bellmores, New York|Bellmore]], New York.<ref name=":1" /> In 1990, he graduated [[Latin honors|cum laude]] from [[Harvard Law School]] with a JD, and received a BA, [[Phi Beta Kappa]], from the [[Binghamton University|State University of New York at Binghamton]] in 1987.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://hls.harvard.edu/event/traphagen-distinguished-alumni-speaker-series-with-gregg-hymowitz-jd90/|title=Traphagen Distinguished Alumni Speaker Series with Gregg Hymowitz JD'90|last=School|first=Harvard Law|website=Harvard Law School|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> He was the 1985 [[Harry S. Truman Scholarship|Harry S. Truman Scholar]] from New York, the 1987 [[Hansard Society|British Hansard Society Scholar]] and the 2004 recipient of the Governor's Committee on Scholastic Achievement Award.<ref name=":2" />

== Career ==
He began his career as an attorney at [[Skadden|Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom]] where he focused on mergers and acquisitions before joining [[Goldman Sachs|Goldman, Sachs & Co]]. as a vice president in 1992. In 1997 he left Goldman to establish Entrust Global (originally EnTrust Capital),<ref name=":0" /> which had over $1.1 billion in assets by December of that year.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Entrust Global, Gregg Hymowitz and Michael Horowitz.|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB88257456061806500|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-21|website=www.wsj.com|language=en-US}}</ref> The firm joined The Permal Group in 2016, an [[investment management]] firm which was acquired and operated as a subsidiary by Legg Mason since 2005, assuming the name of EnTrustPermal, and rebranded to EnTrust Global in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.opalesque.com/industry-updates/5590/entrust-permal-rebrands-as-entrust-global.html|title=EnTrust Permal rebrands as EnTrust Global - Opalesque|last=Knab|first=Matthias|website=www.opalesque.com|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> 

In 2016, the firm launched a maritime lending vehicle. As of March 2019, they have more than fifty vessels financed with a size of an investment of over a billion dollars in commitments, of which $600 has been deployed, followed by the aviation business which provides direct lending to aircraft companies, taking advantage of the lack of bank lenders in the maritime sector.<ref>{{Cite web|title=EnTrust Global: Not Only A Fund Of Funds {{!}} AlphaWeek|url=https://www.alpha-week.com/entrust-global-not-only-fund-funds|access-date=2021-01-21|website=www.alpha-week.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-03-07|title=EnTrustPermal rebrands as EnTrust Global {{!}} Hedgeweek|url=http://www.hedgeweek.com/2019/03/07/273739/entrustpermal-rebrands-entrust-global|access-date=2021-01-21|website=www.hedgeweek.com|language=en}}</ref> 

As of February 2020, [[Legg Mason]] owned 65% of EntTrust Global. In February 2020, Leg Mason was acquired by Franklin Resources for $4.5 billion. Instead of being incorporated into Franklin Resources as a result of the deal, Hymowitz agreed to make EnTrust Global an independent, private company by buying back Legg Mason's share of EnTrust.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Lombardo|first=Justin Baer and Cara|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/franklin-resources-is-in-talks-to-buy-legg-mason-11582009251|title=WSJ News Exclusive {{!}} Franklin Resources to Buy Legg Mason for $4.5 Billion|date=2020-02-18|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2020-03-15|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newyork.citybizlist.com/article/597112|title=EnTrust Global Announces Reacquisition of Legg Mason's Interest|website=Citybizlist|access-date=2020-03-15}}</ref> In April 2020, EnTrust launched Blue Sky strategy for investment in the global aviation industry which has raised $400 million.<ref>{{Cite web|title=EnTrust Global Completes Reacquisition of Legg Mason's Interest {{!}} Hellenic Shipping News Worldwide|url=https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/entrust-global-completes-reacquisition-of-legg-masons-interest/|access-date=2021-01-20|website=www.hellenicshippingnews.com}}</ref>

He also serves as Chair of EnTrust Global's Investment Committee, Compensation Committee and Financial & Control Committee, and is a member of the Blue Ocean Executive Committee.<ref name=":2" /> In December 2020, EnTrust Global closed a $2.1 billion fundraising for the Blue Ocean Fund.<ref>{{Cite web|last=says|first=Capt Gautam Ramaswamy|date=2020-12-10|title=Julian Proctor helps mastermind $2.1bn clean newbuild fundraising|url=https://splash247.com/julian-proctor-helps-mastermind-2-1bn-clean-newbuild-fundraising/|access-date=2021-01-21|website=Splash247|language=en-GB}}</ref>

EnTrust Global and GMF Capital formed EG Acquisitions, a blank-check [[special-purpose acquisition company]]. EG Acquisitions filed for a $250 million [[IPO]] in April 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-04-06|title=EG Acquisition Corp. Locks Up Founder Shares for 3 Years|url=https://spacinsider.com/2021/04/05/eg-acquisition-corp-3-yr-founder-shares-lockup/|access-date=2021-06-27|website=SPACInsider|language=en-US}}</ref>

EnTrust Global raised an additional $925 million for the Blue Ocean Fund between April and June 2021, and announced plans to acquire Maas Capital from Dutch bank [[ABN Amro]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Dixon (g_dixon)|first=Gary|date=2021-06-08|title=EnTrust expands again in deal to buy Maas Capital from ABN Amro {{!}} TradeWinds|url=https://www.tradewindsnews.com/finance/entrust-expands-again-in-deal-to-buy-maas-capital-from-abn-amro/2-1-1021695|access-date=2021-06-27|website=TradeWinds {{!}} Latest shipping and maritime news|language=en}}</ref>

Hymowitz manages Jolli Four, LLC, a real estate firm based in [[Wellington, Florida]]. In 2021, the firm sold an equestrian estate in Wellington to equestrian rider Logan Marksbury for $12 million.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Bandell|first=Brian|date=October 21, 2021|title=Firm tied to software developer buys Wellington equestrian estate for $12M (Photos)|url=https://www.bizjournals.com/southflorida/news/2021/10/21/wellington-equestrian-estate-sells-for-12m.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-10-28|website=www.bizjournals.com}}</ref>

== Philanthropy ==
In 1995, Hymowitz established the Hymowitz Scholarship, which is awarded to full-time undergraduate students of [[Harpur College]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Herbst-Bayliss|first=Svea|date=2019-07-30|title=Karmic reckoning? Investors in activist hedge funds agitate for change|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-hedgefunds-investors-focus-idUSKCN1UP17T|access-date=2021-01-21}}</ref> Hymowitz endowed the Hymowitz Professorship of Global Health at [[Duke University]], which was named after him. In 2017, Professor David Boyd became the inaugural holder of the chair.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Herbst-Bayliss|first=Svea|date=2020-03-24|title=U.S. activist investors Land & Buildings, Engaged raise new capital: sources|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-hedgefunds-fundraising-idUSKBN21B3H1|access-date=2021-01-21}}</ref>

Hymowitz participated in the [[FTI Consulting]] Great Charity Challenge which raised $2.5 million for Florida charities.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Binghamton University Foundation Scholarships, Awards and Fellowships|url=https://www.binghamton.edu:8443/exist/rest/bulletin/2011-2012/11_scholarships_etc/scholarshipsAwardsAndFellowships.xml?_xsl=/bulletin/2011-2012/xsl/compose.xsl|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref> He has also donated to the [[National Multiple Sclerosis Society]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=David Boyd Named Hymowitz Professor of the Practice of Global Health|url=https://bassconnections.duke.edu/about/news/david-boyd-named-hymowitz-professor-practice-global-health|access-date=2021-01-21|website=Bass Connections|language=en}}</ref> [[New York Cares]],<ref>{{Cite web|last=Horsetalk.co.nz|date=2014-02-06|title=Showjumpers earn $2.5m for charity in single competition|url=https://www.horsetalk.co.nz/2014/02/07/showjumpers-earn-2m-charity-competition/|access-date=2021-01-21|website=Horsetalk.co.nz|language=en-US}}</ref> and The Retreat, a non-profit organization in [[The Hamptons]] which provides support to victims of domestic violence.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=NEW YORK CITY - SOUTHERN NEW YORK CHAPTER|url=https://www.nationalmssociety.org/NationalMSSociety/media/New-York-City-Southern-New-York/Stay%20Informed/MSC_Spring2015_Web.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref>

He was on the committee for the 2017 [[YAGP]] "Stars of Today Meet the Stars of Tomorrow Gala",<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=New Beginnings - 2005|url=http://www.theretreatinc.org/newsletters/2005_spring.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref> and is part of the New York Leadership Circle for Mikva Challenge, a non-partisan civic engagement organization.<ref>{{Cite web|title=STARS OF TODAY MEET THE STARS OF TOMORROW GALA 2018 - Ballet Competition -|url=https://yagp.org/stars-of-today-meet-the-stars-of-tomorrow-gala-2017/|access-date=2021-01-21|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Board membership ==
Hymowitz is on the Advisory Board of [[Quibi]], a streaming video platform that offers short-form, mobile content. He was a member of the Board of Trustees at [[Montefiore Medical Center]] and served two terms as a trustee of the [[Riverdale Country School|Riverdale Country Day School]].<ref name=":2" />

== Personal life ==
Gregg was married to Debby Hymowitz until 2004, with whom he had three children. Hymowitz later married Marcella Guarino Hymowitz, and they have a three children.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2015-09-23|title=Meet the Prom Queen of Instagram|url=http://www.thecut.com/2015/09/lilli-hymowitz-prom-queen-of-instagram.html|access-date=2021-01-21|website=The Cut|language=en}}</ref> In 2021, Hymowitz and his wife Marcella purchased five acres of ocean-front property in [[Parrot Cay]] from [[Bruce Willis]] for $6 million.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-09-29|title=Investor makes $6M deal with Bruce Willis for Turks and Caicos estate|url=https://nypost.com/2021/09/29/investor-gregg-hymowitz-buys-from-bruce-willis-in-turks/|access-date=2021-10-28|website=New York Post|language=en-US}}</ref>
