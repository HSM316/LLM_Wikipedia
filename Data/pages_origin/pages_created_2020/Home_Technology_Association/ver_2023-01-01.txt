The '''Home Technology Association''' is an American industry organization for home technology integration dealerships.

==Certification==
The Home Technology Association runs the HTA Certified [[certification program]], which began in 2017. Certification is applied for by home technology integration firms in the United States and Canada,<ref name=Rest>{{Cite web|url=https://restechtoday.com/home-technology-association/|title=HTA Gains Ground in Home Tech Professional Certification Initiative|date=July 9, 2019}}</ref> and indicates the top 10% to 15% of home technology installation dealers within a particular market.<ref name=Resid>{{Cite web|url=https://www.residentialsystems.com/news/home-technology-association-looks-to-certify-top-integrators|title=Home Technology Association Looks to Certify Top Integrators|date=August 31, 2017|website=Residential Systems}}</ref> In addition to running the certification process,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dealerscope.com/article/in-first-full-year-hta-reports-strong-growth-and-engagement/|title=In First Full Year, HTA Reports Strong Growth and Engagement|first=Rob|last=Stott|website=Dealerscope}}</ref> the program also administers the "Best in the US" awards for the home technology integration industry and is an industry advocate for the home technology integration trade to architects, interior designers and general contractors.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cepro.com/audio-video/hta_launches_best_in_the_u-s-_awards_program/|title=HTA Launches 'Best in the U.S.' Awards Program|date=August 29, 2018|website=CEPRO}}</ref>

HTA certification is provided by the Home Technology Association to home technology integration dealers, and is based upon installation experience and business practices,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.connecteddesign.com/article/hta-summit-signals-strength/ |title=HTA Summit Signals Strength - Connected Design |access-date=2019-12-31 |archive-date=2019-12-31 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191231083224/https://www.connecteddesign.com/article/hta-summit-signals-strength/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> including a history of technical competence, marketplace reputation, and quality of aftercare service and support. Additional criteria for certification includes a five year absence of [[bankruptcy]], proper [[liability insurance]], and others.<ref name=Rest /> Certified dealers are provided with resources such as a budgeting tool for dealers to set transparent pricing,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cepro.com/news/home-technology-association-summit/|title=HTA Summit Focuses on Customer Experience|date=October 4, 2019|website=CEPRO}}</ref> in addition to other onboarding tools.<ref name=Rest />

===Tools===
In 2017 the Home Technology Association’s technology created a budget estimator tool for home design and build employees and consumers, in order to provide brand agnostic installed-cost estimates for home technology systems.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cepro.com/business-support/smart-home-technology-cost-hta-budget-calculator/ |title=Determine Your Clients’ Smart Home Technology Budget - HTA Budget Calculator available as free tool on cepro.com to help integrators guide clients through smart home technology budgeting process. |date=October 19, 2021 |publisher=CEPro |author=Jason Knott}}</ref>
