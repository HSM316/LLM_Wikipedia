This is a '''[[Timeline of the Anglophone Crisis|timeline]]''' of the '''[[Anglophone Crisis]]''' during 2020.

The [[Anglophone Cameroonian|Anglophone]] Crisis is an [[List of ongoing armed conflicts|ongoing armed conflict]] in the [[Cameroon|Republic of Cameroon]] in [[Central Africa]], where historically [[Cameroonian English|English-speaking]] [[Ambazonia|Ambazonian]] separatists are seeking the independence of the former British trust territory of [[Southern Cameroons]], which has been unified with Cameroon since 1961.

==January==
* On January 1, suspected separatists burned down the office of the Divisional Officer of [[Muyuka]], after clashing with soldiers in the area. In [[Buea]], separatists clashed with Cameroonian soldiers and burned a vehicle.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-ambazonia-boys-set-fire-on-muyukas-d-os-office/ Cameroon: Suspected Ambazonia boys set ablaze Muyuka's D.O's office], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 3, 2020. Accessed Jan 3, 2020.</ref> In Donga Mantung, six people were killed in a Cameroonian raid.<ref name="crisisgroupjan2020"/> In [[Bamessing]], Cameroonian forces started a four-day clean-up of road blocks mounted by separatists.<ref>[https://allafrica.com/stories/202001070120.html Cameroon: Ngoketunjia Division – Villagers Vent Anger On Fighters, Seize Weapons], AllAfrica, Jan 6, 2020. Accessed Jan 7, 2020.</ref> The same day, the Ambazonia Military Forces declared that a lockdown encompassing all of Southern Cameroons would take place between February 7 and 12, in order to prevent the [[2020 Cameroonian parliamentary election]] from taking place in the Anglophone regions. The separatists declared that anyone caught outdoors in cities and villages during the lockdown would be considered an enemy, and stated that the declaration had been issued weeks in advance in order to give people time stock up food and prepare.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-2020-twin-polls-separatists-announce-five-day-lockdown-in-anglophone-regions/ Cameroon/2020 twin polls: Separatists announce five-day lockdown in Anglophone regions], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 3, 2020. Accessed Jan 3, 2020.</ref>
* On January 2, civilians in [[Balikumbat (village)|Balikumbat]] demonstrated against the separatists, accusing them of forcing people to give them money and of bringing insecurity to the village. The protest was guarded by Cameroonian soldiers.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-youths-in-balikumbat-go-wild-over-separatists-excesses/ Cameroon: Youths in Balikumbat go wild over separatists “excesses”], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 3, 2020. Accessed Jan 3, 2020.</ref>
* On January 4, residents of Balikumbat stormed nearby separatist camp, seized weapons and arrested fighters.<ref>[https://newsghana.com.gh/weapons-of-separatists-seized-by-angry-villagers-in-cameroons-troubled-anglophone-region/ Weapons of separatists seized by angry villagers in Cameroon's troubled Anglophone region], News Ghana, Jan 5, 2020. Accessed Jan 5, 2020.</ref>
* On January 5, [[Fon (title)|Fon]] Ndofoa Zofoa III of [[Babungo (village)|Babungo]] gave separatist fighters in the vicinity 24 hours to release villagers they had abducted, threatening to expel the fighters' families from the village. He also urged villagers to assist the Cameroonian security forces if they could.<ref>[http://cameroonnewsagency.com/babungo-traditional-ruler-bans-ambazonia-activities-in-his-village/ Babungo Traditional Ruler Bans Ambazonia Activities In His Village], Cameroon News Agency, Jan 6, 2020. Accessed Jan 6, 2020.</ref> In response to the ultimatum, armed separatists attempted to storm the palace and abduct the Fon, but villagers intervened and stopped the separatists.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-population-foils-separatist-attempt-to-kidnap-fon-in-nw-region/ Cameroon: Population foils separatist attempt to kidnap fon in NW Region], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 7, 2020. Accessed Jan 7, 2020.</ref> In [[Babessi]], separatist fighters abducted the [[Social Democratic Front (Cameroon)|SDF]] mayor along with at least four councillors.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-babessi-mayor-four-councillors-abducted-in-restive-nw-region/ Cameroon: Babessi mayor, four councillors abducted in restive NW region], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 9, 2020. Accessed Jan 9, 2020.</ref> In [[Ndu, Cameroon|Ndu]], a detainee died after being tortured by Cameroonian soldiers.<ref>[http://cameroonnewsagency.com/donga-mantung-soldiers-torture-one-detainee-to-death-others-transferred-to-bamenda/ Donga Mantung: Soldiers Torture One Detainee To Death, Others Transferred to Bamenda], Cameroon News Agency, Jan 5, 2020. Accessed Jan 6, 2020.</ref>
* On January 6, "General Divine", a separatist commander based in [[Meme (department)|Meme Division]], was killed by his own men, who accused him of terrorizing nearby communities. According to sources, fellow separatists had warned him many times not to commit atrocities against civilians, and when he did not stop, they finally decided to get rid of him.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroonambazonia-general-killed-by-peers-in-sw-region/ Cameroon:’Ambazonia General’ killed by peers in SW Region], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 6, 2020. Accessed Jan 6, 2020.</ref> Another commander was also killed during the incident.<ref>[https://newsghana.com.gh/infighting-between-separatists-kills-2-in-cameroons-restive-anglophone-region/ Infighting between separatists kills 2 in Cameroon's restive Anglophone region], News Ghana, Jan 7, 2020. Accessed Jan 7, 2020.</ref> Meanwhile, in anticipation of the upcoming February 9 election, 350 Cameroonian soldiers were deployed to various locations in [[Southwest Region (Cameroon)|Southwest Region]] to protect voters.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-february-twin-polls-350-soldiers-deployed-to-restive-south-west-region/ Cameroon/February twin polls: 350 soldiers deployed to restive South West region], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 7, 2020. Accessed Jan 7, 2020.</ref>
* On January 7, nine suspected separatists arriving on bikes stormed a gendarme post in [[Bangourain]], [[West Region (Cameroon)|West Region]]. The sole gendarme on duty ran away, enabling the attackers to capture weapons and ammunition without encountering any resistance.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-armed-men-attack-gendarmerie-unit-in-west-region-do-away-with-arms/ Cameroon: Armed men attack Gendarmerie Unit in West region, do away with arms], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 7, 2020. Accessed Jan 7, 2020.</ref> In [[Donga-Mantung]], separatists burned down the ELECAM office in Misaje, as part of their campaign to disrupt the February election.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-suspected-separatists-set-ablaze-elecam-office-in-misaje-nw-region/ Cameroon: Suspected separatists set ablaze ELECAM office in Misaje, NW region], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 8, 2020. Accessed Jan 8, 2020.</ref>
* By January 8, there had been at least five instances where villagers had attacked separatist camps. Separatist leaders openly condemned war crimes committed by fellow separatists, and the [[Ambazonia Defence Forces]] ordered its fighters to arrest anyone caught terrorizing civilians. Separatists also claimed that some of the separatist excesses were actually false-flag actions conducted by Cameroonian forces, an allegation strongly denied by Cameroon.<ref name="voajan82020">[https://www.voanews.com/africa/cameroon-anglophone-villagers-attack-separatist-camps-over-abuse Cameroon Anglophone Villagers Attack Separatist Camps Over Abuse], Voice of America, Jan 8, 2020. Accessed Jan 8, 2020.</ref> In [[Meme (department)|Meme Division]], at least five separatist fighters laid down their arms.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-more-ambazonia-fighters-drop-arms-in-meme/ Cameroon: More Ambazonia fighters drop arms in Meme], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 9, 2020. Accessed Jan 9, 2020.</ref>
[[File:Cameroon gendarmes arrive in the English speaking town of Buea, Cameroon, January 9, 2020.jpg|thumb|Gendarmes arriving in Buea, January 9.]]
* On January 9, 350 gendarmes deployed in Northwest Region to counter the separatist effort to sabotage the upcoming elections, following a similar deployment in Southwest Region.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-350-gendarmes-deployed-to-restive-nw-region-ahead-of-twin-polls/ Cameroon: 350 gendarmes deployed to restive NW region ahead of twin polls], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 10, 2020. Accessed Jan 10, 2020.</ref> In [[Santa, Cameroon|Santa]], Cameroonian soldiers invaded the village Mbenjom Awing, killing two separatist fighters, one civilian as well as livestock, and burning down several houses.<ref>[http://cameroonnewsagency.com/soldiers-burn-houses-kill-civilian-livestock-in-awing/ Soldiers Burn Houses, Kill Civilian, Livestock In Awing], Cameroon News Agency, Jan 10, 2020. Accessed Jan 10, 2020.</ref>
* On January 10, five soldiers of the [[Rapid Intervention Battalion, Cameroon|Rapid Intervention Battalion]] (BIR) were arrested for abducting a man in Buea.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/cameroun-des-soldats-du-bir-accuses-du-kidnapping-dun-homme-cameroun/ Cameroun : des soldats du Bir accusés du kidnapping d'un homme], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 12, 2020. Accessed Jan 12, 2020. (French)</ref>
* On January 11, a prominent officer of the [[Red Dragon (Ambazonian militia)|Red Dragon militia]] was killed while leading an attack on Cameroonian forces in [[Menji]].<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-field-marshals-aid-killed-in-gun-battle-in-lebialem/ Cameroon: Field Marshal's aid killed in gun battle in Lebialem], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 13, 2020. Accessed Jan 13, 2020.</ref>
* On January 12, suspected separatists burned down the residence of [[Cameroon People's Democratic Movement|CPDM]] politician Kennedy Acha in Bamenda.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-residence-of-cpdm-bigwig-set-ablaze-in-bamenda/ Cameroon: Residence of CPDM bigwig set ablaze in Bamenda], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 13, 2020. Accessed Jan 13, 2020.</ref>
* On January 13, separatists ambushed and killed one Cameroonian soldier and injured another in Muyuka.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-suspected-ambazonia-fighters-kill-soldier-in-muyuka/ Cameroon: Suspected Ambazonia fighters kill soldier in Muyuka], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 14, 2020. Accessed Jan 14, 2020.</ref>
* On January 14, Cameroonian troops raided the villages of Bangem and Babubock, burning dozens of houses.<ref name="amnestyfeb62020"/>
* On January 15, separatist fighters ambushed and killed a Cameroonian soldier in [[Bamenda]]. The soldier's girlfriend was also wounded in the incident.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-suspected-amba-fighters-kill-soldier-in-bamenda/ Cameroon: Suspected Amba fighters kill soldier in Bamenda], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 16, 2020. Accessed Jan 16, 2020.</ref> In Bamenda, separatists attacked two civilians who had attempted to circumvent a separatist checkpoint, killing one of them and injuring the other.<ref name="amnestyfeb62020">[https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2020/02/cameroon-rise-in-killings-in-anglophone-regions/ Cameroon: Rise in killings in Anglophone regions ahead of parliamentary elections], Amnesty International, Feb 6, 2020. Accessed Feb 8, 2020.</ref>
* On January 16, separatists ambushed Cameroonian soldiers in [[Mamfe]]. The death toll was not revealed.<ref name="crisisgroupjan2020"/>
* On January 18, a gun battle occurred between Cameroonian soldiers and separatist fighters in Mulang, Bamenda, after the separatists had set fire to a garbage truck. The separatists eventually withdrew to their base in Ntasen.<ref>[http://cameroonnewsagency.com/ambazonia-fighters-set-hysacam-truck-on-fire/ Ambazonia Fighters Set HYSACAM Truck On Fire], Cameroon News Agency, Jan 18, 2020. Accessed Jan 18, 2020.</ref> In Meluf, six dead bodies were discovered by villagers. Local sources reported that the dead were [[Ambazonia Defence Forces|ADF]] fighters who had been abducted and killed by the [[Southern Cameroons Restoration Forces]]. It was also reported that the six dead were among a total of 40 ADF fighters who had been abducted. ADF spokesman Tapang Ivo Tanku confirmed the loss. Both the ADF and the Southern Cameroons Restoration Forces condemned each other, while both claimed that the infighting owed to infiltration by the Cameroonian Army. The Cameroonian Army denied this claim.<ref name="voainfightingjan"/>
* On January 19, "General Chacha" of the Southern Cameroons Restoration Forces posted a video where he demanded that all separatist groups submit to his militia.<ref name="voainfightingjan">[https://www.voanews.com/africa/cameroons-anglophone-separatists-turn-infighting Cameroon's Anglophone Separatists Turn to Infighting], Voice of America, Jan 22, 2020. Accessed Jan 23, 2020.</ref>
* On January 21, suspected separatist abducted 24 school children in [[Kumba]]. Security forces rescued the hostages in an operation later the same day, "neutralizing" two of the abductors in the process.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-15-kids-earlier-kidnapped-rescued-by-military-men-in-sw/ Cameroon: 24 kids earlier kidnapped, rescued by security forces in SW], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 21, 2020. Accessed Jan 21, 2020.</ref> In [[Kondengui Central Prison]], other prisoners broke into the cells of [[Sisiku Julius Ayuk Tabe]] and nine other Ambazonian leaders serving a life sentence, stealing food and money. The victims wrote a letter in which they stated the prison administration had been silent on the matter, despite there being witnesses, and they claimed that there was a conspiracy threaten their lives.<ref>[http://cameroonnewsagency.com/jailed-ambazonia-leaders-fear-for-their-lives/ Jailed Ambazonia Leaders Fear For Their Lives], Cameroon News Agency, Jan 26, 2020. Accessed Jan 26, 2020.</ref>
* On January 22, a soldier was killed in Ndoh. Cameroonian forces retaliated the next day, carrying out a mass shooting inside a market. At least 16 people were killed, and at least five were wounded.<ref name="amnestyfeb62020"/>
* On January 23, former Ambazonian general Leonard Nambere, who had returned to Cameroon on December 31, 2019 upon accepting an amnesty deal, went to visit inmates at the Kondengui Central Prison. He was nearly lynched by Anglophone inmates who blamed him for abandoning the Ambazonian cause, and had to be smuggled out of the prison.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-repented-ambazonia-general-cheats-death-in-kondengui/ Cameroon: ‘Repented Ambazonia General’ cheats death in Kondengui], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 23, 2020. Accessed Jan 23, 2020.</ref> In Kumba, police officers clashed with separatist fighters, killing one fighter.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-security-officers-kill-suspected-ambazonia-fighter-in-kumba/ Cameroon: Security officers kill suspected Ambazonia fighter in Kumba], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 23, 2020. Accessed Jan 23, 2020.</ref>
* On January 26, General Chacha of the Southern Cameroons Restoration Forces was captured and summarily executed when Cameroonian soldiers raided his base in Kikaikom, Kumbo. Some estimated 20 other separatist fighters were also killed during the raid.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-dreaded-ambazonia-general-killed-in-bui/ Cameroon: Dreaded Ambazonia General killed in Bui], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 27, 2020. Accessed Jan 27, 2020.</ref> Following the escalation, the Cameroonian police braced for the possibility that separatists could start carrying out attacks in [[Yaoundé]], warning that there was already a network in place that could make such attacks possible.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20200127193404/http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2020-01/27/c_138737411.htm Cameroonian police on high alert over possible separatist attacks in capital], XinhuaNet, Jan 27, 2020. Accessed Jan 27, 2020.</ref> In [[Ndop, Cameroon|Ndop]], armed men took control of a local radio station and set the building on fire. No one claimed responsibility for the attack.<ref>[https://www.voanews.com/africa/cameroon-rebels-attack-reporters-torch-media-offices Cameroon Rebels Attack Reporters, Torch Media Offices], Voice of America, Jan 30, 2020. Accessed Jan 30, 2020.</ref>
* On January 27, Cameroonian forces launched three simultaneous assaults on separatist camps in Kikaikom, Kumbo. According to the Cameroonian Army, separatist losses were 13 dead, while the Cameroonian side suffered two wounded.<ref>[https://www.newsghana.com.gh/13-militants-killed-in-military-offensive-in-cameroons-troubled-anglophone-region/ 13 militants killed in military offensive in Cameroon's troubled Anglophone region], NewsGhana, Jan 28, 2020. Accessed Jan 28, 2020.</ref>
* Between January 28–29, four people were killed when Cameroonian forces raided Mbiame and Nkambe.<ref name="crisisgroupjan2020">[https://www.crisisgroup.org/crisiswatch#cameroon "CrisisWatch January 2020"], International Crisis Group, Feb 2020. Accessed Feb 28, 2020.</ref>
* On January 30, separatists abducted four staff members at a local NGO in Bamenda. The staff members were subjected to physical violence before being released the next day. Three staff members from a different organization were also abducted in Bamenda, but they were all released after it turned out that the separatists had mistaken them for being involved in organizing the upcoming election.<ref name="tnhfeb6"/> In Buea, separatist fighters abducted a student; they chopped off her finger days later, before releasing her after a ransom had been paid.<ref>[https://www.hrw.org/news/2020/03/12/targeted-going-school-cameroon Targeted for Going to School in Cameroon], Human Rights Watch, Mar 12, 2020. Accessed Mar 15, 2020.</ref>

==February==
* On February 1, separatists carried out two separate attacks on a convoy transporting [[Cameroon People's Democratic Movement|CPDM]] activists who were campaigning in Mbengwi and Bamenda. Four activists and a policeman were injured in the attacks.<ref>[https://actucameroun.com/2020/02/03/menaces-des-separatistes-campagne-electorale-la-caravane-du-rdpc-attaquee-dans-le-nord-ouest/ Menaces des séparatistes – Campagne électorale : la caravane du RDPC attaquée dans le Nord-Ouest], Actu Cameroun, Feb 3, 2020. Accessed Feb 3, 2020. (French)</ref>
* On February 2, security forces raided the villages of Owe and Ikata, Fako Division. Three civilians were killed in Ikata.<ref name="tnhfeb6">[https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/news/2020/02/06/Cameroon-elections-anglophone-separatist-insurgency-Ambazonia Briefing: Cameroon's intensifying conflict and what it means for civilians], The New Humanitarian, Feb 6, 2020. Accessed Feb 8, 2020.</ref>
* On February 3, security forces carried out raids in Muyuka that continued into the following day. Three civilians were killed in these raids, while 45 houses were burned down, 300 people were detained and 3,000 people fled.<ref name="tnhfeb6"/>
* On February 5, there were clashes in Buea that continued into the next day.<ref name="lockdownfeb6"/>
* On February 6, separatists clashed with Cameroonian forces in Kumba.<ref name="lockdownfeb6">[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-lockdown-gunshots-paralyse-activities-in-parts-of-anglophone-regions/ Cameroon: Lockdown, gunshots paralyse activities in parts of Anglophone regions], Journal du Cameroun, Feb 6, 2020. Accessed Feb 6, 2020.</ref> In the village of Bakebe in Mamfe, at least six people were killed by Cameroonian soldiers.<ref>[http://cameroonnewsagency.com/civilians-flee-lockdown-begins-ahead-of-local-elections/ Civilians Flee, Lockdown Begins Ahead Of Local Elections], Cameroon News Agency, Feb 6, 2020. Accessed Feb 8, 2020.</ref>
* On February 7, a separatist-imposed five-day lockdown, designed to prevent the Cameroonian parliamentary election from being held in the Anglophone regions, came into force.<ref name="lockdownfeb6"/>
* On February 8, the [[2020 Cameroonian parliamentary election|Cameroonian parliamentary election]] was initiated. Due to the security situation, the turnout in the Anglophone regions was very low, with many civilians having fled days in advance. In addition, separatists had abducted at least 120 candidates within the past two weeks, half of whom were still in captivity on election day.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/aponline/2020/02/09/world/africa/ap-af-cameroon-election.html Violence, Intimidation Threaten Cameroon's Legislative Vote], The New York Times, Feb 9, 2020. Accessed Feb 9, 2020.</ref> According to the separatists, 98 percent of the population boycotted the election.<ref name="cnafeb92020"/> The Cameroonian government claimed that there had been a massive turnout, and the electoral body Elecam claimed that there had been "no major incidents". The results in 11 constituencies were later nullified due to separatist activities on election day, triggering a partial reelection on March 22.<ref name="xinhuaelectionmar22"/> In Bafut, separatists burned down a public building that was meant to be used as a polling station on election day. In Sabga, separatists attacked a military convoy, injuring three soldiers.<ref name="voafeb92020">[https://www.voanews.com/africa/voting-begins-cameroon-french-areas-timid-english-zones Voting Begins in Cameroon French Areas, Timid in English Zones], Voice of America, Feb 9, 2020. Accessed Feb 9, 2020.</ref> In Ndop and Ndu, Cameroonian forces arrested hundreds of civilians, many of whom were forced to vote.<ref name="cnafeb92020">[http://cameroonnewsagency.com/low-turnout-frustrates-election-in-north-west-south-west-regions/ Low Turnout Frustrates Election In North West, South West Regions], Cameroon News Agency, Feb 9, 2020. Accessed Feb 11, 2020.</ref>
* On February 9, two separatist fighters were killed in Babungu, and one Cameroonian soldier was killed in Befang. Fighting took place around several separatist roadblocks in rural areas.<ref>[https://www.newsghana.com.gh/two-separatists-one-soldier-killed-as-general-elections-underway/ Two separatists, one soldier killed as general elections underway], NewsGhana, Feb 10, 2020. Accessed Feb 10, 2020.</ref> In Muyuka, heavy fighting practically prevented anyone from voting in the election. There were also armed clashes in Buea and Kumba.<ref>[https://www.brecorder.com/2020/02/10/569371/fighting-in-southwest-cameroon-disrupts-voting/ Fighting in southwest Cameroon disrupts voting], Business Recorder, Feb 10, 2020. Accessed Feb 10, 2020.</ref>
* On February 14, at least 22 civilians [[Ngarbuh massacre|were massacred]] in Ntumbo, Northwest Region by Cameroonian soldiers. The Cameroonian Army quickly dismissed claims that a massacre had taken place; it claimed to have killed seven separatist fighters in Ntumbo, and that five civilians had died in a fire caused by an accidental explosion triggered by stray bullets.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-army-confirms-death-of-woman-children-in-ngarbuh/ Cameroon army confirms death of woman, children in Ngarbuh], Journal du Cameroun, Feb 17, 2020. Accessed Feb 17, 2020.</ref> Independent sources were able to quickly establish that the actual number of civilian casualties was much higher than the figure presented by Cameroon.<ref name="CNAfeb19">[http://cameroonnewsagency.com/exclusive-testimonies-from-ngarbuh-about-how-some-survivors-cheated-death-by-hairs-breadth-plus-names-of-victims-survivors Exclusive: Testimonies From Ngarbuh About How Some Survivors Cheated Death By Hair's Breadth, Plus Names of Victims, Survivors], Cameroon News Agency, Feb 19, 2020. Accessed Feb 19, 2020.</ref> The massacre was condemned by the [[United Nations]]<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-un-wants-punishment-for-perpetrators-of-ngarbuh-killings/ Cameroon: UN wants punishment for perpetrators of Ngarbuh killings], Journal du Cameroun, Feb 18, 2020. Accessed Feb 18, 2020.</ref> and the [[United States]].<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-us-embassy-condemns-ngarbuh-attacks-calls-for-independent-investigations/ Cameroon: US Embassy condemns Ngarbuh attacks, calls for independent investigations], Journal du Cameroun, Feb 19, 2020. Accessed Feb 20, 2020.</ref> After running its own investigation, the Cameroonian government maintained that the civilians who died in Ngarbuh had been accidentally killed during a battle with separatists, and that none of its soldiers had killed any civilians on purpose.<ref>[https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/04/cameroon-admits-army-role-february-massacre-civilians-200421185846329.html Cameroon admits army role in February massacre of civilians], Al Jazeera, Apr 22, 2020. Accessed Apr 22, 2020.</ref>
* By February 16, at least 35 civilians had been killed and 40 houses had been burned down within less than a week.
* On February 19, a soldier shot dead a civilian while running from security control. He was arrested the following day.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroonsoldier-arrested-after-killing-civilian-in-bamenda/ Cameroon:Soldier arrested after killing civilian in Bamenda], Journal du Cameroun, Feb 20, 2020. Accessed Feb 20, 2020.</ref>
* On February 24, Cameroonian soldiers arrested a man who had given information about the Ngarbuh massacre to international media.<ref>[http://cameroonnewsagency.com/ngarbuh-whistleblower-arrested/ Ngarbuh Whistleblower Arrested], Cameroon News Agency, Feb 25, 2020. Accessed Feb 26, 2020.</ref>
* On February 25, separatist fighters attacked a command post in Mendankwe, Bamenda, killing one soldier and wounding another three, killing one civilian engineer, abducting five people, destroying a military vehicle and capturing weapons.<ref name="lbpfeb22021">[https://www.lebledparle.com/fr/societe/1118568-cameroun-l-armee-neutralise-deux-hommes-armes-lors-d-un-raid-a-bamenda Cameroun : L’Armée neutralise deux hommes armés lors d’un raid à Bamenda], Le Bled Parle, Feb 2, 2021. Accessed Feb 2, 2021. (French)</ref>
* On February 27, separatists attacked Cameroonian soldiers in Bamendakwe, Mezam Division, killing one soldier and abducting four people. One civilian was also killed during the attack.<ref name="CNAmar12020"/>
* On February 29, Cameroonian soldiers attacked Small Babanki, a village in Mezam Division, storming the home of separatist general Richard Nformumbang Ndango,<ref name="lbpfeb22021"/> known as "General Fire", killing him and his wife. Cameroon claimed to have killed 10 other separatists as well, while local witnesses claimed that the soldiers had massacred 10 civilians. Local sources also claimed that the soldiers had burned down 17 houses and confiscated livestock.<ref name="CNAmar12020">[http://cameroonnewsagency.com/only-ambazonia-general-was-killed-in-small-babanki-the-rest-were-civilians-witnesses/ Only Ambazonia General Was Killed In Small Babanki, The Rest Were Civilians-Witnesses], Cameroon News Agency, Mar 1, 2020. Accessed Mar 2, 2020.</ref> In Bui, an alleged eyewitness to the Ngarbuh massacre was murdered.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-alleged-eye-witness-of-ngarbuh-killings-murdered-in-bui/ Cameroon: Alleged eye witness of Ngarbuh killings murdered in Bui], Mar 2, 2020. Accessed Mar 8, 2020.</ref>

==March==
* On March 1, Cameroonian soldiers raped 20 women, killed one man, and burned and plundered houses in Ebam, Southwest Region. 36 people were taken to a military camp, where many were subjected to severe beating and torture. One person later died in captivity. The others were released between March 4–6, after their families had paid money.<ref>[https://www.hrw.org/news/2021/02/26/cameroon-survivors-military-assault-await-justice Cameroon: Survivors of Military Assault Await Justice], Human Rights Watch, Feb 26, 2021. Accessed Feb 26, 2021.</ref>

Accessed Oct 2, 2020.</ref> Cameroon sent reinforcements to stop the celebrations, and clashes were reported in Bui, Momo and Boyo.<ref name="crisisgroupoct2020">[https://www.crisisgroup.org/crisiswatch#cameroon "CrisisWatch October 2020"], International Crisis Group, OCt 2020. * On October 6, several teachers were abducted by alleged separatists in Bamenda.<ref>[https://actucameroun.com/2020/10/11/crise-anglophone-les-ambazoniens-kidnappent-les-enseignants-a-bamenda/], Actu Cameroun, Oct 11, 2020. Accessed Oct 11, 2020.</ref>
* On October 10, a separatist fighter was killed by alleged government-sponsored Fulani militants in Ngarbuh, [[Ndu, Cameroon|Ndu]]. Separatist fighters pursued and captured one of the militants, who then revealed the identities of two others who were subsequently hunted down and captured as well.<ref>[https://cameroonnewsagency.com/ambazonia-separatists-clash-with-mbororo-militias/ Ambazonia Separatists Clash With Mbororo Militias], Cameroon News Agency, Oct 11, 2020. Accessed Oct 11, 2020.</ref>
* On October 11, Cameroon initiated a three-day offensive against separatists in [[Wabane]],<ref name="jdcoct192020"/> Lebialem.<ref name="xinhuaoct142020">[http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2020-10/15/c_139440553.htm 12 separatists killed in clash in Cameroon's Anglophone region], Xinhua, Oct 14, 2020. Accessed Oct 15, 2020.</ref> Two days later, it was revealed that Cameroonian forces had killed "General Ayeke", commander of the "Gorilla Fighters" militia, in Besali, Wabane, Lebialem.<ref name="cnaoct132020">[https://cameroonnewsagency.com/govt-forces-kill-ambazonia-general-ayeke/ Gov’t Forces Kill Ambazonia General Ayeke], Cameroon News Agency, Oct 13, 2020. Accessed Oct 14, 2020.</ref> Some 13 hostages were freed from the separatist camp, which was subsequently destroyed. One Cameroonian soldier was killed during the same incident, and the operation was described as "bloody", although the other separatist fighters at the base were reported to have escaped. The corpse of "General Ayeke" was flown to Kumba where it was publicly displayed.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-notorious-separatist-fighter-general-ayeke-killed-in-lebialem/ Cameroon: Notorious separatist fighter “General” Ayeke killed in Lebialem], Journal du Cameroun, Oct 13, 2020. Accessed Oct 13, 2020.</ref> In total, three Cameroonian soldiers and at least 12 separatists died during the three-day offensive.<ref name="xinhuaoct142020"/>
* On October 16, Cameroonian troops freed 11 hostages from a separatist camp in Lebialem.<ref>[https://newsghana.com.gh/11-hostages-freed-by-troops-in-cameroons-restive-anglophone-region/ 11 hostages freed by troops in Cameroon’s restive Anglophone region], NewsGhana, Oct 17, 2020. Accessed Oct 18, 2020.</ref> The camp was reportedly run by a separatist commander named Agbor Oscar Nkeng.<ref name="jdcoct192020">[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-11-new-civilians-freed-from-amba-captivity-in-restive-sw/ Cameroon: 11 new civilians freed from Amba captivity in restive SW], Journal du Cameroun, Oct 19, 2020. Accessed Oct 19, 2020.</ref>
* On October 21, according to local sources in Ndop, soldiers started forcing children to go to school, often through violence and threats. The same day, separatist fighters abducted five teachers.<ref>[https://cameroonnewsagency.com/we-are-living-in-a-dilemma-condition-in-ndop-victims/ We Are Living In A Dilemma Condition In Ndop- Victims], Cameroon News Agency, Oct 22, 2020. Accessed Oct 22, 2020.</ref>
* On October 24, the [[Kumba school massacre]] took place,<ref name="aljazeeraoct242020">[https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/10/24/five-children-killed-in-attack-on-cameroonian-school-officials Attackers storm Cameroon school, kill several children], Al Jazeera, Oct 24, 2020. Accessed Oct 24, 2020.</ref> with 10–12 gunmen killing 7 children and wounding another 13.<ref name="jdcoct30202">[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-kumba-massacre-owners-of-mother-francisca-primary-school-still-under-police-custody/ Cameroon/Kumba massacre: Owners of Mother Francisca primary school still under police custody], Journal du Cameroun, Oct 30, 2020. Accessed Oct 30, 2020.</ref> No one claimed responsibility for the attack, and the Cameroonian government and the separatist movements blamed each other.<ref name="aljazeeraoct242020"/>
* On October 26, Cameroonian forces reportedly killed a separatist commander known as "General Mendo Ze" in Fako Division.<ref name="crisisgroupoct2020"/>
* On October 27, a teacher was killed in Bamenda.<ref>[https://agencecamerounpresse.com/societe/soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9/nord-ouest-le-gouvernement-confirme-l%E2%80%99assassinat-d%E2%80%99un-inspecteur-p%C3%A9dagogique-d%E2%80%99%C3%A9conomie-%C3%A0-bamenda.html Nord-Ouest: Le Gouvernement confirme l’assassinat d’un Inspecteur pédagogique d’économie à Bamenda], Agence Cameroun Presse, Oct 29, 2020. Accessed Oct 30, 2020. (French)</ref>
* On October 29, the Cameroonian government announced that the BIR had identified and killed the separatist leader responsible for the Kumba school massacre three days prior.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-ambazonia-general-who-staged-kumba-school-attack-killed/ Cameroon: Ambazonia General who staged Kumba school attack killed], Journal du Cameroun, Oct 29, 2020. Accessed Oct 29, 2020.</ref>

==November==
* On November 2, Cameroonian soldiers killed three civilians in Bali.<ref>[https://twitter.com/CMRNewsAgency/status/1323345291777576966 Twitter], Cameroon News Agency, Nov 2, 2020.</ref> Soldiers also invaded Small Babanki and Bafut, burning down several houses.<ref>[https://twitter.com/CMRNewsAgency/status/1323349093922541568 Twitter], Cameroon News Agency, Nov 2, 2020.</ref>
* On November 3, suspected separatists kidnapped all students and teachers from a school in Kumbo. They released all the students soon afterwards.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-kidnapped-kumbo-school-pupils-released-staff-still-held-captive/ Cameroon: Kidnapped kumbo school pupils released, staff still held captive], Journal du Cameroun, Nov 3, 2020. Accessed Nov 4, 2020.</ref> Armed men also attacked the Longla Comprehensive College in Bamenda.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-govt-accuses-ambazonia-separatists-of-multiple-school-attacks/ Cameroon govt accuses ‘Ambazonia’ separatists of multiple school attacks], Journal du Cameroun, Nov 5, 2020. Accessed Nov 28, 2020.</ref>
* On November 4, armed men attacked a school in Limbe, abusing students and teachers and setting fire to buildings before retreating. The BIR attempted to intervene, but arrived too late.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-armed-men-attack-school-in-limbe/ Cameroon: Armed men attack school in Limbe], Journal du Cameroun, Nov 3, 2020. Accessed Nov 4, 2020.</ref>
* On November 5, a separatist militia known as the "Bui Warriors" abducted the Fon of Nso and ten other people who were travelling with him to Kumbo,<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-fru-ndi-condemns-kidnap-of-nso-fon-calls-for-his-release/ Cameroon: Fru Ndi condemns kidnap of Nso Fon, calls for his release], Journal du Cameroun, Nov 6, 2020. Accessed Nov 7, 2020.</ref> including Cameroon's sole catholic cardinal. Most were released the next day, while the Fon was held for five days.<ref name="cruxnowjan202021">[https://cruxnow.com/church-in-africa/2021/01/cameroon-cardinal-suggests-military-rule-might-end-anglophone-crisis/ Cameroon cardinal suggests military rule might end Anglophone crisis], Crux Now, Jan 20, 2021. Accessed Jan 20, 2021.</ref> The militia conditioned the Fon's release on the release of several Ambazonian prisoners, including Ayuk Tabe.<ref>[https://cameroonnewsagency.com/ambazonia-restoration-forces-use-nso-fon-as-prisoner-of-war-demands-release-of-amba-leaders/ Ambazonia Restoration Forces Use Nso Fon As Prisoner Of War, Demands Release Of Amba Leaders], Cameroon News Agency, Nov 7, 2020. Accessed Nov 7, 2020.</ref> After five days, the Bui Warriors declared that they had released the Fon after he had agreed to go to Yaoundé and to stay out of the politics of the Anglophone regions. The Bui Warriors also said that "he shall be making his triumphant home coming to Nso when the moment shall have been ripped".<ref>[https://cameroonnewsagency.com/bui-warriors-set-conditions-for-nso-fon-to-return-to-palace/ Bui Warriors Set Conditions For Nso Fon To Return To Palace], Cameroon News Agency, Nov 10, 2020. Accessed Nov 11, 2020.</ref> The cardinal who was abducted alongside the Fon, himself a supporter of a united and federal Cameroon, later wrote a book about the incident and argued that the separatists would probably lay down their weapons if the army was confined to barracks and a general amnesty was declared.<ref name="cruxnowjan202021"/>
* On November 6, armed men killed the traditional ruler of Liwuh La Malale, Muea, Buea, and burned down his palace.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-another-traditional-ruler-brutally-murdered-in-sw-region/ Cameroon: Another traditional ruler brutally murdered in SW region], Journal du Cameroun, Nov 9, 2020. Accessed Nov 9, 2020.</ref> While Cameroon blamed the separatists, no one claimed responsibility for the attack. Following the murder, hundreds of civilians fled from the town.<ref>[https://www.voanews.com/africa/hundreds-desert-cameroon-village-after-separatists-kill-village-chief Hundreds Desert Cameroon Village After Separatists Kill Village Chief ], Voice of America, Nov 8, 2020. Accessed Nov 9, 2020.</ref> In Mamfe, clashes were recorded.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-gunshots-reported-in-mamfe/ Cameroon: Gunshots reported in Mamfe], Journal du Cameroun, Nov 6, 2020. Accessed Nov 7, 2020.</ref>
* On November 8, Cameroonian soldiers killed two civilians in Luh, Ndu.<ref>[https://twitter.com/CMRNewsAgency/status/1325403261105545216 Twitter], Cameroon News Agency, Nov 8, 2020.</ref>
* On November 9, at least eight people were abducted by armed men in Limbe.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-about-ten-persons-kidnapped-by-gunmen-in-limbe/ Cameroon: About ten persons kidnapped by gunmen in Limbe], Journal du Cameroun, Nov 10, 2020. Accessed Nov 11, 2020.</ref> The abductors soon released a pregnant woman and a teenager, while keeping the others in captivity. They were rescued by security forces after a day.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-villagers-kidnapped-in-limbe-rescued-by-security-forces/ Cameroon: Villagers kidnapped in Limbe rescued by security forces], Journal du Cameroun, Nov 11, 2020. Accessed Nov 11, 2020.</ref>
* On November 14, a teacher and a student were wounded during an attack on a school in Mamfe.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-teacher-student-severely-injured-after-mamfe-school-shooting/ Cameroon: Teacher, student severely injured after Mamfe school shooting], Journal du Cameroun, Nov 16, 2020. Accessed Nov 16, 2020.</ref>
* On November 18, two soldiers were killed in a separatist ambush in Bui. A civilian also died after getting caught in the crossfire.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-two-soldiers-killed-in-ambush-in-bui-restive-nw-region/ Cameroon: Two soldiers killed in ambush in Bui, restive NW region], Journal du Cameroun, Nov 19, 2020. Accessed Nov 19, 2020.</ref>
* On November 19, two people were injured in a bombing in [[Douala]]. The local police said that the attack was carried out similarly to bombings in Yaoundé.<ref>[http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2020-11/20/c_139528633.htm Two injured in explosion in Cameroon's commercial capital], Xinhua, Nov 20, 2020. Accessed Nov 20, 2020.</ref>
* On November 20–21, separatists attacked several military convoys. According to local sources, at least four soldiers were injured and five military vehicles were damaged in Kumbo.<ref name="voanov222020">[https://www.voanews.com/africa/cameroon-begins-election-campaign-amid-separatist-violence Cameroon Begins Election Campaign amid Separatist Violence], Voice of America, Nov 22, 2020. Accessed Nov 23, 2020.</ref>

==December==
* On December 4, separatist forces started enforcing a four-day lockdown aimed at disrupting the December 6 regional election.<ref>[http://www.xinhuanet.com/english/2020-12/04/c_139564233.htm Separatists disrupt regional elections in Cameroon's restive Anglophone regions], Xinhua, Dec 4, 2020. Accessed Dec 5, 2020.</ref>
* On December 5, there were clashes in Buea.<ref name="cnadec62020"/>
* On December 6, Cameroon held its first-ever regional election. The SDF and the MRC boycotted the election due to the ongoing to war.<ref>[https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/12/6/cameroon-holds-first-regional-election-amid-opposition-boycott Cameroon holds first regional election amid opposition boycott], Al Jazeera, Dec 6, 2020. Accessed Dec 6, 2020.</ref> Separatists had promised to prevent the election from taking place, while Cameroon has deployed additional troops.<ref name="voanov222020"/> Throughout the day, there were shootouts in Bamenda as separatists moved to sabotage the election. A number of people were shot, and one CPDM Councilor was killed after getting caught in the crossfire during a shootout between separatist fighters and police forces.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-cpdm-councillor-shot-dead-in-police-amba-confrontation-in-momo/ Cameroon: CPDM Councillor shot dead in police/Amba confrontation in Momo], Journal du Cameroun, Dec 7, 2020. Accessed Dec 7, 2020.</ref> In Kumbo, separatist fighters entered a church and held the occupants hostage. There were also clashes in Buea.<ref name="cnadec62020">[https://cameroonnewsagency.com/how-cameroons-regional-polls-went-down-in-restive-anglophone-regions/ How Cameroons Regional Polls Went Down In Restive Anglophone Regions], Cameroon News Agency, Dec 6, 2020. Accessed Dec 6, 2020.</ref> In Babessi, a separatist fighter was ambushed and killed in his home by soldiers.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-ambazonia-fighter-suspected-of-kidnapping-cardinal-tumi-fon-of-nso-neutralised/ Cameroon: Ambazonia fighter suspected of kidnapping Cardinal Tumi, Fon of Nso, “neutralised”], Journal du Cameroun, Dec 7, 2020. Accessed Dec 7, 2020.</ref>
* On December 8, Cameroonian authorities suspended all [[Médecins Sans Frontières|Doctors Without Borders]] activities in the Anglophone regions, accusing the organization of being "too close" with separatist groups.<ref>[https://www.msf.org/msf-denied-providing-badly-needed-healthcare-northwest-cameroon People in northwest seek healthcare as MSF denied providing medical services], Doctors Without Borders, Jun 22, 2021. Accessed Jun 22, 2021.</ref>
* On December 12, the Fon of Small Babanki was abducted by gunmen.<ref>[https://twitter.com/CMRNewsAgency/status/1338065661730414597 Twitter], Cameroon News Agency, Dec 13, 2020.</ref> In Bambalang, there were clashes between Cameroonian forces and the Bambalang Marine Forces.<ref>[https://twitter.com/CMRNewsAgency/status/1338067027345469443 Twitter], Cameroon News Agency, Dec 13, 2020.</ref>
* On December 13, three traditional rulers were abducted by armed men. One of them, the Chief of Mile 14-Dibanda, was found dead hours later.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-another-traditional-ruler-killed-in-restive-anglophone-region/ Cameroon: Another traditional ruler killed in restive Anglophone Region], Journal du Cameroun, Dec 14, 2020. Accessed Dec 14, 2020.</ref>
* At some point in late-December, there were armed clashes and explosions, and an eight-year-old was killed when he got caught in the crossfire between Cameroonian troops and separatist fighters.<ref name="dwjan42021">[https://www.dw.com/en/cameroons-anglophone-crisis-no-end-in-sight/a-56106405 Cameroon's Anglophone crisis: No end in sight], Deutsche Welle, Jan 4, 2021. Accessed Jan 4, 2021.</ref>
* On December 31, separatist fighters ambushed a soldier in Wum.<ref>[https://www.journalducameroun.com/en/cameroon-two-suspected-separatist-fighters-killed-in-nw/ Cameroon: Two suspected separatist fighters killed in NW], Journal du Cameroun, Jan 7, 2020. Accessed Jan 7, 2020.</ref>
* In his end-of-year speech, Samuel Ikome Sako of the Interim Government of Ambazonia declared that an All Ambazonia Constitutional Draft Conference would be held during 2021, and that Northerst and Southwest Region would be reorganized into Equatorial Zone, Midland Zone and Savanna Zone. Sako also said that the separatists were purchasing more weapons and that the fight would continue.<ref>[https://cameroonnewsagency.com/separatist-leaders-welcome-biyas-military-option-say-they-are-ready/ Separatist Leaders Welcome Biya’s Military Option, Say They Are Ready], Cameroon News Agency, Jan 4, 2021. Accessed Jan 5, 2021.</ref> Meanwhile, President Paul Biya thanked the population of the Anglophone regions for assisting the government in the fight against separatists, and called on other countries to crack down on separatist activities in the diaspora.<ref>[https://cameroonnewsagency.com/president-paul-biyas-end-of-year-speech-2020/ President Paul Biya’s End of Year speech 2020], Cameroon News Agency, Jan 1, 2021. Accessed Jan 5, 2020.</ref>
