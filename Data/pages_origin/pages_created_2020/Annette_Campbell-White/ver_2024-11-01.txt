Campbell-White''' is a New Zealand-born venture capitalist.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nzedge.com/news/annette-campbell-white-speaks-in-cape-town/|title=Annette-Campbell White Speaks in Cape Town – Education, News, Society|date=16 January 2019|website=NZEDGE|language=en-US|access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.marketscreener.com/business-leaders/Annette-Campbell-White-05FZSQ-E/biography/|title=Annette J. == Biography ==
Campbell-White was born in New Zealand and attended the [[University of Cape Town]] in South Africa.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /> She graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Chemical Engineering and Master of Science in Physical Chemistry.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://blog.wikimedia.org/2016/05/11/annette-campbell-white-endowment/|title=Annette Campbell-White named to the Wikimedia Endowment Advisory Board – Wikimedia Blog|date=11 May 2016 |language=en|access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref> She was the first biotechnology analyst on [[Wall Street]] and the first female partner at [[Hambrecht & Quist]], a leading investment banking firm.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/19222245.Annette_Campbell_White|title=Annette Campbell-White|last1=Center|first1=Harry Ransom|last2=Austin|first2=The University of Texas at|website=www.goodreads.com|access-date=1 January 2020|last3=21st|last4=Guadalupe|last5=Austin|last6=TX|last7=US}}</ref> 

Campbell-White founded MedVenture Associates, a biomedical venture capital firm, in 1986 and was a senior managing director at the firm until her retirement in 2015.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" />

In 2019, she published her memoir, ''Beyond Market Value: A Memoir of Book Collecting and the World of Venture Capital.''<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://utpress.utexas.edu/books/campbell-white-beyond-market-value|title=Beyond Market Value A Memoir of Book Collecting and the World of Venture Capital By Annette Campbell-White|date=6 December 2019|publisher=[[University of Texas Press]]|year=2019|isbn=978-1-4773-1935-2|language=en|access-date=1 January 2020|archive-date=1 January 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101082536/https://utpress.utexas.edu/books/campbell-white-beyond-market-value|url-status=dead}}</ref>

=== Philanthropy ===
In 1997, Campbell-White established the Kia Ora Foundation to provide educational and other opportunities to musicians and artists from New Zealand.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":2" />

In 2016, she joined the Wikimedia Endowment Advisory Board to help administer the endowment fund as an ongoing source of funding for the [[Wikimedia Foundation]].<ref name=":3" />

In 2018, Campbell-White was honoured at the sixth annual [[International Opera Awards]] for her lifelong commitment to opera and support of young artists and the performing arts. She has been a board member for the [[San Francisco Opera]] for many years, and she and her husband have sponsored San Francisco Opera performances since 1995.<ref name=":0" /> She had also sat on the board of [[Cal Performances]], a performing arts organisation at the [[University of California, Berkeley]].<ref name=":3" />
