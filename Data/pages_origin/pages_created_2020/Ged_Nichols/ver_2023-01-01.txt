'''Ged Nichols''' (born 1962) is a British trade union leader.

Nichols grew up in [[Liverpool]], and left school at the age of sixteen, to work for the [[Halifax Building Society]].  On his first day, he joined the recently-founded [[Halifax Building Society Staff Association]].  He studied part-time while becoming increasingly active in the union, including a spell as its North West Region Health and Safety Officer.<ref name="tuc">{{cite web |title=Ged Nichols becomes TUC President |url=https://www.tuc.org.uk/news/ged-nichols-becomes-tuc-president |website=Trades Union Congress |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref><ref name="hse">{{cite web |title=Profile of Ged Nichols |url=https://www.hse.gov.uk/aboutus/hseboard/biographies/board/nichols.htm |website=Health and Safety Executive |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

In 1988, Nichols moved to [[Reading, Berkshire]], to work full-time as a national officer for the union.  He was elected as its general secretary in 1992, aged 30.  In 2002, the union became known as "Accord".  As leader of the union, he gained a reputation as a strong negotiator, and advocated what he described as "constructive industrial relations".  He became a director of the Involvement and Participation Association.<ref name="tuc" /><ref name="hse" />

Nichols was elected to the [[General Council of the Trades Union Congress]] (TUC) in 2000, and became [[President of the TUC]] for 2019/20.  Also in 2019, he was appointed to the board of the [[Health and Safety Executive]].<ref name="tuc" /><ref name="hse" />

Nichols' daughter, [[Charlotte Nichols]], is [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] Member of Parliament for [[Warrington North]].
