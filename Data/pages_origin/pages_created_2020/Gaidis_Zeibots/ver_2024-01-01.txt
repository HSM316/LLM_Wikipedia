Admiral '''Gaidis Andrejs Zeibots'''<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PSy0AAAAIAAJ&q=Gaidis+Andrejs+Zeibots|title=Central and South-Eastern Europe|date=2005|publisher=Europa Publications|isbn=978-1-85743-309-8|language=en}}</ref> (born 26 June 1945 in [[Cirgaļu]], [[Valka District]]) was [[Commander of the Joint Headquarters (Latvia)|Commander of the Joint Headquarters]], head of the [[Latvian National Armed Forces]] from 2003 to 2006.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mod.gov.lv/en/about-us/history|title=History|website=Aizsardzības ministrija|language=en|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> He was elected on 30 January 2003 by the [[Latvian Parliament]]. Zeibots previously served as the [[Chief of staff]] of the [[Latvian Naval Forces]]. He graduated from the High Military Radio Electronics School in the [[Soviet Union]] in 1969.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.baltictimes.com/news/articles/7527/|title=Admiral elected to reform, lead Latvia's armed forces|website=[[baltictimes.com]]|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>
