'''Joe K. Longley''' is an American lawyer. He was the first Chief of the Antitrust and Consumer Protection Division of the Texas Attorney General’s Office in 1973, and in 1979 was cofounder of the law firm Longley & Maxwell, LLP. In 2018 he became the President of the [[Texas State Bar]]. 

==Early life==
After graduating from [[Arlington Heights High School]] in [[Fort Worth, Texas]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.texasbar.com/AM/Template.cfm?Section=articles&Template=/CM/ContentDisplay.cfm&ContentID=40376 |title=The Good Fight |publisher=Texas Bar Journal |page=448 |date=June 2018 |author=Patricia Busa McConnico}}</ref> Joe K. Longley graduated from the [[University of Texas]] law school in 1969.<ref name=Alcade>{{Cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=YM0DAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA72|title=The Alcalde|first=Emmis|last=Communications|date=September 30, 2000|publisher=Emmis Communications|via=Google Books}}</ref> Following law school he worked in the consumer protection office of the Attorney General, before becoming an attorney with Edwards and DeAnda in [[Corpus Christi, Texas]]. As of the early 1970s, Longley had become a solo practitioner, working out of [[Austin, Texas]]. During this time his work included unpaid lobbying for the Texas Consumers Association.<ref>Hill, 19.</ref>

==Career==
Longley was on the campaign team for [[John Hill (Texas politician)|John Hill]] during his successful campaign to become the [[Attorney General of Texas]].<ref name=Hill>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=DL_7D4elEBAC&pg=PA8|title=John Hill for the State of Texas: My Years as Attorney General|first1=John L.|last1=Hill|first2=Ernie|last2=Stromberger|date=December 9, 2008|publisher=Texas A&M University Press|isbn=9781603440721|via=Google Books}}</ref> In 1973, Longley became the first Chief of the Antitrust and Consumer Protection Division of the Texas Attorney General’s Office,<ref>Hill, 116.</ref> where he also drafted the ''Texas Consumer Protection Act'' and ''Deceptive Trade Practices Act''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.statesman.com/article/20140802/NEWS/308029719|title=In protracted insurance case, is Greg Abbott Farmers' friend or foe?|first=Jonathan|last=Tilove|website=Austin American-Statesman}}</ref> He then cofounded the Austin law firm Longley & Maxwell, LLP in 1979 with Philip Maxwell.<ref name=Alcade /> He returned to solo practice in the mid-2000s, where he continued to work on class action cases in Texas, Arkansas, California, and Oklahoma.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.abajournal.com/magazine/article/new_laws_and_med_mal_damage_caps_devastate_plaintiff_and_defense_firms_alik|title=Tort Reform Texas Style|first=A. B. A.|last=Journal|website=ABA Journal}}</ref>

==Presidency of the State Bar of Texas==
Longley was sworn in as the 138th President of the State Bar of Texas in 2018.<ref name=Monitor>{{Cite web|url=https://texasmonitor.org/who-watches-the-watchers-nobody-after-texas-high-court-favors-impunity-for-all/|title=Who watches the watchers? Nobody, after Texas high court favors impunity for all|first=Jon|last=Cassidy|date=March 28, 2018}}</ref> He was the first petition-nominated candidate to win the state-wide ballot.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chron.com/business/article/Texas-Bar-president-questions-mandatory-dues-13556673.php|title=Texas Bar president questions mandatory dues|date=January 25, 2019|website=Houston Chronicle}}</ref> After his term elapsed,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://setexasrecord.com/stories/512684994-dropped-ag-opinion-request-over-texas-bar-dues-election-process|title=Dropped: AG opinion request over Texas Bar dues, election process|first=David|last=Yates|website=SE Texas Record}}</ref> he became the Immediate Past President of the State Bar of Texas. Longley then authored the book ''How We Did It .... 2016-2019'', which records his experience in reforming the State Bar both during his term and afterwards.<ref>https://lrl.texas.gov/scanned/SIRSI/B600.8_L862_2019.pdf</ref>

==Film industry==
Longley was an investor and a completion producer for the 1974 film ''[[The Texas Chainsaw Massacre]]'' through a small group of investors, they called P.I.T.S., an acronym standing for “Pie in the Sky”.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.texasmonthly.com/articles/they-came-they-sawed/|title=They Came. They Sawed.|date=November 1, 2004|website=Texas Monthly}}</ref> In the years since the film’s success, Longley has stated that he and his friends thought the film would flop, allowing them to take a tax write-off on the movie, and were caught off-guard by the film’s success. In exchange for the investment, the group owned 19% of Vortex/Henkel/Hooper Production, the film’s production company.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3xS_QoT2DigC&pg=PT30|title=Chain Saw Confidential: How We Made the World's Most Notorious Horror Movie|first=Gunnar|last=Hansen|date=September 24, 2013|publisher=Chronicle Books|isbn=9781452129501|via=Google Books}}</ref> Following its release, the court assigned Longley as a special master to retrieve the distribution rights from a former partner, before being replaced by a trustee.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KoVOCgAAQBAJ&pg=PA45|title=Chainsaws, Slackers, and Spy Kids: Thirty Years of Filmmaking in Austin, Texas|first=Alison|last=Macor|date=March 1, 2010|publisher=University of Texas Press|isbn=9780292722439|via=Google Books}}</ref>  As an actor, Longley has received credits for the 1981 film ''[[Raggedy Man]]''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0082969/|title=Raggedy Man|via=www.imdb.com}}</ref> and 1987’s ''[[Red Headed Stranger]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0519362/|title=Joe K. Longley|website=IMDb}}</ref>
