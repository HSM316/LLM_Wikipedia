'''Blanche Hinman Dow''' (February 9, 1894, [[Louisiana, Missouri]]-May 25, 1973) was President of the [[American Association of University Women]] (1963-1967) and was President of [[Cottey College]], retiring in 1965 after serving for 16 years.<ref name="NYT">{{cite news |title=BLANCHE DOW, LED UNIVERSITY WOMEN, |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1973/05/26/archives/blanche-dow-led-university-women.html |accessdate=31 December 2019 |newspaper=NY Times |date=May 26, 1973}}</ref>

Dow graduated from [[Smith College]] in 1913, continuing her education at [[Columbia University]] earning a masters degree and doctorate in French.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books/about/Dictionary_of_Missouri_Biography.html?id=6gyxWHRLAWgC|title=Dictionary of Missouri Biography|first1=Lawrence O.|last1=Christensen|first2=William E.|last2=Foley|first3=Gary|last3=Kremer|date=October 31, 1999|publisher=University of Missouri Press|via=Google Books}}</ref>
