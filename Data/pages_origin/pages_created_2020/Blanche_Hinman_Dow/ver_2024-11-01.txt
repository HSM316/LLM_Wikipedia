{{use mdy dates|date=December 2019}}'''Blanche Hinman Dow''' (February 9, 1894 — May 25, 1973) was President of the [[American Association of University Women]] (1963–1967) and was President of [[Cottey College]], retiring in 1965 after serving for 16 years.<ref name="NYT">{{cite news |title=BLANCHE DOW, LED UNIVERSITY WOMEN |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1973/05/26/archives/blanche-dow-led-university-women.html |accessdate=31 December 2019 |newspaper=NY Times |date=May 26, 1973}}</ref>

Dow graduated from [[Smith College]] in 1913, continuing her education at [[Columbia University]] earning a master's degree and doctorate in French.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=6gyxWHRLAWgC|title=Dictionary of Missouri Biography|first1=Lawrence O.|last1=Christensen|first2=William E.|last2=Foley|first3=Gary|last3=Kremer|date=October 31, 1999|publisher=University of Missouri Press|isbn=9780826260161|via=Google Books}}</ref> She went on to serve on the White House Commission on International Cooperation, the National Citizens Committee on Community Relations, and the President's Committee on Employment of the Handicapped.<ref name="NYT"/>

== Legacy ==
The Dow International Scholarship Fund at Cottey College was established in 1973 in memory of Dow and provides funding for international students.<ref name="scholarship">{{cite web |title=CCAA Projects |url=http://community.cottey.edu/s/1449/17/interior.aspx?pgid=318&gid=1 |access-date=4 May 2020 |publisher=[[Cottey College]]}}</ref>
