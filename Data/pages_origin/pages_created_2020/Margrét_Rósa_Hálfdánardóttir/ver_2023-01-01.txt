'''Margrét Rósa Hálfdánardóttir''' (born 2 June 1994) is an Icelandic former [[basketball]] player and a former member of the [[Iceland women's national basketball team|Icelandic national basketball team]]. She won the [[Úrvalsdeild kvenna (basketball)|Icelandic championship]] with [[Haukar women's basketball|Haukar]] in 2009.

==Playing career==
===Haukar (2008–2014)===
After coming up through the junior ranks of [[Haukar women's basketball|Haukar]], Margrét debuted with the senior team during the 2008–2009 season. She won the Icelandic championship in after Haukar beat [[KR women's basketball|KR]] 3-2 in the Úrvalsdeild finals.<ref>{{cite news |author1=Kristján Jónsson |title=Þetta eru bara töffarar |url=https://timarit.is/page/5250056 |accessdate=1 January 2020 |work=[[Morgunblaðið]] |date=2 April 2009 |pages=C1, C4}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Dimovska tryggði titilinn |url=http://timarit.is/view_page_init.jsp?pageId=4149433 |accessdate=1 January 2020 |work=[[Fréttablaðið]] |date=2 April 2009 |pages=1, 48 |language=Icelandic}}</ref>

She broke into the rotation during the 2011–2012 season where she averaged 10.0 points and 3.5 rebounds per game. For her efforts she was selected as the [[Úrvalsdeild Women's Young Player of the Year|Úrvalsdeild Young Player of the Year]]. The following season, she averaged a career high 13.0 points along with 4.4 rebounds and 3.0 assists per game. During her last season with Haukar, Margrét averaged 11.8 points, 4.5 rebounds and 3.3 assists per game.

===Canisius College (2014–2018)===
In 20114, Margrét Rósa joined [[Canisius Golden Griffins women's basketball|Canisius College]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Margrét Rósa heldur vestur um haf |url=https://www.karfan.is/2014/06/margret-rosa-heldur-vestur-um-haf/ |accessdate=1 January 2020 |work=Karfan.is |date=15 June 2014 |language=Icelandic}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |author1=Óskar Ófeigur Jónsson |title=Margrét Rósa nýtti tímann vel |url=https://www.visir.is/g/2014141129140 |accessdate=1 January 2020 |work=[[Vísir.is]] |date=27 November 2014 |language=Icelandic}}</ref> During her freshman season, she appeared in 29 games, averaging 3.7 points and 1.0 rebounds.

In 2015 she was joined at Canisius by fellow Icelander [[Sara Rún Hinriksdóttir]].<ref>{{cite news |author1=Tómas Þór Þórðarson |title=Margrét Rósa: Hlakka mjög til að fá Söru Rún til mín út |url=https://www.visir.is/g/2015150609348/margret-rosa-hlakka-mjog-til-ad-fa-soru-run-til-min-ut |accessdate=1 January 2020 |work=[[Vísir.is]] |date=5 June 2019 |language=Icelandic}}</ref> She appeared in 30 games and started 29, averaging 9.6 points, 2.4 rebounds and 2.0 assists per game while leading the team in three point accuracy, by making 43.6% of her three-point shots.

During her junior season, Margrét appeared in 31 games and made 30 starts. She averaged 9.2 points per game while making 39.2% of her three-point shots.

Margrét started 27 of 30 games played as a senior. She averaged 9.2 points per game and scored a career high 23 points against Monmouth on 16 February 2018.

After graduating from college, Margrét decided to step away from basketball.<ref>{{cite news |author1=Davíð Eldur |title=Margrét Rósa leggur skóna tímabundið á hilluna |url=https://www.karfan.is/2018/05/margret-rosa-leggur-skona-timabundid-a-hilluna/ |accessdate=1 January 2020 |work=Karfan.is |date=10 May 2018 |language=Icelandic}}</ref>

==National team career==
After debuting with the [[Iceland women's national basketball team|Icelandic national team]] in 2014, Margrét went on to play 12 games for the team.<ref>{{cite web |title=A landslið |url=http://www.kki.is/landslid/konur/leikjafjoldi/a-landslid/ |website=kki.is |publisher=[[Icelandic Basketball Association]] |accessdate=1 January 2020 |language=Icelandic}}</ref> Due to commitments 
to the [[Canisius College]], she was unable to join the national team for most of her college career.<ref>{{cite news |author1=Óskar Ófeigur Jónsson |title=Margrét Rósa með 67 prósent þriggja stiga skotnýtingu |url=https://www.visir.is/g/2015151129109/margret-rosa-med-67-prosent-thriggja-stiga-skotnytingu |accessdate=1 January 2020 |work=[[Vísir.is]] |date=26 November 2015 |language=Icelandic}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Margrét's parents, Hálfdán Markússon and Sóley Indriðadóttir, both played basketball for [[Haukar]]. Her sister, [[Sylvía Rún Hálfdánardóttir]], plays for the [[Iceland women's national basketball team|Icelandic national team]].<ref>{{cite news |author1=Guðmundur Hilmarsson |title=Hefur allt til að bera til þess að verða frábær |url=https://www.mbl.is/greinasafn/grein/1588617/ |accessdate=1 January 2020 |work=[[Morgunblaðið]] |date=11 March 2016 |language=Icelandic}}</ref>
