The 1989–90 season was Real Madrid Club de Fútbol's 88th season in existence and the club's 59th consecutive season in the top flight of Spanish football.

==Season==
Real Madrid finished the campaign as league champions for the fifth season running, claiming their 25th league title overall. Furthermore, under the helm of new coach John Benjamin Toshack, the team broke the record for most league goals in a season, with 107.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[El País]]|title=La apoteosis del gol|url=https://elpais.com/deportes/2012/04/15/actualidad/1334514121_389979.html|language=es|date=15 April 2012}}</ref> Mexican striker [[Hugo Sánchez]] scored 38 of them, equaling [[Telmo Zarra]]'s record,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.marca.com/claro-mx/futbol/mexicanos-mundo/2020/05/05/5eb17f58ca47410a128b45f2.html|title=30 years of 'Impossible' record of Telmo Zarra|work=Marca|location=Spain|date=5 May 2020|accessdate=15 July 2020}}</ref> and won the [[European Golden Shoe|European Golden Boot]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rsssf.org/miscellaneous/gboot.html |title=Golden Boot ("Soulier d'Or") Awards |publisher=[[Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation]] (RSSSF) |first=Sorin|last=Arotaritei |author2=Di Maggio, Roberto |author3=Stokkermans, Karel |date=11 December 2009 |accessdate=18 April 2008 }}</ref> Real Madrid were also aiming to clinch the second consecutive [[Double (association football)#Spain|domestic double]], going all the way to the [[1989–90 Copa del Rey|Copa del Rey]] [[1990 Copa del Rey Final|final]] where they were beaten by [[FC Barcelona|Barcelona]] 0–2.
