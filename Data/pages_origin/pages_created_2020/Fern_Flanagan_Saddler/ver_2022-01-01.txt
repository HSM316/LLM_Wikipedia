'''Fern Flanagan Saddler''' (born May 17, 1955)<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.dccourts.gov/press-releases/new-dc-superior-court-judge-be-sworn-friday|title=New D.C. Superior Court Judge To Be Sworn-In Friday {{!}} District of Columbia Courts|website=www.dccourts.gov|access-date=2019-12-31}} {{PD notice}}</ref> is an Associate Judge on the [[Superior Court of the District of Columbia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dccourts.gov/superior-court/judges#associate|title=District of Columbia Superior Court Judges|website=www.dccourts.gov|access-date=December 31, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Bio">{{Cite web|url=https://www.dccourts.gov/sites/default/files/2017-03/DCSC_Bio_Saddler.pdf |title=Bio |last=|first=|date=|website=www.dccourts.gov|language=en|access-date=December 31, 2019}}</ref>

== Education and career ==
Saddler earned her [[Bachelor of Arts]] from [[Wellesley College]] in 1976, and her [[Juris Doctor]] from [[Georgetown University Law Center]] in 1979.

After graduating, she worked in private practice. In 1984, she joined the District of Columbia Office of Bar Counsel as an Assistant Bar Counsel where she investigated complaints of attorney misconduct. In 1988, she joined the [[District of Columbia Court of Appeals]] as a Senior Staff Attorney.<ref name=":0"/>

=== D.C. Superior Court ===
From 1991 to September 2003, Saddler served a [[Magistrate Judge]] (formerly known as Hearing Commissioner) of the [[Superior Court of the District of Columbia]].

On June 11, 2002, President [[George W. Bush]] nominated Saddler to be an associate judge on the Superior Court of the District of Columbia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/news/releases/2002/06/20020611-9.html|title=Nomination|website=georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> Her nomination expired on November 20, 2002, with the end of the 107th United States Congress.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.congress.gov/search|title=Legislative Search Results|website=www.congress.gov|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

President George W. Bush renominated her on January 7, 2003, to a 15-year term as an associate judge on the Superior Court of the District of Columbia to the seat vacated by Patricia A. Wynn.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/news/releases/2003/01/20030107-11.html|title=Nominations Sent to the Senate|website=georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> On June 18, 2003, the [[United States Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs|Senate Committee on Governmental Affairs]] held a hearing on her nomination. On June 26, 2003, the Committee reported her nomination favorably to the senate floor. On June 27, 2003, the full [[United States Senate|Senate]] confirmed her nomination by [[voice vote]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.congress.gov/nomination/108th-congress/42|title=PN42 - Nomination of Fern Flanagan Saddler for The Judiciary, 108th Congress (2003-2004)|date=2003-06-27|website=www.congress.gov|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> She was sworn in on September 12, 2003.<ref name=":0" />

On April 30, 2018, the Commission on Judicial Disabilities and Tenure recommended that [[President Trump]] reappoint her to second 15-year term as a judge on the D.C. Superior Court.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cjdt.dc.gov/publication/honorable-fern-flanagan-saddler-reappointment-report|title=Honorable Fern Flanagan Saddler Reappointment Report {{!}} cjdt|website=cjdt.dc.gov|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Saddler was born and raised in [[Baltimore]], [[Maryland]]. She is married to Reverend Paul Harvey Saddler, an ordained minister in the [[United Church of Christ]] in [[Washington, D.C.]]<ref name=":0" />
