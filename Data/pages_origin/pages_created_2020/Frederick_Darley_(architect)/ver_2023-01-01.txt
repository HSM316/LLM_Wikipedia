'''Frederick Darley''', was an Irish architect, who designed and built a number of buildings in [[Dublin]], including in [[Trinity College Dublin]]. He was also responsible for a number of civic and church buildings across Ireland. He was a son of the builder and architect [[Frederick Darley (alderman)|Frederick Darley Senior]], and his father served as [[Lord Mayor of Dublin]] in 1808–1809. His mother was Elizabeth (Guinness) Darley, eldest daughter of [[Arthur Guinness]] of Beaumont, Drumcondra.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.dia.ie/architects/view/1383/DARLEY,+FREDERICK+%5B1%5D* | title = Frederick Darley (Senior) | work = Dictionary of Irish Architects| publisher = Irish Architectural Archive}}</ref>
In 1833–1843, Darley was the Ecclesiastical Commissioners architect for the [[Church of Ireland]] Diocese of Dublin.<ref>{{cite web|url = http://www.patrickcomerford.com/2017_05_06_archive.html | title = General Synod 2017 and five buildings in Limerick, Barrington’s Hospital | website = patrickcomerford.com}}</ref>

Frederick Darley junior was a pupil of [[Francis Johnston (architect)|Francis Johnston]]. Darley himself was succeeded by his pupil [[John McCurdy (architect)|John McCurdy]] as architect to Trinity College Dublin.<ref>{{cite web|url = https://www.dia.ie/architects/view/1382/DARLEY%2C+FREDERICK+%5B2%5D | title = Frederick Darley | work = Dictionary of Irish Architects | publisher = Irish Architectural Archive }}</ref>

Darley was a founding member of the Royal Institute of the Architects of Ireland (RIAI) and lived on Lower Fitzwilliam Street, Dublin.
