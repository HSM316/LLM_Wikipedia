{{Year in Kazakhstan |2020}}

Events of [[2020]] in [[Kazakhstan]].

== Incumbents ==

* '''[[President of Kazakhstan|President]]:''' [[Kassym-Jomart Tokayev]] 
* '''[[Prime Minister of Kazakhstan|Prime Minister]]:''' [[Askar Mamin]]

== Events ==
=== January ===
3 January – Funeral of [[Serikbolsyn Abdildin]], former chairman of the Supreme Council.
=== February ===
*5 to 8 February – [[2020 Dungan–Kazakh ethnic clashes|Ethnic clashes]] involving [[Dungan people|Dungans]] and [[Kazakhs]] killed 10 people, causing many families to flee to neighboring [[Kyrgyzstan]]. About 30 houses and 15 commercial properties were destroyed during the clash.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-02-09|title=Refugees flee Kazakhstan after ethnic clashes|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-51433824|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-02-09|title=Ethnic clashes in Kazakhstan kill 10|language=en-GB|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-51425938|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref>

=== March ===
*3 March – A court orders the early release of [[Mukhtar Dzhakishev]], the former CEO of state nuclear firm [[Kazatomprom]], who had been sentenced to 14 years in prison for corruption in 2010. Upon his release he will have served more than 11 years in prison.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-03-03|title=Kazakh court frees ex-CEO of state nuclear firm|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-kazakhstan-dzhakishev-idUSKBN20Q0I8|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref>
*13 March – The country reports its first two [[COVID-19 pandemic in Kazakhstan|confirmed cases of COVID-19]]; both are Kazakh nationals who recently returned from Germany.<ref>{{cite web|date=13 March 2020|title=Kazakhstan Confirms First Coronavirus Cases|url=https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2020-03-13/kazakhstan-confirms-first-coronavirus-cases|access-date=13 March 2020|website=[[Usnews.com]]|language=}}</ref>
*15 March – A [[state of emergency]] is declared in the country over the coronavirus pandemic. The presidential decree from [[President of Kazakhstan|President]] [[Kassym-Jomart Tokayev]] imposes a national quarantine and restricts both entry and departures from the country to all except diplomats and individuals invited by the [[Government of Kazakhstan|government]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=COVID-19: First Uzbek Case Recorded As Kazakhstan Declares State Of Emergency|url=https://www.rferl.org/a/in-sparsely-hit-central-asia-kazakh-coronavirus-infections-rise-to-six-first-uzbek-case/30488671.html|access-date=2020-10-02|website=RadioFreeEurope/RadioLiberty|language=en}}</ref>

=== April ===
*14 April – The [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Kazakhstan)|Ministry of Foreign Affairs]] summoned the [[China–Kazakhstan relations|Chinese ambassador]] after an article claiming that [[Kazakhs]] are "eager to return to China" and "do not have too many complaints" about [[China–Kazakhstan relations#History|China's historical invasions]] of the country was published on the private Chinese website [[Sohu]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-04-14|title=Kazakhstan summons Chinese ambassador in protest over article|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-kazakhstan-china-idUKKCN21W1AN|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200422002535/https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-kazakhstan-china-idUKKCN21W1AN|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 22, 2020|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref>

=== June ===
*17 June – The country's [[sovereign wealth fund]] [[Samruk-Kazyna]] announces that plans to sell [[Share (finance)|shares]] in the [[Government of Kazakhstan|state]]-run oil and gas company [[KazMunayGaz]] abroad will be delayed from this year to 2022 due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-06-17|title=UPDATE 1-Kazakhstan plans KazMunayGaz IPO in 2022|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/kazmunaygaz-ipo-idINL8N2DU0IO|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref>

=== July ===
*10 July – Authorities in the country deny a report published by Chinese officials alleging that the country is experiencing an outbreak of "unknown pneumonia" potentially deadlier than COVID-19.<ref>{{Cite web|author=Jessie Yeung, Philip Wang and Martin Goillandeau|title=Kazakhstan denies reports of 'unknown pneumonia' outbreak more deadly than Covid-19|url=https://www.cnn.com/2020/07/10/asia/kazakhstan-pneumonia-intl-hnk-scli-scn/index.html|access-date=2020-10-02|website=CNN}}</ref>
*17 July – The country announces that it will start adding thousands of cases and hundreds of deaths to its COVID-19 tallies starting next month, describing the corresponding [[pneumonia]]-related cases, which had not been confirmed by tests, as most likely linked to the [[Severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2|SARS-CoV-2]] novel coronavirus.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-07-17|title=Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan to report some pneumonia cases as COVID-19|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-kazakhstan-kyrgyzs-idUSKCN24I1DH|access-date=2020-10-02}}</ref>

=== October ===
*1 October – Former President [[Nursultan Nazarbayev]] expresses concern over the escalation of the [[2020 Nagorno-Karabakh war|Nagorno-Karabakh conflict]] and calls for [[Baku]] and [[Yerevan]] to launch [[negotiation]]s.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Nursultan Nazarbayev concerned over escalation of situation surrounding Nagorno-Karabakh|url=https://news.am/eng/news/605363.html|access-date=2020-10-02|website=news.am|language=en}}</ref>

=== December ===
* 19 December - the President signed Law No. 365-VI ZRK1 and Law No. 384-VI ZRK2 (“Amendments“), respectively, which introduced certain changes to the country’s anti-corruption legislation, including the Anti-Corruption Law, Criminal Code and Civil Code which took effect by December 31.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://globalcompliancenews.com/kazakhstan-changes-to-anti-bribery-legislation190121/|title=Kazakhstan: Changes to anti-bribery legislation|author=Azamat A. Kuatbekov and Gulnur Bekmukhanbetova|date=February 10, 2021|accessdate=March 5, 2021}}</ref>
