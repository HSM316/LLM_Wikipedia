'''Lorenzo M. Boardman''' (c. 1832 - July 16, 1897) was an American architect. Born in Pennsylvania, he was an architect in [[Minneapolis]], Minnesota for three decades.<ref name="spokanechronicleoneofour">{{cite news |title=L. M. Boardman |url=https://www.newspapers.com/image/561482551/?terms=%22L.%2BM.%2BBoardman%22%2Barchitect |accessdate=January 1, 2020 |work=The Spokane Chronicle |date=January 27, 1896|page=16|via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> He spent the last ten years of his life in [[Spokane, Washington]], where he designed many buildings,<ref name="semiweeklydeathofapioneer">{{cite news |title=Death of a Pioneer. Lorenzo M. Boardman, the Well Known Pioneer, Passes Away After a Short Illness. |url=https://www.newspapers.com/image/566384432/?terms=%22Lorenzo%2BM.%2BBoardman%22 |accessdate=January 1, 2020 |work=The Semi-Weekly Spokesman-Review |date=July 17, 1897|page=5|via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> including the Traders Block, Temple Court, the Ross Block, and the Windsor Block.<ref name="spokanechronicleobit">{{cite news |title=Death of L. M. Boardman. A Highly Respected Citizen of Spokane Passes Away. |url=https://www.newspapers.com/image/562117404/?terms=%22L.%2BM.%2BBoardman%22%2Barchitect |accessdate=January 1, 2020 |work=The Spokane Chronicle |date=July 17, 1897|page=8|via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> He also designed the NRHP-listed [[Whitten Block]].<ref name="nrhpdocwhitten">{{cite web|url={{NRHP url|id=93000362}}|title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: Whitten Block / Hamer's |publisher=[[National Park Service]]|author=Sally R.
