'''"Something Fishy"''' is a song written and recorded by American singer-songwriter [[Dolly Parton]]. It was released as a single on May 8, 1967, by [[Monument Records]]. It would be included on Parton's 1967 debut album, ''[[Hello, I'm Dolly]]''. The song peaked at number 17 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles]] chart, becoming Parton's first top 20 hit.

==Critical reception==
''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' gave a positive review of the song, calling it "clever material...performed to perfection." They predicted that it would "fast top her "Dumb Blonde" hit and establish her as one of the label's consistent top sellers."<ref>{{cite web |title=Spotlight Singles |url=https://www.americanradiohistory.com/hd2/IDX-Business/Music/Archive-Billboard-IDX/IDX/60s/1967/Billboard%201967-05-20-OCR-Page-0018.pdf |website=American Radio History |publisher=Billboard |accessdate=December 30, 2019 |page=18 |date=May 20, 1967}}</ref> In another positive review, ''[[Cashbox (magazine)|Cashbox]]'' said it "could be another chart item for Parton." They described "Something Fishy as "a bright, bouncy side" with a cute treatment from Dolly," while calling "I’ve Lived My Life" a "feelingful tale of woe."<ref>{{cite web |title=Country Reviews |url=https://www.americanradiohistory.com/hd2/IDX-Business/Music/Archive-Cash-Box-IDX/60s/1967/CB-1967-05-20-OCR-Page-0059.pdf |website=American Radio History |publisher=Cashbox |accessdate=December 30, 2019 |page=59 |date=May 20, 1967}}</ref>

==Commercial performance==
"Something Fishy" debuted at number 61 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles]] chart. It would eventually peak at number 17 and spend 12 weeks on the chart.
