'''Mircea Luca''' (3 August 1921 – 29 July 2008) was a [[Romania]]n football [[Defender (association football)|defender]], [[Manager (association football)|manager]] and president at [[FC Universitatea Cluj|Universitatea Cluj]].<ref name=transilvania/><ref name=gandul/>
 
Mircea Luca was [[FC Universitatea Cluj|"U" Cluj]]'s captain in the hardest period of the club's history. In 1940, the team moved from Cluj to [[Sibiu]] as a result of the [[Second Vienna Award]], when the northern part of Transylvania was ceded to [[Hungary]]. In 1945, after the end of the [[Second World War]] and the [[Paris Peace Treaties, 1947|return of the northern part of Transylvania]] to Romania, "U" returned to its home in Cluj.<ref name=gandul/><ref name=transilvania2/> He also worked as a [[Otorhinolaryngology|ENT]] surgeon.<ref name=transilvania/><ref name=transilvania2/> In 1994 Mircea Luca received the Honorary Citizen of Cluj-Napoca title.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.prosport.ro/fotbal-intern/liga-2/expozitia-u100-a-fost-vandalizata-foto-cum-arata-acum-panourile-care-intruchipeaza-legendele-universitatii-18321287 | title=Expoziția „U100” a fost vandalizată!
