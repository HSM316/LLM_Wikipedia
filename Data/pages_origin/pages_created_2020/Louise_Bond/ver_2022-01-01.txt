'''Louise Bond''' is a New Zealand advertising executive. She was chair of the New Zealand Commercial Communications Council until her retirement in January 2019; she was the first woman to hold the position.<ref>{{Cite web|url= https://commscouncil.nz/news/caanz-appoints-louise-bond-as-new-president/ |title= CAANZ appoints Louise Bond as new President|last=Commercial Communications Council|website=commscouncil.nz|access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref>

In 1999 she and two business partners established Spark, a media agency which became the largest spender of media in the New Zealand market within the first 5 years of operation. In 2000, Bond opened Spark PR & Activate, a marketing communications business, and in 2007 she opened PHDiQ, a digital operation specialising in online strategy, search and social media management.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nzherald.co.nz/business/news/article.cfm?c_id=3&objectid=12188841|title=Departing advertising boss Louise Bond on graveyard shifts, sexism and booze|last=Venuto|first=Damien|date=18 January 2019|access-date=1 January 2020|language=en-NZ|issn=1170-0777}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.stuff.co.nz/business/industries/nz-cannes-2012/7065073/NZ-Cannes-Jury-Panel-Bios|title=NZ Cannes Jury Panel Bios|website=Stuff|language=en|access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref>
