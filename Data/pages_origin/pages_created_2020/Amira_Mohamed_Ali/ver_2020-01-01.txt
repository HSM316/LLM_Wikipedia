'''Amira Mohamed Ali''' (born January 16, 1980 in [[Hamburg]]) is a German politician ([[The Left (Germany)|Die Linke]]) who has been a member of the [[Bundestag]] since 2017. Since November 12, 2019, she has been parliamentary [[chairperson]] of Die Linke in the Bundestag, together with [[Dietmar Bartsch]].

== Life ==
Amira Mohamed Ali grew up in [[Fuhlsbüttel|Hamburg-Fuhlsbüttel]]. Her father is from [[Egypt]] and her mother is [[Germany|German]].<ref name="jung">{{cite web |title=Amira Mohamed Ali (MdB, Die Linke):&#32;Folge 404|periodical=[[Jung & Naiv]]|publisher=|url=http://www.jungundnaiv.de/2019/03/10/amira-mohamed-ali-mdb-die-linke-folge-404/|url-status=|format= |accessdate=2019-03-11|archive-url=|archive-date=|last=|date=2019-03-10|year=|language=|pages= |quote=}}</ref><ref>[https://www.sueddeutsche.de/politik/linke-amira-mohamed-ali-1.4680663 ''Amira Mohamed Ali: Erfrischend unverkrampft in ihrer neuen Chefrolle''], sueddeutsche.de, 13 November 2019</ref> After graduating from the [[Gelehrtenschule des Johanneums]] in [[Winterhude|Hamburg-Winterhude]] in 1998, Mohamed Ali studied law at the universities of [[Heidelberg University|Heidelberg]] and [[University of Hamburg|Hamburg]], where she began and completed her studies.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|title=Profil|periodical=|publisher=|url=https://www.linksfraktion.de/fraktion/abgeordnete/profil/amira-mohamed-ali/|url-status=|format=|accessdate=2019-07-25|archive-url=|archive-date=|last=Fraktion DIE LINKE im Bundestag|date=|year=|language=|pages=|quote=}}</ref> She completed her legal clerkship at the Higher Regional Court of Oldenburg between 2005 and 2007.

She was admitted to the bar in 2008 and worked as an in-house lawyer and contract manager for an automotive supplier until 2017.<ref name=":0" /> She is a member of [[IG Metall]] and the German Animal Welfare Association.

Mohamed Ali is married and has lived in [[Oldenburg]] since 2005.<ref name="jung" /><ref name="wolf">

== Political activity ==
Mohamed Ali has been a board member of the Oldenburg/Ammerland district association of the party Die Linke in [[Lower Saxony]] since 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kreisvorstand – Die Linke Oldenburg|periodical=|publisher=|url=http://www.die-linke-oldenburg.de/?cat=15|url-status=|format=|accessdate=2019-07-25|archive-url=|archive-date=|last=Holger Onken|date=|year=|language=|pages=|quote=}}</ref> She ran for political office for the first time in the 2016 local elections on list number 2 in electoral district VI of the city of Oldenburg.<ref>{{cite web|title=Die Linke stellt Kandidaten vor|periodical=|publisher=|url=https://www.oldenburger-onlinezeitung.de/politik/die-linke-stellt-kandidaten-vor-17148.html|url-status=|format=|accessdate=2019-07-25|archive-url=|archive-date=|last=|date=2016-04-22|year=|language=|pages=|quote=}}</ref> In this election, the Left Party achieved its best result in a local election since its foundation.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kommunalwahl 2016: So hat Oldenburg gewählt|periodical=|publisher=|url=https://www.nwzonline.de/politik/niedersachsen/so-hat-oldenburg-gewaehlt_a_31,1,669979454.html|url-status=|format=|accessdate=2019-07-25|archive-url=|archive-date=|last=Nordwest-Zeitung|date=2016-09-12|year=|language=|pages=|quote=}}</ref>

Mohamed Ali ran as a direct candidate for the [[Oldenburg – Ammerland|Oldenburg-Ammerland]] constituency in the [[2017 German federal election|2017 federal election]]. She was elected number 5 on her party's Lower Saxony state list and was elected to the Bundestag through that list.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kandidaten Für Die Bundestagswahl Oldenburg: Wieder kein She was spokesperson for [[consumer protection]] and for animal protection of the Left parliamentary group in the Bundestag.<ref>{{cite web|title=SprecherInnen|periodical=|publisher=|url=https://www.linksfraktion.de/fraktion/sprecherinnen/|url-status=|format=|accessdate=2019-07-25|archive-url=|archive-date=|last=Fraktion DIE LINKE im On November 12, 2019, she was elected as [[Sahra Wagenknecht]]'s successor–alongside Dietmar Bartsch–as co-chair of the parliamentary group. Mohamed Ali won in a competitive vote against [[Caren Lay]], 36 votes to 29.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.spiegel.de/politik/deutschland/nachfolge-von-sahra-wagenknecht-amira-mohamed-ali-neue-co-chefin-der-linksfraktion-a-1296092.html|title=Nachfolge von Sahra Wagenknecht: Amira Mohamed Ali ist neue Co-Chefin der Linksfraktion|date=2019-11-12|work=Spiegel Online|accessdate=2019-12-31}}</ref>

=== Political positions ===
Mohamed Ali, like her predecessor Wagenknecht, is considered part of the left wing of her party. In contrast to Wagenknecht, however, she is clearly open to a possible [[red-red-green coalition]].<ref>[https://www.zeit.de/politik/deutschland/2019-11/amira-mohamed-ali-linke-fraktion-buendnis-spd-gruene ''Neue Chefin der Linksfraktion ist offen für Bündnis mit Grünen und SPD.''] In ''Zeit Online.'' 30&nbsp;November 2019. In Zeit.de, accessed 8&nbsp;December 2019.</ref>
