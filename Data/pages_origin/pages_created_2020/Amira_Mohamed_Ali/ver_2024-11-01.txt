'''Amira Mohamed Ali''' ({{langx|ar|أميرة محمد علي}}; born 16 January 1980) is a German politician and member of the [[Bundestag]] since 2017. From 12 November 2019 till October 2023, she was the parliamentary co-[[chairperson]] of [[The Left (Germany)|The Left]] alongside [[Dietmar Bartsch]]. In October 2023, she left The Left alongside others like [[Sahra Wagenknecht]] to found a new party. Amira is the chairwoman of the board of the [[Bündnis Sahra Wagenknecht]]-Association which was founded to prepare a new party in January 2024.

== Life ==
Amira Mohamed Ali was born  in [[Hamburg]] and grew up in [[Fuhlsbüttel|Hamburg-Fuhlsbüttel]]. Her father is [[Egyptians|Egyptian]] and her mother is [[Germany|German]].<ref name="jung">{{cite web |title=Amira Mohamed Ali (MdB, Die Linke): Folge 404|periodical=Jung & Naiv|url=http://www.jungundnaiv.de/2019/03/10/amira-mohamed-ali-mdb-die-linke-folge-404/|access-date=2019-03-11|date=2019-03-10}}</ref><ref>[https://www.sueddeutsche.de/politik/linke-amira-mohamed-ali-1.4680663 ''Amira Mohamed Ali: Erfrischend unverkrampft in ihrer neuen Chefrolle''], sueddeutsche.de, 13 November 2019</ref> After graduating from the [[Gelehrtenschule des Johanneums]] in [[Winterhude|Hamburg-Winterhude]] in 1998, Mohamed Ali studied law at the universities of [[Heidelberg University|Heidelberg]] and [[University of Hamburg|Hamburg]], where she began and completed her studies.<ref name=":0">{{cite web|title=Profil|url=https://www.linksfraktion.de/fraktion/abgeordnete/profil/amira-mohamed-ali/|access-date=2019-07-25|last=Fraktion DIE LINKE im Bundestag}}</ref> She completed her legal clerkship at the Higher Regional Court of Oldenburg between 2005 and 2007.

She was admitted to the bar in 2008 and worked as an in-house lawyer and contract manager for an automotive supplier until 2017.<ref name=":0" /> She is a member of [[IG Metall]] and the German Animal Welfare Association.

Mohamed Ali is married and has lived in [[Oldenburg (city)|Oldenburg]] since 2005.<ref name="jung" /><ref name="wolf">

== Political activity ==
Mohamed Ali has been a board member of the Oldenburg/Ammerland district association of the party Die Linke in [[Lower Saxony]] since 2015.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kreisvorstand – Die Linke Oldenburg|url=http://www.die-linke-oldenburg.de/?cat=15|url-status=dead|access-date=2019-07-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190708023857/http://www.die-linke-oldenburg.de/?cat=15|archive-date=8 July 2019|last=Holger Onken}}</ref> She ran for political office for the first time in the 2016 local elections on list number 2 in electoral district VI of the city of Oldenburg.<ref>{{cite web|title=Die Linke stellt Kandidaten vor|url=https://www.oldenburger-onlinezeitung.de/politik/die-linke-stellt-kandidaten-vor-17148.html|access-date=2019-07-25|date=2016-04-22}}</ref> In this election, the Left Party achieved its best result in a local election since its foundation.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kommunalwahl 2016: So hat Oldenburg gewählt|url=https://www.nwzonline.de/politik/niedersachsen/so-hat-oldenburg-gewaehlt_a_31,1,669979454.html|access-date=2019-07-25|last=Nordwest-Zeitung|date=2016-09-12}}</ref>

Mohamed Ali ran as a direct candidate for the [[Oldenburg – Ammerland|Oldenburg-Ammerland]] constituency in the [[2017 German federal election|2017 federal election]]. She was elected number 5 on her party's Lower Saxony state list and was elected to the Bundestag through that list.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kandidaten Für Die Bundestagswahl Oldenburg: Wieder kein Oldenburger im Berliner Reichstag?|url=https://www.nwzonline.de/oldenburg/politik/oldenburg-kandidaten-fuer-die-bundestagswahl-wieder-kein-oldenburger-im-berliner-reichstag_a_32,0,3788914298.html|access-date=2019-07-25|last=Nordwest-Zeitung|date=2017-09-20}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Interaktive Karte Zur Wahl 2017: Wer für den Nordwesten in den Bundestag will|url=https://www.nwzonline.de/reisen/oldenburg-interaktive-karte-zur-wahl-2017-wer-fuer-den-nordwesten-in-den-bundestag-will_a_32,0,999241775.html|access-date=2019-07-25|last=Nordwest-Zeitung|date=2017-07-28}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Gewählte auf Landeslisten der Parteien in Niedersachsen – Der Bundeswahlleiter|url=https://www.bundeswahlleiter.de/bundestagswahlen/2017/gewaehlte/bund-99/land-3.html#c504a14d-0988-4270-b1e9-f7c520a0b636|access-date=2019-07-25}}</ref> In the 19th Bundestag, she is a member of the Committee for Legal Affairs and Consumer Protection and the Committee for Food and Agriculture.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Deutscher Bundestag – Amira Mohamed Ali|url=https://www.bundestag.de/abgeordnete/biografien/M/522090-522090|access-date=2019-07-25}}</ref> She was spokesperson for [[consumer protection]] and for animal protection of the Left parliamentary group in the Bundestag.<ref>{{cite web|title=SprecherInnen|url=https://www.linksfraktion.de/fraktion/sprecherinnen/|access-date=2019-07-25|last=Fraktion DIE LINKE im Bundestag}}</ref>

On 12 November 2019, she was elected as [[Sahra Wagenknecht]]'s successor–alongside Dietmar Bartsch–as co-chair of the parliamentary group. Mohamed Ali won in a competitive vote against [[Caren Lay]], 36 votes to 29.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.spiegel.de/politik/deutschland/nachfolge-von-sahra-wagenknecht-amira-mohamed-ali-neue-co-chefin-der-linksfraktion-a-1296092.html|title=Nachfolge von Sahra Wagenknecht: Amira Mohamed Ali ist neue Co-Chefin der Linksfraktion|date=2019-11-12|work=Spiegel Online|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

In 2023, the dispute between left-wing populist and conservative [[Sahra Wagenknecht|Sarah Wagenknecht]] and the party leadership came to a head. Wagenknecht put forward the prospect of founding her own party. In August 2023, Mohamed Ali, who belongs to the Wagenknecht Group, announced that she would stepping back from co-chair of parliamentary group because of the dispute. It is difficult for her, to represent the course of the party board in the Bundestag, she said.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Rückzug von Mohamed Ali: Bartsch warnt vor Ende der Linksfraktion |url=https://www.tagesschau.de/inland/innenpolitik/linke-bartsch-mohamed-ali-100.html |access-date=2023-08-08 |website=tagesschau.de |language=de}}</ref>  

=== Political positions ===
Like her predecessor Wagenknecht, Mohamed Ali was considered part of the left wing of [[The Left (Germany)|The Left]], her former party. 

=== Founding a new party and leaving The Left ===
Mohamed Ali was involved in the founding of [[Bündnis Sahra Wagenknecht]] (BSW), a registered association with the goal of founding a new political party in Germany. Mohamed Ali serves as the chairperson of the organization. At a press conference on 23 October 2023, which announced BSW to the public, she announced that she had left [[The Left (Germany)|The Left party]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=2023-08-06 |title=Die Linke: Fraktionschefin gibt Amt wegen Streits über Sahra Wagenknecht ab |language=de |work=Der Spiegel |url=https://www.spiegel.de/politik/die-linke-fraktionschefin-gibt-amt-wegen-streit-um-sahra-wagenknecht-ab-a-23f22c86-4065-4ea8-9294-a0679687e96a |access-date=2023-08-08 |issn=2195-1349}}</ref>
