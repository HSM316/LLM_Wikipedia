The ''' Monte Mongioie''' is a mountain of the [[Ligurian Alps]] located in [[Piedmont]] (NW [[Italy]]).

== Toponymy  ==
The mountain was once known also as ''Cima Rascaira'', and appears with this name in the official map of the [[Kingdom of Sardinia (1720–1861)]] printed in [[1852]].<ref name=provcn>{{Cite web |url=http://montagna.provincia.cuneo.it/alpinismo/normale.jsp?id=245 | language = it |title=Provincia di Cuneo - monte Mongioie |access-date=2008-11-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070223180210/http://montagna.provincia.cuneo.it/alpinismo/normale.jsp?id=245 |archivedate=2007-02-23 |url-status=dead }}</ref> It is also referenced as ''Raschera'', which is the name of a lake, of alpine pasture at the foot of the mountain and of the typical [[Raschera|cheese]] of the area.<ref>{{Cite book | chapter-url = https://books.google.com/books?id=fRnGDQAAQBAJ&q=raschera+%22monte+mongioie%22&pg=PA606 | pages = 606 | first = Catherine | last = Donnelly | chapter = Raschera | title = The Oxford Companion to Cheese | publisher = Oxford University Press | year = 2016  | isbn = 9780199330898 | access-date = 2020-01-01}}</ref> Due to its isolation the summit offers a very good view on a wide stretch of the [[Western Alps]].<ref name=provcn />

== Geography ==
[[File:Monte Mongioie da Est.JPG|thumb|View of the mountain from the Pizzo d'Ormea (East)]]
The mountain is the [[tripoint]] where the valleys of [[Tanaro]] (south), [[Ellero]] (NW) and [[Corsaglia]] (NE) meet.<ref>{{Cite map | title = Carta in scala 1:50.000 n. 8 ''Alpi Marittime e Liguri'' | publisher =  [[Istituto Geografico Centrale]] | location =  Torino  }}</ref> Westwards the north water divide of Tanaro Valley continues heading to the [[Cima delle Saline]], while eastwards it connects Monte Mongioie with Monte Rotondo and [[Pizzo d'Ormea]] through a pass named ''Bocchino dell’Aseo'' (2,295 m). The Corsaglia/Ellero ridge branching out from Monte Mongioie heads North towards the [[Po Plain]].

== Geology ==
Monte Mongioie summit and western slopes are made of tabular, marmoreal [[limestone]], dating back to [[Jurassic]]; on its eastern part also emerge [[Triassic]] layers of [[Dolomite (rock)|dolomitic]] limestone, located in a belt oriented from North to South passing through the ''Bocchino dell'Aseo''. On the sub-vertical cliffs near the summit and on the  SW ridge of MOnte Mongioie can be noticed blackish schistose limestones, fine-grained grey [[Dolomite (rock)|dolomite]] and red and yellow [[schist]]s.<ref name=geologica>{{cite web|url=http://www.apat.gov.it/Media/carta_geologica_italia/tavoletta.asp?foglio=91|title=Carta Geologica d'Italia scala 1:100.000 - foglio 91 - Boves|language=Italian|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120314161828/http://www.apat.gov.it/Media/carta_geologica_italia/tavoletta.asp?foglio=91 |archive-date= 2012-03-14|url-status=dead|website=Apat.gov.it}}</ref>

== Access to the summit ==
[[File:Mongioie._Canale_delle_Scaglie.jpg|thumb|Monte Mongioie as seen from Tanaro valley, on the left the ‘’Canale delle Scaglie’’]]

=== Summer ===
The [[normal route]] to the Mongioie requires some [[hiking]] experience but not [[alpinism|alpinistic]] skills.<ref name=provcn /> The summit can be accessed by a waymarked itinerary from ''Viozene'' (in the [[comune]] of [[Ormea]]) passing through a mountain hut named ''Rifugio Mongioie'' (1.555&nbsp;m), and then following by a large footpath up to ''Pian dell'Olio''. From there a gully called ''Canale delle Scaglie'' leads to the ''Bocchino dell'Aseo'', a mountain pass on the Tanaro-Corsaglia water divide. The last part of the ascent runs up the western ridge of the mountain.<ref name=provcn />

=== Winter ===
The mountain is also accessible in winter by [[Ski mountaineering|ski mountaineers]] from Viozene<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.gulliver.it/itinerario/258/ | language = it | publisher = Gulliver.it | title = Mongioie (Monte) da Viozene  | date = 2010-04-28  | access-date = 2020-01-01}}</ref> or Artesina.<ref>{{cite web | url = https://www.gulliver.it/itinerario/51250/ | language = it | publisher = Gulliver.it | title = Mongioie (Monte) da Artesina  | date = 2011-04-13  | access-date = 2020-01-01}}</ref>
