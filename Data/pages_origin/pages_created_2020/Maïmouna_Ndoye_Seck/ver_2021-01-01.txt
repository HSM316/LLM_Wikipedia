'''Maïmouna Ndoye Seck''' (born 1962 in [[Dakar]]) is a [[Senegal]]ese politician. An alumnus of the Ecole Polytechnique of Thiès, she was the Minister of Energy from 2013 to 2015 and the Minister of Tourism and Air Transport from 2015 to 2017, before she became the Minister of Air Transport and Airport Facilities Development in September 2017.

==Biography==
Seck was born in 1962 in [[Dakar]] and studied Engineering and Mechanical Engineering/Design<ref name="rewmi">{{cite web |language=fr |author=Christine Mendy |url=http://www.rewmi.com/nouveau-gouvernement-les-7-etoiles-mohamed-dione.html |title=Nouveau gouvernement : Les 7 étoiles de Mohamed Dione |website=rewmi.com |access-date=April 16, 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160425151425/http://www.rewmi.com/nouveau-gouvernement-les-7-etoiles-mohamed-dione.html |archive-date=April 25, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> at the ''[[École Polytechnique de Thiès]]'' from 1982.<ref name="xalima">{{cite web |language=fr |url=http://xalimasn.com/maimouna-ndoye-premiere-femme-polytechnicienne-du-senegal-une-mathematicienne-decomplexee/ |title=Maïmouna Ndoye, première femme polytechnicienne du Sénégal: Une mathématicienne décomplexée à la tête du ministère de l’énergie |date=September 3, 2013 |website=xalima.com |access-date=April 16, 2016}}</ref> She was the first woman to graduate from that school. She also earned a [[master's degree]] in [[energy management]] and [[energy economics|economics]].<ref name="rewmi"/>

Seck was the Principal Private Secretary of the Ministry of Energy, and a government advisor.<ref>{{cite web |language=fr|url=http://www.tourmag.com/Senegal-Maimouna-Ndoye-Seck-nommee-ministre-du-Tourisme-et-des-Transports-aeriens_a74806.html |title=Maimouna Ndoye Seck, une polytechnicienne au ministère de l’Energie |date=June 30, 2015 |website=tourmag.com |access-date=April 16, 2016}}</ref> In 2013, she joined the [[Touré Government]] as the Minister of Energy, before she became the Minister of Energy and Sustainable Energy Development within the [[First Dionne Government]] in 2014.

Seck has chaired the Electricity Sector Regulatory Committee (CRSE) with the aim of making energy affordable at a lower price.<ref>{{cite web |language=fr |url=http://www.ces.sn/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=165:2013-12-12-10-19-40&catid=3:actualie&Itemid=32 |title=Le Ministre de l’Energie, Mme Maimouna Ndoye Seck, face aux membres du Conseil économique social et environnemental (Cese) |date=December 12, 2013 |publisher=Social and Environmental Economic Council|access-date=April 16, 2016}}</ref>

Following to the 2015 cabinet reshuffle, Seck became Minister of Tourism and Air Transport<ref>{{cite web |language=fr|url=http://seneweb.com/news/Politique/maimouna-ndoye-seck-une-polytechnicienne-au-ministere-de-l-rsquo-energie_n_104813.html |title=Sénégal : Maimouna Ndoye Seck nommée ministre du Tourisme et des Transports aériens |date=September 2, 2013|website=seneweb.com |access-date=April 16, 2016}}</ref> with the aim of re-organising a sector in difficulty notably due to the difficulties of [[Senegal Airlines]].<ref>{{cite web |language=fr |author=Khadim Fall|url=http://www.pressafrik.com/Senegal-Airlines-n-a-jamais-atteint-l-objectif-qui-lui-etait-fixe--Maimouna-Ndoye-Seck_a148607.html |title=" Sénégal Airlines n’a jamais atteint l’objectif qui lui était fixé ", Maimouna Ndoye Seck |date=April 15, 2016 |website=pressafrik.com |access-date=April 16, 2016}}</ref>
