[[Adult Alternative Songs]], also known as Triple A, is a [[record chart]] that ranks the most-played songs on American [[adult album alternative]] radio stations. Currently published by the music industry magazine ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', the chart is formulated based on electronically monitored airplay data compiled by [[Nielsen Broadcast Data Systems]].<ref>{{cite journal |url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/triple-a |title=Triple A |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |accessdate=May 13, 2014 |url-access=subscription }}</ref> from a panel of 32 stations.<ref>{{cite journal |url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/radio/5915622/billboards-triple-a-chart-gets-a-makeover |title=Billboard's Triple A Chart Gets a Makeover |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |date=February 21, 2014 |accessdate=May 13, 2014 |author=Trust, Gary}}</ref> It was introduced in January 1996<ref>{{cite journal |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/chartbeat/481397/triple-a-radio-breaking-rookie-stars |title=Triple A Radio Breaking Rookie Stars |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |date=July 17, 2012 |accessdate=May 13, 2014 |author=Trust, Gary}}</ref> as a feature in ''[[Radio & Records]]'' magazine,<ref name="Shipley">{{cite web |url=http://www.idolator.com/5060978/billboard-breaks-down-dials-up-triple-a |title='Billboard' Breaks Down, Dials Up Triple-A |publisher=[[Idolator (website)|Idolator]]. [[Spin Media]] |date=October 9, 2008 |accessdate=May 13, 2014 |author=Shipley, Al}}</ref> which was later purchased by ''Billboard'' parent company [[Nielsen Holdings|Nielsen]].<ref>{{cite journal |url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/articles/news/1352657/vnu-to-acquire-radio-records |title=VNU To Acquire Radio & Records |work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |date=July 6, 2006 |accessdate=May 17, 2014}}</ref> ''Billboard'' appropriated the ''Radio & Records'' adult alternative chart in July 2008 and became its sole publisher after ''Radio & Records'' ceased publication in June 2009.<ref>{{cite journal |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/268412/chart-beat-pink-black-eyed-peas-shinedown |title=Chart Beat: Pink, Black Eyed Peas, Shinedown |journal=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |date=June 10, 2009 |accessdate=June 28, 2013 |author=Trust, Gary}}</ref> The first number-one single of the 2020s on the Adult Alternative Songs chart was "[[Orphans (song)|Orphans]]" by British recording artists [[Coldplay]].<ref name="No1"/>
