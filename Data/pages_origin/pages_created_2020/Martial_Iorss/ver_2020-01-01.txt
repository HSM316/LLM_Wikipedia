'''Martial Iorss''' (12 January 1891 – 7 May 1966)<ref name=S/> was  [[French Polynesia]]n politician. He served as a member of the [[Assembly of French Polynesia|Territorial Assembly]] between 1953 and 1957.

==Biography==
Iorss was born [[Taiohae]] in 1891.<ref name=A>[http://histoire.assemblee.pf/UserFiles/File/1996-2004/Doc04avecHistEM20.pdf La Présidence de la Polynésie Française] Assembly of French Polynesia</ref> He studied in [[Papeete]] and worked as a teacher in [[Mataiea]] for two years,<ref name=S/> before moving into commerce.<ref name=A/> After four years of military service during [[World War I]] he returned to commerce.<ref name=S/> In 1929 he was appointed Chief Clerk in the court system, a role he held until 1940.<ref name=S/> He was president of the Racing Society between 1929 and 1933 and the Yacht Club from 1932 until 1940 and also headed the Society of Tahitian Youth.<ref name=S>''Publications de la Société des océanistes'', Issue 36, p272</ref>

Iorss was elected to the [[Assembly of French Polynesia|Territorial Assembly]] in the Papeete constituency in the [[1953 French Polynesian legislative election|1953 elections]] as a representative of the [[Democratic and Socialist Union of the Resistance]].<ref>[http://histoire.assemblee.pf/articles.php?id=638 Composition de l’assemblée territoriale] Assembly of French Polynesia</ref> He served until [[1957 French Polynesian legislative election|1957]].

In 1961 he published a book on [[Tahitian language|Tahitian]] grammar. He died in May 1966 at his home in the Paofai area of Papeete.<ref name=PIM>[https://nla.gov.au/nla.obj-331794735/view?partId=nla.obj-331884720#page/n158/mode/1up Mr. Martial Iorss] ''Pacific Islands Monthly'', August 1966, p157</ref><ref name=S/>
