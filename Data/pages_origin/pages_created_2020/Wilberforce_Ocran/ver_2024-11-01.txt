'''Wilberforce Paa Kwesi Ocran''' (born [[Kumasi]], [[Ghana]], 24 September 1999) is an English professional [[Association football|footballer]] who plays for [[Brentwood Town F.C.|Brentwood Town]].

==Club career==
Prior a move to [[Charlton Athletic F.C.|Charlton Athletic]] in May 2018, Ocran enjoyed spells at [[Watford F.C.|Watford]], [[Arsenal F.C.|Arsenal]], [[Leyton Orient F.C.|Leyton Orient]], [[Enfield Town F.C.|Enfield Town]] and [[Barnsley F.C.|Barnsley]].<ref>{{Cite news |title=LONDON TRIALS, YOUTH MIDFIELDER, ENFIELD UK, WILBERFORCE OCRAN |url=https://www.tonys-soccer-school.net/football-trial-players-league-table-division-12/9974-london-trials-youth-midfielder-enfield-uk-wilberforce-ocran-.html |publisher=Tonys Soccer School |access-date=26 November 2020}}</ref>  On 10 November 2018, Ocran joined [[Isthmian League]] South East Division side, [[Cray Wanderers F.C.|Cray Wanderers]]<ref>{{Cite news |title=Cray Wanderers FC on Twitter |url=https://twitter.com/OfficialWands/status/1061232283653410817 |publisher=[[Cray Wanderers F.C.]] Twitter |date=10 November 2018 |access-date=26 November 2020}}</ref> and went onto feature three times, scoring once before returning to Charlton at the end of the month.<ref name="Cray">{{Cite news |title=Cray Wanderers – Appearances – Wilberforce Ocran – 2018-2019 |url=https://www.footballwebpages.co.uk/cray-wanderers/appearances/2018-2019/wilberforce-ocran/550404 |publisher=Football Web Pages |access-date=26 November 2020}}</ref>

On 13 August 2019, Ocran made his professional debut, replacing [[Sam Field (footballer)|Sam Field]] in the 78th minute, during Charlton's  home 0–0 draw with [[Forest Green Rovers F.C.|Forest Green Rovers]] in an [[EFL Cup]] first round tie, in which the visitors eventually claimed the victory on penalties, 5–3.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Charlton Athletic vs. Forest Green Rovers |url=https://int.soccerway.com/matches/2019/08/13/england/league-cup/charlton-athletic-football-club/forest-green-rovers-fc/3038005/ |publisher=Soccerway |date=13 August 2019 |access-date=26 November 2020}}</ref> During the latter stages of the 2019–20, prior to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], Ocran joined Isthmian League North Division side, [[Brentwood Town F.C.|Brentwood Town]] where he featured twice, failing to score.<ref name="Brentwood 1">{{Cite news |title=Brentwood Town – Appearances – Wilberforce Ocran – 2019-2020 |url=https://www.footballwebpages.co.uk/brentwood-town/appearances/2019-2020/wilberforce-ocran/550404 |publisher=Football Web Pages |access-date=26 November 2020}}</ref>  On 2 July 2020, it was confirmed that Ocran had left Charlton after his contract   expired.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.cafc.co.uk/news/view/5efda22caea4a/charlton-confirm-list-of-released-and-retained-players | title=Charlton confirm list of released and retained players | publisher=Charlton Athletic Official Website | date=2 July 2020 | access-date=2 July 2020}}</ref>

Prior to the 2020–21 campaign, Ocran made a move to [[Southern Football League|Southern League]] Premier Division South side, [[Farnborough F.C.|Farnborough]]<ref name="Fboro">{{Cite news |title=Farnborough – Appearances – Wilberforce Ocran |url=https://www.footballwebpages.co.uk/farnborough/appearances/wilberforce-ocran/550404 |publisher=Football Web Pages |access-date=26 November 2020}}</ref>  before returning to Brentwood Town  in October.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Ghanaian striker Wilberforce Ocran joins Brentwood Town |url=https://sportsworldghana.com/ghanaian-striker-wilberforce-ocran-joins-brentwood-town/ |publisher=Sports World Ghana |date=13 October 2020 |access-date=26 November 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Born in Ghana, Ocran grew up in north London .<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ghanasportsonline.com/2018/12/ghanaian-wilberforce-ocran-strikes-match-winner-for-charlton-in-professional-development-league/|title=Ghanaian Wilberforce Ocran strikes match-winner for Charlton in Professional Development League – Ghana Sports Online|date=10 December 2018 }}</ref>
