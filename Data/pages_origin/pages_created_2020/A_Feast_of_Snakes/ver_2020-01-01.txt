'''''A Feast of Snakes''''' is a novel by [[Harry Crews]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=77YgAQAAIAAJ&source=gbs_book_other_versions|title=A Feast of Snakes|first=Harry|last=Crews|date=January 1, 1976|publisher=Atheneum|via=Google Books}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theamericanscholar.org/harry-crews-a-feast-of-snakes/|title=Harry Crewsâ€™s A Feast of Snakes|date=April 27, 2015|website=The American Scholar}}</ref>  It was published by [[Atheneum Books]] in 1976. Many critics consider it to be Crews's best novel.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/03/30/books/harry-crews-writer-of-dark-fiction-is-dead-at-76.html|title=Harry Crews, Writer of Dark Fiction, Is Dead at 76|first=Margalit|last=Fox|date=March 30, 2012|via=NYTimes.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=uY3gFTuSzKwC&pg=PA90&lpg=PA90&dq=a+feast+of+snakes+harry+crews+1976&source=bl&ots=s8wZiqPouD&sig=ACfU3U0Nqwi78k0oxRtNtw_bKHsefVeIXQ&hl=en&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiOkdDOm97mAhWOB80KHUWsCpw4RhDoATAJegQICRAB#v=onepage&q=a+feast+of+snakes+harry+crews+1976&f=false|title=Southern Writers: A New Biographical Dictionary|first1=Joseph M.|last1=Flora|first2=Amber|last2=Vogel|date=June 21, 2006|publisher=LSU Press|via=Google Books}}</ref> It would be his last for more than a decade.<ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/pdf/20078040.pdf|title=Harry Crews after "A Childhood" on JSTOR}}</ref> 

The novel is about an annual snake roundup in [[Mystic, Georgia]].

''[[Kirkus]]'' gave the novel a glowing review, writing that "Crews is a true regional writer out of the heart of the redneck rural south and besides the brutality  . . . there's the humor, the dynamite dialogue, and the real despair--all tetched with talent."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kirkusreviews.com/book-reviews/harry-crews-2/a-feast-of-snakes/|title=A FEAST OF SNAKES by Harry Crews &#124; Kirkus Reviews|via=www.kirkusreviews.com}}</ref> [[Guy Owen (novelist)|Guy Owen]], in ''[[The Georgia Review]]'', called it a "surrealistic study of violence and competitiveness in the South."<ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/41399645|title=Review on JSTOR|author=Owen, Guy|year=1977|journal=The Georgia Review|volume=31|issue=2|pages=525-528|via=JSTOR}}</ref> ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' thought that is was "full of brilliant descriptions and characters attempting to kick and gouge their way through some back door to salvation."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://content.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,914608,00.html|title=Books: Fangs|first=R. Z.|last=Sheppard|date=September 13, 1976|via=content.time.com}}</ref> ''[[The Virginia Quarterly Review]]'' described the paperback edition as "a fierce and funny account of life in Mystic."<ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/26437885|title=REPRINTS & NEW EDITIONS|year=1987|journal=The Virginia Quarterly Review|volume=63|issue=4|pages=142-143|via=JSTOR}}</ref>

''A Feast of Snakes'' was for a time banned in [[South Africa]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/1979/03/29/crews-transforms-improverished-childhood-into-spellbinding-tales/08c8a0cc-6e31-48d8-91ab-411cd7b2ce4d/|title=Crews Transforms Improverished Childhood Into Spellbinding Tales|via=www.washingtonpost.com}}</ref>
