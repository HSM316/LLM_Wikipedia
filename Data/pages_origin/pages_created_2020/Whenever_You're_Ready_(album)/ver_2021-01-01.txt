'''''Whenever You're Ready''''' is the second studio album by [[Seattle]] [[Rock music|rock]] band [[Flop (band)|Flop]]. The band's only major label release,<ref name="AV" /> the album was released in September 1993.<ref name="AllMusic" />

== Recording ==
''Whenever You're Ready'' was recorded at Hanzsek Audio in [[Seattle]], Washington.<ref name="AllMusic" /> The record was produced by punk-era producer [[Martin Rushent]].<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web |last1=Rabid |first1=Jack |title=Whenever You're Ready - Flop {{!}} Review |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/whenever-youre-ready-mw0000102644 |website=AllMusic |accessdate=30 December 2019}}</ref>

==Release==
''Whenever You're Ready'' was released on 21 September 1993.<ref name="AllMusic" /> The record was issued in Europe on CD by [[550 Music]], in Japan through [[Epic Records|Epic]], and in the United States by 550, Epic and [[Frontier Records]] jointly.<ref name="discogs">{{cite web |title=Flop - Whenever You're Ready - Releases |url=https://www.discogs.com/Flop-Whenever-Youre-Ready/master/318631 |website=Discogs |accessdate=30 December 2019}}</ref> On cassette, the album saw release in the US through 550.<ref name="discogs" /> ''Whenever You're Ready'' was made available [[Digital music|digitally]] in 2019.<ref name="AllMusic" />

== Musical style ==
The music of ''Whenever You're Ready'' has been described as "idiosyncratic [[power pop]] that paired soaring, [[Beach Boys|Beach Boy]] harmonies and fuzzy guitars".<ref name="AV">{{cite web |last1=McLevy |first1=Alex |title=One of Seattle's finest power pop acts never found success |url=https://music.avclub.com/one-of-seattle-s-finest-power-pop-acts-never-found-succ-1798276721 |website=AV Club |accessdate=30 December 2019}}</ref>

==Commercial performance==
''Whenever You're Ready'' was apparently deemed a [[commercial failure]] by [[Sony Records|Sony]],<ref name="allmusic2">{{cite web |last1=Raggett |first1=Ned |title=World of Today - Flop - Review |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/world-of-today-mw0000647502 |website=AllMusic |accessdate=30 December 2019}}</ref> with the record failing to chart. Nevertheless, [[J.D. Considine]] of the [[Baltimore Sun]] remarked that the band were "doing pretty well" with the record, having attracted [[college radio]] airplay and even making an appearance on [[MTV]]'s "[[120 Minutes]]".<ref name="baltimoresun">{{cite news |last1=Considine |first1=J.D. |title=Flop is quickly making a name for itself |url=https://webcache.googleusercontent.com/search?q=cache:PMoNIaw3-nMJ:https://www.baltimoresun.com/news/bs-xpm-1994-02-04-1994035180-story.html+&cd=4&hl=en&ct=clnk&gl=uk |accessdate=30 December 2019 |work=The Baltimore Sun |date=4 February 1994}}</ref>
