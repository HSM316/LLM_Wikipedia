'''Jorge Luiz Barcellos''' (born 17 April 1967) is a Brazilian [[Manager (association football)|football manager]].<ref>{{WorldFootball.net}}</ref>

==Career==
Barcellos was the head coach of the [[Brazil women's national football team|Brazil women's national team]] at the [[2007 FIFA Women's World Cup]], [[Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2008 Summer Olympics]] and [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2012 Summer Olympics]]. He was also the head coach of [[Saint Louis Athletica]] in 2009.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.thetelegraph.com/sports/national_17624___article.html/brazil_new.html |title=Brazil national coach to lead St. Louis team in new women's league |publisher=Alton Telegraph |date=26 August 2008 |access-date=21 October 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://www.webcitation.org/5j5J94PlF?url=http://www.thetelegraph.com/sports/national_17624___article.html/brazil_new.html |archive-date=17 August 2009 }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Barcellos was born in [[Nova Iguaçu]], Brazil, in the municipality now known as [[Japeri]].<ref>{{Soccerway}}</ref>
