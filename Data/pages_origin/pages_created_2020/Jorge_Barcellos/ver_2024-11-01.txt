'''Jorge Luiz Barcellos Martins''' (born 17 April 1967) is a Brazilian [[association football|football]] [[Coach (sport)|coach]], currently the head coach of [[Sport Club Internacional|Internacional's]] [[Sport Club Internacional (women)|women's team]].<ref name="WF">{{WorldFootball.net}}</ref>

==Career==
Barcellos was the head coach of the [[Brazil women's national football team|Brazil women's national team]] at the [[2007 FIFA Women's World Cup]], [[Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2008 Summer Olympics]] and [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2012 Summer Olympics]]. He was appointed as head coach of [[Saint Louis Athletica]] on 26 August 2008,<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.thetelegraph.com/sports/national_17624___article.html/brazil_new.html |title=Brazil national coach to lead St. Louis team in new women's league |publisher=Alton Telegraph |date=26 August 2008 |access-date=21 October 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090617210509/http://www.thetelegraph.com/sports/national_17624___article.html/brazil_new.html |archive-date=17 June 2009 }}</ref> remaining in his role until the team's folding in May 2010.<ref>{{cite news |last=French |first=Scott |url=https://www.espn.com/blog/los-angeles/soccer/post/_/id/731/wps-more-on-athleticas-demise |title=WPS: More on Athletica's demise |work=[[ESPN]] |date=May 27, 2010 |access-date=July 16, 2022}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Barcellos was born in [[Nova Iguaçu]], Brazil, in the municipality now known as [[Japeri]].<ref>{{Soccerway}}</ref>
