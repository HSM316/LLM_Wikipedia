'''Traian Georgescu''' (20 March 1931 – 15 May 2008) was a [[Romania]]n football [[Defender (association football)|defender]] who spent his entire career at [[FC Universitatea Cluj|Universitatea Cluj]]. He was [[FC Universitatea Cluj|"U" Cluj]]'s captain when the club won the [[1964–65 Cupa României|1964–65]] [[Cupa României]].<ref name=cluj>{{cite web | url=https://clujcapitala.ro/2018/03/fc-universitatea-cluj-in-urma-cu-87-de-ani-pe-20-martie-se-nastea-doctorul-traian-georgescu-d-2008-unul-dintre-cei-mai-mari-jucatori-care-au-purtat-banderola-universitatii-cluj-si-cel-c/ | title="U" Cluj ne reamintește astăzi de Traian Georgescu căpitanul studenților la finala câștigată în 1965.| publisher=Clujcapitala.ro| language=Romanian |trans-title="U" Cluj reminds us today of Traian Georgescu, the captain of the students at the final won in 1965.| date=20 March 2018 | access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref>

Georgescu also worked as a [[surgeon]] and when his team colleague [[Remus Câmpeanu]] was diagnosed with [[appendicitis]], Georgescu was the one who operated him at Câmpeanu's request.<ref name=romaniadevarati>{{cite web | url=http://www.romani-adevarati.ro/dr-traian-georgescu-a-murit-chirurgul/ | title=A murit Chirurgul - DR. TRAIAN GEORGESCU.| publisher=Clujcapitala.ro| language=Romanian |trans-title=The surgeon has died - DR. TRAIAN GEORGESCU.| date=22 May 2008 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080602074613/http://www.romani-adevarati.ro/dr-traian-georgescu-a-murit-chirurgul/| access-date=1 January 2020| archive-date=2 June 2008}}</ref> He died in 2008 a few years after being diagnosed with [[Alzheimer's disease]].<ref name=romaniadevarati/>
