'''Traian Georgescu''' (20 March 1931 – 15 May 2008) was a [[Romania]]n football [[Defender (association football)|defender]] who spent his entire career at [[FC Universitatea Cluj|Universitatea Cluj]]. He was [[FC Universitatea Cluj|"U" Cluj]]'s captain when the club won the [[1964–65 Cupa României]].<ref name=cluj>{{cite web | url=https://clujcapitala.ro/2018/03/fc-universitatea-cluj-in-urma-cu-87-de-ani-pe-20-martie-se-nastea-doctorul-traian-georgescu-d-2008-unul-dintre-cei-mai-mari-jucatori-care-au-purtat-banderola-universitatii-cluj-si-cel-c/ | title="U" Cluj ne reamintește astăzi de Traian Georgescu căpitanul studenților la finala câștigată în 1965.| publisher=Clujcapitala.ro| language=Romanian |trans-title="U" Cluj reminds us today of Traian Georgescu, the captain of the students at the final won in 1965.| date=20 March 2018 | access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
{{Quote box|
 width=20%
|align=left
|quote="The man of balance. An exponential player who was a conductor of the defense. At the same time, he feeds the midfielders or forwards with the precision of his passes. A special altruist and a special captain. The way he was, is the way a captain must be"
|source=–Former "U" Cluj teammate, [[Remus Câmpeanu]] talking about Georgescu<ref name=4ever/>}}
Traian Georgescu was born on 20 March 1931 in [[Ploiești]], [[Romania]] where he started to play junior level football in 1946 at [[FC Prahova Ploiești|Prahova Ploiești]], switching teams in 1948 when he went at neighboring club,  Concordia Ploiești.<ref name=4ever/> He went to college at the [[Iuliu Hațieganu University of Medicine and Pharmacy|Faculty of Medicine]] from [[Cluj-Napoca]], where he started to play senior level football at local club, [[FC Universitatea Cluj|Universitatea]], making his [[Liga I|Divizia A]] debut on 15 April 1951 under coach [[Iuliu Baratky]] in a 4–1 away loss in front of [[CSM Jiul Petroșani|Flacăra Petroșani]].<ref name=4ever/><ref name=4ever2>{{cite web | url=https://www.4everucluj.ro/istorie/1951-1952/ | title=Universitatea Cluj 1951 season| publisher=4everucluj.ro| language=Romanian}}</ref> In the same year, he scored all the goals of Universitatea, in the 3–1 victory against [[CSA Steaua București|CCA București]], the second goal being considered by the press of the time as the most beautiful goal of that season for Universitatea, when Georgescu made a run from the center of the field and with a shot from 20 meters he defeated [[Goalkeeper (association football)|goalkeeper]] [[Ion Voinescu]].<ref name=4ever/><ref name=4ever2/> By the end of his first season he would score a total of six goals, except for the [[hat-trick]] against CCA, he scored once against each of [[FC Dinamo București|Dinamo București]], [[FC CFR Timișoara|Locomotiva Timișoara]] and [[CS Gaz Metan Mediaș|Flacăra Mediaș]], the latter being in the [[1951 Cupa României|Cupa României]].<ref name=4ever/><ref name=4ever2/> In 1954 he was called up by [[Romania national football team|Romania's national team]] coach, [[Ștefan Dobay]] for a friendly with [[Hungary national football team|Hungary]] in which however he did not play.<ref name=4ever/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://eu-football.info/_match.php?id=14521|title=Hungary 5-1 Romania|publisher=European Football|access-date=4 March 2024}}</ref> After the team relegated in [[1956 Divizia A|1956]], Georgescu stayed with the club, also becoming the team's captain after [[Mircea Luca]] retired, helping it promote back to the first league after one year in which scored four goals in 23 matches.<ref name=4ever/><ref name=4ever3>{{cite web | url=https://www.4everucluj.ro/istorie/1956-1957/ | title=Universitatea Cluj 1956–57 season| publisher=4everucluj.ro| language=Romanian}}<br>{{cite web | url=https://www.4everucluj.ro/istorie/1957-1958/ | title=Universitatea Cluj 1957–58 season| publisher=4everucluj.ro| language=Romanian}}</ref> He helped "U" Cluj win the [[1964–65 Cupa României]], being used all the minutes by coach [[Andrei Sepci]] in the 2–1 victory from the [[1965 Cupa României final|final]] with [[FC Argeș Pitești|Dinamo Pitești]].<ref name=4ever/><ref name=cluj/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.romaniansoccer.ro/romanian_cup/1964_1965.shtml|title=Romanian Cup – Season 1964–1965|publisher=RomanianSoccer|access-date=4 March 2024}}</ref> In the following season, Georgescu made his debut in European competitions, playing three games in the [[1965–66 European Cup Winners' Cup]], helping ''The Red Caps'' eliminate Austrian team, [[1. Wiener Neustädter SC|Wiener Neustadt]] in the first round, being eliminated in the following round by [[Atlético Madrid]].<ref name=4ever/><ref>{{cite web|publisher=WorldFootball|url=https://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/traian-georgescu/ec-der-pokalsieger-1965-1966/universitatea-cluj/3/|title=Traian Georgescu. Cup Winners Cup 1965/1966|access-date=4 March 2024}}</ref> He retired shortly after the double with the Spaniards, having a total of 280 Divizia A games with 13 goals scored.<ref name=4ever/>

==Personal life==
Georgescu also worked as a [[surgeon]] and when his team colleague [[Remus Câmpeanu]] was diagnosed with [[appendicitis]], Georgescu was the one who operated him at Câmpeanu's request.<ref name=romaniadevarati>{{cite web | url=http://www.romani-adevarati.ro/dr-traian-georgescu-a-murit-chirurgul/ | title=A murit Chirurgul - DR. TRAIAN GEORGESCU.| publisher=Clujcapitala.ro| language=Romanian |trans-title=The surgeon has died - DR. TRAIAN GEORGESCU.| date=22 May 2008 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080602074613/http://www.romani-adevarati.ro/dr-traian-georgescu-a-murit-chirurgul/| access-date=1 January 2020| archive-date=2 June 2008}}</ref> He died in 2008 a few years after being diagnosed with [[Alzheimer's disease]].<ref name=romaniadevarati/>
