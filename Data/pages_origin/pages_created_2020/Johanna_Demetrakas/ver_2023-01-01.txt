'''Johanna Demetrakas''' is an American independent filmmaker and television director known for documentaries like ''[[Womanhouse]]'' and ''Right Out of History''. She has worked as a professor at Cal Arts and USC.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.huffpost.com/author/johanna-demetrakas|title=Johanna Demetrakas {{!}} HuffPost|website=www.huffpost.com|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>

== Career ==
She attended [[Rhode Island School of Design]], where she majored in Illustration. In 1971, Demetrakas co-directed the doc ''[[Celebration at Big Sur]]'', which captured performances by Joan Baez, Joni Mitchell, and Dorothy Morrison at the [[Big Sur Folk Festival]]. The film was acquired by 20th Century Fox.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newspapers.com/image/599258627/?terms=%22johanna+demetrakas%22|title=Acquires 'Big Sur'|last=|first=|date=19 Apr 1971|website=The Salt Lake Tribune|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> She followed that up with Womanhouse, a doc about the feminist art installation at Cal Arts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.newspapers.com/image/382845338/?terms=%22johanna+demetrakas%22|title=Documentaries of Woman Artists|last=Gross|first=Linda|date=11 Oct 1975|website=The Los Angeles Times|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>
