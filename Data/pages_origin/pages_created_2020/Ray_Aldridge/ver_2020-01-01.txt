'''Raymon Huebert Aldridge''' is an American author of [[speculative fiction]]. He writes under the name '''Ray Aldridge.'''<ref name="ISFDB">{{isfdb name|id=1434|name=Ray Aldridge}}</ref><ref name="SFE">Clute, John, and Mike Ashley. "Aldridge, Ray." ==Life and writing career==
Aldridge was born in Syracuse, New York.<ref name="SFE"/> The appearance of his first published work of science fiction has been credited to the July 1987 issue of ''[[Amazing Stories]]'',<ref name="SFE"/>, although an earlier work seems to have previously appeared in the anthology ''L. Ron Hubbard Presents Writers of the Future, Volume II'' in March 1986.<ref name="ISFDB"/> Aldridge's style has been likened to those of [[Cordwainer Smith]] and [[Jack Vance]].<ref name="SFE"/>

Aldridge's writing has appeared in various periodicals and anthologies, including ''[[Aboriginal Science Fiction]]'', ''Amazing Stories'', ''The Bulletin of the Science Fiction Writers of America'', ''[[Full Spectrum|Full Spectrum 4]]'', ''L. Ron Hubbard Presents Writers of the Future, Volume II'', ''L. Ron Hubbard Presents Writers of the Future, Volume VII'', ''[[The Magazine of Fantasy & Science Fiction]]'', ''Pulphouse: The Hardback Magazine'', ''Science Fiction Age'', and ''Whatdunits''. His work has been translated into [[German language|German]], [[Italian language|Italian]], and [[French language|French]].<ref name="ISFDB"/>

==Recognition==
Aldridge's story "Gate of Faces" won the 1992 SF Chronicle Award for Best Novelette, and was nominated for the 1992 [[Nebula Award for Best Novelette]]. His story "The Beauty Addict" was nominated for the 1994 [[Nebula Award for Best Novella]] and placed eighth in the 1994 [[Locus Award for Best Novelette|Locus Poll Award for Best Novelette]]. His story "The Spine Divers" placed third in the 1996 [[Theodore Sturgeon Award]]. His stories "Steel Dogs," "Hyena Eyes," "The Cold Cage" were also [[Locus Award|Locus Poll Award]] nominees.<ref name="ISFDB"/>
