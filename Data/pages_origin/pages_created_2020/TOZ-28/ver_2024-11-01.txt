The '''TOZ-28''' (''ТОЗ-28'') is a Soviet hunting [[combination gun]].<ref name=autogenerated1 /><ref name=autogenerated3>А. Соколов. Старые модели. ТОЗ-28 // журнал «Охота и охотничье хозяйство», № 12, декабрь 1991. стр.27</ref><ref name=autogenerated2 />

== History ==
The gun was designed by G. P. Chetverikova (''Г. П. Четверикова'') in early 1960s.<ref name=autogenerated2 />

Since 1963 began the production of this shotgun by [[Tula Arms Plant]].<ref name=autogenerated3 /><ref name=autogenerated2 /> In 1964 it was shown at [[VDNKh (Russia)|VDNKh exhibition]] in [[Moscow]].<ref>Ружейные новинки на ВДНХ // журнал «Охота и охотничье хозяйство», № 10, 1964. (2-я страница обложки)</ref> In 1965, this gun was included in the list of firearms offered for export to other countries.<ref>Охотничье трехствольное ружьё ТОЗ-28 // Спортивно-охотничье оружие и патроны. Бухарест, "Внешторгиздат", 1965. стр.60-61</ref>

On December 13, 1996, president of the Russian Federation [[Boris Yeltsin|B. N. Yeltsin]] signed the federal law № 150, which entered into force on July 1, 1997. In accordance with this law, possession of civilian firearms chambered for [[handgun]] ammunition was prohibited.<ref>ст.3 Гражданское оружие // Федеральный закон № 150-ФЗ от 13 декабря 1996 года "Об оружии"</ref> As a result, since July 1, 1997, all TOZ-28 guns were banned on the territory of [[the Russian Federation]] and they had to be handed over to governmental law enforcement agencies for destruction.

== Design ==
TOZ-28 is a combination gun, with one rifled barrel under two other.<ref name=autogenerated1 /><ref name=autogenerated2 />
* smoothbore barrels are chambered for standard 20/70&nbsp;mm R shotgun shells.<ref name=autogenerated3 /><ref name=autogenerated2 />
* a rifled barrel was chambered for [[7.62×38mmR]] round<ref name=autogenerated1 /><ref name=autogenerated2 /> or special 6.5×38mmR round<ref name=autogenerated3 /><ref name=autogenerated2 />

It is equipped with [[Extractor (firearms)|extractor]].<ref name=autogenerated2 />

All guns have a birch [[Stock (firearms)|shoulder stock]] (with or without cheekpiece) and fore-end.<ref name=autogenerated1 /><ref name=autogenerated3 /><ref name=autogenerated2 />

== Museum exhibits ==
* TOZ-28 shotgun was in collection of Tula State Arms Museum in [[Tula Kremlin]]<ref>Г. М. Чуднов. Тульская коллекция охотничьих ружей // журнал «Охота и охотничье хозяйство», № 12, 1982. стр.33</ref>
