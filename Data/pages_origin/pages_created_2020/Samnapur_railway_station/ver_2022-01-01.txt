'''Samnapur railway station''' is a small railway station in [[Balaghat district]], [[Madhya Pradesh]]. Its code is '''SMC'''. It serves [[Samnapur]] village.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://indiarailinfo.com/station/blog/samnapur-smc/3742| title=SMC/Samnapur | website=India Rail Info}}</ref>

Samnapur was a railway station of the [[Satpura Railway|Satpura]] [[Narrow-gauge railway|narrow gauge]] ({{track gauge|2ft6in}}) railway, today part of [[Nagpur SEC railway division|Nagpur railway division]] of [[South East Central Railway zone|South East Central Railway]] zone. In October 2015 all narrow gauge network in Nagpur division (622&nbsp;km), was closed for [[Track gauge conversion|gauge conversion]], except {{stnlnk|Nagpur}}–[[Nagbhir Junction railway station|Naghbir]] line.<ref>[http://www.business-standard.com/article/pti-stories/narrow-gauge-section-closes-from-oct-1-in-satputda-valley-115092000287_1.html Narrow gauge section closes from 1 October in Satputda valley]</ref><ref>[http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/nagpur/Broad-view-on-narrow-gauge-Full-Stop/articleshow/49612922.cms Broad view on narrow gauge. Full Stop]</ref><ref>[http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/nagpur/Worlds-biggest-narrow-gauge-train-network-set-to-sail-into-history/articleshow/49158676.cms World's biggest narrow gauge train network set to sail into history]</ref> The approval of the gauge conversion works have been involved in controversy, since the [[Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change|Union's environment ministry]] authorized the construction works in Balaghat–Nainpur section (77&nbsp;km), which passes through [[Kanha Tiger Reserve|Kanha]] – [[Pench Tiger Reserve|Pench]] tiger corridor.<ref name="dnaindia">{{cite news | url=https://www.dnaindia.com/india/report-after-nh-7-gondia-jabalpur-rail-line-doubling-allowed-through-kanha-pench-tiger-corridor-2191864 | title=After NH-7, Gondia–Jabalpur rail line doubling allowed through Kanha–Pench tiger corridor | date=21 March 2016 | first=Nikhil M | last=Ghanekar | publisher=DNA | accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref>

The station is on the [[Jabalpur Junction railway station|Jabalpur]]–[[Gondia Junction railway station|Gondia]] line (227&nbsp;km),<ref name="dnaindia" /> almost entirely converted to [[Broad-gauge railway|broad gauge]]. {{As of|January 2020}}, a small stretch of 25&nbsp;km remains closed for conversion, from Samnapur to {{stnlnk|Lamta}} station (25&nbsp;km).
