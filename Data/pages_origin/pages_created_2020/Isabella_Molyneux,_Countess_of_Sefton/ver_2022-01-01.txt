'''Isabella Molyneux, Countess of Sefton''', formerly '''Viscountess Molyneux''', (née '''Lady Isabella Stanhope'''; c. 1748 – 29 January 1819) was a British peeress and society figure.

== Biography ==
Lady Isabella Stanhope was the second child of [[William Stanhope, 2nd Earl of Harrington]] and [[Caroline Stanhope, Countess of Harrington|Lady Caroline FitzRoy]], a daughter of [[Charles FitzRoy, 2nd Duke of Grafton]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.gogmsite.net/grand-ladies-of-the-eightee/lady-isabella-stanhope-daug.html|title=Lady Isabella Stanhope, daughter of the second Earl of Harrington and wife of the 1st Earl of Sefton by ? &#124; Grand Ladies &#124; gogm|website=www.gogmsite.net}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=qGhHAQAAMAAJ&q=Lady+Isabella+Stanhope&pg=PA288|title=The Annual Peerage of the British Empire|date=December 30, 1829|publisher=Saunders and Otley|via=Google Books}}</ref> She was an older sister of [[Charles Stanhope, 3rd Earl of Harrington]].

According to the memoirs of [[Elizabeth Craven|Elizabeth Craven, Baroness Craven]], Lady Isabella refused the hand of the [[Duke of Fitz-James]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=fLLTAAAAMAAJ&q=Lady+Isabella+Stanhope&pg=PA23|title=The Beautiful Lady Craven: The Original Memoirs of Elizabeth, Baroness Craven, Afterwards Margravine of Anspach and Bayreuth and Princess Berkeley of the Holy Roman Empire (1750-1828)|first=Baroness Elizabeth Craven|last=Craven|date=December 30, 1914|publisher=John Lane|via=Google Books}}</ref>

On 27 November 1768 she married [[Charles Molyneux, 1st Earl of Sefton|Charles Molyneux, 8th Viscount Molyneux]], the future [[Earl of Sefton|1st Earl of Sefton]] in the [[Peerage of Ireland]].<ref>{{cite book|title=Debrett's Complete Peerage of The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland|url=https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_Ru4UAAAAQAAJ|editor-last=Courthope|editor-first=William|editor-link =William Courthope (officer of arms)|location=London|year=1838|edition=22nd|page=555}}</ref> On 18 September 1772 Lady Sefton gave birth to a son, the future [[William Molyneux, 2nd Earl of Sefton|2nd Earl of Sefton]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.historyhome.co.uk/people/sefton.htm|title=Sir William Philip Molyneux 2nd Earl of Sefton (1772 - 1838)|website=www.historyhome.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://archive.org/details/historygenealog00unkngoog|title=History, genealogical and biographical, of the Molyneux families|first=Nellie Zada Rice|last=Molyneux|date=December 30, 1904|publisher=Syracuse, N.Y., C. W. Bardeen|via=Internet Archive}}</ref>
[[File:Isabella Stanhope, later Countess of Sefton (1748-1819), by Catherine Read (1723-1778).jpg|thumb|left|Isabella Stanhope, later Countess of Sefton (1748-1819) ([[Catherine Read]],circa 1768)]]
She was painted by notable painters including [[Catherine Read]] and [[Thomas Gainsborough]], and engraved by the artist [[James Watson (engraver)|James Watson]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://artuk.org/discover/artworks/isabella-17481819-viscountess-molyneux-1st-countess-of-sefton-98574|title=Isabella (1748–1819), Viscountess Molyneux, 1st Countess of Sefton &#124; Art UK|website=artuk.org}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.liverpoolmuseums.org.uk/walker/collections/paintings/18c/item.aspx?id=244803|title=Isabella, Viscountess Molyneux, later Countess of Sefton, 18th century|website=www.liverpoolmuseums.org.uk}}</ref> A portrait of her by Gainsborough was unveiled at the inaugural exhibit of the [[Royal Academy of Art]]. A poem written in 1768 by [[Temple Luttrell]], son of [[Simon Luttrell, 1st Earl of Carhampton]], titled ''Ode to Lady Isabella Stanshope on Her Birthday'', was dedicated to her.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=4nAhCwAAQBAJ&q=Lady+Isabella+Stanhope&pg=PA491|title=The Secrets of Generation: Reproduction in the Long Eighteenth Century|first1=Raymond|last1=Stephanson|first2=Darren|last2=Wagner|date=November 26, 2015|publisher=University of Toronto Press|isbn=9781442666931|via=Google Books}}</ref>
