== Events ==
{{expand section|date=December 2020}}
===January===
*9 January – The UK and Irish governments publish [[2020 Northern Ireland Agreement|a deal]] aimed at restoring the [[Northern Ireland Executive]]; the [[Democratic Unionist Party]] gives its backing to the agreement.<ref>{{Cite news|first=Andrew |last=Madden |url=https://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/news/northern-ireland/dup-accept-governments-draft-agreement-to-restore-stormont-sinn-fein-studying-text-38849112.html |title=DUP accept governments' draft agreement to restore Stormont, Sinn Fein 'studying' text |work=Belfast Telegraph |publisher=Independent News and Media |date=9 January 2020 |accessdate=12 January 2020}}</ref>
*10 January – [[Sinn Féin]] gives its backing to [[2020 Northern Ireland Agreement|a deal]] to restore power-sharing government to Northern Ireland for the first time since 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-51068774 |title=Sinn Féin agrees to rejoin NI government after deal |work=[[BBC News]] |publisher=BBC |date=10 January 2020 |accessdate=10 January 2020}}</ref>
*11 January – The [[Northern Ireland Assembly]] reconvenes after a three-year hiatus; [[Democratic Unionist Party|DUP]] leader [[Arlene Foster]] is appointed [[First Minister of Northern Ireland|Northern Ireland's first minister]], while [[Sinn Féin]]'s [[Michelle O'Neill]] is appointed [[Deputy First Minister of Northern Ireland|deputy first minister]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-51077397 |title=Stormont deal: Arlene Foster and Michelle O'Neill new top NI ministers |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=11 January 2020 |accessdate=11 January 2020}}</ref>
*13 January – [[Same-sex marriage]] is [[Same-sex marriage in Northern Ireland|legalised]] in Northern Ireland.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-51086276 |title=Same-sex marriage now legal in Northern Ireland |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=13 January 2020 |accessdate=13 January 2020}}</ref>

===February===
*5 February – [[Brexit Day bomb plot]]: The Police Service of Northern Ireland finds a Continuity Irish Republican Army [[Improvised explosive device|bomb]] [[car bomb|in a lorry]] in Lurgan, County Armagh.
*11 February – The first same-sex marriage ceremony to be held in Northern Ireland takes place at a hotel in [[Carrickfergus]], [[County Antrim]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-51466588 |title=Same-sex marriage: Couple make history as first in NI |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=11 February 2020 |accessdate=11 February 2020}}</ref>
*27 February – Authorities confirm the first case of [[COVID-19 pandemic|coronavirus]] in Northern Ireland.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-51667483 |title=Northern Ireland diagnoses first coronavirus case |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=27 February 2020 |accessdate=27 February 2020}}</ref>

=== March 2020 ===

* 18 March – The [[Northern Ireland Assembly]] reduces its workload by suspending all non-essential Assembly business. It is closed to the public from the following day.<ref>{{cite web|title=Assembly Business and Covid-19|url=http://www.niassembly.gov.uk/news-and-media/press-releases/session-2019--2020/assembly-business-and-covid-19/|access-date=7 April 2020|website=www.niassembly.gov.uk}}</ref>
* 19 March – The first COVID-19 death is confirmed in Northern Ireland.<ref>{{Cite news|date=19 March 2020|title=First coronavirus death in Northern Ireland|work=ITV News|url=https://www.itv.com/news/utv/2020-03-19/first-coronavirus-death-in-northern-ireland/|url-status=live|access-date=19 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200319201706/https://www.itv.com/news/utv/2020-03-19/first-coronavirus-death-in-northern-ireland/|archive-date=19 March 2020}}</ref>
* 23 March – With the UK death toll hitting 335 deaths and 3 in Northern Ireland, [[Boris Johnson]] announces a nationwide 'Stay at Home' order{{Citation needed|reason=The term "order" is not supported by the current source)|date=November 2020}} which will come into effect as of midnight and will be reviewed every 3 weeks.<ref name="address-2020-03-23">{{cite web|date=23 March 2020|title=Prime Minister's National Address (23/3/20)|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/speeches/pm-address-to-the-nation-on-coronavirus-23-march-2020}}</ref> This becomes known as the [[COVID-19 pandemic lockdowns|UK lockdown]].
* 28 March – At 11pm, new regulations come into force in Northern Ireland giving authorities the power to force businesses to close, and impose fines on them if they refuse, as well as on people leaving their homes without a "reasonable excuse". The measures, introduced by the [[Northern Ireland Executive]], bring Northern Ireland into line with the rest of the UK.<ref>{{Cite news|date=28 March 2020|title=New NI Covid-19 regulations come into force|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52079959|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Northern Ireland imposes fines up to £5,000 for those breaching social distancing rules|url=https://www.itv.com/news/2020-03-28/new-regulations-to-enforce-coronavirus-restrictions-agreed-by-stormont/|website=ITV News}}</ref>

=== April 2020 ===

* 6 April – The [[Orange Order|Orange Lodge of Ireland]] announces that the traditional [[Twelfth of July]] parades in Northern Ireland have been cancelled for 2020.<ref>{{cite news|date=6 April 2020|title=Coronavirus: Twelfth of July parades cancelled due to outbreak|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52184743|access-date=7 April 2020}}</ref>
* 7 April – *The Northern Ireland Assembly establishes a COVID-19 Response Committees.<ref>{{cite web|title=Speaker Announces New Ad-Hoc COVID-19 Response Committee Meeting|url=http://www.niassembly.gov.uk/news-and-media/press-releases/session-2019--2020/speaker-announces-new-ad-hoc-covid-19-response-committee-meeting/|access-date=7 April 2020|website=www.niassembly.gov.uk}}</ref>
* 11 April – Occupancy of critical care beds in England peaks at around 58% of capacity. Occupancy in the month of April for Scotland and Wales will only briefly exceed 40%, while Northern Ireland reported a peak of 51% early in the month.<ref>{{cite web|date=27 April 2020|title=Slides to accompany coronavirus press conference|url=https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/881555/2020-04-27_COVID-19_Press_Conference_Slides__10_.pdf|access-date=28 April 2020|website=GOV.UK|publisher=Prime Minister's Office|page=5}}</ref>
* 15 April – [[Arlene Foster]], the [[First Minister of Northern Ireland]], extends the period of lockdown in Northern Ireland to 9 May.<ref>{{cite web|last=McCormack|first=Jayne|date=15 April 2020|title=Three-week extension to NI coronavirus lockdown|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52298748|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 24 April –
** UK Transport Secretary [[Grant Shapps]] announces bilateral discussions with the Irish and French governments to safeguard freight routes, and with the Northern Ireland Executive regarding support for passenger flights.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|date=24 April 2020|title=£17 million government package to protect freight routes to Northern Ireland|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/17-million-government-package-to-protect-freight-routes-to-northern-ireland|access-date=25 April 2020|website=GOV.UK|publisher=Department for Transport}}</ref> Funding is to be provided to support ferry routes to Northern Ireland,<ref name=":1" /> the [[Isle of Wight]] and the [[Isles of Scilly]].<ref>{{cite web|date=24 April 2020|title=Emergency fund of up to £10.5 million to support lifeline transport links to the Isle of Wight and the Isles of Scilly|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/news/emergency-fund-of-up-to-105-million-to-support-lifeline-transport-links-to-the-isle-of-wight-and-the-isles-of-scilly|access-date=25 April 2020|website=GOV.UK|publisher=Department for Transport}}</ref>
** The Northern Ireland Executive agrees to reopen cemeteries in Northern Ireland following public pressure; they had been closed since March.<ref>{{cite web|date=25 April 2020|title=First cemeteries reopen following policy change|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52427769|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>

=== May 2020 ===

* 10 May – The UK government updates its coronavirus message from "stay at home, protect the NHS, save lives" to "stay alert, control the virus, save lives". The Opposition Labour Party expresses concern the slogan could be confusing, and leaders of the devolved governments in Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland say they will keep the original slogan.<ref>{{cite web|date=10 May 2020|title=Minister defends 'stay alert' advice amid backlash|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-52605819|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 12 May – The Northern Ireland Executive publishes a five-stage plan for exiting lockdown. Unlike those announced in England and the Republic of Ireland, the plans do not include any dates when steps may be taken.<ref>{{Cite news|last1=McCormack|first1=Jayne|date=12 May 2020|title=NI five-step plan for easing lockdown published|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52624048}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|date=12 May 2020|title=CORONAVIRUS EXECUTIVE APPROACH TO DECISION-MAKING|url=https://www.executiveoffice-ni.gov.uk/sites/default/files/publications/execoffice/execuitveour-approach-to-decision-making.pdf|publisher=Northern Ireland Executive}}</ref>
* 14 May – Northern Ireland First Minister [[Arlene Foster]] announces the first steps for easing the lockdown in Northern Ireland, with garden centres and recycling centres allowed to reopen from Monday 18 May. Marriage ceremonies where a person is terminally ill will also be allowed.<ref>{{Cite news|date=14 May 2020|title=First moves to ease NI lockdown to start next week|work=BBC News|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52666472}}</ref>
* 19 May – Northern Ireland further eases its lockdown measures. Groups of up to six people who do not share the same household are allowed to meet up outdoors, so long as they maintain social distancing. Churches are allowed to reopen for private prayer, and the playing of sports such as golf and tennis can resume.<ref>{{cite web|date=18 May 2020|title=More NI lockdown measures to be lifted|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52711301|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 21 May – [[Department of Education (Northern Ireland)|Northern Ireland Education Minister]] [[Peter Weir (politician)|Peter Weir]] outlines plans for schools to reopen in Northern Ireland in August, with a phased return for students.<ref>{{cite web|date=21 May 2020|title=Some NI pupils will return to school in August|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52759165|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 26 May –
** For the first day since 18 March, no new COVID deaths are reported in Northern Ireland. [[Robin Swann]], the [[Department of Health (Northern Ireland)|Northern Ireland Health Minister]], describes it as "a clear sign of progress".<ref>{{cite web|date=26 May 2020|title=No Covid-19 deaths reported in NI on Tuesday|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52810422|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
** The [[Northern Ireland Executive]] agrees to further relax lockdown restrictions from 8 June, when large retailers, car showrooms and shops in retail parks will be allowed to open, and outdoor weddings attended by ten people will be permitted.<ref>{{Cite web|date=28 May 2020|title=NI aims to allow small outdoor weddings in June|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52809997|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 31 May – The Northern Ireland Executive agrees to ease the lockdown measures for people shielding at home from 8 June, when they will be allowed outdoors with members of their household, or to meet one member of another household if they are living alone.<ref>{{Cite web|date=1 June 2020|title=People shielding allowed outdoors from 8 June|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52874529|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>

=== June 2020 ===

* 4 June – [[Debenhams]] announces plans to reopen three stores in Northern Ireland from 8 June, followed by 50 in England a week later.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Simpson|first=Emma|date=4 June 2020|title=Debenhams to start reopening shops after lockdown|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-52918565|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 7 June – No new deaths are recorded for Scotland or Northern Ireland over the most recent 24 hour period; it is the first time Scotland has recorded no new deaths since lockdown began in March.<ref>{{Cite web|date=7 June 2020|title=No new coronavirus deaths in 24 hours in Scotland|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-52956223|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Scotland reports no new virus deaths for first time since lockdown as UK reports lowest number of fatalities since March|url=https://www.itv.com/news/2020-06-07/scotland-reports-no-new-coronavirus-deaths-for-first-time-since-lockdown/|website=ITV News}}</ref>
* 11 June – The Northern Ireland Executive meets to agree the easing of a number of lockdown measures, including allowing the reopening of small shops and shopping centres from the following day, and letting those who live alone form a bubble with members of one other household from Saturday 13 June.<ref>{{Cite web|last=McCormack|first=Jayne|date=11 June 2020|title=Single people in NI can stay indoors with loved ones|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-52978413|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 12 June – Non-essential retailers reopen in Northern Ireland.<ref>{{Cite web|last=McCormack|first=Jayne|date=12 June 2020|title=Shops in NI reopen their doors|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53007885|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 13 June – Parts of the [[Health Protection (Coronavirus, Restrictions) (England) Regulations 2020|Health Protection (Coronavirus, Restrictions) (England) (Amendment No. 4) Regulations 2020]] (SI 588) come into effect. In England and Northern Ireland, households with one adult may now become linked with one other household of any size, allowing them to be treated as one for the purpose of permitted gatherings. This also allows the members of one household to stay overnight at the home of the other. The government refers to this as a ”support bubble”.<ref name="Gvt13June">{{Cite web|date=13 June 2020|title=Meeting people from outside your household|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200613131448/https://www.gov.uk/guidance/meeting-people-from-outside-your-household|access-date=13 June 2020|website=gov.uk}}</ref> The rules on gatherings are also relaxed to allow medical appointments and births to be accompanied, and to permit some visits to people in hospital, hospices and care homes.
* 15 June – [[Diane Dodds]], Northern Ireland's [[Department for the Economy|Economy Minister]], announces that bars, restaurants and cafes in Northern Ireland can reopen from 3 July.<ref>{{Cite web|date=15 June 2020|title=NI's hotels and bars can reopen from 3 July|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53048414|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 18 June – The Northern Ireland Executive agrees to reduce social distancing at schools from 2 metres to 1 metre, with a target date for schools to return on 24 August.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Meredith|first=Robbie|date=18 June 2020|title=Pupils to follow 1m social distancing at schools|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53098017|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref> Non-urgent dental treatment will resume in Northern Ireland from 29 June,<ref>{{Cite web|last=Connolly|first=Marie-Louise|date=18 June 2020|title=Non-urgent dental care to resume in June, says BDA|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53094238|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref> and hairdressers, barbers and beauty salons can reopen from 6 July.<ref>{{Cite web|date=18 June 2020|title=Hairdressers can reopen in NI from 6 July|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53068658|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 19 June – Northern Ireland Education Minister [[Peter Weir]] confirms the Northern Ireland Executive have agreed to extend the country's free meals scheme to cover the summer holidays.<ref>{{Cite web|date=19 June 2020|title=NI Executive to extend free school meals scheme|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53111146|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 20 June – *Northern Ireland records one death in the most recent 24 hour period, but no new cases of COVID-19 for the first time since March.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Coronavirus Northern Ireland: Health minister considers permanent memorial to those who have died|url=https://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/news/health/coronavirus/coronavirus-northern-ireland-health-minister-considers-permanent-memorial-to-those-who-have-died-39301148.html|via=www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=20 June 2020|title=NI records no new Covid-19 cases for first time|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53122657|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 21 June – No new COVID-19 deaths are recorded for Scotland and Northern Ireland.<ref>{{Cite web|date=21 June 2020|title=No new coronavirus deaths in Scotland for last 24 hours|url=https://news.stv.tv/scotland/no-new-coronavirus-deaths-in-scotland-for-last-24-hours|website=STV News}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=No new coronavirus-related deaths recorded in NI|url=https://www.itv.com/news/utv/2020-06-21/no-new-coronavirus-related-deaths-recorded-in-ni/|website=ITV News}}</ref>
* 22 June – The Northern Ireland Executive agrees to allow up to six people to meet up indoors from the following day.<ref>{{Cite web|last=McCormack|first=Jayne|date=22 June 2020|title=Groups of six can meet indoors in NI from Tuesday|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53130995|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 25 June – The Northern Ireland Executive agrees to reduce social distancing from two metres to one metre from 29 June, as well as a raft of other lockdown easing measures for Northern Ireland. These include reopening betting shops on 3 July, reopening of close contact services such as spas and tattoo parlours on 6 July, reopening indoor gyms and playgrounds from 10 July, reopening libraries from 16 July, resuming competitive sport from 17 July, reopening bingo halls and arcades, theatres and cinemas from 29 July, and reopening indoor sports facilities, leisure centres, skating rinks and soft play areas from 7 August. In addition, indoor spectators at sporting events will be allowed from 28 August, and open air museums can reopen from late August.<ref>{{Cite web|date=25 June 2020|title=NI to move to 1m distancing rule from Monday|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53154830|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>

=== July 2020 ===

* 1 July – Northern Ireland's Department of Health says that a contact-tracing app will be ready for Northern Ireland by the end of July.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cullen|first=Louise|date=1 July 2020|title=NI contact tracing app due by end of July|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53253628|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 2 July –
** Northern Ireland's First Minister [[Arlene Foster]] calls on [[Michelle O'Neill]] to resign following her attendance at the funeral of [[Bobby Storey]]. Foster says she cannot "stand beside" O'Neill and "give out public health advice" after she attended the gathering of 120, breaking Northern Ireland government restrictions that say no more than 30 should attend a funeral.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2 July 2020|title=Foster adds to calls for O'Neill to step down|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53256618|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
** The Northern Ireland Executive announces that the wearing of face coverings will be compulsory on public transport in Northern Ireland from 10 July. Exceptions will be for those with a medical condition, children under the age of 13, and on school transport.<ref>{{Cite web|last=McClafferty|first=Enda|date=2 July 2020|title=Face coverings on public transport from 10 July|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53265586|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 3 July –
** The UK government published a list of 59 countries for which quarantine will not apply when arriving back in England as from 10 July. They include Greece, France, Belgium and Spain, but Portugal and the United States are among those not on the list. These changes do not apply to Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland, where quarantine restrictions remain in place for all arrivals from outside the UK.<ref>{{Cite web|date=3 July 2020|title=England’s quarantine-free list of places published|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-53283375|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
** Hotels, bars, restaurants and cafes permitted to reopen in Northern Ireland.<ref>{{Cite web|date=3 July 2020|title=NI's hotels and bars reopen their doors|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53264251|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
** Northern Ireland's deputy First Minister [[Michelle O'Neill]] apologised after complaints about her attendance of a funeral a few days earlier.<ref>{{Cite web|date=3 July 2020|title=O'Neill 'sorry for hurt' caused by IRA funeral|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-53271201|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 6 July –
** As concerns about increasing unemployment grow, the UK government announces a £111m scheme to help firms in England provide an extra 30,000 trainee places; £21m will be provided to fund similar schemes in Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland.<ref>{{Cite web|date=6 July 2020|title=Sunak to give firms £1,000 'bonus' to hire trainees|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/business-53301076|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
** Northern Ireland relaxes visiting rules for hospitals and care homes, while birth partners are allowed to attend maternity appointments again. Close contact businesses, including hairdressers and tattoo parlours are also reopened.<ref>{{Cite web|date=6 July 2020|title=Hospital and care home visits resume in NI|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53293931|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
** The Northern Ireland Executive also announces that indoor weddings and baptisms will be allowed from 10 July.<ref>{{Cite web|date=6 July 2020|title=Indoor weddings allowed in NI from 10 July|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53312341|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 9 July – Northern Ireland lifts quarantine regulations on arrivals from 50 countries, including France, Italy, Germany and Spain, effective from 10 July.<ref>{{Cite web|last=McCormack|first=Jayne|date=9 July 2020|title=NI's quarantine regulations to change|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53354822|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 13 July – A survey by [[Ulster Bank]] finds that the [[economy of Northern Ireland]] continued to shrink in June, but at a slower rate than in previous months.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Campbell|first=John|date=13 July 2020|title=NI economy 'declining at a much slower rate'|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53386976|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 14 July –
** With a condensed season of Gaelic football set to begin on the upcoming weekend, the [[Gaelic Athletics Association]]'s COVID Advisory Group agrees to keep indoor facilities, such as dressing rooms and gyms, closed until further notice.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Indoor GAA facilities to remain closed|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/gaelic-games/53378295|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
** As England announces that face coverings will become mandatory in shops from 24 July, Northern Ireland's Infrastructure Minister [[Nichola Mallon]] says she supports the introduction of the same measure for Northern Ireland.<ref>{{Cite web|date=14 July 2020|title=NI minister calls for face coverings in shops|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53402486|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 15 July – The [[Northern Ireland Statistics and Research Agency]] (NISRA) publishes a postcode breakdown of COVID-19 deaths in Northern Ireland; the data indicates the BT4 area of East Belfast, including parts of the Upper Newtownards Road, Holywood Road and Sydenham to have had to largest number of deaths at 36.<ref>{{Cite web|date=15 July 2020|title=East Belfast postcode has most Covid-19 deaths|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53310584|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 16 July – Contact-tracing is under way after Northern Ireland's [[Public Health Agency (Northern Ireland)|Public Health Agency]] identifies a cluster of COVID-19 cases linked to a social gathering in the [[Limavady]] area.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cullen|first=Louise|date=16 July 2020|title=Health authorities identify NI Covid-19 cluster|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53432317|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 18 July – Research conducted by [[Ulster University]] indicates that an estimated 240,000 to 280,000 jobs could be at risk under two metre social distancing regulations, and that reducing it to one metre could save up to 30,000 jobs.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Campbell|first=John|date=18 July 2020|title=Social distance of 1m 'could preserve 30,000 jobs'|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53447743|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 20 July – Routine dental care is allowed to resume, but dentists warn that a shortage of [[personal protective equipment]] (PPE) means not all practices can reopen in Northern Ireland.<ref>{{Cite web|date=20 July 2020|title=Dentists voice concern as routine care resumes|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53458936|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 21 July – The [[Democratic Unionist Party]] rejects a suggestion from [[Sinn Féin]] that travellers from Great Britain to Northern Ireland should quarantine for 14 days as a means of preventing the spread of COVID-19. DUP MP Sir [[Jeffrey Donaldson]] describes the idea as a "non-starter".<ref>{{Cite web|last=McCormack|first=Jayne|date=21 July 2020|title=DUP rejects call to quarantine travellers from GB|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53477661|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 22 July – The [[Health and Social Care (Northern Ireland)|Public Health Agency]] says it has identified 16 clusters of COVID-19 involving 133 cases since its contact-tracing system began operating.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Cullen|first=Louise|date=22 July 2020|title=PHA identifies 16 clusters of Covid-19 in NI|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53501390|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
* 23 July –
** The Northern Ireland Executive gives the go-ahead for swimming pools, spas and community centres to reopen from the following day, and announces that face coverings must be worn in shops from 20 August. The Executive also agrees to request urgent talks with the UK and Irish governments over travel arrangements.<ref name="swimming pools">{{Cite web|date=23 July 2020|title=Face masks could be mandatory in shops by 20 August|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-53513656|via=www.bbc.co.uk}}</ref>
