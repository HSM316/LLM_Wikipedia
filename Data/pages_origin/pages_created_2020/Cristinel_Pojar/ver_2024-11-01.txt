==Playing career==

 width=20%

Cristinel Pojar was born on 19 August 1967 in [[Jucu]], [[Cluj County]], and started playing junior level football at [[FC Universitatea Cluj|Universitatea Cluj]], a team where he would spend all of his senior career that started in the [[1984–85 Divizia B]] season as the team finished it on the first position, thus earning promotion to [[Liga I|Divizia A]] where he made his debut under coach [[Remus Vlad]] on 14 September 1985 in a 1–0 away loss in front of [[CS Universitatea Craiova|Universitatea Craiova]].<ref name=RS/><ref name=4ever>{{cite web | url=https://www.4everucluj.ro/jucatori/cristinel-pojar/ | title=Cristinel Pojar profile| publisher=4everucluj.ro| language=Romanian}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.4everucluj.ro/istorie/1984-1985/ | title=Universitatea Cluj 1984–85 season| publisher=4everucluj.ro| language=Romanian}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.4everucluj.ro/istorie/1985-1986/ | title=Universitatea Cluj 1985–86 season| publisher=4everucluj.ro| language=Romanian}}</ref> At the end of the [[1990–91 Divizia A|1990–91]] season, the team relegated back to [[Liga II|Divizia B]] but Pojar stayed with the club, helping it obtain promotion back to the first league after only one year.<ref name=RS/> At age 27 he suffered an injury which kept him off the field for two years and after he started playing again he suffered a cruciate ligament injury, having a total of 7 operations at his knees, eventually retiring because of these problems by the end of the [[1997–98 Divizia A|1997–98]] season, having a total of 174 [[Liga I|Divizia A]] matches with four goals scored and 20 appearances with four goals in [[Liga II|Divizia B]], receiving a single red card in his whole career.<ref name=RS/><ref name=gsp/><ref name=prosport/><ref name=libertatea/> Pojar also played one game for [[Romania national under-21 football team|Romania's under-21 football team]] when he was sent at halftime by coach [[Mircea Rădulescu]] in order to replace [[Marcel Sabou]] in a 3–0 loss in front of [[Norway national under-21 football team|Norway]].<ref name=4ever/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.11v11.com/players/cristinel-vasile-pojar-168232/|title=Cristinel Pojar profile|publisher=11v11|access-date=7 June ==Coaching career==
Cristinel Pojar started working as an assistant coach for Tiberiu Poraczky at [[FC Universitatea Cluj|Universitatea Cluj]] in the [[1998–99 Divizia A]] season, afterwards working again for the same team, this time with [[Ioan Sabău]] in the 2000–01 [[Liga III|Divizia C]] season, helping the team earn promotion to [[Liga II|Divizia B]].<ref name=prosport/><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.4everucluj.ro/istorie/1998-1999/ | title=Universitatea Cluj 1998–99 season| publisher=4everucluj.ro| language=Romanian}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.4everucluj.ro/istorie/2000-2001/ | title=Universitatea Cluj 2000–01 season| publisher=4everucluj.ro| language=Romanian}}</ref> In 2004 he started to work as an assistant of [[Dan Petrescu]] at [[FC Sportul Studențesc București|Sportul Studențesc]], following him in [[Poland]] at [[Wisła Kraków]], returned back to Romania at [[FC Unirea Urziceni|Unirea Urziceni]] where they won the [[Liga I 2008–09|2008–09 Liga I]] title, afterwards working in [[Russia]] at [[FC Kuban Krasnodar|Kuban Krasnodar]] and [[FC Dynamo Moscow|Dynamo Moscow]].<ref name=gsp/><ref name=prosport/><ref name=libertatea/><ref name=gsp2/><ref name=adevarul/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://ziare.com/unirea-urziceni/stiri-unirea-urziceni/unirea-urziceni-noua-campioana-a-romaniei-video-781820|title=Unirea Urziceni, noua campioana a Romaniei |publisher=Ziare.com|language=Romanian |trans-title=Unirea Urziceni, the new champion of Romania| date=10 June 2009 |access-date= 19 February 2023}}</ref>
