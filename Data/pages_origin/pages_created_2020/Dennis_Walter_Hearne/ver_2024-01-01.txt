'''Dennis Walter Hearne''' is an American diplomat who served as the [[List of ambassadors of the United States to Mozambique|United States ambassador to Mozambique]] from 2019 to 2022.

== Education ==

Hearne earned a [[Bachelor of Arts]] from [[Wake Forest University]] and a [[Master of Science]] from the [[National War College]].<ref name="WHBio">[https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/presidential-actions/president-donald-j-trump-announces-intent-nominate-appoint-personnel-key-administration-posts-17/ "President Donald J. Trump Announces Intent to Nominate and Appoint Personnel to Key Administration Posts" White House, August 31, 2018] {{PD-notice}}</ref>

== Career ==

Hearne is a career member of the Senior Foreign Service, class of Counselor. He has served as an American diplomat since 1985. His previous positions include serving as Deputy Chief of Mission at the United States Embassy in [[Kabul, Afghanistan]], from 2016 to 2017; Senior Advisor to the Under Secretary for Political Affairs from 2015 to 2016; United States Consul General in [[Rio de Janeiro, Brazil]], from 2009 to 2012; and as foreign policy advisor to United States military commanders, including two years of service in combat operations in Afghanistan. Prior to being appointed an Ambassador he was the Principal Deputy High Representative, Office of the High Representative, in [[Sarajevo, Bosnia]].<ref name="WHBio" />

== Ambassador to Mozambique ==

On August 31, 2018, President [[Donald Trump]] nominated Hearne to serve as the United States Ambassador to Mozambique.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://clubofmozambique.com/news/donald-trump-has-appointed-the-next-us-ambassador-to-mozambique/ |title=Donald Trump has appointed the next US ambassador to Mozambique |date=November 1, 2018 |website=Club of Mozambique |language=en-US |access-date=December 31, 2019}}</ref> His nomination was confirmed by the [[United States Senate]] by [[voice vote]] on January 2, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.congress.gov/nomination/115th-congress/2479 |title=PN2479 - Nomination of Dennis Walter Hearne for Department of State, 115th Congress (2017-2018) |date=2019-01-02 |website=www.congress.gov |access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> On April 3, 2019, he presented his credentials to President [[Filipe Nyusi]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://clubofmozambique.com/news/u-s-ambassador-to-mozambique-dennis-walter-hearne-presents-credentials-to-mozambican-president-filipe-jacinto-nyusi/ |title=U.S. Ambassador to Mozambique Dennis Walter Hearne presents credentials to Mozambican President Filipe Jacinto Nyusi |date=April 3, 2019 |website=Mozambique |language=en-US |access-date=December 31, 2019}}</ref> He left his post in January 2022.<ref>{{Cite tweet |number=1483828422048731137 |user=USEmbassyMaputo |title=Today, US Ambassador Dennis W. Hearne held a farewell meeting with PR Filipe Nyusi, where they reviewed the Ambassador's stay in the last 3 years and reflected on the relationship between the #US and #Mozambique , which is stronger than that never. |date=January 19, 2022 |access-date=March 1, 2022}}</ref>

== Awards ==

He is the recipient of the United States Army Outstanding Civilian Service Medal.<ref name="WHBio" />
