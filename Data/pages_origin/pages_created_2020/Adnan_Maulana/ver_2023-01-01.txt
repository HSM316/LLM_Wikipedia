'''Adnan Maulana''' (born 23 October 1999) is an Indonesian [[badminton]] player affiliated with Jaya Raya Jakarta club.<ref>{{cite web |title=Pemain: Adnan Maulana |url=https://badmintonindonesia.org/app/profile/aktif.aspx?id=000014469 |publisher=Badminton Association of Indonesia |access-date=10 January 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201031075532/https://badmintonindonesia.org/app/profile/aktif.aspx?id=000014469 |url-status=live |archive-date=31 October 2020 |language=id}}</ref> He won a bronze medal in the mixed doubles at the [[Badminton at the 2021 Southeast Asian Games|2021 Southeast Asian Games]] partnered with [[Mychelle Crhystine Bandaso]].<ref name="seai22">{{cite web |last=Arif |first=Fauzi Handoko |title=Hasil Bulu Tangkis SEA Games 2021 - Adnan/Mychelle Takluk, Indonesia Tak Capai Target Emas |url=https://www.bolasport.com/read/313291361/hasil-bulu-tangkis-sea-games-2021-adnanmychelle-takluk-indonesia-tak-capai-target-emas |publisher=Bola Sport |date=21 May 2022 |access-date=28 July 2022 |language=id |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220728080803/https://www.bolasport.com/read/313291361/hasil-bulu-tangkis-sea-games-2021-adnanmychelle-takluk-indonesia-tak-capai-target-emas |url-status=live |archive-date=28 July 2022}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2019, Maulana won his first senior international title at the 2019 [[Iran Fajr International]] in the men's doubles event partnering Ghifari Anandaffa Prihardika.<ref name="irn19">{{cite web |last=Tegar |first=Bimo |title=(Iran Fajr International Challenge) Adnan/Ghifari Sumbang Gelar Juara untuk Indonesia |url=https://www.djarumbadminton.com/internasional/berita/read/iran-fajr-international-challenge-adnanghifari-sumbang-gelar-juara-untuk-indonesia/ |publisher=Djarum Badminton |date=2 July 2019 |access-date=28 July 2022 |language=id |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220728085008/https://www.djarumbadminton.com/internasional/berita/read/iran-fajr-international-challenge-adnanghifari-sumbang-gelar-juara-untuk-indonesia/ |url-status=live |archive-date=28 July 2022}}</ref> He then won his World Tour title at the [[2019 Russian Open (badminton)|Russian Open]] in the mixed doubles with Mychelle Crhystine Bandaso. He and Bandaso beating host pair [[Evgenij Dremin]] and [[Evgenia Dimova]] in the final in straight games.<ref name="rus19">{{cite web |last=Tegar |first=Bimo |title=(Russia Open) Adnan/Mychelle Juara, Indonesia Borong Tiga Gelar |url=https://www.djarumbadminton.com/internasional/berita/read/russia-open-adnanmychelle-juara-indonesia-borong-tiga-gelar/ |publisher=Djarum Badminton |date=22 July 2019 |access-date=28 July 2022 |language=id |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220728082323/https://www.djarumbadminton.com/internasional/berita/read/russia-open-adnanmychelle-juara-indonesia-borong-tiga-gelar/ |url-status=live |archive-date=28 July 2022}}</ref> Maulana and Bandaso also finished as runners-up at the World Tour Super 100 in [[2019 Hyderabad Open (badminton)|Hyderabad]] and [[2019 Indonesia Masters Super 100|Indonesia]].<ref name="hyd19">{{cite web |last=Tan |first=Ming Wai |title=Pang Ron-Yee See finally smash down first international title |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/sport/badminton/2019/08/12/pang-ron-yee-see-finally-smash-down-first-international-title |publisher=The Star |date=29 August 2019 |access-date=28 July 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220728091259/https://www.thestar.com.my/sport/badminton/2019/08/12/pang-ron-yee-see-finally-smash-down-first-international-title |url-status=live |archive-date=28 July 2022}}</ref><ref name="inass19">{{cite web |last1=Laksamana |first1=Nugyasa |last2=Gonsaga |first2=Aloysius |title=Indonesia Masters 2019, Adnan/Mychelle Hanya Jadi Runner-up |url=https://bola.kompas.com/read/2019/10/06/19550028/indonesia-masters-2019-adnan-mychelle-hanya-jadi-runner-up?page=all |publisher=Kompas |date=6 October 2019 |access-date=28 July 2022 |language=id |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220728094650/https://bola.kompas.com/read/2019/10/06/19550028/indonesia-masters-2019-adnan-mychelle-hanya-jadi-runner-up?page=all |url-status=live |archive-date=28 July 2022}}</ref>

=== BWF World Tour (1 title, 2 runners-up) ===
The BWF World Tour, which was announced on 19 March 2017 and implemented in 2018,<ref>{{cite web |last=Alleyne |first=Gayle |title=BWF Launches New Events Structure |url=http://bwfbadminton.com/news-single/2017/03/19/bwf-launches-new-event-structure/ |publisher=[[Badminton World Federation]] |date=19 March 2017 |access-date=29 November 2017 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171201164159/http://bwfbadminton.com/news-single/2017/03/19/bwf-launches-new-event-structure/ |archive-date=1 December 2017}}</ref> is a series of elite badminton tournaments sanctioned by the [[Badminton World Federation]] (BWF). The BWF World Tour is divided into levels of World Tour Finals, Super&nbsp;1000, Super&nbsp;750, Super&nbsp;500, Super&nbsp;300 (part of the HSBC World Tour), and the BWF Tour Super&nbsp;100.<ref>{{cite web |last=Sukumar |first=Dev |title=Action-Packed Season Ahead! |url=http://bwfworldtour.com/news-single/2018/01/10/action-packed-season-ahead/ |publisher=Badminton World Federation |date=10 January 2018 |access-date=15 January 2018 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180113162925/http://bwfworldtour.com/news-single/2018/01/10/action-packed-season-ahead/ |archive-date=13 January 2018}}</ref>
