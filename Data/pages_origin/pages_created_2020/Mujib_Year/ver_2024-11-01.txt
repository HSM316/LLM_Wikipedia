'''Mujib Year''' ({{langx|bn|মুজিব বর্ষ}}) was the year declared to celebrate on the occasion of the centennial birth anniversary of the founding leader of [[Bangladesh]], [[Sheikh Mujibur Rahman]]. The [[Government of Bangladesh]] had declared the year 2020–21 as the Mujib Year.<ref>{{Cite news |url= https://www.thedailystar.net/city/news/pm-announces-mujib-year-1717972|title=PM announces 'Mujib Year' |work=[[The Daily Star (Bangladesh)|The Daily Star]] (Bangladesh)|date=21 March 2019|access-date=16 December 2020}}</ref> This year was celebrated from March 17, 2020, to March 31, 2022 (extended by one year, from 17 March 2021 to 31 March 2022).<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-12-16|title=Mujib Borsho extended till March 31|url=https://www.thedailystar.net/news/bangladesh/news/mujib-borsho-extended-till-march-31-2934996}}</ref> Bangabandhu Mujib was born on March 17, 1920, in Bengal Presidency (now in [[Tungipara Upazila|Tungipara]] village of the [[Gopalganj District, Bangladesh|Gopalganj district]] of Bangladesh).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mujib100.gov.bd/pages/mujib/timeline.html|title=Mujib Timeline|access-date=18 December 2020}}</ref>

Again, on March 26, 2021, [[Bangladesh]] marked the half-centenary of its independence.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://dailyasianage.com/news/241628/bangladesh-on-way-towards-half-century|title=Bangladesh on way towards half century|work=[[The Asian Age]]|date=18 September 2020|access-date=18 December 2020}}</ref> The announcement of the year 1971 is particularly important as Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was actively involved in Bangladesh's struggle for independence.

[[Narendra Modi|Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi]]'s visit to Bangladesh was cancelled<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kolkata24x7.com/modis-bangladesh-tour-cancelled-over-corona-virus-fear/|title=করোনা ভাইরাস: মুজিব বর্ষে বাতিল হল মোদীর ঢাকা সফর|author=((Bengali Desk))|date=2020-03-08|website=Kolkata24x7|language=bn|access-date=2020-03-12}}</ref> after the Government of Bangladesh decided to avoid mass-scale celebrations on March 17 following the detection of three cases of coronavirus infection. Modi joined the birth centenary celebration of Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman on March 17 through video conference.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thedailystar.net/backpage/news/mujib-borsho-celebrations-modi-join-thru-video-conference-1879456|title=Mujib Borsho Celebrations: Modi to join thru' video conference|date=2020-03-12|website=The Daily Star|language=en|access-date=2020-03-12}}</ref> Later, all public gatherings and occasions were declared as postponed due to the coronavirus.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/2020/03/09/mujib-year-inauguration-to-be-celebrated-at-a-later-date|title = Mujib Year inauguration program postponed|date = 9 March 2020}}</ref>

Following the global [[coronavirus]] outbreak and the identification of infected patients in Bangladesh, on March 8, the Government of Bangladesh and the National Implementation Committee to Celebrate the Birth Centenary announced a small pre-March event in public interest and public welfare.<ref>{{cite web|title = ১৭ মার্চ মুজিব বর্ষের মূল অনুষ্ঠান হচ্ছে না, অনুষ্ঠানে কাটছাঁট | url= https://www.prothomalo.com/bangladesh/article/1643802/%E0%A6%AE%E0%A7%81%E0%A6%9C%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AC-%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%B0%E0%A7%8D%E0%A6%B7%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B0-%E0%A6%95%E0%A6%B0%E0%A7%8D%E0%A6%AE%E0%A6%B8%E0%A7%82%E0%A6%9A%E0%A6%BF-%E0%A6%AA%E0%A7%81%E0%A6%A8%E0%A6%B0%E0%A7%8D%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%A8%E0%A7%8D%E0%A6%AF%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%B8%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B0-%E0%A6%B8%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%8D%E0%A6%A7%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A8%E0%A7%8D%E0%A6%A4| website = First Light | access-date = 12 March 2020 | language = bn}}</ref> At the same time, it was announced that the visit of the invited heads of state of different countries had also been canceled.<ref>{{cite web|title = আসছেন না বিদেশিরা| url= https://www.dailyinqilab.com/article/273969/%E0%A6%86%E0%A6%B8%E0%A6%9B%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%A8-%E0%A6%A8%E0%A6%BE-%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%B0%E0%A6%BE| website = Inquilab |access-date = 12 March 2020 | language = bn}}</ref>

== Global Celebration ==
[[File:Memorabilia Paperweight for Mujib Year (cropped).jpg|thumb|Memorabilia paperweight for the Mujib Year]]
The UN General Assembly, UNESCO, had decided to jointly celebrate the Mujib Year with Bangladesh at the [[UNESCO]] 40th General Assembly.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/dhaka/2019/11/27/unesco-to-celebrate-mujib-year-with-bangladesh|title=Unesco to celebrate Mujib Year with Bangladesh|date=2019-11-27 |work=Dhaka Tribune |access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url= https://unesdoc.unesco.org/ark:/48223/pf0000371424_eng|title=Proposals by Member States for the celebration of anniversaries in 2020–2021 with which UNESCO could be associated |website=unesdoc.unesco.org|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> The decision was made in the presence of all UNESCO members on November 12–27 in Paris, held on November 25, 2019.

Washington D.C. Mayor [[Muriel Bowser]] has issued a proclamation about Mujib Year. In the proclamation, he had declared Mujib Year from March 17, 2020, to September 30, 2020, marking the birth centenary celebration of Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dhakatribune.com/world/north-america/2020/03/03/washington-dc-declares-mujib-year|title=Washington DC declares March 17, 2020-March 17, 2021 as Mujib Year|date=3 March 2020}}</ref>

== Program ==
In addition to promoting the role of Bangabandhu at the grassroots level in Bangladesh's independence struggle, the return anniversary of Bangabandhu, the founding anniversary of the Awami League, National Mourning Day and Jail Killing Day will be celebrated every year as well.<ref>{{Cite web |url= http://www.infocom.gov.bd/site/notices/d54a26d7-99c4-4ea9-9602-1acd047cd241/site/photogallery/34e9f3d9-8e4e-4ff1-98b9-a8882f8668af/%E0%A6%85%E0%A6%A8%E0%A7%81%E0%A6%B7%E0%A7%8D%E0%A6%A0%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A8-%E0%A6%B8%E0%A7%82%E0%A6%9A%E0%A6%BF/-|script-title=bn:জাতির পিতা বঙ্গবন্ধু শেখ মুজিবুর রহমান-এর জন্মশতবার্ষিকী ও মুজিববর্ষ |website=infocom.gov.bd |language=bn |access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> There are also plans to produce short films and documentaries to celebrate the birth anniversary and Mujib anniversary. Chief coordinator of the program is [[Kamal Chowdhury]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-09-25|title=Dr Kamal Naser Chowdhury gets status of principal secretary|url=https://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/government-affairs/2019/09/25/dr-kamal-naser-chowdhury-gets-status-of-principal-secretary|access-date=2020-10-12|website=Dhaka Tribune}}</ref>
