{{Year in Bulgaria |2020}}

Events in the year '''2020 in [[Bulgaria]]'''.

==Incumbents==
* [[President of Bulgaria|President]]: [[Rumen Radev]]
* [[Prime Minister of Bulgaria|Prime Minister]]: [[Boyko Borisov]]

==Events==
Ongoing – [[2020 Bulgarian protests]]; [[COVID-19 pandemic in Bulgaria]]

=== January ===
*8 January – [[Prime Minister of Bulgaria|Prime Minister]] [[Boyko Borisov]], along with the heads of state of [[Turkey]], [[Russia]], and [[Serbia]], formally inaugurate the [[TurkStream]] gas pipelines.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-01-08|title=Turkey, Russia launch TurkStream pipeline carrying gas to Europe|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-turkey-russia-pipeline-idUSKBN1Z71WP|access-date=2020-08-07}}</ref>
*13 – 19 January – The [[2020 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships – Division III]] was held in [[Sofia]].<ref>{{cite web|title=IIHF Ice hockey U20 World Championship Div III – Competition schedule|url=http://reports.iihf.hockey/Hydra/792/IHM792000_08_1_0.pdf|accessdate=1 January 2020|website=reports.iihf.hockey}}</ref> [[Iceland men's national junior ice hockey team|Iceland]] won the tournament.<ref>{{Cite web|title=IIHF – Iceland triumphs in U20 Division III|url=https://www.iihf.com/en/events/2020/wm20iii/news/17697/iceland-triumphs-in-u20-division-iii|access-date=2020-03-16|website=IIHF International Ice Hockey Federation|language=en}}</ref>

=== February ===

* 4 February – [[President of Bulgaria|President]] [[Rumen Radev]] withdraws his support for the [[Third Borisov Government|ruling government coalition]] between the [[GERB|GERB party]] and the [[United Patriots]], blaming it for the prevalent [[Corruption in Bulgaria|corruption in the country]] and lack of [[reform]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-02-04|title=Bulgarian President Withdraws Support for Government|url=https://balkaninsight.com/2020/02/04/bulgarian-president-withdraws-support-for-government/|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Balkan Insight|language=en-US}}</ref>
* 5 February – The [[U.S.-Bulgaria relations|U.S. places sanctions]] on [[Law of Bulgaria|Bulgarian judge]] Andon Mitalov who the [[U.S. State Department]] claimed was involved in "significant" corruption and had "severely compromised the independence" of the [[Government of Bulgaria|country's democratic institutions]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=US Blacklists Bulgarian Judge Over Alleged Involvement In 'Significant' Corruption {{!}} Voice of America – English|url=https://www.voanews.com/europe/us-blacklists-bulgarian-judge-over-alleged-involvement-significant-corruption|access-date=2020-08-07|website=www.voanews.com|language=en}}</ref>
* 22 February – Following a [[Hanau shootings|far-right attack]] in [[Germany]] that killed at least nine people, the annual rally held by [[Neo-Nazism|neo-Nazis]] in [[Sofia]] was banned, the first since it began in 2003.<ref>{{Cite web|title=In the wake of Hanau, an annual neo-Nazi rally is banned in Sofia|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/02/wake-hanau-annual-neo-nazi-rally-banned-sofia-200221134324004.html|access-date=2020-08-07|website=www.aljazeera.com}}</ref>

=== March ===
*8 March – The [[COVID-19 pandemic in Bulgaria|first confirmed cases of COVID-19]] are reported in the country: a 27-year-old man from [[Pleven]] and a 75-year-old woman from [[Gabrovo]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Проф. Тодор Кантарджиев: От Плевен имаме две положителни проби за коронавирус, от Габрово също|url=http://focus-news.net/news/2020/03/08/2754995/prof-todor-kantardzhiev-ot-pleven-imame-dve-polozhitelni-probi-za-koronavirus-ot-gabrovo-sashto.html|access-date=2020-08-06|website=Информационна Агенция "Фокус"|language=bg}}</ref>
*11 March – A 66-year-old women from [[Lom, Bulgaria|Lom]] tested positive for COVID-19 and died, becoming the first victim of [[COVID-19 pandemic in Bulgaria|the virus in the country]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Дневник|first=Екип на|date=2020-03-11|title=Коронавирусът в България: първа жертва и нов случай на заразен|url=https://www.dnevnik.bg/bulgaria/2020/03/11/4039345_koronavirusut_v_bulgariia_purva_jertva_i_nov_sluchai/|access-date=2020-08-06|website=Dnevnik|language=bg}}</ref>
*13 March – After the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Bulgaria|number of confirmed cases in the country]] more than tripled to 23 over one day, a [[state of emergency]] was declared until April 13. Schools, shopping centres, cinemas, restaurants, and other places of business were closed with only essential businesses remaining open. All sports events, including [[Bulgarian Football Union]] matches, were suspended. Travel was [[Tourism in Bulgaria|restricted to 14 countries]] and police were given special powers for [[quarantine]] enforcement.<ref>{{Cite web|title=МВнР: България не е въвела ограничения на държавните си граници|url=https://www.bgonair.bg/a/2-bulgaria/188624-mvnr-balgariya-ne-e-vavela-ogranicheniya-na-darzhavnite-si-granitsi|access-date=2020-08-06|website=Bgonair|language=bg}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-03-13|title=Bulgaria closes schools, restricts travel over coronavirus|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-bulgaria-emergency-idUSKBN21015D|access-date=2020-03-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Foundation|first=Thomson Reuters|title=Bulgaria declares state of emergency over coronavirus|url=https://news.trust.org/item/20200313085517-41uxh/|access-date=2020-08-07|website=news.trust.org}}</ref>

=== April ===
*3 April – The [[National Assembly (Bulgaria)|National Assembly]] approved the government's proposal to extend the [[state of emergency]] by one month until 13 May.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-04-03|title=ГЕРБ удължи извънредното положение с подкрепата на ДПС|url=https://www.segabg.com/node/128311|access-date=2020-08-06|website=СЕГА|language=bg}}</ref>
*30 April – [[Bulgaria]] applies for [[European Exchange Rate Mechanism|ERM II]], a mandatory step to joining the [[Eurozone]], due to join along with [[Croatia]] in July.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Bulgaria economy briefing: BULGARIA CHANGES LEGISLATION TO JOIN ERM2 EXCHANGE RATE MECHANISM – China-CEE Institute|url=https://china-cee.eu/2020/03/31/bulgaria-economy-briefing-bulgaria-changes-legislation-to-join-erm2-exchange-rate-mechanism/|access-date=2020-05-22|language=en-GB}}</ref>

=== May ===
*4 May – Director of [[European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control|ECDC]] Dr. [[Andrea Ammon]] listed Bulgaria as the only country monitored by the centre still seeing an increase in [[COVID-19 pandemic in Bulgaria|newly registered cases]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-05-04|title=UK among European states not yet on COVID-19 downward slope: EU says|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-eu-britain-idUSKBN22G14A|access-date=2020-08-06}}</ref>
*9 May – [[Targovishte Province|Targovishte]], the last [[Provinces of Bulgaria|Bulgarian province]] without any registered [[COVID-19 pandemic in Bulgaria|COVID-19 cases]], confirmed its first COVID-19 case.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Нови 46 случая на Covid-19 у нас, общо стават 1911|url=https://bnr.bg/post/101272749/novi-46-sluchaa-na-covid-19-u-nas-obshto-stavat-1911|access-date=2020-08-06|website=bnr.bg|language=bg}}</ref>
*11 May &ndash; The [[Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology]] in [[Leipzig]], [[Germany]], publishes the result of [[Radiocarbon dating|radiocarbon]] and [[DNA analysis]] from [[fossil]]s that were found in the [[Bacho Kiro cave]], [[Gabrovo Province]]. The result, showing that the fossils belong to ''[[Homo sapiens]]'' instead of [[Neanderthal]]s, indicates that modern humans may have arrived in [[Europe]] thousands of years earlier than previously thought.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Welle (dw.com)|first=Deutsche|title=Bulgarian fossils suggest Homo sapiens arrived in Europe earlier than previously thought {{!}} DW {{!}} 12.05.2020|url=https://www.dw.com/en/bulgarian-fossils-suggest-homo-sapiens-arrived-in-europe-earlier-than-previously-thought/a-53399586|access-date=2020-06-03|website=DW.COM|language=en-GB}}</ref>
*25 May – The [[Bulgarians|Bulgarian]] captain of the [[cargo ship]] ''Tommi Ritscher'', who was [[kidnap]]ped at the port of [[Cotonou]], [[Benin]], in the [[Piracy in the Gulf of Guinea|Gulf of Guinea by pirates]], is freed.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-05-25|title=Kidnapped captain of Portugal-flagged ship freed-Bulgaria|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-benin-piracy-bulgaria-idUSKBN23117U|access-date=2020-08-07}}</ref>

*9 July – [[2020 Bulgarian protests|Protests]] begin throughout the country with the goal of removing [[Third Borisov Government|Borisov's cabinet]] and [[Office of the General Prosecutor of Bulgaria|Chief Prosecutor]] [[Ivan Geshev]] from office.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Протест пред президентството|url=http://www.btv.bg/video/shows/lice-v-lice/videos/protest-pred-prezidentstvoto.html|access-date=2020-08-06|website=bTV|language=en}}</ref><ref>https://www.euronews.com/2020/07/14/bulgaria-s-anti-government-protests-keep-pressure-on-pm-boyko-borissov</ref>

=== July ===
*10 July
**The [[European Central Bank|ECB]] accepts [[Bulgaria and the euro|Bulgaria]] and [[Croatia and the euro|Croatia]] into [[European Exchange Rate Mechanism|ERM II]], paving the way for their adoption of the [[euro]]. This is also the [[Eurozone]]'s first major expansion in half a decade.<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-07-10|title=Bulgaria, Croatia take vital step to joining euro|language=en|agency=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-eurozone-economy-croatia-bulgaria-idUSKBN24B2JM|access-date=2020-07-11}}</ref>
**[[2020 Bulgarian protests|Thousands of protesters]] blocked the center of [[Sofia]] on [[Tsar Osvoboditel Boulevard, Sofia|Tsar Osvoboditel Blvd]]., between [[Orlov Most]] and [[Sofia University]]. Later in the day, head of the [[National Intelligence Service (Bulgaria)|National Security Service]] General Krassimir Stanchev resigned after being asked to do so by President [[Rumen Radev]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Втори ден на протести в центъра на София*|url=https://www.investor.bg/ikonomika-i-politika/332/a/vtori-den-na-protesti-v-centyra-na-sofiia-308046/|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Investor.bg|language=bg}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Началникът на НСО ген. Красимир Станчев подаде оставка|url=https://btvnovinite.bg/bulgaria/nachalnikat-na-nso.html|access-date=2020-08-07|website=bTV Новините}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Президентът поиска оставката на шефа на НСО – България|url=https://dariknews.bg/novini/bylgariia/prezidentyt-poiska-ostavkata-na-shefa-na-nso-2232853|access-date=2020-08-07|website=dariknews.bg|language=bg}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Напрежението ескалира: Полицията отцепи МС в полунощ заради антиправителствения протест|url=https://www.mediapool.bg/naprezhenieto-eskalira-politsiyata-ottsepi-ms-v-polunosht-zaradi-antipravitelstveniya-protest-news309649.html|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Mediapool.bg|language=bg-BG}}</ref>
*14 July
**The National Representation of [[Student council|Student Councils]] in Bulgaria accused pro-government [[National Police Service (Bulgaria)|police forces]] of illegally beating protesting students and demanded the resignations of police officers found responsible.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Students demanding police who beat them fired|url=https://bnr.bg/en/post/101309934|access-date=2020-08-07|website=bnr.bg|language=en}}</ref>
**[[Office of the General Prosecutor of Bulgaria|Chief Prosecutor]] [[Ivan Geshev]] released a series of wiretaps allegedly collected by the [[State Agency for National Security]] in which [[Aleksandar Paunov]], [[Communist Party of Bulgaria]] leader and opposition MP, held a conversation with a Bulgarian exile. This led the [[Union of Democratic Forces (Bulgaria)|Union of Democratic Forces]] to call on the prosecution to ban the communist party entirely, stating that it had supported the protests and "contributed to the destabilization of the country".<ref>{{Cite web|title=Гешев: Радев подкрепи бегълците от правосъдието|url=https://btvnovinite.bg/bulgaria/geshev-radev-podkrepi-begalcite-ot-pravosadieto.html|access-date=2020-08-07|website=bTV Новините}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=news.bg|date=2020-07-17|title=От МСДС искат разпускане на Комунистическата партия с лидер Александър Паунов|url=https://news.bg/politics/ot-msds-iskat-razpuskane-na-komunisticheskata-partiya-s-lider-aleksandar-paunov.html|access-date=2020-08-07|website=News.bg|language=bulgarian}}</ref>
*15 July – The opposition [[Socialist Party of Bulgaria|socialist party]] introduced a [[motion of no confidence]] against [[Third Borisov Government|Borisov's government]] in [[Parliament of Bulgaria|parliament]], accusing it of collaborating with the [[Bulgarian mafia]]. Concurrently, in response to the [[2020 Bulgarian protests|continued protests]] and reports of the use of [[Police brutality|violence]] by [[Bulgarian police|the police]], [[Prime Minister of Bulgaria|Prime Minister]] [[Boyko Borisov]] asks his [[Ministry of Finance (Bulgaria)|ministers for finance]], [[Ministry of Economy, Energy and Tourism (Bulgaria)|the economy]], and [[Ministry of Interior (Bulgaria)|the interior]] to resign. However, the majority of protesters rejected these concessions and vowed to continue to protest until the entire government resigns.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Bulgarian government faces no-confidence vote amid protests|url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/bulgarian-government-faces-confidence-vote-amid-protests-71795056|access-date=2020-08-07|website=ABC News|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Gotev|first=Georgi|date=2020-07-16|title=Faced with anti-mafia protests, Bulgaria's Borissov sacks three ministers|url=https://www.euractiv.com/section/elections/news/faced-with-anti-mafia-protests-bulgarias-borissov-sacks-three-ministers/|access-date=2020-08-07|website=www.euractiv.com|language=en-GB}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Борисов поиска три министерски оставки, но протестът остава – България|url=https://dariknews.bg/novini/bylgariia/borisov-poiska-tri-ministerski-ostavki-no-protestyt-ostava-2233420|access-date=2020-08-07|website=dariknews.bg|language=bg}}</ref>
*21 July – The [[Third Borisov Government|government]] survives a [[motion of no confidence]] by 124 votes against, 102 votes in favour, amidst the still ongoing [[2020 Bulgarian protests|large-scale anti-government protests]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=2020-07-21|title=Bulgarian government survives no-confidence vote, protests continue|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-bulgaria-government-idUSKCN24M0ZJ|access-date=2020-08-07}}</ref>

=== August ===
*7 August – [[Armed forces of Bulgaria|Bulgarian security forces]] clamped down on [[2020 Bulgarian protests|demonstrators and dismantled anti-government barricades]] across the country in a major police sweep. The police operation comes a day after the [[Third Borisov Government|Bulgarian government]] declared that it had no plans to step down from power.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-08-07|title=Bulgarian Police Clamp Down on Anti-Govt Protests|url=https://balkaninsight.com/2020/08/07/bulgarian-police-clamp-down-on-anti-govt-protests/|access-date=2020-08-07|website=Balkan Insight|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== September ===
*3 September – The [[2020 Bulgarian protests|protests in the country]] became violent as protesting crowds attempt to storm the nation's [[National Assembly (Bulgaria)|parliament]], leading to a night of fighting between demonstrators and [[National Police Service (Bulgaria)|security forces]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Welle (www.dw.com)|first=Deutsche|title=Bulgaria riots: Police, anti-government protesters clash in Sofia {{!}} DW {{!}} 03.09.2020|url=https://www.dw.com/en/bulgaria-riots-police-anti-government-protesters-clash-in-sofia/a-54798039|access-date=2020-10-07|website=DW.COM|language=en-GB}}</ref>
*21 September – A [[court]] in the country sentenced two men, a [[Lebanese Australian]] and a [[Lebanese Canadian]], to [[life in prison]] ''[[Trial in absentia|in absentia]]'', for their roles in the [[2012 Burgas bus bombing|suicide bombing of a bus in Burgas]] in 2012 that killed five [[Tourism in Bulgaria|Israeli tourists]], the driver and the bomber. The whereabouts of the men is unknown and they are the subject of an [[Interpol]] [[red notice]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-09-21|title=2 sentenced to life in absentia over Bulgarian bus bombing|url=https://apnews.com/article/bombings-archive-black-sea-bulgaria-0620d303fdc33bcff08119beba8d6084|access-date=2020-10-07|website=AP NEWS}}</ref>

=== October ===
*7 October – [[COVID-19 pandemic in Bulgaria|The country registers 436 new cases in the past 24 hours]], a record high for a single day since the [[COVID-19 pandemic|beginning of the pandemic]], as shown by data from the national information platform on the disease. A total of 22,306 cases have been registered in the country.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Bulgaria's daily coronavirus cases hit record high of 436|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/health-coronavirus-bulgaria-cases-idINKBN26S0VU|access-date=2020-10-07}}</ref>
