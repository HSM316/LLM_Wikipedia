'''Nicholas C. Ragus''' (1908 – April 12, 1981) was an [[American football]] and [[basketball]] coach. He served as the head football coach at Arizona State Teachers College at Flagstaff—now known as [[Northern Arizona University]]—from 1947 to 1948, compiling a record of 5–12.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sports-reference.com/cfb/coaches/nick-ragus-1.html|title=Nick Ragus|publisher=Sports-Reference College Football|access-date=December 30, 2019}}</ref> Ragus was also the school's head basketball coach from 1947 to 1949, tallying a mark of 24–18. He previously served as a head coach at a number of high schools in Arizona, including [[Miami High School (Arizona)|Miami High School]] in [[Miami, Arizona]].<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=nrtPCgAAQBAJ|title=Coach: The Life and Times of US Hall of Fame Coach Emil Nasser|first=Gerald M|last=Knowles|date=August 5, 2015|publisher=Victory Publishing|access-date=December 30, 2019}}</ref>

Ragus played [[college football]] at [[Saint Mary's College of California]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://newspapers.com/clip/4410270/nick_ragus_interview_silver_belt/|title=Ragus Feels Part Of Local Soil Born, Raised And Works in Area|publisher=newspapers.com|newspaper=[[Arizona Silver Belt]]|date=July 7, 1960|access-date=December 30, 2019}}</ref>
