'''Leonard Graves''' (1927-2000) was an American actor and singer best known for narrating the 1952 television documentary ''[[Victory at Sea]].'' Graves was the only voice on the 26-part series,<ref>{{cite journal|title=Persuasive History: A Critical Comparison of Television's Victory at Sea and The World at War|first=Donald J. |last=Mattheisen|journal=The History Teacher| volume=25 |issue=2 |date=February 1992 |pages=239–251 |publisher=Society for History Education|doi = 10.2307/494277|jstor = 494277}}</ref>
which won multiple awards, including a 1954 [[Emmy]] for Best Public Affairs Program,<ref>[https://www.emmys.com/shows/victory-sea Emmy awards —Victory at Sea]</ref> a special [[Peabody award]]<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0046658/awards IMDB award page for Victory at Sea]</ref> and the [[Freedoms Foundation]]'s George Washington Medal.<ref>[https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.0022-3840.1973.0603_463.x Peter C. Rollins, ''Victory at Sea: Cold War Epic,''] Gary R. Edgerton & Peter C. Rollins (eds.), ''Television Histories. Shaping Collective Memory in the Media Age'', Kentucky 2001, pp.&nbsp;103–122</ref>

Graves was born in [[Memphis, Tennessee]] in 1927 and began his performing career in radio. He joined the Broadway cast of ''[[The King and I]]'' as the Interpreter and also understudied for [[Yul Brynner]] as the King, filling in on many occasions. Graves took over the lead when Brynner left the show, and after a two-year run on Broadway, he joined a national tour.<ref name=askvance1>[https://memphismagazine.com/ask-vance/leonard-graves/ Ask Vance: Leonard Graves], Vance Lauderdale, Memphis Magazine, January 11, 2018</ref>

Graves appeared in several motion pictures, including:<ref>[https://www.rottentomatoes.com/celebrity/leonard_graves Rotten Tomato biography]</ref>

*''[[Three Brave Men]]'' (1956) as Flaxman (uncredited)
*''[[The Joker Is Wild]]'' (1957) as Tim Coogan
*''[[The Brothers Karamazov (1958 film)|The Brothers Karamazov]]'' (1958) as Third Court Officer (uncredited)
*''[[The Buccaneer (1958 film)|The Buccaneer]]'' (1958) as Chighizola
*''[[Pork Chop Hill]]''	(1959) as Lt. Cook
*''[[A Private's Affair]]'' (1959) as General's Aide (uncredited)
*''[[Pay or Die]]'' (1960) as Opera Singer (uncredited)
*''[[The Story of Ruth]]'' (1960) as Reaper (uncredited)
*''A Message from the Future (שדר מן העתיד)'' (1981) as Anderson<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0152394/fullcredits A Message from the Future, Full Cast and Crew IMDB]</ref> (final film role)

Graves also had guest roles in a number of television series<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0336316/?ref_=ttfc_fc_cl_t7 IMDB filmography for Leonard Graves]</ref> including ''Navy Log'' and ''The Big Picture (Army In Action episodes)''.<ref>[http://www.armypictorialcenter.com/ Army Pictorial Center website]</ref> In 1968 he was appointed manager of the [[Opera Memphis|Memphis Opera Theater]].<ref name=askvance1 /> Graves later moved to Israel with his wife where he was active in the [[Israel National Opera]]. He reportedly died circa 2008.<ref>[https://www.thegardenisland.com/2019/08/11/lifestyles/kauai-and-victory-at-sea/ Kauai and Victory at Sea], Hank Soboleski, The Garden Island, August 11, 2019</ref><ref name=askvance2>[https://memphismagazine.com/ask-vance/an-update-on-memphis-singer-and-actor-leonard-graves/ An update on Memphis Singer and Actor Leonard Graves], Vance Lauderdale, Memphis Magazine, January 31, 2018</ref>
