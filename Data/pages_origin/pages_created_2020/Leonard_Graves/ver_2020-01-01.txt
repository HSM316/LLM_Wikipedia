'''Leonard Graves''' was an actor and singer best know for narrating the 1952 television documentary ''[[Victory at Sea]].'' Graves was the only voice on the 26-part series,<ref>{{cite journal|url= https://www.jstor.org/stable/494277 |title=Persuasive History: A Critical Comparison of Television's Victory at Sea and The World at War|first=Donald J. |last=Mattheisen|journal=The History Teacher| volume=25 |issue=2 |date=February 1992 |pages=239-251 |publisher=
Society for History Education|accessdate=December 30, 2019}}</ref>
which won multiple awards, including a 1954 [[Emmy]] for Best Public Affairs Program,<ref>[https://www.emmys.com/shows/victory-sea Emmy awards —Victory at Sea]</ref> a special [[Peabody award]].<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0046658/awards IMDB award page for Victory at Sea]</ref> and the [[Freedoms Foundation]]'s George Washington Medal.<ref>[https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.0022-3840.1973.0603_463.x Peter C. Rollins, ''Victory at Sea: Cold War Epic,''] Gary R. Edgerton & Peter C. Rollins (eds.), ''Television Histories. Shaping Collective Memory in the Media Age'', Kentucky 2001, pp.&nbsp;103–122</ref>

Graves was born in [[Memphis, Tennessee]] in 1927 and began his performing career in radio. He joined the Broadway cast of ''[[The King and I]]'' as the Interpreter and also understudied for [[Yul Brynner]] as the King, filling in on many occasions. Graves took over the lead when Brynner left the show, and after a two year run on Broadway, he joined a national tour.<ref>[https://memphismagazine.com/ask-vance/leonard-graves/ Ask Vance: Leonard Graves], Vance Lauderdale, Memphis Magazine, January 11, 2018</ref> Graves later moved to Israel with his wife where he was active in the [[Israel National Opera]]. He reportedly died circa 2008.<ref>[https://www.thegardenisland.com/2019/08/11/lifestyles/kauai-and-victory-at-sea/ Kauai and Victory at Sea], Hank Soboleski, The Garden Island, August 11, 2019</ref><ref>[https://memphismagazine.com/ask-vance/an-update-on-memphis-singer-and-actor-leonard-graves/ An update on Memphis Singer and Actor Leonard Graves], Vance Lauderdale, Memphis Magazine, January 31, 2018</ref>

Graves appeared in several motion pictures, including:<ref>[https://www.rottentomatoes.com/celebrity/leonard_graves Rotten Tomato biography]</ref>

*''[[Three Brave Men]]''	Flaxman	1956
*''[[The Joker Is Wild]]''	Tim Coogan	1957
*''[[The Buccaneer (1958 film)|The Buccaneer]]''	Chighizola	1958
*''[[Pork Chop Hill]]''	Lt. Cook	1959
*''A Message from the Future''    Anderson 1981<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0152394/fullcredits A Message from the Future, Full Cast and Crew IMDB]</ref>

He also had guest roles in a number of television series.<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0336316/?ref_=ttfc_fc_cl_t7 IMDB filmography for Leonard Graves]</ref>
