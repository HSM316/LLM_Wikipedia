'''Kondom Agaundo''' (c. 1917 – 28 August 1966) was a Papua New Guinean tribal leader and politician. He served as a member of the [[Legislative Council of Papua and New Guinea|Legislative Council]] between 1961 and 1964.

==Biography==
Agaundo was born near [[Kundiawa]] around 1917.<ref name=ADB>[http://adb.anu.edu.au/biography/agaundo-kondom-9316 Agaundo, Kondom (1917–1966)] Australian Dictionary of Biography</ref> His father was a war leader, but he was orphaned as a child. He subsequently developed a close relationship with the Australian authorities, carrying milk from the Catholic mission at [[Mingende]] to the government compound at [[Kundiawa]].<ref name=ADB/> In the 1940s he became ''luluai''  of his tribe. Encouraged by Australian officials, he built a house and community hall and oversaw the development of coffee farming in the area.<ref name=ADB/> Despite being illiterate, when a local government council was formed in the region in 1959, Agaundo became its first president.<ref name=ADB/>

He contested the Highlands seat in the [[1961 Papua New Guinean general election|1961 elections]], and was elected to the Legislative Council, where he demanded more development of the Highlands. In the [[1964 Papua New Guinean general election|first full elections]] in 1964 he contested the Chimbu seat, but finished third, losing to [[Waiye Siune]]. However, he continued as a member of the Eastern Highlands District Advisory Council,<ref name=ADB/> and became chairman of the Kundiawa Coffee Society, the largest co-operative society in the territory.<ref name=PIM/>

Aguando had at least eight wives.<ref name=ADB/> He was killed in a road accident on the Daulo Pass in the [[Eastern Highlands Province|Eastern Highlands]] in August 1966.<ref name=PIM>[https://nla.gov.au/nla.obj-370541289/view?partId=nla.obj-370560082#page/n156/mode/1up Mr. Kondon Agundo] ''Pacific Islands Monthly'', October 1966, pp154–155</ref> He was buried at [[Waindi]].<ref name=ADB/>
