'''William H. Moser''' (born 1964)<ref name="History">{{Cite web |url=https://history.state.gov/departmenthistory/people/moser-william-h |title=William H. Moser - People - Department History - Office of the Historian |website=history.state.gov |access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> is an American diplomat who serves as the [[List of ambassadors of the United States to Kazakhstan|United States Ambassador to Kazakhstan]].

== Education ==

Moser earned a [[Bachelor of Arts]] at the [[University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill]] and studied at [[Universitaet zu Koeln]] in [[Cologne, Germany]].<ref name="WHBio">[https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/president-donald-j-trump-announces-intent-nominate-personnel-key-administration-posts-59/ "President Donald J. Trump Announces Intent to Nominate Personnel to Key Administration Posts" White House, August 13, 2018] {{PD-notice}}</ref>

== Career ==

He is a career member of the [[Senior Foreign Service]], class of [[Minister-Counselor]]. He has served as an American diplomat since 1984. He has served at seven United States Missions overseas, including as [[List of ambassadors of the United States to Moldova|United States Ambassador to Moldova]] from 2011 to 2015, and in senior leadership positions at the [[United States Department of State]].<ref name="WHBio" />

== Ambassadorships ==

=== Moldova ===

In 2011, President [[Barack Obama]] nominated Moser to be the [[United States Ambassador to Moldova]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.whitehouse.gov/the-press-office/2011/05/05/president-obama-announces-more-key-administration-posts |title=President Obama Announces More Key Administration Posts |date=May 5, 2011 |archive-url=https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/the-press-office/2011/05/05/president-obama-announces-more-key-administration-posts |archive-date=January 20, 2019}}</ref> He served from September 21, 2011 to January 22, 2015.<ref name="History" />

=== Kazakhstan ===

On August 13, 2018, President Trump nominated Moser to be the [[United States Ambassador to Kazakhstan]].<ref name="WHBio" /> His nomination was confirmed by [[voice vote]] in the [[United States Senate]] on January 2, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.congress.gov/nomination/115th-congress/2393 |title=PN2393 - Nomination of William H. Moser for Department of State, 115th Congress (2017-2018) |date=2019-01-02 |website=www.congress.gov |access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> He was sworn in on February 12, 2019.<ref>{{Cite tweet |number=1095439762033389568 |user=state_sca |title=William H. Moser was sworn in as the next U.S. Ambassador to #Kazakhstan today, in the presence of friends, family, and even Kazakhstan Ambassador to the U.S. Erzhan Kazykhanov. Our sincere congratulations and best wishes to Ambassador Moser! |date=February 12, 2019}}</ref> He presented his credentials on February 18, 2019.<ref name="History" /> Moser previously served in Kazakhstan in 1996, in the then-Embassy in Almaty as a management officer and then as energy attaché.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.foreign.senate.gov/imo/media/doc/101118_Moser_Kazakhstan.pdf|title=Testimony of William H. Moser: Nominee to be U.S. Ambassador to the Republic of Kazakhstan|publisher=Senate Foreign Relations Committee|accessdate=October 11, 2018}}</ref>

== Personal life ==

Moser speaks Russian and German.<ref name="WHBio" />
