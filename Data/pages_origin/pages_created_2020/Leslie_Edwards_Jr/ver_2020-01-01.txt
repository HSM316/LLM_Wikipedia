SSG '''Leslie Edwards Jr.''' (1924-2019) was born in [[Memphis, Tennessee]]. He was an aircraft technician and a member of the famed group of [[World War II]]-era [[African-American]]s known as the [[Tuskegee Airmen]]. In 2007 he was awarded the [[Congressional Gold Medal]] by President [[George W. Bush|George W.Bush]].

==Military service==
===[[World War II]]===
[[File:Tuskegee P-51.jpg|thumb|left|The Tuskegee Airmen's aircraft had distinctive markings that led to the name, "Red Tails."{{#tag:ref|The red markings that distinguished the Tuskegee Airmen included red bands on the noses of P-51s as well as a red rudder; their P-51B and D Mustangs flew with similar color schemes, with red propeller spinners, yellow wing bands and all-red tail surfaces.<ref name="Rice">Rice, Markus. [http://logicalthinker2.tripod.com/Tuskegeeaircraft.html "The Men and Their Airplanes: The Fighters."] ''Tuskegee Airmen'', 1 March 2000.</ref>|group=N}}]]

Edwards was drafted in 1943. He was sent to [[Sheppard Field]] in Texas and was trained to work on the engines of bombers. Edwards was assigned to the 477th Bomb Group. The group was scheduled to participate in the War in the Pacific, but the war ended before they could be deployed.<ref name="Legacy">{{cite web |title=Leslie Edwards |url=https://www.legacy.com/amp/obituaries/tributes/193987482 |website=Legacy.com |publisher=Legacy |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref><ref name="USAF">{{cite web |title=Staff Sergeant Leslie Edwards Jr. |url=https://www.477fg.afrc.af.mil/News/Article-Display/Article/1342796/staff-sergeant-leslie-edwards-jr/fbclid/IwAR0K-upzXOvrs1NQFOA1qFAD4BQ_QtfuqraI4VRLEi-gHVojazO_Q4q0530/ |website=477fg.afrc.af.mil |publisher=Official United States Air Force Website |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref>

The 477th was sent to [[Godman Field]] in 1945. In 1945 Edwards was discharged. The 447th bomb group was participated in the [[Freeman Field mutiny]].<ref name="Legacy"/> in 1946 Edwards was honorably discharged. His rank was Staff Sergeant, Edwards never achieved the rank of [[Master sergeant]]. In the 1940's typically only white flight chiefs were held the rank of Master sergeant.<ref name="USA">{{cite news |title=Leslie Edwards Jr., local Tuskegee Airman, dead at age 95 |url=https://www.cincinnati.com/story/news/2019/09/26/leslie-edwards-jr-tuskegee-airman-cincinnati-dead-age-95/2432791001/ |accessdate=1 January 2020 |agency=USA Today |publisher=The Cincinati Enquirer |date=26 September 2019}}</ref>

==Death==
Edwards died at the age of 95: he passed away at the Cincinnati VA Medical Center in [[Corryville, Cincinnati]].<ref name="USA"/> On October 4, 2019 his funeral was held at [[First Unitarian Church (Cincinnati, Ohio)|First Unitarian Church in Avondale]].<ref name="Hearst">{{cite news |title=Overflow crowd pays final respects to Cincinnati Tuskegee Airman Leslie Edwards |url=https://www.wlwt.com/article/overflow-crowd-pays-final-respects-to-cincinnati-tuskegee-airman-leslie-edwards/29364793 |accessdate=1 January 2020 |agency=WLWT-TV |publisher=Hearst Television Inc. |date=4 October 2019}}</ref> 

==Personal life==
Edwards was one of three children, and the youngest. His father was a hotel bellboy and his mother was a stay at home mom.<ref name="TET">{{cite news |last1=BEVAN |first1=LOUISE |title=One of the First African-American Air Force Members Dies at Age 95, Receives Full Honors |url=https://www.theepochtimes.com/one-of-the-first-african-american-air-force-members-dies-at-age-95-receives-full-honors_3097049.html |accessdate=1 January 2020 |publisher=The Epoch Times |date=2 December 2019}}</ref> When he was five his father passed away. The family moved to Ohio to avoid violence against blacks. He had to leave school in the tenth grade to support his 2 sisters and his mother.<ref name="USA"/> Edwards married Anna Mae in 1943 and they had four children. The he couple stayed married 72 years, until the death of Anna Mae in 2016. Both had dropped out of high school, and both returned to finish high school in 1961.<ref name="Legacy"/><ref name="USAF"/>
