'''Leslie Edwards Jr.''' (1924â€“2019) was trained as an aircraft technician and became a flight chief with the rank of [[staff sergeant]] and a member of the [[Tuskegee Airmen]], the famed group of [[World War II]]-era [[African-American]] military pilots. In 2007 he was awarded the [[Congressional Gold Medal]] by President [[George W. Bush]].

==Early life==
The youngest of three children, he was born in [[Memphis, Tennessee]] on August 9, 1924.<ref name=EdwardsHerald>[http://thecincinnatiherald.com/2019/10/leslie-edwards-jr-was-ww-ii-tuskegee-airman/ Leslie Edwards Jr. was WW II Tuskegee Airman], ''[[The Cincinnati Herald]]'' (October 2019).</ref> His family moved to [[Cincinnati, Ohio]], the year after Edwards was born,<ref name=EdwardsHerald/> settling in the [[West End, Cincinnati|West End]].<ref name="USA">{{cite news |title=Leslie Edwards Jr., local Tuskegee Airman, dead at age 95 |url=https://www.cincinnati.com/story/news/2019/09/26/leslie-edwards-jr-tuskegee-airman-cincinnati-dead-age-95/2432791001/ |accessdate=1 January 2020 |agency=USA Today |publisher=The Cincinnati Enquirer |date=26 September 2019}}</ref> The family moved from the South to the North to avoid violence against blacks.<ref name="USA"/> Edwards' father, a [[bellhop]],<ref name=EdwardsHerald/> died when Edwards was five years old.<ref name="USA"/> Edwards had to leave the Harriet Beecher Stowe School in Cincinnati in the tenth grade to support his two sisters and mother.<ref name="USA"/><ref name="Legacy"/>

==World War II service as a Tuskegee Airman==
[[File:Tuskegee P-51.jpg|thumb|The Tuskegee Airmen's aircraft had distinctive markings that led to the name, "Red Tails."{{#tag:ref|The red markings that distinguished the Tuskegee Airmen included red bands on the noses of P-51s as well as a red rudder; their P-51B and D Mustangs flew with similar color schemes, with red propeller spinners, yellow wing bands and all-red tail surfaces.<ref name="Rice">Rice, Markus. [http://logicalthinker2.tripod.com/Tuskegeeaircraft.html "The Men and Their Airplanes: The Fighters."] ''Tuskegee Airmen'', 1 March 2000.</ref>|group=N}}]]

Edwards was drafted in 1943.<ref name="USA"/> He was sent to [[Sheppard Field]] in Texas and was trained to work on the engines of bombers. Edwards was assigned to the [[447th Bomb Group|477th Bomb Group]]. The group was scheduled to participate in the [[Pacific War|War in the Pacific]], but the war ended before they could be deployed.<ref name="Legacy">{{cite web |title=Leslie Edwards|url=https://www.legacy.com/amp/obituaries/tributes/193987482 |website=Legacy.com |publisher=Legacy |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref><ref name="USAF">{{cite web|author=Michael Campbell|title=Staff Sergeant Leslie Edwards Jr. |url=https://www.477fg.afrc.af.mil/News/Article-Display/Article/1342796/staff-sergeant-leslie-edwards-jr/fbclid/IwAR0K-upzXOvrs1NQFOA1qFAD4BQ_QtfuqraI4VRLEi-gHVojazO_Q4q0530/|publisher=477th Fighter Group, United States Air Force|date=October 6, 2017}}</ref> The 477th was sent to [[Godman Field]] in 1945. The 447th bomb group participated in the [[Freeman Field mutiny]].<ref name="Legacy"/> Edwards are [[honorably discharged]] in 1946 with the rank of staff sergeant.<ref name="USA"/> Edwards never achieved the rank of [[master sergeant]], a rank that in the 1940s was typically held only by white flight chiefs.<ref name="USA"/>

==Post-war life==
After the end of his military service, Edwards worked at the Kahn's meatpacking plant in [[Camp Washington, Cincinnati]], for two decades, and then worked for 25 years as a meat inspector.<ref name="USA"/> He and his wife, who had both dropped out of high school, returned to finish high school in 1961.<ref name="Legacy"/><ref name="USAF"/> He earned a bachelor's degree in commerce from Salmon P. Chase College in Cincinnati.<ref name="Legacy"/>

Edwards spoke about his wartime experiences to schoolchildren and other groups.<ref name="USA"/> He was honored with the [[Congressional Gold Medal]] in 2007, and attended the [[presidential inauguration]]s of [[Barack Obama]] in both [[First inauguration of Barack Obama|2009]] and [[Second inauguration of Barack Obama|2013]].<ref name="USA"/>

==Death==
Edwards died at the age of 95 at the Cincinnati VA Medical Center in [[Corryville, Cincinnati]].<ref name="USA"/> On October 4, 2019, his funeral was held at [[First Unitarian Church (Cincinnati, Ohio)|First Unitarian Church in Avondale]].<ref name="Hearst">{{cite news |title=Overflow crowd pays final respects to Cincinnati Tuskegee Airman Leslie Edwards |url=https://www.wlwt.com/article/overflow-crowd-pays-final-respects-to-cincinnati-tuskegee-airman-leslie-edwards/29364793 |accessdate=1 January 2020 |agency=WLWT-TV |publisher=Hearst Television Inc. |date=4 October 2019}}</ref> He was survived by three daughters, one son and 19 grandchildren.<ref name="USA"/>

==Personal life==
Edwards married Anna Mae in 1943 and they had four children. The couple stayed married 72 years, until the death of Anna Mae in 2016.<ref name="Legacy"/><ref name="USAF"/>
