'''Christopher Bernard Tanzey''' (born 6 March 1957) is an English-Australian former [[Association football|footballer]] and [[Manager (association football)|manager]].<ref>{{WorldFootball.net}}</ref>

==Career==
In his playing career, Tanzey was a professional with [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]], playing for the club's [[Liverpool F.C. Reserves and Academy|reserve side]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://trove.nla.gov.au/newspaper/article/120883565 |title=Tanzey thirsty for a new challenge |newspaper=[[The Canberra Times]] |page=20 |date=9 March 1990 |access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref> He then played in Australia for [[Canberra Olympic FC|Downer Olympic]] in the [[National Premier Leagues Capital Football|ACT League]], and later Griffith City FC and [[Tuggeranong United FC|Tuggeranong United]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://trove.nla.gov.au/newspaper/article/120851028 |title=Tanzey to coach cup-winning team |newspaper=[[The Canberra Times]] |page=20 |date=8 September 1989 |access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref> He also played [[futsal]] for the Canberra Strikers in Australia's National Indoor Soccer Tanzey was the head coach of the [[Australia women's national soccer team|Australia women's soccer team]] at the [[Football at the 2000 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2000 Summer Olympics]]. He was also the coach of the [[Belconnen United FC|Belconnen Blue Devils]] during the [[2003–04 NSW Premier League season]], earning the coach of the year award.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ozfootball.net/ark/States/NSW/2004/2004PLR.html |title=2003/4 New South Wales Premier League |website=ozfootball.net |access-date=31 December 2019 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304202856/http://www.ozfootball.net/ark/States/NSW/2004/2004PLR.html |archive-date=4 March 2016}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Tanzey is a native of [[Birkenhead]], England,<ref>{{cite news |last=Cooke |first=Graham |url=https://trove.nla.gov.au/newspaper/article/118147976 |title=Hong Kong club lures Fernandez |newspaper=[[The Canberra Times]] |page=20 |date=4 June 1991 |access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref> but moved to Australia during his career.<ref name="backup"/>
