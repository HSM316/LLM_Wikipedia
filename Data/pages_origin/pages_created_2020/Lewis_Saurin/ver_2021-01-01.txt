'''Lewis Saurin''' (died 1749) was an [[Anglican]] [[priest]] in [[Ireland]]<ref>'Donnybrook Parish Magazine' Vol. X. SEPTEMBER i, 1899. No. 117.</ref> during the first half of the [[18th-century|18th Century]].<ref>'Ulster Journal of Archaeology' Ulster Archaeological Society Vol IV p59: Belfast; McCaw, Stephenson & Orr; 1898</ref>

The brother of [[Jacques Saurin]],<ref>{{Cite book|title=Chambers's Encyclopædia: A Dictionary of Universal Knowledge for the People, Volume 8|publisher=W. and R. Chambers|year=1876|isbn=|location=Edinburgh|pages=498}}</ref> he was appointed [[Dean of Ardagh]]<ref>"Fasti Ecclesiae Hibernicae: The succession of the prelates Volume 3" [[Henry Cotton (divine)|Cotton,H.]] p188: Dublin, Hodges & Smith, 1848-1878</ref> and [[Precentor]] of [[Christ Church Cathedral, Dublin]] on 22 March 1727.<ref>"Fasti Ecclesiae Hibernicae: The succession of the prelates Volume 2" [[Henry Cotton (divine)|Cotton,H.]] p54: Dublin, Hodges & Smith, 1848-1878</ref> holding both posts until his death in 1749.

[[James Saurin (priest)|His son]] was [[Archdeacon of Dromore]];<ref>'Death of Archdeacon Saurin' [[Belfast News Letter]] (Belfast, Ireland), Tuesday, May 13, 1879; Issue 19870</ref> [[James Saurin|his grandson]], [[Bishop of Dromore]];  [[William Saurin|his great nephew]], [[Attorney General for Ireland]];<ref>Dunlop, Robert  '''William Saurin'''  ''Dictionary of National Biography 1885–1900'' Vo.50 p.333</ref> and [[William Saurin Lyster|his great nephew]], an [[Australia|Australian]] [[impresario]].<ref>{{Dictionary of Australian Biography|First=William|Last=Lyster|shortlink=0-dict-biogL.html#lyster1|accessdate=29 August 2016}}</ref>
