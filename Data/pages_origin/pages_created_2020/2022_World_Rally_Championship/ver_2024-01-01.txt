The '''2022 FIA World Rally Championship''' was the 50th season of the [[World Rally Championship]] (WRC), an international [[rallying]] series organised by the [[Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile]] (FIA). Teams and crews competed for the [[List of World Rally Championship Drivers' champions|World Rally Championships for Drivers]], [[List of World Rally Championship Co-Drivers' champions|Co-drivers]] and [[List of World Rally Championship Manufacturers' champions|Manufacturers]]; each won by [[Kalle Rovanperä]], [[Jonne Halttunen]] and [[Toyota Gazoo Racing WRT]].

Crews were free to compete in cars complying with [[Groups Rally|Groups Rally1 to Rally5]] regulations; however, only manufacturers competing with [[Group Rally1|Rally1]] cars [[Homologation (motorsport)|homologated]] under radically new regulations were eligible to score points in the manufacturers' championship. The championship began in January 2022 with the [[Monte Carlo Rally|Rallye Monte-Carlo]] and concluded in November 2022 with [[Rally Japan]]. The series was [[World Rally Championship support categories|supported]] by [[2022 World Rally Championship-2|World Rally Championship-2]] and [[2022 World Rally Championship-3|World Rally Championship-3]] at every round of the championship with the junior category at selected events.

[[Sébastien Ogier]] and [[Julien Ingrassia]] were the reigning drivers' and co-drivers' champions, having secured their eighth championship titles at the [[2021 Rally Monza]]. However, Ingrassia did not defend his title as he retired from competition at the end of [[2021 World Rally Championship|2021 season]]. [[Toyota Gazoo Racing WRT|Toyota]] were the defending manufacturers' champions.

Rovanperä and Halttunen won their first World Rally Championship titles after winning the [[2022 Rally New Zealand]]. At the age of twenty-two, Rovanperä became the youngest ever [[List of World Rally Championship Drivers' champions|World Rally Champion]]. At the conclusion of the championship, [[Ott Tänak]] and [[Martin Järveoja]] finished second, trailing by fifty points. [[Thierry Neuville]] and [[Martijn Wydaeghe]] were third, a further twelve points behind. In the manufacturers' championship, Toyota Gazoo Racing WRT successfully defended their title at the [[2022 Rally Catalunya (World Rally Championship)|2022 Rally Catalunya]], a seventy-point lead over [[Hyundai World Rally Team|Hyundai Shell Mobis WRT]], with [[M-Sport World Rally Team|M-Sport Ford WRT]] in third.

===Location changes===
The headquarters of the [[Monte Carlo Rally]] moved from [[Gap, Hautes-Alpes|Gap]], [[Hautes-Alpes]] to [[Monaco]] alone. The rally was previously headquartered solely in Monaco in {{WRC|2006}}.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2021/wrc/monaco-base-for-new-look-rallye-monte-carlo/|title=Monaco base for new-look Rallye Monte-Carlo|website=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=24 July 2021|access-date=24 July 2021}}</ref> [[Rally Sweden]] returned to the championship after a one-year absence. Because of a lack of snow, the organisers moved the rally headquarters for the first time in WRC history.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://matraxlubricants.com/lack-of-snow-forces-rally-sweden-move-up-north/|title=Lack of snow forces Rally Sweden move up north|website=matraxlubricants.com|date=9 April 2021|access-date=3 February 2022}}</ref> It relocated from [[Torsby]], [[Värmland]] northwards to [[Umeå]] in [[Västerbotten County]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2021/wrc/rally-sweden-moves-north-to-umea/|title=Rally Sweden moves north to Umea|website=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=8 April 2021|access-date=26 April 2021}}</ref> The rally was initially scheduled to cover {{convert|303.74|km|mi|1|abbr=on}} in nineteen [[Special stage (rallying)|special stages]], but it was reduced to seventeen in a total of {{convert|264.81|km|mi|1|abbr=on}} due to [[reindeer]] movements.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2022/wrc/reindeer-movements-erase-saturday-sweden-test-from-schedule/|title=Reindeer movements erase Saturday Sweden test|website=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=17 February 2022|access-date=19 February 2022}}</ref><ref name="Sweden Route"/> [[Rally Italia Sardegna]] relocated its rally base back to [[Alghero]] following a one-off headquarter in [[Olbia]] for the [[2021 Rally Italia Sardegna|2021 event]].<ref name="Sardinia Route">{{cite web|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2022/wrc/olbia-suburb-test-to-launch-sardinia-event/|title=Olibia suburb test to launch Sardinia event|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=2 February 2022|access-date=3 February 2022}}</ref>

===Calendar changes===
[[Rally New Zealand]] returned to the championship for the first time since {{WRC|2012}}.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.motorsport.com/wrc/news/new-zealand-secures-spot-on-2022-wrc-calendar/6687153/|title=New Zealand secures spot on 2022 WRC calendar|first=Tom|last=Howard|work=[[Motorsport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=16 October 2021|access-date=16 October 2021}}</ref> The rally had also secured a spot in the calendar in {{WRC|2020}}, but [[2020 Rally New Zealand|the event]] was not held in response to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in New Zealand|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/new-zealands-2020-wrc-return-off/|title=New Zealand's 2020 WRC return off|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=4 June 2020|access-date=4 June 2020}}</ref> [[Rally Japan]] took the final spot in the calendar. It was scheduled as the final round in {{WRC|2020}} and {{WRC|2021}}, before being called off due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Japan|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/151507/belgium-gets-wrc-round-after-rally-japan-axed|title=Belgium gets WRC round for the first time after Rally Japan axed|first=Jamie|last=Klein|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=19 August 2020|access-date=19 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2021/wrc/pandemic-forces-cancellation-of-japans-wrc-fixture/|title=Pandemic Forces Cancellation of Japan's WRC Fixture|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=7 September 2021|access-date=7 September 2021}}</ref> [[Rally Mexico]] had contracts to hold the WRC event in 2022 and 2023, but the rally was not included on the calendar.<ref>{{cite news|first=Jesús|last=Muñoz|url=https://soymotor.com/coches/noticias/el-rally-de-mexico-no-estara-en-el-wrc-2021-pero-volvera-en-2022-y-2023-981086|title=El Rally de México no estará en el WRC 2021, pero volverá en 2022 y 2023|trans-title=Rally Mexico will not be in the 2021 WRC, but will return in 2022 and 2023|language=Spanish|work=soymotor.com|date=9 October 2020|access-date=12 April 2021}}</ref> A national event was held in the bid of a 2023 return.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://dirtfish.com/rally/wrc/rally-mexico-to-host-nations-rally-in-wrc-return-bid/|title=Rally Mexico to host nations rally in WRC return bid|first=David|last=Evans|website=dirtfish.com|publisher=DirtFish|date=10 February 2022|access-date=19 February 2022}}</ref> [[Rally Chile]] found itself in a similar situation to Mexico as their contract with [[WRC Promoter GmbH]] lasted till 2022. Chile had previously hosted [[2019 Rally Chile|the event]] in {{WRC|2019}}.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/147405/rally-chile-cancelled-due-to-political-unrest|title=WRC's 2020 Rally Chile cancelled due to political and social unrest|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=29 November 2019|access-date=31 December 2019|quote=Rally Chile's Felipe Horta said: 'The decision was to wait a year to take the world championship. We have talked with the FIA and the WRC [Promoter] in Germany, where they have fortunately understood very favourably what is happening and are allowing us to cancel the 2020 date and resume the contract we have established for three years.'}}</ref> [[Rally GB]] was bidding for a 2022 return as the event was planned to hold in [[Northern Ireland]], but the proposal was ultimately failed.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.motorsport.com/wrc/news/uk-misses-out-on-2022-wrc-round-as-northern-ireland-plans-collapse/7385708/|title=UK misses out on 2022 WRC round as Northern Ireland plans collapse|first=Tom|last=Howard|work=[[Motorsport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=18 January 2020|access-date=23 January 2022}}</ref>

==Regulation changes==
===Technical regulations===
The championship introduced a new set of technical regulations known as "[[Group Rally1|Rally1]]" to replace the [[World Rally Car]]. The Rally1 regulations placed a greater emphasis on [[One-Design#Motorsport|standardised parts]] than in previous years to make the sport more accessible.<ref name="pyramid">{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/146456/fia-steps-up-plan-to-simplify-wrc-career-ladder|title=FIA steps up plan to simplify WRC into five-tier career ladder|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=8 October 2019|access-date=16 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/148682/neuville-doesnt-see-point-of-s%2A%2A%2A-2022-wrc-rules|title=Neuville doesn't see point of "s***" 2022 proposed WRC rules|first=Nick|last=Garton|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=16 March 2020|access-date=16 March 2020}}</ref>

Rally1 was also introduced [[Hybrid vehicle drivetrain|hybrid drivetrains]] to the sport for the first time.<ref name="hybrids">{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/147699/wrc-cars-will-get-hybrid-boost-on-stages-from-2022|title=WRC cars will get a boost from hybrid power on stages from 2022|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=21 December 2019|access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref> This took the form of an e-motor that produces {{convert|100|kW|hp|1|abbr=on}} fitted to current 1.6&nbsp;L [[turbocharged]] [[inline-4]] engine and must be used to power the car when travelling around service parks and through built-up areas when driving between [[Special stage (rallying)|stages]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/existing-engines-to-stay-in-wrc-hybrid-switch/|title=Existing engines to stay in WRC Hybrid switch|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=31 March 2021|access-date=9 April 2021}}</ref> Drivers were free to use the [[Electric motor|e-motor]] to offer additional power when competing in a stage, with the FIA dictating how much power can be used and how long a driver can deploy it for.<ref name="hybrids"/> The hybrid system and the software governing its use will be standardised for three years as a way of keeping the costs of competing down.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/engineering/feature/9834/digging-into-the-cramble-for-hybrid|title=Digging into the 'scramble for hybrid|first1=James|last1=Newbold|first2=David|last2=Evans|first3=Matt|last3=James|first4=David|last4=Malsher|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=26 January 2020|access-date=26 January 2020}}</ref> The system was provided by Compact Dynamics, a subsidiary of [[Formula E]] team [[Abt Sportsline|Audi Sport ABT]] supplier [[Schaeffler Group|Schaeffler]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/148858/wrc-announces-exclusive-22-hybrid-systems-supplier|title=WRC announces exclusive 2022 hybrid systems supplier|first=James|last=Newbold|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=31 March 2020|access-date=1 April 2020}}</ref>

The championship was also introduced with a standardised safety structure in a bid to improve safety standards. This coincided with the [[Homologation (motorsport)|homologation]] requirements being re-written to allow teams to enter a scaled chassis based on production cars rather than having to adapt a chassis to fit a roadgoing model.<ref name="hybrids"/>

===Sporting regulations===
The eligibility requirements for crews entering events were simplified and streamlined into a system called the "[[Rally Pyramid|FIA Rally Pyramid]]".<ref name="pyramid"/> The top tier of the sport was known as "[[Group Rally1|Rally1]]". The second tier, "[[Group Rally2|Rally2]]", was for manufacturer teams and professional independent teams in the [[2022 World Rally Championship-2|World Rally Championship-2]]. This was followed by "[[Group Rally3|Rally3]]" for [[Privateer (motorsport)|privately entered]] and "[[Pay driver|gentlemen driver]]" crews competing in the [[2022 World Rally Championship-3|World Rally Championship-3]]. "[[Group Rally4|Rally4]]" and "[[Group Rally5|Rally5]]" entries would not contest their own dedicated championship, but would be permitted to enter WRC rallies.<ref name="pyramid"/>

A team taking part in the Teams' championship will only be able to score points in a rally if a manufacturer competing with the same make of car has been entered into the event. Teams competing in the Teams' championship must contend a minimum of seven rallies, one of which must be outside Europe to be eligible for the championship. Under the new regulations, individual teams will compete against one another for the Teams' championship.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fia.com/sites/default/files/wrcsr_2022_16-12-21_b_green_blue.pdf|title=2022 FIA World Rally Championship – Sporting regulations|publisher=[[Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile]]|page=10|access-date=24 January 2022}}</ref> -->
Specific liaison sections in which Rally1 competitors must drive in full electric mode was introduced into the championship.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2021/wrc/electric-only-zones-compulsory-in-hybrid-era/|title=Electric-only zones compulsory in hybrid era|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=27 October 2021|access-date=28 October 2021}}</ref> Following calls from WRC manufacturers, time penalty for competitors who missed a [[Special stage (rallying)|special stage]] due to hybrid issue was reduced from ten minutes to two minutes from the [[2022 Croatia Rally]] onwards.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.motorsport.com/wrc/news/understanding-hybrid-failures-top-priority-fia/10113614/|title=Understanding WRC hybrid failures top of FIA priority list|first=Tom|last=Howard|work=[[Motorsport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=22 April 2022|access-date=16 December 2022}}</ref>

Rovanperä and Halttunen turned the table around as they won [[2022 Rally New Zealand|the following event]], which was enough for the Finns to secure their first world titles.<ref>{{cite news|title=Rovanperä becomes youngest ever WRC champion|url=https://dirtfish.com/rally/wrc/rovanpera-becomes-youngest-ever-wrc-champion/|first=Luke|last=Barry|website=dirtfish.com|publisher=DirtFish|date=2 October 2022|access-date=2 October 2022}}</ref> At the age of 22 years and 1 day, Rovanperä also became [[List of World Rally Championship Drivers' champions|the youngest World Rally Champion]], breaking the previous record set by [[Colin McRae]] at the age of 27 years and 89 days in {{WRC|1995}}.<ref>{{cite news|title=Breaking: Rovanperä seals WRC crown with historic victory down under|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2022/wrc/rovanpera-seals-wrc-crown/|website=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=2 October 2022|access-date=2 October 2022}}</ref> Toyota also secured the manufacturers' title at [[2022 Rally Catalunya (World Rally Championship)|the following round]], with Ogier and Veillas — who sealed the first victory in his career — claimed the win.<ref>{{cite news|title=Double delight in Spain for Ogier and Toyota|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2022/wrc/double-delight-in-spain-for-ogier-and-toyota/|website=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=23 October 2022|access-date=23 October 2022}}</ref>

The final round of the season, the [[2022 Rally Japan|Rally Japan]] started with the Hyundai of Sordo and Carrera destroyed by fire on the second stage.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://dirtfish.com/rally/sordo-explains-what-happened-in-dramatic-ss2-fire/|title=Sordo explains what happened in dramatic SS2 fire|first1=Luke|last1=Barry|first2=Colin|last2=Clark|website=dirtfish.com|publisher=DirtFish|date=12 November 2022|access-date=12 November 2022}}</ref> With Ogier and new co-driver Landais collecting a puncture and Breen and new co-driver [[James Fulton (co-driver)|James Fulton]] crashing out, Evans and Martin took the early lead ahead of Neuville and Rovanpera at the end of Friday. Rovanperä and Halttunen later dropped down to 12th after changing a puncture and damaging a wheel, with Neuville and Wydaeghe surpassing Evans and Martin for the lead at the end of Saturday.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://dirtfish.com/rally/wrc/neuville-leads-into-final-day-of-rally-japan/|title=Neuville leads into final day of Rally Japan|first=Luke|last=Barry|website=dirtfish.com|publisher=DirtFish|date=12 November 2022|access-date=12 November 2022}}</ref> Neuville and Wydaeghe eventually won the rally, with teammate Tänak and Järveoja completed a Hyundai 1–2 at their final outing for the team.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://dirtfish.com/rally/wrc/neuville-spoils-toyotas-party-with-victory-in-japan/|title=Neuville spoils Toyota's party with victory in Japan|first=Luke|last=Barry|website=dirtfish.com|publisher=DirtFish|date=13 November 2022|access-date=13 November 2022}}</ref> Local hero Katsuta and Johnston rounded out of the podium.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://us.motorsport.com/wrc/news/katsuta-wrc-podium-on-home-soil-a-special-feeling/10399290/|title=Katsuta: WRC podium on home soil a special feeling|first=Tom|last=Howard|work=[[Motorsport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=13 November 2022|access-date=14 November 2022}}</ref> An early puncture and a late struggling in the rain on Sunday saw Evans further dropped down to fifth.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://dirtfish.com/rally/wrc/rain-finally-arrives-in-japan-but-neuville-still-clear/|title=Rain finallt arrives in Japan, but Neuville still clear|first=Luke|last=Barry|website=dirtfish.com|publisher=DirtFish|date=13 November 2022|access-date=13 November 2022}}</ref>

===Scoring system===
Points were awarded to the top ten classified finishers in each event. In the manufacturers' championship, teams were eligible to nominate three crews to score points, but these points were only awarded to the top two classified finishers representing a manufacturer and driving a 2022-specification [[Group Rally1|Rally1 car]]. There were also five bonus points awarded to the winners of the [[Power Stage]], four points for second place, three for third, two for fourth and one for fifth. Power Stage points were awarded in the drivers', co-drivers' and manufacturers' championships.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/power-stage-points-extended-to-manufacturers--wrc2-and-wrc3/|title=Power Stage points extended to manufacturers, WRC2 and WRC3|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=17 December 2020|access-date=17 December 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.wrc.com/en/wrc/news/september-2016/2017-calendar/page/3911--12-12-.html|title=2017 WRC dates confirmed|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=28 September 2016|access-date=29 September 2016}}</ref>
