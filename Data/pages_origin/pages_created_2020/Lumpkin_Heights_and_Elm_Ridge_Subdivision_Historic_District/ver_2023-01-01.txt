The '''Lumpkin Heights and Elm Ridge Subdivision Historic District''' is a residential historic district in [[Mattoon, Illinois|Mattoon]], [[Illinois]]. The district encompasses the Lumpkin Heights and Elm Ridge Subdivision neighborhoods in eastern Mattoon. Lumpkin Heights was originally developed as Lafayette Heights in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries, and it only became known by its current name after further development in the mid-twentieth century. The Elm Ridge Subdivision was developed entirely within the mid-twentieth century. The original Lafayette Heights section of the district includes examples of many popular architectural styles from the time of its development, with several examples of [[Colonial Revival architecture|Colonial Revival]], [[Tudor Revival architecture|Tudor Revival]], and [[American Craftsman]] designs. The remainder of the district exhibits trends common in post-[[World War II]] residential development, including several [[Modernist architecture|Modernist]] designs, [[Ranch-style houses]], and prefabricated homes.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Thompson |first1=Stephen A. |last2=Cloyd |first2=Carolyn |title=National Register of Historic Places Registration Form: Lumpkin Heights and Elm Ridge Subdivision Historic District |url=http://gis.hpa.state.il.us/pdfs/803144.pdf |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191231035729/http://gis.hpa.state.il.us/pdfs/803144.pdf |url-status=dead |archive-date=December 31, 2019 |publisher=[[Illinois Historic Preservation Division]] |access-date=December 30, 2019 |date=July 1, 2017}}</ref>

The district was added to the [[National Register of Historic Places]] on December 26, 2017.<ref name="NPS"/>
