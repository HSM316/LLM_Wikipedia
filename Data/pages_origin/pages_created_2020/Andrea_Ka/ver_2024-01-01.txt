'''Andrea Daravy Ka''' (born 25 April 1992) is a Cambodian former [[tennis]] player.

Ka has career-high [[WTA rankings]] of 560 in singles, reached in June 2018, and 466 in doubles, achieved on 24 September 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/players/324170/andrea-ka | title=Andrea Ka's profile at WTA}}</ref> She is the first Cambodian tennis player to win an [[International Tennis Federation|ITF]] tennis tournament.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Manjunath |first1=H. S. |title=Ka becomes first Cambodian to win ITF event {{!}} Phnom Penh Post |url=https://www.phnompenhpost.com/sport/ka-becomes-first-cambodian-win-itf-event |work=www.phnompenhpost.com |language=en}}</ref>

In 2017, Ka won a bronze medal in the [[Tennis at the 2017 Southeast Asian Games – Women's singles|women's singles]] event at the [[2017 Southeast Asian Games]].<ref name=khmertimes1 /> She also represented Cambodia at the [[2014 Asian Games]] in Incheon.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Manjunath |first1=H. S. |title=No cheer yet at Incheon Games |url=https://www.phnompenhpost.com/sport/no-cheer-yet-incheon-games |work=[[Phnom Penh Post]] |date=26 September 2014 |language=en}}</ref>
