'''Gabriele Hiller-Ohm''' (née ''Ohm''; born February 28, 1953, in [[Lübeck]]) is a German politician ([[Social Democratic Party of Germany|SPD]]). She has been a member of the [[Bundestag]] since 2002. Since 2014, she has been the SPD parliamentary group's spokesperson for tourism policy.

== Life and work ==
After graduating from high school, Gabriele Hiller-Ohm first studied [[German studies]], [[history]], and [[pedagogy]] at the [[University of Hamburg]]. She then began training as an [[electrician]], which she had to break off due to the insolvency of the teaching company. From 1982, she worked as an [[Contributing editor|editor]] and in 1987, she moved to the [[Technical University of Applied Sciences Lübeck]] as a press and public relations officer. In 1997, she took over the management of the Technical University of Applied Sciences Lübeck's office for the participation in the "[[Leonardo da Vinci programme|Leonardo Da Vinci]]" European vocational training and mobility programme.

Hiller-Ohm is divorced and has two children.

== Party ==
Gabriele Hiller-Ohm has been a member of the SPD since 1983. From 2008 to 2014, she was deputy chairwoman of the SPD district association of Lübeck. Since 2013, she has been chairwoman of the Lübeck Working Group of Social Democratic Women (ASF). Hiller-Ohm belongs to the left wing of the SPD. Within the SPD parliamentary faction, she is a member of the Parliamentary Left.<ref name="ln-onlin-Die_Tite">{{cite web|title=Die Titelverteidigerin|periodical=Ln-online.de|url=http://www.ln-online.de/Lokales/Luebeck/Die-Titelverteidigerin|access-date=2019-03-07|date=2017-09-24}}</ref>

== Member of parliament ==
Gabriele Hiller-Ohm was a member of the parliament of the Hanseatic City of Lübeck from 1990 to 2002, and was chairwoman of the SPD parliamentary group there from 1999 to 2002.

She has been a member of the German Bundestag since 2002. She is a full member of the Committee for Labour and Social Affairs and the Committee for Tourism.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gabriele Hiller-Ohm, SPD |url=https://www.bundestag.de/abgeordnete/biografien/H/hiller_ohm_gabriele-520376 |access-date=2019-03-07}}</ref> In the 18th Bundestag she is a deputy member of the Committee for Food and Agriculture.<ref>{{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/*/http://www.bundestag.de/bundestag/ausschuesse18/a10/mitglieder/260624}} [[Bundestag]], online, retrieved 18 September 2014.</ref> Since 2014, Gabriele Hiller-Ohm has been chairman of the working group on tourism and thus spokeswoman for tourism policy of the SPD parliamentary group in the Bundestag.<ref>{{Cite book|url=http://www.spdfraktion.de/fraktion/arbeitsgruppen/arbeitsgruppe-tourismus|title=Arbeitsgruppe Tourismus {{!}} SPD-Bundestagsfraktion|date=2011-06-20}}</ref> Since 2005, she has been deputy spokesperson of the [[Schleswig-Holstein]] state group within the SPD parliamentary group.

Gabriele Hiller-Ohm entered the Bundestag as a directly elected representative of the Lübeck constituency from 2002 to 2013. She received 50.8% of the first votes in her first [[2002 German federal election|federal election in 2002]]. In the [[2005 German federal election|2005 federal election]], she received 49.7% of the first votes here. In the [[2009 German federal election|2009 federal election]], she again received the direct mandate with 36.7% of the first votes.<ref>''SPD bleibt stärkste Kraft in Lübeck.'' In: ''[[Lübecker Nachrichten]].'' Special election edition, 28 September 2009, p. 15.</ref> In the 2013 federal election, she reached 40.7% of the first votes.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ergebnis Bundestagswahl 2013 Erststimme |website=Stadt Lübeck |url=http://www.luebeck.de/stadt_politik/rathaus/wahlen/bundestagswahl13/bw13_1.html |access-date=2014-05-03 |archive-date=2014-05-03 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140503095927/http://www.luebeck.de/stadt_politik/rathaus/wahlen/bundestagswahl13/bw13_1.html |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In the [[2017 German federal election|2017 federal election]], she ran for her constituency's direct mandate again,<ref>{{cite web|title=Hiller-Ohm kandidiert erneut für den Bundestag |url=https://www.hl-live.de/aktuell/text.php?id=108131 |access-date=2017-08-18}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=SPD schickt erneut Hiller-Ohm ins Rennen um Berlin|periodical=Lübecker Nachrichten |url=http://www.ln-online.de/Lokales/Luebeck/SPD-schickt-erneut-Hiller-Ohm-ins-Rennen-um-Berlin |access-date=2017-08-18 |last=Kai Dordowsky}}</ref> but was defeated by the CDU candidate [[Claudia Schmidtke]].<ref>Josephine von Zastrow: [http://m.ln-online.de/Lokales/Luebeck/Luebeck-wird-schwarz ''Lübeck wird schwarz''.]{{Dead link|date=December 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }} In: ''ln-online''. 25 September 2017.</ref> Hiller-Ohm returned to the Bundestag via her party's state list.<ref>{{cite web|title=Bundestagswahl 2017: Gewählte Bewerber |website=Bundeswahlleiter|url=https://www.bundeswahlleiter.de/bundestagswahlen/2017/gewaehlte/bund-99/land-1.html#5721217c-43f2-485b-b57c-d05468ed471f |access-date=2017-09-25}}</ref>

Hiller-Ohm participates in the project "Transparent Members of Parliament" and regularly publishes her income and appointments.<ref>{{cite web|title=Einkünfte und Termine|website=Gabriele Hiller-Ohm |url=https://www.hiller-ohm.de/persoenliches/einkuenfte/index.html |access-date=2017-08-18|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160623194739/http://www.hiller-ohm.de/persoenliches/einkuenfte/index.html|archive-date=2016-06-23 |date=2010-06-02}}</ref>

== Political positions ==
Gabriele Hiller-Ohm is a firm opponent of combat missions of the [[Bundeswehr]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Die Titelverteidigerin|url=http://www.ln-online.de/Lokales/Luebeck/Die-Titelverteidigerin|access-date=2017-09-06|last=Lübecker Nachrichten, Lübeck, Schleswig-Holstein, Germany}}</ref> In roll-call votes, she repeatedly voted against the majority of her [[parliamentary group]]. On the other hand, she voted in favor of humanitarian missions.<ref>{{cite web|title=Profil von Gabriele Hiller-Ohm, SPD – Bundestag 2013–2017|periodical=Abgeordnetenwatch.de|url=https://www.abgeordnetenwatch.de/profile/gabriele-hiller-ohm/archive/216620?page=2#block-pw-vote-profile|access-date=2017-08-18}}</ref>

Hiller-Ohm has long campaigned for the introduction of the statutory minimum wage and for a pension without deductions from 63 after 45 contribution years.<ref>{{Cite book|url=http://www.spdfraktion.de/presse/pressemitteilungen/gesetzlicher-mindestlohn-ohne-ausnahmen-hilft-hunderttausenden-gastgewerbe|title=Gesetzlicher Mindestlohn ohne Ausnahmen hilft Hunderttausenden im Gastgewerbe {{!}} SPD-Bundestagsfraktion|date=2014-03-12}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.hiller-ohm.de/presse/hiller-ohm-versprechen-gehalten-rentenpaket-kommt|title=Hiller-Ohm: Versprechen gehalten - das Rentenpaket kommt|date=2014-01-29}}{{Dead link|date=February 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> As the responsible rapporteur for the SPD parliamentary group in the Bundestag,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hiller-ohm.de/politische_arbeit/arbeit_und_soziales/index.html|title=Menschen in Arbeit bringen - Sozialstaat sichern!|date=2012-10-29|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121029105707/https://www.hiller-ohm.de/politische_arbeit/arbeit_und_soziales/index.html|archive-date=2012-10-29|access-date=2020-02-28}}</ref> she also lobbied for the quota of women on supervisory boards and for the Wage Justice Act.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.hiller-ohm.de/presse/internationalen-frauentag-grund-mehr-feiern-quote-gesetz|title=Zum Internationalen Frauentag ein Grund mehr zum Feiern: Die Quote ist Gesetz!|date=2015-03-06}}{{Dead link|date=February 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.hiller-ohm.de/presse/langem-widerstand-union-kommt-endlich-lohngerechtigkeitsgesetz|title=Nach langem Widerstand der Union – kommt jetzt endlich das Lohngerechtigkeitsgesetz|date=2016-10-07}}{{Dead link|date=February 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
