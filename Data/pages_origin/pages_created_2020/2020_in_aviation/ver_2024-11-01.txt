Many aviation-related events took place in 2020. The [[Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on aviation|aviation industry was impacted]] by the onset of the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].

==Events==

=== January ===
; 2 January
: A [[Sikorsky UH-60 Black Hawk]] helicopter operated by the [[Republic of China Air Force]] (ROCAF) [[2020 New Taipei helicopter crash|crashes]] in the [[Wulai District]] in [[New Taipei City]], [[Taiwan]]. Eight people are killed, including Taiwan's [[Chief of the General Staff (Republic of China)|Chief of the General Staff]], Gen. [[Shen Yi-ming]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ranter|first=Harro|date=|title=Accident Sikorsky UH-60M Black Hawk 933, 02 Jan 2020|url=https://aviation-safety.net/wikibase/wiki.php?id=231864|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200102114322/https://aviation-safety.net/wikibase/wiki.php?id=231864 |archive-date=2020-01-02 |access-date=2020-01-05|website=aviation-safety.net|publisher=[[Aviation Safety Network]]}}</ref>

; 8 January
: A [[Boeing 737-800]] operating as [[Ukraine International Airlines Flight 752]] from [[Tehran]], Iran to [[Kyiv]], Ukraine, crashes shortly after takeoff from [[Tehran Imam Khomeini International Airport]], killing all 167 passengers and 9 crew.<ref>{{cite web|title=Ukrainian Boeing plane crashes in Iran, 176 people dead|date=8 January 2020 |url=https://edition.cnn.com/middleeast/live-news/iran-plane-crash-live-intl-hnk/index.html|publisher=CNN|access-date=8 January 2020}}</ref> Iran subsequently admits that the aircraft was mistakenly shot down by a surface-to-air missile.<ref>{{cite news |title=Iran admits 'unintentionally' shooting down plane |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-51073621 |date=11 January 2020}}</ref> The shootdown would become the deadliest aviation disaster of 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ranter|first=Harro|date=|title=ASN Aircraft accident Boeing 737-8KV (WL) UR-PSR Sabashahr|url=https://aviation-safety.net/database/record.php?id=20200108-0|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200108091643/https://aviation-safety.net/database/record.php?id=20200108-0 |archive-date=2020-01-08 |access-date=2021-01-14|website=aviation-safety.net|publisher=Aviation Safety Network}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Ranter|first=Harro|date=|title=2020|url=https://aviation-safety.net/database/dblist.php?Year=2020|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200120031154/http://aviation-safety.net:80/database/dblist.php?Year=2020 |archive-date=2020-01-20 |access-date=2021-01-14|website=aviation-safety.net|publisher=Aviation Safety Network}}</ref>

; 9 January
: A [[Lockheed C-130 Hercules]] operated by the [[South African Air Force]] from [[Beni, Democratic Republic of the Congo|Beni]] to [[Goma]] in the Democratic Republic of the Congo crashes upon landing at [[Goma International Airport]]. A fire which broke out on the left engine upon landing was contained and all 59 passengers and 8 crew members were safely evacuated with no casualties.<ref>{{cite web|title=UN: South African military plane crash-lands in Congo|url=https://www.stripes.com/news/africa/un-south-african-military-plane-crash-lands-in-congo-1.614136|publisher=Stars and Stripes|access-date=11 January 2020}}</ref>
 
; 23 January
: [[N134CG]], a [[Lockheed C-130 Hercules]] operated by [[New South Wales Rural Fire Service]] crashed while fighting bushfires near [[Cooma]], [[New South Wales]] in Australia. Three United States personnel were killed in the accident.<ref>{{cite web|title=Three dead as air tanker fighting bushfires crashes near Snowy Mountains|date=23 January 2020 |url=https://www.smh.com.au/national/nsw/air-tanker-fighting-bushfires-reportedly-crashes-in-snowy-mountains-20200123-p53u3r.html|publisher=Sydney Morning Herald|access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Three firefighters dead after Large Air Tanker crashes while fighting bushfires in southern NSW|url=https://www.abc.net.au/news/2020-01-23/nsw-rfs-air-tanker-crashes-near-numeralla-bushfire/11893554?pfmredir=sm|work=ABC News| date=23 January 2020 |access-date=23 January 2020}}</ref>
: China starts shutting down regular air traffic to and from [[Wuhan Airport]] in [[Hubei|Hubei province]] due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite news | author = Jakob Wert | title = China shuts down Wuhan Airport amid virus outbreak | newspaper = International Flight Network | date = 23 January 2020 | url = https://www.ifn.news/posts/china-shuts-down-wuhan-airport-amid-virus-outbreak/ | access-date = 5 February 2020}}</ref>

; 25 January
: The [[Boeing 777X]] takes off from [[Paine Field]], [[Washington (state)|Washington]] on its first ever flight lasting roughly three hours.<ref name=Boeing25jan2020>{{cite press release |url= https://investors.boeing.com/investors/investor-news/press-release-details/2020/New-Boeing-777X-Completes-Successful-First-Flight/default.aspx |title= New Boeing 777X Completes Successful First Flight |date= January 25, 2020 |publisher= Boeing}}</ref>

; 26 January
: [[Caspian Airlines Flight 6936]], a McDonnell Douglas MD-83, overruns the runway on landing at Mahshahr Airport, Iran, landing on a road beyond the runway. All 144 passengers and crew survive.

; 27 January 
: An [[Bombardier Global Express#Military variants|E-11A]], a [[United States Air Force]] plane, [[Deh Yak airplane crash|crashed]] in the [[Dih Yak District]], [[Ghazni Province]], Afghanistan. At least two people were killed.<ref name=AH>{{cite web |url=http://avherald.com/h?article=4d282570&opt=0 |title=No Afghan Ariana Airlines Crash |first=Simon |last=Hradecky |publisher=Aviation Herald |access-date=27 January 2020}}</ref>

; 28 January
: At the [[Heli-Expo]] convention in Anaheim, California, Italian helicopter manufacturer [[Leonardo S.p.A.|Leonardo]] announces it will acquire Swiss helicopter company [[Kopter]] (320 people), developer of the single-engined [[Kopter SH09]], for at least $185 million in 2020.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/hai-heli-expo-2020/leonardo-acquires-kopter-in-a-deal-worth-at-least-185m/136406.article |title= Leonardo acquires Kopter in a deal worth at least $185m |author= Pilar Wolfsteller |date= 28 January 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

; 29 January
: [[Boeing]] releases its fourth quarter 2019 earnings report, including data for the full 12 months of 2019. The company made an annual loss for the first time since 1997.<ref>{{cite news | author = Jakob Wert | title = Boeing posts first annual loss since 1997 in 2019 earnings | newspaper = International Flight Network | date = 29 January 2020 | url = https://www.ifn.news/posts/boeing-posts-first-annual-loss-since-1997-in-2019-earnings/ | access-date = 5 February 2020}}</ref>

===February===
; 5 February
: A Boeing 737-800 operating [[Pegasus Airlines Flight 2193]] from [[İzmir Adnan Menderes Airport|Izmir]] skids off the runway on landing at [[Istanbul Sabiha Gökçen International Airport]], and breaks into pieces. Three people were killed and 179 injured.<ref>{{cite news |title=Three dead, 179 hurt as plane skids off runway |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-51384667 |work=BBC News |date=6 February 2020}}</ref>

; 12 February
: [[Bombardier Aviation|Bombardier]] exits the commercial aviation business, selling its share in the [[Airbus A220|A220]] programme to Airbus for $591 million.<ref>{{cite press release |title=Airbus and the Government of Québec become sole owners of the A220 Programme as Bombardier completes its strategic exit from Commercial Aviation |url=https://ir.bombardier.com/en/press-releases/press-releases/86019-airbus-and-the-government-of-quebec-become-sole-owners-of-the-a220-programme-as-bombardier-completes-its-strategic-exit-from-commercial-aviation |website=ir.bombardier.com |publisher=Bombardier Inc. |access-date=13 February 2020}}</ref>

; 17 February
: The solar-powered [[BAE Systems PHASA-35]] [[Atmospheric satellite|HAPS]] built by [[BAE Systems]] makes its [[maiden flight]] from the [[Woomera Test Range]] in South Australia.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/aerospace/bae-joins-high-altitude-race-with-maiden-phasa-35-flight/136767.article |title= BAE joins high-altitude race with maiden PHASA-35 flight |author= Dan Thisdell |date= 17 February 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

=== March ===
; 5 March 
: UK regional carrier [[Flybe (1979–2020)|Flybe]] ceases operations of its 60 [[Q400]] and [[E-Jet]]s, and files for administration after the UK government rejected a £100 million ($129 million) loan, amid the [[COVID-19 outbreak]].<ref name=Flight5march2020>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/airlines/beleaguered-flybe-ceases-operations/137095.article |title= Beleaguered Flybe ceases operations |author= Alfred Chua |date= 5 March 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

; 11 March
: Due to the [[COVID-19 outbreak]], US President [[Donald Trump]] announces a travel ban from Europe's [[Schengen area]] to the United States from 13 March, for 30 days.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/air-transport/us-bans-travel-from-europe/137206.article |title= US bans all travel from Europe |author= Pilar Wolfsteller |date= 12 March 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref> Numerous other countries implement similar bans in the following days.

; 13 March
: [[American Airlines]] accelerates the retirement of their 757s and 767s, and later the A330s and E190s, most of the fleet was inherited from US Airways.

; 17 March
: [[Trans States Airlines]] announces it will shut down operations on 1 April amid the coronavirus crisis, nine months earlier than previously planned due to a pilot shortage and consolidation.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/strategy/trans-states-airlines-to-fold-in-april/137349.article |title= Trans States Airlines to fold in April |author= Pilar Wolfsteller |date= 17 March 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

; 18 March
: The average daily number of flights in [[Eurocontrol]] airspace falls below half that of the previous year, due to [[travel restrictions related to the COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/air-transport/european-flight-activity-halves-under-coronavirus-onslaught/137446.article |title= European flight activity halves under coronavirus onslaught |author= David Kaminski-Morrow |date= 20 March 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

; 19 March
: [[Compass Airlines (North America)|Compass Airlines]], owned by Trans States Holdings and flying regional routes for American Airlines as [[American Eagle (airline brand)|American Eagle]] from Los Angeles, writes to its employees it will shut down completely on 7 April.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/strategy/compass-airlines-to-shut-down-operations/137408.article |title= Compass Airlines to shut down operations |author= Pilar Wolfsteller |date= 19 March 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

=== April ===
; 14 April
: The [[Government of South Africa]] refuses a request by [[South African Airways]] for [[South African rand|R]]10 billion (600 Million USD) in emergency funding needed to continue operation of the airline, which was already insolvent and undergoing [[South African insolvency law|business rescue]] when all flights were suspended due to the coronavirus.<ref>{{cite news |last=Dlulane |first=Bonga |date=14 April 2020 |title=Gordhan rejects SAA's request for R10 billion  |url=https://ewn.co.za/2020/04/14/gordhan-rejects-saa-s-request-for-another-r10-billion |work=[[Eyewitness News (South Africa)|Eyewitness News]] |location=South Africa |access-date=27 April 2020 }}</ref>

; 15 April
: As demand continues to collapse due to the [[impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on aviation]], over two-thirds of the worldwide fleet of 22,000 mainline passenger airliners are inactive, leaving 7,635 in operation.<!--ref name=Flight15apr2020--> Europe is most affected, with less than 15% of aircraft operating, compared to 45% in North America and 49% in Asia.<!--ref name=Flight15apr2020--> A greater proportion of [[narrowbody aircraft]] are inactive (37%) than [[widebody aircraft]] (27%).<ref name=Flight15apr2020>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/fleets/mixed-fortunes-globally-as-active-fleet-drops-towards-7000-aircraft/137893.article |title= Mixed fortunes globally as active fleet drops towards 7,000 aircraft |author= Max Kingsley-Jones |date= 15 April 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

; 17 April
: The [[Government of Kazakhstan]] revokes the [[air operator's certificate]] of [[Bek Air]] and all [[airworthiness certificate]]s for its [[Fokker 100]] aircraft, citing the airline's failure to correct safety violations discovered after the fatal 2019 crash of [[Bek Air Flight 2100]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Sadikhova |first=Nargiz |title=Operations certificate of Kazakhstan's Bek Air recalled following plane crash |url=https://en.trend.az/business/transport/3225252.html |website=en.trend.az |publisher=[[Trend News Agency]] |date=17 April 2020 |location=Baku, Azerbaijan |access-date=23 April 2020 }}</ref>

; 23 April
:The US [[Federal Aviation Administration]] reduces the operating hours of about 100 [[air traffic control]] facilities due to the drastic drop in air traffic caused by the coronavirus pandemic.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.aopa.org/news-and-media/all-news/2020/april/23/faa-to-scale-back-atc-service-as-traffic-declines |title=FAA to scale back ATC service as traffic declines |author=<!--staff writers--> |date=23 April 2020 |website=aopa.org |publisher=[[Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association]] |access-date=24 April 2020}}</ref>

; 25 April
: [[Boeing]] announces it has terminated the planned [[Boeing–Embraer joint venture]] with [[Embraer]], after the April 24 delay expired.<ref>{{cite press release |url= https://boeing.mediaroom.com/2020-04-25-Boeing-Terminates-Agreement-to-Establish-Joint-Ventures-with-Embraer |title= Boeing Terminates Agreement to Establish Joint Ventures with Embraer |date= April 25, 2020 |publisher= Boeing}}</ref>

; 27 April
: In the first total closure of a US commercial airport for pandemic-related reasons, local leaders close [[Westchester County Airport]] to airlines in order to expedite a major runway repaving project, citing the indefinite suspension of almost all airline flights there.<ref>{{cite news |last=Negroni |first=Christine |date=23 April 2020 |title=Few Travelers, Few Flights and Now, a Total Airport Shutdown |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/04/23/nyregion/westchester-county-airport-closing-coronavirus.html |url-access=limited |work=[[The New York Times]] |location=New York City |access-date=24 April 2020 }}</ref>

=== May ===
; 4 May
:An [[Embraer EMB 120 Brasilia]], operated by [[African Express Airways|East African Express Airways]] on an [[air charter]] flight delivering pandemic relief supplies, [[2020 East African Express Airways Brasilia crash|crashes on approach]] to [[Berdale]], [[Somalia]], killing all 2 crew and 4 non-revenue passengers on board. On 10 May, a leaked [[African Union Mission to Somalia|African Union peacekeeping force]] report alleges that the aircraft was mistakenly shot down by ground troops of the [[Ethiopian National Defense Force]] who were operating outside the peacekeeping force's authority.<ref>{{cite news |last=Dahir |first=Abdi Latif |date=10 May 2020 |title=Ethiopian Troops May Have Shot Down Aid Plane in Somalia, Report Says |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/05/10/world/africa/coronavirus-aid-plane-crash-somalia.html |url-access=limited |work=[[The New York Times]] |location=New York City |access-date=11 May 2020 }}</ref>

; 5 May
: The term "[[preighter]]" is coined by [[Lufthansa]] CEO [[Carsten Spohr]] to describe the increasingly common practice of using passenger aircraft to operate cargo-only flights.<ref>{{cite news |title=Lufthansa coins "preighter" term for passenger-cargo flights |url=https://www.businesstraveller.com/business-travel/2020/05/05/lufthansa-coins-preighter-term-for-passenger-cargo-flights/ |first=Alex |last=McWhirter |work=Business Traveller |date=5 May 2020 |access-date=28 May 2021}}</ref>

; 17 May
: The prototype [[Cessna 408 SkyCourier]] makes its first flight from [[Beech Factory Airport]], to be used for testing leading to certification along five additional test airframes.<ref name=Textron17may2020>{{cite press release |title=First Cessna SkyCourier twin utility turboprop takes flight |url=https://txtav.com/en/newsroom/2020/05/first-cessna-skycourier-twin-utility-turboprop-takes-flight|date=May 17, 2020 |publisher=Textron Aviation }}</ref>

; 18 May
: The Pipistrel E-811 is the first electric [[aircraft engine]] to be awarded a [[type certificate]] by [[EASA]] for use in [[general aviation]]. The E-811 powers the [[Pipistrel Velis Electro]].<ref name="E811_TCDS">{{cite web |title=TCDS for E811 engine, model 268MVLC |url=https://www.easa.europa.eu/sites/default/files/dfu/E.234%20TCDS%20Pipistrel%20electric%20engine%20E-811_Issue%2001.pdf |website=European Union Aviation Safety Agency |access-date=18 August 2020 |date=18 May 2020}}</ref><ref name="E811_Flyer">{{cite news |last1=Calderwood |first1=Dave |title=Pipistrel offers type certified electric motor |url=https://www.flyer.co.uk/pipistrel-offers-type-certified-electric-motor-to-others/ |access-date=18 August 2020 |agency=FLYER Magazine |publisher=Seager Publishing |date=9 July 2020}}</ref>

; 22 May
: [[Pakistan International Airlines Flight 8303]], an [[Airbus A320]] flying from Lahore to Karachi with 91 passengers and 8 crew, reports failures of both engines while in final approach and crashes into a residential area. Ninety-seven people on board are killed in the accident, while two passengers survived with injuries.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://avherald.com/h?article=4d7a6e9a&opt=0 |title= Crash: PIA A320 at Karachi on May 22nd 2020, impacted residential area during final approach |author= Simon Hradecky |date= May 22, 2020 |access-date= May 24, 2020 |work= The Aviation Herald}}</ref>

=== June ===
; 4 June
:[[Zipair Tokyo]], a subsidiary of [[Japan Airlines]], begins operations with the [[Boeing 787 Dreamliner|Boeing 787-8 Dreamliner]].

; 10 June
: The [[Velis Electro]] variant of the two-seat [[Pipistrel Virus]] is the first [[electric aircraft]] to secure certification, from the [[European Union Aviation Safety Agency|EASA]].<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/news/pipistrel-velis-electro-earns-first-all-electric-aircraft-type-certification/138779.article |title= Pipistrel Velis Electro earns first all-electric aircraft type certification |author= Kate Sarsfield |date= 10 June 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

; 11 June
: The [[AIDC T-5 Brave Eagle]], a [[jet trainer]], makes its [[maiden flight]] in [[Taiwan]].<ref name=Flight11June2020>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/taiwans-brave-eagle-trainer-conducts-first-flight/138788.article |title= Taiwan's Brave Eagle trainer conducts first flight |author= Greg Waldron |date= 11 June 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

; 30 June
: [[Aeroméxico]] files for bankruptcy in the United States due to pandemic-related losses, but expects to continue flying during restructuring.

=== July ===
; 1 July
: After 1500 hours of flight tests and demo flights with three prototypes, the [[Airbus Helicopters H160]] is type certified by the [[European Union Aviation Safety Agency|EASA]] on 1 July 2020, before FAA certification and delivery to a US customer planned for the same year.<ref>{{cite press release |url= https://www.airbus.com/newsroom/press-releases/en/2020/07/the-h160-receives-easa-approval.html |title= The H160 receives EASA approval |date= 1 July 2020 |publisher= Airbus}}</ref>

; 2 July
: The [[Stratos 714|Stratos 716X]] single-engine kit [[business jet]] made a 22 minute [[maiden flight]] in [[Redmond, Oregon]], climbing to 13,500 ft and launching a several month flight-test program.<ref name=Flight3July2020>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/business-aviation/stratos-716x-makes-maiden-sortie/139142.article |title= Stratos 716X makes its maiden sortie |author= Kate Sarsfield |date= 3 July 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

=== August ===
; 7 August
: [[Air India Express Flight 1344]], a [[Boeing 737]] flying from [[Dubai International Airport]] to [[Calicut International Airport]], suffers a runway excursion upon landing. Both pilots and 18 passengers are killed in the accident.<ref>{{cite news |title=India begins examination of plane's black box after deadly crash |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-india-crash/india-begins-examination-of-planes-black-box-after-deadly-crash-idUSKCN25509N |work=Reuters |date=9 August 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

; 11 August
:[[Azul Brazilian Airlines|Azul]] launches a regional subsidiary with both a passenger and a cargo branch, dubbed [[Azul Conecta]]. It flies [[Cessna 208 Caravan|Cessna 208]]s.

; 22 August
: An [[Antonov An-26]] operating a [[South West Aviation (South Sudan)|South West Aviation]] cargo flight from Juba to Aweil, South Sudan, [[2020 Juba An-26 crash|crashes]] upon take-off, killing 8 of the 9 occupants on board.

; 27 August
: The [[Otto Celera 500L]], an experimental high-efficiency business and utility aircraft, is unveiled.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/airframers/otto-aviation-reveals-celera-business-aircraft-with-super-efficient-laminar-flow/139929.article |title= Otto Aviation reveals Celera business aircraft with super-efficient 'laminar flow' |publisher=FlightGlobal |last=Hemmerdinger |first= Jon  |date= 28 August 2020 |access-date=7 October 2020}}</ref>

===September===

:Retrofitted [[Piper M350]] Malibu completes first commercial passenger [[hydrogen fuel cell]]-powered [[zero emission]]s flight from [[Cranfield]], Bedfordshire.{{citation needed|date=September 2020}} 
:[[United Airlines]] declares that it will be the first [[United States|US]] airline to roll out a [[COVID-19]] testing program for passengers, giving flyers from [[San Francisco]] bound for [[Hawaii]] the option to order an at-home testing kit, or to reserve a time for a rapid test at the airport.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/united-airlines-covid-testing-program/|title=United Airlines to be first U.S. airline with COVID-19 testing program for passengers|access-date=24 September 2020|website=CBS News|date=24 September 2020 }}</ref>

; 30 September
: US firm [[General Atomics]] bought from [[RUAG]] the [[Dornier 228]] production line in Oberpfaffenhofen, including the transfer of all 450 employees, pending regulatory approval.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.flightglobal.com/aerospace/general-atomics-buys-ruags-dornier-228-programme-and-german-mro-business/140648.article |title= General Atomics buys RUAG's Dornier 228 programme and German MRO business |author= Murdo Morrison |date= 16 October 2020 |work= Flightglobal}}</ref>

===October===
; 1 October
: Boeing announces the [[Boeing 787|787]] will be produced only in [[North Charleston]] from mid-2021 due to the impact of the pandemic, as its production rate fall to six per month.<ref>{{cite press release |url= https://boeing.mediaroom.com/2020-10-01-Boeing-to-Consolidate-787-Production-in-South-Carolina-in-2021 |title= Boeing to Consolidate 787 Production in South Carolina in 2021 |date= Oct 1, 2020 |publisher= Boeing}}</ref>
: [[CARES Act|Federal government COVID-19 pandemic relief]] for US airlines expires with the [[United States Congress]] deadlocked over extending further aid. Various US airlines respond by commencing massive layoffs and furloughs, with American Airlines to cut 19,000 jobs, and United Airlines to cut 13,000.<ref name=DMN_AA_Sep30>{{cite news |last=Arnold |first=Kyle |date=30 September 2020 |title=American Airlines says it will start furloughing 19,000 workers Thursday |url=https://www.dallasnews.com/business/airlines/2020/09/30/american-airlines-will-start-furloughing-for-19000-workers-thursday-after-deal-for-aid-falls-through/ |url-access=limited |work=[[The Dallas Morning News]] |location=Dallas, Texas |access-date=7 October 2020 }}</ref>

; 5 October
: [[AirAsia Japan]] permanently ceases flights due to mounting losses stemming from COVID-19 international flight restrictions.

; 8 October
: As several airlines [[Boeing 747-400#Retirement and economic value|accelerate retirement of the Boeing 747-400 due to the COVID-19 pandemic]], the last two 744s of [[British Airways]] - the type's largest operator until this point - leave [[Heathrow Airport|London Heathrow]], officially completing BA's retirement of this type.<ref>{{Cite web|date=8 October 2020|title=Final flight for British Airways' last two Heathrow-based Boeing 747 planes|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/av/uk-54457434|website=BBC News}}</ref>

;31 October
: After almost 10 years of construction-related delays and {{Currency|4 billion|code=euro|passthrough=yes}} in cost overruns, [[Berlin Brandenburg Airport]] opens to commercial passenger flights, to replace both [[Berlin Tegel Airport]] and the recently closed [[Berlin Schönefeld Airport]] as the primary international airport for the city of [[Berlin]] and the state of [[Brandenburg]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cnn.com/travel/article/brandenburg-new-berlin-airport-opens/index.html |title=Nearly a decade late, Berlin's Brandenburg Airport finally opens (during a pandemic) |last1=Lopez |first1=Julyssa |last2=O'Hare |first2=Maureen |date=30 October 2020 |website=cnn.com |publisher=CNN |access-date=31 October 2020}}</ref>

===November===
; 18 November
: The [[Federal Aviation Administration]] clears the [[Boeing 737 MAX]] to return to flight after its [[Boeing 737 MAX grounding|grounding]].<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.faa.gov/documentLibrary/media/Notice/N_8900.570_FAAWeb.pdf |title= Boeing 737-8 and 737-9 Airplanes: Return to Service |publisher= Federal Aviation Administration |date= Nov 18, 2020}}</ref>

===December===
; 3 December
: [[Ryanair]] orders 75 [[Boeing 737 MAX]] 8-200s, the first large order since the grounding, for a list value of $7bn but an actual value estimated at $3bn or less.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://leehamnews.com/2020/12/03/ryanair-orders-737-max-giving-boost-to-boeing/ |title= Ryanair orders 737 MAX, giving boost to Boeing |author= Scott Hamilton |date= Dec 3, 2020 |work= Leeham News}}</ref>

; 7 December

==Deadliest crash==
The deadliest crash of this year was [[Ukraine International Airlines Flight 752]], a [[Boeing 737]] which was shot down shortly after takeoff from [[Tehran]], [[Iran]] on 8 January, killing all 176 people on board.
