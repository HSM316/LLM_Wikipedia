'''Cheng Cheng-chung''' ({{zh|t=鄭正忠|s=郑正忠|first=t,s}}; born 9 July 1936) is a Taiwanese [[Olympic weightlifting|weightlifter]]. He competed in the [[Weightlifting at the 1964 Summer Olympics – Men's 82.5 kg|men's light heavyweight event]] at the [[1964 Summer Olympics]].<ref name="SportsRef">{{cite Sports-Reference |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/ch/cheng-cheng-chung-1.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200418064628/https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/ch/cheng-cheng-chung-1.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=18 April 2020 |title=Cheng Cheng-chung Olympic Results |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

==Biography==
Cheng is from the [[Gangshan District]] of Taiwan's [[Kaohsiung]]. He graduated from a {{ill|national school (Taiwan)|lt=national school|zh|國民學校}}. Cheng was the owner of a watch shop in Gangshan in 1960. In 1959, he served in Taiwan's military.<ref name="UDN1960-10-28">{{cite news |date=1960-10-28 |title=舉重出現奇葩 鄭正忠創全國新紀 陳培基蔡振德刷新大會 |trans-title=Weightlifting Breakthrough: Cheng Cheng-chung Sets New National Record. Chen Pei-ji and Cai Zhen-deng Break Competition Records |language=zh |newspaper=[[United Daily News]] |page=2 }}</ref> At Taiwan's Provincial Games ({{zh|t=省運比賽}}) in 1958, he represented [[Tainan]] and won in the [[middleweight]] weightlifting category.<ref name="UDN1960-10-28"/> Cheng competed in the middleweight category at the 3rd Provincial Weightlifting Championship ({{zh|t=全省舉重錦標賽}}) in 1959. He did a [[bench press]] of {{convert|215|lb|kg|abbr=on|order=flip}}, a [[snatch (weightlifting)|snatch]] of {{convert|215|lb|kg|abbr=on|order=flip}}, and a [[clean and jerk]] of {{convert|270|lb|kg|abbr=on|order=flip}}. In total, he lifted {{convert|700|lb|kg|abbr=on|order=flip}} and placed second in his weight category.<ref name="UDN1959-08-24">{{cite news |date=1959-08-24 |title=舉重賽四人破全國 劉志偉鍾正明楊國典韋基堯分別建功 另羽量級劉銀道亦平全國紀錄 每項前三名初選為國手 |trans-title=Four Athletes Break National Records in Weightlifting Competition: Liu Zhi-wei, Zhong Zheng-ming, Yang Guo-dian, and Wei Ji-yao Shine, While Liu Yin-dao Ties National Record in Featherweight; Top Three in Each Event Selected for National Team Consideration |language=zh |newspaper=[[United Daily News]] |page=3 }}</ref> He competed in an international weightlifting competition in [[Manila]] on 27 May 1960 and placed second in the middleweight category.<ref name="UDN1960-05-28">{{cite news |date=1960-05-28 |title=台港岷埠際舉重賽 我國代表獲勝榮歸 |trans-title=Taiwan-Hong Kong-Philippines International Weightlifting Competition: Our country's representatives achieved victory and returned triumphantly |language=zh |newspaper=[[United Daily News]] |page=2 }}</ref> On 27 October 1960, Cheng represented Tainan in Taiwan's Provincial Games ({{zh|t=省運比賽}}) in the [[light heavyweight]] division. In the snatch event, he lifted {{convert|230|lb|kg|abbr=on|order=flip}}. In the bench press event, he lifted {{convert|250|lb|kg|abbr=on|order=flip}}, which broke a competition record. He lifted {{convert|320|lb|kg|abbr=on|order=flip}} in the clean and jerk, which set a [[list of Taiwanese records in athletics|national record]]. In total, he lifted {{convert|805|lb|kg|abbr=on|order=flip}} which set a national record and earned him first place.<ref name="UDN1960-10-28"/>

Cheng competed in the [[weightlifting at the 1964 Summer Olympics – Men's 82.5 kg|men's light heavyweight event]] at the [[1964 Summer Olympics]]. After he placed last in Group A, the [[Central News Agency (Taiwan)|Central News Agency]] said he underperformed relative to expectations. He did a [[military press]] of {{convert|115|kg|lb|abbr=on}}, a snatch of {{convert|120|kg|lb|abbr=on}}, and a clean and jerk of {{convert|150|kg|lb|abbr=on}}. The total weight he lifted was {{convert|385|kg|lb|abbr=on}} which was below his top weight of {{convert|885|lb|kg|abbr=on|order=flip}} at home.<ref name="UDN1964-10-17">{{cite news |date=1964-10-17 |title=輕重量級舉重 鄭正忠落選 俄國普洛費德奪魁 四七五公斤新世運 |trans-title=Lightweight Weightlifting: Cheng Cheng-chung Eliminated.
