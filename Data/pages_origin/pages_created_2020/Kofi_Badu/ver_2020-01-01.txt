'''Kofi Badu''' was a [[Ghanaian]] [[politician]] and [[journalist]]. He served as a member of parliament during the first republic<ref>{{cite journal |title=Africa, Issues 77-88 |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=hXkdAQAAMAAJ&q=Kofi+Badu+cpp&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjp35LKu87mAhXjUxUIHVuCD98Q6AEIGzAA |publisher=Africa Journal Limited |page=63 |date=1978 |journal=Africa}}</ref><ref>{{cite report |title=Committee of Enquiry into the State Furniture and Joinery Corporation |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=8Sk0k4KxfRgC&q=Kofi+Badu+cpp&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&hl=en&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjBtYa2j9HmAhUIxoUKHVeEDgg4HhDoATAFegQIARAm |publisher=Ghana Publishing Corporation |page=90 |date=1968}}</ref> and a minister of state during the [[Supreme Military Council (Ghana)|Supreme Military Council]] (SMC) era and the [[Armed Forces Revolutionary Council, Ghana|Armed Forces Revolutionary Council]] (AFRC) era. As a journalist, he was editor for various newspapers.

Badu was editor of various newspapers including the ''Cape Coast Daily Mail'' and the ''Daily Gazette''.<ref>{{cite book |last=Asante |first=C. E. |date=1996 |title=The Press in Ghana: Problems and Prospects |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=gspZAAAAMAAJ&dq=Cape+Coast+Daily+Mail+badu&focus=searchwithinvolume&q=Cape+Daily+Mail+badu |publisher=University Press of America |page=6}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |title=Africa Diary, Volume 7 |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=KlQOAQAAMAAJ&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&focus=searchwithinvolume&q=Kofi+Badu |publisher=Africa Publications (India) |page=3379 |date=1966 |journal=Africa Diary}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |title=Ghana Today, Volume 10 |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=CN8qAAAAMAAJ&q=Kofi+Badu+cpp&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&hl=en&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiSsrCJkNHmAhUJLBoKHRPJC0s4KBDoATADegQIARAb |publisher=Information Section, Ghana Office |page=4 |date=1966}}</ref> He assisted T. D. Baffoe as a publicist for the [[Trades Union Congress of Ghana|Trades Union Congress]] and later became editor of the ''[[Ghanaian Times]]'' with T. D. Baffoe as the chief editor.<ref>{{cite book |author=American University (Washington, D.C.). Foreign Areas Studies Division. |date=1962 |title=Special warfare area handbook for Ghana |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=ERJBAQAAIAAJ&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&focus=searchwithinvolume&q=Kofi+Badu |publisher=U.S. Govt. Print. Off. |page=286}}</ref> In June 1965, he became the member of parliament for the Manso constituency as a member of the [[Convention People's Party]].<ref>{{cite journal |title=Parliamentary Debates; Official Report, Part 2 |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=fwpIAQAAIAAJ&q=Kofi+Badu+manso&dq=Kofi+Badu+manso&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjI3JqGktHmAhWsQRUIHdWtBvUQ6AEIGzAA |page=444 |publisher=Ghana National Assembly |date=1965 |journal=Parliamentary Debates}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |title=West Africa Annual, Issue 8 |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=nRBBAQAAIAAJ&dq=Kofi+Badu+manso&focus=searchwithinvolume&q=Badu |publisher=James Clarke |page=80 |date=1965 |journal=West Africa Annual}}</ref> He remained in parliament until the overthrow of the [[Nkrumah government]] in 1966. After the overthrow, he became the editor of the ''Evening Standard'', a newspaper that was owned by [[Komla Agbeli Gbedemah]].<ref>{{cite book |last1=Busia |first1=K. A. |last2=Akyeampong |first2=H. K. |last3=Ofosu-Appiah |first3=L. H. |date=1969 |title=Routledge's handbook of sports and politics |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=yWoMAQAAIAAJ&q=Kofi+Badu+cpp&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&hl=en&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjBtYa2j9HmAhUIxoUKHVeEDgg4HhDoATADegQIARAa |publisher=George Boakie Publishing Company |page=76}}</ref> 

In the [[1969 Ghanaian parliamentary election|1969 parliamentary election]], he contested for the Adansi constituency seat on the ticket of the [[National Alliance of Liberals]] but lost to Stephen Nuamah of the [[Progress Party (Ghana)|Pogress Party]] who polled 7,812 votes against his 2,292 votes.<ref>{{cite journal |title=The Legon Observer, Volume 4, Issues 18-26 |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=cP8NAQAAMAAJ&dq=Kofi+Badu+manso&focus=searchwithinvolume&q=Nuamah+Badu |publisher=Legon Society on National Affairs |page=9 |date=1969}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last=Danquah |first=Moses |date=1969 |title=The Birth of the Second Republic |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=Qy00AAAAIAAJ&dq=Kofi+Badu+manso&focus=searchwithinvolume&q=Nuamah+Badu |page=129}}</ref> During the second republic, he was the editor and owner of the ''Spokesman'' newspaper.<ref>{{cite book |last=Asante |first=C. E. |date=1996 |title=The Press in Ghana: Problems and Prospects |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=gspZAAAAMAAJ&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&focus=searchwithinvolume&q=Kofi+Badu |publisher=University Press of America |page=55}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |title=Talking Drums, Volume 2, Issues 26-47 |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=GDAuAQAAIAAJ&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&focus=searchwithinvolume&q=Kofi+Badu |publisher=Talking Drums Publications |page=6 |date=1985}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=Ansu-Kyeremeh |first1=Kwasi |last2=Karikari |first2=Kwame |date=1998 |title=Media Ghana: Ghanaian Media Overview, Practitioners and Institutions |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=0xUcAQAAIAAJ&q=Kofi+Badu+cpp&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&hl=en&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjk9LvQjdHmAhVEXBoKHUo4DNI4FBDoATADegQIARAW |publisher=School of Communication Studies, University of Ghana |page=3}}</ref> 

After the overthrow of the [[Busia government]], he was appointed the [[National Redemption Council]] (NRC) spokesman for the press.<ref>{{cite book |last=Marable |first=Manning |date=1987 |title=African and Caribbean Politics: From Kwame Nkrumah to the Grenada Revolution |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=RUByAAAAMAAJ&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&focus=searchwithinvolume&q=Kofi+Badu |publisher=Verso |page=147}}</ref> In early 1978 he was appointed Commissioner for Consumer Affairs by the then ruling government, the Supreme Military Council (which was then the NRC from 1972 until 1975).<ref>{{cite journal |last=Uwechue |first=Ralph |title=Africa Today |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=9T8OAQAAMAAJ&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&focus=searchwithinvolume&q=Kofi+Badu |publisher=Africa Journal Limited |page=980 |date=1991 |journal=Africa Today}}</ref> In 1979 he became the Commissioner of Sports and Local Goverment. He remained in this post until June 1979 when the SMC was overthrown. 

During the [[Armed Forces Revolutionary Council, Ghana|Armed Forces Revolutionary Council]] (AFRC) rule, he was reverted to the ministry of Consumer Affairs in June 1979. He served in this capacity until September 1979 when the military government handed over to civilian rule. Badu later became Chairman of the Press Commission.<ref>{{cite journal |title=West Africa, Issues  3336-3360 |url=https://books.google.com.gh/books?id=1atWAAAAYAAJ&q=Kofi+Badu+cpp&dq=Kofi+Badu+cpp&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjp35LKu87mAhXjUxUIHVuCD98Q6AEIITAB |publisher=West Africa Publishing Company Limited |page=2046 |date=1981 |journal=West Africa}}</ref>
