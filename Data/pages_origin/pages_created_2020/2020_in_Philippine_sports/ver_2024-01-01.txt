The following is a list of notable events and developments that are related to '''Philippine sports''' in 2020.

==Events==

===Athletics===
* August 17 – Filipino pole vaulter EJ Obiena bagged a silver in his first online tournament, fresh from his historic bronze-medal finish in the 2020 Monaco Diamond League.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://rappler.com/sports/ej-obiena-silver-medal-finest-vaulter-virtual-tournament-august-2020 | title=EJ Obiena cops silver in 'challenging' 1st virtual tournament | work=Rappler | date=August 18, 2020 | access-date=August 18, 2020}}</ref>
* August 30 – Kristina Knott, the 2019 Southeast Asian Games champion for 200m, wins the silver medal and broke the longstanding record which was previously held by athletics legend [[Lydia de Vega]], who registered 11.28 seconds during the 1987 SEA Games in Jakarta in historic fashion in the Drake Blue Oval Showcase in [[Iowa]], USA.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/sports/08/30/20/kristina-knott-breaks-de-vegas-33-year-old-record-in-100-meters | title=Kristina Knott breaks De Vega's 33-year old record in 100-meters | work=ABS CBN News | date=August 30, 2020 | access-date=August 30, 2020}}</ref>
* September 9 – EJ Obiena has recorded a season-high 5.74 meters to win the 59th Ostrava Golden Spike competition in [[Czech Republic]].<ref>{{cite news | url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/sports/09/09/20/obiena-wins-pole-vault-gold-in-czech-republic-amid-strong-field | title=Obiena wins pole-vault gold in Czech Republic amid strong field | work=ABS CBN News | date=September 9, 2020 | access-date=September 9, 2020}}</ref>

====Professional====

* January 17 – The [[Barangay Ginebra San Miguel]] were crowned as the [[2019 PBA Governors' Cup]] champions after clinching a 105–93 against the [[Meralco Bolts]] in a Game 5 for the 12th straight title at the Mall of Asia Arena.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://cnnphilippines.com/sports/2020/1/17/ginebra-2019-pba-governors--cup-champions.html | title=Barangay Ginebra captures 2019 PBA Governors' Cup title | publisher=[[CNN Philippines]] | date=January 28, 2020 | access-date=January 29, 2020}}</ref>
* March 8 – The opening of the [[2020 PBA season|45th season of PBA]] was held at the [[Smart Araneta Coliseum]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.gmanetwork.com/news/sports/basketball/728829/sanya-lopez-sarah-geronimo-kathryn-bernardo-among-muses-for-pba-s-45th-season/story/ | title=Sanya Lopez, Sarah Geronimo, Kathryn Bernardo among muses for PBA's 45th season | publisher=[[GMA News]] | date=March 8, 2020 | access-date=March 8, 2020}}</ref>

* December 9 – The [[Barangay Ginebra San Miguel]] were crowned as the [[2020 PBA Philippine Cup]] champions after beating [[TNT Tropang Giga]] 4–1 in the finals bubble series, winning 13th straight title at the [[Angeles University Foundation|AUF Sports Arena]], and ending their 13-year All-Filipino drought since the last time they won in the year 2007.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://tv5.espn.com/basketball/pba/story/_/id/30482097/ginebra-finishes-tnt-captures-2020-philippine-cup-crown | title=Barangay Ginebra closes out TNT to win Philippine Cup title | work=ESPN5 | date=December 9, 2020 | access-date=December 11, 2020}}</ref>
