===March===
* 24 March – [[Andrew Cheung]] appointed as Chief Justice of the CFA when Geoffrey Ma retires on 11 January 2021.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.info.gov.hk/gia/general/202003/24/P2020032400260.htm |title= Senior Judicial Appointment: Chief Justice of the Court of Final Appeal |date=24 March 2020 |publisher=[[Government of Hong Kong]]|access-date=29 June 2020}}</ref>

===April===
* 9 April – A court ruled that it is okay to [[Prohibition on Face Covering Regulation|prohibit face covering]] at certain "unauthorised assemblies".<ref>{{cite web |title=政府部分上訴得直 引用緊急法訂立禁蒙面法沒有違憲 |url=https://hk.on.cc/hk/bkn/cnt/news/20200409/bkn-20200409153000978-0409_00822_001.html |website=Oriental Daily News |access-date=1 June 2020}}</ref> On the other hand, it is legal to wear masks in permitted assemblies.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chinadailyhk.com/article/127518|title=Judgment on mask ban strengthens rule of law|last=Kwok|first=Tony|date=15 April 2020|website=China Daily|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200421142219/https://www.chinadailyhk.com/article/127518|archive-date=21 April 2020}}</ref>
===May===
* 8 May – The ban on gathering of more than 4 people relaxes to 8 people.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Pang |first1=Jessie |title=Hong Kong to relax some restrictions as new coronavirus cases dwindle |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-hongkong/hong-kong-to-relax-some-restrictions-as-new-coronavirus-cases-dwindle-idUSKBN22H0TH |work=Reuters |date=5 May 2020 |access-date=1 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Wong |first1=Rachel |last2=Creery |first2=Jennifer |title=Hong Kong to relax business restrictions with gathering limit upped to 8 people |url=https://hongkongfp.com/2020/05/05/breaking-coronavirus-hong-kong-relaxes-business-restrictions-as-gathering-limit-upped-to-8-people/ |website=Hong Kong Free Press |date=5 May 2020 |access-date=1 June 2020}}</ref>
* 15 May – The [[Independent_Police_Complaints_Council#Report_on_Hong_Kong_protests|Independent Police Complaints Council exonerates the police]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://hongkongfp.com/2020/05/15/in-full-hong-kong-police-watchdog-releases-report-on-protest-conduct-but-no-evidence-of-yuen-long-mob-attack-collusion/|title=Hong Kong police watchdog clears force of misconduct citing online 'propaganda', but says 'room for improvement'|date=15 May 2020|website=Hong Kong Free Press}}</ref>
* 27 May
** The [[National People's Congress|Chinese National People's Congress]] votes in favour of [[National People's Congress Decision on Hong Kong national security legislation|national security legislation]] that criminalizes "[[Hong Kong independence|secession]]," "subversion," "terrorism,"  and foreign interference in Hong Kong;<ref>{{cite web |title=Hong Kong security bill backed by China's parliament |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-china-52829176 |work=BBC News |access-date=28 May 2020 |date=28 May 2020}}</ref> the legislation grants sweeping powers to the Chinese central government to suppress the [[2019–20 Hong Kong protests|Hong Kong democracy movement]], including by banning activist groups and curtailing [[civil liberties]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/05/28/world/asia/china-hong-kong-crackdown.html|author=Keith Bradsher|date=28 May 2020|title=China Approves Plan to Rein In Hong Kong, Defying Worldwide Outcry|newspaper=The New York Times}}</ref> The U.S. government responds by declaring Hong Kong is "no longer autonomous" under the [[United States-Hong Kong Policy Act]].<ref>Nick Wadhams and Ben Bartenstein. [https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-05-27/pompeo-finds-hong-kong-is-no-longer-autonomous-from-china U.S. Says Hong Kong's Autonomy Is Gone, Sowing China Trade Doubt] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200720190139/https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-05-27/pompeo-finds-hong-kong-is-no-longer-autonomous-from-china |date=20 July 2020 }}, Bloomberg (27 May 2020).</ref>
** After being closed for months, schools resume classes.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.scmp.com/news/hong-kong/education/article/3075521/coronavirus-little-if-any-possibility-hong-kong-schools |title=Coronavirus: 'little, if any, possibility' Hong Kong schools resume fully on April 20, Lam says|website=South China Morning Post |date=17 March 2020|access-date=23 March 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Coronavirus: Hong Kong to relax business restrictions with gathering limit upped to 8 people |url=https://hongkongfp.com/2020/05/05/breaking-coronavirus-hong-kong-relaxes-business-restrictions-as-gathering-limit-upped-to-8-people/|first1=Jennifer|last1=Creery|first2=Rachel|last2=Wong|work=[[Hong Kong Free Press]]|date=5 May 2020|access-date=12 May 2020}}</ref>
