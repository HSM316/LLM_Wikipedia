'''''West of Texas''''' is a 1943 American [[Western (genre)|Western]] film written and directed by [[Oliver Drake (filmmaker)|Oliver Drake]]. The film stars [[Dave O'Brien (actor)|Dave O'Brien]], [[James Newill]], [[Guy Wilkerson]], Frances Gladwin, Henry Hall and Marilyn Hare. The film was released on May 10, 1943, by [[Producers Releasing Corporation]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tcm.com/tcmdb/title/95341/West-of-Texas/ |title=West of Texas (1943) - Overview |publisher=TCM.com |date= |accessdate=2019-12-30}}</ref><ref name=allmovie>{{cite web|author=Hans J. Wollstein |url=https://www.allmovie.com/movie/west-of-texas-v116277 |title=West of Texas (1943) - Oliver Drake |publisher=AllMovie |date= |accessdate=2019-12-30}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://catalog.afi.com/Film/749-WEST-OFTEXAS |title=West of Texas |publisher=Catalog.afi.com |date= |accessdate=2019-12-30}}</ref>

Newill and O'Brien composed and sing three songs in the movie: "Whistle a Song," "El Lobo," and "Tired of Rambling".<ref name=allmovie/>

==Plot==
The three Texas Rangers help a rancher falsely accused of sabotaging the railroad.<ref name=allmovie/>
