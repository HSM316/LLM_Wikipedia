'''Jørgen Hvidemose''' ({{IPA-da|ˈjɶːʌn ˈviːðəˌmoːsə}}; born 12 May 1940) is a Danish [[Municipalities of Denmark|municipal politician]] and former [[association football|football]] [[Manager (association football)|manager]].<ref>{{WorldFootball.net}}</ref> He led [[Lyngby Boldklub]] to the [[Danish Superliga|Danish championship]] in 1983 and won the [[Danish Cup]] with the club in 1984 and 1985.

==Managerial career==
Hvidemose made his breakthrough as a coach in the [[Danish Superliga|1st Division]] (first-tier) for [[Akademisk Boldklub]] (AB) at just 30 years old in 1970. The club was only one goal short of winning the Danish championship, which instead went to [[Boldklubben 1903|B 1903]] on the last matchday.<ref>{{cite web |title=Historie - Akademisk Boldklub Gladsaxe |url=https://www.ab-fodbold.dk/klubben/historie/ |website=AB-Fodbold.dk |publisher=[[Akademisk Boldklub]] |accessdate=11 August 2020}}</ref> He became head coach of lower tier [[Lyngby Boldklub]] in 1972 and led them to promotion to the [[Danish 1st Division|2nd Division]] (second-tier) in 1974.<ref>{{cite web |title=Danmarksturneringen 1974, slutstillingen |url=https://www.danskfodbold.com/tabel.php?seasonid=1974 |website=DanskFodbold.com |publisher=Dansk Fodbold |accessdate=12 August 2020}}</ref> He repeated this feat in 1977, where he as head coach of [[Brøndby IF]] ensured the club's promotion to the 2nd Division &ndash; a crucial promotion for Brøndby, as professional football was introduced in Denmark the following year.<ref>{{cite web |title=1971-1980 |url=https://brondby.com/klub/historie/1980-1971/ |website=Brondby.com |publisher=Brøndby IF |accessdate=12 August 2020}}</ref> Hvidemose was dismissed from his position halfway through the 1979 season after which [[Tom Køhlert]] took over.<ref name=bif19642014/> 

In 1981, Hvidemose returned as head coach of Lyngby. In his first season in charge, the club managed to finish in second place in the 1st Division. Two years later, the club won its first Danish championship,<ref name="lbk-historie">{{cite web |title=Historie - Lyngby Boldklub |url=https://lyngby-boldklub.dk/historie/ |website=Lyngby-Boldklub.dk |publisher=Lyngby Boldklub |accessdate=12 August 2020}}</ref> and Hvidemose also led the team to two [[Danish Cup]] wins in both 1984 and 1985.<ref name="lbk-historie" /> In 1989, he was appointed as the new coach coach of [[Kjøbenhavns Boldklub]] (KB), and manage to win promotion from the 2nd Division once again. In total, Hvidemose managed to achieve eight promotions with different clubs during his career.<ref name=bif19642014>{{cite book |last1=Thoby |first1=Esben |title=Brøndby IF - 1964-2014 : 50 fortællinger fra 50 profiler |date=2013 |publisher=Turbine |location=[Aarhus] |isbn=978-87-7141-333-5 |pages=34–37 |edition=1. udgave}}</ref>

In 1996, Hvidemose signed a contract with the [[Danish Football Association]] (DBU) to manage the [[Denmark women's national football team|Danish women's national team]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Thomsen |first1=Steen Rudi |title=Nyt nederlag |url=https://jyllands-posten.dk/premium/sport/ECE3476203/Nyt-nederlag/ |website=Jyllands-Posten.dk |publisher=Jyllands-Posten |accessdate=12 August 2020 |date=1 September 1996}}</ref> which under his leadership qualified for the [[UEFA Women's Euro 1997]] and the [[1999 FIFA Women's World Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ankerdal |first1=Steen |title=Spillere kræver Hvidemose fyret |url=https://www.berlingske.dk/sport/spillere-kraever-hvidemose-fyret |website=Berlingske.dk |publisher=Berlingske Tidende |accessdate=12 August 2020 |date=28 June 1999}}</ref>

==Political career==
Hvidemose was elected to the [[Ølstykke Municipality]] city council in 1993 as a representative of the [[Social Democrats (Denmark)|Social Democrats]]. In 2005, he was elected to the city council in the newly created [[Egedal Municipality]] &ndash; which Ølstykke had merged into as part of the [[Municipalities of Denmark#Municipal Reform 2007|''Kommunalreformen'' ("The Municipal Reform" of 2007)]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Egedal sammenlægningsudvalg |url=https://www.kmdvalg.dk/kv/2005/a071A.htm |website=KMDvalg.dk |publisher=KMDValg |accessdate=11 August 2020 |date=22 November 2005}}</ref> He was a representative there until 2013. From 2010, he became an independent member of the city council.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Holm |first1=Maj-Britt |title=Jørgen Hvidemose bryder med S igen |url=https://sn.dk/Egedal/Joergen-Hvidemose-bryder-med-S-igen/artikel/78813 |website=SN.dk |publisher=Sjællandske Nyheder |accessdate=11 August 2020 |date=23 August 2010}}</ref>
