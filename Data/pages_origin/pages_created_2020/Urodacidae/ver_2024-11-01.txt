'''Urodacidae''' is a [[family (biology)|family]] containing two genera of [[scorpion]]s, both of which are [[endemism|endemic]] to [[Australia]]. It was first described by British [[zoology|zoologist]] [[Reginald Innes Pocock]] in 1893. Formerly a [[subfamily]] (Urodacinae) of the [[Scorpionidae]], it was later raised to family rank. Its [[sister group|sister taxon]] is the [[monotypic taxon|monotypic]] family [[Heteroscorpionidae]], the species of which are confined to [[Madagascar]].<ref>{{cite web |author=Jan Ove Rein |year=2023 |title=Urodacidae Pocock, 1893 |work=The Scorpion Files |publisher=Norges teknisk-naturvitenskapelige universitet |accessdate=8 February 2023|url=https://www.ntnu.no/ub/scorpion-files/urodacidae.php}}</ref>
