'''Craig S. Iscoe''' is an associate judge on the [[Superior Court of the District of Columbia]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dccourts.gov/superior-court/judges#associate|title=District of Columbia Superior Court Judges|website=www.dccourts.gov|access-date=December 31, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Bio">{{Cite web|url=https://www.dccourts.gov/sites/default/files/2017-03/DCSC_Bio_Iscoe.pdf |title=Bio |last=|first=|date=|website=www.dccourts.gov|language=en|access-date=December 31, 2019}}</ref> 

== Education and career ==
Iscoe earned his [[Bachelor of Arts]] from [[University of Texas]] in 1974, his [[Juris Doctor]] from [[Stanford Law School]] in 1978 and his [[Master of Laws]] from [[Georgetown University Law Center]] in 1979.

From 1980 to 1982, Iscoe was an attorney for the [[Federal Trade Commission]].<ref name="Bio" /> In 1982, he joined the law firm [[Arent Fox]]. In 1984, he became an [[Assistant United States Attorney]]

Iscoe has served as an adjuct professor at [[Georgetown University]]. He also taught at the [[National Institute for Trial Advocacy]] and was an assistant professor at [[Vanderbilt University Law School]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.law.gwu.edu/craig-s-iscoe|title=Craig S. Iscoe|website=www.law.gwu.edu|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

=== D.C. Superior Court ===
President George W. Bush nominated Iscoe on July 14, 2003, to a 15-year term as an associate judge on the Superior Court of the District of Columbia to the seat vacated by Frederick D. Dorsey.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov/news/releases/2003/07/20030714-8.html|title=Nominations Sent to the Senate|website=georgewbush-whitehouse.archives.gov|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> On September 30, 2003, the [[United States Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs|Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs]] held a hearing on his nomination. On October 22, 2003, the Committee reported his nomination favorably to the senate floor. On October 24, 2003, the full [[United States Senate|Senate]] confirmed his nomination by [[voice vote]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.congress.gov/nomination/108th-congress/812|title=PN812 - Nomination of Craig S. Iscoe for The Judiciary, 108th Congress (2003-2004)|date=2003-10-24|website=www.congress.gov|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> He was sworn in on January 5, 2004.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dccourts.gov/press-releases/local-prosecutor-craig-iscoe-become-superior-court-judge|title=Local Prosecutor Craig Iscoe to become Superior Court Judge {{!}} District of Columbia Courts|website=www.dccourts.gov|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

On August 29, 2018, the Commission on Judicial Disabilities and Tenure recommended that [[President Trump]] reappoint him to second 15-year term as a judge on the D.C. Superior Court.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cjdt.dc.gov/publication/honorable-craig-s-iscoe-reappointment-report|title=Honorable Craig S. Iscoe Reappointment Report {{!}} cjdt|website=cjdt.dc.gov|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>
