'''Karnak Mountain''' is a {{convert|3411|m|ft|0|adj=on}} mountain summit located {{convert|42|km|mi|abbr=on}} west-southwest of [[Invermere]] in the [[Purcell Mountains]] of southeast [[British Columbia]], [[Canada]].<ref name=bcgnis>{{cite bcgnis|id=3039|name=Karnak Mountain|access-date=2021-05-18}}</ref> The nearest higher peak is&nbsp;[[Jumbo Mountain (Canada)|Jumbo Mountain]], {{convert|0.79|km|mi|abbr=on}} to the east-northeast, and [[The Lieutenants]] is set {{convert|2|km|mi|abbr=on}} to the northwest.<ref name="pb">{{cite peakbagger|id=27286|name=Karnak Mountain, British Columbia|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> Karnak and Jumbo form a [[double summit]] [[massif]] which is the second-highest mountain in the Purcells.<ref name="pb"/> The [[first ascent]] of Karnak Mountain was made August 14, 1915, by A.H. & E.L. MacCarthy, M. & W.E. Stone, and [[Conrad Kain]] via the southwest slopes.<ref name=bivouac/> The peak was named in 1910 by Stone and MacCarthy after [[Karnak]], the Egyptian Temple Complex.<ref name=bivouac/> The mountain's name was officially adopted June 9, 1960, when approved by the [[Geographical Names Board of Canada]].

Based on the [[Köppen climate classification]], Karnak Mountain is located in a [[subarctic climate]] zone with cold, snowy winters, and mild summers.<ref name=Peel>{{cite journal | author = Peel, M. C. |author2=Finlayson, B. L. |author3=McMahon, T. A. | year = 2007 | title = Updated world map of the Köppen−Geiger climate classification | journal = Hydrol. Earth Syst. Sci. | volume = 11 | pages = 1633–1644 | issn = 1027-5606}}</ref> Temperatures can drop below −20&nbsp;°C with wind chill factors &nbsp;below −30&nbsp;°C. Precipitation [[Surface runoff|runoff]] from the mountain drains into Jumbo Creek which is a tributary of the [[Columbia River]].
