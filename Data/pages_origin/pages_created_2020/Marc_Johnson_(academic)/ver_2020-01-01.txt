Marc Johnson is an American agricultural economist and academic administrator, serving as the 16th president of the [[University of Nevada, Reno]] since April 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.unr.edu/president/biography|title=About the University President Marc Johnson {{!}} Office of the President|website=University of Nevada, Reno|language=en-us|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>

== Early life and Education ==
Johnson was raised on a family farm near [[Wichita, Kansas]]. He earned a bachelor's degree in Biology from [[Emporia State University]], Master of Technology in International Development from [[North Carolina State University]], Master of Economics from [[Michigan State University]], and a Ph.D in Agricultural Economics from [[Michigan State University]].

== Career ==
Johnson served as dean of the [[Colorado State University College of Agricultural Sciences]] and [[Kansas State University College of Agriculture]]. Johnson joined the faculty of the [[University of Nevada, Reno]] in June 2008 as vice president and provost before serving for one year as interim president in 2011.

In October 2019, it was announced that Johnson would step down as president in June 2020 and transition to a faculty position in the UNR College of Business.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.unr.edu/nevada-today/news/2019/president-johnson-faculty|title=President Marc Johnson to transition to faculty at end of June 2020|website=University of Nevada, Reno|language=en-us|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kolotv.com/content/news/UNR-President-Marc-Johnson-to-transition-to-faculty-564182421.html|title=UNR President Marc Johnson to transition to faculty|last=Release|first=Staff / Press|website=www.kolotv.com|language=english|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>
