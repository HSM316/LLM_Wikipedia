'''Marc Anton Johnson''' (born June 20, 1948) is an American [[agricultural economist]] and academic administrator. He became the 16th president of the [[University of Nevada, Reno]] on April 16, 2011 after his predecessor [[Milton Glick]] died while in office.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.unr.edu/president/biography|title=About the University President Marc Johnson {{!}} Office of the President|website=University of Nevada, Reno|language=en-us|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Johnson was raised on a family farm near [[Wichita, Kansas]]. He earned a bachelor's degree in Biology from [[Emporia State University]], Master of Technology in International Development from [[North Carolina State University]], Master of Economics from [[Michigan State University]], and a Ph.D. in Agricultural Economics from [[Michigan State University]].

==Career==
Johnson served as dean of the [[Colorado State University College of Agricultural Sciences]] and [[Kansas State University College of Agriculture]]. Johnson joined the faculty of the [[University of Nevada, Reno]] in June 2008 as vice president and provost.

On October 31, 2019, it was announced that Johnson step down as president on June 30, 2020 and transition to a position in the UNR College of Business.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.unr.edu/nevada-today/news/2019/president-johnson-faculty|title=President Marc Johnson to transition to faculty at end of June 2020|website=University of Nevada, Reno|language=en-us|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kolotv.com/content/news/UNR-President-Marc-Johnson-to-transition-to-faculty-564182421.html|title=UNR President Marc Johnson to transition to faculty|last=Release|first=Staff / Press|website=www.kolotv.com|language=english|access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> However, it was delayed until [[Brian Sandoval]] succeeded him as president on October 5, 2020.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rgj.com/story/news/2020/04/10/unr-president-marc-johnsons-contract-extended-through-december/5135477002/|title=Regents extend UNR President Johnson's contract to Dec. 31|date=April 10, 2020|agency=[[Associated Press]]|publisher=[[Reno Gazette-Journal]]|access-date=May 8, 2020}}</ref>
