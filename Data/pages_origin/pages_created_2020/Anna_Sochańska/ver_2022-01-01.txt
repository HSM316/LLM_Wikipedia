'''Anna Jolanta Sochańska''' is a Polish diplomat. Since 2019 she has been [[Ambassador]] to the [[Republic of Ireland]].

== Life ==
Sochańska received master's degree from the Institute of Social Prevention and Resocialization at [[University of Warsaw]], writing her thesis about the informal behavior of prisoners in concentration camps. She has been studying also at the [[College of Europe]] in [[Bruges]] (1993–1994).<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://wirlandii.pl/2019/09/nominacja-na-ambasadora-rp-w-irlandii/|title=Nominacja na Ambasadora RP w Irlandii • wIrlandii.pl|last=Adamowicz|first=Olgierd|date=2019-09-01|website=wIrlandii.pl|language=pl-PL|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref>

In 1991, Sochańska started her professional career at the [[Ministry of Health (Poland)|Ministry of Health]], being responsible for international scientific cooperation. In 1994, she worked for the Office of the Council of Ministers, at the office of the Plenipotentiary for European Integration. In 1995, she joined the [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Poland)|Ministry of Foreign Affairs]]. She was a member of the team negotiating the accession of Poland to the European Union, being in charge of foreign and security policy issues and external affairs. In 1997, she served at the embassy in [[the Hague]] during the Dutch and Luxembourg [[President of the European Council|EU presidency]]. Between 2001 and 2006 she was a [[Diplomatic rank|Counselor]] for EU matters at the embassy in [[London]]. Upon her return to the MFA, Sochańska was the head of the EU enlargement policy division and afterwards – a deputy director for South-Eastern Europe, EU Enlargement and the external aspects of migration.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gov.pl/web/ireland/ambassador--|title=Ambassador - Poland in Ireland - Gov.pl website|last=|first=|date=2019-12-30|website=gov.pl|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191230180003/https://www.gov.pl/web/ireland/ambassador--|archive-date=2019-12-30|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> In 2018, she became director of the Department of European Policy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tvpparlament.pl/aktualnosci/pozytywne-oceny-kandydatur-anders-i-sochanskiej-na-stanowiska-ambasadorow/43052717|title=Pozytywne oceny kandydatur Anders i Sochańskiej na stanowiska ambasadorów - TVP Parlament. Telewizja Polska SA|last=|first=|date=2019-06-12|website=www.tvpparlament.pl|language=pl|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> In August 2019, Sochańska was nominated Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of Poland to Ireland.<ref name=":0" /> She presented her [[letter of credence]] in October 2019 to the [[President of Ireland|President]] [[Michael D. Higgins]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.dfa.ie/news-and-media/press-releases/press-release-archive/2019/october/new-ambassadors-present-credentials.php|title=October - New Ambassadors present Credentials - Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade|last=Department of Foreign Affairs|date=2019-10-31|website=www.dfa.ie|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref>

In 2014, Sochańska was awarded with Gold [[Cross of Merit (Poland)|Cross of Merit]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.msz.gov.pl/pl/ministerstwo/sluzba_zagraniczna/dzien_sluzby_zagranicznej/odznaczenia_i_wyroznienia_z_okazji_dsz_2014|title=Odznaczenia i wyróżnienia przyznane z okazji Dnia Służby Zagranicznej 2014|last=|first=|date=2014-11-14|website=www.msz.gov.pl|language=pl|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref>
