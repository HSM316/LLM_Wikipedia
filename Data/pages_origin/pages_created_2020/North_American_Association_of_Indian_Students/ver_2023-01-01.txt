The '''North American Association of Indian Students''' (NAAIS) is a [[nonprofit]] and non-partisan [[Indian Americans|Indian-American]] organization that functions as an umbrella group to cater to Indian and Indian-American students studying in the United States and Canada.<ref>{{Cite news|title='Living in two realities': Indian diaspora reckons with crisis abroad|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/health/2021/05/01/india-diaspora-covid-response/|access-date=2021-11-26|newspaper=Washington Post|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.guidestar.org/profile/84-4455237|title = North American Association of Indian Students - GuideStar Profile}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=NAAIS|url=http://www.naais.org/|url-status=live|access-date=2019-12-30|website=NAAIS}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Callimachi|first=Rukmini|date=2021-06-03|title=Colleges Say Students Must Get a Covid Vaccine. But No, Not That One.|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/06/03/us/coronavirus-vaccine-college-students.html|access-date=2021-11-26|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> The organization has 23,000 members and aims to connect the variety of Indian groups on college and university campuses to create better resources of Indian and Indian-American students. With over 200,000 Indian students and over 500,000 Indian-American students studying in US institutions, young Indians are one of the largest identity groups within the United States, behind China.<ref>{{Cite book|title=The Other One Percent|last1=Chakravorty|first1=Sanjoy|last2=Kapur|first2=Devesh|last3=Singh|first3=Nirvikar|date=2017-01-26|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=978-0-19-064874-9|doi = 10.1093/acprof:oso/9780190648749.001.0001}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theprint.in/india/education/21-fewer-indian-students-went-abroad-last-year-as-us-figures-see-biggest-drop/326433/|title=21% fewer Indian students went abroad last year as US figures saw biggest drop|last=Sharma|first=Kritika|date=2019-11-26|website=ThePrint|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theprint.in/india/governance/no-let-up-in-number-of-indians-going-to-study-in-the-us/148859/|title=India holds on to its position as one of the top two countries that send students to US|last=Sharma|first=Kritika|date=2018-11-13|website=ThePrint|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref>In early 2020, NAAIS partnered with [[Radio Mirchi]] to set up a phone helpline for Indian students impacted by the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic. == Programs and Initiatives ==

=== Mentorship Program ===
The NAAIS mentorship program is a student-led initiative that aims to connect Indian-origin/Indian-American/Indian undergraduate students with experienced Indian alumni mentors. The program encourages support of Indian individuals from underrepresented backgrounds including but not limited to non-Hindu, LGBTQIA+, low-income, and non-professional students through mentorship.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.naaindian.org/work/chotto-motto-hy23k|title=Community|website=North American Association of Indian Students|language=en-US|access-date=2020-03-11}} {{dead link|date=November 2021}}</ref>

=== Partnership with ZEE5 ===
ZEE5 created a strategic partnership with NAAIS to engage more young Indians around college campuses in the Summer and Fall of 2021. The partnership was announced by Priyanka Chopra and Chief Business Officer, Archana Anand.<ref>{{Cite web|last=News|first=The PIE|title=NAAIS holds workshops across US to support Indian students|url=https://thepienews.com/news/naais-holds-workshops-across-us-to-support-indian-students/|access-date=2021-11-20|website=thepienews.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Patel|first=Nirali|date=2021-09-30|title=Sudhanshu Kaushik speaks to UMich Indian American Student Association|url=http://www.michigandaily.com/campus-life/sudhanshu-kaushik-speaks-to-umich-indian-american-student-association/|access-date=2021-11-20|website=The Michigan Daily|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|author=Staff Writer|title=ZEE5 launches in the U.S., ushers in a new era of South Asian entertainment {{!}} News India Times|url=https://www.newsindiatimes.com/zee5-launches-in-the-u-s-ushers-in-a-new-era-of-south-asian-entertainment/|access-date=2021-11-20|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title='This year is still not normal': International students wade through a confusing mishmash of vaccine, visa rules|url=https://theworld.org/stories/2021-07-07/year-still-not-normal-international-students-wade-through-confusing-mishmash|access-date=2021-11-26|website=The World from PRX|language=en}}</ref> As of November 2021, the partnership program had visited over 50 college campuses in the United States, as claimed by both organizations.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-11-04|title=SASA Bollywood Night brings community together with trivia|url=https://dbknews.com/2021/11/04/sasa-bollywood-night-naais-community/|access-date=2021-11-26|website=The Diamondback|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Stakeholders call for clear comms on US uni vaccine rules for students|url=https://thepienews.com/news/communication-needed-around-us-uni-vaccine-requirements/|access-date=2021-11-26|website=thepienews.com|language=en}}</ref>
