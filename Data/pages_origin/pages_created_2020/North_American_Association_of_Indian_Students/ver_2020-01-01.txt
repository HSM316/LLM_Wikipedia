The '''North American Association of Indian Students''' (NAAIS) is a [[nonprofit]] and non-partisan [[Indian Americans|Indian-American]] organization that functions as an umbrella group to cater to Indian and Indian-American students studying in the United Stats and Canada.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.naaindian.org/|title=NAAIS|website=www.naaindian.org|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> With over 200,000 Indian students and over 500,000 Indian-American students studying in US institutions, young Indians are one of the largest identity groups within the United States, behind China.<ref>{{Cite book|url=http://dx.doi.org/10.1093/acprof:oso/9780190648749.001.0001|title=The Other One Percent|last=Chakravorty|first=Sanjoy|last2=Kapur|first2=Devesh|last3=Singh|first3=Nirvikar|date=2017-01-26|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=978-0-19-064874-9}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theprint.in/india/education/21-fewer-indian-students-went-abroad-last-year-as-us-figures-see-biggest-drop/326433/|title=21% fewer Indian students went abroad last year as US figures saw biggest drop|last=Sharma|first=Kritika|date=2019-11-26|website=ThePrint|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theprint.in/india/governance/no-let-up-in-number-of-indians-going-to-study-in-the-us/148859/|title=India holds on to its position as one of the top two countries that send students to US|last=Sharma|first=Kritika|date=2018-11-13|website=ThePrint|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-30}}</ref> The organization is aiming to connect the variety of Indian groups on college and university campuses.
