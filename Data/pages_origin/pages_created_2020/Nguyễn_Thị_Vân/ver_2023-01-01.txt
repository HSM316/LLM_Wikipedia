'''Van Thi Nguyen''' ({{Lang-vi|Nguyễn Thị Vân}}, {{IPA-vi|ŋwiən˦ˀ˥ tʰi˧˨ʔ van˧˧|}}, born 1985/86) is a [[Vietnamese people|Vietnamese]] [[social entrepreneur]] and [[disability rights]] activist.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://m.en.baobacgiang.com.vn/bg/society/315996/two-vietnamese-among-world-s-100-most-influential-women.html|title=Bac Giang Online|website=BaoBacGiang}}</ref>

==Early life==
Born in a village {{convert|300|km}} from [[Hanoi]],<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=http://www.rinagpatel.com/van-thi-nguyen|title=Van Thi Nguyen|website=Rina G. Patel}}</ref> Vân has [[spinal muscular atrophy]], as did her brother Cong Hung Nguyen. She worried about their future, seeing the many disabled beggars in Vietnam, and became depressed, withdrawing from school and attempting suicide.

==Career==
Vân and Hung founded the Will to Live Center in 2003, which provides training for the disabled. Hung died, age 31, and Vân continued to run it alone.<ref name="auto"/>

Vân also runs Imagtor, a [[social enterprise]] which offers photo, video and IT solutions, employing many disabled Vietnamese.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cvdvn.net/2019/10/16/two-inspirational-vietnamese-women-honoured-by-bbc/|title=Two inspirational Vietnamese women honoured by BBC|first=~ Phạm Thu|last=Hương|date=October 16, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/MonTECH.Montana/posts/1128385654032713|title=MonTECH|website=www.facebook.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://noticias.uol.com.br/ultimas-noticias/bbc/2019/10/16/bbc-100-women-2019-quem-esta-na-lista.htm|title=BBC 100 Women 2019: quem está na lista?|website=noticias.uol.com.br}}</ref>

In 2019, she was listed among the BBC's [[100 Women (BBC)|100 Women]], a list of 100 inspiring and influential women. She was also listed by Forbes Vietnam on a list of influential Vietnamese women in 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sea.mashable.com/social-good/6924/bbc-includes-7-inspirational-southeast-asians-in-its-100-women-list|title=BBC includes 7 inspirational Southeast Asians in its 100 Women of 2019 list|date=October 18, 2019|website=Mashable SEA}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://seasia.co/2019/10/28/7-southeast-asian-on-bbc-s-100-women-of-2019-list|title=7 Southeast Asians on BBC's 100 Women of 2019 List &#124; Seasia.co|website=Good News from Southeast Asia}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vietnam-expat.com/myblog/two-vietnamese-among-worlds-100-most-influential-women/|title=Two Vietnamese among world’s 100 most influential women|first1=Tram|last1=Bich|website=vietnam-expat.com}}</ref>
