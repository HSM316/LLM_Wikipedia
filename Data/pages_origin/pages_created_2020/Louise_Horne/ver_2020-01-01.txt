Dame '''Louise Horne''' (born 12 May 1912) is a former Trinidadian politician and nutritionist who introduced the school meals programme.<ref name="pierre" /><ref name="former" /> In 2012 she still lived in the house in [[Arima]] where she was born, which she had inherited from her parents,<ref name=clyne /> and {{As of|June 2019|lc=yes}} she was living in a care home in the same area.<ref name=douglas /><ref name="kongsoo" />

She served as an independent [[Senate (Trinidad and Tobago)|Senator]] in the [[Parliament of Trinidad and Tobago]] from 1976 to 1991.<ref name=pierre />

She was awarded the honour of Lady of the [[Order of St. Gregory the Great]] by [[Pope John Paul II]], one of just two Trinidadian women he so honoured. This honour gave her the title of Dame.<ref name="webb" />
