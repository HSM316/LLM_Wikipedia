{{Short description|Trinidadian politician and nutritionist (1912–2021)}}

'''Louise Horne''' [[Order of St. Gregory the Great|DCSG]] (12 May 1912 – 28 March 2021) was a [[Trinidad and Tobago]] politician and nutritionist who introduced the school meals programme.<ref name="pierre"/><ref name="former"/><ref name="dancing"/> 

In 2012, she still lived in the house in [[Arima]] where she had been born a century earlier, which she had inherited from her parents,<ref name="clyne"/> and in 2019 was living in a care home in the same area.<ref name="douglas"/><ref name="kongsoo"/>

Horne served as an independent [[Senate (Trinidad and Tobago)|Senator]] in the [[Parliament of Trinidad and Tobago]] from 1976 to 1991.<ref name="pierre"/>

She was named [[Dame of the Order of St. Gregory the Great]] by [[Pope John Paul II]], one of just two Trinidadian women whom he so honoured.<ref name="webb"/> 

A postage stamp in her honour was issued in 1980.<ref name="hansard">{{cite web|url=http://www.ttparliament.org/hansards/hs20041109.pdf|title=Senate Debates|publisher=Trinidad and Tobago Parliament|pages=694–695|access-date=30 March 2021|date=9 November 2004}}</ref> 

In 2003, she published a book, ''The Evolution of Modern Trinidad and Tobago'' (Eniath's Printing Company; {{ISBN|9789768193117}}).<ref name="worldcat"/>

==Death==
Louise Horne died in March 2021, at the age of 108.<ref name="webb2"/><ref name="loop"/>
