The '''Luxe Cinema''' is a [[movie theater|cinema]] in [[Wisbech]], [[Isle of Ely]], [[Cambridgeshire]], [[England]].

==History==
The cinema is located in Alexandra Road, this Road also is the location of the [[Angles Theatre]], and the former Selwyn Theatre. Along with the [[Wisbech & Fenland Museum]], Wisbech Library, [[Wisbech Castle]], [[St Peter and St Paul's Church, Wisbech|St. Peters Church]], hall & gardens and The Crescent this area of the town constitutes the 'Cultural Quarter'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.visitcambridgeshirefens.org|title=Wisbech|website=www.visitcambridgeshirefens.org|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

The Luxe cinema was converted from a redundant [[Women's Institute]] Hall and opened in March, 2009 styled as a luxury cinema with leather armchairs and two-seater sofas, and ‘premier’ sofas with service. It has a licensed bar.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cinematreasures.org/theaters/28926|title=The Luxe|website=www.cinematreasures.org|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
The Women's Institute branch now (2019) meets in the Rosmini Centre, Queens Road.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thewi.org.uk|title=Wisbech Centre|website=www.thewi.org.uk|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

The town's last operating cinema the 'Unit One' (previously The Hippodrome) had been demolished in the 1980s to make way for the Horsefair shopping centre development that opened in 1988.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cinematreasures.org/theaters/38336|title=Hippodrome Theatre|website=www.cinematreasures.org|accessdate= 31 December 2019}}</ref>

On 23 May 2014 an eight-screen cinema was opened near the new [[Tesco]] store, Cromwell Road by [[The Light Cinemas]] chain.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cinematreasures.org/theaters/46143|title=Light Cinema|website=www.cinematreasures.org|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

The Luxe was part of 'The Brinks Festival 2015' hosting 'The Secret World of [[Charles Darwin]]' by [[The Magic Circle (organisation)|Magic Circle]] Comedy Award winner Ian Keebler on Monday 18 May.<ref>{{cite news|title=Art showcased at Wisbech Festival|newspaper=Fenland Citizen|date=13 May 2015}}</ref>

In 2015 ''[[The Goob]]'' an award winning Independent film shot across [[the Fens]], was shown followed by an audience question and answer session with film director Guy Myhill and Martin Ferguson.<ref>{{cite news|title=Wisbech cinemas screens The Goob|newspaper=Fenland Citizen|date=29 July 2015}}</ref>

In 2016 [[Leverington]] Primary Academy pupils created animated films as part of the children's arts award with [[Trinity House]] to achieve the Gold Standard and attended the official screening in June 2016.<ref>{{cite news|title=Oscars Ceremony|newspaper=Wisbech Standard|date=23 June 2016}}</ref>

Currently a single screen cinema, ownership changed in August 2017.<ref>{{cite news|title=Stories of the silver screen|newspaper=Wisbech Standard|date=25 August 2017}}</ref> Becoming part of the Picturedrome cinema group alongside cinemas in 
[[Blackwood, Caerphilly|Blackwood]], [[Bognor Regis]], [[Clacton on Sea]], [[Devizes]], [[Dorchester, Dorset|Dorchester]], [[Wednesbury]] and [[Westgate on Sea]].<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.wisbechcinema.com|website= www.wisbechcinema.com|accessdate= 11 October 2022|title= Luxe}}</ref>

In October 2018 the digitally remastered vintage war footage film ''[[They Shall Not Grow Old]]'' was shown in the run up to the 100th anniversary of [[WW1]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Fen students to sell poppies|newspaper=Wisbech Standard|date=30 October 2018}}</ref>

In March 2019 the cinema launched 'Wisbech: Made in Minecraft', the town brought to virtual life by Bunny Schindler and Adam Clarke.
(https://github.com/collusion-org-uk/minecraft-wisbech)
<ref>{{cite news|title=Wisbech 2019:Made in Minecraft|newspaper=Wisbech Standard|date=22 March 2019}}</ref>

The cinemas also functions as an arts centre. It has hosted groups including a games club, the poetry group Wisbech Stanza and the Friends of [[Wisbech & Fenland Museum]].
