The '''Luxe Cinema''' is a [[movie theater|cinema]] in [[Wisbech]], [[Isle of Ely]], [[Cambridgeshire]], [[England]].

== History ==
The cinema is located in Alexandra Road, this also is the location of the [[Angles Theatre]], and the former Selwyn Theatre. Along with the [[Wisbech & Fenland Museum]], Wisbech Library, [[Wisbech Castle]], St.Peters church, hall & gardens and [[The Crescent]]{{dn|date=January 2020}} this area of the town constitutes the 'Cultural Quarter'.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.visitcambridgeshirefens.org|title=Wisbech|website=www.visitcambridgeshirefens.org|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

The Luxe cinema was converted from a redundant [[Women's Institute]] Hall and opened in March, 2009 styled as a luxury cinema with leather armchairs and two-seater sofas, and ‘premier’ sofa’s with waitress service. It has a licensed bar.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cinematreasures.org/theaters/28926|title=The Luxe|website=www.cinematreasures.org|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
The Women's Institute branch now (2019) meets in the Rosmini Centre, Queens Road.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://thewi.org.uk|title=Wisbech Centre|website=www.thewi.org.uk|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

The town's last operating cinema the 'Unit One' (previously The Hippodrome) had been demolished in the 1980s to make way for the Horsefair shopping centre development that opened in 1988.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cinematreasures.org/theaters/38336|title=Hippodrome Theatre|website=www.cinematreasures.org|accessdate= 31 December 2019}}</ref>

On 23 May, 2014 an eight-screen cinema was opened near the Tesco store, Cromwell Road by the Light Cinemas.<ref{{cite web|url=http://cinematreasures.org/theaters/46143|title=Light Cinema|website=www.cinematreasures.org|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>


The cinema was part of 'The Brinks Festival 2015' hosting 'The Secret World of Charles Darwin' by [[The Magic Circle (organisation)|Magic Circle]] Comedy Award winner Ian Keebler on Monday 18th May.<ref>{{cite newspaper|title=Art showcased at Wisbech Festival|newspaper=Fenland Citizen|date=13 May 2015}}</ref>

In 2015 ''The Goob'' an award winning Independent film shot across the Fens, was shown followed by an audience question and answer session with film director Guy Myhill and Martin Ferguson.<ref>{{cite newspaper|title=Wisbech cinemas screens The Goob|newspaper=Fenland Citizen|date=29 July 2015}}</ref>

In 2016 Leverington Primary Academy pupils created animated films as part of the children's arts award with Trinity House to achieve the Gold Standard and attended the official screening in June 2016.<ref>{{cite newspaper|title=Oscars Ceremony|newspaper=Wisbech Standard|date=23 June 2016}}</ref>

Currently a single screen cinema, ownership changed in August 2017.<ref>{{cite newspaper|title=Stories of the silver screen|newspaper=Wisbech Standard|date=25 August 2017}}</ref>

In October 2018 the digitally remastered vintage war footage film ''They Shall Not Grow Old'' was shown in the run up to the 100th anniversary of WW1.<ref>{{cite newspaper|title=Fen students to sell poppies|newspaper=Wisbech Standard|date=30 October 2018}}</ref>

In March 2019 the cinema launched 'Wisbech: Made in Minecraft', the town brought to virtual life by Bunny Schindler and Adam Clarke.
