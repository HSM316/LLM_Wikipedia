{{Arab cuisine}}
'''Libba'''', '''Laba''' ({{lang-ar|اللباء}}) is a type of [[Yemen]]i cheese. It is mostly eaten in rural areas of Yemen.<ref name="Moheet">{{Cite web|url=https://almoheet.net/%d8%a7%d9%84%d9%84%d8%a8%d8%a7%d8%a1-%d8%a7%d9%84%d9%8a%d9%85%d9%86%d9%8a-%d9%88%d8%b7%d8%b1%d9%8a%d9%82%d8%a9-%d8%aa%d8%ad%d8%b6%d9%8a%d8%b1%d9%87/|title=اللباء اليمني وطريقة تحضيره|date=2017-10-24|website=موسوعة المحيط|language=ar|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> The libba is not sold as a product or in restaurants.

== Etymology ==
The word libba means [[colostrum]] in Arabic.<ref name="Moheet"/>

== Preparation ==

=== Traditional way ===
After a cow or a goat give birth, her milk is taken and boiled in a tannour. Shathab (ruta graveolens), nigella sativa seeds, salt and smoked ghee (Samn makbi) are added to the milk while it is boiling.<ref name="Moheet"/>

===Modern way===
Yemeni people in the cities who don't have goats or cows prepare the libba using different ingredients: 8 eggs, 1 cup of milk, 1 cup of dairy milk, 2 cup of condensed milk, a small amount of citric acid or lemon and salt, half teaspoon of yeast, ghee, shathab (ruta graveolens), nigella sativa seeds.<ref name="Moheet"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.encyclopediacooking.com/arabic/lesson-16840-1.html|title=طريقة اللبا اليمني بالصور|website=www.encyclopediacooking.com|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>
