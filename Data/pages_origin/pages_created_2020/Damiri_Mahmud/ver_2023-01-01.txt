'''Damiri Mahmud''' (17 January 1945 – 30 December 2019) was an Indonesian writer. He also wrote poetry and various articles for newspapers and magazines.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://id.loc.gov/authorities/names/n2017220857.html |title=LC Name Authority File |website=Library of Congress |access-date=April 2, 2020}}</ref> His writings in the form of cultural, political, and religious articles, appeared in various dailies and magazines in Indonesia and Malaysia, among others: Buana News, Pelita, [[Kompas]], [[Republika (Indonesian newspaper)|Republika]], People's Thoughts, Analysis, Alert, Free, People's Mind, [[Media Group|Lampung Post]], Media Indonesia, Community Flag, Sovereign, [[Horison]], Base, Council for Literature and Daily News (Malaysia).

==Career==
His work in the literary world of [[Medan]] began in 1969 after seven short stories were published in the magazine "Stars, Sport, and Film". His short stories, among others, were titled ''Ronggeng'', ''Old Bloody Old Injury Again'', and ''News from the Sea''. His short story ''Mata'' was later published in the Jakarta [[Horison]] magazine in 1970. Besides writing modern literary works, Damiri Mahmud also wrote [[folklore]]. He rewrote existing folklore with a new version.<ref name="Badan1">{{cite web |url=http://badanbahasa.kemdikbud.go.id/lamanbahasa/node/2308 |title=Damiri Mahmud |website=Badan Pengembangan Dan Pembinaan Bahasa (Language Development and Cultivation Agency)|publisher=Kemeterian Peninikan dan Kebudayaan (Ministry of Education and Culture) |language=Indonesian |access-date=April 3, 2020}}</ref> His folktale entitled ''Father′s Testament'' was published by Firma Hasmar, Medan in 1976. He also received an award from the North Sumatra Library in 1978 for his folklore, entitled ''Reply to Budi''. In addition, he has also produced a novel entitled ''Teka-Teki'',<ref>{{cite book |last=Mahmud |first=Damiri |date=1988 |title=Teka–teki |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=WHq-NwAACAAJ |location=Malaysia |publisher=Marwilis Publisher |isbn=9789838310154}}</ref> published by Marwilis Publisher, [[Selangor]], Malaysia in 1988.<ref>[https://medan.tribunnews.com/2019/12/30/sastrawan-asal-sumut-damiri-mahmud-tutup-usia-sakit-paruparu-dan-sempat-masuk-rs-pirngadi Sastrawan Asal Sumut Damiri Mahmud Tutup Usia, Sakit Paruparu dan Sempat Masuk RS Pirngadi] {{in lang|id}}</ref> In the year 2000, 59 of his poems were included in a collection entitled ''Damai di Bumi'' published by the North Sumatra Deparsenibud Regional Office and Hotel Garuda Plaza, Medan.<ref name="Badan1" />
