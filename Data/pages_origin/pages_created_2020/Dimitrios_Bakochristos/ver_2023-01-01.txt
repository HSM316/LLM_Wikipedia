'''Dimitrios Bakochristos''' ({{lang-el|Δημήτρης Μπακοχρήστος}}, born 16 April 1983)<ref name="profile_summer_paralympics_2020">{{Cite web|title=Dimitrios Bakochristos Profile|url=https://olympics.com/tokyo-2020/paralympic-games/en/results/powerlifting/athlete-profile-n1717117-bakochristos-dimitrios.htm|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210828144002/https://olympics.com/tokyo-2020/paralympic-games/en/results/powerlifting/athlete-profile-n1717117-bakochristos-dimitrios.htm|archive-date=28 August 2021|access-date=28 August 2021|website=2020 Summer Paralympics}}</ref> is a Greek [[Paralympic powerlifting|Paralympic powerlifter]] of short stature.<ref name="paralympic_bio">{{Cite web|url=https://www.paralympic.org/dimitrios-bakochristos|title=Dimitrios Bakochristos|website=paralympic.org|url-status=live|access-date=1 January 2020|publisher=[[International Paralympic Committee]]|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200102081857/https://www.paralympic.org/dimitrios-bakochristos |archive-date=2 January 2020 }}</ref> He is a two-time bronze medalist at the [[Summer Paralympics]] and a two-time bronze medalist at the [[World Para Powerlifting Championships]].<ref name="bakochristos_wins_bronze_tokyo_paralympics_2021">{{Cite news|date=27 August 2021|title=Greek weightlifter Dimitrios Bakochristos wins bronze at Tokyo Paralympics|work=The Greek Herald|url=https://greekherald.com.au/news/greece/greek-weightlifter-dimitrios-bakochristos-wins-bronze-at-tokyo-paralympics/|access-date=17 January 2022}}</ref>

== Career ==

He [[Greece at the 2016 Summer Paralympics|represented Greece]] at the [[2016 Summer Paralympics]] held in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil and he won the bronze medal in the [[Powerlifting at the 2016 Summer Paralympics – Men's 54 kg|men's 54 kg]] event.<ref name="paralympic_bio"/> Bakochristos lifted 162 kg which was sufficient for the bronze medal; although [[Bruno Carra]] (representing Brazil) lifted the same amount as Bakochristos, he did not win the medal as his body weight was higher.<ref name="mexico_city_2017_top_lifters">{{Cite news|url=https://www.paralympic.org/news/mexico-city-2017-top-lifters-americas|title=Mexico City 2017: Top lifters from the Americas|date=24 August 2017|work=[[International Paralympic Committee]]|access-date=1 January 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> He also won the bronze medal in the [[Powerlifting at the 2020 Summer Paralympics – Men's 54 kg|men's 54 kg]] event at the [[2020 Summer Paralympics]] held in Tokyo, Japan.<ref name="nigeria_claims_first_gold_tokyo_2020_paralympics_as_guo_breaks_powerlifting_world_record_2021">{{Cite news|last=Houston|first=Michael|date=26 August 2021|title=Nigeria claims first gold at Tokyo 2020 Paralympics as Guo breaks powerlifting world record|work=[[Inside the Games|InsideTheGames.biz]]|url=https://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1112137/tokyo-2020-paralympics-powerlifting|url-status=live|access-date=26 August 2021}}</ref><ref name="world_record_debut_for_guo_lingling_summer_paralympics_2020">{{Cite news|date=26 August 2021|title=World record debut for Guo Lingling on day of first-time champions|work=Paralympic.org|url=https://www.paralympic.org/news/world-record-debut-guo-lingling-day-first-time-champions|access-date=26 August 2021}}</ref>

At the [[2019 World Para Powerlifting Championships]] held in Nur-Sultan, Kazakhstan, he won the bronze medal in the men's 54 kg event.<ref name="nur_sultan_sherif_osman_2019">{{Cite news|url=https://www.paralympic.org/news/nur-sultan-2019-sherif-osman-enhances-his-legend|title=Nur-Sultan 2019: Sherif Osman enhances his legend|date=14 July 2019|work=[[International Paralympic Committee]]|access-date=1 January 2020|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="rahman_to_watch_world_para_2019">{{Cite news|url=https://www.insidethegames.biz/articles/1081983/world-para-powerlifting-champs-starting|title=Rahman among ones to watch at World Para Powerlifting Championships|last=Etchells|first=Daniel|date=12 July 2019|work=InsideTheGames.biz|access-date=1 January 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> He also won the bronze medal in this event at the [[2017 World Para Powerlifting Championships]] held in Mexico City, Mexico.
