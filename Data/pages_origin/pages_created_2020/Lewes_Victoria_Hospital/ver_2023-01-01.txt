'''Lewes Victoria Hospital''' is a health facility at Nevill Road in [[Lewes]], [[East Sussex]], England. It is managed by [[NHS Property Services]] with services operated principally by [[Sussex Community NHS Foundation Trust]], [[Sussex Partnership NHS Foundation Trust]], [[University Hospitals Sussex NHS Foundation Trust]], [[East Sussex Healthcare NHS Trust]], and with parts of community medical cover provided by [[general practitioners]] from Foundry Healthcare Lewes, the local [[primary care]] network.

==History==
The facility has its origins in the Lewes Dispensary which was established in the High Street in 1847.<ref name=na>{{cite web|url=http://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/hospitalrecords/details.asp?id=534|title=Victoria Hospital, Lewes|publisher=National Archives|accessdate=30 December 2019}}</ref> It moved to larger premises at School Hill where it opened as the Lewes Dispensary and Infirmary in 1855.<ref name=na/> It moved again to its current building, which was designed by Ernest Runtz and George Ford in the [[Queen Anne style architecture|Queen Anne style]], and which opened as the Victoria Hospital and Infirmary in 1909.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://historic-hospitals.com/english-hospitals-rchme-survey/sussex-east-sussex/|title=Lewes Victoria Hospital|publisher=Historic Hospitals|accessdate=30 December 2019}}</ref> It joined the [[National Health Service]] as the Victoria Hospital in 1948.<ref name=na/> In July 2018 the trust announced plans for an urgent treatment centre at the hospital.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sussexexpress.co.uk/health/new-urgent-treatment-centre-planned-at-lewes-victoria-hospital-1-8553208|title=New urgent treatment centre planned at Lewes Victoria Hospital|publisher=Sussex Express|date=2 July 2018|accessdate=30 December 2019}}</ref>
