'''Zachary Merton''', born '''Zachary Moses''', (1843-1915) was an Anglo-German industrialist and philanthropist.<ref>{{cite journal |title=Patrick Anthony Merton. 8 October 1920 – 13 June 2000 |date=1 December 2006 |last1=Rothwell |first1=John |last2=Glynn |first2=Ian |journal=[[Biographical Memoirs of Fellows of the Royal Society]] |volume=52 |pages=189–201 |doi=10.1098/rsbm.2006.0014}}</ref> 

==Biography==
Merton's family had founded [[Metallgesellschaft]] in Germany and Henry R. Merton and Co. in Britain, which were among the leading metal trading companies of their respective countries.<ref>{{cite encyclopedia |url =https://www.jewishvirtuallibrary.org/jsource/judaica/ejud_0002_0014_0_13713.html |title=Merton |last=Berman |first=Morton Mayer |encyclopedia=[[Jewish Virtual Library]] |publisher=[[American–Israeli Cooperative Enterprise]] |accessdate=28 January 2016 }}</ref> Merton was a director and one of the largest shareholders of the British company.<ref>{{cite news |title=News in Brief. The Late Mr Z. Merton |newspaper=[[The Times]] |location=London |date=8 December 1915 |page=12 }}</ref>

Merton's wife, Antonie, had come to England from Germany with her previous husband, [[Hermann Schmiechen]], a portrait painter.<ref name=brown106/> She was a follower of [[theosophy]].<ref name=wife>{{cite book |title=The Architect and his Wife: A Life of Edwin Lutyens |last=Ridley |first=Jane |year=2002 |publisher=Chatto & Windus |location=London |isbn=0-7011-7201-0 |pages=266-267}}</ref> Merton and Antonie lived at [[Folly Farm, Sulhamstead]].<ref name=brown106>{{cite book |title=Lutyens and the Edwardians |last=Brown |first=Jane |year=1996 |publisher=Viking |location=London |isbn=0-670-85871-4|page=106}}</ref>

Merton died in 1915 but left £350,000 in his will to establish a series of convalescent homes.<ref name=lost>{{cite web|url=https://ezitis.myzen.co.uk/zacharymertonbanstead.html|title=Zachary Merton|publisher=Lost Hospitals of London|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> These included:

*the Zachary Merton Convalescent Home in [[Banstead]], [[Surrey]]<ref name=lost/>
*the Zachary Merton Convalescent Home in [[Bearsden]], [[East Dunbartonshire]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canmore.org.uk/site/203397/bearsden-switchback-road-canniesburn-hospital|title=Bearsden, Switchback Road, Canniesburn Hospital|publisher=Canmore|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
*the Zachary Merton Convalescent Home in [[Fulwood, Sheffield|Fulwood]], [[South Yorkshire]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.genesreunited.co.uk/searchbna/results?memberlastsubclass=none&searchhistorykey=0&keywords=zachari&from=1938&to=1938|title=General view of the Zachary Merton Convalescent Home|publisher=Sheffield Independent|date=29 August 1938|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
*the Zachary Merton Convalescent Home in [[Grayshott]], [[Hampshire]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk/details/r/C3765728|title=Grayshott, Zachary Merton Convalescent Home and School|publisher=National Archives|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
*the Zachary Merton Convalescent Home in [[Northwood, London|Northwood]], [[Middlesex]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/1973/452/pdfs/uksi_19730452_en.pdf|title=The National Health Service (Designation of London Teaching Hospitals) Amendment Order 1973|publisher=Legislation.gov.uk|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
*the Zachary Merton Convalescent Home in [[Pendlebury]], [[Greater Manchester]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://historic-hospitals.com/english-hospitals-rchme-survey/greater-manchester/|title=Royal Manchester Children’s Hospital|publisher=Historic Hospitals|date=31 December 2019}}</ref>
*the [[Zachary Merton Hospital|Zachary Merton Convalescent Home]] in [[Rustington]], [[West Sussex]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://historic-hospitals.com/english-hospitals-rchme-survey/sussex-west-sussex/|title=Zachary Merton Hospital|publisher=Historic Hospitals|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
*the Zachary Merton Convalescent Home in [[Woodhouse Eaves]], [[Leicestershire]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.woodhouseparishcouncil.org.uk/recovery-homes.html/|title=Woodhouse Eaves Recovery Homes|publisher=Woodhouse Parish Council|date=31 December 2019}}</ref>
