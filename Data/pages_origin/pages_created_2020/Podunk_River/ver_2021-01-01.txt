The '''Podunk River''' is a stream in [[Hartford County, Connecticut]].<ref name=gnis>{{GNIS|209872}}</ref> It is a tributary to the [[Connecticut River]].<ref name=topo>''Hartford North CT,'' 7.5 Minute Topographic Quadrangle, USGS, 1964 (1992 rev.)</ref>

The stream [[headwaters]] arise at {{coord|41|51|09|N|72|31|31|W|display=inline}}<ref name=gnis/> at an elevation of {{convert|305|ft|abbr=on}}.<ref>''Manchester, CT,'' 7.5 Minute Topographic Quadrangle, USGS, 1963 (1992 rev.)</ref> The stream flows to the southwest and enters the Connecticut River just northwest of [[East Hartford, Connecticut|East Hartford]]<ref name=topo/> at {{coord|41|47|08|N|72|38|37|W|display=inline,title}} and an elevation of {{convert|7|ft|abbr=on}}.<ref name=gnis/>

The name is derived from the [[Algonquian peoples|Algonquin]] words ''pod'' for ''low'' and ''unk'' for ''beyond'' or "the low land beyond" or along the Connecticut and its tributary the Podunk River where they farmed the fertile soil along the streams.<ref>[https://www.courant.com/news/connecticut/hc-xpm-2007-01-22-0701220423-story.html Jim Shea, ''Proud to be Podunk'', The Hartford Courant, January 22, 2007 ]</ref>
