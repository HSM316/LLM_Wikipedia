'''William Gerald Knox Boswell''' (24 June 1892 – 28 July 1916) was an [[English people|English]] first-class [[cricket]]er and [[British Army]] officer.

The son of William Albert Boswell and Florence Helen Rotch, he was born at [[Chelsea, London|Chelsea]] in June 1892. He was educated at [[Eton College]], before going up to [[New College, Oxford]].<ref name="BOOK">{{cite book|last=McCrery|first=Nigel|title=Final Wicket: Test and First Class Cricketers Killed in the Great War|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=6mphCgAAQBAJ|date=30 July 2015|publisher=[[Pen and Sword]]|isbn=978-1473864191|page=258}}</ref> While studying at Oxford, Boswell played [[first-class cricket]] for [[Oxford University Cricket Club|Oxford University]], making his debut against the [[Free Foresters Cricket Club|Free Foresters]] at [[University Parks#Cricket ground|Oxford]] in 1912. He played first-class cricket for Oxford until 1914, making fourteen appearances.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/28/28020/First-Class_Matches.html |title=First-Class Matches played by William Boswell |publisher=CricketArchive |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> He scored a total of 756 runs in his fourteen matches, at an [[batting average (cricket)|average]] of 30.24.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/28/28020/f_Batting_by_Team.html |title=First-class Batting and Fielding For Each Team by William Boswell |publisher=CricketArchive |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> His high score of 101 [[not out]], which was his only first-class [[century (cricket)|century]], came against [[Hampshire County Cricket Club|Hampshire]] in 1913.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/9/9057.html |title=Hampshire v Oxford University, 1913 |publisher=CricketArchive |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> With the ball, he took 14 wickets at a [[bowling average]] of 24.50 and best figures of 4 for 22.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/28/28020/f_Bowling_by_Team.html |title=First-class Bowling For Each Team by William Boswell |publisher=CricketArchive |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref>

Boswell served in the [[British Army]] during the [[First World War]], being commissioned as a [[Second lieutenant#United Kingdom & other Commonwealth countries|second lieutenant]] with the [[Rifle Brigade (The Prince Consort's Own)|Rifle Brigade]] in August 1914,<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=28902|date=15 September 1914|page=7304}}</ref> with confirmation in the rank coming in January 1915.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=29053|date=26 January 1915|page=920}}</ref> He was promoted to [[Lieutenant (British Army and Royal Marines)|lieutenant]] in July 1915,<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=29279|date=27 August 1915|page=8631|supp=y}}</ref> while in October 1915 he was made a temporary [[Captain (British Army and Royal Marines)|captain]].<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=29376|date=19 November 1915|page=11578|supp=y}}</ref> Boswell died from wounds received in action at [[Thiepval]] during the [[Battle of the Somme]] on 28 July 1916.<ref name="BOOK"/>
