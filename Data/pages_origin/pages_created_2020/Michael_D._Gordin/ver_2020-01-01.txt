'''Michael Dan Gordin''' (born November 3 1974 in [[New Jersey]]) is an American science historian and [[Slavist]].

Gordin studied at [[Harvard University]] with a bachelor's degree in 1996 and a doctorate in 2001. From 2003 he was at [[Princeton University]], where he is now a professor.<ref name=homepage>{{cite web|title=Michael Gordin|website=Department of Slavic Languages and Literatures, Princeton University|url=https://slavic.princeton.edu/people/michael-gordin}}</ref>

He has done research on the early development of the natural sciences in Russia in the 18th century, biological warfare in the Soviet Union, the relationship of Russian literature to the natural sciences, [[Lysenkoism]], [[Immanuel Velikovsky]] and pseudosciences, the early history of the [[atomic bomb]]s and the [[Cold War]], [[Albert Einstein]] in Prague, history of global scientific languages, and the life of [[Dmitri Mendeleyev]] and the history of the [[periodic table]].<ref name=homepage/>
