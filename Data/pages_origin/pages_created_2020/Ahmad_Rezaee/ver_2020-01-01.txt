'''Tom J. Anderson''' (born '''Ahmad Rezaee Mirghaed''' ({{lang-fa|احمد رضایی میرقائد}}), {{circa}} 1975/1976) was the eldest son of [[Mohsen Rezaee]]. 

He [[Defection|defected]] to the [[Embassy of the United States, Vienna]] in 1998, which led to the [[Central Intelligence Agency]] (CIA) bringing him to [[New York City]] and granting him [[political asylum]].<ref name="Persian Mirrors">{{cite book|last =Sciolino|first=Elaine|title=Persian Mirrors: The Elusive Face of Iran|publisher=Simon & Schuster|year=2001|pages=287–288|isbn= 0-7432-1779-9|url-access=registration|url=https://archive.org/details/persianmirrors00elai/page/79}}</ref> He returned to Iran in 2005, without being prosecuted.<ref name="Tehran Bureau"/> 
== Personal life ==
He married daughter of his father's close friend when he was 19 and studied mathematics at a teacher training college in Tehran, but the couple soon separated.<ref name="Persian Mirrors"/> He reportedly married an American citizen later.<ref name="Tehran Bureau">{{cite web|url=https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/pages/frontline/tehranbureau/2011/11/news-questions-over-death-of-ahmad-rezaei-ahmadinejad-power-play.html|title=Questions over Death of Ahmad Rezaei; Ahmadinejad Power Play|work=Tehran Bureau|author=Muhammad Sahimi|accessdate=21 August 2015|date=14 November 2011|publisher=PBS}}</ref> According to [[Kenneth R. Timmerman]], he had a daughter who was 7 years old and lived in California as of 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2011/dec/2/a-dubious-death-in-dubai/|title=A dubious death in Dubai|author=Kenneth R. Timmerman|accessdate=21 August 2015|date=2 December 2011|publisher=Washington Times}}</ref>
== Death ==
On 12 November 2011, his body was found dead on the floor of room 23 on the 18th floor of Gloria Hotel, located in [[Dubai Media City]]. Officials at [[Dubai Police Force]] declared that he was identified as a U.S. citizen named Tom Anderson, and the cause of death was "an [[Drug overdose|overdose]] of a medicine used to fight depression and schizophrenia".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.naharnet.com/stories/en/20543|title= UAE: Iranian Official's Son Died of Medicine Overdose|publisher=Naharnet|accessdate=21 August 2015|date= 17 November 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thenational.ae/uae/hotel-death-was-us-businessman-say-police-1.433834|title=Hotel death was US businessman, say police|publisher=The National|accessdate=21 August 2015|date= 17 November 2011}}</ref> His death had been described as "suspicious" and alternative reasons such as suicide, murder and assassination were suggested as the cause of death.<ref name="Tehran Bureau"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.huffpost.com/entry/the-mysterious-death-of-a_b_1100074|title=The Mysterious Death of Ahmad Rezaee: Whodunit?|author=Haggai Carmon|accessdate=21 August 2015|date=2 December 2011|publisher=HuffPost}}</ref>
