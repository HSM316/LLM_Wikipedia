'''''The Man Who Disappeared''''' (a.k.a. '''''Sherlock Holmes: The Man Who Disappeared''''') is a 1951 British [[television film|made-for-television]] [[mystery film]] directed by Richard M. Grey and starring [[John Longden]] as [[Sherlock Holmes]] and [[Campbell Singer]] as [[Doctor Watson|Dr. John H. Watson]].<ref name="davies">{{cite book|last=Davies|first=David Stuart|authorlink=David Stuart Davies|title=Starring Sherlock Holmes|publisher=[[Titan Books]]|year=2007|pages=73-74|ISBN=978-1845765378}}</ref> The movie is based on [[Arthur Conan Doyle]]'s [[Sherlock Holmes]] story "[[The Man with the Twisted Lip]]".<ref name="barnes">{{cite book |last=Barnes|first=Alan|authorlink=Alan Barnes (writer) |title=Sherlock Holmes on Screen |year=2011 |publisher=[[Titan Books]] |pages=111 |ISBN=9780857687760}}</ref> It was the first British attempt to create a Sherlock Holmes television series.<ref name="davies"/>

==Production==
The initial plan was to make six, one-hour adaptations<ref name="tele">{{cite book |last=Haining|first=Peter|authorlink=Peter Haining (author) |title=The Television Sherlock Holmes |year=1994 |publisher=Virgin Books |pages=51 |ISBN=0-86369-793-3}}</ref> but only one film was made and it was ultimately released cinematically.<ref name="tele"/> It was filmed both on location in London and on various studio sets.<ref name="barnes"/>

==Reception==
The film was not well regarded upon release with one reviewer saying "This three-reeler is directed and acted in a most shoddy manor and the plot development moves at some points at the most startling speed."<ref>{{cite book|last=Davies|first=David Stuart|authorlink=David Stuart Davies|title=Holmes of the Movies|publisher=Bramhall House|page=105|year=1978|ISBN=0-517-232790}}</ref> ''Kinematograph'' described the direction as "uninspired" causing the film "to border on the burlesque."<ref name="davies"/>
