'''''The Chicago Crusader''''', known from 1940 to the 1950s as ''The Crusader'' and from the 1950s to 1981 as ''The New Crusader'',<ref name="loc-new">{{Cite web
  | url = https://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn82014399/
  | accessdate = 2019-12-30
  | title = About The new crusader. [volume] (Chicago, Ill.) 195?-1981 
  | work = Chronicling America 
  | publisher = Library of Congress}}</ref> is a weekly [[African-American newspaper]] serving [[Chicago]]. It is one of two newspapers in the Crusader Newspaper Group, the other being the ''[[Gary Crusader]]''.  

Founded by Balm L. Leavell and Joseph H. Jefferson in 1940 and published by Balm Leavell until his death in 1968, ''The Chicago Crusader'' has subsequently been operated by his widow [[Dorothy Leavell]].  It is Chicago's longest-running African-American weekly.{{sfn|Carter|2017|p=480}}

== History ==

When first established in 1940, ''The Crusader'' occupied a single page and was operated out of an apartment in the [[Ida B. Wells Homes]] on Chicago's [[South Side, Chicago|South Side]].<ref name="king">{{Cite news
  | url =  https://chicagocrusader.com/the-king-of-kings/
  | accessdate = 2019-12-30
  | title =  The King of Kings: The 50th Anniversary of Dr. Martin Luther King Drive
  | first = Erick 
  | last = Johnson
  | newspaper = Chicago Crusader
  | date = 2018-02-15 }}</ref> In this early period it served as the official organ of the [[Negro Labor Relations League]],{{sfn|Carter|2017|p=480}} an organization established in 1937 to battle against the racial discrimination in employment in Chicago.  The newspaper later moved to its current location on King Drive in Chicago's [[Woodlawn, Chicago|Woodlawn]] neighborhood.<ref name="king"/>  By the late 1960s, its circulation had risen to 31,000.<ref name="jet-obit">{{Cite news
  | magazine = Jet
  | date = 1968-11-14
  | title = Balm Leavell Jr., Chicago Publisher, Dies at 58
  | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=0TgDAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA51
}}</ref>

''The Crusader'''s historical significance includes running a column titled "Muhammad Speaks", by [[Elijah Muhammad]] of the [[Nation of Islam]], in the 1950s prior to the creation of the Nation's own ''[[Muhammad Speaks]]'' newspaper. <ref>{{Cite web
  | title =  The Man of the Century: The Honorable Elijah Muhammad
  | url = http://www.croe.org/thembio.htm
  | accessdate = 2019-12-30
  | work = CROE Online
  | publisher = Coalition for the Remembrance of the Honorable Elijah Muhammad}}</ref> It was one of very few newspapers to run the column, another being the ''[[Pittsburgh Courier]]''. During the same period, ''The Crusader'' also published a column on "Women of Islam" by [[Tynnetta Muhammad]] (under the name Tyneta Denear),<ref>{{Cite book
  | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=T0pl9ck2N_4C&pg=PA113
  | title = Cooper v. Pate: Appendix of Defendants-Appellants
  | page = 113
  | chapter = Testimony of Balm L. Leavell, Jr.
}}</ref>  who later published a column by the same title in ''Muhammad Speaks''.  When the Nation of Islam established ''Muhammad Speaks'', it hired [[Dan Burley]], who had formerly worked for ''The Crusader'' as well as the ''[[Chicago Defender]]'', to edit it.<ref>{{Cite journal
  | journal = Africana Islamic Studies 
  | page = 23
  | title = “Raising Her Voice”: Writings by, for, and about Women in Muhammad Speaks Newspaper, 1961–1975
  | first = Bayyinah S. 
  | last = Jeffries}}</ref>

In 1960, the newspaper extended a formal invitation to the imprisoned Kenyan independence leader [[Jomo Kenyatta]] to speak in Chicago.<ref>{{Cite book
  | page = 146
  | title = Kenyatta and Britain: An Account of Political Transformation, 1929-1963
  | first=W. O. 
  | last = Maloba
  | publisher = Springer
  | year = 2017}}</ref>

Dorothy Leavell joined the newspaper as its office manager in 1961, marrying Balm Leavell soon after.{{sfn|Carter|2017|p=480-481}}  She became the newspaper's business manager in 1964, and upon her husband's death she took charge of the entire operation.{{sfn|Carter|2017|p=481}} Having served in prominent roles in numerous African-American publishing organizations and having been inducted into the National Black Journalists Hall of Fame in 2016, Dorothy Leavell achieved new fame in 2018 when she also took over Chicago's [[alternative weekly]], the ''[[Chicago Reader]]''.<ref>{{Cite news
  | work = WTTW News
  | title = Meet Dorothy Leavell, the Chicago Reader’s New Publisher 
  | first = Nick 
  | last = Blumberg 
  | date = July 5, 2018
  | accessdate = 2019-12-30
  | url = https://news.wttw.com/2018/07/05/meet-dorothy-leavell-chicago-reader-s-new-publisher}}</ref>
