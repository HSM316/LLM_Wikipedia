'''Ronald A. Wilford''' (Nov. 4, 1927 [[Salt Lake City]] - June 13, 2015 [[New York City])<ref name="ONM">{{cite news |title=Obituaries |url=https://www.operanews.com/Opera_News_Magazine/2015/9/Departments/Obituaries.html |accessdate=1 January 2020 |publisher=Opera News Magazine |date=September 2015}}</ref> has been described as “classical music's biggest power broker”. He spent 50 years at [[Columbia Artists Management]], Inc. (CAMI), his clients included conductors [[James Levine]], [[Seiji Ozawa]] and [[Riccardo Muti]].<ref name="Naomi">{{cite web |url= https://www.wqxr.org/story/after-ronald-wilford-classical-musics-super-agent-who-calls-shots |last1=Wise |first1=Brian |last2=Lewin |first2=Naomi |title=After Ronald Wilford, Classical Music's Super-Agent, Who Calls the Shots? |website=WQXR |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref>

==Biography==
Wilford described his childhood in [[Salt Lake City]] as miserable where he lived with his Greek Orthodox father and Mormon mother.  At the time of his death, he was married to [[Sara Wilford]].  Wilford is a graduate of the [[University of Utah]].<ref name=NYT”>{{cite news|last1=Cooper|first1=Michael|title=Ronald Wilford, Manager of Legendary Maestros, Dies at 87|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2015/06/14/arts/music/ronald-wilford-manager-of-legendary-maestros-dies-at-87.html?ref=obituaries|accessdate=January 1, 2020 |work=New York Times|date=June 13, 2015}}</ref>
 
==Career==
Credited with bringing [[Marcel Marceau]] to the United States when Wilford was starting his career, he was hired by Columbia Artists to begin a theatrical division.  He became President in 1970 and stepped down in 2000, taking on the titles of chairman and chief executive until his death.<ref name=NYT” />
