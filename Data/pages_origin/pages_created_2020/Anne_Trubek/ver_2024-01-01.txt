'''Anne Trubek''' (born 1966/1967) is an author and founder and director of [[Belt Magazine]]. She has written ''The History and Uncertain Future of Handwriting,'' and ''A Skeptic’s Guide to Writers’ Houses''.<ref name="Bloomsbury Publishing">{{cite web | title=The History and Uncertain Future of Handwriting | website=Bloomsbury Publishing | url=https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/the-history-and-uncertain-future-of-handwriting-9781620402153/ |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref name="Anne Trubek">{{cite web | title=A Skeptic's Guide to Writers' Houses | website=Anne Trubek |url=https://www.upenn.edu/pennpress/book/14811.html |accessdate=2020-01-11}}</ref>

== Biography ==

=== Career ===
She co-edited ''Rust Belt Chic: The Cleveland Anthology'' and edited ''Voices from the Rust Belt.''<ref name="US Macmillan 2016">{{cite web |title=Voices from the Rust Belt - Anne Trubek | website=US Macmillan | date=2016-06-14 | url=https://us.macmillan.com/voicesfromtherustbelt/annetrubek/9781250162977 |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> ''Rust Belt Chic'' became serialized as Belt Magazine, a web publication and the first publication from Belt Publishing.<ref name="Errico">{{cite web | last=Errico | first=Sally |title=The Woman Who Is Telling the Story of the Rust Belt | website=Lenny Letter |url=https://www.lennyletter.com/story/anne-trubek-rust-belt-publishing |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> Since Trubek founded it as a membership-driven nonprofit in 2013, Belt Publishing has brought out essay collections, guidebooks, and fiction about the culture and landscapes of the Midwest, developing a reputation for combining a regional focus with political and intellectual seriousness and a sense of literary history.<ref name="Trubek12">{{cite web |last=Kirch |first=Claire |title=Broader List Spurs Growth at Belt Publishing | website=Publishers Weekly | date=2018-06-21 | url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/pw/by-topic/industry-news/publisher-news/article/77890-broader-list-spurs-growth-at-belt-publishing.html/ |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> Trubek also publishes articles about the Midwest in other media, as for instance her 2016 essay in [[The Atlantic]] on her "low-overhead life" in the Midwest, an article that was a response to [[Neal Gabler]]'s essay on his own troubled finances.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Trubek |first1=Anne |title=Opting Out of Coastal Madness to Live a Low-Overhead Life |url=https://www.theatlantic.com/business/archive/2016/05/low-overhead-life/480612/ |website=The Atlantic |accessdate=9 January 2020 |date=2 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Gabler |first1=Story by Neal |title=The Secret Shame of Middle-Class Americans |url=https://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/2016/05/my-secret-shame/476415/ |website=The Atlantic |accessdate=9 January 2020}}</ref>

Trubek was a professor of rhetoric and composition at [[Oberlin College]] between 1997 and 2015, and then began freelance writing to earn more money.<ref name="Trubek04">{{cite web | last=Trubek | first=Anne | title=When a Spousal Hire Becomes a Single Mom | website=The Chronicle of Higher Education | date=2004-02-20 | url=https://www.chronicle.com/article/when-a-spousal-hire-becomes-a/44769/ |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref name="Trubek13">{{cite web | last=Trubek | first=Anne | title=Giving Up Tenure? Who Does That? | website=The Chronicle of Higher Education | date=2013-04-08 | url=https://www.chronicle.com/article/Giving-Up-Tenure-Who-Does/138345/ |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> She was a literary columnist for GOOD Magazine.<ref name="NPR.org 2008">{{cite web | title=Moving Beyond 'Catcher' On School Reading Lists | website=NPR.org | date=2008-08-30 | url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=94129299 |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref> Trubek's works are often concerned with the social history of widespread aspects of reading and writing, including the history of handwriting—she was an early advocate of phasing out the teaching of cursive—the emergence of paperback novels, and the trend toward making popular writers’ houses into museums.<ref name="Trubek10">{{cite web | last=Trubek | first=Anne | title=How the Paperback Novel Changed Popular Literature | website=Smithsonian Magazine | date=2010-03-30 | url=https://www.smithsonianmag.com/arts-culture/how-the-paperback-novel-changed-popular-literature-11893941/ |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref name="The Chronicle of Higher Education 2016">{{cite web | title=Bye-Bye, Cursive – Lingua Franca - Blogs | website=The Chronicle of Higher Education |date=2016-10-23 |url=https://www.chronicle.com/blogs/linguafranca/2016/10/23/bye-bye-cursive/ |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref name="Anne Trubek" />

===Personal life===

Trubek is from Madison, Wisconsin.<ref name="Errico" /> She attended [[Oberlin College]]. She has been divorced since 2002 and has one son.<ref name="Trubek10a">{{cite web | last=Trubek | first=Anne | title=Was joint custody a mistake? | website=Salon | date=2010-02-26 | url=https://www.salon.com/2010/02/26/trubek_half_a_child/ |accessdate=2020-01-01}}</ref>
