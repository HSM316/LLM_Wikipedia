'''Anne Trubek''' is an author and founder and director of [[Belt Publishing]]. She has written ''The History and Uncertain Future of Handwriting,'' and ''A Skeptic’s Guide to Writers’ Houses,''.<ref name="Bloomsbury Publishing">{{cite web | title=The History and Uncertain Future of Handwriting | website=Bloomsbury Publishing | url=https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/the-history-and-uncertain-future-of-handwriting-9781620402153/ | access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref name="Anne Trubek">{{cite web | title=A Skeptic's Guide to Writers' Houses | website=Anne Trubek | url=https://www.upenn.edu/pennpress/book/14811.html | access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> She co-edited ''Rust Belt Chic: The Cleveland Anthology'' and edited ''Voices from the Rust Belt.''<ref name="US Macmillan 2016">{{cite web | title=Voices from the Rust Belt - Anne Trubek | website=US Macmillan | date=2016-06-14 | url=https://us.macmillan.com/voicesfromtherustbelt/annetrubek/9781250162977 | access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> ''Rust Belt Chic'' became serialized as Belt Magazine, a web publication and the first publication from Belt Publishing.<ref name="Errico">{{cite web | last=Errico | first=Sally | title=The Woman Who Is Telling the Story of the Rust Belt | website=Lenny Letter | url=https://www.lennyletter.com/story/anne-trubek-rust-belt-publishing | access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>

Trubek was a professor of rhetoric and composition at [[Oberlin College]] and then began freelance writing to earn more money.<ref name="Trubek04">{{cite web | last=Trubek | first=Anne | title=When a Spousal Hire Becomes a Single Mom | website=The Chronicle of Higher Education | date=2004-02-20 | url=https://www.chronicle.com/article/when-a-spousal-hire-becomes-a/44769/ | access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref name="Trubek13">{{cite web | last=Trubek | first=Anne | title=Giving Up Tenure? Who Does That? | website=The Chronicle of Higher Education | date=2013-04-08 | url=https://www.chronicle.com/article/Giving-Up-Tenure-Who-Does/138345/ | access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> She was a literary columnist for GOOD Magazine.<ref name="NPR.org 2008">{{cite web | title=Moving Beyond 'Catcher' On School Reading Lists | website=NPR.org | date=2008-08-30 | url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=94129299 | access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref> Trubek's writing focuses on the various aspects that make up writing including the history of handwriting--she was an early advocate pf phasing out the teaching of cursive--to the history of paperback novels.<ref name="Trubek10">{{cite web | last=Trubek | first=Anne | title=How the Paperback Novel Changed Popular Literature | website=Smithsonian Magazine | date=2010-03-30 | url=https://www.smithsonianmag.com/arts-culture/how-the-paperback-novel-changed-popular-literature-11893941/ | access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref><ref name="The Chronicle of Higher Education 2016">{{cite web | title=Bye-Bye, Cursive – Lingua Franca - Blogs | website=The Chronicle of Higher Education | date=2016-10-23 | url=https://www.chronicle.com/blogs/linguafranca/2016/10/23/bye-bye-cursive/ | access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>

==Personal life==

Trubek is from Madison, Wisconsin.<ref name="Errico" /> She attended Oberlin College. She is divorced and has one son.<ref name="Trubek10a">{{cite web | last=Trubek | first=Anne | title=Was joint custody a mistake? | website=Salon | date=2010-02-26 | url=https://www.salon.com/2010/02/26/trubek_half_a_child/ | access-date=2020-01-01}}</ref>
