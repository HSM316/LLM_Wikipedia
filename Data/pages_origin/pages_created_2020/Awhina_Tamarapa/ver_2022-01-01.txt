'''Awhina Tamarapa''' is a New Zealand Māori museum curator and writer in the field of museum studies.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tepapa.govt.nz/about/te-papa-press/our-authors/awhina-tamarapa-biography-and-interview|title=Awhina Tamarapa: biography and interview|date=12 July 2019|website=Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa, Wellington, NZ|language=en|access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref> She has tribal affiliations to [[Ngāti Kahungunu]], [[Ngāti Ruanui]] and [[Ngāti Pikiao]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.wgtn.ac.nz/stout-centre/about/staff/awhina-tamarapa|title=Awhina Tamarapa {{!}} Stout Research Centre for New Zealand Studies {{!}} Victoria University of Wellington|website=www.wgtn.ac.nz|access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.waateanews.com/waateanews/x_news/MTkxNjE/Breakfast%20with%20Dale/Bicultural-vision-boosts-museum-career|title=Bicultural vision boosts museum career|website=www.waateanews.com|language=en|access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref>

Tamarapa has a Master of Philosophy in Museum Studies from [[Massey University]], a Bachelor of Māori Laws and Philosophy from [[Te Wānanga o Raukawa|Te Wānanga o Raukawa, Otaki]] and a Bachelor of Arts from [[Victoria University of Wellington]], majoring in anthropology.<ref name=":0" />

She has held several positions at the [[Museum of New Zealand Te Papa Tongarewa]]: collection manager, concept developer and curator. Exhibitions she has curated for the museum include Kahu Ora/Living Cloaks (2012) and the [[Ngāti Toa]] Rangatira iwi exhibition, Whiti Te Ra! The Story of Ngāti Toa Rangatira.

Tamarapa has been a guest speaker in the museum heritage studies post-graduate programme at Victoria University of Wellington for a number of years. She has also written and edited publications on Māori artefacts. She wrote ''Whatu Kākahu/Māori Cloaks'' (2011) with [[Rangi Te Kanawa]] and Anne Peranteau. This book was a finalist in the [[New Zealand Post Book Awards]] 2012 and winner of the arts category in the Kupu Ora Māori Book Awards in 2012.<ref name=":0" />
