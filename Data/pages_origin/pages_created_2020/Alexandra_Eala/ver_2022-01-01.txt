'''Alexandra Maniego Eala''' (born May 23, 2005) is a Filipina [[tennis]] player.

Eala is the current [[Australian Open]] [[2020 Australian Open – Girls' Doubles|girls' doubles]] title holder. She is the No. 2 ranked [[International Tennis Federation|ITF]] junior, achieved on October 6, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|date=October 12, 2020|title=Alexandra Eala|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/rankings/world-tennis-tour-junior-rankings/|via=www.itftennis.com}}</ref> Eala has a career high [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]] singles ranking of 505 achieved on Aug 9, 2021.

Her mother Rizza is a [[1985 Southeast Asian Games]] bronze medalist in the [[Aquatics at the 1985 Southeast Asian Games|100-meter backstroke]] and currently serves as the [[chief financial officer]] of [[Globe Telecom]]. She is the niece of former [[Philippine Basketball Association]] commissioner [[Noli Eala]]. Her brother, Michael, plays tennis for the [[Pennsylvania State University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/players/michael-francis-eala/800452344/phi/jt/s/overview|title=Michael Francis Eala Overview}}</ref>

==Junior career==
At the age of 12, Eala won the 2018 [[Les Petit As]] 14-and-under tournament, beating [[Linda Nosková]] in the finals.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lilov & Eala win at Les Petits As |url=https://www.tenniseurope.org/news/117748/Lilov-Eala-win-at-Les-Petits-As/ |website=tenniseurope |accessdate=January 8, 2020}}</ref> She made her junior [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] debut at the [[2019 US Open (tennis)|2019 US Open]].<ref>{{cite news|url = https://sports.inquirer.net/363922/filipina-alex-eala-makes-us-open-juniors-debut-vs-tough-aussie| title = Filipina Alex Eala makes US Open juniors debut vs tough Aussie|date= September 1, 2019| work = [[Philippine Daily Inquirer]]}}</ref> She was named the [[2019 PSA Annual Awards|2019 Milo Junior Athlete of the Year]]. <ref>{{Cite news|date=February 22, 2019|title=Top Juniors Honored|work=Business Mirror|url=https://businessmirror.com.ph/2019/02/22/top-juniors-honored/|access-date=January 16, 2020}}</ref>

Eala won the [[2020 Australian Open – Girls' Doubles|2020 Australian girls' doubles]] event, partnering [[Priska Madelyn Nugroho]]. They defeated [[Živa Falkner]] and Matilda Mutavdzic in the finals.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala wins first juniors Grand Slam title in 2020 Australian Open|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/australian-open-junior-doubles-finals-game-results-alex-eala-priska-nugroho-january-31-2020|access-date=October 12, 2020|website=Rappler|language=en}}</ref>

Eala peaked in the Girl's Junior rankings at No. 2 after reaching the semifinals at the 2020 [[French Open]] [[2020 French Open – Girls' Singles|girls' singles]] competition.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala clinches juniors world No. 2 after French Open romp|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/alex-eala-ranking-itf-world-juniors-2020|access-date=October 12, 2020|website=Rappler|language=en}}</ref>

Eala paid tribute to her roots on Independence Day following another Grand Slam triumph this time on the clay court in Paris. Eala and her Russian partner [[Oksana Selekhmeteva]] captured the French Open girls' doubles title Saturday. They were the top seeds in the tournament, won after knocking off Maria Bondarenko of Russia and [[Amarissa Kiara Tóth]] of Hungary, 6–0, 7–5, in the final.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala wins Grand Slam on Independence Day: ‘I hope I made my contribution to the country’|url=https://sports.inquirer.net/425785/alex-eala-wins-grand-slam-win-on-independence-day-i-hope-i-made-my-contribution-to-the-country|access-date=June 13, 2021|website=Inquirer.net|language=en}}</ref>

==Professional career==
Eala made her debut on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]] on March 4, 2020 as a junior reserved in the $15K event at [[Monastir, Tunisia|Monastir]], where she won her first professional match.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.philstar.com/sports/2020/02/27/1996528/alex-eala-make-pro-debut-tunisian-tourney|title=Alex Eala to make pro debut in Tunisian tourney |website=Philstar|access-date=March 5, 2020}}</ref> She leaped to the top 1000 in the WTA that was established by tennis legend Billie Jean King for professional women players comes after the teen prodigy secured her first pro tennis championship title at the first leg of the ITF W15 Manacor in Spain held in January 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.msn.com/en-ph/news/national/alex-eala-enters-top-1000-in-women-e2-80-99s-tennis-association-world-ranking/ar-BB1dj6W6|title=Alex Eala enters top 1000 in Women’s Tennis Association World Ranking |website=msm news|access-date=February 4, 2021}}</ref>

Eala made to her first final for ITF doubles with [[Oksana Selekhmeteva]] and ended their run in the W25 Platja D’Aro in Spain Saturday night after falling short to Lithuania’s [[Justina Mikulskyte]] and Romanian [[Oana Georgeta Simion]], 6-3, 7-5, in the final.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sports.inquirer.net/423856/alex-eala-partner-finish-second-place-at-w25-spain|title=Alex Eala, partner finish second place at W25 Spain |website=sports.inquirer.net|access-date=May 28, 2021}}</ref>
