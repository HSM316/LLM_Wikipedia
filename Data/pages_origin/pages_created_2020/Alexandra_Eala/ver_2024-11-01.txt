'''Alexandra Maniego Eala''' (born May 23, 2005) is a Filipina professional [[tennis]] player.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.wtatennis.com/players/330332/alexandra-eala | title=Alexandra Eala &#124; Player Stats & More – WTA Official }}</ref>
She has a career-high singles ranking of world No. 143, by the [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]], achieved on 22 July 2024. She is the highest-ranked Filipino female singles player in [[WTA Tour]] history, surpassing [[Maricris Gentz]], who peaked at No. 284 on 18 October 1999.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Mina |first1=Rosy |title=Alex Eala reigns at US Open for first major junior girls' singles title |url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/sports/09/11/22/history-made-alex-eala-wins-us-open-juniors-title |access-date=September 11, 2022 |work=ABS-CBN News |date=September 11, 2022}}</ref> 

Eala was the No. 2 ranked [[ITF Junior Circuit|ITF junior]] on 6 October 2020.<ref>{{cite web|date=October 12, 2020|title=Alexandra Eala|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/rankings/world-tennis-tour-junior-rankings/|via=www.itftennis.com}}</ref> Eala won her first junior singles title at the [[2022 US Open – Girls' singles|2022 US Open]], making her the first Filipino player to win a junior [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] singles title.

==Personal life==
Her mother Rosemarie "Rizza" Maniego-Eala is a [[1985 Southeast Asian Games]] bronze medalist in the [[Aquatics at the 1985 Southeast Asian Games|100-meter backstroke]] and currently serves as the [[chief financial officer]] of [[Globe Telecom]]. She is the niece of [[Philippine Sports Commission]] chairperson and former [[Philippine Basketball Association]] commissioner [[Noli Eala]]. Her brother, Michael (Miko), plays tennis for the [[Pennsylvania State University]] [[Penn State Nittany Lions|Nittany Lions]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/players/michael-francis-eala/800452344/phi/jt/s/overview|title=Michael Francis Eala Overview}}</ref>
She has been a student of the [[Rafa Nadal Academy]] in [[Manacor]] (Mallorca, Spain), since she was 12 years old.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sportskeeda.com/amp/tennis/news-rafa-nadal-academy-player-alex-eala-philippines-clinches-second-pro-title-thailand|title=Alex Eala|date=April 11, 2022 }}</ref>

====2018====
At the age of 12, Eala won the 2018 [[Les Petit As]] 14-and-under tournament, beating [[Linda Nosková]] in the finals.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lilov & Eala win at Les Petits As |url=https://www.tenniseurope.org/news/117748/Lilov-Eala-win-at-Les-Petits-As/ |website=tenniseurope |accessdate=January 8, 2020}}</ref> She made her junior [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] debut at the [[2019 US Open (tennis)|2019 US Open]].<ref>{{cite news|url = https://sports.inquirer.net/363922/filipina-alex-eala-makes-us-open-juniors-debut-vs-tough-aussie| title = Filipina Alex Eala makes US Open juniors debut vs tough Aussie|date= September 1, 2019| newspaper = [[Philippine Daily Inquirer]]}}</ref> She was named the [[2019 PSA Annual Awards|2019 Milo Junior Athlete of the Year]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=February 22, 2019|title=Top Juniors Honored|work=Business Mirror|url=https://businessmirror.com.ph/2019/02/22/top-juniors-honored/|access-date=January 16, 2020}}</ref>

====2020: First junior doubles title====
Eala won the [[2020 Australian Open – Girls' doubles|2020 Australian girls' doubles]] event, partnering [[Priska Madelyn Nugroho]]. They defeated [[Živa Falkner]] and Matilda Mutavdzic in the final.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala wins first juniors Grand Slam title in 2020 Australian Open|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/australian-open-junior-doubles-finals-game-results-alex-eala-priska-nugroho-january-31-2020|access-date=October 12, 2020|website=Rappler|date=January 31, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

Eala peaked in the junior rankings at No. 2, after reaching the semifinals at the 2020 [[2020 French Open – Girls' singles|French Open girls' singles]] competition.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala clinches juniors world No. 2 after French Open romp|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/alex-eala-ranking-itf-world-juniors-2020|access-date=October 12, 2020|website=Rappler|date=October 12, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

====2021: Second junior doubles title====
Eala paid tribute to her roots on Independence Day following another major triumph, this time on the clay court in Paris. Eala and her Russian partner [[Oksana Selekhmeteva]] captured the French Open girls' doubles title Saturday. They were the top seeds in the tournament, won after knocking out [[Maria Bondarenko]] of Russia and [[Amarissa Kiara Tóth]] of Hungary, 6–0, 7–5, in the final.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala wins Grand Slam on Independence Day: 'I hope I made my contribution to the country'|url=https://sports.inquirer.net/425785/alex-eala-wins-grand-slam-win-on-independence-day-i-hope-i-made-my-contribution-to-the-country|access-date=June 13, 2021|website=Inquirer.net|date=June 12, 2021 |language=en}}</ref>

====2022: First junior Grand Slam singles title====
On 11 September, Eala became the first Filipino to win a [[Junior tennis|junior]] Grand Slam singles championship and the only Filipino with multiple junior Grand Slam titles. She defeated the No. 2 seed, [[Lucie Havlíčková]] of the Czech Republic, in the [[2022 US Open – Girls' singles|girls' singles final]] of the [[2022 US Open (tennis)|US Open]] at the [[USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center]] in New York.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala reigns at US Open for first major junior girls' singles title|url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/sports/09/11/22/history-made-alex-eala-wins-us-open-juniors-title|access-date=September 14, 2022|website=abs-cbn.com|language=en}}</ref>

===Professional===
====2020-2021: First ITF title, top 1000 & WTA debuts====
On 4 March 2020, Eala made her debut on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]], as she played in the $15k event at Monastir, Tunisia where she won her first professional match.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.philstar.com/sports/2020/02/27/1996528/alex-eala-make-pro-debut-tunisian-tourney|title=Alex Eala to make pro debut in Tunisian tourney |website=Philstar|access-date=March 5, 2020}}</ref>

In January 2021, she leaped to the top 1000 in the [[WTA rankings]], after winning the title at the first leg of the $15k Manacor event in Spain.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.msn.com/en-ph/news/national/alex-eala-enters-top-1000-in-women-e2-80-99s-tennis-association-world-ranking/ar-BB1dj6W6|title=Alex Eala enters top 1000 in Women's Tennis Association World Ranking |website=msm news|access-date=February 4, 2021}}</ref>

She received a wildcard into the qualifying draw of the [[2021 Miami Open – Women's singles|Miami Open]] where she lost to [[Viktória Kužmová]] in a three-set battle in the first round.

Eala made her first ITF doubles final at the $25k [[Castell-Platja d'Aro|Platja d'Aro]] in Spain, playing with [[Oksana Selekhmeteva]]. They lost to Lithuania's [[Justina Mikulskytė]] and Romanian [[Oana Georgeta Simion]], 3–6, 5–7.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sports.inquirer.net/423856/alex-eala-partner-finish-second-place-at-w25-spain|title=Alex Eala, partner finish second place at W25 Spain |website=sports.inquirer.net|date=May 22, 2021 |access-date=May 28, 2021}}</ref>

In August 2021, she made her [[WTA Tour]] main-draw debut at the [[2021 Winners Open]] in Cluj-Napoca, Romania after receiving a wildcard. In her first match, she defeated [[Paula Ormaechea]] in straight sets. In her next match she lost to [[Mayar Sherif]], also in straight sets.

====2022-2024:  WTA 1000, major and top 200 debuts and first win====
She received a wildcard making her WTA 1000 debut in [[2022 Miami Open – Women's singles|2022 Miami Open]] before losing to [[Madison Brengle]] in the first round.

In 2023, Eala made her debut in the qualifying draw of the [[2023 Australian Open|Australian Open]] but she lost her first qualifying match to [[Misaki Doi]] in a tight three-setter. Her next appearance was at the [[2023 Thailand Open – Singles|Hua Hin Championship]] where she beat [[Han Xinyun]] and [[Kristina Dmitruk]] in straight sets to reach the main draw. In her first-round match, she fell to [[Tatjana Maria]].
She received wildcards for the main draw at the [[2023 Miami Open]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/3110338/erika-andreeva-brenda-fruhvirtova-awarded-miami-open-wild-cards |title= Erika Andreeva, Brenda Fruhvirtova awarded Miami Open wild cards|date=14 March 2023 }}</ref> and at the [[2023 Mutua Madrid Open|2023 Madrid Open]].
She entered the top 200 on 28 August 2023 and reached a new career-high ranking of world No. 191 on 18 September 2023.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://sports.inquirer.net/529837/alex-eala-soars-to-new-high-in-wta-rankings-ahead-of-asian-games | title=Alex Eala soars to new high in WTA rankings ahead of Asian Games | date=September 20, 2023 }}</ref>
At the [[2022 Asian Games|Asian Games]], she won two bronze medals, one in the women's singles and the second in the mixed doubles with [[Francis Alcantara]].<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ramos |first1=Gerry |title=Eala, Alcantara win bronze on another slow day for Team Philippines |url=https://www.spin.ph/multisport/asian-games/alex-eala-alcantara-tennis-bronze-adds-to-ph-medal-tally-a793-20230929 |access-date=30 September 2023 |work=Sports Interactive Network Philippines |date=29 September 2023 |language=en}}</ref>

She received wildcards for the qualifying draw at the [[2024 Miami Open]], and for the main draw at the [[2024 Mutua Madrid Open|Madrid Open]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wtatennis.com/news/3972096/madrid-2024-dates-draws-prize-money-and-everything-you-need-to-know|title=Madrid 2024: Dates, draws, prize money and everything you need to know|date=18 April 2024}}</ref> During the 2024 Miami Open, she stunned former world No. 5, Sara Errani, during the first round of qualifying winning in straight sets.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/tennis-results-alex-eala-sara-errani-miami-open-march-17-2024/ |title=Alex Eala stuns former world No. 5 in Miami Open qualifiers |date=March 18, 2024 }}</ref> However, in the second round of qualifying, she suffered cramps and lost to [[Emiliana Arango]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.philstar.com/sports/2024/03/19/2341714/eala-succumbs-cramps-falls-miami-open-qualifying-draw |title=Eala succumbs to cramps, falls in Miami Open qualifying draw |website=[[The Philippine STAR]] }}</ref> At the [[2024 Mutua Madrid Open]], she recorded her first WTA 1000 win over [[Lesia Tsurenko]]. She then lost in a tight three-set match against 27th seed [[Sorana Cirstea]].

Eala entered the qualifying rounds of the 2024 French Open where she managed to beat Ma Yexin of China and Taylah Preston of Australia to reach the qualifying competition. During the final round to qualify in the main draw, coincidentally on her 19th birthday, she lost in another tight three-set match to Julia Riera of Argentina. Nonetheless, her run during the 2024 French Open resulted to a new career high ranking in singles, on 27 May 2024, she reached No. 157 in the world.

For the 2024 Wimbledon Championships, she entered the qualifying rounds and reached the final qualifying match where she lost to eventual quarterfinalist Lulu Sun. Her run marked her second final qualifying match for the year after her French Open performance a month earlier.

After the Wimbledon, Eala entered the Open Araba en Femenino, a W100 tournament, where she triumphed in both singles and doubles. She won the title in singles without dropping a set. Indoubles, she and her partner Estelle Cascino, won their second doubles crown. She reached a new career-high ranking in both singles and doubles, at 143 and 208 respectively.

Eala entered the 2024 US Open qualifying rounds where she again reached the final qualifying round, losing to 20th seed Elena-Gabriela Ruse. After her US Open run, she got into the main draw of the 2024 Guadalajara Open Akron, a WTA500 event. To enter the main draw, she had to qualify by winning 2 qualifying matches.
