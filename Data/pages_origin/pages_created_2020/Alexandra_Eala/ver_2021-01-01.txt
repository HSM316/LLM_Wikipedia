Eala is the current [[Australian Open]] [[2020 Australian Open – Girls' Doubles|girls' doubles]] title holder. She is the No. 2 ranked [[International Tennis Federation|ITF]] junior, achieved on 6 October 2020.<ref>{{cite web|date=12 October 2020|title=Alexandra Eala|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/rankings/world-tennis-tour-junior-rankings/|via=www.itftennis.com}}</ref> Eala has a career high [[Women's Tennis Association|WTA]] singles ranking of 1180 achieved on 30 November 2020.

Her mother Rizza is a [[1985 Southeast Asian Games]] bronze medalist in the [[Aquatics at the 1985 Southeast Asian Games|100-meter backstroke]] and the current [[Chief financial officer]] of [[Globe Telecom]]. She is the niece of former [[Philippine Basketball Association|PBA]] commissioner [[Noli Eala]]. Her brother, Michael, plays tennis for [[Pennsylvania State University|Penn State University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/players/michael-francis-eala/800452344/phi/jt/s/overview|title=Michael Francis Eala Overview}}</ref>

==Junior career==
At the age of 12, Eala won the 2018 [[Les Petit As]] 14-and-under tournament, beating Linda Nosková in the finals.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lilov & Eala win at Les Petits As |url=https://www.tenniseurope.org/news/117748/Lilov-Eala-win-at-Les-Petits-As/ |website=tenniseurope |accessdate=8 January 2020}}</ref> She made her junior [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] debut at the [[2019 US Open (tennis)|2019 US Open]].<ref>{{cite news|url = https://sports.inquirer.net/363922/filipina-alex-eala-makes-us-open-juniors-debut-vs-tough-aussie| title = Filipina Alex Eala makes US Open juniors debut vs tough Aussie|date= September 1, 2019| work = [[Philippine Daily Inquirer]]}}</ref> She was named the [[2019 PSA Annual Awards|2019 Milo Junior Athlete of the Year]]. <ref>{{Cite news|date=Feb 22, 2019|title=Top Juniors Honored|work=Business Mirror|url=https://businessmirror.com.ph/2019/02/22/top-juniors-honored/|access-date=January 16, 2020}}</ref>

Eala won the [[2020 Australian Open – Girls' Doubles|2020 Australian girls' doubles]] event, partnering [[Priska Madelyn Nugroho]]. They defeated Živa Falkner and Matilda Mutavdzic in the finals.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala wins first juniors Grand Slam title in 2020 Australian Open|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/australian-open-junior-doubles-finals-game-results-alex-eala-priska-nugroho-january-31-2020|access-date=2020-10-12|website=Rappler|language=en}}</ref>

Eala peaked in the Girl's Junior rankings at No. 2 after reaching the semifinals at the 2020 [[French Open]] [[2020 French Open – Girls' Singles|girls' singles]] competition.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala clinches juniors world No. 2 after French Open romp|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/alex-eala-ranking-itf-world-juniors-2020|access-date=2020-10-12|website=Rappler|language=en}}</ref>

==Professional career==
Eala made her debut on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]] on 4 March 2020 as a junior reserved in the $15K event at [[Monastir, Tunisia|Monastir]], where she won her first professional match.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.philstar.com/sports/2020/02/27/1996528/alex-eala-make-pro-debut-tunisian-tourney|title=Alex Eala to make pro debut in Tunisian tourney |website=Philstar|access-date=5 Mar 2020}}</ref>
