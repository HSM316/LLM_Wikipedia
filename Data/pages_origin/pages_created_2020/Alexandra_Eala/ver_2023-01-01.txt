'''Alexandra Maniego Eala''' (born 23 May 2005) is a Filipino [[tennis]] player.

Eala was the No. 2-ranked [[International Tennis Federation|ITF]] [[Junior tennis|junior]] on 6 October 2020.<ref>{{cite web|date=October 12, 2020|title=Alexandra Eala|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/rankings/world-tennis-tour-junior-rankings/|via=www.itftennis.com}}</ref> She has a career-high singles ranking by the [[Women's Tennis Association]] (WTA) of 214, achieved on 31 October 2022. Eala is the highest-ranked Filipino female singles player in [[WTA Tour]] history, surpassing [[Maricris Gentz]], who peaked at No. 284 on 18 October 1999.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Mina |first1=Rosy |title=Alex Eala reigns at US Open for first major junior girls' singles title |url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/sports/09/11/22/history-made-alex-eala-wins-us-open-juniors-title |access-date=September 11, 2022 |work=ABS-CBN News |date=September 11, 2022}}</ref> Eala won her first junior singles title at the [[2022 US Open – Girls' singles|2022 US Open]], making her the first Filipino player to win a junior [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] singles title.

==Personal life==
Her mother Rosemarie "Rizza" Maniego-Eala is a [[1985 Southeast Asian Games]] bronze medalist in the [[Aquatics at the 1985 Southeast Asian Games|100-meter backstroke]] and currently serves as the [[chief financial officer]] of [[Globe Telecom]]. She is the niece of [[Philippine Sports Commission]] chairperson and former [[Philippine Basketball Association]] commissioner [[Noli Eala]]. Her brother, Michael (Miko), plays tennis for the [[Pennsylvania State University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.itftennis.com/en/players/michael-francis-eala/800452344/phi/jt/s/overview|title=Michael Francis Eala Overview}}</ref>
She has been a student of the [[Rafael Nadal #Off the court|Rafa Nadal Academy]] by [[Movistar]] in [[Manacor]] (Mallorca, Spain), since she was 12 years old.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sportskeeda.com/amp/tennis/news-rafa-nadal-academy-player-alex-eala-philippines-clinches-second-pro-title-thailand|title=Alex Eala|date=April 11, 2022 }}</ref>

===2018===
At the age of 12, Eala won the 2018 [[Les Petit As]] 14-and-under tournament, beating [[Linda Nosková]] in the finals.<ref>{{cite web |title=Lilov & Eala win at Les Petits As |url=https://www.tenniseurope.org/news/117748/Lilov-Eala-win-at-Les-Petits-As/ |website=tenniseurope |accessdate=January 8, 2020}}</ref> She made her junior [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] debut at the [[2019 US Open (tennis)|2019 US Open]].<ref>{{cite news|url = https://sports.inquirer.net/363922/filipina-alex-eala-makes-us-open-juniors-debut-vs-tough-aussie| title = Filipina Alex Eala makes US Open juniors debut vs tough Aussie|date= September 1, 2019| newspaper = [[Philippine Daily Inquirer]]}}</ref> She was named the [[2019 PSA Annual Awards|2019 Milo Junior Athlete of the Year]]. <ref>{{Cite news|date=February 22, 2019|title=Top Juniors Honored|work=Business Mirror|url=https://businessmirror.com.ph/2019/02/22/top-juniors-honored/|access-date=January 16, 2020}}</ref>

===2020: First junior doubles title===
Eala won the [[2020 Australian Open – Girls' doubles|2020 Australian girls' doubles]] event, partnering [[Priska Madelyn Nugroho]]. They defeated [[Živa Falkner]] and Matilda Mutavdzic in the final.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala wins first juniors Grand Slam title in 2020 Australian Open|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/australian-open-junior-doubles-finals-game-results-alex-eala-priska-nugroho-january-31-2020|access-date=October 12, 2020|website=Rappler|language=en}}</ref>

Eala peaked in the junior rankings at No. 2, after reaching the semifinals at the 2020 [[2020 French Open – Girls' singles|French Open girls' singles]] competition.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala clinches juniors world No. 2 after French Open romp|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/alex-eala-ranking-itf-world-juniors-2020|access-date=October 12, 2020|website=Rappler|date=October 12, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

===2021: Second junior doubles title===
Eala paid tribute to her roots on Independence Day following another major triumph, this time on the clay court in Paris. Eala and her Russian partner [[Oksana Selekhmeteva]] captured the French Open girls' doubles title Saturday. They were the top seeds in the tournament, won after knocking out [[Maria Bondarenko]] of Russia and [[Amarissa Kiara Tóth]] of Hungary, 6–0, 7–5, in the final.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala wins Grand Slam on Independence Day: 'I hope I made my contribution to the country'|url=https://sports.inquirer.net/425785/alex-eala-wins-grand-slam-win-on-independence-day-i-hope-i-made-my-contribution-to-the-country|access-date=June 13, 2021|website=Inquirer.net|date=June 12, 2021 |language=en}}</ref>

===2022: First junior Grand Slam singles title===
On September 11, 2022, Eala became the first Filipino to win a [[Junior tennis|junior]] [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] singles championship and the only Filipino with multiple junior Grand Slam titles. She defeated the No. 2 seed, [[Lucie Havlickova]] of the [[Czech Republic]], 6–2, 6–4, at the [[2022 US Open – Girls' singles|girls' singles]] final of the [[2022 US Open (tennis)|2022 US Open]] at the [[USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center]] in [[New York City]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alex Eala reigns at US Open for first major junior girls' singles title|url=https://news.abs-cbn.com/sports/09/11/22/history-made-alex-eala-wins-us-open-juniors-title|access-date=September 14, 2022|website=abs-cbn.com|language=en}}</ref>

==Professional career==
===2020: Debut===
Eala made her debut on the [[ITF Women's Circuit]] on 4 March 2020, as she played in the $15k event at [[Monastir, Tunisia|Monastir]], where she won her first professional match.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.philstar.com/sports/2020/02/27/1996528/alex-eala-make-pro-debut-tunisian-tourney|title=Alex Eala to make pro debut in Tunisian tourney |website=Philstar|access-date=March 5, 2020}}</ref> 

===2021: First ITF senior title & top 1000===
She leaped to the top 1000 in the [[WTA rankings]], after winning the title at the first leg of the $15k Manacor event in Spain in January 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.msn.com/en-ph/news/national/alex-eala-enters-top-1000-in-women-e2-80-99s-tennis-association-world-ranking/ar-BB1dj6W6|title=Alex Eala enters top 1000 in Women's Tennis Association World Ranking |website=msm news|access-date=February 4, 2021}}</ref>

Eala made her first ITF doubles final at the $25k [[Castell-Platja d'Aro|Platja d’Aro]] in Spain, playing with [[Oksana Selekhmeteva]]. They lost to Lithuania’s [[Justina Mikulskyte]] and Romanian [[Oana Georgeta Simion]], 3-6, 5-7.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://sports.inquirer.net/423856/alex-eala-partner-finish-second-place-at-w25-spain|title=Alex Eala, partner finish second place at W25 Spain |website=sports.inquirer.net|date=May 22, 2021 |access-date=May 28, 2021}}</ref>
