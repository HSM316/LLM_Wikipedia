'''Noni MacDonald''' {{Post-nominals|country=CAN|FRCP|ONS}} is a Canadian physician. She is a Professor in the Department of Pediatrics and former Dean of the Faculty of Medicine at [[Dalhousie University]]. In 2019 she was invested into the [[Order of Nova Scotia]] and [[Order of Canada]].

== Early life and education ==
MacDonald earned her bachelor's degree at [[Queen's University]] in 1970.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.longwoods.com/content/16679|title=Dr. Noni MacDonald, Dean of Medicine, Dalhousie University|last=Martin|first=Cynthia|date=2002-03-15|website=Healthcare Quarterly|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> She moved to the [[University of Ottawa]] for her graduate studies, where she specialised in microbiology.<ref name=":0" /> She remained here for her medical degree, which she completed [[Magna cum Laude]] in 1975.<ref name=":0" /> MacDonald was awarded the university medal for highest academic attainment.<ref name=":0" /> She remained in [[Ottawa]] for her residency training and became board certified in 1978. MacDonald completed her speciality training in paediatric infectious diseases at [[McGill University]] and the [[University of Rochester]].<ref name=":0" /> In 1983 MacDonald was the first paediatrician in [[Canada]] to complete the [[Royal College of Physicians and Surgeons of Canada]] certification in [[paediatric]] infectious diseases.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.dal.ca/news/2019/11/04/dr--noni-macdonald-to-be-invested-into-order-of-nova-scotia.html|title=Dr. Noni MacDonald to be invested into Order of Nova Scotia|website=Dalhousie News|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 1981 MacDonald joined the faculty at the [[University of Ottawa]], where she founded the Division of Infectious Diseases.<ref name=":1" /> She led the Paediatric Infectious Diseases Service at the [[Children's Hospital of Eastern Ontario]].<ref name=":0" /> In 1996 MacDonald founded the journal [[Paediatrics & Child Health]], which was the first Canadian journal on paediatric medicine.<ref name=":1" /> Her research considered the microbiology of [[cystic fibrosis]], [[Sexually transmitted infection|sexually transmitted infections]] in adolescents and the development of vaccinations for infectious diseases.<ref name=":0" /> 

In 1999 MacDonald left [[Ottawa]] and moved to [[Halifax, Nova Scotia]].<ref name=":1" /> At [[Dalhousie University]] she became the first woman in [[Canada]] to be elected Dean of a Faculty of Medicine, and held this position until 2004.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://signalhfx.ca/doctor-who-broke-glass-ceiling-for-women-in-medicine-inducted-to-order-of-nova-scotia/|title=Doctor who broke glass ceiling for women in medicine inducted to Order of Nova Scotia|last=Miller|first=Simon|website=The Signal|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> That year she was a founding member of the [[World Health Organization|World Health Organisation]] Global Advisory Committee on Vaccine Safety, and has held various positions on technical committees and in training development since then. She remains on their Strategic Advisory Committee on Immunisation, which considers the demand for vaccines, as well as serving as a consultant for vaccine safety.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.who.int/immunization/sage/members/bio_macdonald/en/|title=WHO {{!}} Professor Noni E MacDONALD|website=WHO|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> As part of her work with the [[WHO]] MacDonald is developing the 2021 – 2030 Global Vaccine Action Plan.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=http://www.csih.org/en/about/membership/lifetime-achievement-award|title=Lifetime Achievement Award {{!}} CSIH|website=www.csih.org|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

In 2004 with support from the [[Canada Foundation for Innovation]] she founded the Canadian Center for Vaccinology (CCfV).<ref name=":2" /> The CCfV was established to implement and evaluate vaccine technologies as well as training experts in infectious diseases and global health.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://centerforvaccinology.ca/about-ccfv/|title=About CCfV - Participate in a Study|website=Canadian Center For Vaccinology|language=en-CA|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> She has spoken about the dangers of [[vaccine hesitancy]] and how the internet permits the dangerous messaging of [[Anti-vax|anti-vaxxers]].<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite journal|last=MacDonald|first=Noni E.|last2=Butler|first2=Robb|last3=Dubé|first3=Eve|date=2018-01-02|title=Addressing barriers to vaccine acceptance: an overview|url=https://doi.org/10.1080/21645515.2017.1394533|journal=Human Vaccines & Immunotherapeutics|volume=14|issue=1|pages=218–224|doi=10.1080/21645515.2017.1394533|issn=2164-5515|pmc=5791591|pmid=29048975}}</ref> At [[Dalhousie University]] MacDonald teaches a course ''Addressing Evidence Denial in Public''.<ref name=":2" />

MacDonald serves on the advisory board of the [[National Collaborating Centre for Infectious Diseases]] and on the Board of Directors of ''Academics Without Borders''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://nccid.ca/partner/noni-macdonald/|title=Noni MacDonald|website=National Collaborating Centre for Infectious Diseases|language=en-CA|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.awb-usf.org/awb-team/noni-macdonald/|title=Noni MacDonald – Academics Without Borders / Universitaires Sans Frontieres|language=en|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> MacDonald is interested in building the healthcare capacity of the developing world.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://medicine.dal.ca/departments/department-sites/pediatrics/our-people/our-faculty/noni-macdonald.html|title=Noni MacDonald|website=Dalhousie University|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> She co-founded [[East Africa]]'s ''MicroResearch'', a program that looks to support local health providers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.microresearch.ca/leadership|title=Leadership|website=MicroResearch|language=en-CA|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> MicroResearch is modelled on principles from [[microfinance]], developing solutions that are appropriate for the context and culture of African communities.<ref name=":3" /> From 2008 to 2019 MicroResearch had led more than forty two-week workshops across seven African countries, training in excess of 1000 healthcare professionals and community members.<ref name=":3" /> In particular MicroResearch looked to train women, improving their opportunities for leadership and tackling the gender gap in African research.<ref name=":3" /> The success of the programme resulted in a similar version being launched in [[Canada]] in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.microresearch-international.ca/|title=Micro Research International - Nova Scotia|website=www.microresearch-international.ca|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

She was invested into the [[Order of Nova Scotia]] in 2019.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://novascotia.ca/iga/2019recipients.asp|title={{!}} novascotia.ca|website=novascotia.ca|access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> That year she was also awarded the Lifetime Achievement Award from the Canadian Society for International Health.<ref name=":3" />

== Personal life ==
MacDonald is married with children.<ref name=":0" /> Alongside her research she enjoys skiing and pottery.<ref name=":0" />
