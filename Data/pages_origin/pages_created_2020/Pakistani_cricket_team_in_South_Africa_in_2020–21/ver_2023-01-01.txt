The [[Pakistan national cricket team|Pakistan cricket team]] toured South Africa in April 2021 to play three [[One Day International]] (ODI) and four [[Twenty20 International]] (T20I) matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/_/id/30200626/sri-lanka-set-play-two-test-series-south-africa-december-january-2020-21 |title=Sri Lanka set to play two-Test series in South Africa |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=27 October 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pcb.com.pk/press-release-detail/pakistan-confirms-south-africa-tour-in-april-2021-.html |title=Pakistan confirms South Africa tour in April 2021 |work=Pakistan Cricket Board |access-date=27 October 2020}}</ref> The ODI series formed part of the inaugural [[2020–2023 ICC Cricket World Cup Super League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/news/742337 |title=Schedule for inaugural World Test Championship announced |work=International Cricket Council |access-date=11 January 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://icc-static-files.s3.amazonaws.com/ICC/document/2018/06/20/6dc2c8d4-e1a5-4dec-94b4-7121fab3cd7f/ICC_Tours.pdf |title=Men's Future Tours Programme |access-date=11 October 2019 |work=International Cricket Council}}</ref>

Originally, the matches were scheduled to be played in October 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thenational.ae/sport/cricket/complete-schedule-of-pakistan-cricket-team-in-2020-including-asia-cup-and-t20-world-cup-1.957244 |title=Complete schedule of Pakistan cricket team in 2020 including Asia Cup and T20 World Cup |work=The National |access-date=30 December 2019}}</ref> However, in August 2020, the tour was postponed due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.geosuper.tv/latest/6215-pakistans-tour-of-south-africa-postponed-over-covid-19-fear |title=Pakistan's tour of South Africa postponed over Covid-19 fear |work=Geo Super |access-date=28 August 2020}}</ref> The following month, it was confirmed that both cricket boards were looking for a window in the international calendar to play the matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.geo.tv/latest/309477-pcb-might-arrange-limited-overs-series-with-south-african-board-soon |title=PCB in talks with South Africa for limited-overs series: report |work=Geo TV |access-date=23 September 2020}}</ref> In October 2020, [[Cricket South Africa]] (CSA) announced that the tour had been rescheduled for April 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricket.co.za/news/36182/Bumper-2020-2021-international-season-ahead-for-the-Proteas-men |title=Bumper 2020/2021 international season ahead for the Proteas men |work=Cricket South Africa |access-date=27 October 2020}}</ref> In January 2021, the [[Pakistan Cricket Board]] (PCB) confirmed that the tour was still on their schedule for the 2021 calendar year.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pcb.com.pk/press-release-detail/outcomes-of-pcb-cricket-committee-meeting-2.html |title=Outcomes of PCB Cricket Committee meeting |work=Pakistan Cricket Board |access-date=12 January 2021}}</ref> In February 2021, the tour dates were confirmed,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricket.co.za/news/37562/CSA-confirm-Pakistan-Tour-schedule |title=CSA confirm Pakistan Tour schedule |work=Cricket South Africa |access-date=12 February 2021}}</ref> including the addition of a fourth T20I fixture.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pcb.com.pk/press-release-detail/pcb-confirms-super-league-fixtures-against-south-africa.html |title=PCB confirms Super League fixtures against South Africa |work=Pakistan Cricket Board |access-date=12 February 2021}}</ref> On 4 March 2021, CSA announced that [[Temba Bavuma]] had been appointed as South Africa's [[limited overs cricket|limited overs]] captain,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricket.co.za/news/37749/Bavuma-and-Elgar-to-captain-the-Proteas |title=Bavuma and Elgar to captain the Proteas |work=Cricket South Africa |access-date=4 March 2021}}</ref> taking over the role from [[Quinton de Kock]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/south-africa-name-dean-elgar-test-captain-and-temba-bavuma-odi-and-t20i-captain-1253753 |title=South Africa name Dean Elgar Test captain and Temba Bavuma ODI and T20I captain |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=4 March 2021}}</ref>

Pakistan won the first ODI by three wickets,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pcb.com.pk/news-detail/babar-s-brilliance-take-pakistan-1-0-up.html |title=Babar's brilliance takes Pakistan 1-0 up |work=Pakistan Cricket Board |access-date=4 April 2021}}</ref> with South Africa winning the second match by seventeen runs to level the series.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricket.co.za/news/38074/Batsmen-draw-Proteas-level-in-spite-of-Fakhar-Zaman-onslaught |title=Batsmen draw Proteas level in spite of Fakhar Zaman onslaught |work=Cricket South Africa |access-date=4 April 2021}}</ref> Pakistan won the third ODI by 28 runs to win the series 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/series/pakistan-tour-of-south-africa-2021-1251556/south-africa-vs-pakistan-3rd-odi-1251574/match-report |title=Fakhar 101, Babar 94 set up Pakistan's second ODI series win in South Africa |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=7 April 2021}}</ref> In the T20I series, Pakistan won the first match by four wickets,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricket.co.za/news/38118/Rizwan-puts-Pakistan-1-0-up-in-KFC-T20I-Series |title=Rizwan puts Pakistan 1-0 up in KFC T20I Series |work=Cricket South Africa |access-date=14 April 2021}}</ref> with South Africa winning the second match by six wickets.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pcb.com.pk/news-detail/south-africa-beat-pakistan-by-six-wickets-in-second-t20i.html |title=South Africa beat Pakistan by six wickets in second T20I |work=Pakistan Cricket Board |access-date=14 April 2021}}</ref> The third match saw Pakistan win by nine wickets, with their captain [[Babar Azam]] scoring his first century in the format.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/cricket/56748140 |title=South Africa v Pakistan: Babar Azam hits first international Twenty20 century in thumping win |work=BBC Sport |access-date=14 April 2021}}</ref> Pakistan won the fourth T20I by three wickets to win the series 3–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.geo.tv/latest/345674-pak-vs-sa-pakistan-win-toss-decide-to-field-first-in-final-t20i |title=Pak vs SA: Pakistan beat South Africa in final T20I to claim series |work=Geo News |access-date=16 April 2021}}</ref>

Ahead of the tour, [[Saud Shakeel]] was ruled out of Pakistan's ODI squad due to an injury, with [[Asif Ali (cricketer, born 1991)|Asif Ali]] named as his replacement.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/pakistan-in-south-africa-injured-saud-shakeel-ruled-out-of-odi-series-in-south-africa-1256802 |title=Injured Saud Shakeel ruled out of ODI series in South Africa |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=25 March 2021}}</ref> [[Quinton de Kock]], [[David Miller (South African cricketer)|David Miller]], [[Kagiso Rabada]], [[Lungi Ngidi]] and [[Anrich Nortje]] were only available for the first two ODIs, before leaving South Africa's squad to play in the [[2021 Indian Premier League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cricbuzz.com/cricket-news/116888/boucher-takes-positives-from-ipl-bound-players-missing-pakistan-series |title=Boucher takes positives from IPL-bound players missing Pakistan series |work=CricBuzz |access-date=3 April 2021}}</ref> Pakistan's [[Shadab Khan]] injured his toe during the second ODI and was ruled out of the rest of the tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espncricinfo.com/story/sa-vs-pak-2020-21-toe-injury-puts-shadab-khan-out-of-remainder-of-pakistan-s-tour-of-africa-zimbabwe-1257825 |title=Toe injury puts Shadab Khan out of remainder of Pakistan's tour of Africa |work=ESPN Cricinfo |access-date=5 April 2021}}</ref> [[Fakhar Zaman (cricketer)|Fakhar Zaman]] was named as Shadab Khan's replacement for the T20I matches.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.pcb.com.pk/press-release-detail/zahid-replaces-shadab-for-zimbabwe-tour.html |title=Zahid replaces Shadab for Zimbabwe tour |work=Pakistan Cricket Board |access-date=7 April 2021}}</ref>

Before the T20I series, South Africa's captain [[Temba Bavuma]] was ruled out of the squad after suffering a hamstring injury in the third ODI, with [[Heinrich Klaasen]] named as captain of the team in Bavuma's place.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cricket.co.za/news/38092/Bavuma-ruled-out-of-KFC-T20-series |title=Bavuma ruled out of KFC T20 series |work=Cricket South Africa |access-date=9 April 2021}}</ref> [[Dwaine Pretorius]] was also ruled out due to rib injury, and [[Reeza Hendricks]] opted out of the series due to personal reasons.<ref>{{cite web| url=https://www.cricbuzz.com/cricket-news/116977/bavuma-ruled-out-of-pakistan-t20i-series | title= Bavuma ruled out of Pakistan T20I series | work = Cricbuzz| accessdate=9 April 2021}}</ref> [[Aiden Markram]], [[Andile Phehlukwayo]], [[Daryn Dupavillon]] and [[Wiaan Mulder]] were added to South Africa's T20I squad.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.icc-cricket.com/news/2097739 |title=Hamstring injury rules Temba Bavuma out of Pakistan T20Is |work=International Cricket Council |access-date=9 April 2021}}</ref>
