'''Joseph Haydar''' (18 November 1938 – 8 October 2024) was an Australian [[Olympic weightlifting|weightlifter]]. He competed in the [[Weightlifting at the 1964 Summer Olympics – Men's 75 kg|men's middleweight event]] at the [[1964 Summer Olympics]].<ref name="SportsRef">{{cite Sports-Reference |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/ha/joseph-haydar-1.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200418114011/https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/ha/joseph-haydar-1.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=18 April 2020 |title=Joseph Haydar Olympic Results |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

==Biography==
Haydar was born in Lebanon on 18 November 1938. After his weightlifting career he settled in [[Bunbury, Western Australia]], where he established a pizzeria,  Joe's Pizza. In 1979 he opened one of Bunbury's first high-rise buildings, Haydar House.<ref name=obit>{{cite news|url=https://thewest.com.au/news/south-western-times/vale-joe-haydar-bunbury-pizza-king-and-olympic-weightlifter--c-16330537|title=Vale Joe Haydar: Bunbury pizza king and Olympic weightlifter|newspaper=South Western Times|date=9 October 2024|first1=Oliver|last1=Lane|first2=Amber|last2=Lilley|access-date=9 October 2024}}</ref>

Haydar died on 8 October 2024, at the age of 85.<ref name=obit/>
