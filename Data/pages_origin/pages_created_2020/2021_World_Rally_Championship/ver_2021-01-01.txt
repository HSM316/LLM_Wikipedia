The '''2021 FIA World Rally Championship''' is the forty-ninth season of the [[World Rally Championship]], an [[auto racing]] competition recognised by the [[Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile]] (FIA) as the highest class of international [[rallying]]. Teams and crews are competing in twelve rallies for the [[List of World Rally Championship Drivers' champions|World Rally Championships for Drivers]], [[Co-driver]]s, [[List of World Rally Championship Teams' champions|Teams]] and [[List of World Rally Championship Constructors' champions|Manufacturers]].<ref name="teams cship">{{cite web|url=https://www.fia.com/sites/default/files/wrcsr_2021_16-12-20_published.pdf|title=2021 FIA World Rally Championship – Sporting regulations|publisher=[[Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile]]|page=9|accessdate=31 December 2020}}</ref> Crews are free to compete in cars complying with [[World Rally Car|Rally1]] and [[R5 (rallying)|Rally2]] regulations;{{efn|Rally1 cars were previously known as [[World Rally Car]]s and Rally2 cars were known as [[R5 (rallying)|R5]] before changes to the sporting regulations were introduced in [[2020 World Rally Championship|2020]].<ref name="pyramid"/>}} however, only manufacturers competing with Rally1 cars [[Homologation (motorsport)|homologated]] under regulations introduced in [[World Rally Car#2017|2017]] are eligible to score points in the Manufacturers' championship. The championship is due to begin in January 2021 with the [[Monte Carlo Rally|Rallye Monte-Carlo]] and conclude in November 2021 with [[Rally Japan]]. The series will be supported by the [[2021 World Rally Championship-2|World Rally Championship-2]] and [[2021 World Rally Championship-3|World Rally Championship-3]] categories at every round of the championship and by the [[2021 Junior World Rally Championship|Junior World Rally Championship]] at selected events.<ref name="pyramid">{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/146456/fia-steps-up-plan-to-simplify-wrc-career-ladder|title=FIA steps up plan to simplify WRC into five-tier career ladder|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=8 October 2019|accessdate=21 December 2020}}</ref>

[[Sébastien Ogier]] and [[Julien Ingrassia]] will be the reigning drivers' and co-drivers' champions, having secured their seventh championship titles at the [[2020 Rally Monza]]. [[Hyundai Motorsport|Hyundai]] are the reigning manufacturers' champions and will defend their manufacturers' title for the second consecutive year.

===Calendar changes===
With the addition of [[Rally Chile]] to the calendar in [[2019 World Rally Championship|2019]], the FIA opened the tender process for new events to join the championship in 2020.<ref name="FIA-11">{{cite news|url=https://www.fia.com/news/fia-announces-world-motor-sport-council-decisions-11|title=FIA announces World Motor Sport Council decisions|work=fia.com|publisher=[[Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile]]|date=12 October 2018|accessdate=12 October 2018}}</ref> Three events were successful,{{efn|[[Rally New Zealand]] was successful in its bid to join the championship, but was cancelled because of the pandemic.<ref name="nz off">{{Cite web|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/new-zealands-2020-wrc-return-off/|title=New Zealand's 2020 WRC return off|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=4 June 2020|accessdate=4 June 2020}}</ref> It was not included on the 2021 calendar, but a separate, later bid from [[Croatia Rally|Rally Croatia]] was also successful.<ref name="2021 calendar"/>}} but the championship was affected by a series of cancellations in 2019 and 2020 that necessitated changes to the 2021 calendar:
* [[Rallye Catalunya]] is due to return to the championship. The rally was removed from the [[2020 World Rally Championship|2020 schedule]] as part of an event-sharing agreement that would see it removed from the calendar for one year, but be guaranteed a spot on the calendar for the next two.<ref name="2021 calendar" />
* [[Rally Chile]] is due to return after a one-year absence. The rally had been included on the original draft of the 2020 calendar, but was later cancelled in the face of [[2019 Chilean protests|ongoing civil unrest]] in the country. Organisers of the event negotiated a return to the calendar for the 2021 championship.<ref name="unrest">{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/147405/rally-chile-cancelled-due-to-political-unrest|title=WRC's 2020 Rally Chile cancelled due to political and social unrest|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=29 November 2019|accessdate=31 December 2019|quote=Rally Chile's Felipe Horta said: 'The decision was to wait a year to take the world championship. We have talked with the FIA and the WRC [Promoter] in Germany, where they have fortunately understood very favourably what is happening and are allowing us to cancel the 2020 date and resume the contract we have established for three years.'}}</ref>
* [[Croatia Rally|Rally Croatia]] will make its championship début, replacing [[Rally Mexico]].<ref name="2021 calendar"/> Croatia will become the 34th country to host a World Rally Championship round. It will be based in [[Zagreb]], and use tarmac roads that had been part of the INA Delta Rally.
* [[Rally Deutschland]] was removed from the calendar. The event had [[2020 Rallye Deutschland|planned to run in 2020]], but was cancelled due to the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref name="Germany out">{{cite news|title=Rally Germany cancelled as Italian WRC round moves to avoid Imola F1 clash|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/151671/rally-germany-cancelled-as-italy-changes-date|first=Jason|last=Craig|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=26 August 2020|accessdate=27 December 2020}}</ref> It was not included on the 2021 calendar.
* The [[Rally Finland|Rallies of Finland]], [[Wales Rally GB|Great Britain]], and [[Rally de Portugal|Portugal]] are due to return to the championship after a one-year absence. The 2020 events were cancelled [[Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on motorsport|in response to the COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name="finland back">{{Cite web|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/no-neste-rally-finland-for-2020/|title=No Neste Rally Finland for 2020|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=3 June 2020|accessdate=3 June 2020}}</ref><ref name="wales back">{{cite news|url=https://www.speedcafe.com/2020/06/09/rally-gb-cancelled/|title=Rally GB cancelled|first=Dan|last=Herrero|work=[[Speedcafe|speedcafe.com]]|publisher=[[Speedcafe]]|date=9 June 2020|accessdate=9 June 2020}}</ref>
* [[Rally Japan]] is scheduled to return to the calendar for the first time since [[2010 World Rally Championship|2010]].<ref name="2021 calendar"/> It was originally included on the 2020 calendar, but was cancelled due to the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/151507/belgium-gets-wrc-round-after-rally-japan-axed|title=Belgium gets WRC round for the first time after Rally Japan axed|first=Jamie|last=Klein|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=19 August 2020|accessdate=19 August 2020}}</ref>
* The [[Safari Rally]] is scheduled to be run as a World Championship event for the first time since [[2002 World Rally Championship|2002]]. The event is to be based in the Kenyan capital [[Nairobi]] and feature stages around [[Lake Naivasha]].<ref name="nairobi">{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/wrc/news/september-2019/kenya-rally-2020-wrc/page/6692--12-12-.html|title=Safari back in 2020|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=27 September 2019|accessdate=28 September 2019}}</ref> The event had been planned to make its return to the championship in 2020, but was cancelled in response to the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref name="safari covid">{{cite news|title=WRC News: Kenya's Safari Rally cancelled due to coronavirus|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/149453/safari-rally-wrc-event-cancelled-due-to-coronavirus|first=Rachit|last=Thukral|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=15 May 2020|accessdate=16 May 2020}}</ref>
* [[Rally Sweden]] was included on the first draft of the calendar with its traditional February date,<ref name="Sweden route">{{cite web|url=https://rallysweden.com/en/the-race/|title=The rally|work=[[Rally Sweden|rallysweden.com]]|publisher=[[Rally Sweden]]|accessdate=15 December 2020}}</ref> but was cancelled before the start of the season due to the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref>{{cite news|title=WRC announces 2021 Rally Sweden cancellation|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/154210/wrc-announces-2021-rally-sweden-cancellation|first=Rachit|last=Thukral|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=15 December 2020|accessdate=18 December 2020}}</ref><ref name="Sweden off">{{cite web|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/rally-sweden-cancelled-due-to-coronavirus/|title=Rally Sweden cancelled|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=15 December 2020|accessdate=15 December 2020}}</ref>
In light of the disruption caused by the pandemic in 2020 and in anticipation of further delays, the calendar included an additional six reserve rounds that could be included in the event of rallies being cancelled. These events include rallies in [[Acropolis Rally|Greece]], [[Ypres Rally|Belgium]], [[Rally Turkey|Turkey]], [[Rally Argentina|Argentina]], [[Monza Rally Show|Monza]] and Latvia.<ref name="2021 calendar"/><ref name="reserve list why">{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/154344/fia-quite-confident-rally-monte-carlo-will-go-ahead|title=FIA "quite confident" 2021 WRC opener in Monte Carlo will go ahead|first=Jason|last=Craig|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=24 December 2020|accessdate=26 December 2020}}</ref>

requirements.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.speedcafe.com/torquecafe/toyotas-wrc-monster-for-the-road/|title=Toyota’s WRC monster for the ==Changes==
===Technical regulations===
[[Pirelli]] will replace [[Michelin]] as the sport's sole tyre supplier.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/147696/pirelli-wins-tyre-tender-to-supply-wrc-from-2021|title=Pirelli wins tyre tender to supply WRC top tier and R5s from 2021|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=20 December 2019|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> Under the terms of the agreement, Pirelli will supply tyres to all teams entering [[World Rally Car|Rally1]] and [[R5 (rallying)|Rally2]] cars.

===Sporting regulations===
2021 will see the creation of the [[List of World Rally Championship Teams' champions|World Rally Championship for Teams]], a new championship title that will exist alongside the [[List of World Rally Championship Drivers' champions|World Rally Championships for Drivers]], [[List of World Rally Championship Co-Drivers' champions|Co-drivers]] and [[List of World Rally Championship Constructors' champions|Manufacturers]].<ref name="teams cship"/><!-- Under the new regulations, individual teams will compete against one another for the Teams' championship. However, teams competing with the same car will work together to score points for their manufacturer.-->

As the [[Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on motorsport|impact of the COVID-19 pandemic]] meant that only seven of the thirteen events planned for the 2020 championship took place, the World Motorsport Council passed a resolution declaring that for the drivers', co-drivers' and manufacturers' championship titles to be awarded a minimum of six rallies must be held.<ref name="reserve list why"/>

Manufacturers will be awarded [[Power Stage]] bonus points for the first time. The scoring system will be the same as that used by drivers and co-drivers, with five points awarded for the fastest manufacturer car down to one point for the fifth quickest. Only the two fastest drivers from a single manufacturer will eligible to score.<ref name="manu ps points">{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/power-stage-points-extended-to-manufacturers--wrc2-and-wrc3/|title=Power Stage points extended to manufacturers, WRC2 and WRC3|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=17 December 2020|accessdate=17 December 2020}}</ref><!--

===Scoring system===
Points were awarded to the top ten classified finishers in each event. In the manufacturers' championship, teams were eligible to nominate three crews to score points, but these points were only awarded to the top two classified finishers representing a manufacturer and driving a 2017-specification World Rally Car. There were also five bonus points awarded to the winners of the [[Power Stage]], four points for second place, three for third, two for fourth and one for fifth. Power Stage points were awarded in the drivers', co-drivers' championships and manufacturers'.<ref name="manu ps points"/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.wrc.com/en/wrc/news/september-2016/2017-calendar/page/3911--12-12-.html|title=2017 WRC dates confirmed|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=28 September 2016|accessdate=29 September 2016}}</ref>
