The '''2021 FIA World Rally Championship''' was the forty-ninth season of the [[World Rally Championship]], an [[auto racing]] competition recognised by the [[Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile]] (FIA) as the highest class of international [[rallying]]. Teams and crews are competing in twelve rallies for the [[List of World Rally Championship Drivers' champions|World Rally Championships for Drivers]], [[Co-driver]]s and [[List of World Rally Championship Constructors' champions|Manufacturers]]. Crews were free to compete in cars complying with [[World Rally Car]], [[Rally Pyramid]] and some [[Group R]] regulations; however, only manufacturers competing with World Rally Cars [[Homologation (motorsport)|homologated]] under regulations introduced in [[World Rally Car#2017–2021|2017]] were eligible to score points in the Manufacturers' championship.<ref name="teams cship">{{cite web|url=https://www.fia.com/sites/default/files/wrcsr_2021_16-12-20_published.pdf|title=2021 FIA World Rally Championship – Sporting regulations|publisher=[[Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile]]|page=9|access-date=31 December 2020}}</ref> The championship began in January 2021 with the [[Monte Carlo Rally|Rallye Monte-Carlo]] and concluded in November 2021 with [[Monza Rally Show|Rally Monza]]. The series was supported by the [[2021 World Rally Championship-2|World Rally Championship-2]] and [[2021 World Rally Championship-3|World Rally Championship-3]] categories at every round of the championship and by the [[2021 Junior World Rally Championship|Junior World Rally Championship]] at selected events.<ref name="pyramid">{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/146456/fia-steps-up-plan-to-simplify-wrc-career-ladder|title=FIA steps up plan to simplify WRC into five-tier career ladder|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=8 October 2019|access-date=21 December 2020}}</ref>

[[Sébastien Ogier]] and [[Julien Ingrassia]] were the defending drivers' and co-drivers' champions, having secured their seventh championship titles at the [[2020 Rally Monza]]. [[Hyundai Motorsport|Hyundai]] were the defending manufacturers' champions and were defending their manufacturers' title for the second consecutive year.

At the conclusion of the championship, Ogier and Ingrassia won their eighth world titles after winning the [[2021 Rally Monza]]. [[Elfyn Evans]] and [[Scott Martin (co-driver)|Scott Martin]] finished second, trailing by twenty-three points. [[Thierry Neuville]] and [[Martijn Wydaeghe]] were third, a further thirty-one points behind. In the manufacturers' championship, [[Toyota Gazoo Racing WRT]] clinched the title, a massive fifty-nine-point lead over the defending manufacturer champion [[Hyundai Motorsport|Hyundai Shell Mobis WRT]], with [[M-Sport World Rally Team|M-Sport Ford WRT]] in third.

===Calendar changes===
With the addition of [[Rally Chile]] to the calendar in [[2019 World Rally Championship|2019]], the FIA opened the tender process for new events to join the championship in 2020.<ref name="FIA-11">{{cite news|url=https://www.fia.com/news/fia-announces-world-motor-sport-council-decisions-11|title=FIA announces World Motor Sport Council decisions|work=fia.com|publisher=[[Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile]]|date=12 October 2018|access-date=12 October 2018}}</ref> Three events were successful,{{efn|[[Rally New Zealand]] was successful in its bid to join the championship, but was cancelled because of the pandemic.<ref name="nz off">{{Cite web|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/new-zealands-2020-wrc-return-off/|title=New Zealand's 2020 WRC return off|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=4 June 2020|access-date=4 June 2020}}</ref> It was not included on the 2021 calendar, but a separate, later bid from [[Croatia Rally|Rally Croatia]] was also successful.<ref name="2021 calendar"/>}} but the championship was affected by a series of cancellations in 2019 and 2020 that necessitated changes to the 2021 calendar:
* [[Rally de Catalunya|Rally Catalunya]] returned to the championship. The rally was removed from the [[2020 World Rally Championship|2020 schedule]] as part of an event-sharing agreement that would see it removed from the calendar for one year, but was guaranteed a spot on the calendar for the next two.<ref name="2021 calendar"/> The rally returned to running exclusively on [[Asphalt concrete|tarmac]] roads for the first time since {{WRC|2009}}.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dirtfish.com/calendar/rally-spain/|title=Rally Spain|work=dirtfish.com|access-date=9 January 2021}}</ref>{{efn|[[Rally de Catalunya|Rally Catalunya]] had previously been run as a mixed surface rally, with the first leg of the event held on gravel roads and the final two legs on tarmac.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/catalunya-to-run-mixed-surface-stage-4438499/4438499/|title=Catalunya to run mixed-surface stage|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=20 May 2010|access-date=25 May 2021}}</ref>}}
[[File:Acr2011 641.jpg|thumb|right|250px|The [[Acropolis Rally]] of [[Greece]] returned to the championship for the first time since {{WRC|2013}}.]]
* [[Rally Chile]] was due to return after a one-year absence. The rally had been included on the original draft of the 2020 calendar, but was later cancelled in the face of [[2019–2020 Chilean protests|ongoing civil unrest]] in the country.<ref name="unrest">{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/147405/rally-chile-cancelled-due-to-political-unrest|title=WRC's 2020 Rally Chile cancelled due to political and social unrest|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=29 November 2019|access-date=31 December 2019|quote=Rally Chile's Felipe Horta said: 'The decision was to wait a year to take the world championship. We have talked with the FIA and the WRC [Promoter] in Germany, where they have fortunately understood very favourably what is happening and are allowing us to cancel the 2020 date and resume the contract we have established for three years.'}}</ref> Organisers of the event negotiated a return to the calendar for the 2021 championship, but it was again cancelled due to continued travel and other restrictions amid the COVID-19 pandemic. [[Acropolis Rally]] is set to replace the rally after a seven-year absence on the calendar.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/acropolis-rally-returns-to-wrc-for-the-first-time-since-2013/5918965/|title=Acropolis Rally returns to WRC for the first time since 2013|first=Jason|last=Craig|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=26 March 2021|access-date=27 March 2021}}</ref>
* [[Croatia Rally|Rally Croatia]] made its championship debut, replacing [[Rally Mexico]].<ref name="2021 calendar"/> Croatia thus became the 34th country to host a [[List of World Rally Championship rallies|World Rally Championship round]]. It was based in [[Zagreb]], and ran on tarmac roads.
* [[Rally Deutschland]] was removed from the calendar. The event had [[2020 Rallye Deutschland|planned to run in 2020]], but was cancelled due to the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref name="Germany out">{{cite news|title=Rally Germany cancelled as Italian WRC round moves to avoid Imola F1 clash|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/151671/rally-germany-cancelled-as-italy-changes-date|first=Jason|last=Craig|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=26 August 2020|access-date=27 December 2020}}</ref> It was not included on the 2021 calendar.
* The Rallies of [[Rally Finland|Finland]] and [[Rally de Portugal|Portugal]] also returned to the championship after a one-year absence. The 2020 events were cancelled [[Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on motorsport|in response to the COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name="finland back">{{Cite web|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/no-neste-rally-finland-for-2020/|title=No Neste Rally Finland for 2020|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=3 June 2020|access-date=3 June 2020}}</ref><ref name="wales back">{{cite news|url=https://www.speedcafe.com/2020/06/09/rally-gb-cancelled/|title=Rally GB cancelled|first=Dan|last=Herrero|work=[[Speedcafe|speedcafe.com]]|publisher=[[Speedcafe]]|date=9 June 2020|access-date=9 June 2020}}</ref>
[[File:RY Ypres action 1.jpg|thumb|left|250px|The [[Ypres Rally]]'s debut made [[Belgium]] the 35th nation to hold a [[List of World Rally Championship rallies|World Rally Championship event]].]]
* [[Rally GB]] was replaced by the [[Ypres Rally]] in Belgium.<ref name="no gb">{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/154497/uk-loses-2021-slot-as-belgium-added-to-calendar|title=UK loses 2021 WRC calendar slot to inaugural Belgian round|first=Jason|last=Craig|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=8 January 2021|access-date=8 January 2021}}</ref> Rally GB had originally planned to move from Wales to Northern Ireland, but the event was replaced when organisers were unable to come to an agreement with the government of Northern Ireland to support the rally.
* [[Rally Japan]] was scheduled to return to the calendar for the first time since {{WRC|2010}},<ref name="2021 calendar"/> but it was ultimately called off due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Japan|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref name="Japan cancelled"/> The rally was also originally included on the 2020 calendar, but was also cancelled because of the pandemic.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/151507/belgium-gets-wrc-round-after-rally-japan-axed|title=Belgium gets WRC round for the first time after Rally Japan axed|first=Jamie|last=Klein|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=19 August 2020|access-date=19 August 2020}}</ref> Rally Monza was confirmed to hold the season finale for the second year in a row.<ref name="Monza 2021"/>
* The [[Safari Rally]] was run as a World Championship event for the first time since {{WRC|2002}}. The event was based in the Kenyan capital [[Nairobi]] and featured stages around [[Lake Naivasha]].<ref name="nairobi">{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/wrc/news/september-2019/kenya-rally-2020-wrc/page/6692--12-12-.html|title=Safari back in 2020|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=27 September 2019|access-date=28 September 2019}}</ref> The event had been planned to make its return to the championship in 2020, but was cancelled in response to the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref name="safari covid">{{cite news|title=WRC News: Kenya's Safari Rally cancelled due to coronavirus|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/149453/safari-rally-wrc-event-cancelled-due-to-coronavirus|first=Rachit|last=Thukral|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=15 May 2020|access-date=16 May 2020}}</ref>
[[File:Kimi-tunturi10-ss8-03.png|thumb|right|250px|The [[Arctic Rally]] became the first World Rally Championship round to be held inside the [[Arctic Circle]].]]
* [[Rally Sweden]] was included on the first draft of the calendar with its traditional February date,<ref name="Sweden route">{{cite web|url=https://rallysweden.com/en/the-race/|title=The rally|work=[[Rally Sweden|rallysweden.com]]|publisher=[[Rally Sweden]]|access-date=15 December 2020}}</ref> but was cancelled before the start of the season due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic in Sweden|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite news|title=WRC announces 2021 Rally Sweden cancellation|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/154210/wrc-announces-2021-rally-sweden-cancellation|first=Rachit|last=Thukral|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=15 December 2020|access-date=18 December 2020}}</ref><ref name="Sweden off">{{cite web|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/rally-sweden-cancelled-due-to-coronavirus/|title=Rally Sweden cancelled|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=15 December 2020|access-date=15 December 2020}}</ref> The [[Arctic Rally]] in northern Finland was chosen as a replacement to ensure that a winter rally was included on the calendar.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/154570/wrc-adds-arctic-rally-finland-to-2021-calendar|title=WRC adds Arctic Rally Finland to 2021 calendar as Sweden replacement|first=Jason|last=Craig|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=14 January 2021|access-date=14 January 2021}}</ref>{{efn|The [[Arctic Rally]] was held twice during the 2021 calendar year. The first running in January was part of the [[Finnish Rally Championship]] and the second running in February was the World Championship round.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ewrc-results.com/events/104/|title=Arctic Rally|work=ewrc-results.com|access-date=15 January 2021}}</ref>}}
In light of the disruption caused by the pandemic in 2020 and in anticipation of further delays, the calendar included an additional six reserve rounds that could be included in the event of rallies being cancelled. ==Changes==
===Technical regulations===
[[File:Logo Pirelli.svg|thumb|right|200px|[[Pirelli]] became the official tyre supplier.]]
[[Pirelli]] has become the championships' sole nominated tyre supplier with the removal of [[Michelin]] and [[Yokohama Rubber Company|Yokohama]].<ref name="pirelli">{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/147696/pirelli-wins-tyre-tender-to-supply-wrc-from-2021|title=Pirelli wins tyre tender to supply WRC top tier and R5s from 2021|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=20 December 2019|access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref> Under the terms of the agreement, Pirelli will supply tyres to all entrants of four-wheel drive cars.<ref name="teams cship" />

===Sporting regulations===
As the [[Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on motorsport|impact of the COVID-19 pandemic]] meant that only seven of the thirteen events planned for the 2020 championship took place, the World Motorsport Council passed a resolution declaring that for the drivers', co-drivers' and manufacturers' championship titles to be awarded a minimum of six rallies must be held.<ref name="reserve list why"/>

Manufacturers are awarded [[Power Stage]] bonus points for the first time. The scoring system remains the same as that used by drivers and co-drivers, with five points awarded for the fastest manufacturer car down to one point for the fifth quickest. Only the two fastest drivers from a single manufacturer are eligible to score.<ref name="manu ps points">{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/season-2020/wrc/power-stage-points-extended-to-manufacturers--wrc2-and-wrc3/|title=Power Stage points extended to manufacturers, WRC2 and WRC3|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=17 December 2020|access-date=17 December 2020}}</ref>

==Season report==
===Opening rounds===
The 2021 FIA World Rally Championship went underway in [[2021 Monte Carlo Rally|Monaco]]. The [[Hyundai Motorsport|Hyundai]] crew of [[Ott Tänak]] and [[Martin Järveoja]] took an early lead,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2021/wrc/taenak-double-earns-rallye-monte-carlo-lead/|title=Tänak double earns rallye Monte-Carlo lead|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=21 January 2021|access-date=25 January 2021}}</ref> but their lead was wiped out when they were compromised by the loss of power in [[Hairpin turn|hairpins]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2021/wrc/ogier-leads-monte-carlo-after-clean-sweep/|title=Ogier leads Monte-Carlo after clean sweep|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=22 January 2021|access-date=25 January 2021}}</ref> The Estonian pair's rally was further hampered by two [[Flat tire|punctures]], which meant that they did not have enough rubber on one of their wheels for the car to be considered [[Roadworthiness|road legal]]. Unable to complete the liaison between [[Special stage (rallying)|special stages]], Tänak and Järveoja were ruled out for the second consecutive year in Monte-Carlo.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/154720/hyundai-cannot-be-proud-of-rally-monte-carlo-efforts|title=Hyundai "cannot be proud" of WRC Rally Monte Carlo efforts|first=Jason|last=Craig|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=24 January 2021|accessdate=24 January 2021}}</ref> The [[M-Sport World Rally Team|M-Sport]] crew of [[Teemu Suninen]] and [[Mikko Markkula]] also retired from the rally when they crashed out on the very first stage of the event.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2021/wrc/watch-big-roll-for-suninen-on-ss1/|title=Watch: big roll for Suninen on SS1|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=21 January 2021|access-date=25 January 2021}}</ref> Local heroes [[Sébastien Ogier]] and [[Julien Ingrassia]] were the favourites for the weekend. Despite a [[flat tire]] that lost the lead to their teammates [[Elfyn Evans]] and [[Scott Martin (co-driver)|Scott Martin]],<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2021/wrc/leader-evans-under-attack-from-monte-master-ogier/|title=Leader Evans under attack from Monte master Ogier|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=22 January 2021|accessdate=25 January 2021}}</ref> the reigning world champions set fastest stage time after fastest stage time to regain the top spot and eventually won their eighth Monte Carlo victory, a new record for wins in [[Monte Carlo Rally|Monte Carlo]].<ref name="monte winner">{{cite news|url=https://www.wrc.com/en/news/2021/wrc/breaking-news-ogier-claims-record-monte-carlo-victory/|title=Ogier claims record eighth Monte-Carlo victory|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=24 January 2021|accessdate=25 January 2021}}</ref> They also became the first crew to win the rally with five different manufacturers. Evans and Martin finished second to complete a [[Toyota Gazoo Racing WRT|Toyota]] one-two. The Japanese manufacturer's party was further flourished by the dominance at the [[Power Stage]], which saw them build a twenty-two-point lead over the reigning manufacturers' champions Hyundai.<ref name="monte winner"/> Last year victor [[Thierry Neuville]] joined them on the podium with his new [[co-driver]] [[Martijn Wydaeghe]], whose first ever podium in the championship.<ref name="monte winner"/>

===Scoring system===
Points were awarded to the top ten classified finishers in each event. In the manufacturers' championship, teams were eligible to nominate three crews to score points, but these points were only awarded to the top two classified finishers representing a manufacturer and driving a 2017-specification World Rally Car. There were also five bonus points awarded to the winners of the [[Power Stage]], four points for second place, three for third, two for fourth and one for fifth. Power Stage points were awarded in the drivers', co-drivers' championships and manufacturers'.<ref name="manu ps points"/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.wrc.com/en/wrc/news/september-2016/2017-calendar/page/3911--12-12-.html|title=2017 WRC dates confirmed|work=wrc.com|publisher=[[WRC Promoter GmbH]]|date=28 September 2016|access-date=29 September 2016}}</ref>
