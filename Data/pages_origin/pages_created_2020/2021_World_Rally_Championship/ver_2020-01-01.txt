The '''2021 FIA World Rally Championship''' will be the forty-ninth season of the [[World Rally Championship]], an [[auto racing]] competition recognised by the [[Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile]] (FIA) as the highest class of international [[rallying]]. Teams and crews are due to compete for the [[List of World Rally Championship Drivers' champions|World Rally Championships for Drivers]], [[Co-driver]]s and [[List of World Rally Championship Constructors' champions|Manufacturers]]. Crews are free to compete in cars complying with [[World Rally Car]] and [[Group R]] regulations; however, only manufacturers competing with World Rally Cars [[Homologation (motorsport)|homologated]] under regulations introduced in [[World Rally Car#2017|2017]] are eligible to score points in the Manufacturers' championship.

===Calendar changes===
The [[Rally Catalunya|Rallies of Catalunya]] and [[Rally Chile|Chile]] are scheduled to return to the calendar after a one-year absence. Rally Catalunya was removed from the [[2020 World Rally Championship|2020 schedule]] as part of an event-sharing agreement that would see the event removed from the calendar for one year, but be guaranteed a spot on the calendar for the next two.<ref name="spain"/> Rally Chile had been included on the original draft of the 2020 calendar, but the event was cancelled in the face of [[2019 Chilean protests|ongoing civil unrest]] in the country. Organisers of the event negotiated a return to the calendar for the 2021 championship.<ref name="unrest"/> [[Rally Australia]] is due to return after a two-year absence;<ref name="20 calendar"/> the event was included on the [[2019 World Rally Championship|2019 calendar]], but was cancelled due to [[2019–20 Australian bushfire season|a bushfire emergency]]. It was omitted from the 2020 calendar as part of an event-sharing agreement with [[Rally New Zealand]], which will not be run in 2021.<ref name="20 calendar"/>

===Technical regulations===
[[Pirelli]] will replace [[Michelin]] as the sport's sole tyre supplier.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.autosport.com/wrc/news/147696/pirelli-wins-tyre-tender-to-supply-wrc-from-2021|title=Pirelli wins tyre tender to supply WRC top tier and R5s from 2021|first=David|last=Evans|work=[[Autosport|autosport.com]]|publisher=[[Motorsport Network]]|date=20 December 2019|accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref> Under the terms of the agreement, Pirelli will supply tyres to all teams entering [[World Rally Car]]s and [[R5 (rallying)|R5]] cars.
