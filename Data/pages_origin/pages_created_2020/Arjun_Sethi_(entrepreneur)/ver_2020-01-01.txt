'''Arjun Sethi''' is an American internet entrepreneur, investor and executive. He is co-founder and partner at [[venture capital]] firm Tribe Capital. He previously was partner at [[Social Capital (venture capital)|Social Capital]] and served as an executive at [[Yahoo!]] where he launched [[Yahoo! Livetext]]. Before that, he was co-founder and CEO of [[MessageMe]] (acquired by Yahoo!) and he was CEO of Lolapps, the developer behind [[Ravenwood Fair]].

== Career ==
=== Lolapps ===
In 2008, Sethi took the role as CEO of Lolapps, which developed social games on the [[Facebook platform]]. Lolapps developed [[Ravenwood Fair]] which had 10 million monthly active users.<ref>[https://venturebeat.com/2011/02/22/lolapps-launches-a-novel-expansion-for-ravenwood-fair-facebook-game/ LOLapps launches a novel expansion for Ravenwood Fair Facebook game]</ref> At its peak, Lolapps had 150 million monthly active users across its portfolio of games.<ref>[https://venturebeat.com/2010/10/19/creator-of-the-fierce-doom-and-quake-games-tries-his-hand-at-a-cute-facebook-game/ Creator of the fierce Doom and Quake games tries his hand at a cute Facebook app]</ref>

Lolapps merged with game publisher 6waves in 2011<ref>[https://www.forbes.com/sites/tomiogeron/2011/07/18/lolapps-merges-with-6waves-in-social-gaming-developer-publisher-tie-up/#44a77cff6ac8 Lolapps Merges With 6Waves In Social Gaming Developer-Publisher Tie-Up]</ref> which made the merged entity 6waves Lolapps the second largest game publisher on Facebook measured by monthly active users after [[Zynga]].<ref>[https://venturebeat.com/2011/07/18/lolapps-6waves-merger/ Lolapps merges with 6waves to build social gaming powerhouse]</ref> Sethi became [[Chief Product Officer]] in 6waves Lolapps, which went on to raise $35 million in funding from Korean gaming company [[Nexon]].<ref>[https://techcrunch.com/2012/03/19/6waves-lolapps-layoffs/ 6waves Lolapps Sheds Most Of Its Development Team To Focus on Publishing Games]</ref>

In 2012, 6waves Lolapps laid off most of its development team and Sethi left to join venture capital firm [[Social Capital (venture capital)|Social Capital]] as an [[entrepreneur in residence]].<ref>[https://techcrunch.com/2012/05/08/former-lolapps-ceo-arjun-sethi-lands-at-former-facebook-vps-chamath-palihapitiyas-fund-as-an-eir/ Former Lolapps CEO Arjun Sethi Lands At Former Facebook VP’s Chamath Palihapitiya’s Fund As An EIR]</ref>

=== MessageMe and Yahoo ===
Later in 2012, Sethi became co-founder and CEO of [[mobile messaging]] app [[MessageMe]]. MessageMe reached 5 million users within 75 days of launch.<ref>[https://www.engadget.com/2013/05/22/messageme-reaches-5-million-users-in-75-days/ MessageMe chat app amasses 5 million users in 75 days across Android and iOS]</ref> The company raised close to $12 million across two rounds of funding led by [[Greylock Partners]].<ref>[https://techcrunch.com/2013/05/16/messageme-raises-a-10m-series-a-led-by-greylock-as-it-gears-up-for-money-and-premium-services-in-its-rich-messaging-app/ MessageMe Raises A $10M Series A Led By Greylock As It Gears Up For Money And Premium Services In Its Rich Messaging App]</ref>

Yahoo acquired MessageMe in 2014 for a price between $30 million and $40 million<ref>[https://www.pcmag.com/news/328132/yahoo-beats-snapchat-to-the-punch-with-messageme-acquisition Yahoo Beats Snapchat to the Punch With MessageMe Acquisition]</ref>, shut down the app MessageMe, and put Sethi and the ex-MessageMe team to work on a Yahoo messaging app meant to compete with [[Snapchat]] and [[WhatsApp]]<ref name="wsj-scp">[https://blogs.wsj.com/digits/2016/02/04/yahoo-loses-mobile-entrepreneur-arjun-sethi-to-venture-firm/ Yahoo Loses Mobile Entrepreneur Arjun Sethi to Venture Firm]</ref>. Yahoo's acquisition of MessageMe was part of CEO [[Marissa Mayer]]'s larger strategy to bring in new talent via small startup acquisitions.<ref>[https://www.businessinsider.com/startup-founders-that-left-yahoo-less-than-3-years-after-getting-acquired-2016-1 These people left Yahoo less than 3 years after their startups got acquired by Marissa Mayer]</ref>

In 2015, Sethi and the former MessageMe team launched [[Yahoo! Livetext]], an audio-free video messaging app. It never gained significant traction and Yahoo shut down the product in 2016.<ref name="wsj-scp"></ref>

=== Social Capital ===
In early 2016, Sethi left Yahoo to join Social Capital as an investing partner focused on consumer startups.<ref name="wsj-scp"></ref>

At Social Capital, Sethi backed the firm's investments in and served on the [[board of directors]] of [[EShares|Carta]], [[Relativity Space]], Cover, and CryptoMove.<ref>[https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-06-16/another-social-capital-partner-departs-venture-capital-firm Another Social Capital Partner Departs Venture Capital Firm]</ref>

=== Tribe Capital ===
In 2018, three former Social Capital investing partners, Sethi, Ted Maidenberg and Jonathan Hsu left to form Tribe Capital with $200 million targeted for its first fund.<ref name="wsj">[https://www.wsj.com/articles/pro-vc-exclusive-former-social-capital-partners-seek-200-million-for-tribe-capital-1536060603 Pro VC Exclusive: Former Social Capital Partners Seek $200 Million for Tribe Capital]</ref> Tribe invests in early-stage startups<ref>[https://www.businessinsider.com/tribe-capital-new-vc-firm-from-social-capital-partners-2018-7 Tribe Capital: What we know about Arjun Sethi's new VC firm]</ref> and uses quantitative data to inform investment decisions<ref>[https://techcrunch.com/2019/06/05/tribe-capital/ Social Capital Reincarnated]</ref>.

At Tribe, Sethi made its first investment in [[cryptocurrency]] trading platform SFOX<ref>[https://www.wsj.com/articles/sfox-secures-22-7-million-series-a-for-crypto-asset-management-1534518931 Former Social Capital Partners Make First Investment Since Exit]</ref>. He has also followed on to his investments in [[EShares|Carta]]<ref>[https://techcrunch.com/2018/12/27/cap-table-management-tool-carta-valued-at-800m-with-new-funding/ Cap table management tool Carta valued at $800M with new funding]</ref>, [[Relativity Space]], and Cover.
