'''Rafael Arcángel Quispe Flores''' (born 24 October 1969), often referred to as '''Tata Quispe''', is a Bolivian [[Indigenous peoples in Bolivia|indigenous]] activist and politician who served as general executive director of the Indigenous Development Fund from 2019 to 2020. He previously served as a substitute [[party-list]] member of the [[Chamber of Deputies (Bolivia)|Chamber of Deputies]] from [[La Paz Department (Bolivia)|La Paz]] under [[María Eugenia Calcina]] from 2015 to 2019.

Born in [[Coro Coro Municipality|Coro Coro]] and raised in [[rural poverty]], Quispe completed [[secondary education]] in [[El Alto]]. He studied law at multiple universities, though he never completed a degree. He held prominent positions of local leadership in and around his [[Aymara people|Aymara]] community, serving as ''mallku'' of the [[wiktionary:marka#Quechua|Caquingora Marka]] and later ''mallku'' of the [[Pacajes Province|Pakajaqi Suyu]]. In 2010, he was elected to the governing board of the [[National Council of Ayllus and Markas of Qullasuyu]], serving as ''mallku'' of the organization's Extractive Industries Commission. His opposition to President [[Evo Morales]] gained him national prominence during the conflict over the [[Isiboro Sécure National Park and Indigenous Territory]], during which time he led [[2011 Bolivian indigenous rights protests|indigenous protests]] against a planned [[Route 24 (Bolivia)|highway]] crossing through the [[protected area]].

Quispe entered national politics in the [[2014 Bolivian general election|2014 general election]], losing a race for a seat in the Chamber of Deputies but later being authorized to serve as a substitute deputy. Quispe's humorous but polemic style of politics made him a controversial figure, with members of both the ruling [[Movement for Socialism]] and his own caucus denouncing him for various acts. In 2019, President [[Jeanine Áñez]] appointed him to direct the country's Indigenous Development Fund, and he later briefly served as vice minister of decolonization. In late 2020, Quispe launched his candidacy for the [[Governor of La Paz|governorship]] of La Paz, running on a ticket led by his own Somos Pueblo party. He narrowly failed to move to the second round, placing third in the [[2021 La Paz gubernatorial election|election]]. Shortly thereafter, Quispe announced his retirement from politics.

== Early life and career ==
An ethnic [[Aymara people|Aymara]], Rafael Quispe was born on 24 October 1969 in the [[Ayllu|Sicuypata Ayllu]] of [[La Paz Department (Bolivia)|La Paz]]'s [[Coro Coro Municipality]], a locale that, according to local [[Indigenous peoples in Bolivia|indigenous]] custom, is situated in the [[wiktionary:marka#Quechua|Caquingora Marka]] of the [[Pacajes Province|Pakajaqi Suyu]].<ref name=":U.BO-2019-02-25">{{Cite news |date=25 February 2019 |title=Rafael Quispe: Hago marketing político y la gente me conoce mejor |language=es |trans-title=Rafael Quispe: By Doing Political Marketing, the People Know Me Better |agency=Urgente.bo |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://urgente.bo/noticia/rafael-quispe-hago-marketing-pol%C3%ADtico-y-la-gente-me-conoce-mejor |url-status=live |access-date=5 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220805175149/https://urgente.bo/noticia/rafael-quispe-hago-marketing-pol%C3%ADtico-y-la-gente-me-conoce-mejor |archive-date=5 August 2022}}</ref>{{efn-lg|Per indigenous custom, the Aymara realm ([[Qullasuyu]]) is divided between ''[[Suyu (Inca Empire)|suyus]]'' ([[Provinces of Bolivia|provinces]]) made up of ''[[wiktionary:marka#Quechua|markas]]'' (regions), agglomerations of multiple autonomous ''[[ayllu]]s'', the [[Collective ownership|collective landholdings]] of the Aymara. They are represented by a ''[[mallku]]'' ([[Tribal chief|traditional leader]]) elected through ''[[usos y costumbres]]''.<ref name=":NACLA-2010-09-02">{{Cite news |last=Weinberg |first=Bill |author-link=Bill Weinberg |date=2 September 2010 |title=Beyond Extraction: An Interview With Rafael Quispe |language=en |work=[[NACLA Report on the Americas]] |publisher=[[Routledge]] for the [[North American Congress on Latin America]] |publication-place=New York City |url=https://nacla.org/article/beyond-extraction-interview-rafael-quispe |url-status=live |access-date=5 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120306113429/https://nacla.org/article/beyond-extraction-interview-rafael-quispe |archive-date=6 March 2012}}</ref>}} He is the youngest of eight siblings born to Manuela Flores; their father, a local [[miner]], died when he was 18. Raised in [[rural poverty]], Quispe completed [[Secondary education|lower secondary education]] in [[Coro Coro, Bolivia|Coro Coro]] before dropping out to focus on [[Pastoral farming|cattle grazing]] and [[Shepherd|sheepherding]]. As a young adult, he entered the [[Armed Forces of Bolivia|Armed Forces]] to fulfill his period of [[Conscription|mandatory military service]]. Despite being an exceptional serviceman, Quispe was precluded from attaining the rank of [[corporal]] due to his lack of a high school diploma, a fact that motivated him to complete his education. He moved to [[El Alto]], graduating from the Center for Accelerated Secondary Education before going on to work in a variety of [[Blue-collar worker|blue-collar]] fields, including as a [[Shoemaking|cobbler]], mechanic, and [[Vehicle for hire|service driver]]. During this time, Quispe intermittently studied law at multiple institutions, including the [[Higher University of San Andrés]], before settling at the University of Valle, though he ultimately did not finish his degree.<ref name=":ERBOL-2013-11-04">{{Cite news |date=4 November 2013 |title=Quispe: No creo que lleguemos al poder este 2014 |language=es |trans-title=Quispe: I Don't Think We'll Arrive to Power This 2014 |agency=ERBOL |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://anteriorportal.erbol.com.bo/noticia/indigenas/04112013/quispe_no_creo_que_lleguemos_al_poder_este_2014 |url-status=live |access-date=5 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200907005502/https://anteriorportal.erbol.com.bo/noticia/indigenas/04112013/quispe_no_creo_que_lleguemos_al_poder_este_2014 |archive-date=7 September 2020}}</ref><ref name="PS-2021-02-07">{{Cite news |last=Romero |first=Daniela |date=7 February 2021 |title='Tata' Quispe: 'Yo llego a la clase media y a los indios, es mi fortaleza' |language=es |trans-title="Tata" Quispe: "I Reach the Middle Class and the Indians; It's My Strength" |work=[[Página Siete]] |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://www.paginasiete.bo/especial02/2021/2/7/tata-quispe-yo-llego-la-clase-media-los-indios-es-mi-fortaleza-283643.html |url-status=dead |access-date=5 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210228090234/https://www.paginasiete.bo/especial02/2021/2/7/tata-quispe-yo-llego-la-clase-media-los-indios-es-mi-fortaleza-283643.html |archive-date=28 February 2021}}</ref>

(51915421980).jpg|thumb|left|290x290px|As a member of CONAMAQ, Quispe gained notoriety for his vocal criticism of the government, particularly concerning its environmental policy and fraught relationship with indigenous communities.|alt=Rafael Quispe, dressed in indigenous garb, holds up a copy of the Bolivian Constitution.]]

Returning to Coro Coro, in 2000, Quispe, together with his mother, assumed a position of local leadership within his ''ayllu''. He held prominent posts in the region until 2005, being named ''[[mallku]]'' of his ''marka'' and later ''mallku'' of the entire ''suyu''. In the [[2002 Bolivian general election|2002 general election]], he threw his weight behind the [[Pachakuti Indigenous Movement]], supporting the presidential campaign of [[Felipe Quispe]]. Following the election of [[Gonzalo Sánchez de Lozada]], Quispe was an active opposition leader, taking part in protests in El Alto during the [[Bolivian gas conflict|2003 gas conflict]]. Quispe's rise within [[Indigenismo|indigenist]] circles culminated in his 2010 election as a member of the governing board of the [[National Council of Ayllus and Markas of Qullasuyu]] (CONAMAQ), serving as ''mallku'' of the organization's Extractive Industries Commission. Upon the conclusion of his term, he served as CONAMAQ's representative at the Andean Coordinator of Indigenous Organizations, an [[international organization]] with presence in six countries and consultative status with the [[United Nations]].<ref name=":ERBOL-2013-11-04"/><ref>{{Cite news |date=4 August 2014 |title=¿Quién es quién?: Rafael Arcángel Quispe Flores |language=es |trans-title=Who's Who?: Rafael Arcángel Quispe Flores |work=[[El Diario (La Paz)|El Diario]] |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://www.pub.eldiario.net/noticias/2014/2014_08/nt140804/politica.php?n=71&-rafael-quispe-flores |url-status=live |access-date=5 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220806061052/https://www.pub.eldiario.net/noticias/2014/2014_08/nt140804/politica.php?n=71&-rafael-quispe-flores |archive-date=6 August 2022}}</ref>

Originally a supporter of President [[Evo Morales]], Quispe later soured on the indigenous leader. In particular, he accused Morales's policies of failing to reflect his [[environmentalist]] rhetoric, citing the president's support for [[Extractivism|extractive industries]] such as [[mining]] and [[Petroleum industry|petroleum]]. In an interview with [[Bill Weinberg]] for the ''[[NACLA Report on the Americas]]'', Quispe denounced the government's environmental policy as a "systematic violation of the rights of the peoples and of the [[Pachamama]]," accusing Morales of being "the foremost violator of [[Mother Nature|Mother Earth]]."<ref name=":NACLA-2010-09-02" /> Quispe's active opposition to the Morales administration gained him national notoriety in 2011 when he became one of the primary leaders of the [[2011 Bolivian indigenous rights protests|indigenous march]] in defense of the [[Isiboro Sécure National Park and Indigenous Territory]], which protested the government's attempt to construct a [[Route 24 (Bolivia)|highway]] running through the [[protected area]] without prior consultation with the native population.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Kenner |first=Dario |date=21 September 2011 |title=Bolivia: The TIPNIS conflict in-depth |work=Latin America Bureau |location=La Paz |publication-place=London |url=https://lab.org.uk/bolivia-the-tipnis-conflict-in-depth-part-two/ |url-status=live |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210805212616/https://lab.org.uk/bolivia-the-tipnis-conflict-in-depth-part-two/ |archive-date=5 August 2021 }}</ref>

== Chamber of Deputies ==
=== Election ===
{{Further|2014 Bolivian general election}}
By late 2013, Quispe, having risen to national prominence, sought to position himself as an indigenous alternative to the ruling [[Movement for Socialism]] (MAS-IPSP), with his name emerging as a potential candidate for the presidency on behalf of CONAMAQ. Despite stating that he would accept heading the ticket if nominated, Quispe admitted that his chances of reaching the presidency were negligible. Nonetheless, he outlined that his front's main goal was not the presidency but rather to attain independent indigenous representation in the [[Plurinational Legislative Assembly]].<ref name=":ERBOL-2013-11-04" />

In November, Quispe's faction of CONAMAQ brokered a political agreement with the [[Green Party of Bolivia|Green Party]] (PVB-IEP) to jointly contest the 2014 general elections.<ref>{{Cite news |date=28 November 2013 |title=El Partido Verde y CONAMAQ firman acuerdo político rumbo a las elecciones |language=es |trans-title=The Green Party and CONAMAQ Sign a Political Accord in View of the Elections |work=[[Opinión]] |publication-place=Cochabamba |url=https://www.opinion.com.bo/articulo/el-pais/partido-verde-conamaq-firman-acuerdo-politico-rumbo-elecciones/20131128113100455301.html |url-status=live |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220807034919/https://www.opinion.com.bo/articulo/el-pais/partido-verde-conamaq-firman-acuerdo-politico-rumbo-elecciones/20131128113100455301.html |archive-date=7 August 2022}}</ref> To this was added the "freethinkers," a faction of MAS dissidents led by former [[Chamber of Deputies (Bolivia)|Chamber of Deputies]] president [[Rebeca Delgado]]. However, internal disputes over the coalition's presidential ballot quickly fractured the alliance, with the "freethinkers" pushing Delgado for the presidency while CONAMAQ lobbied for Quispe to head the ticket.<ref>{{Cite news |date=29 October 2013 |title=¿Rebeca o Quispe?: CONAMAQ, CIDOB y librepensantes no se ponen de acuerdo |language=es |trans-title=Rebeca or Quispe?: CONAMAQ, CIDOB, and the Freethinkers Disagree |agency=ERBOL |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://eju.tv/2013/10/rebeca-o-quispe-conamaq-cidob-y-librepensantes-no-se-ponen-de-acuerdo/ |url-status=live |access-date=5 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220807034927/https://eju.tv/2013/10/rebeca-o-quispe-conamaq-cidob-y-librepensantes-no-se-ponen-de-acuerdo/ |archive-date=7 August 2022 |via=eju!}}</ref> In February 2014, Quispe broke CONAMAQ's pact with the PVB, opting instead to sign an alliance with [[Samuel Doria Medina]]'s [[National Unity Front]] (UN). As part of their five-point agreement, Doria Medina pledged to give indigenous candidates a twenty percent share on the alliance's [[electoral list]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=26 February 2014 |title=Rafael Quispe se alía al Frente Amplio |language=es |trans-title=Rafael Quispe Aligns with the Broad Front |work=[[Los Tiempos]] |publication-place=Cochabamba |url=https://www.lostiempos.com/actualidad/nacional/20140226/rafael-quispe-se-alia-al-frente-amplio |url-status=live |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220807034938/https://www.lostiempos.com/actualidad/nacional/20140226/rafael-quispe-se-alia-al-frente-amplio |archive-date=7 August 2022}}</ref>

As a means of consolidating a single presidential ticket, UN and its allies presented five pre-candidates as possible contenders for the nomination, including Quispe. The winner of this makeshift [[Partisan primary|primary election]] was selected through a nationwide poll conducted between 19 and 20 April. On 21 April, Doria Medina was presented as the winning candidate, attaining the preference of sixty-nine percent of the 2,652 individuals surveyed. Unofficial data released by [[La Razón (La Paz)|''La Razón'']] placed Quispe in third place with ten percent of the vote.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Vacaflor |first=Nancy |date=1 April 2014 |title=Frente Amplio presenta cinco precandidatos presidenciales |language=es |trans-title=The Broad Fronts Presents Five Presidential Pre-candidates |work=[[Página Siete]] |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://www.paginasiete.bo/nacional/frente-amplio-presenta-cinco-precandidatos-presidenciales-DKPS17702 |url-status=live |url-access=subscription |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140403231049/http://www.paginasiete.bo:80/nacional/2014/4/1/frente-amplio-presenta-cinco-precandidatos-presidenciales-17702.html |archive-date=2014-04-03}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |last=Ariñez |first=Rubén |date=29 April 2014 |title=El candidato del Frente Amplio es Doria Medina |language=es |trans-title=Doria Medina Is the Broad Front's Candidate |work=[[La Razón (La Paz)|La Razón]] |publication-place=La Paz |url=http://www.la-razon.com/nacional/Elecciones-candidato-Frente_Amplio-Doria_Medina_0_2042795750.html |url-status=dead |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170801073305/http://www.la-razon.com/nacional/Elecciones-candidato-Frente_Amplio-Doria_Medina_0_2042795750.html |archive-date=1 August 2017}}</ref> In the ensuing weeks, Quispe was presented on the coalition's preliminary electoral list as a possible candidate for substitute senator together with [[Soledad Chapetón]]. However, Quispe rejected the nomination, maintaining that he was unwilling to be the running mate of someone "who did not fight for the demands of the country's indigenous movement."<ref>{{Cite news |date=8 July 2014 |title=Rafael Quispe no quiere ser senador suplente de Chapetón |language=es |trans-title=Rafael Quispe Refuses to Be Chapetón's Substitute |agency=ERBOL |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://anteriorportal.erbol.com.bo/noticia/indigenas/08072014/quispe_rechaza_ser_senador_suplente_de_chapeton |url-status=live |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220807035356/https://anteriorportal.erbol.com.bo/noticia/indigenas/08072014/quispe_rechaza_ser_senador_suplente_de_chapeton |archive-date=7 August 2022 |quote=Quispe dijo que no acepta ser suplente de una persona que no luchó por las reivindicaciones del movimiento indígena del país.}}</ref> He also expressed his discontent with the lack of indigenous presence on the final list of candidates, threatening to withdraw CONAMAQ's support if the issue was not rectified.<ref>{{Cite news |date=8 July 2014 |title=Rafael Quispe no quiere ser senador suplente de Chapetón |language=es |trans-title=Rafael Quispe Refuses to Be Chapetón's Substitute |agency=ERBOL |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://anteriorportal.erbol.com.bo/noticia/indigenas/08072014/quispe_rechaza_ser_senador_suplente_de_chapeton |url-status=live |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220807035356/https://anteriorportal.erbol.com.bo/noticia/indigenas/08072014/quispe_rechaza_ser_senador_suplente_de_chapeton |archive-date=7 August 2022}}</ref>

Ultimately, Quispe was nominated as a candidate for deputy, placing fifth on the [[Democratic Unity]] (UD) coalition's electoral list.<ref>{{Cite news |year=2014 |title=Listado de Candidatos y Candidatas {{!}} Elecciones Generales 2014 |language=es |pages=8 |work=[[Plurinational Electoral Organ]] |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://www.oep.org.bo/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/ud_candidatos_2014.pdf |url-status=live |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211229014523/https://www.oep.org.bo/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/ud_candidatos_2014.pdf |archive-date=29 December 2021}}</ref> UD's mediocre performance in the La Paz Department garnered it just four party-list deputies, precluding Quispe from entering the chamber. However, in November, UD requested that the [[Supreme Electoral Tribunal of Bolivia|Supreme Electoral Tribunal]] (TSE) authorize Quispe to serve as a substitute deputy, filling the vacancy left by [[María Eugenia Calcina]], who assumed the primary position due to Jaime Navarro's last-minute withdrawal from the race.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Rojas |first=Christian |date=1 September 2014 |title=Rafael Quispe pide la renuncia de Jaime Navarro a la UD tras polémica por el audio |language=es |trans-title=Rafael Quispe Calls for Jaime Navarro's Resignation from UD After Audio Controversy |agency=Oxígeno.bo |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://oxigeno.bo/3665 |url-status=live |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220807035505/https://oxigeno.bo/3665 |archive-date=7 August 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=12 November 2014 |title=UD solicita al TSE que habilite a Rafael Quispe como diputado suplente |language=es |trans-title=UD Requests That the TSE Authorize Rafael Quispe as a Substitute Deputy |agency=[[Agencia de Noticias Fides]] |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://www.noticiasfides.com/nacional/politica/ud-solicita-al-tse-que-habilite-a-rafael-quispe-como-diputado-suplente-347821-347805 |url-status=live |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220807035555/https://www.noticiasfides.com/nacional/politica/ud-solicita-al-tse-que-habilite-a-rafael-quispe-como-diputado-suplente-347821-347805 |archive-date=7 August 2022}}</ref>

{{Main|2021 La Paz gubernatorial election}}

== Ideology and personal life ==
Quispe has defined himself as a figure without political origins, "neither left nor right [nor] center."<ref>{{Cite news |date=25 February 2019 |title=Rafael Quispe: Hago marketing político y la gente me conoce mejor |language=es |trans-title=Rafael Quispe: By Doing Political Marketing, the People Know Me Better |agency=Urgente.bo |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://urgente.bo/noticia/rafael-quispe-hago-marketing-pol%C3%ADtico-y-la-gente-me-conoce-mejor |url-status=live |access-date=5 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220805175149/https://urgente.bo/noticia/rafael-quispe-hago-marketing-pol%C3%ADtico-y-la-gente-me-conoce-mejor |archive-date=5 August 2022 |quote=...{{nbsp}}yo no tengo orígenes políticos ni de izquierda ni derecha y centro.}}</ref> An opponent of both [[capitalism]] and [[socialism]]—which he described as effectively "the same" for their "extractive, [[Consumerism|consumerist]], [and] [[Developmentalism|developmentalist]]" principals—Quispe has advocated for an indigenous [[Communitarianism|communitarian]] development model. He has promoted moving the country's economy away from its dependence on extractive industries, supporting a push towards [[Sustainable energy|clean energy]] within the framework of "respect for our Pachamama."<ref name=":NACLA-2010-09-02" /> In 2010, he lobbied the government to replace the [[Ministry of Environment and Water (Bolivia)|Ministry of Environment and Water]] with a "Ministry of Pachamama," stating that it was the "only way to defend the rights of [Mother Earth]."<ref>{{Cite news |date=22 January 2010 |title=Indígenas le piden crear el Ministerio de la Pachamama |language=es |trans-title=Indigenous Peoples Ask Evo to Create the Ministry of Pachamama |work=[[Opinión]] |agency=ERBOL |publication-place=Cochabamba |url=https://www.opinion.com.bo/articulo/tendencias/indigenas-piden-crear-ministerio-pachamama/20100122191939341664.html |url-status=live |access-date=6 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220806164000/https://www.opinion.com.bo/articulo/tendencias/indigenas-piden-crear-ministerio-pachamama/20100122191939341664.html |archive-date=6 August 2022}}</ref>

As with most modern Aymara,<ref>{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Uy8OAQAAMAAJ |title=Encyclopedia of the World's Minorities |publisher=[[Routledge]] |year=2015 |isbn=978-1-57958-468-9 |editor-last=Skutsch |editor-first=Carl |volume=1 |location=New York and London |pages=160 |language=en |editor-last2=Ryle |editor-first2=Martin}}</ref> Quispe practices a [[Religious syncretism|syncretic]] form of [[Christianity]] infused with Aymara indigenous beliefs. He has expressed faith in both [[God in Christianity|God]] and Pachamama, stating that "leaving the house, we pray to God, but at the moment when you pass a large mountain{{Nbsp}}... we pray to Pachamama."<ref>{{Cite news |last=Baldivieso |first=Gina |date=1 December 2019 |title=Un aimara boliviano afirma que Evo Morales mantuvo estancados a los indígenas |language=es |trans-title=A Bolivian Aymara Affirms That Evo Morales Kept the Indigenous Peoples Stagnant |agency=[[EFE]] |location=La Paz |publication-place=Madrid |url=https://www.efe.com/efe/america/politica/un-aimara-boliviano-afirma-que-evo-morales-mantuvo-estancados-a-los-indigenas/20000035-4123235 |url-status=dead |access-date=13 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201112014814/https://www.efe.com/efe/america/politica/un-aimara-boliviano-afirma-que-evo-morales-mantuvo-estancados-a-los-indigenas/20000035-4123235 |archive-date=12 November 2020 |quote=Saliendo de la casa, pedimos a Dios, pero en el momento en que pasas por un cerro grande{{nbsp}}... le pedimos a la Pachamama.}}</ref> In one viral instance, Quispe famously stated: "I fear only God... and my wife."<ref>{{Cite news |date=11 December 2018 |title='Miedo, solo a Dios... y a mi esposa', la respuesta viral del diputado Rafael Quispe |language=es |trans-title="I Fear Only God… and My Wife," Deputy Rafael Quispe's Viral Response |work=[[El Deber]] |publication-place=Santa Cruz de la Sierra |url=https://eldeber.com.bo/extra/miedo-solo-a-dios-y-a-mi-esposa-la-respuesta-viral-del-diputado-rafael-quispe_32384 |url-status=live |url-access=registration |access-date=13 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220813222309/https://eldeber.com.bo/extra/miedo-solo-a-dios-y-a-mi-esposa-la-respuesta-viral-del-diputado-rafael-quispe_32384 |archive-date=13 August 2022}}</ref> Quispe is married to Rocío Patty, with whom he has lived in El Alto for over twenty years, together with their two children. According to Quispe, it was his wife who kept their family afloat during his tenure in CONAMAQ, while he continued to make an income working as a service driver during that time. He has criticized political leaders for leaving public office much richer than when they entered.<ref name=":ERBOL-2013-11-04" /> Quispe has also been noted for his connection to El Alto's [[nightclub]] scene, having previously led the Association of Bars, Canteens, and Restaurants of El Alto. In 2015, Deputy María Eugenia Calcina accused him of being the owner of a network of [[Bar (establishment)|bars]] and [[brothel]]s in the city, a concept Quispe vehemently rejected as a politically motivated attack against his character.<ref>{{Cite news |date=8 October 2015 |title=Diputada de UN dice que Rafael Quispe sería propietario de bares y lenocinios |language=es |trans-title=UN Deputy Alleges That Rafael Quispe Is the Owner of Bars and Brothels |agency=[[Agencia de Noticias Fides]] |publication-place=La Paz |url=https://www.noticiasfides.com/nacional/politica/diputada-de-un-dice-que-rafael-quispe-seria-propietario-de-bares-y-lenocinios-357887-357839 |url-status=live |access-date=13 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200828064521/https://www.noticiasfides.com/nacional/politica/diputada-de-un-dice-que-rafael-quispe-seria-propietario-de-bares-y-lenocinios-357887-357839 |archive-date=28 August 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=2 June 2019 |title=Quispe rechaza denuncia de ser dueño de lenocinios |language=es |trans-title=Quispe Rejects Accusation That He Is the Owner of Brothels |work=[[El Deber]] |publication-place=Santa Cruz de la Sierra |url=https://eldeber.com.bo/bolivia/quispe-rechaza-denuncia-de-ser-dueno-de-lenocinios_145604 |url-status=live |url-access=registration |access-date=13 August 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220813222529/https://eldeber.com.bo/bolivia/quispe-rechaza-denuncia-de-ser-dueno-de-lenocinios_145604 |archive-date=13 August 2022}}</ref>
