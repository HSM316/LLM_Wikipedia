{{Year in Morocco |2020}}

Events in the year '''2020 in [[Morocco]]'''.

==Incumbents==
* [[List of rulers of Morocco|King]]: [[Mohammed VI of Morocco|Mohammed VI]]
* [[President of the Government of Morocco|President of the Government]]: [[Saadeddine Othmani]]

==Events==
{{See also|COVID-19 pandemic in Morocco}}

=== January ===
* January 24 – Several organizations in [[Ecuador]] express their support for the rights of the Sahrawi people.<ref>[https://allafrica.com/stories/202001260004.html Western Sahara: Several Ecuadorian Organizations Reiterate Support for Right of Sahrawi People to Self-Determination] allAfrica, 24 Jan 2020, retrieved 11 Feb 2020</ref>
* January 28 – February 7 – [[2020 Africa Futsal Cup of Nations]]: Morocco was the defending champion and successfully managed to defend their title after beating [[Egypt]] 5–0 in the final.{{Citation needed|date=October 2020}}

=== February ===
* February 9 – The [[African Union]] reaffirms its support for self-determination for the [[Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic]].<ref>[https://allafrica.com/stories/202002100479.html Western Sahara: South African President Reiterates Support for Right of Sahrawi People to Self-Determination and Independence] allAfrica, 11 Feb 2020</ref>
* February 17 – Morocco tightens its control over the Spanish cities of [[Ceuta]] and [[Melilla]].<ref>[https://www.elespanol.com/espana/politica/20200216/marruecos-estrangula-ceuta-melilla-cortando-fronteras-sanchez/467703295_0.html Morocco strangles Ceuta and Melilla,  cutting borders to take advantage of Sánchez] {{in lang|es}} El Español, 17 Feb 2020</ref>
* February 22 to February 23: [[2020 African Taekwondo Olympic Qualification Tournament]], Rabat.{{Citation needed|date=October 2020}}
* February 29 – [[2020 Marrakesh ePrix]], [[Marrakesh]].{{Citation needed|date=October 2020}}

=== March ===
* March 2 – First confirmed case of coronavirus in the country.{{Citation needed|date=October 2020}}
* March 10 – First death from coronavirus, a French tourist<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://lematin.ma/express/2020/maroc-annonce-premier-cas-deces-nouveau-coronavirus/333247.html|title=Le Matin - Le Maroc annonce le premier cas de décès du nouveau coronavirus|last=MATIN|first=M. Se, LE|website=Le Matin|language=fr|access-date=30 March 2020|trans-title=Morning - Morocco announces first case of death from new coronavirus}}</ref>
* March 12 – At least 18 African countries, including Morocco, report cases of the novel [[Timeline of the 2019–20 coronavirus pandemic in March 2020|coronavirus]]. Most are in single figures, but five deaths have been reported in North Africa.<ref>[https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/03/coronavirus-spreads-african-countries-200313153229327.html Coronavirus spreads to more African countries] Al Jazeera, 12 March 2020</ref>
* March 13 – The government shuts down all schools, effective March 16 until further notice.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://www.mapnews.ma/en/actualites/general/courses-suspended-morocco-march-16-until-further-notice|title=Courses Suspended in Morocco from March 16 Until Further Notice|date=13 March 2020|work=[[Maghreb Arabe Press]]|access-date=30 March 2020}}</ref>
* March 17 – Journalist and rights activist [[Omar Radi]], 33, is given a four-month suspended sentence and a U $50 fine on charges of insulting a judge on [[Twitter]], in a case that outrages advocates of free speech.<ref>{{cite web |title=Morocco sentences activist for criticising 'judiciary' |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/03/morocco-sentences-activist-criticising-judiciary-200316131446326.html |website=www.aljazeera.com |accessdate=June 3, 2020}}</ref>
* March 19 – The country declares a [[State of emergency|state of medical emergency]] beginning on March 20, [[2020 in North Africa|2020]] and remaining in effect until April 20, 2020 with the possibility of extending.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.moroccoworldnews.com/2020/03/296213/covid-19-morocco-declares-public-health-emergency/|title=COVID-19: Morocco Declares State of Emergency|date=2020-03-19|website=Morocco World News|language=en-US|access-date=2020-03-30}}</ref>

=== May ===
* May 14 – [[2020 in Algeria|Algeria]] summons the country's ambassador to [[Algiers]] in protest against when the Moroccan consul in [[Oran]] purportedly referred to Algeria as an "enemy country."<ref>{{cite web |title=Algeria summons Moroccan ambassador over 'enemy country' remark |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/05/algeria-summons-moroccan-ambassador-enemy-country-remark-200514181909333.html |website=www.aljazeera.com |accessdate=June 3, 2020}}</ref>

=== June ===
* June 26 – The government of Morocco "categorically denies" an [[Amnesty International]] report that they used surveillance software to spy on the phone of a prominent journalist and human rights activist Omar Radi.<ref>{{cite web |title=Moroccan authorities deny using spyware to monitor critics |url=https://abcnews.go.com/International/wireStory/moroccan-authorities-deny-spyware-monitor-critics-71489891 |website=ABC News |accessdate=June 27, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>

=== September ===
* September 4 – [[Netherlands|Dutch]] right-wing extremist and leader of the [[Party for Freedom]] (PVV), [[Geert Wilders]] is acquitted of a hate crime despite his "unnecessarily offensive" call in 2014 for "fewer Moroccans."<ref>{{cite web |title=Wilders, absuelto de incitar al odio al pedir "menos marroquíes" en Países Bajos |url=https://www.msn.com/es-mx/noticias/espana/wilders-absuelto-de-incitar-al-odio-al-pedir-menos-marroqu%C3%ADes-en-pa%C3%ADses-bajos/ar-BB18IkNC?ocid=msedgdhp |website=www.msn.com |publisher=EFE |accessdate=September 4, 2020}}</ref>

===November===
* November 15 – A 30-year cease-fire between Morocco and [[Polisario Front]] forces is broken as the government tries to open a road in the [[Guerguerat]] buffer zone near the border with Mauritania.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Brennan |first1=David |title=Fighting in Morocco May Present Biden with Fresh Africa Crisis Amid COVID Surge |url=https://www.msn.com/en-us/news/world/fighting-in-morocco-may-present-biden-with-fresh-africa-crisis-amid-covid-surge/ar-BB1b1Yfg |accessdate=November 15, 2020 |work=msn.com |publisher=Newsweek |date=November 15, 2020}}</ref>

===December===
* December 11 – Morocco normalizes relations with [[Israel]] and in return U.S. President Trump acknowledges Morocco's sovereignty over the Western Sahara.<ref>{{cite news |last1=LAVALLÉE |first1=Guillaume |title=Trump hace un regalo a Marruecos al reconocer su soberanía sobre el Sáhara Occidental |url=https://espanol.yahoo.com/noticias/trump-regalo-marruecos-reconocer-soberanía-124409656.html |access-date=December 11, 2020 |work=es-us.noticias.yahoo.com |agency=AFP |publisher=Yahoo News |date=December 11, 2020 |language=es-US}}</ref> The deal also includes US$1 billion in military aid.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Zengerle |first1=Patricia |last2=Stone |first2=Mike |title=Exclusive-Trump administration moves forward with $1 billion Moroccan arms deal |url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/exclusive-trump-administration-moves-forward-215412898.html |access-date=December 12, 2020 |work=news.yahoo.com |agency=Reuters |publisher=Yahoo News |date=December 11, 2020}}</ref>
* December 16 – [[Couscous]] is added to the [[UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage Lists]].<ref>{{cite news |title=UNESCO adds couscous to list of intangible world heritage |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/12/16/unesco-adds-couscous-to-list-of-intangible-world-heritage |access-date=December 17, 2020 |work=aljazeera.com |publisher=Al Jazeera English |date=December 16, 2020 |language=en}}</ref>
* December 17 – Moroccan Ayoub El Khazzani is sentenced to life in prison for planning the [[2015 Thalys train attack]] in France.<ref>{{cite news |title=French court jails Moroccan for life over foiled 2015 train attack |url=https://www.msn.com/en-ca/news/world/french-court-jails-moroccan-for-life-over-foiled-2015-train-attack/ar-BB1c0IoJ |access-date=December 17, 2020 |work=msn.com |agency=AFP |date=December 17, 2020}}</ref>
* December 31 – Historian and human rights activist [[Maati Monjib]] is arrested for [[money laundering]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Morocco detains rights activist for alleged money laundering |url=https://apnews.com/article/morocco-money-laundering-rabat-c0a24f7b7bbb960215328495fbb4eddd |access-date=January 1, 2021 |work=AP NEWS |date=31 December 2020}}</ref>
