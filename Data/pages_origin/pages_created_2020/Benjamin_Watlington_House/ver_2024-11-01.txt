The '''Benjamin Watlington House''', at 206 W. Court St. in [[Weiser, Idaho]], was built in 1890.  It was listed on the [[National Register of Historic Places]] in 1991.<ref name=nris/>

It is a two-story wood-frame house which was deemed "architecturally important as an example of the [[Queen Anne architecture in the United States|Queen Anne style]] and as the work of [[James King (architect)|James King]], Idaho's first architect with professional training."<ref name="nrhpdoc">{{cite web|url={{NRHP url|id=91000458}}|title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: Benjamin Watlington House / Steve and Cheri Clausen House; 013844 |publisher=[[National Park Service]]|author=Cheri Clausen |author2=Elizabeth Egleston |date=October 3, 1990 |accessdate=December 30, 2019}} With {{NRHP url|id=91000458|photos=y|title=accompanying four photos from 1990}}</ref>
