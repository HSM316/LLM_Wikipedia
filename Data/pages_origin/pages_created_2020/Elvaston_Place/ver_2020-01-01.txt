'''Elvaston Place''' is a street in [[South Kensington]], London.

Elvaston Place runs west to east from [[Gloucester Road]] to [[Queen's Gate]].

Much of the street, 1-20 and 32-46, was built by the porperty developer [[Charles Aldin]] in the early 1860s.<ref name="Survey of London">{{cite web |title=Domestic Buildings after 1851: The Italianate Tradition |url=https://www.british-history.ac.uk/survey-london/vol38/pp262-307 |website=Survey of London |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref> 26-31 were built in 1866-68.<ref name="Survey of London"/>

From 1 January 1968 until at least May 1969, the musician [[Al Stewart]] lived in a basement flat at number 10, and his song ''Elvaston Place'' is about his time there.<ref name="Songfacts">{{cite web |title=Elvaston Place by Al Stewart |url=https://www.songfacts.com/facts/al-stewart/elvaston-place |website=Songfacts |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref>
