'''Mabo Ismaila''' (15 July 1944 – 13 March 2023) was a Nigerian [[Manager (association football)|football manager]] and international footballer.<ref>{{WorldFootball.net}}</ref>

==Career==
Ismaila was the head coach of the [[Nigeria women's national football team|Nigeria women's national team]] at the [[1999 FIFA Women's World Cup]], [[Football at the 2000 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2000 Summer Olympics]] and [[Football at the 2004 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2004 Summer Olympics]]. He led Nigeria to the quarter-finals of the World Cup, the team's best ever result. Before then, Mabo played for Nigeria national team, then known as the Green Eagles. He was the captain of the  great Mighty Jet of Jos. In the team, he played along players such as his younger brother Uba Junior, Sule Kekere, Jide Dina, Alloysius Atuegbu, Lawandi Layiwola Olagbemiro, Baba Otu Mohammed and Sam Garba Okoye.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1999/06/23/sports/women-s-world-cup-flamboyant-nigeria-plays-exuberantly.html |title=WOMEN'S WORLD CUP; Flamboyant Nigeria Plays Exuberantly |date=23 June 1999 |website=New York Times |access-date=27 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://punchng.com/falcons-loss-to-ghana-not-a-surprise-mabo/ |title=Falcons loss to Ghana, not a surprise – Mabo |website=Punch |access-date=27 May 2018}}</ref>

== Personal life and death ==
Ismaila confirmed that he was ill to ''[[The Punch]]'' in January 2023, following the death of his wife.<ref>{{cite news |title=Ex-Falcons coach Mabo dies at 78 |url=https://punchng.com/ex-falcons-coach-mabo-dies-at-78/ |access-date=14 March 2023 |publisher=Punch |date=13 March 2023}}</ref> He died on 13 March 2023, at the age of 78.<ref>[https://www.premiumtimesng.com/sports/football/587463-former-super-falcons-coach-ismaila-mabo-is-dead.html Former Super Falcons coach Ismaila Mabo is dead]</ref>
