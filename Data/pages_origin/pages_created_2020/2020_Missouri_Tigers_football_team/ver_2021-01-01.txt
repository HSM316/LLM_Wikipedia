{{2020 Southeastern Conference football standings}}
The '''2020 Missouri Tigers football team''' represented the [[University of Missouri]] in the [[2020 NCAA Division I FBS football season]]. The Tigers played their home games at [[Faurot Field]] in [[Columbia, Missouri]], and competed in the Eastern Division of the [[Southeastern Conference]] (SEC). They were led by first-year head coach [[Eliah Drinkwitz]].

In a season impacted by the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], the Tigers compiled a 5–5 record, all in conference games. The team was set to face [[2020 Iowa Hawkeyes football team|Iowa]] in the [[Music City Bowl]], but had to withdraw due to COVID-19 issues.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.cbssports.com/college-football/news/2020-music-city-bowl-canceled-as-covid-19-outbreak-forces-missouri-to-pull-out-of-game-vs-iowa/ |title=2020 Music City Bowl canceled as COVID-19 outbreak forces Missouri to pull out of game vs. Iowa |first=David |last=Cobb |website=[[CBS Sports]] |date=December 27, 2020 |accessdate=December 29, 2020}}</ref>

==Previous season==
The Tigers finished the [[2019 Missouri Tigers football team|2019 season]] 6–6, 3–5 in SEC play to finish in a tie for fourth place in the Eastern Division. Head coach [[Barry Odom]] was fired on November 30, 2019 following an overall four-year record of 25–25 and SEC record of 13–19.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mutigers.com/news/2019/11/30/statement-on-the-mizzou-football-program.aspx|title=STATEMENT ON THE MIZZOU FOOTBALL PROGRAM|date=November 30, 2019|newspaper=Missouri Athletics}}</ref> [[Eliah Drinkwitz]] was hired on December 10 following a 12–1 conference championship season at [[2019 Appalachian State Mountaineers football team|Appalachian State]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mutigers.com/news/2019/12/10/eliah-drinkwitz-chosen-as-mizzous-head-football-coach.aspx|title=Eliah Drinkwitz Chosen as Mizzou’s Head Football Coach|date=December 10, 2019|newspaper=Missouri Athletics}}</ref>
