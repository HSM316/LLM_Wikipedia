'''Sharon Pardo''' (born 17 February 1971<ref>[http://in.bgu.ac.il/humsos/politics/Documents/StaffCV/SharonPardoCV2017.pdf Curriculum Vitae, August 2017].</ref>) is an [[Israelis|Israeli]] Professor of [[International relations|International Relations]] and a ''Jean Monnet Chair<ref>See European Commission: [https://ec.europa.eu/programmes/erasmus-plus/opportunities/jean-monnet-chair_en Jean Monnet Chairs].</ref> ad personam in [[European studies| European Studies]]'' at [[Ben-Gurion University of the Negev]].

== Biography ==
Pardo was born in [[Haifa]]. He received his [[Bachelor of Laws | LL.B.]] (1996) and his [[Master of Laws | LL.M.]] (1997) from the [[University of Sheffield|University of Sheffield's]] School of Law, and his [[Doctor of Philosophy | Ph.D.]] (2003) from [[Ghent University]], Faculty of Political and Social Studies. His doctoral thesis dealt with “The Euro-Mediterranean Partnership: From European Unilateralism to Regional Regime.”

Pardo is a senior researcher at The [[Simone Veil]] Research Centre for Contemporary European Studies – The National Jean Monnet Centre of Excellence at [[Ben-Gurion University of the Negev]] (BGU).<ref name="bio">{{cite web |title=Prof. Sharon Pardo |url=https://in.bgu.ac.il/en/humsos/politics/pages/staff/sharon_pardo.aspx |website=in.bgu.ac.il |publisher=Department of Politics and Government, Ben-Gurion University of the Negev |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref>

Pardo served as the chairperson of the Department of Politics and Government at BGU from 2016 to 2018. He is a senior adjunct fellow at the [[National Centre for Research on Europe]] (NCRE),<ref name="bio"/> [[University of Canterbury]], [[New Zealand]], and a member of the board of the [[Israeli Association for International Studies]] (IAIS).

Pardo is a member of the board of the [[Israel Council on Foreign Relations]] (ICFR), a member of the International Advisory Council of the [[Konrad Adenauer Foundation]],<ref>''[https://in.bgu.ac.il/en/pages/news/pardo-KAS.aspx Prof. Sharon Pardo Invited to Join the German Konrad Adenauer Foundation’s International Advisory Council],'' [[Ben-Gurion University of the Negev|BGU]] notification, 29 November 2015.</ref> and a Member of the [[Israel Bar Association]].<ref name="bio"/> He is the co-editor of ''Europe and the World'' book series by Lexington Books.<ref>{{cite web |title=Europe and the World |publisher= Rowman & Littlefield |url=https://rowman.com/Action/SERIES/_/LEXEAW/Europe-and-the-World |website=rowman.com |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref>
