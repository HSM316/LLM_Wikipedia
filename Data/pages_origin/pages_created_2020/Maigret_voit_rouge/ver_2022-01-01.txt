'''''Maigret voit rouge''''' (English: '''''Maigret Sees Red''''') is a 1963 [[Cinema of France|French]]-[[Cinema of Italy|Italian]] [[crime film]] directed by [[Gilles Grangier]] and starring [[Jean Gabin]], [[Françoise Fabian]] and [[Roland Armontel]]. Based on the 1951 novel ''[[Maigret, Lognon and the Gangsters]]'' by [[Georges Simenon]], it is Gabin's third appearance as [[Belgium|Belgian]] writer [[Georges Simenon]]'s fictional detective [[Jules Maigret]].<ref name="hardy">{{cite book|last=Hardy|first=Phil|authorlink=Phil Hardy (journalist)|title=The BFI Companion to Crime|year=1997|page=[https://archive.org/details/bficompaniontocr0000unse/page/217 217]|publisher=[[University of California Press]]|ISBN=9780520215382|url=https://archive.org/details/bficompaniontocr0000unse/page/217}}</ref>

It was shot at the [[Billancourt Studios]] in [[Paris]] and [[location shooting|on location]] across the city. The film's sets were designed by the [[art director]] [[Jacques Colombier (art director)|Jacques Colombier]].

==Synopsis==
A man is run down and injured by a car near the [[Gare du Nord]]. However, by the time the police arrive on the scene the victim has disappeared.
