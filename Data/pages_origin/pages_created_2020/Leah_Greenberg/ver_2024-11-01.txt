'''Leah Greenberg''' is an American political activist and co-founder of the progressive non-profit organization [[Indivisible movement|Indivisible]]. She is co-author of ''We Are Indivisible: A Blueprint for Democracy After Trump'', published in 2019. Greenberg, along with Indivisible co-founder, [[Ezra Levin]], was named by ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' in 2019 as one of [[Time 100|''Time''{{'}}s 100 most influential people in the world]]. She and Levin were selected by ''[[Politico]]'' in 2017 and ''[[GQ]]'' in 2018 for their annual lists of most powerful and influential people in [[Washington DC]]. She is currently the co-Executive Director of Indivisible.

==Early life and education==
Greenberg was raised in [[Chevy Chase, Maryland]]. She is Jewish.<ref name="Times of Israel" /> She graduated from Carleton College in 2008 with a Bachelor of Arts Degree.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.carleton.edu/news/stories/leah-greenberg-08-ezra-levin-07-named-to-time-100-list/ |title=Leah Greenberg '08, Ezra Levin '07 named to Time 100 list |publisher=Carleton College |date=April 17, 2019}}</ref> She later studied at Tufts University, where she received a Master's Degree in Law and Diplomacy.<ref name="Concordia">{{cite web|title=Leah Greenberg bio |url=https://www.concordia.net/community/leah-greenberg/ |website=Concordia |date=January 19, 2016}}</ref>

==Career==
Greenberg began her career working for the philanthropic foundation, Humanity United, where she managed projects to combat human trafficking and slavery. She was an Advisor on human trafficking at the [[State Department of the United States|State Department's]], [[Quadrennial Diplomacy and Development Review]] and later was hired as a staff assistant in the Office of Congressman [[Tom Perriello]] of [[Virginia]]. Greenberg was the policy director for Perriello's gubernatorial campaign in 2017.<ref name="Concordia" /><ref name="Times of Israel">{{cite web |last1=Dolsten |first1=Josefin |title=Meet the Jewish couple leading the Trump resistance |url=https://www.timesofisrael.com/meet-the-jewish-couple-leading-the-trump-resistance/ |newspaper=The Times of Israel |date=March 4, 2017}}</ref>

Greenberg, Levin, Jeremy Haile, and Angel Padilla – all former Congressional staffers – created the online publication ''Indivisible: A Practical Guide for Resisting the Trump Agenda'' in late 2016 in [[Trump resistance|response to the election]] of [[Donald Trump]] as [[President of the United States]]. The guide went viral and the project quickly became a progressive movement.{{citation needed|date=April 2020}} Levin and Greenberg created a website and encouraged supporters to form their own local chapters.<ref name="Time">{{cite magazine|last=Pressley |first=Ayanna |author-link=Ayanna Pressley |title=Time 100 — Leah Greenberg and Ezra Levin |url=https://time.com/collection/100-most-influential-people-2019/5567710/leah-greenberg-ezra-levin/ |magazine=Time Magazine |year=2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Tolan |first1=Casey |title=Meet the husband-wife duo who are sparking a liberal Tea Party movement |url=https://www.mercurynews.com/2017/05/13/anti-trump-indivisible-tea-party/ |newspaper=San Jose Mercury News |date=May 13, 2017 |url-access=subscription}}</ref> In February, 2017, the Indivisible co-founders formed a [[501(c) organization#501(c)(4)|501(c) organization]], with Levin designated as Indivisible's first President and Greenberg as Vice-President.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Schor |first1=Elana |last2=Bade |first2=Rachael |authorlink2=Rachael Bade |title=Inside the protest movement that has Republicans reeling |url=https://www.politico.com/story/2017/02/protest-movement-republicans-234863 |website=Politico |date=February 10, 2017}}</ref>
