'''Wesley Person Jr.''' (born August 1, 1995) is an American professional [[basketball]] player who last played for [[KK Rabotnički|Rabotnički]] of the [[Macedonian Premier League (basketball)|Macedonian League]]. Person played college basketball for the [[Troy Trojans men's basketball|Troy Trojans]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.alabamanews.net/2014/04/18/brantley-star-wesley-person-jr-signs-with-troy|title=Brantley star Wesley Person Jr. signs with Troy|date=April 18, 2014|website=alabamanews.net|accessdate=April 18, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/ncaab/2017/03/15/wesley-person-jr-makes-it-where-dad-didnt-ncaa-tourney/99212494|title=Wesley Person Jr. makes it where Dad didn't: NCAA tourney|date=March 15, 2017|website=[[USA Today]]|accessdate=March 15, 2017}}</ref>

==Professional career==
After going undrafted in the [[2018 NBA draft]], it was reported on July 18, 2018, that Person signed his first professional basketball contract, a one-year deal with the [[Roseto Sharks]] of the [[Serie A2 Basket]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://troytrojans.com/news/2018/7/18/basketball-m-former-trojan-wesley-person-signs-professional-contract.aspx|title=Former Trojan Wesley Person Signs Professional Contract|date=July 18, 2018|website=troytrojans.com|accessdate=July 18, 2018}}</ref>

On October 26, 2019, Person was selected by the [[Maine Red Claws]] with the 4th pick in the 2nd round of the [[2019 NBA G League Draft]] and was included in the training camp roster.<ref>{{Cite web|first1=Evans|last1=Boston|url=https://maine.gleague.nba.com/news/2019-draft|title=2019 NBA G League Draft|date=October 26, 2019|website=NBA.com|accessdate=October 26, 2019}}</ref> However, he was later waived on November 9, 2019, before the [[Maine Red Claws]] finalized the opening night roster.<ref>{{Cite web|first1=Evans|last1=Boston|url=https://maine.gleague.nba.com/news/opening-night-roster-110719|title=Red Claws Announce Opening Night Roster|date=November 7, 2019|website=NBA.com|accessdate=November 7, 2019}}</ref>

On February 19, 2020, [[BC Körmend|Egis Körmend]] announced that they had brought Person to replace [[Erjon Kastrati]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://bckormend.hu/index.php?op=hirek&hirop=egyhir&sid=1969|title=Játékos cserél csapatunk, az Egis Körmend Erjon Kastratinak megköszönjük a munkáját, helyette Wesley Person Jr. érkezik.|language=Hungarian|date=February 19, 2020|website=bckormend.hu|accessdate=February 19, 2020}}</ref>

On September 8, 2021, the [[Newcastle Eagles]] announced that they had signed Person to replace [[Matt Scott (basketball, born 1996)|Matt Scott]], who had suffered a season-ending injury.<ref>{{Cite web|first1=Simon|last1=Rushworth|url=https://newcastle-eagles.com/news/wesley-is-person-of-interest/|title=Wesley is person of interest|date=September 8, 2021|website=newcastle-eagles.com|accessdate=September 8, 2021}}</ref>

For the 2023–24 season, Person signed with [[Korihait]] in Finland.

==Personal life==
Born in [[Brantley, Alabama]], Wesley Person Jr. is the son of former NBA player [[Wesley Person]], and nephew of former NBA player [[Chuck Person]].<ref name=chuckperson>{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nba/columns/story?id=5278158|title=McMenamin: Person more than just a mentor|date=June 12, 2010|website=ESPN.com|accessdate=September 8, 2021}}</ref> Wesley Jr.'s cousin, Adrian Person, is a former nationally ranked JUCO basketball player who averaged a national best 31 points per game at [[Southern Union State Community College]] in [[Wadley, Alabama]] in 1997.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.news-gazette.com/sports/illini-sports/tate-juco-guard-real-deal-coach-says/article_35f40144-7e01-5194-9709-716d67dc5e57.html|title=Tate: Juco guard real deal, coach says|first=Loren |last=Tate|website=The News-Gazette|date=November 5, 1997|accessdate=September 8, 2021}}</ref>
