'''Caylee Hammack''' is an American [[country music]] singer. She is signed to [[Capitol Records Nashville]] and has released one single, "Family Tree".

Hammack was born in [[Ellaville, Georgia]] and began performing locally at age 13. At age 17, she began touring as a member of a cover band.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/singer-songwriter-caylee-hammack-opens-up-about-breaking-through-in-nashville-902010/ | title=Singer/Songwriter Caylee Hammack Opens Up About Breaking Through in Nashville | work=Rolling Stone Country | date=October 22, 2019 | accessdate=December 30, 2019}}</ref> She was discovered by country music singer [[Luke Bryan]] and encouraged by him to move to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] once she had become an adult.<ref name="allmusic"/> Upon moving to Nashville, she found a job at [[Tootsie's Orchid Lounge]], but shortly afterward lost all of her possessions in a house fire.<ref name="people">{{cite web | url=https://people.com/country/caylee-hammack-country-singer-things-to-know/ | title=Meet Caylee Hammack: 5 Things to Know About the 'Family Tree' Singer | work=People | date=November 12, 2019 | accessdate=December 30, 2019 | author=Nancy Kruh}}</ref>

She signed a songwriting deal at age 19. In 2018, she signed to [[Capitol Records Nashville]] and began recording her debut album, which has charted the single "Family Tree".<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web | url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/caylee-hammack-mn0003706939/biography | title=Caylee Hammack biography | publisher=AllMusic | accessdate=December 30, 2019 | author=Stephen Thomas Erlewine}}</ref>
