'''''Astragalus balearicus''''', commonly known as the '''Balearic milkvetch''' or '''gatovell''', is a small [[legume]] of the genus ''[[Astragalus]]'' that is found on the [[Balearic Islands]] off the coast of Spain.

==Description==
[[File:Astragalus.balearicus.jpg|thumb|right|Astragalus balearicus]]
''Astragalus balearicus'' is a small rotund bush with dense leafy foliage. Its leaves are {{convert|1|to|3|cm|in|sigfig=2|abbr=on}} long and commonly divide into between three and five leaflets. It has flowers that are between {{convert|10|and|13|mm|in|sigfig=2|abbr=on}} long and appear between March and July. Its legumes measure between {{convert|7|and|9|mm|in|sigfig=2|abbr=on}}, are glabrous and are slightly ovate.<ref name=herb>{{cite web | title=''Astragalus balearicus'' Chater | website = Herbari Virtual del Mediterrani Occidental | url=http://herbarivirtual.uib.es/ca/general/1247/especie/astragalus-balearicus-chater | accessdate=30 December 2019 | language= es}}</ref><ref>{{cite book | last = De Bolòs | first = Oriol | title = Flora manual dels països Catalans | publisher = Editorial Pòrtic | location = Barcelona | year= 1990 | isbn = 978-8-473-06400-2 | page = 203}}</ref>

In appearance, it resembles a hedgehog, hence its popular name of {{lang|es|eriçóns}} (hedgehog). It is also known locally as {{lang|es|coixinet de monja}} (nun's pad) and {{lang|es|eixorba-rates negre}} (black rat).<ref>{{cite book | last1 = Morales | first1= Ramón | last2 = Maciá | first2 = Manuel Juan | last3 = Dorda | first3 = Elena | last4 = García Villaraco | first4 = Antonio 1993|  year = 2017 | chapter = The Balearic Islands | editor-first=Javier | editor-last=Loidi | title = The Vegetation of the Iberian Peninsula, Volume 2 | journal= Archivos de Flora Ibérica | publisher = CSIC | location = Madrid | issn = 1131-5199 | page=22}}</ref>

==Etymology and taxonomy==
''Astragalus balearicus'' was first described by Arthur Oliver Chater in 1968. Assigned to the genus ''Astragalus'', derived from the Greek {{lang|el|άστράγαλος}}, Chater gave it a geographical epithet due to its prevalence on the Belearic islands. The species is similar to ''[[Astragalus massiliensis]]'' which is found in Sardinia, from which it probably diverged at the end of the [[Oligocene]] when the islands separated from mainland France.<ref>{{cite journal | last = Nimis | first = Pier-Luigi | title = The thorny-cushions vegetation in Mediterranean Italy: phytogeographical problems | journal = Anales del Jardín Botánico de Madrid | volume = 37 | number = 2 | page = 341 | year = 1980}}</ref><ref name=llorens>{{cite book | last1 = Llorens | first1 = Lleonard | last2 = Gil | first2 = Lorenzo |  year = 2017 | chapter = The Balearic Islands | editor-first=Javier | editor-last=Loidi | title = The Vegetation of the Iberian Peninsula, Volume 2 | publisher = Springer International Publishing | location= Cham, Switzerland | isbn = 978-3-319-54866-1 | page=14}}</ref>

==Distribution and habitat==
The legume is endemic to [[Mallorca]] and [[Minorca]] in the Mediterranean Sea, where the cushion-like thorns of the plant create a landscape typical of the islands. It is particularly abundant in the [[Serra de Tramuntana]]. <ref name=llorens/><ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Gil |first1 = Llorenç | last2 = Tébar | first2 = Francisco Javier | last3=Boi | first3 = Marzia | year = 1996 | title = Notes florístiques de les illes Balears (VIII) | journal = Bol/ Soco Hist. Nat. Balears | volume = 39 | page = 118 | issn = 0212-260X | lang=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite book | last = Beckett | first = Elspeth | year = 1988 | title = Wild flowers of Majorca, Minorca and Ibiza | publisher = Balkema | location = Rotterdam | isbn = 978-9-061-91634-5 | page=71}}</ref>

It grows on limestone soils that are more than {{convert|1000|m|ft}} above sea level. It prefers dry, windy, particularly exposed mountainous or coastal, environments which have deep [[calcareous]] soils. It is found with species like [[Paeonia cambessedesii]]. The species is considered to be of Least Concern in the [[IUCN Red List]].<ref name=herb/><ref>{{cite book| last = Bolòs I Capdevila | first = Oriol | title = La vegetació de les Illes Balears: comunitats de plantes | series = Arxius de les Seccions de Ciències | volume = 114 | year = 1996 | location = Barcelona | publisher = Institut d'Estudis Catalans | isbn = 978-8-472-83354-8 | page = 267 | language= es}}</ref>
