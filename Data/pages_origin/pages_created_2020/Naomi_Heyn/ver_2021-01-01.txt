'''Naomi Heyn''' (born 20 September 1998)<ref name="FIH1">{{cite web |url=https://tms.fih.ch/competitions/1162/reports/teams |title=Team Details – Germany |publisher=[[International Hockey Federation]] |website=tms.fih.ch |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref> is a [[field hockey]] player from [[Germany]], who plays as a forward.<ref name="DHB">{{cite web |url=https://web.hockey.de/natio/natio_Damen/news/v5xurz.html |title=DANAS: Reckingers erweiterter Olympiakader steht |publisher=[[Deutscher Hockey-Bund]] |website=web.hockey.de |language=German |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref>

==Early Life==
Naomi Heyn started playing Hockey at the age of 7. In her youth she played for RTHC only. She eventually changed to Rot-Weiss Köln, as she was keen on improving her skills in a team that was in the [[Feldhockey Bundesliga (Women's field hockey)|Bundesliga]]. This Change greatly helped her improve her skills. Because she was performing well in the [[Feldhockey Bundesliga (Women's field hockey)|Bundesliga]], she drew a lot of attention towards her, and her talent was ultimately noticed by coaches of the national team.

==Career==
===Club hockey===
In the German [[Feldhockey Bundesliga (Women's field hockey)|Bundesliga]], Heyn plays club hockey for [[Mannheimer HC]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mannheimerhc.de/bundesliga/damen/ |title=WOMEN - SEASON 19/20 - 1ST BUNDESLIGA |publisher=[[Mannheimer HC]] |website=mannheimerhc.de |language=German |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref><ref name="DHB"/>

===National teams===
====Under–18====
Naomi Heyn made her first appearance in German colours in 2015 at the [[2015 Women's EuroHockey Youth Championship|EuroHockey Youth Championship]] in [[Santander, Spain]]. She went on to represent the team at the Youth Championship the following year in [[Cork (city)|Cork, Ireland]], winning silver medals at both events.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://eurohockey.altiusrt.com/people/4859 |title=HEYN Naomi |publisher=[[European Hockey Federation]] |website=eurohockey.altiusrt.com |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref>

====Under–21====
Heyn represented the [[Germany women's national under-21 field hockey team|Germany U–21]] side on numerous occasions throughout her junior career. Her most notable performance with the team was at the [[2019 Women's EuroHockey Junior Championship|2019 EuroHockey Junior Championship]] in [[Valencia, Spain]], where the team won a bronze medal.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://eurohockey.org/spain-makes-history-as-they-claim-the-eurohockey-junior-championship/ |title=SPAIN MAKES HISTORY AS THEY CLAIM THE EUROHOCKEY JUNIOR CHAMPIONSHIP |publisher=[[European Hockey Federation]] |website=eurohockey.org |accessdate=1 January 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190724160425/https://eurohockey.org/spain-makes-history-as-they-claim-the-eurohockey-junior-championship/ |archive-date=24 July 2019 |url-status=dead }}</ref> 

====Damen====
In 2017, Heyn made her debut for the [[Germany women's national field hockey team|German]] national team during a test series against [[Ireland women's national field hockey team|Ireland]] in [[Düsseldorf, Germany]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://eurohockey.altiusrt.com/people/16346 |title=HEYN Naomi |publisher=[[International Hockey Federation]] |website=tms.fih.ch |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref>

Heyn also appeared for the national team during the [[2019 Women's FIH Pro League|inaugural tournament]] of the [[Women's FIH Pro League|FIH Pro League]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.fihproleague.com/women/players/heyn-naomi__73115__4873 |title=HEYN Naomi |publisher=[[Women's FIH Pro League|FIH Pro League]] |website=fihproleague.com |accessdate=1 January 2020}}</ref> 

In December 2019, Heyn was named in the preliminary German Olympic squad to train for the [[2020 Summer Olympics]] in [[Tokyo, Japan]].<ref name="DHB"/>
