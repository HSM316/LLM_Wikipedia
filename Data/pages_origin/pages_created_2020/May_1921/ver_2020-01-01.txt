The following events occurred in '''May 1921''':

==[[May 1]], 1921 (Sunday)==
*[[1921 Jaffa riots]]: The activities of the [[Jewish Communist Party (Palestine)|Jewish Communist Party]], calling for the establishment of a "[[Soviet Union|Soviet]] Palestine", culminate in a march through the Jewish-Arab border neighbourhood of [[Al-Manshiyya, Jaffa|Manshiyya]], where they clash with the rival socialist [[Ahdut HaAvoda]] group, leading to large-scale rioting involving Jews, Christians, Arabs and Muslims.<ref name=segev>{{cite book |last=Segev |first=Tom |authorlink=Tom Segev |title=One Palestine, Complete |year=1999 |publisher=Metropolitan Books |isbn=0-8050-4848-0 |pages=[https://archive.org/details/onepalestinecomp00sege/page/173 173–190] |url-access=registration |url=https://archive.org/details/onepalestinecomp00sege/page/173 }}</ref> 

==[[May 2]], 1921 (Monday)==
*The [[Silesian_Uprisings#Third_Silesian_Uprising_(1921)|third Silesian civil war]] begins with "Operation Bridges", in which the [[Wawelberg Group]] (Polish forces, under the leadership of [[Wojciech Korfanty]]), destroy German rail bridges.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wyborcza.pl/1,86176,2682777.html|title=2 V 1921. III powstanie śląskie|date=2 May 2005|author=Włodzimierz Kalicki
|language=pl|website=wyborcza.pl|access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref> 
*[[Giovanni Ancillotto]] flies across the [[Andes]] in [[Peru]] in an [[Ansaldo A.1 Balilla]], making the journey from [[Lima]] to [[Cerro de Pasco]] in 1 hour 35 minutes, at an average altitude of 5,500 meters (18,044 feet), reaches a maximum altitude of 7,000 meters (22,966 feet) while passing [[Mount Meiggs]]; he covers the 76 miles from Lima to [[La Oroya]] at an average speed of 230&nbsp;km/hr (143&nbsp;mph).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pueblomartir.wordpress.com//?s=Ancilotto&search=Ir|title=LA AVIACIÓN EN EL CERRO DE PASCO (Cuarta parte)|language=es|trans-title=THE AVIATION IN THE CERRO DE PASCO (Part Four)|date=July 31, 2015|work=PUEBLO MÁRTIR – César Pérez Arauco}}</ref>
*The Japanese passenger ship ''Tokuyo Maru'' catches fire and sinks in the [[Pacific Ocean]]. The US army ship ''Buford'' rescues 65 survivors.<ref>{{Cite newspaper The Times |articlename=Imperial and Foreign News Items |day_of_week=Thursday |date=5 May 1921 |page_number=9 |issue=42712 |column=G }}</ref>
*'''Born:''' [[Satyajit Ray]], Indian filmmaker, screenwriter, music composer, graphic artist, lyricist and author, in Calcutta<ref>{{Cite book|last1=Seton|given1=Marie|authorlink=Marie Seton|year=1971|title=Satyajit Ray: Portrait of a director|publisher=Indiana University Press|isbn=978-0-253-16815-3|url-access=registration|url=https://archive.org/details/portraitofdirect0000seto|page=36}}</ref> (died 1992)

==[[May 3]], 1921 (Tuesday)==
*The province of [[Northern Ireland]] is created within the United Kingdom, under the [[Government of Ireland Act 1920|Government of Ireland Act]].<ref>Alvin Jackson, ''Home Rule – An Irish History'', Oxford University Press, 2004, pp368-370.</ref>
*'''Born:''' [[Sugar Ray Robinson]], US boxer, in Ailey, Georgia<ref>Robinson, Sugar Ray, and [[Dave Anderson (sportswriter)|Anderson, Dave]]. ''Sugar Ray'', London: Da Capo Press, 1994 {{ISBN|0-306-80574-X}} Page 7</ref> (died 1989)

==[[May 5]], 1921 (Thursday)==
*The London Schedule of Payments sets [[World War I reparations]] for Germany and its allies at 132 billion gold marks.<ref>{{cite book|author=Carl Bergmann|title=The History of Reparations|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ECggAAAAMAAJ|year=1927|publisher=E. Benn|page=74}}</ref>
*The lowest attendance in the history of [[The Football League]] is recorded when only 13 paying spectators attend a football match between [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] and [[Stockport County F.C.]] in England. (However, the number of non-paying spectators at the match may have been between 1000 and 2000.)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.footballsite.co.uk/Statistics/Articles/GroundsDoupleUp.htm|title=Two grounds have doubled up on staging League matches on the same day|work=footballsite.co.uk|accessdate=2012-05-08}}</ref>

The violence results in the deaths of five Republicans, and the capture of another 80 by the [[British Army]].<ref>{{cite book|title=Michael Collins's Intelligence War|first=Michael T.|last=Foy|isbn=0-7509-4267-3|pages=214–218}}</ref>
