'''Pa Joseph Ladipo''' (10 July 1941 – 9 May 2013) was a Nigerian [[association football|football]] player and [[Manager (association football)|manager]].<ref>{{WorldFootball.net}}</ref>

==Career==
During his playing career, Ladipo played for [[Shooting Stars S.C.|Shooting Stars]] until 1973. Upon his retirement, he became the club's assistant manager, before being promoted to the first team coach in 1977. From 1982 to 1988, he was the manager of [[Leventis United F.C.|Leventis United]]. He then returned to manage Shooting Stars from 1990 to 1992.{{citation needed|date=October 2024}}

Ladipo was the head coach of the [[Nigeria women's national football team|Nigeria women's national team]] at the [[Football at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2008 Summer Olympics]]. He also led Nigeria to win the [[Football at the 2007 All-Africa Games – Women's tournament|2007 All-Africa Games]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2013/05/coaches-react-to-death-of-jossy-lad/ |title=Coaches react to death of Jossy Lad |date=9 May 2013 |website=Vanguard |access-date=11 May 2018}}</ref> and finished third place at the [[2008 African Women's Championship]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.pmnewsnigeria.com/2014/10/10/awc-can-super-falcons-conquer-africa-again/ |title=AWC: Can Super Falcons Conquer Africa Again? |last=Paul |first=Sam |date=10 October 2014 |website=PM News |access-date=11 May 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://allafrica.com/stories/200811180993.html |title=Nigeria/Ghana: 2008 African Women Championship - Super Falcons Begin Campaign Against Ghana Today |website=Leadership |access-date=11 May 2018}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Ladipo was born in [[Ibadan]], and was nicknamed Jossy Lad.{{citation needed|date=October 2024}} He died on 9 May 2013 at his home in Ibadan at the age of 71.<ref>{{cite news |last=Sanni |first=Tunde |url=https://allafrica.com/stories/201305101191.html |title=Nigeria: Ex-Falcons' Coach, Jossy Lad, Dies At 72 |website=[[AllAfrica.com]] |date=10 May 2013 |access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref>
