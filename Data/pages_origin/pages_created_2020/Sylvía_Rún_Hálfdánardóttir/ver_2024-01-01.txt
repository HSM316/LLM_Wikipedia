'''Sylvía Rún Hálfdánardóttir''' (born 20 September 1998) is an Icelandic former basketball player and a former member of the [[Iceland women's national basketball team|Icelandic national basketball team]].<ref>{{cite news |author1=Bjarni Helgason |title=Hefði viljað vera barn aðeins lengur |url=https://www.mbl.is/sport/korfubolti/2021/06/24/hefdi_viljad_vera_barn_adeins_lengur/ |access-date=24 June 2021 |work=[[Morgunblaðið]] |date=24 June 2021 |language=Icelandic}}</ref>

==Playing career==
Sylvía Rún came up through the junior ranks of [[Haukar women's basketball|Haukar]] and broke into the senior team in 2012. She played with Haukar until 2016, winning the [[Icelandic Women's Basketball Cup|Icelandic Basketball Cup]] in 2014 and the [[Icelandic Women's Basketball Company Cup|Icelandic Company Cup]] in 2015.<ref name="mbl-2019-05-24"/>

After starting the 2016-2017 season with [[Stjarnan women's basketball|Stjarnan]],<ref>{{cite news |author1=Davíð Eldur |title=Sylvía Rún, Danielle og Sigrún Guðný í Stjörnuna |url=https://www.karfan.is/2016/09/sylvia-run-danielle-og-sigrun-gudny-i-stjornuna/ |accessdate=31 December 2019 |work=Karfan.is |date=17 September 2016 |language=Icelandic}}</ref> she stepped away from basketball after three games for personal reasons<ref>{{cite news |author1=Guðrún Ósk Guðjónsdóttir |title=Sylvía er komin á góðan stað eftir erfiða baráttu við kvíða og þunglyndi – Tekur þátt í Ungfrú Ísland |url=https://www.dv.is/bleikt/2017/06/09/sylvia-er-komin-a-godan-stad-eftir-erfida-barattu-vid-kvida-og-thunglyndi-tekur-thatt-i-ungfru-island/ |accessdate=31 December 2019 |work=[[Dagblaðið Vísir]] |date=9 June 2017 |page=9 June 2017 |language=Icelandic}}</ref> but returned to the team the following season where she went on to average 11.0 points and 7.6 rebounds in 12 games.

In September 2018, Sylvía Rún joined [[1. deild kvenna (basketball)|1. deild kvenna]] club [[Þór Akureyri women's basketball|Þór Akureyri]].<ref>{{cite news |author1=Páll Jóhannesson |title=Sylvía Rún Hálfdánardóttir til liðs við Þór |url=http://thorsport.is/frett/?id=12667 |accessdate=31 December 2019 |work=[[Þór Akureyri women's basketball|Þór Akureyri]] |date=10 September 2018 |language=Icelandic}}</ref> On 5 January 2019, Sylvía posted a [[quadruple-double]] with 11 points, 13 rebounds, 10 assists and 10 steals in a victory against [[Njarðvík women's basketball|Njarðvík]].<ref>{{cite news |author1=Kristinn Bergmann Eggertsson |title=Þrennuvaktin: Sylvía Rún með fjórfalda tvennu! |url=https://www.karfan.is/2019/01/thrennuvaktin-sylvia-run-med-fjorfalda-tvennu/ |accessdate=31 December 2019 |work=Karfan.is |date=6 January 2019 |language=Icelandic}}</ref> On 16 March 2019, she scored a career high 40 points in a 70-67 victory against [[Hamar women's basketball|Hamar]]. For the season she averaged 21.7 points, 12.3 rebounds and 5.1 assists. After the season she was named to the [[1. deild kvenna (basketball)#Domestic All-First team|1. deild kvenna Domestic All-First Team]].<ref>{{cite news |author1=Ólafur Þór Jónsson |title=Hrund best í 1. deild kvenna |url=https://www.karfan.is/2019/05/hrund-best-i-1-deild-kvenna/ |accessdate=31 December 2019 |work=Karfan.is |date=15 May 2019 |language=Icelandic}}</ref>

In May 2019, Sylvía signed with reigning national champions [[Valur women's basketball|Valur]].<ref name="mbl-2019-05-24">{{cite news |title=Sylvía Rún til meistaranna |url=https://www.mbl.is/sport/korfubolti/2019/05/24/sylvia_run_til_meistaranna/ |accessdate=31 December 2019 |work=[[Morgunblaðið]] |date=24 May 2019 |language=Icelandic}}</ref> She averaged 10.3 points and 6.6 rebounds in 22 games for Valur before the season was canceled due to the [[2020 coronavirus pandemic in Iceland|coronavirus pandemic in Iceland]]. Following the season, she retired from competitive basketball.<ref>{{cite news |author1=Óskar Ófeigur Jónsson |title=Pálína: Valur missti ekki bara Helenu heldur líka langmesta karakterinn í Valsliðinu í fyrra |url=https://www.visir.is/g/20202015697d/palina-valur-missti-ekki-bara-helenu-heldur-lika-langmesta-karakterinn-i-valslidinu-i-fyrra |accessdate=23 September 2020 |work=[[Vísir.is]] |date=23 September 2020 |language=Icelandic}}</ref>

==National team career==
In 2016, she was named to the Tournament All-First team during the [[2016 FIBA U18 Women's European Championship Division B]] after leading Iceland to a 4th place finish. During the tournament she averaged 16.7 points, 10.7 rebounds and 3.4 steals. Her best game came against Finland where she had 28 points and 20 rebounds.<ref>{{cite news |author1=Ingvi Þór Sæmundsson |title=Sylvía Rún valin í úrvalslið EM |url=https://www.visir.is/g/2016160809987 |accessdate=31 December 2019 |work=[[Vísir.is]] |date=1 August 2016 |language=Icelandic}}</ref> From 2016 to 2019, she appeared in 4 games for the [[Iceland women's national basketball team|Icelandic national basketball team]].<ref>{{cite web |title=A landslið |url=http://kki.is/landslid/konur/leikjafjoldi/a-landslid/ |website=kki.is |publisher=[[Icelandic Basketball Association]] |accessdate=23 September 2020 |language=Icelandic}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Sylvía's parents, Hálfdán Markússon and Sóley Indriðadóttir, both played basketball for [[Haukar]]. Her sister, [[Margrét Rósa Hálfdánardóttir]], played college basketball for [[Canisius Golden Griffins women's basketball|Canisius College]] and played 12 games for the [[Iceland women's national basketball team|Icelandic national team]].<ref>{{cite news |author1=Guðmundur Hilmarsson |title=Hefur allt til að bera til þess að verða frábær |url=https://www.mbl.is/greinasafn/grein/1588617/ |accessdate=31 December 2019 |work=[[Morgunblaðið]] |date=11 March 2016 |language=Icelandic}}</ref>
