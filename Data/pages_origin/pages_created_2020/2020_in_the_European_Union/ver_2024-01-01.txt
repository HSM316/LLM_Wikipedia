== Events ==

=== January ===

* 31 January – The [[United Kingdom]] and [[Gibraltar]] left the [[European Union]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Brexit: UK leaves the European Union |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-51333314 |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=31 January 2019}}</ref>

=== July ===
* July - The European Union refuses an offer of 500 million doses of Covid-19 vaccine from Pfizer-BioNTech.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.france24.com/en/europe/20210206-how-the-eu-s-covid-19-vaccine-rollout-became-an-advert-for-brexit |title=How the EU's Covid-19 vaccine rollout became an 'advert for Brexit' |publisher=France24 |date=6 February 2021}}</ref>

=== November ===

* 17 November – [[Hungary]] and [[Poland]] veto the seven-year EU budget, over attempts to link funding to respect for rule of law. They both said that linking funding to rule of law will mean a loss to their nation's sovereignty.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/nov/16/eu-hungary-veto-budget-viktor-orban|title=EU faces crisis as Hungary and Poland veto seven-year budget|website=[[TheGuardian.com]]|date=16 November 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aa.com.tr/en/economy/hungary-poland-veto-eu-budget-deal/2045854|title=Hungary, Poland veto EU budget deal}}</ref>

=== December ===

* 17 December – [[Court of Justice of the European Union|The European Court of Justice]] rules that EU states can ban kosher and halal ritual slaughter.<ref>{{cite web| url = https://www.politico.eu/article/eu-states-can-ban-kosher-and-halal-ritual-slaughter-court-rules| title = EU states can ban kosher and halal ritual slaughter, court rules – POLITICO| date = 17 December 2020}} </ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.i24news.tv/en/news/international/europe/1608201127-european-court-of-justice-upholds-ban-on-kosher-halal-slaughter-in-belgium|title = I24NEWS}}</ref>
