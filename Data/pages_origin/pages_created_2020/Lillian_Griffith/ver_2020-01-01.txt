'''Lillian Elizabeth A Griffith''' (born 14 July 1877&nbsp;-1972) was a British artist and sculptor.

==Biography==
Griffith was born at [[Abersychan]] in [[Monmouth]] and studied at the [[Wimbledon College of Art]], the [[Slade School of Fine Art]] and the [[Westminster School of Art]] where she studied sculpture under the artists [[Alfred Gilbert]] and [[Alfred Drury]].<ref name="BuckmanVol1">{{cite book|author=David Buckman|publisher=Art Dictionaries Ltd|year=2006|title=Artists in Britain Since 1945 Vol 1, A to L |ISBN=0 953260 95 X}}</ref> She produced sculptures and portrait busts, often in marble or bronze, and plaques and medallions.<ref name="Mackay">{{cite book|author=James Mackay|publisher=Antique Collectors' Club|year=1977|title=The Dictionary of Western Sculptors in Bronze |ISBN=}}</ref><ref name="SG0019">{{cite book|author=Sara Gray|publisher=Dark River|year=2019|title= British Women Artists. A Biographical Dictionary of 1000 Women Artists in British Decorative Arts |ISBN=978 1 911121 63 3}}</ref> From 1902 to 1959 Griffith regularly had works shown at the [[Royal Academy]] in London.<ref name="Benezit6">{{cite book|authors=|publisher=Editions Grund, Paris|year=2006|title=Benezit Dictionary of Artists Volume 6 Gemignani - Herring|ISBN=2 7000 3076 1}}</ref> She also exhibited at the [[Salon des Artistes Francais]] in Paris in 1911 and at a number of British galleries, including the [[Walker Art Gallery]] in [[Liverpool]].<ref name="BuckmanVol1"/> She also exhibited with the South Wales Art Society between 1927 and 1936.<ref name="SG0019"/> Griffith also taught miniature painting at the [[Swansea Art School]] and for [[Glamorgan County Council]].<ref name="BuckmanVol1"/> For a long period she lived at [[Hengoed]] in [[Glamorgan]] but also at [[Maesteg]] and [[Pentre]].<ref name="Mackay"/><ref name="SG0019"/> The [[National Museum Cardiff]] holds examples of her work.
