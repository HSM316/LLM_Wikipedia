'''Ricardo Rozo Ocampo''' (born 7 September 1969) is a Colombian former [[Association football|footballer]] and [[Manager (association football)|manager]].<ref>{{WorldFootball.net}}</ref>

==Career==
Rozo began his career as a footballer, playing for [[Millonarios F.C.|Millonarios]], [[Academia F.C.|Academia]], [[Bogotá F.C.|Bogotá]] and [[Girardot F.C.|Girardot]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://ricardorozofc.com/index.php?action=club |title=Quiénes somos |trans-title=About us |website=Ricardorozo FC |access-date=31 December 2019 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191231033950/https://ricardorozofc.com/index.php?action=club |archive-date=31 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.eltiempo.com/olimpicos-2012/deportes/ricardo-rozo-el-hombre-detras-de-las-superpoderosas-de-colombia/12062643 |title=Olímpicos 2012: Ricardo Rozo, el hombre detrás de las Superpoderosas de Colombia |trans-title=2012 Olympics: Ricardo Rozo, the man behind the Powerpuffs of Colombia |website=[[El Tiempo (Colombia)|El Tiempo]] |date=25 July 2012 |access-date=31 December 2019 |language=Spanish |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161005154524/http://www.eltiempo.com/olimpicos-2012/deportes/ricardo-rozo-el-hombre-detras-de-las-superpoderosas-de-colombia/12062643 |archive-date=5 October 2016}}</ref> He later began coaching in women's football. He was the head coach of the [[Colombia women's national football team|Colombia women's national team]] at the [[2011 FIFA Women's World Cup]] and [[Football at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's tournament|2012 Summer Olympics]].
