'''Ranki Oberoi''' (born 21 September 1993)<ref name="men_long_jump_summer_paralympics_2016">{{Cite web |title=Men's long jump T20 |url=https://www.paralympic.org/static/info/rio-2016/resIPC/pdf/PG2016/AT/PG2016_AT_C73P_ATML20101.pdf |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170104170125/https://www.paralympic.org/static/info/rio-2016/resIPC/pdf/PG2016/AT/PG2016_AT_C73P_ATML20101.pdf |archive-date=4 January 2017 |access-date=21 August 2020 |website=2016 Summer Paralympics}}</ref> is a Dutch [[Para-athletics|Paralympic athlete]] competing in [[T20 (classification)|T20]]-classification [[long jump]] and [[triple jump]] events.<ref name="paralympic_bio">{{Cite web |url=https://www.paralympic.org/ranki-oberoi |title=Ranki Oberoi |website=[[International Paralympic Committee]] |access-date=30 December 2019}}</ref> At the [[2019 World Para Athletics Championships|2019 World Championships]] held in Dubai, United Arab Emirates, he won the gold medal in the [[2019 World Para Athletics Championships – Men's long jump|men's long jump T20]] event.<ref name="bolt_inspired_oberoi_2019">{{Cite news |url=https://www.paralympic.org/news/bolt-inspired-oberoi-now-looks-himself |title=Bolt-inspired Oberoi now looks to himself |date=13 November 2019 |work=[[International Paralympic Committee]] |access-date=30 December 2019}}</ref><ref name="para_atleet_oberoi_wk_2019">{{Cite news |url=https://nos.nl/l/2310246 |title=Para-atleet Oberoi springt naar goud: 'Ik ben gewoon wereldkampioen!' |date=13 November 2019 |work=[[Nederlandse Omroep Stichting|NOS]] |access-date=30 December 2019 |url-status=live |language=Dutch |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191230195115/https://nos.nl/artikel/2310246-para-atleet-oberoi-springt-naar-goud-ik-ben-gewoon-wereldkampioen.html |archive-date=30 December 2019}}</ref> He also [[Netherlands at the 2020 Summer Paralympics|represented the Netherlands]] at the [[2020 Summer Paralympics]] in Tokyo, Japan

== Career ==

In 2014, Oberoi finished in 4th place in the [[2014 IPC Athletics European Championships – Men's long jump|men's long jump T20]] event at the [[2014 IPC Athletics European Championships|IPC Athletics European Championships]] held in Swansea, United Kingdom.

Oberoi competed at the [[2015 IPC Athletics World Championships|2015 World Championships]] in Doha, Qatar where he won the bronze medal in the [[2015 IPC Athletics World Championships – Men's triple jump|men's triple jump T20]] event. He also competed at the [[2017 World Para Athletics Championships|2017 World Championships]] where he finished in 4th place in the [[2017 World Para Athletics Championships – Men's long jump|men's long jump T20]] event. At the [[2018 World Para Athletics European Championships|2018 European Championships]] held in Berlin, Germany, he won the bronze medal in the [[2018 World Para Athletics European Championships – Men's long jump|men's long jump T20]] event.<ref name="para_atleet_oberoi_wk_2019"/>

He also [[Netherlands at the 2016 Summer Paralympics|represented the Netherlands]] at the [[2016 Summer Paralympics]] in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil and he finished in 4th place in the [[Athletics at the 2016 Summer Paralympics – Men's long jump|men's long jump T20]] event.<ref name="men_long_jump_summer_paralympics_2016"/><ref name="paralympic_bio"/>

Oberoi competed in the [[2023 World Para Athletics Championships – Men's long jump|men's long jump T20]] event at the [[2023 World Para Athletics Championships]] held in Paris, France.<ref name="results_book_world_para_athletics_championships_2023">{{Cite web |title=Results Book |url=https://www.paralympic.org/static/info/resATHD23/pdf/ATHD23/AT/ATHD23_AT_B99_AT0000000.pdf |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240809035448/https://www.paralympic.org/static/info/resATHD23/pdf/ATHD23/AT/ATHD23_AT_B99_AT0000000.pdf |archive-date=9 August 2024 |access-date=10 September 2024 |website=2023 World Para Athletics Championships}}</ref> He finished in 5th place.<ref name="results_book_world_para_athletics_championships_2023"/>
