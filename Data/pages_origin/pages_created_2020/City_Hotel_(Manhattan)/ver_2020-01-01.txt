[[File:New York Broadway 1831.jpg|thumb|300px|City Hotel, 1831]]
The '''City Hotel''' (1794–1849) stood at 113–119 Broadway, occupying the whole block bounded by Cedar, Temple, and Thames Streets, in the [[Financial District, Manhattan|Financial District]] of [[Manhattan]], New York City. It has been called probably "the first newly built structure in America that qualifies as a hotel".{{r|Landau}} Designed by [[John McComb Jr.]], it offered not only luxurious accommodations, but also such amenities as shops, a barroom, and a coffeehouse, as well as public dining and dancing.{{r|Gotham}} It replaced the former home of [[Stephen Delancey]] (French: ''Étienne de Lancy''), built in 1700, which had become an inn.{{r|Archaeological}}
