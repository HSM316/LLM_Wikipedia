'''Vrouwenvoetbal Alkmaar''', commonly known as '''VV Alkmaar''', was a Dutch [[association football|football]] club that competed in the [[Eredivisie (women)|Eredivisie]], the top women's league in the Netherlands, until 2023. During its time in the Eredivisie, it was the only team in the league not affiliated with a men's professional club.

==History==
From 2007 until 2011, [[AZ Alkmaar (women)|AZ Alkmaar]] had a successful women's club, but when it decided to dissolve the women's team for financial reasons, most of the players moved to nearby [[Telstar (women)|SC Telstar]] in Velsen, which started a women's club in 2011. Telstar continued to train in [[Alkmaar]] because its offices were there. After the [[2016–17 Eredivisie (women)|2016–17 season]], the women's club decided to separate from Telstar, move to Alkmaar, and form a new club.<ref>[http://nos.nl/artikel/2160763-vrouwen-van-telstar-zoeken-hun-heil-onder-de-vlag-van-alkmaar.html (Dutch) Vrouwen van Telstar zoeken hun heil onder de vlag van Alkmaar], NOS, 1 maart 2017</ref> 

In late 2022, the club announced that [[AZ Alkmaar (women)|AZ Alkmaar]] would compete in the Eredivisie beginning in the [[2023–24 Eredivisie (women)|2023-2024 season]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Molen |first=Paul van der |date=2022-12-28 |title=Vrouwenvoetbal terug bij AZ |url=https://www.vvalkmaar.nl/2022/12/28/vrouwenvoetbal-terug-bij-az/ |access-date=2024-08-02 |website=VV Alkmaar, Eredivisie club voor Vrouwenvoetbal in Alkmaar |language=nl}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-12-28 |title=Drievoudig landskampioen AZ terug in eredivisie; neemt plek in van VV Alkmaar |url=https://nos.nl/artikel/2457966-drievoudig-landskampioen-az-terug-in-eredivisie-neemt-plek-in-van-vv-alkmaar |access-date=2024-08-02 |website=nos.nl |language=nl}}</ref>
