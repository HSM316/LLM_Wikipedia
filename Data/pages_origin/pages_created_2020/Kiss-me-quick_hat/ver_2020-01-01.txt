A '''kiss-me-quick hat''' is  a British seaside novelty hat, typically bearing the words "Kiss me quick" or "Kiss me quick, squeeze me slow".<ref name="DT">https://www.telegraph.co.uk/expat/life/a-very-british-day-at-the-beach-in-pictures/kiss-me-quick-hat0/</ref><ref>https://www.culture24.org.uk/history-and-heritage/historic-buildings/art557041-The-great-British-seaside-collections-come-together-for-Seaside-Heritage-project</ref>  Culturally, the [[Daily telegraph]] dcsirbes them as "one step up from a knotted handkerchief".<ref name="DT" />

==Rhyming slang and derivative terms==
"Kiss me quick" hsa been used as rhyming slang for "prick".<ref name="Partridge 2015">{{cite book|author1=Tom Dalzell|author2=Terry Victor|title=The New Partridge Dictionary of Slang and Unconventional English|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=bbcBCgAAQBAJ&pg=PA1332|date=26 June 2015|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-1-317-37252-3|pages=1332â€“}}</ref>   Also used as a ism as in  "kiss-me-kwik seaside towns." - describing "cheap, dated, seaside towns" according to [[Eric Partridge|Partridge]].<ref name="Partridge 2015" />
