'''''Proletarian Revolution''''' ([[Russian language|Russian]]: Пролетарская революция) was a [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] historical journal published in Moscow from 1921 to 1941.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://explore.bl.uk/BLVU1:LSCOP-ALL:BLL01003203931|title=Пролетарская Революция. Исторический журнал Истпарта. No. 1-113; 1933. No. 1, 2; 1934. No. 3; 1938. No. 4-7, 9; 1939. No. 1-4; 1940. No. 1-4; 1941. No. 1. - British Library}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://dic.academic.ru/dic.nsf/sie/14144/%D0%9F%D0%A0%D0%9E%D0%9B%D0%95%D0%A2%D0%90%D0%A0%D0%A1%D0%9A%D0%90%D0%AF|title="ПРОЛЕТАРСКАЯ РЕВОЛЮЦИЯ" - это... Что такое "ПРОЛЕТАРСКАЯ РЕВОЛЮЦИЯ"?}}</ref> In a review of the first issue, [[Illarion Mgeladze|Ilya Vardin]] reported the journal as declaring "Our goal is precisely to help the writing of the history of the proletarian revolution in Russia. Nobody will read documentary raw materials, except the historians themselves, and we need books that would be read by both workers and students."<ref name="Vardin">{{cite web |title=SovLit - СовЛит: Вардин И. Пролетарская Революция. Без места. 1921. N 1 |last=Vardin|first=Vardin|url=http://www.ruthenia.ru/sovlit/j/167.html |website=www.ruthenia.ru |access-date=19 January 2021}}</ref>

==Istpart period==
From 1921 to 1928 it was published by [[Istpart]], the History of the Party Department, a commission originally founded by the [[Communist Party of the Soviet Union|Russian Communist Party (Bolsheviks)]].<ref name="MIA">{{cite web |title=Some Questions Concerning the History of Bolshevism |url=https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/stalin/works/1931/x01/x01.htm |website=www.marxists.org |publisher=MIA |access-date=19 January 2021}}</ref> The purpose of the journal was to publish articles, documents and memoirs on the history of the workers' movement, the Communist Party, the [[October Revolution]] and the [[Russian Civil War|Civil War]] as well as materials about prominent leaders of the party and the workers' and social democratic movement.<ref name=":0" />

==Lenin Institute period==
From 1928 to 1931 it was published by the [[Marx–Engels–Lenin Institute#Lenin Institute|Lenin Institute]].<ref name="MIA"/>

In 1931, [[Stalin|Joseph Stalin]] sent a letter, "Some Questions Concerning the History of Bolshevism" to the magazine sharply criticizing the published article by [[Anatoly Slutsky]], "The Bolsheviks on German Social Democracy in the Period of its Pre-War Crisis", describing it as anti-party and half-Trotskyist. In turn, this letter was also published in the journal.<ref name="MIA"/>

==Marx-Engels-Lenin Institute period==
From 1933 to 1941 it was published by the [[Marx-Engels-Lenin Institute]].<ref name="MIA"/>

== Editors ==
At various times the journal was edited by: [[Mikhail Olminsky|M. S. Olminsky]], [[Semyon Kanatchikov|S. I. Kanatchikov]], [[Maximilian Saveliev|M. A. Saveliev]], [[Vilhelm Knorin|V. G. Knorin]] , [[Vasily Sorin|V. G. Sorin]] and [[Mark Borisovich Mitin|M. B. Mitin]].
