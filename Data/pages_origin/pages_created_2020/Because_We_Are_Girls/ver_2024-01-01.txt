'''''Because We Are Girls''''' is a Canadian documentary film, directed by [[Baljit Sangra]] and released in 2019.<ref>Zofeen Maqsood, [https://www.americanbazaaronline.com/2019/07/15/because-we-are-girls-documentary-438235/ "¡®Because We Are Girls¡¯, a new documentary, talks about an immigrant Punjabi family with a dark secret"]. ''The American Bazaar'', July 15, 2019.</ref> The film centres on Jeeti, Kira and Salakshana Pooni, three [[Punjabi Canadian]] sisters from [[Williams Lake, British Columbia|Williams Lake]], [[British Columbia]] who have gone public in adulthood about allegations of childhood [[sexual abuse]] by a cousin who frequently babysat them as children.<ref>Ken Eisner, [https://www.straight.com/movies/1261941/bleak-family-history-unfolds-nfbs-because-we-are-girls "A bleak family history unfolds in the NFB's Because We Are Girls"]. ''[[The Georgia Straight]]'', July 3, 2019.</ref>

The film was slated to premiere at the 2018 [[Vancouver International Film Festival]], but the screening was pulled from the festival as the sisters' court case against their cousin was still pending.<ref>Craig Takeuchi, [https://www.straight.com/movies/1140161/bc-sexual-abuse-documentary-because-we-are-girls-pulled-viff-due-legal-case "B.C. sexual-abuse documentary Because We Are Girls pulled from VIFF due to legal case"]. ''[[The Georgia Straight]]'', September 21, 2018.</ref> It instead premiered at the 2019 [[Hot Docs Canadian International Documentary Festival]],<ref>Jagdeesh Mann, [https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/film/article-hot-docs-2019-the-riveting-painful-because-we-are-girls-shines-a/ "Hot Docs 2019: The riveting, painful Because We Are Girls shines a #MeToo spotlight on Canada¡¯s South Asian community"]. ''[[The Globe and Mail]]'', April 22, 2019.</ref> and had its first screening in British Columbia at the [[DOXA Documentary Film Festival]].<ref>Janet Smith, [https://www.straight.com/movies/1232211/doxa-2019-trauma-turns-sisterhood-because-we-are-girls "DOXA 2019: Trauma turns to sisterhood in Because We Are Girls"]. ''[[The Georgia Straight]]'', April 24, 2019.</ref>

The film received two nominations at the [[Vancouver Film Critics Circle Awards 2019]], for Best Canadian Documentary and [[Vancouver Film Critics Circle Award for Best British Columbia Film|Best British Columbia Film]].<ref name=mack>Adrian Mack, [https://www.straight.com/movies/1337036/marriage-story-dominates-vancouver-film-critics-circle-noms "Marriage Story dominates Vancouver Film Critics Circle noms"]. ''[[The Georgia Straight]]'', December 13, 2019.</ref>
