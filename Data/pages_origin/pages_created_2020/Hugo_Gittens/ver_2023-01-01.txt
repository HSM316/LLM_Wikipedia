'''Hugo Gittens''' (born 24 September 1936) is a [[Olympic weightlifting|weightlifter]] from [[Trinidad and Tobago]]. He represented the country at the [[1964 Summer Olympics]] and the [[1968 Summer Olympics]].<ref name="SportsRef">{{cite Sports-Reference |url=https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/gi/hugo-gittens-1.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200418102304/https://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/gi/hugo-gittens-1.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=18 April 2020 |title=Hugo Gittens Olympic Results |access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=17 November 2020|title=Hugo GITTENS - Olympic Weightlifting {{!}} Trinidad and Tobago|url=https://www.olympic.org/hugo-gittens|access-date=20 November 2020|website=International Olympic Committee|language=en}}</ref> Additionally, Gittens won a gold medal at the [[1966 Commonwealth Games]] and placed fourth in the [[1970 British Commonwealth Games|1970 Commonwealth Games]] in the 67.5&nbsp;kg combined men's weightlighting event.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Hugo Gittens {{!}} Commonwealth Games Federation|url=https://thecgf.com/results/athletes/55433|access-date=20 November 2020|website=thecgf.com|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Liburd|first=Lasana|date=15 April 2018|title=I Dream in gold! Wired868 reviews the good, bad and ugly of the 2018 Commonwealth Games|url=https://wired868.com/2018/04/15/i-dream-in-gold-wired868-reviews-the-good-bad-and-ugly-of-the-2018-commonwealth-games/|access-date=20 November 2020|website=Wired868|language=en-US}}</ref>
