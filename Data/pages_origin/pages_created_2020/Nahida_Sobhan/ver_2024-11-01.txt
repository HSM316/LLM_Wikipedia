'''Nahida Sobhan''' is a Bangladeshi diplomat. She is the first female diplomat representing Bangladesh in the Middle East as she became ambassador to Jordan on 21 February 2020.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-02-20 |title=First Bangladeshi female envoy to Mideast vows to promote women's cause |url=https://www.thedailystar.net/international/news/first-bangladeshi-female-envoy-mideast-vows-promote-womens-cause-1870690 |access-date=2024-02-23 |website=The Daily Star |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=King accepts credentials of new ambassadors to Jordan |url=https://kingabdullah.jo/en/news/king-accepts-credentials-new-ambassadors-jordan-2 |access-date=2024-02-23 |website=kingabdullah.jo}}</ref> Earlier, she served as the director general of the UN Department in the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/2019/12/31/nahida-sobhan-made-bangladesh-ambassador-to-jordan |title=Nahida Sobhan, 1st female ambassador in the Middle East |date=2019-12-31 |work=Dhaka Tribune |access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

== Education ==
Sobhan completed her SSC from [[Agrani School and College|Agrani Girls' School]] in 1984 and HSC from [[Holy Cross College, Dhaka|Holy Cross College]] in 1986.<ref name=mofa>{{Cite web |url=https://amman.mofa.gov.bd/bn/site/page/Brief-Curriculum-Vitae-of-Nahida-Sobhan,-Ambassador-of-Bangladesh |access-date=2024-02-23 |website=amman.mofa.gov.bd |title=Brief Curriculum Vitae of Nahida Sobhan, Ambassador of Bangladesh}}</ref> She holds a bachelor of arts (honors) and a master's degree in English literature from the [[University of Dhaka]] in 1992 and in 1994 respectively.<ref name=mofa/> She was trained in public international law at [[The Hague Academy of International Law]] in Netherlands. She also obtained a diploma in international relations from the Institute of Public Administration in Paris, France. She is equally fluent in French, English and Bengali.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://en.prothomalo.com/bangladesh/news/207250/Nahida-Sobhan-new-envoy-to-Jordan |title=Nahida Sobhan new envoy to Jordan |work=Prothom Alo |language=en |access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.unb.com.bd/category/Bangladesh/nahida-sobhan-new-bangladesh-ambassador-to-jordan/39911 |title=Nahida Sobhan new Bangladesh ambassador to Jordan |work=UNB |language=en |access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>

==Career==
Sobhan is a career foreign service officer, belonging to 15th batch of [[Bangladesh Civil Service]] (BCS) foreign affairs cadre. She served in various capacities at Bangladesh missions in Rome, Kolkata and Geneva.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://bdnews24.com/people/bangladesh-appoints-nahida-sobhan-as-first-female-ambassador-in-middle-east |title=Bangladesh appoints Nahida Sobhan as first female ambassador in Middle East |work=bdnews24.com |date=31 December 2019 |access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref> Sobhan was appointed Bangladesh High Commissioner to Canada in May 2024 by the [[Government of Bangladesh|Government of the People's Republic of Bangladesh]]. <ref>{{Cite web |last=Islam Hasib |first=Nurul |date=30 May 2024 |title=Bangladesh appoints Nahida Sobhan as new High Commissioner to Canada |url=https://www.dhakatribune.com/bangladesh/foreign-affairs/347895/bangladesh-appoints-nahida-sobhan-as-new-high |url-status=live |access-date=1 October 2024 |website=Dhaka Tribune}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Sobhan is married and has a son, and a daughter.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://bangla.bdnews24.com/bangladesh/article1707029.bdnews |script-title=bn:মধ্যপ্রাচ্যে প্রথম বাংলাদেশি নারী রাষ্ট্রদূত নাহিদা |work=bdnews24.com |language=bn |access-date=2019-12-31}}</ref>
