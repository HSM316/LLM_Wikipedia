'''''Eucalyptus × stoataptera''''' is a small tree that is endemic to a small area on the south coast of Western Australia. It has a dense crown, smooth bark on the trunk and branches, glossy, oblong leaves, single flower buds in leaf [[wikt:axil|axil]]s, lemon-orange flowers, and fruit that are square in cross-section. It is a natural [[Hybrid (biology)|hybrid]] between ''[[Eucalyptus stoatei]]'' and ''[[Eucalyptus tetraptera|E. tetraptera]]''.

==Description==
''Eucalyptus × stoataptera'' is a tree that typically grows to a height {{cvt|4|m}}. It has smooth dark grey and light grey bark on the trunk and branches. Adult leaves are arranged alternately, the same shade of glossy green on both sides, oblong with a long-pointed tip, {{cvt|90-110|mm}} long and {{cvt|30-35|mm}} wide and [[Petiole (botany)|petiolate]]. The flower buds are arranged singly in leaf axils on a downturned [[Peduncle (botany)|peduncle]] {{cvt|30-35|mm}} long becoming flattened near the [[Hypanthium|floral cup]], the individual buds pendent on a very short [[Pedicel (botany)|pedicel]]. Mature buds are red, square in cross-section with a wing on each corner, {{cvt|42-48|mm}} long and {{cvt|24-28|mm}} wide with a conical [[Operculum (botany)|operculum]]. The fruit is a pendulous, woody, red [[Capsule (botany)|capsule]] {{cvt|36-58|mm}} long and {{cvt|25-32|mm}} wide with a wing on each corner and up to five small ribs between each pair of wings.<ref name="Nuytsia">{{cite journal |last1=Bennett |first1=Eleanor Marion |title=Hybrid between ''Eucalyptus tetraptera'' and ''Eucalyptus stoatei'' from Jerdacuttup, Western Australia |journal=Nuytsia |date=1995 |volume=10 |issue=1 |pages=1–5 |url=https://www.biodiversitylibrary.org/item/224896#page/7/mode/1up |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref><ref name=FloraBase>{{FloraBase|name=''Eucalyptus × stoataptera''|id=15182}}</ref>

==Taxonomy and naming==
''Eucalyptus × stoataptera'' was first formally described in 1995 by [[Eleanor Marion Bennett]] in the journal ''[[Nuytsia (journal)|Nuytsia]]'' from specimens she collected between [[Ravensthorpe, Western Australia|Ravensthorpe]] and [[Hopetoun, Western Australia|Hopetoun]] in 1991. The [[Botanical name|specific epithet]] is a combination of the names of its parents, ''[[Eucalyptus stoatei|E. stoatei]]'' and ''[[Eucalyptus tetraptera|E. tetraptera]]''.<ref name="Nuytsia" />

==Distribution and habitat==
This eucalypt grows in low to medium [[Mallee Woodlands and Shrublands|mallee shrubland]] with the parent species and ''[[Eucalyptus kessellii|E. kessellii]]'' in the [[Esperance Plains]] [[IBRA|biogeographic region]].<ref name="Nuytsia" /><ref name="FloraBase" />
