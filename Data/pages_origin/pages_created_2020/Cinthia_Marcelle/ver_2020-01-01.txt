'''Cinthia Marcelle''' (born 1974, Belo Horizonte) is a Brazilian installation artist. She studied at the [[Federal University of Minas Gerais|Universitadad Federal de Minas Gerais]].<ref name="Phaidon Editors">{{cite book |last1=Phaidon Editors |title=Great women artists |date=2019 |publisher=Phaidon Press |isbn=0714878774 |page=261}}</ref> She has had solo exhibitions at [[MoMA PS1]]<ref name="MoMA PS1">{{cite web |title=Projects 105: Cinthia Marcelle |url=https://www.moma.org/calendar/exhibitions/3606?locale=en |website=The Museum of Modern Art |accessdate=31 December 2019 |language=en}}</ref>, and the [[Secession Building|Secession]]<ref name="Secession">{{cite web |title=Cinthia Marcelle |url=https://www.secession.at/en/exhibition/cinthia-marcelle-2/ |website=Secession |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>.<ref name="Artsy">{{cite web |title=Cinthia Marcelle |url=https://www.artsy.net/artist/cinthia-marcelle |website=Artsy |accessdate=31 December 2019 |language=en}}</ref> She has been included in the the 2018 [[Berlin Biennale]]<ref name="bb10.berlinbiennale">{{cite web |title=Cinthia Marcelle |url=https://bb10.berlinbiennale.de/artists/c/cinthia-marcelle |website=bb10.berlinbiennale |accessdate=31 December 2019 |language=en}}</ref>, the 2013 [[Istanbul Biennial]], the 2012 [[List of New Museum Triennial Artists|New Museum Triennial]]<ref name="New Museum">{{cite web |title=The Generational Triennial: "The Ungovernables" |url=http://ca.newmuseum.org/index.php/Detail/Occurrence/Show/occurrence_id/1462 |website=New Museum |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>, the 2015 [[Sharjah Biennial]]<ref name="Sharjah Art Foundation">{{cite web |title=Projects |url=http://sharjahart.org/sharjah-art-foundation/projects/at-the-risk-of-the-real |website=Sharjah Art Foundation |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>, and the 2017 [[Venice Biennale]]<ref name="Biennial Foundation">{{cite web |title=Cinthia Marcelle's "Chão de caça" Pavilion of Brazil at the 57th International Art Exhibition |url=http://www.biennialfoundation.org/2017/04/cinthia-marcelle-pavilion/ |website=Biennial Foundation |accessdate=31 December 2019 |date=21 April 2017}}</ref>.<ref name="Artsy"/> She is the recipient of the 2010 [[Future Generation Art Prize|Future Generation Prize]].<ref name="NOW Modern Art">{{cite web |title=Cinthia Marcelle |url=http://www.nowmodernart.com/cinthia-marcelle |website=NOW Modern Art |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>

Marcelle lives and works in Belo Horizonte.<ref name="Sprovieri Gallery">{{cite web |title=Cinthia Marcelle |url=https://www.sprovieri.com/artists/cinthia-marcelle |website=Sprovieri Gallery |accessdate=31 December 2019}}</ref>
