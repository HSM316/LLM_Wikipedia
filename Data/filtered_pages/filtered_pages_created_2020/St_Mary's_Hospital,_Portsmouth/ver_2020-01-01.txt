St Mary's Hospital is a health facility in Milton Road, Portsmouth, Hampshire, England. It is managed by Solent NHS Trust.

History
The facility has its origins in the Portsea Island Union Workhouse and Infirmary which was designed by Augutus Livesay and Thomas Ellis Owen and which opened in 1846. The facility became St Mary's Hospital in 1930 and joined the National Health Service in 1948. In May 2018 the trust announced plans to refurbish the buildings and turn the site into a health campus.
