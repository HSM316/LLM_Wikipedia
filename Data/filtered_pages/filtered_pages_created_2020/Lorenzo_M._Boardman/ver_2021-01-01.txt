Lorenzo M. Boardman (c. 1832 - July 16, 1897) was an American architect. Born in Pennsylvania, he was an architect in Minneapolis, Minnesota for three decades. He spent the last ten years of his life in Spokane, Washington, where he designed many buildings, including the Traders Block, Temple Court, the Ross Block, and the Windsor Block.
