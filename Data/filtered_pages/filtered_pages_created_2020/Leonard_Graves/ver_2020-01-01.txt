Leonard Graves was an actor and singer best know for narrating the 1952 television documentary Victory at Sea. Graves was the only voice on the 26-part series,
which won multiple awards, including a 1954 Emmy for Best Public Affairs Program,Emmy awards —Victory at Sea a special Peabody award.IMDB award page for Victory at Sea and the Freedoms Foundation's George Washington Medal.Peter C. Rollins, Victory at Sea: Cold War Epic, Gary R. Edgerton & Peter C. Rollins (eds.), Television Histories. Shaping Collective Memory in the Media Age, Kentucky 2001, pp. 103–122

Graves was born in Memphis, Tennessee in 1927 and began his performing career in radio. He joined the Broadway cast of The King and I as the Interpreter and also understudied for Yul Brynner as the King, filling in on many occasions. Graves took over the lead when Brynner left the show, and after a two year run on Broadway, he joined a national tour.Ask Vance: Leonard Graves, Vance Lauderdale, Memphis Magazine, January 11, 2018 Graves later moved to Israel with his wife where he was active in the Israel National Opera. He reportedly died circa 2008.Kauai and Victory at Sea, Hank Soboleski, The Garden Island, August 11, 2019An update on Memphis Singer and Actor Leonard Graves, Vance Lauderdale, Memphis Magazine, January 31, 2018
