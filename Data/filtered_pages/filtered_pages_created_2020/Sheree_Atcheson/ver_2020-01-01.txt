Sheree Atcheson (born February 28, 1991) is a Sri-Lankan Irish computer scientist and Head of Diversity and Inclusion at Monzo. Atcheson has been recognised as one of the Most Influential Women in UK Technology. She is the Global Ambassador for Women Who Code.

 Early life and education 
Atcheson was born in Sri Lanka. At only three weeks old Atcheson was adopted by a family in County Tyrone. As a child she played computer games with her brother. When her cousin was appointed as a senior engineer at the New York Stock Exchange in Belfast Atcheson decided that she wanted to pursue a career in technology.BBC. (2019). BBC - Sheree Atcheson.  Career 
After graduating Atcheson joined Kainos as a software engineer before joining SR Laboratories.Kainos. (2019). Kainos: transformational Digital Services and market-leading Digital Platforms. [online] Available at: https://www.kainos.com/ In 2016 Atcheson joined Deloitte, where she worked as a business consultant for strategy and architecture. She was eventually promoted lead for inclusion, and helped to design and implement the Deloitte Inclusion Strategy. At Deloitte Atcheson coordinated the BrightStart scheme programme, where Deloitte employees study toward degrees while working. In 2019 she was appointed as Head of Diversity and Inclusion at Monzo. She has written for Forbes and The Guardian.

 Awards and honours 
Atcheson was selected as one of the most influential women in the UK tech sector. She was selected by WeAreTheCity as one of the United Kingdom's Rising Stars. In 2019 she was selected as Queen's University Belfast Graduate of the Year.
