 Description 
It was the first commercial product of the research center CSELT in the field of voice technology: it was built with diphone-technology aimed to reach a high computational efficiency. As a result, the produced voice is still "robotic", but more natural than the previous generation voice implemented in MUSAPaolo Baggia, Speech Technologies and Standards, DIT Seminars, Povo, Trento, June 7th, 2006 by CSELT itself. In the past, ELOQUENS was applied to automatically read timetables in the Italian train stations and some telephone services provided by Telecom Italia, e.g. the automated address book service.

Nowadays, it is released as freeware: it is nowadays typically used in spoken comments in many YouTube videos.
