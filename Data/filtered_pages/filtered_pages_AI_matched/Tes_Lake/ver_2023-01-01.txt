Tes Lake (, ) is a natural tourist attraction in the Lebong Regency. It is one of the largest lakes in Indonesia. Tes Lake is the main hydroelectric (hydropower) supplier for nearly all of Bengkulu Province. The lake covers an area of approximately 750 hectares.

 Area description 
Based on the Decree of Forestry Minister No. 385/Kpts-II/1985, December 27, 1985, Tes Lake covers an area of 3.230 Hectares.

Flora
Because of its climate, crops such as coffee are mainly grown. Other florae that grow around the area are Meranti, Dyera costulata, Kayu Gadis, Pulai, Gelam, and other plants which also grow elsewhere in Bengkulu Province.

Fauna
A lot of animals can be found in Tes Lake including tigers, gibbons, monkeys, snakes, bears, many kinds of domestic birds, dogs, many types of domestic fish and many other creatures. 

Location
Tes Lake lies in the south of Lebong Regency, Bengkulu. Settlements near the lake include Karang Anyar, Kutai Donok (Central Village), Talang Ratu Village and Tes Village.

 Topography 
Its elevation is 950m to 600m above sea level.

Geology and climate
Based on information from an exploration conducted in 1964, the main types of soil found in Tes Lake are regosol, andosol, latosol, and podzol (brown podzol soil).

With more wet seasons on average,
Tes Lake receives an average annual rainfall intensity of 2.700-3.500 mm. The area is classified as type A, with a Q value of approximately 0.9% - 7.7%, according to classifications from Schimidt and Ferguson.
