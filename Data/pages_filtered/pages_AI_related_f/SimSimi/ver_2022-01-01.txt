SimSimi is an artificial intelligence conversation program created in 2002 by ISMaker. The application has led to controversy and protests in Thailand for some of its responses containing profanity and criticisms of leading politicians. It grows its artificial intelligence day by day assisted by a feature that allows users to teach it to respond correctly. SimSimi, pronounced as "shim-shimi", is from a Korean word simsim (심심) which means "bored". It has an application designed for Android, for Windows Phone (developed by Kevin Tan) and for iOS.

SimSimi reached some popularity around 2015 when it was featured in a YouTube video by YouTuber Comedy Shorts Gamer, the video reached 4 million views by 2017.
