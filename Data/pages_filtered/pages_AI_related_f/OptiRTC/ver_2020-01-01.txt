OptiRTC is an American technology company that has developed a software as a service platform for civil infrastructure. The OptiRTC platform is a cloud-native platform that integrates sensors, forecasts, and environmental contexts to actively control stormwater infrastructure.OptiRTC Platform Retrieved January 02, 20155 Innovations in Cyber-Water Retrieved August 22, 2016 The OptiRTC platform is built on Microsoft Azure and uses internet of things technology to predicatively manage distributed water systems.Internet of Everything Meets Water Retrieved December 30, 2014

History
In June 2011, the OptiRTC team partnered with ioBridge to develop smart city tech based on ioBridge's hardware solution and OptiRTC's platform.Geosyntec, ioBridge to Develop Storm Water Management Technology Retrieved January 03, 2015

In November 2013, the OptiRTC team was assigned a patent for "Combined water storage and detention system and method of precipitation harvesting and management" that was co-invented by Marcus Quigley.OptiRTC Patent Retrieved August 24, 2015

In November 2014, the New York Economic Development Corporation (NYCEDC) selected Opti technology as the winner of the first Smart City Expo World Congress.NYC EDC Selects Opti Technology Retrieved August 24, 2015

In December 2014, OptiRTC was formally incorporated as an independent company through a spin-out from Geosyntec Consultants.OptiRTC Inc Formed Retrieved December 30, 2014

In January 2015, the Water Environment Research Foundation (WERF) published a research paper on High Performance Green Infrastructure, which focused primarily on distributed real-time control of stormwater infrastructure.WERF Report Retrieved January 11, 2015

In January 2016, Opti began working with Particle to provide the communications layer for Opti's products.How Opti Leverages the IoT to Improve Stormwater Management Retrieved August 18, 2016
